## Recurring constitutional evidence

### 1. Pressure is orientation evidence, not question authority

`possible_observation_to_lawful_question_competency_interrogation.md` gives the closest prior recovery. That same recovery says pressure, disturbance, concern, or unbounded possibility may become a bounded investigation only when constrained by evidence, scope, authority, negative authority, unknowns, and stop condition. `pressure_visibility_competency_frontier.md` independently preserves pressure as reportable evidence classification rather than recovery authority. **Recurring constitutional effect:** investigation pressure can orient attention, but it does not ask the lawful bounded question by itself.

### 2. Orientation preserves possible relevance without promotion

`question_to_inquiry_transition_characterization.md` characterizes the transition from observation / preserved note / operator concern to implementation-backed inquiry. It supports a weakened sequence: possible questions may become visible to an operator or document author, but Seed does not semantically enumerate, authorize, or spawn them. A lawful path requires either an exact operator-supplied `QuestionFamily` or a preserved inquiry note, then implementation-backed eligibility / boundary checks, map-backed bounded-work selection or surface-local orientation, dispatch / answer-surface execution, and surface-local evidence collection.

`candidate_inquiry_reconciliation.md` makes the same distinction for ambiguity. Candidate inquiries are a supported recovery/methodology pattern, but not an automatic runtime token-expansion artifact. Ambiguity is reduced by bounded, evidence-bearing inquiry or safe stop, not by direct semantic interpretation.

**Recurring constitutional effect:** orientation can preserve a possible direction and related material, but it must remain non-promotional unless bounded admission occurs.

### 3. Eligibility constrains claim strength before selection or answer

`inquiry_eligibility_characterization.md` recovers a distributed eligibility discipline that precedes methodological selection. Its recurring work is to compare candidate inquiry, boundary, vocabulary, family, or implementation direction against repository observation, prior work, evidence strength, claim strength, and authority boundary. It may reject, defer, preserve as open/unsupported, narrowly authorize, or stop with insufficient evidence.

The same characterization distinguishes eligibility from Methodological Selection: eligibility determines whether a candidate has earned consideration at all; Selection Path explains ordering, non-selection, unsupported targets, and selected outcomes after a candidate set or implemented surface exists.

**Recurring constitutional effect:** pressure is transformed toward a lawful investigation only when the candidate boundary is eligible at the claimed strength.

### 4. Exact bounded identity is required for app dispatch

The question-family implementation evidence in `seed_runtime/question_surface_inventory.py` and `scripts/seed_local.py` supports exact identity rather than semantic routing. Known question families are static inventory rows with bounded status, authority boundary, required arguments, answer responsibility, and dispatch relationship. Bounded ask requires an exact family string and exact required arguments where applicable; unknown, diagnostic-only, or not-dispatchable families stop lawfully.

`question_family_registration_boundary_audit.md` reinforces this boundary by finding no first-class `QuestionFamilyRegistration`, `FamilyRegistration`, `InquirySubjectRegistration`, `RecoveredFamily`, or `FamilyRecovery` implementation that automatically converts recovered vocabulary into public answerable family identity. Public answerability currently enters through static question-family inventory and bounded ask maps.

**Recurring constitutional effect:** a lawful bounded investigation needs bounded subject identity and admissible surface identity. Recurrence of vocabulary is insufficient.

### 5. Admissible evidence and excluded evidence are explicit parts of the work

Across constitutional and implementation recoveries, lawful investigation does not simply gather everything. It identifies admissible evidence, excludes unsupported evidence, and preserves excluded material as limitation, unknown, counterexample, negative authority, or future question.

Diagnostic inventory and diagnostic shape audit implement this as declaration-vs-shape evidence. Reasoning Path implements it by exposing derivation evidence when available and unknowns when unavailable. Selection Path implements it by exposing candidates, factors, non-selected alternatives, and unknown outcomes for unsupported targets. Inquiry artifacts implement it by preserving supported conclusions, unsupported conclusions, open questions, gaps, pressure, boundaries, and unknowns without creating workflow or planning behavior.

**Recurring constitutional effect:** the transformation is not from pressure to answer; it is from pressure to a bounded evidence contract.

### 6. Lawful stop is a successful constitutional outcome

The reviewed recoveries consistently treat insufficient evidence, unknown family, diagnostic-only status, not-dispatchable status, no derivation evidence, unsupported selection target, unsupported owner, unsupported vocabulary stabilization, and presentation-only labels as successful stops rather than failures requiring invention.

`unknown_currency_survey.md` describes unknown as a stable non-settlement and bounded-inquiry currency. `inquiry_eligibility_characterization.md` treats `Insufficient implementation evidence` as a completed eligibility check. `possible_observation_to_lawful_question_competency_interrogation.md` says lawful termination is required because stronger claims would require unsupported architecture such as a universal inquiry engine, automatic question generator, potential-question object, registration lifecycle, scheduler, or semantic interpretation.

**Recurring constitutional effect:** pressure becomes lawful investigation only if the investigation has a lawful stop condition before answer, promotion, or implementation.

## Recovered recurring transitions

### Transition A: Pressure to orientation

- **Input:** operator pressure, disturbance, broad concept, candidate vocabulary, or investigation pull.
- **Work:** preserve as possible relevance without truth, command, plan, selection, implementation, or question-family identity.
- **Output:** orientation pressure, not a bounded investigation yet.
- **Stop:** if no admissible boundary appears, preserve pressure/unknown/frontier only.

### Transition B: Orientation to bounded investigation candidate

- **Input:** oriented pressure or possible bounded question.
- **Work:** name the subject, scope, family, candidate boundary, excluded evidence, and authority source at the smallest supportable scale.
- **Output:** bounded investigation candidate.
- **Stop:** if subject identity or family identity is unsupported, preserve as open question or unsupported conclusion.

### Transition C: Candidate to eligible bounded investigation

- **Input:** bounded investigation candidate.
- **Work:** compare claim strength to repository evidence; check prior work; distinguish implementation-local, family-local, methodology-local, document-visible, and presentation-only evidence.
- **Output:** eligible bounded investigation at a narrowed scope, or insufficient evidence.
- **Stop:** if evidence is insufficient, stop lawfully without promoting the candidate.

### Transition D: Eligible investigation to bounded evidence contract

- **Input:** eligible bounded investigation.
- **Work:** define admissible evidence, excluded evidence, preserved Unknowns, authority boundary, and lawful stop.
- **Output:** one lawful bounded constitutional investigation.
- **Stop:** when the bounded question is answered, unknown remains preserved, or evidence cannot support further movement.

### Transition E: Investigation to answer / non-answer

- **Input:** bounded evidence contract.
- **Work:** review implementation and constitutional evidence; distinguish support, counterexample, limitation, and unknown.
- **Output:** supported recovery, smaller invariant, family-local transitions, or no recurring recovery.
- **Stop:** answer exactly the bounded question without optimizing for elegance, implementation, or architecture.

## Supported distinctions

### Investigation pressure != orientation

Pressure is the unresolved force or pull. Orientation is bounded preservation of possible relevance. Orientation may include related material and uncertainty, but it still refuses truth, command, plan, implementation, selection, and dispatch authority.

### Orientation != bounded investigation

Orientation can help locate a subject, but it does not itself define admissible evidence, excluded evidence, claim strength, scope, or stop authority. A bounded investigation begins only after those limits are explicit.

### Bounded investigation != answer

A bounded investigation is a lawful evidence contract. It may recover an answer, a smaller invariant, several family-local transitions, or no recurring recovery. It may also stop with preserved Unknowns.

### Admissible evidence != all visible evidence

App output, recurring vocabulary, reports, frontiers, presentation labels, and pressure are visible evidence only at their own authority level. They become admissible for a claim only when the claim strength matches their evidence strength.

### Preserved Unknown != failure

Unknowns are lawful non-settlement. They preserve unsupported boundaries without inventing owners, engines, schedulers, planners, ontology, or implementation readiness.

### Lawful stop != abandoned work

A lawful stop is the constitutional end of the bounded investigation when the evidence cannot support further movement. It prevents pressure from becoming architecture by force.

## Direct answer to the bounded question

Across recurring repository recoveries, the constitutional work that consistently transforms investigation pressure into one lawful bounded constitutional investigation is **not question formation** and not an implemented question owner.

The recurring work is:

```text
bounded inquiry admission discipline:
pressure is held as non-promotional orientation until repository evidence can
bound the subject, scope, family/surface identity, admissible evidence,
excluded evidence, claim strength, Unknowns, authority boundary, and lawful
stop condition for exactly one investigation.
```

If any of those conditions cannot be supported, the lawful result is not a bounded constitutional investigation. The lawful result is a preserved Unknown, unsupported candidate, diagnostic-only or not-dispatchable status, frontier-only pressure, or explicit insufficient evidence.

Therefore the evidence supports **one smaller shared invariant** rather than a new universal participant:

```text
No investigation pressure becomes constitutional work until boundedness,
eligibility, evidence boundary, Unknown preservation, and stop authority are
explicit and evidence-proportional.
```
