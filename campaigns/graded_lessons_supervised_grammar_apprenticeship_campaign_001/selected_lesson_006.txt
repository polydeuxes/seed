LESSON 6. ANALYSIS. On the following sentences, let the pupils be exercised according to the model. +Model+.--_Intemperance degrades_. Why is this a _sentence?_ Ans.--Because it expresses a thought. Of what is something thought? Ans.--Intemperance.
Which word tells what is thought? Ans.--_Degrades_. 1. Magnets attract. 2. Horses neigh. 3. Frogs leap. 4. Cold contracts. 5. Sunbeams dance. 6. Heat expands. 7. Sunlight gleams. 8. Banners wave. 9. Grass withers. 10. Sailors climb. 11. Rabbits burrow. 12. Spring advances. You see that in these sentences there are two parts. The parts are the _+Subject+_ and the _+Predicate+_. +DEFINITION.--The _Subject of a sentence_ names that of which something is thought+.
+DEFINITION.--The _Predicate of a sentence_ tells what is thought+. +DEFINITION.--The _Analysis of a sentence_ is the separation of it into its parts+. Analyze, according to the model, the following sentences. +Model+.--_Stars twinkle_. This is a _sentence_, because it expresses a thought. _Stars_ is the _subject_, because it names that of which something is thought; _twinkle_ is the _predicate_, because it tells what is thought.
+To the Teacher+.--After the pupils become familiar with the definitions, the "Models" may be varied, and some of the reasons maybe made specific; as, "_Plants_ names the things we tell about; _droop_ tells what plants do," etc. Guard against needless repetition. 1. Plants droop. 2. Books help. 3. Clouds float. 4. Exercise strengthens. 5. Rain falls. 6. Time flies. 7. Rowdies fight. 8. Bread nourishes. 9. Boats capsize. 10. Water flows. 11. Students learn. 12. Horses gallop.
