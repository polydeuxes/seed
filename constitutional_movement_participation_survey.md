Repository authority wins.

## Boundary

This is exactly one bounded Constitutional Movement Participation Survey.

It determines only what recurring constitutional participants are presently visible before lawful movement may occur, according to already recovered repository evidence.

It does not recover implementation, execution architecture, a movement framework, a constitutional event model, a lifecycle, scheduler, planner, registry, participant taxonomy, universal physiology, or canonical constitutional movement.

The word `participant` is used only as a survey handle for independently recurring material that appears before or within lawful movement. It is not promoted into canonical repository vocabulary.

## Core answer

According to recurring repository evidence, lawful constitutional movement presently requires a bounded movement situation with enough participants to prevent unsupported movement: a bounded subject, repository evidence and support/provenance, competent authority with boundary and negative authority, local boundary/locality, eligibility or admissibility where the movement has prerequisites, preserved unknowns, confidence/evidence-strength limits, and lawful stop/refusal conditions.

The evidence does not support a canonical participant taxonomy, a universal ordering, or a rule that every participant must appear as a separately named section in every artifact. Several participants overlap locally. Some are mandatory only for movements that make a claim, rely, admit, promote, translate, project, record, or cross a boundary. Repository authority wins.

## Recurring movement relationships

The reviewed evidence supports these movement relationships without recovering canonical movement:

- **Constrain** requires boundary, negative authority, locality, evidence limits, and stop/refusal. It may operate without producing a new domain artifact.
- **Evaluate** requires bounded subject, evidence/support, eligibility or admissibility where applicable, unknown preservation, confidence, and stop conditions.
- **Transform / translate / project / record / transition** require provenance/support preservation, competent local authority, boundary and locality, unknown preservation, confidence limits, and termination when claim strength or authority would be exceeded.
- **Promote** requires family-local evidence, competent family-local authority, eligibility/admissibility, boundary, negative authority, preserved unknowns, confidence, and stop conditions.
- **Rely / consume** requires warrant: evidence/support, provenance, role, authority boundary, negative authority, unknowns, and confidence must remain available.
- **Observe structurally** may occur with fewer semantic participants, but only because it is bounded as read-only structure visibility and explicitly refuses semantic interpretation, authority inference, ontology promotion, event-ledger writes, and mutation.
- **Preserve unknown** may occur when other participants are missing, but it is a stop-preserving movement, not a substitute for promotion or reliance.

## Mandatory and optional participation at survey resolution

### Recurring mandatory participants where independently supported

For lawful constitutional movement that makes, changes, promotes, translates, projects, records, relies on, evaluates, or crosses a constitutional boundary, recurring repository evidence presently supports these mandatory participants:

1. bounded subject;
2. repository evidence/support/provenance sufficient for the movement;
3. competent granted authority;
4. authority boundary and negative authority;
5. locality / competent local boundary;
6. eligibility/admissibility/prerequisites where the movement type requires admission or readiness;
7. preserved unknowns when evidence does not resolve them;
8. confidence/evidence-strength limits for claim or reliance strength;
9. lawful stop/refusal conditions.

### Recurring optional or locally variable participants

- A separately named bounded-question section is not proven mandatory for every movement, even though bounded subject recurs.
- A separate eligibility/admissibility finding is locally variable where the movement makes no admission or promotion claim.
- A new unknown is not mandatory where no unresolved claim is being introduced, but existing unknowns must not be erased.
- A separate confidence section is not proven mandatory for purely structural visibility, but confidence limits are mandatory for stronger claims and reliance.
- Output/handoff may occur, but performed movement is constitutionally distinct from produced artifact.
- Independent inspection is mandatory for this survey discipline and recurs broadly, but repository evidence does not prove it is separately named in every lawful movement.
