# Book Projection Pass 037 — Constitutional Kind Labels Are Not Ontology Closure

## Current Book comparison

The current Book states that artifact representation differs from constitutional standing, that artifact shape differs from constitutional kind, and that a dedicated dataclass or concordance entry does not by itself establish a durable constitutional kind. That coverage is directionally adequate, but the independent no-ontology-closure boundary is not addressable as a clause.

## Projection made

Added `01.Standing.B — Constitutional kind labels are not ontology closure` to `book_of_seed/01-grammar-and-standing/constitutional-kinds-and-artifact-standing.md`.

The clause preserves that a label, artifact form, characterization category, dataclass name, concordance entry, inventory row, or recurring report type may preserve a bounded standing distinction only for the subject and consumer it names. It forbids treating such labels or forms as ontology closure, universal equivalence among similarly named materials, mandatory artifact grammar, or production authority without preserved evidence, provenance, scope, confidence limits, Unknowns, and the applicable establishment boundary.

## Boundaries preserved

The projection does not create an exhaustive kind taxonomy, implementation schema, artifact grammar mandate, or completion claim.
