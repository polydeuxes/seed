## Recovered handoff relation

A bounded examination output crosses into comparison or finding standing only when a consumer preserves the output's source, provenance, method or support basis, subject, scope, authority, confidence or uncertainty, Unknowns, standing, and forbidden inferences.

The producer is the bounded examination or probe-result boundary. The constitutional act is bounded evidentiary characterization or cross-examination. The crossing artifact is not a final answer; it is testimony, evidence, comparison standing, conflict, contradiction, agreement, refinement, insufficiency, or Unknown within the comparison boundary. The next consumer becomes eligible to use the scoped result for sufficiency, warrant, explanation, reliance, refusal, deferment, or further examination only within preserved limits.

## Authority change

The authority changes from permission to examine to scoped evidentiary or comparison use. This change does not promote the output into established fact, source independence, warrant, reliance, implementation permission, or reconciliation merely because the output exists, agrees with another output, contradicts another output, repeats a claim, or appears complete.

Where the output lacks provenance, subject binding, method basis, scope, or authority, the lawful standing is insufficiency or Unknown. Where outputs conflict, the relation may support conflict standing rather than answer standing.

## Branches, bypasses, convergence, and stop

Movement may branch into multiple local findings, partial findings, conflicts, Unknowns, and additional probe needs. It may bypass comparison when the inquiry asks only for a scoped explanation of one adequately preserved output or when authority/refusal stops earlier. It may converge when independently preserved findings are compared without erasing their source-local limits. It may stop when comparison shows insufficiency, contradiction without resolving authority, exhausted evidence territory, missing provenance, stale material, unavailable material, or enough bounded support for the next local consumer.

## Cross-examination

### Constitutional Grammar

The act produces bounded standing for a finding or relation, not universal knowledge. The grammar requires each input's subject, support, scope, confidence, Unknowns, and forbidden inferences to remain inspectable.

### External Grammar

If an examination output contains external or translated material, comparison cannot assimilate the source grammar. It must preserve source role, provenance, translation scope, uncertainty, refusal conditions, and the distinction between source testimony and Seed-side characterization.

### Fidelity

Fidelity requires downstream consumption to remain limited to tested preservation. A comparison artifact is unfaithful if it converts method output into truth, drops refusal conditions, erases source-local uncertainty, or expands the compared boundary beyond the examined evidence.

### Frontier standing

The original admitted inquiry remains bounded by passes 051 through 054. A partial finding can narrow the inquiry, expose a neighboring pressure, or support a stop, but it does not retire the frontier or neighboring possible inquiry unless a separate stopping artifact has that scope.

### Responsibility

The comparison consumer owns only the bounded comparison act. It does not inherit probe ownership, source ownership, admission ownership, or completion ownership merely because it consumes their artifacts.

### Reliance

Reliance is not automatic. Later consumers may rely only on the scoped comparison or finding standing actually produced, including negative authority, Unknowns, conflicts, and untested boundaries.

### Stopping

Lawful stops include insufficiency, conflict, contradiction, unsupported provenance, stale or unavailable evidence, outside-scope material, refusal, deferment, and Unknown. A stop here is not a global finding, global impossibility, or complete inquiry pipeline result.

## Book projection decision

No Book change is warranted. `04.Examination.C` already preserves cross-examination without source-local erasure and forbids truth establishment, warrant, reliance, source independence, implementation permission, or reconciliation merely from comparison. Evidence and authority clauses already preserve the non-promotion boundary. This pass records the pipeline topology for current-state use.
