## Bounded ingress relation recovered

Operator expression can become constitutionally addressable pressure only as source-attributed pressure with preserved limits.

```text
operator or external expression
+ source/provenance or preserved Unknown
+ bounded reference to the need, uncertainty, refusal, or pressure being carried
= addressable operator pressure
!= Constitutional Grammar, inquiry identity, admitted inquiry, answer standing, authority, evidence, or implementation ownership
```

The expression consumed is the operator ask, prompt phrase, uncertainty statement, urgency, presentation label, or interface wording. The constitutional act is not admission. It is addressability: the repository may hold the expression as external/operator pressure for examination, translation assessment, clarification, refusal, narrowing, or frontier consideration while refusing to let the expression's wording create Seed grammar.

## Cross-examination

### External Grammar

The operator's wording remains external/operator expression. It may be quoted, summarized, compared, or attached only with source role, provenance or provenance Unknown, source grammar boundary, uncertainty, confidence or urgency limits, authority limits, and refusal conditions.

### Constitutional Grammar

Addressability does not make operator vocabulary native Seed grammar. Terms such as continuation, source navigation, current work position, or active edge remain presentation/operator vocabulary unless implementation evidence and constitutional authority independently establish them.

### Fidelity

A faithful ingress representation preserves what was received and what was not established. It is unfaithful to erase known loss, invent an owner, mutate urgency into authority, relocate constitutional authority to the interface, or summarize addressability as admission.

### Inquiry-frontier identity

Addressable pressure is weaker than frontier identity. The pressure may orient possible inquiry, but a frontier still requires conjunctive identity: need/uncertainty, source and provenance, context, included and excluded scope, evidence territory, sufficiency and stop conditions, Unknowns, conflicts, and negative authority.

### Admission and responsibility

Admission requires additional authority. Addressable pressure has no universal owner, no automatic current-task priority, no admission to work, and no examination eligibility.

### Lawful stop

If source, provenance, scope, authority, or identity cannot be sufficiently preserved, the lawful result is refusal, clarification need, deferment, narrowing, or Unknown. Refused or untranslated pressure remains visible when evidence supports preservation; it is not silently deleted.
