# Implementation Visibility, Readiness, Projection, and Diagnostic Family

## Independent evidence inspected

Primary witnesses: `constitutional_implementation_pressure_characterization.md`, `constitutional_readiness_implementation_audit.md`, `constitutional_read_model_family_audit.md`, `constitutional_pipeline_operations.md`, `constitutional_recurrence_survey_implementation_completion.md`, `projection_diagnostics_family_completion_audit.md`, `implementation_grammar_visibility_audit.md`, `diagnostic_inventory`-related tests and audits referenced by repository instructions, and `constitutional_pipeline_*` implementation slices.

## Smallest recurring distinctions

- **Pressure is not readiness.** A gap or need can justify inquiry, not implementation by itself.
- **Readiness is not ownership.** Implementation readiness still requires owner, shape, evidence, and tests.
- **Projection is not source truth.** Read models and projection surfaces display or derive bounded state; they are not the source of law.
- **Diagnostic record is not cluster mutation.** Diagnostic outputs should be scoped, recordable as diagnostic runs, and non-mutating unless intentionally operational.
- **Visibility is part of operational work.** New operational surfaces must be inventoried and shape-audited.

## Contradictions and refinements

This family compresses several constitutional families in implementation: a diagnostic may expose question, evidence, Unknown, projection, and capability boundaries at once. The repository instructions refine this compression by requiring diagnostic inventory, shape audit, `record_scope=diagnostic_run`, and `mutates_cluster=false` proofs for diagnostic surfaces.

## Local findings vs wider recurrence

Pipeline implementation slices are local operational witnesses. Wider recurrence is that implementation must not silently become constitutional authority; it must expose its shape, limits, scope, and non-mutating status.

## Implementation witnesses

Read-model contracts, pipeline public surfaces, diagnostic inventory tests, diagnostic shape-audit tests, and implementation completion audits are direct witnesses. They prove concrete surfaces only.

## Preserved Unknowns

- Which recurring reports deserve implementation.
- Exact ownership of all projection/read-model families.
- Whether diagnostic inventories cover every operational surface.

## What may be relied upon

Implementation witnesses can prove that a bounded surface exists and behaves as specified. They may be used as evidence for recovery when scope and command are preserved.

## What may not be relied upon

Do not treat implementation existence as Book law, source truth, mutation authority, or complete coverage of the corpus.
