## Selected topology pressure

`07.Realization.B` says a Fidelity determination examines a specific realization boundary. The unresolved topology pressure is how that boundary is identified strongly enough to support a scoped finding without merely listing protected constitutional boundaries.

The recovered relation is:

```text
review boundary under Fidelity
is selected by implementation realization evidence
and constrained by the constitutional authority claimed to be realized
```

Fidelity does not first choose a favored constitutional clause and then search for nearby implementation. It also does not let any implementation object define the constitutional subject by convenience. A lawful review boundary appears where implementation evidence shows a concrete changed-form, handoff, distribution, narrowing, compatibility, rendering, diagnostic, or other realization seam that purports to carry or preserve a bounded constitutional authority.

## Evidence followed

`constitutional_fidelity_characterization.md` gives the two-sided evidence rule: constitutional authority is determined only by completed constitutional evidence, while implementation ownership and mechanics are determined only by implementation evidence. It names lawful freedoms such as narrowed realization, distributed realization, audit-local combined realization, compatibility-only mechanics, and orchestration-only mechanics, but refuses to turn those patterns into required topology.

`07.Realization.B` preserves the same relation at Book level: the determination consumes the authority to be preserved and the implementation realization evidence for the translated, handed off, distributed, narrowed, or changed-form mechanism under review.

Passes 041 through 043 sharpen this selection rule. Pass 041 requires a translated artifact or form-change seam before warrant survival can be examined. Pass 042 requires a producer/artifact/consumer or handoff relation before consumer eligibility can be tested. Pass 043 requires the finding to stop at the reviewed evidence, boundary, surface, artifact, and time of review.

## Recovered Fidelity relation

A Fidelity review boundary is not a constitutional topic name. It is the concrete realization seam at which constitutional authority is alleged to survive changed form.

The boundary under review is supported only when both sides are present:

1. a bounded constitutional authority, warrant, limit, Unknown, stop condition, recording boundary, mutation boundary, or consumer limit that must survive; and
2. implementation realization evidence showing a specific translated form, handoff, distribution, narrowing, compatibility object, diagnostic row, rendering, dispatch request, payload, or other changed-form mechanism that is capable of carrying, preserving, obscuring, or crossing that authority.

If the constitutional side is missing, the implementation seam may be visible but Fidelity cannot infer the authority being preserved. If the implementation side is missing, the constitutional authority may be known but Fidelity cannot identify a realization boundary. In either case the topology remains Unknown.

## Cross-examination

### Authority

The relation preserves derivative authority. Fidelity may identify the reviewed boundary only by pairing authority evidence with realization evidence. It may not create the authority to be preserved, and it may not promote implementation convenience into constitutional authority.

### Evidence

The evidence burden is conjunctive rather than substitutive. Constitutional evidence alone supports a protected responsibility but not the realization seam. Implementation evidence alone supports a mechanism but not its constitutional authority. The Fidelity boundary exists where the two are connected by evidence, not where their vocabulary resembles each other.

### Responsibility

Fidelity's responsibility is selection for review, not ownership of the selected responsibility or mechanism. Selecting a boundary for Fidelity review does not make Fidelity the owner of admission, orientation, recovery, diagnostic recording, dispatch, or the implementation component.

### Handoff

A handoff can define the review boundary only when the producer/artifact/consumer relation is evidenced. A passing artifact, request, rendered payload, or row does not by itself prove responsibility transfer or producer occurrence.

### Reliance

Later reliance may cite the Fidelity finding only for the selected boundary. It may not rely on the selection of one realization seam as proof that adjacent seams, upstream producers, downstream consumers, or all implementations are faithful.

### Lawful stop

The lawful stop is reached when the selected boundary is sufficiently identified for the scoped inquiry or when either side of the required pair is missing. Missing topology is not filled by analogy; it is preserved as Unknown.

## Preservation, crossing, and Unknown

Supported preservation can be recognized only for the identified seam. Boundary crossing can be recognized when evidence shows that this seam erases, invents, mutates, or relocates the authority that was supposed to survive. Unknown is required when no concrete realization seam is evidenced, when the constitutional authority is not established, or when their connection is merely convenient vocabulary.

## Book projection decision

No Book change is warranted in this pass. `07.Realization.B` already states that Fidelity examines a specific realization boundary by consuming both constitutional authority and implementation realization evidence. This pass recovers the internal selection topology of that clause for current-state use, but it does not add an independently new Book relation beyond the existing bounded-act grammar.
