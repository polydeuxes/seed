## Recovered relation

An inquiry frontier identity is conjunctive. It is established only when the possible inquiry is bound to the exact need or uncertainty at issue, the source/provenance of that need, the current movement or consumer-local boundary in which it matters, included and excluded scope, available evidence territory, sufficiency and lawful stopping conditions, Unknowns, conflicts, and negative authority.

The identity cannot be repaired by prompt wording, report titles, family names, adjacency, uniqueness, stale evidence, unavailable evidence, or the fact that a need was selected for consideration. Those items may be testimony or pressure, but they are not identity establishment.

## Cross-examination

### Constitutional Grammar

Question standing already separates question posture from answer standing. The frontier relation deepens that boundary: even before answer standing, the possible inquiry must be identifiable as this bounded uncertainty under this boundary. Constitutional Grammar therefore receives a frontier possibility only when identity, scope, evidence territory, Unknowns, and stop limits are preserved rather than inferred.

### External Grammar

Operator asks, presentation labels, external representations, and prompt phrasing may supply addressable material only through bounded translation. Passes 048 through 050 require source role, provenance, grammar boundary, uncertainty, limits, Unknowns, and refusal conditions to survive cross-seam consumption. That grammar prevents external wording from becoming inquiry identity by assimilation.

### Fidelity

Fidelity reconciliation requires a bounded determination to consume constitutional authority and realization evidence and to produce a scoped finding. By analogy and constraint, a frontier identity must preserve the invariants it claims to carry. If the seam from pressure to frontier drops source, scope, Unknowns, negative authority, or stop limits, the frontier is Unknown or not established rather than a faithful inquiry identity.

### Admission and authority

Admission as bounded participation does not ratify truth, mutation authority, or reusable approval. A selected inquiry need can participate in frontier consideration, but admission to inquiry movement requires another authority change. Frontier identity is therefore weaker than admitted inquiry and stronger than raw pressure.

### Responsibility

No universal owner map is recovered. Existing ingress, need projection, selection, horizon, frontier, and question artifacts each own local preservation boundaries. Frontier identity cannot assign inquiry ownership, deletion authority, retirement authority, or execution responsibility by adjacency.

### Unknown and lawful stop

Missing selected-need binding, ambiguous native lineage, conflicting horizon identity, unsupported scope, stale evidence, unavailable evidence, or absent stopping testimony must be preserved as Unknown, conflict, stale, unavailable, or not-established frontier standing. Lawful stop occurs before source selection, observation authorization, question formulation, execution, recording, ledger writing, or mutation when these boundaries are absent.
