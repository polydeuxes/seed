# Book Projection Pass 028 — Movement Is Not Mutation

## Book comparison

The Book already distinguishes orientation from movement, act from artifact, execution from recording, event from fact, and access/capability from use authority. It did not directly preserve the recovered cross-family movement/mutation boundary in the movement chapter.

## Projection decision

A direct Book change was warranted because the distinction is independently supported, is broader than implementation mechanics, and does not promote the unresolved general action/mutation architecture.

## Book change

Added `03.Movement.A — Movement is not mutation` to `book_of_seed/03-goals-and-advancement/orientation-and-movement.md`.

The clause preserves movement as a warranted transition in lawful position, standing, focus, or advancement posture, which may occur without mutating runtime state, external systems, the event ledger, or cluster truth. It also preserves that runtime writes, recorded events, route changes, or projected state updates do not establish constitutional movement without the responsible warrant and limits.

## Not projected

General action/mutation architecture, exact access-transition topology, exhaustive act inventory, and runtime implementation mechanics remain report-only.
