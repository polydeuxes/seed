# Grammar Seam Pass 048 — Addressability Without Assimilation

Repository authority wins.

## Prior evidence consumed

This pass begins from the recovery inventory, projection reconciliation 044, and Fidelity topology passes 045 through 047. It also consumes the existing observation/provider/external-source family and the current Book clause `01.External.A`.

The selected boundary is not a provider taxonomy and not a universal external-source ontology. It asks what makes an external representation constitutionally addressable before, and without, becoming Constitutional Grammar.

## Bounded grammar relation recovered

An external representation becomes constitutionally addressable when it can be held at an attributed reference boundary:

```text
addressable external representation
= source-bearing representation + bounded reference + provenance/limits sufficient for examination
!= native constitutional grammar, adopted law, repository authority, or cluster truth
```

Addressability is therefore weaker than translation, attachment, adoption, or consumption. It means Seed can point to, compare, quote, inspect, or reason about the representation as external material while preserving the source grammar as external.

## Constitutional Fidelity cross-examination

### What remains external

The source vocabulary, representation structure, confidence language, owner categories, provider categories, and source-native relations remain external unless independently translated or projected with bounded warrant. The representation may be addressable without its grammar becoming constitutional grammar.

### What may be translated or attached

Only the referenced material within the stated source, scope, provenance, uncertainty, and authority limit may be translated or attached. Translation may create a Seed term or bounded claim for examination, but the external carrier and its source grammar remain attributed.

### What Constitutional Grammar may consume

Constitutional Grammar may consume the fact that an attributed external representation exists for the bounded purpose of examination, comparison, evidence orientation, or translation assessment. It may not consume the source's categories as native kinds, the source's confidence as repository authority, or availability as standing.

### Standing received

The material receives addressable external standing: it can be examined as source-attributed material. It does not receive Book standing, adopted-law standing, established-fact standing, cluster-truth standing, or implementation authority by addressability alone.

### Survivals required

The following must survive any addressability claim:

1. source role or preserved Unknown source role;
2. provenance or preserved Unknown provenance;
3. source grammar boundary;
4. scope of referenced material;
5. uncertainty and confidence limits;
6. authority limit;
7. refusal to infer native standing from visibility, usefulness, recurrence, or attachment.

### Lawful narrowing or form change

A reference may narrow the external material to a quotation, field, example, row, witness, comparison target, or translated claim if the narrowing is declared and does not pretend the narrowed form is the whole source grammar. Changed form is lawful only when it preserves attribution, boundary, and limits.

### Erasure, invention, mutation, or relocation of authority

It is erasure to drop provenance, source grammar, Unknowns, confidence limits, or authority limits. It is invention to supply a source role, owner, taxonomy, or constitutional kind not present in evidence. It is mutation to treat the external representation as repository truth or cluster fact. It is relocation of authority to make source availability, provider confidence, attachment, or lexical recurrence act as constitutional authority.

## Unknowns preserved

Complete external-source taxonomy, provider taxonomy, source-owner map, translation owner, canonical attachment form, and universal external representation schema remain Unknown.

## Book projection decision

Book change is warranted. The current Book preserves the source grammar translation boundary, but the central seam also needs the weaker prior standing: constitutional addressability without assimilation. Without that seam, translation could be mistaken as the first lawful contact with external material, or addressability could be mistaken for adoption.

The smallest projection is a new clause in `external-and-constitutional-grammar.md` stating that addressability is source-attributed standing for examination and does not assimilate source grammar or source authority.

## Result

Pass 048 recovers the first seam relation: external representations may become constitutionally addressable as attributed external material without becoming Constitutional Grammar. Fidelity requires provenance, source role or Unknown, source grammar boundary, scope, uncertainty, authority limits, and refusal conditions to survive.
