## Recovered corpus summary

The non-Book corpus recovers a constitutional grammar of conservative movement. Evidence may support movement, but authority bounds it. Artifacts may carry findings, but do not become law by existence. Questions bind work, but do not settle outcomes or establish answers. Access and capability do not authorize use. Movement is not mutation. Correlation or sequence is not causation. Attribution is not responsibility. Analytical competence is not adjudicative authority. Lawful realization preserves authority without requiring structural symmetry; faithful form change requires warrant survival, handoff creates bounded consumer eligibility rather than authority transfer, Fidelity findings remain scope-limited rather than global certification, review boundaries require paired authority and realization evidence, applicable invariants derive from survival demands, and downstream consumption is limited to tested preservation. Observability classification is not provenance. Constitutional reality is bounded repository-governed standing, not objective reality. Inquiry origination requires bounded translation. Constitutional kind labels are not ontology closure. Bounded competency rows, artifact shapes, direct construction, and calculated views remain bounded witnesses rather than canonical competency kinds, producer-occurrence proof, or universal activity. Observations and provider outputs require source-attributed addressability, bounded translation when translated, and refusal-condition-preserving consumption before evidence. Implementation witnesses prove concrete surfaces, not universal truth. Responsibility and handoff must be explicit; governance coordinates bounded duties without becoming a universal adjudicator. Unknowns remain preserved standing rather than silent falsehood. Comparison may evaluate ownership claims but does not alone establish constitutional ownership; operational mechanics remain report-only unless independently warranted.
