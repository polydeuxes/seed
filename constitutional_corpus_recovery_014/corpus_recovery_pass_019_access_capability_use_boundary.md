The updated frontier left **Acts, Movement, Access, Capability, and Constraint** unresolved around access-state specifics and access-transition topology. This pass selected only the narrower pressure independently supported by the corpus: access and capability visibility must not collapse into use authority or mutation.

## Recovered distinctions

- Act is not artifact: an artifact can report or preserve an act-shaped assertion without proving the act occurred.
- Movement is not mutation: read-only selection, projection, composition, translation, or evaluation can be constitutional movement without cluster change.
- Access state is not capability: candidate, read-only, observation-bearing, projected, history-bearing, or consumer-visible access does not grant operational capability.
- Capability availability is not use authority: catalog, candidate, documented, projected, or verified capability evidence does not authorize execution or prove actual use.
- Constraint is orthogonal to acts: identity, provenance, scope, authority, warrant, applicability, admissibility, currentness, availability, stopping, and mutation boundaries recur as local constraints, not one universal pipeline.

## Contradictions and negative authority

The topology audit contradicts a mandatory universal sequence among translation, interpretation, binding, establishment, evaluation, selection, examination, realization, recording, projection, composition, and communication. Capability/use evidence blocks the move from can-do to did-do, vendor claim to deployment use, documented feature to enabled feature, enabled feature to actual use, authorized use to actual use, and actual use to lawful use. Access-transition evidence does not support a complete access topology or general action/mutation architecture.

## Dependencies and Unknowns

Lawful use depends on separate authority, warrant, constraints, local responsibility, and evidence appropriate to the claimed movement. Preserved Unknowns remain: exhaustive act inventory, exact access-transition ownership, whether action/mutation vocabulary will mature, and complete capability handoff boundary.

## Cross-examination

Authority/reliance evidence confirms that capability or access evidence cannot create permission. Evidence/Unknown evidence prevents non-use, no-harm, or no-event conclusions without separate support. Implementation visibility evidence confines diagnostics and projections to their declared read/write/mutation boundaries. Artifact standing evidence prevents public visibility or artifact availability from becoming constitutional status.

## Mature result

The mature constitutional result is not a complete access topology. It is the stable boundary that access, availability, and capability may support bounded conditions but do not authorize use, execution, mutation, or stronger factual claims without separate boundaries.
