# Fidelity Recovery Pass 043 — Scope-Limited Fidelity Finding

## Prior-pass evidence consumed

Pass 041 recovered warrant survival through translation. Pass 042 recovered consumer eligibility without authority transfer. This pass asks how Fidelity itself must stop: what bounded artifact or finding may be produced without turning Fidelity into an engine, score, owner map, or completion declaration.

## Selected Fidelity pressure

A fidelity determination is lawful only as a bounded finding about a specific realization boundary. It may identify preservation, violation, or Unknown for the reviewed handoff, translation, compression, distribution, or compatibility mechanism. It must not declare global implementation lawfulness, complete constitutional ownership, or final Fidelity completion.

The pressure is:

```text
fidelity finding
!= global certification
```

## Evidence followed

This pass cross-examined the two prior passes against `constitutional_fidelity_characterization.md`, `constitutional_fidelity_investigation.md`, `constitutional_evidence_readiness_audit.md`, `constitutional_absence_of_evidence_boundary_investigation.md`, `constitutional_completion_audit_characterization.md`, `constitutional_lawful_stop_characterization.md`, `constitutional_process_implementation_ownership_audit.md`, and implementation diagnostic evidence summarized in `seed_runtime/diagnostic_inventory.py` and `seed_runtime/diagnostic_shape_audit.py`.

## Recovered pressure

Fidelity must itself obey the evidence and stop boundaries it protects. The output of a Fidelity inquiry is not a runtime participant, score, mapping table, implementation engine, or universal pipeline. It is a bounded preservation finding tied to the evidence reviewed and to the specific authority boundary under examination.

A faithful determination can say, for example, that a translated form preserved warrant, that a handoff made only a bounded consumer eligible, that authority relocation was detected, or that the reviewed evidence leaves the topology Unknown. It cannot say that all future realizations are lawful, that structural similarity proves lawfulness, that operational success proves preservation, or that unsupported topology should be filled in.

## Cross-examination against authority and implementation evidence

`constitutional_fidelity_characterization.md` identifies Fidelity artifacts as characterizations, boundary statements, classifications, dependency statements, Unknowns, and confidence classifications; it explicitly refuses implementation owners, implementation slices, process engines, projection grammar, mapping tables, stages, and universal handoff objects. `constitutional_fidelity_investigation.md` preserves Unknowns around question construction, universal orientation, shared recovery artifacts, separate cross-exam/completion stages, stop inventories, Projection Grammar, and public Fidelity surfaces.

`constitutional_absence_of_evidence_boundary_investigation.md` prevents Fidelity from treating missing support as global falsity, impossibility, or proof of absence. `constitutional_completion_audit_characterization.md` and lawful-stop evidence prevent a bounded sufficiency result from becoming an infinite obligation to continue or a universal completion claim. Diagnostic implementation evidence supplies an analogy: diagnostic inventory and shape audit expose rows, declarations, observed shapes, statuses, record scopes, and mutation boundaries; they do not silently become cluster truth or complete operational authority.

Passes 041 and 042 therefore constrain the finding form. If warrant survival cannot be shown, the lawful finding may be violation or Unknown. If a consumer role cannot be bounded, the lawful finding may be authority-relocation risk or Unknown. If implementation evidence is absent, Fidelity may not invent the missing topology.

## Constitutional invariants

1. Fidelity findings are scoped to reviewed evidence, boundary, surface, artifact, and time of review.
2. Preservation requires evidence that necessary authority limits survived; it is not inferred from compatibility, success, similarity, or convenience.
3. Violation findings require a supported boundary crossing such as erasure, invention, mutation, or relocation of authority.
4. Unknown is the required result where topology, owner, consumer, warrant, or preservation evidence is insufficient.
5. Later reliance may consume a fidelity finding only within the finding's scope.

## Lawful implementation freedom

Implementation may expose bounded diagnostics, reports, rows, summaries, and status fields that help a Fidelity inquiry. It may also remain silent where no public Fidelity surface exists. Fidelity does not require a runtime participant, public CLI flag, score, registry, universal stage, or complete topology. Existing diagnostics can serve as evidence when they preserve their read-only and non-mutating boundaries.

## Erasure, invention, mutation, or relocation of authority

A Fidelity inquiry erases authority if it omits the scope or Unknowns that limit its own finding. It invents authority if it treats the finding as global certification or owner recovery. It mutates authority if a report-only finding becomes cluster truth, repository fact, or execution permission without separate authority. It relocates authority if later work consumes a local fidelity finding as if it were the constitutional owner of the protected responsibility.

## Bounded fidelity determination

The supported artifact is a bounded Fidelity finding with at least:

```text
reviewed boundary
reviewed evidence
realization or handoff under examination
preserved invariants
lawful form changes
detected boundary crossings, if any
Unknowns
consumer limits for later reliance
```

The possible determination vocabulary remains qualitative and bounded:

```text
faithful within scope / unfaithful boundary crossing / Unknown
```

No score, universal certification, implementation owner map, or completion declaration is recovered.

## Later responsibility consumption

Later work may consume the finding for reliance, lawful stop, responsibility transfer, diagnostic recording, implementation ownership recovery, or Book projection only if it preserves the finding's scope. A finding from one realization cannot be generalized to all realizations without new evidence.

## Preserved Unknowns

- Whether a future public Fidelity diagnostic should exist remains Unknown.
- Whether a canonical schema for Fidelity findings is warranted remains Unknown.
- Whether all implementation realizations can be inventoried for Fidelity remains Unknown.
- Whether Fidelity can ever be declared complete remains Unknown and is not declared here.

## Result

Pass 043 deepens Fidelity by recovering its own lawful stopping form: a scope-limited qualitative finding, not global certification or implementation topology.
