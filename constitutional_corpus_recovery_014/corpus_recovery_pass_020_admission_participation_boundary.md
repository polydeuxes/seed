## Frontier pressure selected

After pass 019, the frontier left **Authority, Reliance, Warrant, and Admission** unresolved around admission-family edges. This pass selected the narrower pressure with independent evidence: admission permits bounded participation in a next posture but is not ratification, truth, implementation authority, or reusable permission.

## Recovered distinctions

- Evidence is not authority.
- Warrant is not proof, execution authority, or reusable blanket permission.
- Reliance is scoped downstream use, not facthood.
- Sufficiency is an earnedness gate, not the whole admission family.
- Admissibility is local participation in a bounded downstream context, not a universal service.
- Projection readiness is not ratification, and Book projection readiness is not implementation permission.
- Lawful stop and Unknown preservation are positive admission outcomes when further movement is unsupported.

## Contradictions and negative authority

The admission characterization rejects a proof calculus, legal framework, universal evaluator, global gateway, implementation mandate, and automatic promotion from candidate to truth. Ratification/readiness evidence blocks treating recurrence, confidence, sufficiency, or projection as constitutional closure. Warrant evidence blocks treating translation, provider syntax, report form, or repeated vocabulary as lawful reliance unless evidence and authority boundaries survive.

## Dependencies and Unknowns

Admission depends on bounded investigation, competent evidence, independent testimony where required, cross-examination or equivalent inspection, sufficiency, warrant, owner-local admissibility, preserved Unknowns, and lawful stop. Unknowns remain around exact edges among sufficiency, admissibility, readiness, reconciliation, ratification, and which reliance forms are mature enough for further Book projection.

## Cross-examination

Evidence/Unknown recovery requires admission findings to preserve limitations and Unknowns rather than strengthen claims. Question/inquiry recovery prevents operator pressure from becoming admission. Responsibility/handoff recovery prevents admission from transferring ownership. Implementation visibility recovery prevents implementation witnesses from becoming implementation authority.

## Mature result

The mature result is admission as bounded participation: a supported subject may enter the next repository-supported posture only within preserved limits. The corpus does not support a complete admission topology or single admission owner.
