## Smallest recurring distinctions

- **Observation is not fact.** Observation is source-bearing input that can support evidence only through bounded interpretation.
- **Provider language is not Seed grammar.** Provider vocabulary must be translated with provenance and limits.
- **External material is not repository authority.** External sources can witness or orient, but do not become constitutional law by import.
- **Attachment is not adoption.** Repository attachment preserves source, translation, boundary, and uncertainty.
- **Unknown source is not harmless.** Unknown provenance must stop or limit reliance.

## Contradictions and refinements

This family contradicts silent normalization: provider labels, external representations, and observations cannot be promoted into knowledge merely because they are useful or visible. Translation responsibility is a constitutional responsibility, not a presentation preference.

## Local findings vs wider recurrence

Provider contracts are local to provider-facing surfaces. Wider recurrence is provenance discipline: source, scope, grammar, translation, and confidence must travel with evidence.

## Implementation witnesses

Provider contract handoff audits, external orientation slices, observation-derived capability slices, and campaigns can witness particular translations. They do not grant external authority.

## What may be relied upon

The repository supports a hard boundary between source-bearing observation, translated evidence, repository knowledge, and constitutional authority.

## What may not be relied upon

Do not treat provider vocabulary, external source confidence, or imported representation as native Seed law or cluster truth.
