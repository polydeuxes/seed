## Competing interpretations

One interpretation would leave all provider translation mechanics report-only because the existing Book already says external grammar is not constitutional grammar. A competing interpretation would project provider-specific contract ownership into the Book. The repository evidence supports neither extreme. The mature recurrence is the translation boundary: provider/source vocabulary may be used only through attributed, bounded translation, and Unknown provenance limits reliance.

## Distinctions already preserved

- External grammar is not constitutional grammar.
- Lexical recurrence is not constitutional authority.
- Recovered representation is not adopted law.
- Evidence is not fact.
- Testimony and explanation do not establish truth by presentation alone.

## Projection made

A new clause, `01.External.A — Source grammar translation boundary`, was added to `book_of_seed/01-grammar-and-standing/external-and-constitutional-grammar.md`. It preserves the mature grammar that provider vocabulary, external representations, and source confidence labels remain attributed source grammar unless a bounded translation states source, scope, context, translated term or claim, uncertainty, and authority limit. It also forbids converting provider language, imported representation structure, external confidence, or attachment convenience into native constitutional grammar, cluster truth, repository authority, or adopted law.
