# Book Projection Pass 048 — Addressability Without Assimilation

Repository authority wins.

## Projection question

Should the Book distinguish constitutionally addressable external material from translated or adopted Constitutional Grammar?

## Evidence bearing on projection

Grammar seam pass 048 recovered a bounded relation: an external representation can be constitutionally addressable as source-attributed material for examination without assimilating its source grammar. The observation/provider/external-source family, projection pass 016, pass 024, and Fidelity reconciliation/topology passes 044 through 047 support this relation while refusing provider taxonomy, owner topology, and universal external schemas.

## Book comparison

`01.External.A` preserves what is required before external vocabulary or representations are carried into Seed reasoning through bounded translation. It does not expressly preserve the prior and weaker standing of addressable external material: Seed can point to and examine an external representation as external without converting it into translated Seed grammar, adopted law, repository authority, or cluster truth.

## Book change

A new clause, `01.External.B — Addressability without assimilation`, was added to `book_of_seed/01-grammar-and-standing/external-and-constitutional-grammar.md`.

The clause preserves:

- addressability as source-attributed standing for examination, comparison, quotation, attachment, or translation assessment;
- the requirement that source role, provenance or provenance Unknown, source grammar, scope, uncertainty, confidence limits, and authority limits remain attached;
- the refusal to infer native constitutional standing from reference, attachment, availability, usefulness, recurrence, or provider confidence;
- the distinction between lawful narrowing or changed form and erasure, invention, mutation, or authority relocation.

## Report-only material

Complete provider/source taxonomy, source-owner maps, translation-owner maps, canonical attachment forms, and universal external representation schemas remain report-only or Unknown.

## Result

Projection made. The Book now preserves addressability as a bounded external standing that permits examination without assimilation.
