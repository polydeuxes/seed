## Recovered pressure

The mature distinction is that a surface may make something directly observable, reconstructable from implementation, constitutionally inferable from recovered process evidence, or Unknown. Those classifications do not collapse into provenance. Repository-visible output proves what the surface emitted within its boundary; reconstruction can explain how implementation-visible pieces relate; constitutional inference can identify a supported constitutional pattern; provenance requires applicable preserved lineage. None of these alone establishes a full provenance chain, runtime trace, execution log, producer occurrence, or semantic explanation.

## Cross-examination

Neighboring findings constrain the pressure:

- Evidence provenance clauses already distinguish represented lineage from verified provenance.
- Projection visibility clauses already prevent diagnostic or read-model output from becoming source truth.
- Fidelity prevents structural asymmetry or compatibility mechanics from being treated as provenance failure.
- Question standing and admission prevent an answer surface from proving all upstream question movements.
- Correlation/causation and attribution/responsibility boundaries prevent reconstructed adjacency from proving cause, responsibility, or producer occurrence.

The current Book covers provenance representation and projection limits, but it does not directly preserve the intermediate observability classifications that prevent direct observation, implementation reconstruction, constitutional inference, and Unknown from collapsing into provenance.

The clause should preserve the standing distinctions without importing the bounded answer studied by the investigations, the report-local observability table, implementation file names, runtime tracing design, or a provenance engine.
