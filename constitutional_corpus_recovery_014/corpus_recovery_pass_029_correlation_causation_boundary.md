The recurring distinction is stable: observed association, order, adjacency, pattern, repeated linkage, source-attributed causal claim, model-inferred causal claim, diagnostic adjacency, and log adjacency can be evidence of the relation observed, but not proof that one event produced another. Claim-appropriate evidence, authority, provenance, confidence limits, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop remain required for stronger causation claims.

## Mature finding

Correlation and sequence are constitutionally separate from causation. They may lawfully orient inquiry or support a bounded observed-relation claim, but they do not establish causation, explanation, conduct, responsibility, legal causation, root cause, or proof that one event produced another.
