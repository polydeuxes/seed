## Bounded ingress relation recovered

A possible inquiry becomes admitted inquiry movement only when a local authority accepts bounded participation for the preserved inquiry identity.

```text
possible inquiry standing
+ local admission authority
+ preserved identity bundle, scope, evidence demand, authority limit, stop/refusal conditions, Unknowns, conflicts, negative authority
= admitted inquiry movement
!= answer standing, examination eligibility, method selection, execution, implementation ownership, mutation, or completion
```

The pressure consumed is the translated possible inquiry from pass 058. The constitutional act is local admission for bounded participation. The artifact produced is admitted inquiry movement or an admission/refusal/clarification record with preserved limits. The lawful next consumer is admitted-inquiry-to-examination handoff, scoped explanation/refusal, frontier update, clarification, or lawful stop.

## Additional authority required

Admission requires evidence that the local responsibility has authority over this exact inquiry identity, scope, evidence demand, and movement boundary. The authority may accept bounded participation, refuse participation, request clarification, narrow scope, defer, or preserve Unknown. It may not be inferred from operator urgency, prompt wording, CLI/API dispatchability, adjacency to a surface, or the mere existence of a possible inquiry.

## Artifact and lawful next consumer

The crossing artifact is an admitted-inquiry artifact or a refusal/clarification/Unknown artifact. If admitted, its next consumer may be a local examination-responsibility, selection, handoff, or scoped-result owner, but only within the admitted boundary. If refused or unclear, the next consumer is stop, clarification, deferment, frontier preservation, or Unknown preservation.

## Cross-examination

### External Grammar

Admission does not assimilate any remaining operator or external wording. Source role, provenance, translated claim scope, uncertainty, confidence limits, authority limits, refusal conditions, and Unknowns must still travel.

### Constitutional Grammar

Admission changes participation standing only. It does not create constitutional grammar, identity beyond the preserved bundle, evidence truth, answer standing, or repository knowledge.

### Fidelity

The admission handoff is faithful only if downstream consumers can see what was admitted, what was excluded, what remains Unknown, what was refused or deferred, and which invariants survived. Treating admission as completion, answer, execution, or ownership relocation is unfaithful.

### Inquiry frontier identity

Admission consumes possible inquiry identity; it does not create identity from interface mechanics. Neighboring possible inquiries remain visible unless the admission/refusal artifact has evidence and authority over them.

### Responsibility

The admission owner is local. The repository does not recover a universal ingress gate, prompt adjudicator, parser, dispatcher, or operator-intent owner. Missing owner or mismatched owner requires stop or Unknown.

### Lawful stop

Supported stops include refusal, clarification need, narrowing, deferment, outside authority, ambiguous identity, insufficient evidence territory, conflict, stale or unavailable evidence, missing owner, missing capability, and Unknown. Clarification is not admission; refusal is not erasure; stop is not completion of neighboring inquiry.

## Book projection decision

No Book change is warranted. Existing question, frontier, admission, handoff, refusal, stopping, External Grammar, and Fidelity clauses already preserve the supported relation and block the forbidden inferences. This pass records ingress topology, not a new Book responsibility.
