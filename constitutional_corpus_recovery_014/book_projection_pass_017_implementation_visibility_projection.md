## Competing interpretations

One interpretation would canonize the diagnostic inventory and shape-audit implementation as Book law. Another would leave all diagnostic boundaries operational and report-only because current Book chapters already say projections are not constitutional sources. Repository evidence supports a middle boundary: visibility surfaces have constitutional limits, while local diagnostic registry mechanics remain operational.

## Distinctions already preserved

- Projection is not a constitutional source.
- Read model is not the underlying record.
- Cache freshness is not truth.
- Execution is not recording.
- Recording is not knowledge extraction.
- Implementation ownership is not constitutional authority.

## Projection made

A new clause, `06.Projection.A — Projection and diagnostic visibility boundary`, was added to `book_of_seed/06-state-and-projection/projection-and-current-state.md`. It preserves that projections, read models, diagnostics, audits, and inventory surfaces expose visibility only within recorded scope, inputs, freshness, shape, and mutation boundary. It forbids treating their existence or output as source truth, Book law, complete corpus coverage, readiness, ownership, or mutation authority. It also preserves that diagnostic/audit records remain diagnostic-scope findings unless separately promoted through evidence, authority, and state boundaries.

## Contradictions and Unknowns

No Book contradiction was found. Unknowns remain around full diagnostic inventory coverage, exact ownership of all projection/read-model families, and which recurrent reports should become implementation surfaces.

## What future work may rely upon

Future work may rely on the constitutional distinction between implementation visibility and implementation authority, and on the requirement that diagnostic/audit findings remain bounded by scope and mutation boundary unless separately warranted.

## What future work must not rely upon

Future work must not rely on this pass as an implementation design, a complete diagnostic registry specification, proof that all diagnostics are inventoried, or authority to treat diagnostic output as cluster truth.
