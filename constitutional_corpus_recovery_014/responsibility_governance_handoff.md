## Smallest recurring distinctions

- **Responsibility is not authority.** A responsibility identifies an owner or duty; authority bounds what that owner may do.
- **Ownership is not inherited from prose.** Implementation/process ownership must be explicit.
- **Handoff is not silent transfer.** A transition of responsibility needs evidence, scope, and preserved limits.
- **Governance is not adjudication.** Governance preserves constraints and responsibility without becoming a universal controller.
- **Operator authority is bounded.** Operator goals and handoffs require scope and do not automatically become repository law.

## Contradictions and refinements

This family resists flattening responsibility into one authority and resists assuming that a report, operator ask, or implementation surface owns future movement. Handoff audits refine advancement by requiring bounded transfer evidence.

## Local findings vs wider recurrence

Operator handoff audits are local to goal advancement. Wider recurrence is local responsibility: owners, duties, authority, and transitions are separate and must be named.

## Implementation witnesses

Process view slices, governance view slices, implementation ownership audits, and handoff audits witness specific owners or transfers. They do not create universal ownership.

## What may not be relied upon
