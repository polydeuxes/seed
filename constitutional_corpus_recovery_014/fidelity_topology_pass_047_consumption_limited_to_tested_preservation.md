## Recovered Fidelity relation

Downstream consumption is lawful only when it preserves the tested-preservation envelope:

1. the reviewed realization boundary;
2. the constitutional authority and invariants tested;
3. the implementation evidence reviewed;
4. the qualitative result for those invariants;
5. detected crossings, if any;
6. Unknowns and untested neighboring topology;
7. consumer limits for the downstream use.

This relation prevents a subtle failure mode: a finding can be accurate within scope and still be unlawfully consumed if later work treats it as evidence of untested realization topology, unreviewed producer occurrence, complete implementation ownership, or general constitutional readiness.

## Cross-examination

### Authority

The finding does not carry the upstream authority itself. It carries a scoped preservation judgment about whether that authority survived the reviewed seam. Downstream work must still obtain any authority it independently needs for action, reliance, projection, recording, transfer, or stop.

### Evidence

The finding is evidence of what it reviewed and concluded, not evidence that unreviewed seams exist or that all invariants survived. Its evidentiary force is bounded by the reviewed implementation evidence and established constitutional authority.

### Responsibility

A downstream responsibility may use the finding as an input, but responsibility does not transfer unless separate transfer evidence supports it. Implementation ownership recovery may use the finding as bounded support, not as an owner map.

### Handoff

If the finding travels as a handoff artifact, the handoff must preserve the finding's boundary, tested invariants, Unknowns, and consumer limits. Stripping those limits would erase the very topology that made the finding lawful.

### Reliance

Reliance is lawful only for the tested preservation. A consumer may rely on `faithful within scope` for the reviewed boundary and invariant set; it may not rely on that phrase as proof of adjacent preservation, public diagnostic completeness, canonical schema compliance, mutation authority, or Fidelity completion.

### Lawful stop

A Fidelity finding can support lawful stop for the reviewed question when the tested-preservation envelope is sufficient for that question. It cannot stop unrelated inquiries or close the unresolved topology that the finding preserved as Unknown.

## Preservation, crossing, and Unknown

Supported preservation is consumable only as tested preservation. Unfaithful boundary crossing is consumable only for the detected crossing and its supported consequences. Unknown is a positive constraint on consumption: downstream work must not fill it with assumptions, structural symmetry, implementation convenience, or presentation vocabulary.
