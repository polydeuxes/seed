## Smallest recurring distinctions

- **Act is not artifact.** An act may produce or use an artifact, but the artifact is not the act.
- **Movement is not mutation.** Lawful movement may be conceptual, evidentiary, or projection-bound without runtime mutation.
- **Access state is not capability.** Candidate, read-only, observation-bearing, history-bearing, or projected access does not grant operational capability.
- **Capability availability is not use authority.** Catalog or projected capability does not authorize execution.
- **Lens is not road.** Views and lenses can inspect pressure without becoming mandatory stages.
- **Constraint is not optional decoration.** Constraints shape lawful acts, branch topology, and stopping.

## Contradictions and refinements

Topology audits contradict mandatory-stage claims and universal roads. Action/mutation survey refines the boundary by showing that observation, reliance, and mutation authority are separate, while `Action` and `Mutation` are not mature general constitutional participants.

## Local findings vs wider recurrence

Local surfaces include access transitions, capability catalogs, action candidates, act inventories, and topology branches. Wider recurrence is the separation of availability, observation, reliance, authority, movement, capability, and mutation.

## Implementation witnesses

Access surfaces, capability catalogs, diagnostic surfaces, and tests can witness concrete access or capability states. They do not authorize operational use beyond their bounded command or test.
