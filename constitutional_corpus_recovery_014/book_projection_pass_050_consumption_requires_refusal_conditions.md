# Book Projection Pass 050 — Consumption Requires Refusal Conditions

Repository authority wins.

## Projection question

Should the Book state that consuming addressable or translated external material must preserve provenance, authority limits, Unknowns, and refusal conditions?

## Evidence bearing on projection

Pass 050 consumed passes 048 and 049 plus Fidelity reconciliation/topology, especially pass 047's downstream-consumption limit. The recovered relation is that a later consumer may use only the addressed or translated scope and must preserve the source boundary, provenance or Unknown, uncertainty, authority limit, negative authority, Unknowns, refusal conditions, and any tested-preservation boundary.

## Book comparison

`01.External.A` preserves bounded translation and refuses assimilation. The new `01.External.B` preserves addressability without assimilation. `07.Realization.B` preserves Fidelity downstream consumption limits. However, the External Grammar chapter did not yet state the cross-seam consumer obligation in the vocabulary of external material itself.

## Book change

A new clause, `01.External.C — Cross-seam consumption preserves refusal conditions`, was added to `book_of_seed/01-grammar-and-standing/external-and-constitutional-grammar.md`.

The clause preserves:

- consumption only within addressable or translated scope;
- mandatory survival of source role or Unknown, provenance or provenance Unknown, source grammar boundary, translated claim and scope, uncertainty, authority limits, negative authority, Unknowns, and refusal conditions;
- lawful narrowing, rendering, citation, comparison, indexing, attachment, or projection only when limits travel;
- failure modes of erasure, invention, mutation, and authority relocation.

## Report-only material

Complete consumer inventories, canonical envelopes, runtime diagnostics, provider registries, translation engines, and complete External Grammar topology remain Unknown or report-only.

## Result

Projection made. The Book now preserves that cross-seam consumption must carry the refusal conditions and limits that made addressability or translation lawful.
