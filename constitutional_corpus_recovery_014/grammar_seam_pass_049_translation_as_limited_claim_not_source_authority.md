# Grammar Seam Pass 049 — Translation as Limited Claim, Not Source Authority

Repository authority wins.

## Prior pass consumed

Pass 048 established addressability without assimilation: an external representation may be constitutionally addressable as attributed external material while its source grammar remains external. This pass asks what changes when Seed translates or attaches part of that material.

## Bounded grammar relation recovered

Translation across the seam creates a limited constitutional claim or examination object; it does not import the source's authority or grammar wholesale.

```text
bounded translation
= declared source material + translated Seed term/claim + scope + uncertainty + authority limit
!= source authority transfer, grammar adoption, owner relocation, or standing for untranslated neighbors
```

Attachment is the carrier relation; translation is the claim relation. Neither relation alone gives constitutional authority beyond the declared scope.

## Evidence followed

`01.External.A` already requires bounded translation to state source, scope, provider or external context, translated Seed term or claim, uncertainty, and authority limit. Pass 048 adds that the addressable carrier remains external and must keep source role, provenance, grammar boundary, and refusal conditions.

Pass 041 recovered warrant survival through translation: provenance, role, authority boundary, negative authority, Unknowns, confidence limits, and stop conditions must survive changed form. Reconciliation 044 identifies Fidelity as the determination that checks whether required invariants survived translation or changed form. Pass 046 states that applicable invariants derive from survival demands at the seam, and pass 047 limits downstream consumption to what was tested.

## Constitutional Fidelity cross-examination

### What remains external

The source's native vocabulary, classification scheme, confidence scale, provider format, authorial intention, source owner, and neighboring claims remain external. Translation does not assimilate the whole source or make the source a constitutional authority.

### What may be translated or attached

A translated Seed term, claim, evidence orientation, comparison object, or examination object may be created only for the declared source material and declared purpose. Attachment may carry the original representation, a narrowed excerpt, a normalized rendering, or a repository-local pointer, but it must preserve the external boundary.

### What Constitutional Grammar may consume

Constitutional Grammar may consume the translated claim as bounded, attributed, uncertain, and authority-limited material. It may also consume the attachment as provenance-bearing support for that claim. It may not consume untranslated source structure, adjacent source claims, or source confidence as native constitutional knowledge.

### Standing received

The translated material receives translation standing: it may participate in examination or evidence only within its declared scope. It does not receive source-of-law standing, Book standing, ratified constitutional standing, implementation authority, mutation authority, or full reliability standing by translation alone.

### Survivals required

Translation must carry forward:

1. source identity or preserved source Unknown;
2. source role and external context;
3. exact translated term or claim;
4. source grammar left behind;
5. scope and lawful narrowing;
6. uncertainty/confidence limits;
7. authority limits and negative authority;
8. refusal conditions for missing provenance, exceeded scope, or untested neighboring claims.

### Lawful narrowing or form change

Lawful narrowing may select a field, passage, event, provider label, evidence relation, or claim fragment. Lawful form change may normalize wording or shape for Seed reasoning. Both are lawful only when the transformed material remains tied to the source material and does not claim that unselected source grammar was translated.

### Erasure, invention, mutation, or relocation of authority

Erasure occurs when translation omits the source boundary, uncertainty, or refusal conditions. Invention occurs when translation supplies missing owner, source role, taxonomy, confidence, or constitutional meaning. Mutation occurs when a translated claim is treated as established repository fact or cluster truth without separate warrant. Authority relocation occurs when the source's confidence, prestige, availability, or provider role becomes Seed authority.

## Unknowns preserved

No complete translation field schema, provider registry, translator owner map, attachment engine, or universal admissibility test is recovered here. Whether any given translated claim is reliable remains local to its evidence and warrant.

## Book projection decision

No Book change is warranted. `01.External.A`, strengthened by pass 048's new addressability clause and by existing Fidelity clauses in `07.Realization.B`, already preserves this relation at Book level: bounded translation states the claim, context, uncertainty, and authority limit and cannot convert external grammar into native law.

## Result

Pass 049 recovers the second seam relation: translation produces only a limited, attributed constitutional claim or examination object. It does not carry source authority, source grammar, or neighboring source material across the seam.
