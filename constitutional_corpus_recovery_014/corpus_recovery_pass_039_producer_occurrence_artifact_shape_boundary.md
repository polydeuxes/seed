# Corpus Recovery Pass 039 — Producer Occurrence and Artifact Shape Boundary

## Selected constitutional pressure

This pass selected the recurring pressure that artifact shape, direct construction, field equality, row identity, or consumer-local validation may look like proof that the named producer act occurred.

## Evidence recovered

Book-side characterization and uptake reports repeatedly recover the distinction. `constructors-and-production-authority.md` states that mechanical construction proves constructibility only, while witnessed return from a responsible owner can add observer-held occurrence standing not carried by an independently constructed artifact with identical fields. `acts-and-act-artifacts.md` states that an act artifact reports or preserves an assertion, but constructing it does not prove the act; occurrence must be evidenced by the responsible validated production boundary and, for operational acts, by execution or recording. `uptake_standing_characterization_pass_006.md` shows direct construction can imitate fields and even satisfy consumer-local coherence, while live pipeline return has occurrence standing only in call context. `constitutional_occurrence_evidence_survey_007.md` separates establishment assertions preserved in artifacts from evidence that the producer occurrence happened. SQLite friction reports add that primary keys, rows, view outputs, and status columns do not establish occurrence.

## Implementation witnesses

Python constructors, dataclasses, and pipeline functions witness concrete differences between direct construction and live responsible production. SQLite rows and views witness represented standing but explicitly fix or deny producer-occurrence standing where not represented. These are implementation witnesses for the boundary, not a mandate for Python or SQLite topology.

## Cross-examination

- Against pass 021: reachability or preservation of an artifact does not promote it to accepted, admitted, relied-upon, or mutation-authorized standing.
- Against pass 028: movement is not runtime mutation; likewise artifact construction is not automatically the constitutional occurrence it describes.
- Against pass 033: observability or reconstruction does not itself establish provenance or producer occurrence.
- Against current constructors and act-artifact chapters: the Book already states the relevant consequence directly.

## Constitutional consequence test

The pressure independently affects what may be relied upon: a consumer may rely on consumer-local validation only for its local assertion, not as proof of upstream producer occurrence. However, that consequence is already separately addressable in the current Book through constructors, act artifacts, evidence/provenance, and recording clauses. Adding another clause would multiply citation without preserving a new consequence.

## Determination

No Book change is directly warranted. The pass records the pressure as already preserved and keeps any exhaustive occurrence topology, producer-seal design, or artifact-encoding scheme outside the Book.
