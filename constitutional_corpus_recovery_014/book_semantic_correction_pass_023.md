# Book Semantic Correction Pass 023 — Ownership Comparison Boundary

## Starting point

This pass reviewed the temporal-neutrality replacement in `book_of_seed/06-state-and-projection/ownership-discrepancy-and-residue.md` that changed backward-looking recovery language into the sentence `Ownership is established by comparing implementation behavior, producers, consumers, declarations, and constitutional warrants.`

The review was limited to that local semantic issue and directly adjacent wording. It did not reopen the wider ownership, governance, or Book style subject.

## Semantic issue

The phrase `Ownership is established by comparing...` strengthened the Book beyond the repository evidence preserved in the recovery corpus. The relevant family reports distinguish implementation/process ownership, responsibility, governance, and constitutional authority, and they preserve owner maps and governance topology as report-only where explicit evidence is incomplete.

Comparison is a lawful method for evaluating claims and exposing support, discrepancies, or residue. Comparison alone is not a sufficient constitutional establishment act.

## Correction made

The bounded resolution now states that ownership claims are evaluated by comparing implementation behavior, producers, consumers, declarations, and constitutional warrants. It further states that comparison can expose current ownership support, discrepancies, or residue, but does not by itself establish constitutional ownership.

The adjacent sentence was also corrected from manually freezing discrepancies and residue into the Book to keeping them as projected findings rather than Book-frozen claims.

## Boundary preserved

This pass preserves the intended ownership boundary:

- implementation adjacency does not create constitutional ownership;
- comparison can support evaluation without becoming establishment;
- discrepancies and residue remain projection material unless independently warranted;
- no complete owner map or governance topology is promoted.
