# Corpus Recovery Pass 021 — Artifact Reachability Without Promotion

## Frontier pressure selected

After pass 020, the frontier left **Artifact Grammar, Kind, Standing, and Availability** unresolved around availability details and constitutional-kind closure. This pass selected only the evidence-supported pressure: artifact availability is bounded reachability without promotion, not a standalone constitutional participant or closed kind.

## Evidence followed

Evidence examined included `artifact_grammar_standing.md`, `constitutional_artifact_availability_characterization.md`, `constitutional_artifact_identity_characterization.md`, `constitutional_artifact_characterization_grammar_survey.md`, `constitutional_artifact_competency_survey.md`, `constitutional_kind_cross_examination.md`, implementation-visibility reports, observation/source reports, and Book chapters on constitutional kinds, constructors, recording, projection, and handoff.

## Recovered distinctions

- Artifact is not law.
- Artifact shape, schema validity, public visibility, recurrence, or dedicated dataclass is not constitutional kind or standing.
- Availability is not authorization, acceptance, admission, reliance, projection truth, event-ledger write, or mutation.
- Reachability can be local to a consumer, observer, diagnostic, projection, or later inquiry.
- Support is not admission; presentation is not authority; orientation is not truth; candidate is not verified; projection is not source truth.

## Contradictions and negative authority

Artifact availability characterization rejects `artifact availability` as a standalone constitutional participant, service, lifecycle, scheduler, queue, store, notification mechanism, or runtime behavior. Kind cross-examination rejects closed ontology claims: purpose, responsibility, and mechanism may be interrogated, but kind vocabulary does not establish a closed type system. Artifact identity evidence rejects global identity tokens and preserves local family roles.

## Dependencies and Unknowns

Reachability may create lawful possibility for a later consumer, but acceptance, reliance, admission, recording, projection, and mutation depend on their own family-local boundaries. Unknowns remain around exact closure of constitutional kinds, which artifact families deserve independent Book standing, and whether artifact grammar should become mandatory form.

## Cross-examination

Authority/reliance recovery prevents visible artifacts from becoming permission. Evidence/Unknown recovery prevents unsupported visibility from becoming fact or absence. Implementation visibility recovery confines diagnostic rows and shape checks to declared scope. Responsibility/handoff recovery blocks reachability from transferring ownership. Access/capability recovery from pass 019 reinforces availability-without-use.

## Mature result

The mature result is bounded reachability-without-promotion. The corpus does not support a new availability doctrine or closure of constitutional kinds, but it does support a general Book boundary preventing reachable artifacts from being treated as admitted or action-authorizing merely because they are visible.
