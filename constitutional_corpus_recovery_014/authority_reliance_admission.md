# Authority, Reliance, Warrant, and Admission Family

## Independent evidence inspected

Primary witnesses: `constitutional_authority_characterization.md`, `constitutional_warrant_characterization.md`, `constitutional_reliance_characterization.md`, `constitutional_admission_family_characterization.md`, `constitutional_admissibility_gate_investigation.md`, `constitutional_book_of_law_projection_readiness_assessment.md`, and `constitutional_ratification_readiness_assessment.md`.

## Smallest recurring distinctions

- **Evidence is not authority.** Evidence may support movement; authority determines what movement the evidence permits.
- **Warrant is not proof.** Warrant allows bounded use of support without becoming truth, universal permission, or execution authority.
- **Reliance is not facthood.** Reliance is scoped, defeasible, and bounded by evidence, source, confidence, and negative authority.
- **Sufficiency is not the whole gate.** Sufficiency recurs as one gate inside broader admissibility, readiness, and owner-local admission practices.
- **Projection readiness is not ratification.** Read-only projection may be mature before Book-level ratification or implementation authority.

## Contradictions and refinements

The family refines broad claims that authority, warrant, sufficiency, or confidence could settle truth. Later admission and ratification reports constrain earlier readiness language: recurrence can justify bounded projection, but not canonical completeness, implementation, or automatic Book promotion.

## Local findings vs wider recurrence

Local findings include owner-specific admissibility rules and particular readiness surfaces. Wider recurrence is stronger: every artifact requires support path, boundary, negative authority, Unknown preservation, and scoped confidence before reliance.

## Implementation witnesses

Implementation is only a witness when a specific surface proves scoped behavior. This family may cite diagnostics, tests, read models, or pipeline slices as evidence of bounded reliance, but those witnesses do not become general authority.

## Preserved Unknowns

- Exact family edges among sufficiency, admissibility, readiness, reconciliation, and ratification.
- Whether `constitutional boundary condition` is the repository-preferred name for authority.
- Which reliance forms are mature enough for Book projection.

## What may be relied upon

It may be relied upon that repository authority separates evidence, warrant, reliance, admission, readiness, and ratification, and that none of these alone authorizes truth, mutation, implementation, or Book promotion.

## What may not be relied upon

Do not rely on this family as a universal proof calculus, legal adjudication framework, implementation design, or single mandatory sequence.
