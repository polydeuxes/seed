# Grammar Seam Pass 050 — Consumption Requires Refusal Conditions

Repository authority wins.

## Prior passes consumed

Pass 048 recovered addressability without assimilation. Pass 049 recovered translation as a limited claim rather than source authority transfer. This pass asks what Constitutional Grammar may consume after addressability, attachment, or translation have occurred.

## Bounded grammar relation recovered

Constitutional consumption of cross-seam material is lawful only when the consuming act preserves the refusal conditions that came from the external boundary and any translation boundary.

```text
lawful cross-seam consumption
= consume only the addressed/translated scope + provenance + authority limit + Unknowns + refusal conditions
!= consumption of external grammar, untested translation, missing provenance, or source authority
```

The seam is therefore not complete when material becomes addressable or translated. The consumer must remain bound by the same limits.

## Evidence followed

Pass 048 required source role or Unknown, provenance or Unknown, source grammar boundary, scope, uncertainty, authority limits, and refusal to infer native standing. Pass 049 required source identity, translated claim, external context, source grammar left behind, narrowing, uncertainty, authority limits, negative authority, and refusal conditions.

Pass 047 provides the decisive Fidelity topology: downstream consumption is limited to the selected boundary and tested invariants. Reconciliation 044 gives the failure vocabulary: erasure, invention, mutation, or relocation of authority. The observation/provider/external-source family and pass 016 supply the external-source refusal rules: unknown provenance limits reliance, external confidence is not repository authority, and attachment is not adoption.

## Constitutional Fidelity cross-examination

### What remains external

Even after consumption begins, source-native grammar, provider categories, owner categories, source confidence scales, neighboring claims, untranslated structure, and untested translation hypotheses remain external. Consumption does not retroactively assimilate them.

### What may be translated or attached

Only the material already made addressable or translated under passes 048 and 049 may be consumed. Further narrowing or re-rendering is permitted only if it keeps the same source boundary and states any new narrowing.

### What Constitutional Grammar may consume

A consumer may consume:

- the attributed existence of the external representation;
- the bounded translated Seed term or claim;
- the preserved provenance, uncertainty, authority limit, and Unknowns;
- the Fidelity finding, if one exists, only for tested preservation;
- the refusal conditions themselves as constraints on reliance, projection, stop, or further translation.

A consumer may not consume source grammar as native grammar, source confidence as authority, missing provenance as harmless, attachment as adoption, translation as truth, or a scoped Fidelity finding as global certification.

### Standing received

Consumed material receives only the standing supported by the consumer's warrant: examination support, evidence orientation, bounded evidence, translated claim, or scope-limited Fidelity support. It does not receive broader constitutional standing merely because a later responsibility uses it.

### Survivals required

Every downstream consumer must preserve:

1. source role or Unknown;
2. provenance or provenance Unknown;
3. source grammar boundary;
4. translated claim and translated scope, if any;
5. uncertainty and confidence limits;
6. authority limits and negative authority;
7. Unknowns;
8. refusal conditions;
9. tested-preservation boundary for any Fidelity finding.

### Lawful narrowing or form change

Lawful consumption may summarize, index, cite, compare, render, attach, or project cross-seam material when the output carries the same limits. A narrower output is lawful when it declares its narrowing and refuses to stand for the unconsumed remainder.

### Erasure, invention, mutation, or relocation of authority

Erasure is a consumer dropping the limits that made consumption lawful. Invention is a consumer filling Unknown source, owner, taxonomy, reliability, or authority. Mutation is a consumer turning an external or translated claim into cluster truth or operational state without separate warrant. Relocation is a consumer treating the source, provider, attachment, translation artifact, or Fidelity finding as the authority owner.

## Unknowns preserved

Complete consumer inventories, canonical envelopes, diagnostics for every cross-seam consumption, provider registries, translation engines, and Book completeness for all External Grammar seams remain Unknown.

## Book projection decision

Book change is warranted. Existing clauses state addressability, translation, and Fidelity consumption limits, but the seam needs one present-facing rule in the External Grammar chapter: downstream consumption of addressable or translated external material remains bound by provenance, scope, limits, Unknowns, and refusal conditions. This is not a runtime design and does not close the ontology.

## Result

Pass 050 recovers the third seam relation: cross-seam consumption is lawful only as limit-preserving consumption. Refusal conditions are not metadata that may be stripped after translation; they are part of the constitutional standing of the consumed material.
