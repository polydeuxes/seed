# Corpus Recovery Pass 026 — Question Standing and Answer Boundary

## Selected pressure

Question, inquiry, advancement, and bounded work remain a frontier family because exact question-form kinds and inquiry ownership are unresolved. This pass selected the independently supported pressure that a bounded question can organize inquiry without settling the answer.

## Non-Book witnesses followed

Primary witnesses reviewed: `constitutional_corpus_recovery_014/questions_inquiry_advancement.md`, `constitutional_question_grammar_characterization.md`, `constitutional_inquiry_origination_boundary_audit_001.md`, `constitutional_asymmetrical_question_characterization.md`, `bounded_question_discipline_investigation.md`, `bounded_constitutional_question_slice_001.md`, `bounded_inquiry_frontier_audit_001.md`, `bounded_inquiry_frontier_question_handoff_audit_001.md`, and `advancement_need_family_coverage_audit_001.md`.

The recurring distinction is stable across those witnesses: question-shaped text is not answer standing. A question can bind scope, evidence demand, uncertainty, and stop conditions; it does not establish the requested outcome or promote operator wording into repository knowledge.

## Neighbor cross-examination

- Evidence and Unknown boundaries require a question to preserve absence, mismatch, uncertainty, and negative authority rather than resolving them by phrasing.
- Inquiry frontier witnesses make question visibility and frontier membership weaker than selection, examination, execution, or resolution.
- Advancement need witnesses keep need, consideration, selection, and authorization separate, preventing a question from becoming work authority by itself.
- Authority witnesses prevent operator ask, asymmetrical expectation, or reformulation from widening scope without a bounded authority boundary.

## Mature finding

A bounded question may have constitutional standing as inquiry posture, but question standing is not answer standing, selection, authorization, or repository knowledge.
