## Pass boundary

## Recovered relation

A possible inquiry is identity-preserved consideration standing. An admitted inquiry is bounded participation standing for a later inquiry movement under an applicable authority, warrant, scope, evidence, and stop boundary.

The authority change is not supplied by the possible inquiry's identity. It must be inspectable in an admission, selection, handoff, authority/scope binding, advancement, or other local owner artifact that says what participation is permitted and what remains excluded. Without that change, the possible inquiry remains visible and bounded but not opened, selected, sourced, observed, answered, recorded, ledger-written, or mutated.

## Cross-examination

### Constitutional Grammar

Question standing can establish an inquiry posture, but it does not select the outcome or authorize unbounded investigation. Pass 051 made frontier identity stronger than raw pressure; this pass keeps it weaker than admitted inquiry. The frontier can say “this is the bounded inquiry possibility,” not “this inquiry may now move.”

### External Grammar

External operator pressure can be translated into a bounded question or possible inquiry only with preserved source, scope, uncertainty, and limits. Admission cannot be inferred from the source's urgency, wording, or presentation. Source grammar remains source grammar; admitted inquiry movement requires Seed-side authority.

### Fidelity

A faithful handoff from possible inquiry to admitted inquiry must preserve the identity bundle recovered in pass 051 and add only the authority actually established. It is unfaithful to convert eligible evidence territory into selected sources, frontier scope into observation authorization, or stopping conditions into conclusion.

### Admission and authority

Pass 020 already projected that admission is bounded participation, not ratification. Applied to the frontier, admission permits only the named inquiry movement and does not prove truth, resolve uncertainty, approve implementation, establish reusable permission, or retire neighboring questions.

### Responsibility

The authority-changing artifact must be local and inspectable. The repository does not support assigning a universal inquiry-admission owner or deriving ownership from the fact that one artifact consumed another.

### Unknown and lawful stop

When an identified possible inquiry lacks an inspectable authority-changing artifact, the lawful state is not-admitted or Unknown, not silent progress. If authority is partial, stale, conflicting, unavailable, or outside the present horizon, the inquiry may lawfully stop without erasing the possible inquiry or its neighbors.

## Book projection decision

No Book change is warranted. `04.Question.A`, `04.Question.B`, `04.Frontier.A`, `08.Authority.B`, needs/opened movement, and selection/authorization already preserve the Book-level consequence: possible inquiry/frontier standing is not selection, authority, opening, execution, resolution, or ratification. The recovered relation narrows live topology rather than requiring a new clause.
