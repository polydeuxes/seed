# Book Temporal Neutrality Correction 022

## Scope

This correction reviewed the current Book chapter files under `book_of_seed/` for wording that gave constitutional content a backward-looking status based on when or how humans discovered, recovered, added, projected, or corrected it.

Recovery and projection reports were not edited. Those artifacts retain provenance about how clauses were found, tested, projected, or corrected.

## Contamination found

The Book chapter files contained temporal/provenance contamination in three forms:

1. Resolution headings that labeled current constitutional content as an `Initial resolution`.
2. Clause-section headings that labeled current clauses as `Recovered clauses`.
3. Surrounding Book prose that used recovery-status vocabulary where the Book should state only current bounded evidence, including `recovered`, `recovery`, and `separately recovered` in constitutional chapter text.

The affected Book areas were the chapter files in Books I, II, III, IV, V, VI, VII, and VIII that presented constitutional content through these backward-looking labels.

## Neutralization performed

The correction neutralized the Book presentation without changing constitutional holdings:

- `Initial resolution` headings were changed to `Bounded resolution`.
- `Recovered clauses for ...` headings were changed to `Addressable boundaries for ...`.
- Book prose that described current constitutional vocabulary or standing as `recovered` was changed to neutral current-evidence wording such as `identified`, `attributed`, `established`, or `derivation`, depending on the local sentence.
- Clause identifiers, numbering, titles, distinctions, and evidence anchors were preserved.
- Recovery and projection reports were left unchanged.

## Meaning preservation confirmation

No constitutional finding was reopened. The correction did not add clauses, remove clauses, renumber clauses, change evidence anchors, or continue corpus recovery.

The resulting Book presentation states current bounded evidence neutrally and asymmetrically: constitutional subject, question, bounded resolution, addressable boundaries, distinctions, evidence anchors, counterexamples, related chapters, and unresolved unknowns where already present. It no longer marks constitutional Book content as original, recovered, newly projected, later corrected, or previously added.
