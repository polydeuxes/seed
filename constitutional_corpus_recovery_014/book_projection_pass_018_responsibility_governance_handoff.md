## Competing interpretations

One interpretation would leave the family adequately preserved because authority scope, ownership discrepancy, and communication/handoff already carry adjacent limits. Another would project local owner maps and governance implementation surfaces into the Book. Repository evidence supports a narrower projection: explicit responsibility transfer is mature, while owner maps and governance implementation details remain report-only.

## Distinctions already preserved

- Authority scope is bounded.
- Implementation ownership is not constitutional authority.
- Handoff is not execution.
- Communication is not establishment.
- Naming recurrence is not shared responsibility.

## Projection made

A new clause, `08.Handoff.A — Explicit responsibility transfer`, was added to `book_of_seed/08-authority-communication-and-stopping/communication-and-handoff.md`. It states that responsibility, owner, or governance duty handoff requires a bounded transfer preserving subject, holder information, authority scope, evidence, and surviving limits. It forbids inferring universal authority, assertion adoption, operational approval, implementation ownership, or future repository law from a report, operator goal, process surface, or adjacent implementation naming a responsible party.

## Contradictions and Unknowns

No Book contradiction was found. Unknowns remain around the complete owner map, whether governance has a single implementation surface or remains distributed, and which handoff forms are mature enough for general reliance.

## What future work may rely upon

Future work may rely on explicit, bounded responsibility transfer as a constitutional requirement and may reject silent ownership or authority transfer where evidence and limits are absent.

## What future work must not rely upon

Future work must not rely on this pass as a complete owner map, governance implementation design, universal controller, or authority to treat operator intent or report prose as repository law.
