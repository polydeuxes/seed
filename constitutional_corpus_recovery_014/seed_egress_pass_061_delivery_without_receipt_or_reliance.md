# Seed Egress Pass 061 — Delivery Without Receipt or Reliance

Repository authority wins.

## Prior pass consumed

Pass 061 consumes pass 060's bounded-result-to-representation relation and asks what happens when the outward representation is delivered, made available, acknowledged, or otherwise exposed to a recipient.

## Bounded egress relation recovered

Successful egress delivery is a bounded availability or communication occurrence, not proof of recipient uptake.

```text
outward representation with preserved limits
+ bounded delivery / availability / communication occurrence
= delivered or available representation standing
!= receipt, understanding, agreement, lawful reliance, action authority, responsibility transfer, or inquiry completion
```

The bounded result consumed is the pass-060 outward representation. The constitutional act is delivery or availability while preserving the representation boundary. The artifact or standing produced is a delivered, available, or communicated representation of the bounded result. The lawful recipient becomes eligible to inspect, question, compare, preserve, refuse, or seek admission of a new inquiry, but not to rely or act beyond separately warranted authority.

## Evidence followed

Pass 060 projected that representation does not strengthen result standing. The Book communication chapter states that handoff records a boundary and required external responsibility without provider trust, approval, execution, or full adoption. Evidence/provenance clauses distinguish observability from provenance and explanation from establishment. Pass 040's view-evaluation/activity boundary blocks treating calculated rows or representations as notification, observation, uptake, broadcast, or universal activity. Pass 056 blocks result formation from silently resolving neighboring inquiries.

## Recipient eligibility

Delivery makes the bounded representation available for bounded consumption. It may allow the recipient to see what was claimed, what was refused, what remains Unknown, what scope was stopped, what completion was declared, and what cannot be inferred. It does not prove the recipient actually received, read, understood, agreed, relied, assumed responsibility, or gained authority to act.

## Cross-examination

### Constitutional Grammar

A delivery occurrence may be evidence that a representation was exposed or made available in a bounded channel. It is not evidence that the representation's content became stronger Constitutional Grammar.

### External Grammar

If the recipient is external or operator-facing, external vocabulary and operator vocabulary do not become Seed authority through delivery. Source and translation limits remain attached.

### Fidelity

Delivery is another changed-form and handoff boundary. Compression for channel limits is lawful only if it preserves the egress invariants. Omitted Unknowns, collapsed confidence, or hidden refusal conditions are erasure.

### Inquiry-result standing

A delivered bounded result remains a bounded result. Delivery does not convert a scoped stop into proof of understanding or a completed neighboring inquiry.

### Communication, reliance, responsibility, and lawful stop

Delivery can satisfy a communication attempt or handoff availability condition where that condition is actually the bounded goal. It cannot satisfy reliance, responsibility transfer, or action permission unless the relevant artifact and authority say so. Lawful stop may be "delivered representation available" without becoming "recipient understood" or "recipient may act."

## Book projection decision

No additional Book change is warranted. `08.Handoff.C` now preserves the delivery/receipt/understanding/reliance/action distinctions at the egress seam, and existing evidence, observability, handoff, reliance, refusal, and stopping clauses preserve the neighboring consequences. This pass records topology and refusal conditions rather than adding another clause.
