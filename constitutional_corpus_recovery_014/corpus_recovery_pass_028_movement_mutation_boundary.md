# Corpus Recovery Pass 028 — Movement and Mutation Boundary

## Selected pressure

Access-transition topology and action/mutation vocabulary remain unresolved. This pass selected the narrower independently supported pressure that movement is not mutation and mutation is not movement by itself.

## Non-Book witnesses followed

Primary witnesses reviewed: `constitutional_corpus_recovery_014/acts_movement_access_constraints.md`, `constitutional_act_survey.md`, `constitutional_acts_constraints_topology_audit_001.md`, `constitutional_movement_characterization.md`, `constitutional_action_mutation_frontier_survey.md`, `constitutional_access_state_survey.md`, `constitutional_access_transition_characterization.md`, `constitutional_access_topology_audit_001.md`, and `constitutional_capability_use_boundary_investigation.md`.

The recurring distinction is stable even though general action/mutation vocabulary remains immature: lawful constitutional movement can be conceptual, evidentiary, selection-bearing, responsibility-bearing, or projection-bound without runtime mutation; runtime mutation or recording does not prove constitutional movement unless the responsible boundary preserves the warrant and limits for the transition claimed.

## Neighbor cross-examination

- Act/artifact witnesses distinguish occurrence from an artifact describing occurrence.
- Execution/recording witnesses distinguish operation performance, event recording, and knowledge extraction.
- Projection witnesses distinguish state visibility from truth or mutation authority.
- Access/capability witnesses distinguish access, availability, capability, use authority, and mutation.

## Mature finding

Movement and mutation are constitutionally separate. Movement requires a warranted transition in lawful position or standing; mutation requires its own operational authority and does not establish movement by itself.
