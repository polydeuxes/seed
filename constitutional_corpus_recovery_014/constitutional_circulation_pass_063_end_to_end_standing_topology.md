## Relation graph

### R1 — External or operator expression to addressable pressure

- **Consumed standing:** external representation, provider vocabulary, operator ask, prompt phrase, urgency, presentation label, or other source-attributed expression.
- **Constitutional act:** bounded addressability for examination, comparison, quotation, attachment, translation assessment, clarification, refusal, deferment, or frontier consideration.
- **Authority change:** no native constitutional authority is gained. The expression becomes addressable source-attributed pressure only.
- **Crossing artifact:** source-attributed addressable material or pressure, with source role or source role Unknown, provenance or provenance Unknown, scope, uncertainty, confidence and authority limits, negative authority, refusal conditions, and Unknowns.
- **Next-consumer eligibility:** translation, clarification, refusal, deferment, or frontier consideration may consume it within the preserved limits.
- **Preserved limits:** it is not Constitutional Grammar, adopted law, repository authority, cluster truth, evidence of answer, runtime dispatch, or mutation.
- **Stopping boundary:** if source, provenance, scope, authority, or refusal conditions cannot be preserved, the relation stops as Unknown or refusal rather than assimilation.
- **Branches and bypasses:** addressable pressure may be clarified, refused, deferred, held as Unknown, or translated toward possible inquiry. It need not become inquiry.

### R2 — Addressable pressure to translated need or possible inquiry

- **Consumed standing:** addressable pressure plus its source, provenance, scope, limits, exclusions, and Unknowns.
- **Constitutional act:** bounded translation from source expression into a Seed-facing need, uncertainty, or possible inquiry posture.
- **Authority change:** translation may create limited claim or examination standing for the translated scope. It does not transfer source authority to Seed and does not make the operator wording repository knowledge.
- **Crossing artifact:** translated need or possible inquiry bundle carrying identity, source, provenance, evidence demand, authority limit, lawful stop, exclusions, negative authority, refusal conditions, and Unknowns.
- **Next-consumer eligibility:** inquiry-frontier consideration, clarification, refusal, or local admission may consume the translated bundle.
- **Preserved limits:** prompt restatement, convenience wording, or dispatchability is insufficient for admission or execution.
- **Stopping boundary:** unsupported translation, absent evidence demand, unresolved authority, or non-preserved Unknowns stop as Unknown, refusal, or clarification.
- **Branches and bypasses:** the pressure may remain translated orientation without becoming admitted movement; it may support neighboring inquiry rather than the apparent prompt route.

### R3 — Possible inquiry to admitted inquiry movement

- **Consumed standing:** possible inquiry identity bundle with need or uncertainty, source, provenance, scope, evidence demand, authority limits, stop conditions, exclusions, conflicts, negative authority, and Unknowns.
- **Constitutional act:** consumer-local admission of bounded inquiry movement.
- **Authority change:** possible inquiry gains bounded participation or movement standing only inside local authority. Admission is not ratification of truth, answer standing, method selection, examination eligibility, execution, ownership, mutation, completion, or neighboring-inquiry retirement.
- **Crossing artifact:** admitted inquiry movement, admission artifact, selected bounded movement posture, or locally admitted interpretation.
- **Next-consumer eligibility:** admitted movement may be used by downstream establishment, frontier, or bounded examination-selection responsibilities if those responsibilities independently preserve their own prerequisites.
- **Preserved limits:** admitted movement carries the preserved identity bundle and does not erase unsupported neighboring inquiry.
- **Stopping boundary:** absent local admission authority, mismatch, conflicts, unresolved Unknowns, refusal conditions, or lack of applicable downstream responsibility stop movement without deleting the possible inquiry.
- **Branches and bypasses:** possible inquiry may remain possible, be refused, deferred, narrowed, clarified, or admitted by a neighboring local consumer.

### R4 — Admitted movement to bounded examination eligibility

- **Consumed standing:** admitted inquiry movement and any relevant frontier or handoff evidence.
- **Constitutional act:** bounded relevance or applicability determination for examination.
- **Authority change:** the output is permission to examine further inside the bounded responsibility; it is not selection, execution, mutation, truth establishment, or required action.
- **Crossing artifact:** examination eligibility, applicability finding, frontier classification, or lawful inactivity finding.
- **Next-consumer eligibility:** a method selector, probe request, comparison responsibility, or lawful-stop decision may consume the bounded eligibility output.

- **Stopping boundary:** irrelevant, unsupported, insufficiently bound, outside-authority, stale, unavailable, conflicting, or Unknown material produces lawful inactivity or Unknown rather than silent execution.
- **Branches and bypasses:** admitted movement can stop locally, select no work, produce lawful inactivity, or proceed to one or more bounded examinations. Not every admitted inquiry traverses every method.

- **Consumed standing:** attributed testimony, probe output, comparison input, or bounded examination output.
- **Constitutional act:** comparison, cross-examination, relevance judgment, or finding formation within the stated boundary.
- **Authority change:** the output may gain bounded comparison or finding standing. It does not become established truth, warrant, reliance, source independence, implementation permission, reconciliation, or completion merely by agreeing, contradicting, repeating, or being comparable.

### R6 — Scoped finding to bounded result

### R7 — Bounded result to outward representation

- **Consumed standing:** bounded result with preserved identity and limits.
- **Constitutional act:** representation, communication, rendering, compression, report inclusion, API/CLI emission, or handoff.
- **Authority change:** the representation gains presentation or communication standing for the bounded result. It does not strengthen truth, authority, reliance, action permission, receipt, understanding, completion, or mutation.
- **Crossing artifact:** operator-facing or external representation of the bounded result.

### R8 — Representation to delivered or available standing

- **Consumed standing:** outward representation.
- **Constitutional act:** delivery or availability.
- **Authority change:** only bounded delivered/available representation standing is added. Receipt, understanding, reliance, action authority, responsibility transfer, or completion require separate evidence and authority.
- **Crossing artifact:** delivered/available representation record or communication surface.
- **Next-consumer eligibility:** operator response, recipient action inquiry, receipt proof inquiry, or no further action may follow.
- **Preserved limits:** all result and representation limits continue to bind delivery.
- **Stopping boundary:** no receipt or response evidence means the circulation lawfully stops at availability.
- **Branches and bypasses:** no response, delayed response, clarification, refusal, disagreement, correction claim, or new request are all possible without reopening the prior result.

### R9 — Operator response to new ingress pressure

- **Consumed standing:** operator response, acknowledgement, correction claim, disagreement, follow-up, clarification, or new ask related or unrelated to the delivered representation.
- **Constitutional act:** new addressability and, only if warranted, translation and admission through the ingress boundary.
- **Authority change:** response may create new source-attributed pressure, possible inquiry, or admitted movement. It does not silently mutate, correct, supersede, reopen, complete, or relocate authority over the prior result.
- **Crossing artifact:** new addressable pressure or translated possible inquiry with provenance to the response and, where evidenced, relation to the prior representation.
- **Next-consumer eligibility:** the same ingress, translation, clarification, refusal, deferment, frontier, and admission responsibilities may consume it.
- **Preserved limits:** prior-result identity and limits remain prior-result limits unless a separate correction, supersession, reopening, or responsibility-transfer authority is established.
- **Stopping boundary:** if the response only evidences receipt or opinion without admission authority, circulation stops as response-standing or new pressure Unknown.
- **Branches and bypasses:** response can start a neighboring inquiry, request clarification, refuse, add evidence, or do nothing. It is re-entry, not automatic reopening.

## Joinder analysis

The graph supports adjacent handoffs only where the later relation consumes the standing and limits produced by the earlier relation. R1 joins R2 when bounded translation preserves addressability limits. R2 joins R3 only where local admission authority acts. R3 joins R4 only where bounded examination responsibilities independently establish relevance or applicability. R4 joins R5 only where output is examined or compared under preserved attribution and scope. R5 joins R6 only where local sufficiency or stopping authority forms a result. R6 joins R7 through the communication/handoff clause. R7 joins R8 only as delivery or availability, not uptake. R8 joins R9 only when an operator response itself becomes source-attributed pressure.

Unsupported joins remain Unknown: universal owner topology; canonical ingress, inquiry, result, egress, or response envelopes; runtime orchestrator; complete proof topology for receipt, understanding, reliance, action, correction, supersession, reopening, and neighboring-inquiry closure; and any claim that every inquiry traverses every relation.

Every seam must preserve inherited limits or stop as refusal, deferment, lawful inactivity, partial finding, local completion, or Unknown.
