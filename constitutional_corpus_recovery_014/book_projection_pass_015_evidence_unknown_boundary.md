# Book Projection Pass 015 — Evidence, Unknown, Absence, and Boundary-Claim

## Question answered

The recovered constitutional family currently carrying the strongest unresolved pressure against the Book is **Evidence, Unknown, Absence, and Boundary-Claim**.

The part now lawfully projected into the Book is the mature negative-claim boundary: a missing, unmatched, unsupported, or unpreserved support result may be preserved only as a bounded negative finding with its corpus, surface, claim form, temporal boundary, authority boundary, and limitations; it must not become falsehood, disproof, global nonexistence, legal invalidity, historical absence, operational absence, no-harm, no-event, no-authority, or established fact without separate authority and evidence.

## Why this family was selected

`book_coverage_readiness.md` marks this family as only partially preserved, while several other families are marked adequately preserved. The same readiness comparison identifies absence-of-evidence stronger-claim analysis and legal/adjudicative boundary investigations as report-only findings. `cross_examination.md` also gives this family cross-family force: evidence and Unknown constraints bind authority/reliance, and unsupported forms must not cross into authority, truth, mutation, ownership, Book law, or universal topology.

This family therefore exerted the strongest unresolved pressure because it is both partially Book-covered and foundational to the lawful projection of every other family.

## What the Book already adequately preserved

The Book already preserves that evidence is not fact, explanation is not establishment, provenance representation is not verified provenance, testimony is not established fact, diagnostic findings are not cluster truth, recording is not knowledge extraction, and bounded reliance does not expand authority or prove truth.

These clauses cover much of the family’s positive evidence/provenance/testimony boundary and much of its reliance boundary.

## What remained report-only before this pass

The Book did not directly preserve the family’s mature negative-claim grammar. It implied non-promotion through evidence/testimony/recording/authority clauses, but it did not expressly state that bounded nonmatches, unsupported results, unpreserved support, or mismatch may remain only bounded negative findings and Unknowns rather than becoming stronger absence, falsity, invalidity, historical, operational, legal, or authority conclusions.

## Projection boundary resolved in this pass

### What belongs in the Book

A single general clause belongs in the evidence chapter: **bounded negative findings and preserved Unknowns**. It is mature because the same pattern recurs across absence evidence, boundary mismatch, analytical competence, legal authority/warrant, repetition/proof, and evidence readiness. It is not tied to one implementation surface or one legal investigation.

### What is already adequately preserved

The Book already preserves evidence/fact, explanation/establishment, testimony/fact, recording/knowledge extraction, diagnostic-finding/cluster-truth, and bounded-reliance/authority-expansion boundaries.

### What must remain outside the Book

The following remain report-only:

- the full absence-of-evidence checklist;
- diagnostic command details and app-visible output shapes;
- legal/adjudicative examples and legal-source mechanics;
- stronger evidence-of-absence readiness analysis;
- any general truth engine, legal validity engine, contradiction engine, search-quality engine, or implementation design;
- the full Unknown taxonomy and implementation-local fields such as `unknown_type`, `area`, and `reason`.

### What remains unresolved

- Whether stronger evidence-of-absence claims can ever be supported for particular corpora or surfaces.
- Which Unknown categories are stable constitutional kinds rather than implementation-local preservation fields.
- Whether boundary-confusion review becomes constitutional checklist grammar or remains report grammar.
- How exact Book-readiness thresholds should be stated for recurring negative laws.
