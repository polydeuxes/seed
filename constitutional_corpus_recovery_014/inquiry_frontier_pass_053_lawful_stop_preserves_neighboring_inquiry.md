## Recovered relation

Lawful stop changes only the bounded standing it has authority to change. It may preserve a possible inquiry as unresolved, deferred, refused, not established, admitted-but-stopped, answered within scope, exhausted within searched territory, or Unknown. It does not erase neighboring inquiry possibilities, collapse them into the stopped inquiry, or prove their answers unless the stopping artifact expressly has that scope and evidence.

The inspectable change is carried by the stopping, refusal, completion, answer, exhaustion, or Unknown-preservation artifact itself: it must show the inquiry identity, authority boundary, evidence territory, scope, confidence or sufficiency basis, negative authority, Unknowns, and what remains outside the stop.

## Cross-examination

### Constitutional Grammar

A bounded question includes lawful stop conditions, but those conditions do not settle all neighboring questions. Answer standing remains scoped to the admitted question and evidence territory. Question identity and frontier identity therefore survive a stop as boundary testimony even when the inquiry does not move further.

### External Grammar

External pressure can remain preserved as source-attributed material without becoming native inquiry, admitted inquiry, or completion. Refusing or stopping a translated inquiry does not assimilate or delete untranslated source neighbors.

### Fidelity

A stop is faithful only if downstream consumers preserve the tested scope and refusal conditions. It is unfaithful to summarize “stopped,” “answered,” or “exhausted” as global completion when the artifact tested only one frontier identity, one admitted movement, one evidence territory, or one authority boundary.

### Admission and authority

Admission permitted bounded participation; stopping terminates or limits that participation. It does not ratify unrelated inquiry, create reusable refusal authority, or authorize mutation. A refused inquiry may still preserve pressure as Unknown or deferred if evidence supports that preservation.

### Responsibility

Stopping authority is local. The repository does not recover a universal retainer, deleter, retirement owner, or neighbor-pruning authority. If an artifact lacks authority over neighboring inquiries, it must leave them visible or Unknown rather than erase them.

### Unknown and lawful stop

Unknown is a lawful preserved standing, not a defect to hide. Exhausted evidence territory supports only a scoped exhaustion claim; absence of evidence requires search/corpus boundaries; deferred pressure requires preserved reason; refusal requires authority boundary; answered standing requires scoped answer support. Missing owner, missing evidence, stale evidence, unavailable evidence, conflict, outside-scope material, and unsupported implementation realization all constitute lawful stop conditions without frontier completion.

## Book projection decision

No Book change is warranted. The current Book already preserves lawful stopping, refusal/non-performance, unresolved material, bounded negative findings, Unknowns, question standing, frontier identity, admission limits, and diagnostic/projection non-promotion. This pass records topology and live-frontier consequences rather than a new independently missing Book clause.

## Preserved Unknowns

- Complete vocabulary for stopped, deferred, exhausted, answered, refused, and unresolved inquiry standings.
- Owner-local authority for retiring, deleting, merging, splitting, or reopening neighboring inquiry.
- Whether implementation has all required stop/refusal/answer artifacts.
- Complete frontier topology after multiple adjacent stopped inquiries.

## Live frontier after pass 053

The inquiry frontier is not complete. It is narrowed to three recovered relations: frontier identity is conjunctive and not wording-derived; possible inquiry is not admitted inquiry movement; and lawful stop changes only scoped standing while preserving neighboring inquiry unless authority and evidence support a broader change. Remaining unresolved work includes owner maps, exact standing vocabulary, runtime realization, and complete neighboring-inquiry topology.
