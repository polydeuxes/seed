# Book Projection Pass 039 — Producer Occurrence and Artifact Shape Boundary

## Pressure examined

Whether artifact shape, direct construction, field equality, row identity, or consumer-local validation should receive a separate Book clause denying proof of producer occurrence.

## Projection decision

No Book change.

## Reason

The Book already preserves the independent consequence. Constructors and production authority distinguish constructibility from lawful production and live producer return from durable producer-to-result evidence. Acts and act artifacts distinguish consumer validation from producer occurrence and require responsible validated production, execution, or recording evidence where occurrence is claimed. Evidence, provenance, reachability, observability, and recording clauses further block the same over-inference.

## Frontier update

Producer-occurrence topology remains available for future recovery only if new evidence supports a new consequence such as a general occurrence-recording grammar or producer-seal boundary. Direct construction, field equality, and local admissibility remain inadequate grounds for a new Book clause.
