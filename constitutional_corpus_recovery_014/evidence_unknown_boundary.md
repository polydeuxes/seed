## Smallest recurring distinctions

- **Absence of evidence is not evidence of absence** unless corpus, scope, search method, and temporal boundary support a bounded absence claim.
- **Boundary mismatch is not invalidity.** It creates pressure, support gaps, or incompatibility, not legal or constitutional nullification by itself.
- **Analytical competence is not adjudicative authority.** Seed may analyze support without binding legal effect.
- **Repetition is not proof.** Recurring prose can indicate pressure but cannot replace evidence, implementation witness, or authority.
- **Unknown is a preserved standing.** Unknowns are lawful stops, not failures to be erased.

## Contradictions and refinements

This family contradicts shortcuts that convert missing evidence into falsehood, mismatch into invalidity, confidence into authority, or repeated language into law. The strongest refinement is that negative findings are lawful only when they state corpus boundary, search boundary, authority boundary, and claim strength.

## Local findings vs wider recurrence

Legal-boundary artifacts are local to legal/adjudicative claims, but their competence/authority distinction recurs widely. Absence analysis is local to negative claims but generalizes to all corpus recovery: missing Book clauses or missing implementation cannot be invented.

## Implementation witnesses

The typed Unknown owner is a direct implementation witness where it preserves `unknown_type`, `area`, and `reason`. Diagnostic audits and tests can witness bounded absence or boundary mismatch only for their inspected surfaces.
