## Pressure selected

The selected pressure is from the Artifact Grammar, Kind, Standing, and Availability family: a constitutional kind label or recurring artifact form can preserve a bounded standing distinction, but it does not close Seed's ontology or supply production authority by label alone.

The independently supported distinction is: kind labels, artifact forms, characterization categories, dataclass names, concordance entries, inventory rows, and recurring report types may preserve bounded standing for named subjects and consumers; they do not close the ontology, equate similarly named materials, mandate artifact grammar, or supply kind-specific production authority.
