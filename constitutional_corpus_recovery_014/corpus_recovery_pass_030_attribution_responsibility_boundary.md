## Selected pressure

After the causation boundary, a neighboring evidence/provenance pressure remained: repository surfaces may attribute claims, sources, accounts, devices, owners, deployments, events, or diagnostics without proving responsibility. This pass selected the independently supported `Attribution != Responsibility` boundary.

The recurring distinction is stable: attribution can preserve provenance, association, attachment, or inquiry pressure, but it does not by itself prove accountability, fault, blame, intent, negligence, liability, moral responsibility, implementation ownership, or implementation pressure.

## Neighbor cross-examination

- The causation boundary prevents attributed causal language or system causation from becoming actor responsibility.
- Access/capability/use witnesses prevent account, device, IP, role, or access association from proving action or responsible use.
- Source and model-output witnesses prevent external blame or generated attribution from becoming evidence of responsibility without translation and support.
- Responsibility/governance witnesses keep implementation responsibility local and explicit rather than inferred from attachment or diagnostic ownership candidates.
- Diagnostic visibility witnesses keep ownership candidates and discrepancy surfaces from mutating cluster truth or assigning fault.

## Mature finding

Attribution and responsibility are constitutionally separate. Attribution may support narrow attribution claims and lawful inquiry orientation, but stronger responsibility claims require separate evidence, authority boundary, temporal provenance, support path, confidence limits, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop.
