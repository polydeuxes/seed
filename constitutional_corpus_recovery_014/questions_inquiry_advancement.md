## Smallest recurring distinctions

- **Question is not answer.** A question binds inquiry, evidence demand, and stop conditions; it does not settle the outcome.
- **Operator ask is not constitutional inquiry by itself.** Origination requires bounded translation into repository-evidenced inquiry.
- **Presentation vocabulary is not knowledge.** Terms such as frontier or current position require implementation/repository evidence before preservation.
- **Advancement need is not authorization.** Need can open movement consideration but does not select or authorize work by itself.
- **Asymmetry matters.** Some questions are designed to correct expectation rather than confirm a desired topology.

## Contradictions and refinements

This family resists converting operator stories into constitutional grammar and resists turning every question into design work. Advancement reports refine inquiry by showing that need, consideration, selection, and authorization are separate acts.

## Local findings vs wider recurrence

Inquiry-frontier reports are local to frontier and handoff surfaces. Wider recurrence is bounded-question discipline: every work item needs question, corpus boundary, evidence, supported finding, negative authority, Unknowns, and lawful stop.

## Implementation witnesses

Question projection and pipeline slices witness some inquiry surfaces. Tests and CLI outputs witness only their implemented question forms, not a universal Seed language.

## What may be relied upon

The corpus supports bounded inquiry as a constitutional responsibility: scope first, evidence before promotion, and lawful stop when evidence or authority is missing.

## What may not be relied upon

Do not infer a universal inquiry topology, operator-intent ontology, or implementation design from question vocabulary alone.
