# Fidelity Recovery Pass 042 — Consumer Eligibility Is Not Authority Transfer

## Prior-pass evidence consumed

Pass 041 recovered that faithful realization through changed form requires warrant survival. This pass uses that result to examine what happens after the translated or constructed artifact reaches a downstream consumer.

## Selected Fidelity pressure

A faithful realization may make a downstream consumer eligible to inspect, render, validate, evaluate, or rely within its role. That eligibility is not a transfer of constitutional authority to the consumer and not proof that the consumer may decide a stronger constitutional question.

The pressure is:

```text
boundary-crossing artifact
-> consumer eligibility
!= authority relocation
```

## Evidence followed

This pass cross-examined Pass 041 against `constitutional_action_mutation_frontier_survey.md`, `responsibility_recovery_evaluation_readiness_investigation.md`, `constitutional_process_implementation_ownership_audit.md`, `constitutional_fidelity_investigation.md`, `constitutional_responsibility_transfer_investigation.md`, and `constitutional_reliance_characterization.md`.

## Recovered pressure

A faithful handoff requires a concrete producer/artifact/consumer relationship, but the existence of that relationship only establishes bounded eligibility. The consumer receives a usable artifact under preserved warrant; it does not receive the upstream responsibility, all constitutional authority named in the artifact, or permission to generalize beyond its local role.

This distinguishes fidelity from convenient orchestration. Orchestration may route work correctly while still being unfaithful if it lets the route decide authority. Fidelity requires the downstream surface to remain a consumer with a bounded role unless separate evidence proves responsibility transfer.

## Cross-examination against authority and implementation evidence

`constitutional_process_implementation_ownership_audit.md` repeatedly asks whether producer, artifact/helper, and consumer are visible. It accepts exact admission, selected dispatch, diagnostic shape audit, and orientation internals where local producers and consumers exist, but refuses to recover a general Recovery owner, construction owner, universal Cross-Examination owner, Completion Audit owner, or global Stop owner where the only evidence would be wrapping or renaming.

`constitutional_action_mutation_frontier_survey.md` states the handoff discipline directly: producer completion, boundary-crossing artifact, and consumer eligibility are not autonomous action. A consumer may rely, evaluate, render, validate, or proceed only within the artifact's authority and the consumer's role. Pass 041 supplies why: the artifact must carry surviving warrant rather than expanded authority.

`constitutional_responsibility_transfer_investigation.md` prevents a stronger interpretation. Responsibility transfer requires explicit transfer evidence; it cannot be inferred merely from adjacency, dependency, output availability, or downstream consumption. `constitutional_reliance_characterization.md` likewise treats reliance as lawful downstream use of warranted content within a preserved authority boundary, not ownership of the authority that warranted it.

Implementation evidence fits this pattern. `BoundedWorkDispatchRequest` makes selected surfaces invocable; it does not make the dispatch layer the Recovery owner or make selected surfaces owners of admission. Diagnostic rows make shape status visible; they do not give formatters universal Completion Audit authority. Inquiry orientation payloads make supports renderable; they do not give rendering authority to mutate cluster truth.

## Constitutional invariants

1. A handoff artifact must preserve its producer boundary, consumer boundary, and warrant scope.
2. Consumer eligibility is limited to the consumer's local role.
3. Responsibility transfer requires explicit evidence; dependency or consumption is insufficient.
4. Upstream authority is not relocated merely because an artifact becomes available downstream.
5. Downstream success does not retroactively prove upstream authority.

## Lawful implementation freedom

Implementation may lawfully orchestrate through requests, results, rows, payloads, lookup results, summaries, and compatibility objects. It may select a downstream consumer and pass a bounded artifact. It may combine comparison and status inside one audit-local owner where implementation evidence supports that owner. It may distribute stop across local consumers with stopping evidence.

## Erasure, invention, mutation, or relocation of authority

Erasure occurs when a handoff hides which local owner produced the stopping or warranting evidence. Invention occurs when a downstream consumer treats artifact availability as authority to decide admission, recovery, truth, completion, or mutation. Mutation occurs when successful downstream rendering, validation, or diagnostic recording becomes cluster truth. Relocation occurs when the consumer is treated as owner of the upstream constitutional responsibility without explicit transfer evidence.

## Bounded fidelity determination

The bounded determination asks whether a specific handoff preserves consumer eligibility without authority relocation. It may produce:

```text
eligible consumer within preserved role / authority relocation detected / Unknown
```

Later responsibilities may consume that finding when evaluating reliance, responsibility transfer, implementation ownership recovery, or lawful stop. The finding is not a complete owner map.
