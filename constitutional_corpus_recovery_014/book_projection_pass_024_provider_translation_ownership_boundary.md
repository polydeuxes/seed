# Book Projection Pass 024 — Provider Translation Ownership Boundary

## Projection question

Should the Book receive a new clause assigning provider-translation ownership or canonizing a provider/source taxonomy?

## Evidence bearing on projection

The selected corpus pressure confirms a mature boundary against source grammar promotion, but not a mature topology. The family report preserves Unknowns for complete provider-source taxonomy and for whether provider translation has one owner or multiple local owners. Cross-examination also lists translation ownership as missing.

## Current Book coverage

`book_of_seed/01-grammar-and-standing/external-and-constitutional-grammar.md` already states that provider vocabulary, external representations, and source confidence labels remain attributed source grammar until bounded translation carries source, scope, context, translated Seed term or claim, uncertainty, and authority limit. It also blocks conversion into native constitutional grammar, cluster truth, repository authority, or adopted law.

## Decision

No Book change was made. A stronger clause assigning a translation owner or taxonomy would exceed repository evidence and would collapse local provider-contract witnesses into universal constitutional ownership.

## Remaining frontier

Complete provider/source taxonomy and provider-translation ownership remain report-only. Future work would need independent owner evidence before projecting those details.
