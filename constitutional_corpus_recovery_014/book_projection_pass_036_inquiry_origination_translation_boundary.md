# Book Projection Pass 036 — Inquiry Origination Requires Bounded Translation

## Current Book comparison

The current Book states that question-shaped text is not automatically constitutional inquiry and that question standing is not answer standing. It also identifies lineage loss during reformulation as a failure mode. Those clauses support the direction but do not make origination-by-translation an addressable boundary.

## Projection made

Added `04.Question.B — Inquiry origination requires bounded translation` to `book_of_seed/04-inquiry-and-examination/questions-and-inquiry.md`.

The clause preserves that operator asks, prompt phrases, uncertainty statements, reformulations, and presentation labels may initiate work only after translation into a bounded inquiry with identity, source, scope, evidence demand, authority limit, uncertainty, and lawful stop. It also states the forbidden promotions: operator wording is not repository knowledge, does not assign universal inquiry ownership, retire neighboring questions, establish answers, or authorize investigation beyond the translated boundary.

## Boundaries preserved

The projection does not establish exact question-form kinds, universal inquiry ownership, deletion or retirement authority, prompt ontology, implementation pipeline, or a general presentation-vocabulary taxonomy.
