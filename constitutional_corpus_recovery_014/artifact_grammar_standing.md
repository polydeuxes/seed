# Artifact Grammar, Kind, Standing, and Availability Family

## Independent evidence inspected

Primary witnesses: `constitutional_artifact_identity_characterization.md`, `constitutional_artifact_characterization_grammar_survey.md`, `constitutional_artifact_competency_survey.md`, `constitutional_artifact_availability_characterization.md`, `constitutional_kind_cross_examination.md`, `constitutional_artifact_boundary_confusion_review_readiness_investigation.md`, and `constitutional_artifact_availability_characterization.md`.

## Smallest recurring distinctions

- **Artifact is not law.** An artifact may carry evidence, local identity, and bounded use without becoming constitutional law.
- **Kind is not ontology.** Constitutional kinds describe standing distinctions; they do not impose a universal type system.
- **Availability is not authorization.** A visible or available artifact is not automatically dispatchable, reliable, operational, or public.
- **Characterization is not implementation.** Characterizations preserve shape, evidence, Unknowns, and negative authority without producing runtime machinery.
- **Identity is local.** Artifact identity depends on evidence, continuity, owner, and use boundary.

## Contradictions and refinements

This family refines earlier tendencies to treat reports, surveys, audits, or slices as if their existence settled standing. Availability reports especially constrain artifact enthusiasm: available evidence may still be diagnostic-only, candidate, stale, or unsupported for the requested use.

## Local findings vs wider recurrence

Local artifact forms include scout, survey, topology, characterization, investigation, audit, slice, reconciliation, and report. Wider recurrence is that every form must state boundary, evidence, supported finding, negative authority, Unknowns, and confidence before it can be relied upon.

## Implementation witnesses

Read-model slices, diagnostic inventory surfaces, and implementation slices can witness artifact availability or shape. They do not make the artifact true, canonical, or mutation-capable.

## Preserved Unknowns

- Whether artifact grammar should become mandatory form.
- Exact closure of constitutional kinds.
- Which artifact families deserve independent Book standing.

## What may be relied upon

It may be relied upon that repository artifacts have bounded standing and that artifact form, availability, and recurrence are not sufficient for law, implementation, or universal authority.

## What may not be relied upon

Do not treat file presence, report maturity, kind labels, or inventory inclusion as automatic constitutional authority.
