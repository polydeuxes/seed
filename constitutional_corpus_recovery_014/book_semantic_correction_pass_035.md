# Book Semantic Correction Pass 035 — Recovery Chronology Removal

## Boundary

This pass performs only the requested semantic correction against current Book clauses affected by recovery-status wording introduced during recent projection work. It does not reopen the underlying projections, add new constitutional distinctions, alter implementation, or conduct a general style sweep.

## Problem

The Book must present current constitutional evidence, authority, standing, and boundaries neutrally. Phrases such as `recovered authority` and `recovered constitutional evidence` import the chronology of corpus recovery into Book law. That chronology belongs in recovery and projection reports, not in the Book's current-facing clauses.

## Corrections made

- In `07.Realization.A`, `preserves the recovered authority` was corrected to `preserves the authority` while retaining the same boundary: lawful realization depends on preserving authority, evidence boundary, warrant, scope, Unknowns, handoff, recording boundary, and mutation boundary, not on structural symmetry.
- In `05.Evidence.F`, `recovered constitutional evidence` was corrected to `constitutional evidence` while retaining the same boundary: observability classification, reconstruction, inference, and Unknown are distinct standings and do not themselves establish provenance.

## Cross-examination

The corrections do not change the constitutional meaning of passes 032 or 033. They remove only backward-looking status from Book wording. The reports may continue to describe what was recovered or projected; the Book now states the current authority and evidence boundary without recovery chronology.

## Negative authority

This pass does not authorize any new Book clause, implementation change, diagnostic surface, topology, inventory, provider taxonomy, legal/adjudicative machinery, or corpus-completion claim.
