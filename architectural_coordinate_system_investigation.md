# Architectural Coordinate System Investigation

## Status

This is a bounded architectural investigation. It does not introduce implementation, recover ownership, add runtime machinery, redesign the Constitution, stabilize vocabulary, or propose a universal framework.

Repository authority wins. The question is whether recent repository work supports two independent architectural coordinate systems, or whether that reading is only another attractive compression over existing evidence.

## Architectural question

Recent work repeatedly failed to recover another architectural layer. Instead, it recovered recurring necessities that cut across many responsibilities.

This investigation asks:

```text
Has the repository independently recovered
an architectural kind axis
and
an architectural pressure axis

rather than one larger hierarchy?
```

Candidate kind axis:

```text
Constitution
Physiology
Behavior
```

Candidate pressure axis:

```text
Continuity
Evidence
Locality
Self-observation
```

The candidate axes are treated as hypotheses, not repository law.

Several findings constrain the investigation:

1. The pressure investigation explicitly treats recurring vocabulary as non-proof and asks what failure each pressure prevents.
2. The architectural-law characterization distinguishes structure questions from lawful-movement questions and warns that recurring constraints do not become a new owner, layer, or family.
3. The constitutional / physiological / behavioral characterization separates lawful givens, response tendencies, and visible acts, while warning that the layers must not backfill one another.
4. Responsibility recovery repeatedly stops when remaining evidence belongs to adjacent pressure rather than same-family ownership.
5. Attachment and participation work shows that visibility, candidate preservation, local records, promotion, and repository participation are separately admitted and can stop at different boundaries.

## Candidate coordinate systems

### Candidate coordinate system 1: architectural kind

The repository has some support for a kind-like question:

```text
What kind of architectural phenomenon is this?
```

The strongest current candidate kinds are:

| Candidate kind | Question it answers | Repository-supported characterization |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Constitution | What lawful facts, permissions, prohibitions, identities, invariants, or boundaries govern movement? | Durable authority and boundary discipline. |
| Physiology | What tendencies, pressures, capacities, or regulatory responses arise under lawful conditions? | Response structure without law-making or literal output scripting. |
| Behavior | What visible action, output, record, rendering, stop, or handoff occurred? | Observable expression constrained by law and mediated by response tendencies. |
| Responsibility / structure | What bounded work, surface, artifact, owner, or handoff exists? | Implementation-backed local ownership where evidence supports it. |
| Artifact / record | What preserved thing carries bounded evidence, status, unknowns, or compatibility shape? | Handoff and preservation object without automatic authority. |

The Constitution / Physiology / Behavior sequence is not sufficient by itself. Repository work also uses responsibility, structure, artifact, evidence, claim, diagnostic, frontier, candidate, participation, and attachment distinctions. Therefore, `Constitution / Physiology / Behavior` is a strong kind lens for organism discussions, but not a complete taxonomy for the repository.

### Candidate coordinate system 2: architectural pressure

The repository has stronger evidence for pressure-like questions:

```text
What architectural necessity is this satisfying?
What failure would occur if this pressure were absent?
```

The pressure investigation recovers pressures such as bounding mutable behavior, separating enduring constraints from current condition, requiring evidence before movement, bounding authority, specializing work, handing off bounded artifacts, permitting lawful unknown, detecting internal drift, and revising under new evidence.

Those pressures can be compressed cautiously into the task's candidate pressure names:

| Candidate pressure | Repository-supported form | Failure prevented |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Continuity | Preserve identity, lawful transition, provenance, status, and handoff across change. | Drift, self-authorizing behavior, loss of why something moved. |
| Evidence | Require admissible support before movement, promotion, verification, or ownership recovery. | Unsupported transition, vocabulary promotion, mutation from presentation. |
| Locality | Bound authority, specialize work, keep provider/read-model/diagnostic/inquiry decisions local unless admitted. | Universal ownership, global side effects, untestable abstractions. |
| Self-observation | Expose shape, diagnostics, lineage, agreement, recurrence, and auditability without making visibility truth. | Invisible drift, unauditable current condition, presentation becoming knowledge. |

These names are not stabilized vocabulary. They are a compact way to describe recurring necessities already stated in more specific repository language.

## Evidence that both coordinates are useful

### Answer Composition

- **Kind:** primarily behavior/artifact composition. It produces bounded answer material, compatibility objects, reasoning/support separation, limitations, unknowns, and stop conditions.
- **Pressures:** evidence, continuity, locality, and self-observation.
- **Evidence:** answer-composition work does not create facts or mutation; it composes read-only answer material over evidence and authority. It preserves support and boundaries, keeps answer surfaces local, and exposes why an answer stops or claims less.
- **Coordinate finding:** one kind-like phenomenon satisfies multiple pressures. It is not just a pressure, and not just a layer.

### Structure Observation

- **Kind:** structure/observation artifact and behavior-visible diagnostic surface.
- **Pressures:** evidence, locality, and self-observation.
- **Evidence:** structure observation can expose methods, containment, routes, and relations while refusing unsupported behavior, ownership, semantic interpretation, or participation.
- **Coordinate finding:** self-observation recurs here, but the artifact is not Constitution, Physiology, or Behavior alone. It is an observation surface whose pressure is to make structure inspectable without over-promoting it.

### Provider Translation

- **Kind:** local translation responsibility and provider-local artifact boundary.
- **Pressures:** locality, evidence, continuity.
- **Evidence:** provider translation preserves provider-specific observation boundaries without earning a shared provider abstraction, schema change, or cross-provider layer. Provider-local records do not automatically become shared facts or semantic truth.
- **Coordinate finding:** locality pressure explains why the repository resisted a universal provider layer; kind explains what the artifact is doing locally.

### Inquiry Lineage

- **Kind:** lineage/provenance artifact.
- **Pressures:** continuity, evidence, self-observation.
- **Evidence:** lineage preserves derivation, uncertainty, prior artifacts, family vocabulary status, and frontier boundaries without authorizing conclusions by itself.
- **Coordinate finding:** continuity pressure recurs strongly, but lineage is not merely continuity. It is a preserved artifact that supports later evidence assessment.

### Attachment

- **Kind:** candidate relation or preserved association.
- **Pressures:** continuity, locality, evidence.
- **Evidence:** candidate attachment is distinct from repository participation. Attachment alone does not imply fact, answer authority, policy, execution, or mutation.
- **Coordinate finding:** attachment can preserve possible relation continuity while locality and evidence block automatic promotion.

### Participation

- **Kind:** admission boundary into a repository responsibility or shared surface.
- **Pressures:** evidence, locality, continuity.
- **Evidence:** participation is distributed across local owners such as observation ingestion, projection/current-fact selection, provider extraction, agreement rules, grammar recurrence, read-only audit builders, and readiness checks. There is no universal participation gateway.
- **Coordinate finding:** the same pressure of locality recurs across many participation decisions; the kind is an admission boundary, not a layer.

### Current Work Position

- **Kind:** presentation or orientation label unless implementation evidence says more.
- **Pressures:** continuity and self-observation when bounded to orientation.
- **Evidence:** repository instructions explicitly warn that presentation vocabulary such as current work position may exist only as visibility or presentation labels and should not be promoted into preserved/projected knowledge without implementation evidence.
- **Coordinate finding:** this is a counterexample to over-stabilizing either axis. It may satisfy orientation pressure, but its kind is not safely recoverable as repository knowledge.

### Grammar Observation

- **Kind:** recurrence observation / read-only knowledge surface.
- **Pressures:** evidence, self-observation, locality.
- **Evidence:** grammar observation requires recurrence and still rejects grammar, truth, and responsibility promotion.
- **Coordinate finding:** evidence and self-observation operate together: recurrence may make a pattern observable without making it authoritative.

### Capability Candidates

- **Kind:** candidate inventory / readiness inspection surface.
- **Pressures:** evidence, locality, continuity.
- **Evidence:** capability candidates are preserved but are not execution decisions, policy evaluations, tool invocations, capability permissions, facts, goals, plans, or event-ledger entries.
- **Coordinate finding:** candidate preservation supports continuity, while evidence/locality prevent premature promotion.

## Answers to required questions

### 1. Does every recovered artifact possess both architectural kind and architectural pressure?

Not every artifact has a safely stabilized kind, but every reviewed recovered artifact can be asked both questions.

The better formulation is:

```text
Every recovered artifact can be characterized by
what it is doing architecturally
and
what necessity or failure-boundary it is satisfying.
```

But the repository does not prove that every artifact possesses a stable two-field classification. Some artifacts, such as `Current Work Position`, may remain presentation vocabulary unless implementation evidence supports preservation.

### 2. Can one architectural kind satisfy multiple pressures?

Yes. Answer Composition is the clearest case. It is a bounded answer/artifact composition kind, while satisfying evidence, continuity, locality, and self-observation pressures at once.

Structure Observation similarly exposes self-observation, protects evidence boundaries, and preserves locality. Provider Translation preserves locality while supporting evidence and continuity.

### 3. Can one pressure recur across multiple kinds?

Yes. Evidence pressure recurs across Constitution, responsibility recovery, answer composition, structure observation, provider translation, inquiry lineage, grammar observation, attachment, participation, and capability candidates.

Locality also recurs across provider-local translation, distributed participation admission, answer surfaces, read-only diagnostics, and bounded responsibility ownership.

Continuity recurs through lineage, answer support, attachment, current-fact/projection boundaries, and frontier preservation.

Self-observation recurs through diagnostics, structure observation, inquiry lineage, grammar observation, pressure inventories, and audits.

### 4. Does this better explain why the repository resisted another architectural layer?

Yes, boundedly. The repository often resisted another layer because the remaining evidence was not same-family ownership. It was adjacent pressure: evidence gating, locality, continuity, or self-observation appearing across many surfaces.

This explains why investigations kept failing to recover a single new owner: a pressure can require changes or explanations in many owners without itself becoming an owner.

### 5. Have previous investigations accidentally mixed these coordinate systems?

Likely yes, in some cases. The repeated search for another architectural layer appears to have treated recurring pressure as if it should become a kind, owner, or hierarchy level.

Examples:

- treating `architectural law` as a possible new layer, then concluding it is mostly recurring constitutional constraint;
- treating participation as a possible universal gateway, then finding distributed local admission;
- treating provider translation recurrence as a possible shared provider abstraction, then finding provider-local boundaries;
- treating current/presentation vocabulary as possible knowledge, then requiring implementation evidence before promotion.

### 6. Are kinds and pressures truly orthogonal, or merely another useful characterization?

They are **partially orthogonal and currently useful**, but not proven as a fully stable coordinate system.

Evidence for orthogonality:

- one kind can satisfy multiple pressures;
- one pressure recurs across multiple kinds;
- pressure recurrence does not imply ownership;
- kind classification does not explain all movement constraints.

Evidence against full orthogonality:

- Constitution is both a kind-like authority source and the source of many pressure constraints;
- physiology is partly described using pressure language;
- behavior is often where pressure becomes visible;
- responsibility/structure sometimes already includes locality as part of its kind.

Bounded conclusion: the repository supports a two-question discipline more strongly than it supports a formal two-axis ontology.

### 7. Does either coordinate system require vocabulary stabilization?

No, not yet.

Vocabulary stabilization would be premature. The repository supports local questions and bounded characterizations, but not stable terms such as `architectural kind axis`, `architectural pressure axis`, `Continuity`, `Evidence`, `Locality`, or `Self-observation` as preserved repository vocabulary.

If future work needs these terms, it should first prove implementation-backed reachability or repeated artifact-level usefulness without collapsing into ownership recovery.

### 8. What important architectural questions remain unresolved?

Unresolved questions include:

1. Whether `Constitution / Physiology / Behavior` is repository-general or only organism-discussion-local.
2. Whether `Continuity / Evidence / Locality / Self-observation` are the right pressure names or only a convenient compression of more precise pressures.
3. Whether any implementation-visible surface should ever expose these coordinates.
4. Whether responsibility recovery should explicitly distinguish same-family ownership evidence from cross-family pressure recurrence.
5. Whether pressure recurrence can be tested without creating a new diagnostic or conceptual framework.
6. Whether current-work-position and similar presentation labels have any repository-supported knowledge status.
7. Whether pressure conflicts can be characterized without ranking or stabilizing a policy system.

## Counterexamples and collapse risks

### Counterexample: only one hierarchy exists

The Constitution already explains many movement constraints. Architectural law may simply be the Constitution applied locally. If so, no second coordinate system exists; there is only constitutional discipline constraining responsibilities.

Response: this counterexample is strong. The two-coordinate reading survives only as a characterization of how different questions recur, not as an authority beside the Constitution.

### Counterexample: pressures are sublayers

Pressures may look like missing layers: evidence layer, continuity layer, locality layer, self-observation layer.

Response: repository evidence rejects this. Pressures are not recovered as owners or universal layers. They recur across answer composition, provider translation, structure observation, inquiry lineage, participation, diagnostics, and responsibility recovery without becoming a single implementation family.

### Counterexample: kinds are merely another pressure family

Constitution, Physiology, and Behavior may be pressure names rather than kinds: continuity pressure becomes Constitution, response pressure becomes Physiology, visibility pressure becomes Behavior.

Response: possible, but incomplete. The CPB characterization asks what role a phenomenon plays in lawful fact, response tendency, or visible act. That is not identical to asking what failure it prevents. Still, the boundary is not clean enough to stabilize an ontology.

### Counterexample: examples naturally belong to only one coordinate system

Some examples can be explained by one question alone. Provider Translation might simply be locality. Answer Composition might simply be behavior. Structure Observation might simply be self-observation.

Response: these simplifications lose repository-supported distinctions. Provider Translation is local, but also has evidence/provenance obligations. Answer Composition is visible behavior/artifact composition, but also preserves support and mutation boundaries. Structure Observation observes, but must still prevent unsupported promotion.

### Counterexample: elegant compression

The four pressure names may be too elegant. They risk compressing many precise repository distinctions into a memorable square.

Response: accepted. The investigation treats the four names as candidate compression only. The repository more directly supports specific pressures: evidence before movement, visibility not authority, diagnostic output not truth, pressure not recovery, answer not mutation, attachment not participation, and frontier not implementation readiness.

## Unsupported interpretations

The repository does not support the following conclusions:

- There is now a formal `architectural coordinate system` in the repository.
- `Constitution / Physiology / Behavior` is a complete hierarchy.
- `Continuity / Evidence / Locality / Self-observation` are stable vocabulary.
- Pressures are new owners, layers, runtime mechanisms, or implementation responsibilities.
- Kinds and pressures are perfectly orthogonal.
- Every artifact must be assigned a canonical kind and pressure.
- A diagnostic, CLI, registry, or app surface should be added for this characterization.
- Presentation labels such as `Current Work Position` should become knowledge without implementation evidence.

## Bounded conclusion

The repository supports this statement with medium confidence:

```text
Recent investigations were often not missing another architectural layer.
They were encountering recurring architectural pressures
that cut across multiple kinds, responsibilities, artifacts, and surfaces.
```

The repository also supports this weaker two-question discipline:

```text
For a recovered artifact, ask both:

1. What kind of architectural phenomenon is this?
2. What architectural necessity or failure-boundary does it satisfy?
```

The repository does **not** yet support a stabilized two-axis ontology.

The best characterization is therefore:

```text
Kinds and pressures are partially independent lenses.
They explain why one pressure recurs across many artifacts
and why one artifact can satisfy multiple pressures.

But they remain investigative lenses,
not repository law,
not vocabulary stabilization,
and not implementation architecture.
```

## Confidence

**Medium confidence** that repository evidence supports the distinction between architectural-kind questions and architectural-pressure questions.

**High confidence** that recurring pressures should not be recovered as another implementation owner, layer, runtime mechanism, or universal hierarchy.

**Low-to-medium confidence** in the specific compressed pressure names `Continuity`, `Evidence`, `Locality`, and `Self-observation`, because repository evidence more often supports narrower boundary statements than those stabilized names.

**Low confidence** that `Constitution / Physiology / Behavior` is complete outside organism architecture discussions.

Believe the repository, not the coordinate system.
