# Constitutional Inquiry Capability Vocabulary Investigation

## Boundary

This is exactly one bounded Constitutional Vocabulary Investigation.

It asks only whether recurring repository evidence supports a better constitutional concept than **growth** for describing how the repository earns the ability to answer new classes of constitutional questions.

It does not recover implementation, ownership, runtime architecture, frameworks, lifecycle models, engines, or canonical vocabulary. Candidate words are treated as investigative prompts only. Repository authority wins.

## Evidence summary

### Prior growth finding

The prior inquiry capability growth investigation concluded that recurring prerequisites include bounded question identity, independently recovered evidence, evidence posture, authority boundary, non-promotion of labels or pressure, unknown preservation, confidence discipline, lawful stop, explicit non-change statements, and repository-authority priority.

It also preserved equally strong non-growth evidence: no new owner by recurrence alone, no implementation by readiness alone, no runtime architecture by answerability, no canonical vocabulary by presentation label, no artifact by projection capability, no general mechanism by investigation pattern, and no automatic conversion from possible question to running inquiry.

Its confidence was medium-high in the pattern of earning new bounded inquiry capabilities, high that the pattern does not recover implementation or ownership, and only medium in the word `growth` because the evidence supported admissibility growth more strongly than canonical growth vocabulary.

### Constitutional growth sequence evidence

The growth sequence characterization supports a recurring movement from preserved unknown, through bounded constitutional question, evidence review, recurring evidence under authority boundary, recovered distinction, characterization with negative authority and unknown preservation, recovered constitutional grammar, higher resolution, and preserved new unknown.

That document names a `constitutional growth sequence`, but it also preserves uncertainty about whether that is the final repository-preferred name and refuses methodology, roadmap, process, learning system, or engine promotion.

### Law Projection and Book of Law readiness evidence

The Law Projection readiness investigation found that the exact label `Law Projection` was not already preserved or projected implementation knowledge, while recurring evidence supported a narrower bounded, read-only inquiry capability over already recovered constitutional evidence.

The Book of Law readiness assessment similarly supported only bounded read-only projection readiness, not a Book of Law artifact, law engine, runtime manager, registry, framework, planner, scheduler, universal evaluator, owner, or canonical storage.

Both investigations repeatedly support laws or projected boundaries only where evidence, provenance, confidence, unknown preservation, non-mutation, non-ownership, and non-implementation boundaries remain visible.

### Constitutional readiness evidence

The readiness reconciliation explains that lawful beginning is not one function. It is constrained movement from Null or uncommitted status through bounded presence, evidence, warrant, local eligibility, selection/refusal where supported, and lawful stop or handoff.

Readiness is therefore supported as a decomposed condition for lawful movement, not as a global runtime activation, lifecycle, scheduler, implementation readiness, or single callable owner.

### Transition and bounded inquiry evidence

The transition family characterization says a possible question becomes eligible or executable only when it matches exact implemented surfaces, passes status/eligibility checks, satisfies required arguments, and maps to dispatch where implemented. Eligibility changes answerability/routability, not truth.

The possible-observation-to-lawful-question competency interrogation similarly rejects a universal competency. It supports family-local question boundedness and question-surface admission through exact QuestionFamily inventory, eligibility, selection, dispatch, and surface-local orientation paths. It explicitly stops before universal inquiry engines, automatic question generators, first-class Potential Question objects, recovered-family registration lifecycles, runtime schedulers, or semantic interpretation of operator prose.

### Question Family evidence

Question Shape / Question Family / Survey Warrant evidence distinguishes recurring question forms from implementation-backed public inquiry identities. Question Families are exact static inventory rows and dispatch identities with eligibility, required arguments, authority boundaries, diagnostic relationships, and tests. Question Shapes and Survey Warrant Families are adjacent investigation or methodological patterns, not equivalent concepts and not a single owner.

## Independent candidate investigations

Each candidate testifies independently before comparison.

### Candidate: Growth

**Does recurring repository evidence support this concept?**

Partially. Recurring evidence supports a bounded pattern in which the repository earns new answerability after prior recovery supplies bounded question identity, evidence, authority boundary, confidence discipline, unknown preservation, and stop conditions.

**What evidence supports it?**

- Prior inquiry capability investigation explicitly found medium-high confidence that the repository grows in a bounded sense by earning new bounded inquiry capabilities.
- Growth sequence characterization supports a movement from preserved unknown through bounded evidence discrimination to recovered constitutional grammar and preserved new unknown.
- The pattern captures that more classes of constitutional questions can become answerable over time.

**What evidence refuses it?**

- The prior investigation had only medium confidence in the word `growth` and stated that admissibility growth was better supported than canonical growth vocabulary.
- Recurring evidence refuses new owners, new implementation, new runtime architecture, canonical vocabulary, general mechanisms, or automatic question-to-inquiry conversion.
- `Growth` risks sounding like expansion, lifecycle, learning, or framework unless heavily bounded.

**What remains unknown?**

Whether future repository evidence will stabilize `growth`, replace it with a narrower term, or preserve only a descriptive phrase such as earning bounded inquiry capabilities remains unknown.

**Confidence.**

Medium. The pattern is supported; the word is only partially supported.

### Candidate: Jurisdiction

**Does recurring repository evidence support this concept?**

Weakly, and only as an analogy around local authority boundaries and admissible scope. It is not supported as the best constitutional concept for the whole pattern.

**What evidence supports it?**

- Question Families define exact public inquiry identities, authority boundaries, required arguments, eligibility, and dispatch boundaries.
- Lawful movement requires local authority boundaries and family-local gates.
- Projection readiness requires visible boundaries around what a surface may project and what it must not project.

**What evidence refuses it?**

- No reviewed investigation recovers jurisdiction as a canonical constitutional concept, owner, registry, or engine.
- Readiness and transition evidence emphasize decomposed movement, eligibility, warrant, and stop conditions rather than a court-like jurisdiction model.
- The narrative-neighborhood evidence treats courtroom vocabulary as a metaphorical neighborhood when supported, not as repository architecture.

**What remains unknown?**

Whether future evidence will use jurisdiction-like language for a specific local boundary remains unknown. Current evidence does not support promoting it.

**Confidence.**

Low.

**Confidence.**

### Candidate: Admissibility

**Does recurring repository evidence support this concept?**

Yes. Recurring evidence strongly supports admissibility as the narrowest better explanation for how new classes of constitutional questions become answerable: they become admissible to bounded inquiry only after evidence, authority, eligibility, confidence, unknown preservation, and stop conditions are satisfied.

**What evidence supports it?**

- Prior inquiry capability growth investigation explicitly says repository evidence supports admissibility growth more strongly than canonical growth vocabulary.
- Transition evidence says possible inquiry does not become running inquiry automatically; it must pass exact family lookup, status/eligibility checks, required-argument checks, and dispatch mapping where implemented.
- Readiness evidence decomposes lawful beginning into bounded presence, evidence, warrant, local eligibility, selection/refusal where supported, and lawful stop or handoff.
- Question Family evidence supports exact registered public inquiry identity, bounded eligibility, required arguments, diagnostic-only refusal, and unknown-family rejection rather than inference.
- Law Projection and Book of Law readiness both ask whether a projected answer is permitted over already recovered evidence while refusing artifact, owner, implementation, and runtime promotion.

**What evidence refuses it?**

- Admissibility is not truth, proof, projection, implementation, ownership, or capability execution by itself.
- Not all reviewed reports use the exact word as canonical vocabulary.
- Admissibility may be too narrow if treated as only courtroom procedure; repository evidence supports family-local evidence gates and eligibility boundaries, not a universal court.

**What remains unknown?**

Whether the final repository-preferred name should be `admissibility`, `inquiry admissibility`, or a non-canonical descriptive phrase remains unknown.

**Confidence.**

Medium-high. Strongest among reviewed candidates, with vocabulary caution.

## Comparison

Comparison begins only after the independent candidate investigations above.

### Strongest supported concept

**Admissibility** is the strongest supported concept, with vocabulary caution.

It better explains the recurring pattern than unqualified `growth` because the evidence does not show expansion by owner, implementation, architecture, framework, or runtime capability. It shows that a new class of constitutional question becomes answerable only after it is admitted through bounded question identity, recovered evidence, authority boundary, local eligibility, confidence discipline, unknown preservation, and stop/refusal conditions.

The safest phrasing is therefore not a new framework name. It is:

```text
inquiry admissibility
```

or, if avoiding any canonical promotion:

```text
admissibility to bounded inquiry
```

This is a descriptive conclusion, not a recovered canonical vocabulary mandate.

### Unsupported concepts

Unsupported as the best explanation:

- **Jurisdiction** — too court-like and not recovered as repository vocabulary, owner, registry, or engine.
- **Frontier** — marks unresolved pressure or candidate future work, not earned answerability.
- **Responsibility** — risks recovering ownership and overstates the evidence.

Unsupported as frameworks, engines, or architecture:

- jurisdiction engine;
- capability engine;
- authority framework;
- lifecycle model;
- runtime architecture;
- constitutional growth framework.

### Partially supported concepts

- **Growth** — supported only in a bounded descriptive sense, but less precise than admissibility and vulnerable to over-expansion.
- **Authority** — necessary constraint, not the full explanation.
- **Competence** — captures family-local ability but is not stabilized as a constitutional competency.
- **Capability** — names the earned result more than the admission mechanism.
- **Readiness** — describes precondition posture, not the complete movement into answerability.
- **None of the above** — true only if the standard is canonical stabilized vocabulary; false if the standard is best-supported explanatory concept.

### Preserved unknowns

- Whether `admissibility`, `inquiry admissibility`, or `admissibility to bounded inquiry` will stabilize as repository vocabulary remains unknown.
- Whether future Question Shapes will become registered Question Families remains unknown.
- Whether readiness-to-inquiry admissibility will eventually need implementation remains unknown and unsupported here.
- Whether future repository evidence will support a different concept remains unknown.
- Whether `growth` remains acceptable as a bounded informal description remains unresolved but secondary.

### Confidence

- **Medium-high** confidence that admissibility best explains the recurring evidence.
- **High** confidence that the evidence does not support implementation, ownership, runtime architecture, frameworks, engines, lifecycle models, or canonical vocabulary promotion.
- **Medium** confidence in using `admissibility` as vocabulary, because the concept is strongly supported but exact canonical naming remains unsettled.
