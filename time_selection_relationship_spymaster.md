This is exactly one bounded Spymaster investigation. It interrogates already recovered Time and Selection topologies together to determine whether recurring repository evidence supports independence, constitutional interaction, partial overlap, or a different topology.

It does not recover implementation, runtime behavior, scheduling, dispatch, ownership, implementation recommendations, implementation slices, a Town Clock, a global selector, or a promoted architecture from the orientation. Repository authority wins.

### Supported independence

- Time owns temporal meaning boundaries; Selection owns lawful local choosing/classification boundaries.
- Time can exist as provenance/support/claim separation without selecting any lawful movement.
- Selection can classify eligibility, select a Question Family, or refuse a candidate without resolving any temporal topology beyond whatever evidence is locally relevant.
- Time-related operational visibility does not become Selection authority.
- Selection-related eligibility does not become Time authority.
- Both dimensions are governed by shared constitutional warrant, but shared warrant is not identity.

### Supported interactions

1. **Temporal evidence may participate in eligibility.** If a selection claim depends on freshness, observation date, source sample date, preservation date, knowledge timing, or a temporal claim, Time supplies bounded evidence/provenance that Selection must not collapse.
2. **Selection may bound a Time inquiry.** A Time-related subject can be selected for investigation or refused if unsupported, under the same local selection boundaries as other subjects.
3. **Readiness language overlaps.** Time readiness and Selection eligibility both ask whether movement is lawful now for a bounded subject, but Time readiness is about Time-related movement under temporal non-collapse, while Selection eligibility is about lawful local choosing under evidence/authority gates.
4. **Stop conditions align.** Insufficient temporal evidence can force Selection to stop or refuse; unsupported selection pressure can leave Time questions preserved as unknown rather than selected for movement.

### Investigation C result

Recurring repository evidence supports Time and Selection as independent constitutional dimensions that can interact through shared warrant, current-lawful-condition discipline, and evidence/provenance dependencies. Time does not govern Selection globally. Selection does not subsume Time. Their strongest relationship is local and conditional: temporal evidence can constrain or support selection eligibility when relevant, and selection can bound a Time-related inquiry when lawful, but neither dimension collapses into the other.

### Should the sequence be refined?

Yes. Repository evidence supports replacing the orientation with multiple local progressions rather than one relationship between `Global Time` and a universal selection chain.

A more faithful refinement is:

```text
temporal evidence / temporal provenance / temporal claim
↓
local evidence-strength and authority-boundary assessment
↓
supported use, insufficient support, unknown, or stop
```

and, independently:

```text
candidate or exact bounded surface
↓
local eligibility / authority / evidence assessment
↓
unsupported, deferred, open, stopped, or narrowly selected
```

with a conditional bridge:

```text
when the selection claim depends on temporal support,
Time evidence participates as bounded evidence,
not as selector, scheduler, registration authority, or global clock.
```

### Should multiple local progressions exist instead?

Yes. The reviewed evidence better supports multiple local progressions:

1. **Temporal non-collapse progression** — temporal support is identified, authority is bounded, and a temporal claim is supported, revised, unknown, or refused.
2. **Selection eligibility progression** — candidate or exact bounded surface is checked for evidence/authority/eligibility, then selected locally or stopped/refused.
3. **QuestionFamily public-answerability progression** — explicit static QuestionFamily row creates known public answerable identity, with bounded ask status and required-argument boundaries.
4. **Constitutional lawful movement progression** — bounded subject plus current lawful condition plus warrant permits supported movement, continued unknown/Null, or stop.

### Should the orientation be rejected?

The orientation should be rejected as architecture. It remains useful only as a falsified investigative aid.

- `Global Time` is too strong: the repository supports multiple Time neighborhoods and higher-order recurrence, not a global Time owner, topology, or clock.
- `Observation opportunity` is too weak and too ambiguous: opportunity is not observation, evidence, eligibility, selection, or authority.
- `Candidate → Eligible → Selected → Registered` is too linear: candidate-to-eligible and eligibility-constrained selection recur, but selected-to-registered is narrow and not universal.
- The two diagrams are not completely unrelated: temporal evidence can participate in eligibility or selection when the claim depends on time.
- The two diagrams are not one architecture: Time and Selection preserve different constitutional responsibilities.

## Competing hypothesis comparison

### Hypothesis A

```text
Time governs when constitutional re-evaluation may occur.
Selection governs what lawful movement may occur afterward.
```

**Falsification attempt:** Time reports reject scheduling, cadence authority, runtime behavior, and Town Clock recovery. Selection reports reject universal progression. Therefore Time cannot be said to govern `when re-evaluation may occur` as a global responsibility.

**Supported residue:** Time can supply temporal evidence relevant to whether movement is currently warranted. Selection governs local lawful choosing/classification once evidence, eligibility, and authority boundaries are satisfied.

**Disposition:** Partially supported only after weakening. The repository supports `Time may contribute bounded temporal evidence to current-lawful-condition assessment`; it does not support `Time governs when` globally.

**Confidence:** Medium.

### Hypothesis B

```text
Time is itself part of the constitutional selection process.
```

**Falsification attempt:** Selection has distinct candidate, eligibility, selector, and registration boundaries that are not Time boundaries. Time has distinct temporal authority/provenance/support boundaries that are not selection statuses.

**Supported residue:** Temporal evidence may be part of a selection process when temporal support is relevant to the candidate's eligibility or selection factors.

**Disposition:** Mostly unsupported as stated. Time can participate as evidence inside local selection, but Time is not itself the constitutional selection process.

**Confidence:** High.

### Hypothesis C

```text
Selection implicitly carries its own notion of time, making a separate Time responsibility unnecessary.
```

**Falsification attempt:** Time evidence independently preserves occurrence, observation, source assertion, knowledge, preservation, temporal claim, operational elapsed timing, progress cadence, and cache visibility. Selection evidence does not own these distinctions and cannot safely collapse them into eligibility or selected status.

**Supported residue:** Selection often operates under present-tense current-lawful-condition language and may ask whether a candidate is eligible now.

**Disposition:** Unsupported as stated. Selection can require present eligibility, but that does not eliminate separate Time responsibilities for temporal non-collapse.

**Confidence:** High.

### Different topology better supported

The best-supported topology is neither global Time over Selection nor Selection subsuming Time. It is:

```text
Time:
  temporal meaning / provenance / support non-collapse

Selection:
  candidate / eligibility / local selector / refusal or narrow registration boundary

Shared constitutional constraint:
  evidence-bound warrant + current lawful condition + authority boundary + unknown preservation

Conditional interaction:
  temporal evidence participates in selection only when the selection claim depends on temporal support;
  selection bounds Time-related inquiry only when local eligibility and authority are satisfied.
```

**Disposition:** Best supported.

**Confidence:** Medium-high.
