## Comparative bridge survey

According to recurring repository evidence, what lawfully crosses between these neighboring concepts?

Lawfully crossing artifacts are bounded and evidence-limited:

- Between Question Shape and Survey Warrant Family, the bounded question form crosses as one warrant-support criterion, along with evidence posture, investigation shape, produced artifact pattern, unsupported conclusions, and confidence.
- Between Survey Warrant Family and Question Family, implementation-backed Question Family row evidence can cross into a Survey as reviewed evidence, while survey discipline crosses back as methodological constraint. No registration, dispatch, ownership, or authority grant crosses.
- Between Question Shape and Question Family, exact registered rows can expose shape-like public question identities through names, examples, statuses, authority boundaries, required arguments, and implementation reasons. Unregistered question shapes do not cross into Question Families without explicit repository evidence.

The recurring shared exchange is therefore not implementation, topology, ownership, registration, runtime dispatch, or vocabulary stabilization. The recurring lawful exchange is:

```text
bounded question form
+ evidence posture
+ artifact/report pattern
+ exact registered row evidence where present
+ authority and boundary statements
+ unsupported-conclusion preservation
+ confidence limits
```

Question Shapes contribute observable bounded forms. Survey Warrant Families contribute methodological authorization and conclusion-strength limits. Question Families contribute exact implementation-backed public inquiry identity where a row exists. Each bridge preserves non-exchanges: shapes are not warrants, warrants are not families, and families are not arbitrary shapes.

Survey complete.
