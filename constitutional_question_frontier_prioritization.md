## Prioritization methodology

A candidate question was admitted only when recurring repository evidence suggests that answering it would likely do at least one of the following:

- recover previously unseen constitutional anatomy;
- clarify recurring constitutional pressure;
- resolve recurring preserved Unknowns;
- improve constitutional locality;
- improve lawful inquiry;
- strengthen bounded lawful reliance;
- expose implementation recovery opportunities without implementing them.

A candidate was excluded when it was merely philosophically interesting, novel, broad, speculative, implementation convenient, planner-shaped, roadmap-shaped, or unsupported by recurring repository evidence.

Admitted questions are not ranked as a roadmap or schedule. Their order reflects expected constitutional recovery density under current repository evidence.

## Excluded candidate questions

### Excluded: What is the universal constitutional grammar?

**Reason excluded:** Grammar recovery artifacts and compatibility audits support bounded grammar as a recovery discipline and local discrimination tool, not a universal language, ontology, or framework. This question is too broad and would invite unsupported promotion.

### Excluded: What should Seed implement next?

**Reason excluded:** Frontier is not implementation readiness, answer is not mutation, and inquiry orientation is not planning. The present task forbids roadmap and planning behavior. Repository evidence supports stop, frontier preservation, and bounded investigations, not implementation selection.

### Excluded: Can Seed generate the highest-value questions automatically?

**Reason excluded:** The inquiry-generation frontier survey rejects autonomous question generation, curiosity, planning, natural-language routing, and automatic next-question engines. This prioritization is a bounded investigation artifact, not an implemented generator.

### Excluded: Is authority the fundamental constitutional primitive?

**Reason excluded:** Authority is recovered as boundary work around movement, reliance, promotion, and stop, but the authority characterization explicitly rejects authority as the whole of constitutional grammar or a universal framework. The stronger primitive question is speculative.

### Excluded: Is identity a constitutional ontology?

**Reason excluded:** Identity frontier evidence supports identity-pressure as a boundary-preserving attachment condition, not as ontology, universal property, type system, subsystem, or fundamental grammar.

### Excluded: What is the complete taxonomy of all constitutional participants?

**Reason excluded:** Current grammar improves resolution and recovers local distinctions, but repository evidence repeatedly rejects universal ownership, universal representation, and complete vocabulary. A complete taxonomy would exceed current authority.

### Excluded: How should a repository-neutral review engine rank defects?

**Reason excluded:** Repository-neutral review artifacts support human-scoped methodology and bounded evidence packets, not autonomous review, defect ranking, program-purpose inference, or implementation recommendations.

### Excluded: Should diagnostic findings become cluster facts when repeated?

**Reason excluded:** The Constitution and operational visibility contract explicitly reject diagnostic output becoming cluster truth. Repetition does not grant promotion authority.

### Excluded: Are all Unknowns one lifecycle state?

**Reason excluded:** Unknown Currency Survey supports local Unknown artifacts but explicitly preserves uncertainty about lifecycle, ownership, topology, runtime, exchange, and equivalence. A narrower relationship question is admitted above; the lifecycle-state framing is currently unsupported.
