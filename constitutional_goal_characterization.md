# Constitutional Goal Characterization

## Investigation scope

This is exactly one bounded Constitutional Characterization.

Question answered:

```text
Across recurring constitutional recoveries,
what constitutional work, if any,
does a goal consistently perform?
```

The word `goal` was treated only as investigation pressure. This characterization does not assume that goals are constitutional participants, owners, execution authorities, plans, priorities, schedules, or runtime objects. Repository authority wins.

Reviewed recurring constitutional evidence concerning the Constitution, bounded lawful reliance, constitutional authority, inquiry orientation, bounded inquiry admissibility, implementation recovery methodology, responsibility recovery, producer -> artifact -> consumer handoffs, constitutional translation, constitutional compatibility audits, pressure investigations, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop. Implementation evidence was used only where it reinforces recurring constitutional behavior.

## Recurring constitutional evidence

### 1. Repository authority does not allow goals to move Null by appearing

The Constitution's central discipline begins at `Null / unknown / uncommitted` and moves only through observation, bounded unknown, inquiry, evidence, supported transition or explicit stop, and bounded handoff artifact. It states that a subject remains Unknown / uncommitted until observable evidence justifies movement, and that a note, provider response, report, audit row, code corridor, compatibility residue, or operator concern may orient work but does not become repository truth by appearing.

This directly constrains goal-shaped prose such as `Fix Seed`, `Fix diagnostics`, `Fix this district`, or `Fix this helper`. Such prose may be observable pressure, but it does not itself create repository truth, ownership, priority, implementation authority, or course selection.

### 2. Pressure may orient inquiry, but it does not command work

The Constitution explicitly recovers the boundary:

```text
Pressure informs inquiry; it does not command it.
```

Pressure identifies what deserves attention, but does not automatically select work, authorize implementation, create purpose, recover ownership, or promote a frontier. A pressure row, pressure report, operator concern, or residual pressure item remains orientation evidence until repository evidence supports a bounded inquiry or implementation change.

A goal-like phrase behaves constitutionally like pressure when it exposes an intended target or desired outcome. Its consistent work is therefore not goal authority. Its lawful contribution is to orient possible inquiry while remaining subordinate to evidence, explicit authority, boundedness, and stop discipline.

### 3. Inquiry is bounded work, not global purpose execution

Recurring inquiry discipline answers one bounded question and preserves observation, evidence, classification/conclusion, authority, and stop condition. The repository does not authorize inquiry from global purpose, planner intent, architectural preference, or desire to complete a framework. Inquiry begins when an observation creates a bounded unknown.

A goal can sometimes help expose the candidate bounded unknown: for example, `Fix diagnostics` can raise pressure around a diagnostic surface, success condition, scope, ambiguity, or missing evidence. But the constitutional participant is the bounded inquiry, not the goal. If the goal phrase is too broad, the lawful result may be clarification, narrowed admissibility, Unknown preservation, or stop.

### 4. Authority must be explicit

The Constitution requires every surface to know what it may and may not do. It preserves distinctions such as visibility is not authority, observation is not fact, pressure is not recovery, frontier is not implementation readiness, and answer is not mutation.

This means a goal does not consistently perform authority-bearing work. It does not authorize execution, mutation, implementation, planning, scheduling, prioritization, autonomous decomposition, or mission behavior. At most, it can be evidence of operator pressure whose authority remains to be established by repository-visible evidence and explicit bounded surfaces.

### 5. Stop is a positive result

The repository treats insufficient implementation evidence, unsupported conclusion, unknown target, adjacent-family pressure, methodology-only, frontier-only, and not-implementation-backed outcomes as valid stops. A goal phrase cannot force continuation through those stops. If the evidence does not support a bounded target, owner, admissible interpretation, or success condition, the recurring lawful behavior is to preserve Unknown or stop.

### 6. Frontiers preserve pressure without creating roadmaps

Frontiers preserve important pressure that is not yet implementation-recoverable. They do not create planners, priorities, schedules, runtime behavior, or implementation mandates. Goal-like pressure may therefore be preserved as frontier pressure when recovery cannot yet relieve it, but preservation is not course selection.

### 7. Bounded handoff artifacts, not private intention, cross boundaries

Recurring constitutional handoff discipline says competencies exchange bounded artifacts, not shared internal state or private reasoning. Handoffs should preserve observation, evidence, conclusion, authority, and stop condition. A goal can appear in a handoff only as bounded pressure, subject identity, requested outcome, ambiguity, or limitation where supported. It does not carry hidden intent authority across the boundary.

### 8. Constitutional translation preserves warrant under boundary, not assumed meaning

Faithful constitutional translation does not preserve grammar, representation, meaning, or organization. It preserves bounded warranted content under provenance, role, authority limit, negative authority, preserved unknowns, locality/dimensions where supported, and confidence/evidence-strength calibration where supported.

Therefore, the repository does not authorize translating a goal phrase into assumed intent, plan, priority, or execution path. Translation may preserve the warranted content of the phrase under boundary: the raw pressure, candidate target, supported scope, negative authority, unresolved ambiguity, and confidence limits.

## Recurring implementation evidence where applicable

### 1. Inquiry orientation rejects goal promotion

`seed_runtime/inquiry_orientation.py` is the strongest direct implementation evidence. It defines inquiry orientation as a minimized read-only probe. Rendering reads projected state but never mutates it, appends events, calls providers, executes tools, or creates facts, goals, tool needs, decisions, proposals, or plans.

Its authority boundary states that preserved operator prose is not a fact, claim, goal, tool need, requirement, capability, decision, proposal, plan, authorization, command, or runtime instruction. Lexical matches do not assert importance, ownership, intent, concern, recommended action, or next safe move.

This implementation evidence supports a narrow constitutional boundary: operator prose may be preserved and may orient deterministic related-material inspection, but it is not promoted into a goal object or execution authority.

### 2. Bounded ask dispatch uses explicit registered question families, not goal inference

`question_bounded_work_invocation_investigation.md` reports that known question families are statically registered and that bounded work eligibility derives from explicit maps. Free text does not infer question-family identity at runtime. Dispatch maps exact question-family names to existing surface flags and executes existing direct surfaces; it does not create a generic work unit.

This supports a distinction between goal-shaped input and bounded work invocation. The implementation accepts only explicit, registered, bounded question surfaces for dispatch. Goal prose alone does not select work.

### 3. Pressure audit separates observed evidence from pressure presentation

`pressure_evidence_characterization.md` reports that pressure evidence begins with concrete subjects and observable repository material and stops before classifying material as architectural pressure, assigning pressure status, choosing recovery, or changing implementation. Pressure audit rows carry evidence, score, reason, and recommended command separately, and the audit is read-only.

This supports treating goal-shaped language as possible pressure evidence only. Even when pressure is visible, it recommends inspection and preserves evidence boundaries; it does not create goals or select courses.

### 4. App-visible surfaces remain bounded visibility surfaces

The app was used to inspect existing visibility surfaces:

```bash
python scripts/seed_local.py --question-surface-inventory --json
python scripts/seed_local.py --pressure-audit --json
```

The question-surface inventory showed registered question families with authority boundaries such as read-only summaries, no recording, no event-ledger writes, and no cluster mutation. The pressure audit showed pressure categories with evidence, reasons, scores, and recommended inspection commands. These outputs reinforce the recurring boundary: visibility and pressure can orient inspection, but they are not goal authority or execution selection.

## Recovered constitutional role, if any

Recurring evidence does **not** support goals as constitutional participants.

The smaller recovered role is:

```text
A goal-shaped expression may serve as bounded investigation pressure
that helps expose a candidate target, desired outcome, success/completion
condition, ambiguity, authority boundary, or clarification need.

It performs constitutional work only when preserved under repository
authority as pressure/orientation evidence for bounded inquiry.

It does not create goals as repository truth, constitutional authority,
priority, course selection, execution ownership, plan, schedule,
or implementation mandate.
```

The consistent constitutional work is therefore **pressure-to-boundedness exposure under authority boundary**, not goal authority.

A goal can lawfully contribute to:

- identifying the operator-visible pressure phrase;
- surfacing a possible subject or target;
- exposing desired outcome language that may need translation into a bounded question;
- revealing missing scope, success condition, completion condition, or admissible interpretations;
- requiring clarification where target or authority is ambiguous;
- preserving Unknown where evidence does not support narrowing;
- supporting lawful stop when bounded inquiry, authority, or evidence is insufficient.

## Unsupported candidate ideas

The reviewed evidence does not support these candidate conclusions:

1. Goals are constitutional participants.
2. Goals own execution.
3. Goals create or select priorities.
4. Goals authorize planning, scheduling, implementation, mutation, or autonomous decomposition.
5. Goals are repository truth because operator prose contains desired-outcome language.
6. A goal phrase supplies its own success condition without bounded evidence.
7. A goal phrase resolves its own scope.
8. A goal phrase carries hidden operator intent across handoffs.
9. A goal phrase can bypass Null, Unknown preservation, bounded inquiry, explicit authority, compatibility preservation, or lawful stop.
10. `Fix Seed`, `Fix diagnostics`, `Fix this district`, or `Fix this helper` are sufficient constitutional commands.

## Preserved Unknowns

The following remain Unknown because recurring repository evidence does not decide them:

- Whether a future implementation-backed surface will introduce an explicit goal artifact.
- Whether any narrow family uses `goal` as implementation vocabulary with a different local meaning.
- Whether some future bounded inquiry will recover a stable success-condition artifact adjacent to goal-shaped pressure.
- Whether goal-shaped pressure should be split into smaller recurring artifacts such as target, scope, intended outcome, or completion condition.
- Whether clarification behavior around goal-shaped prose has a recoverable implementation owner beyond existing bounded ask, inquiry orientation, and pressure visibility surfaces.

## Confidence

Medium-high confidence for the negative boundary: recurring constitutional and implementation evidence rejects promotion from operator prose, pressure, visibility, frontier, or presentation vocabulary into authority, priority, planning, or implementation mandate.

Medium confidence for the smaller recovered role: goal-shaped expressions can expose pressure toward boundedness, including target, outcome, success/completion condition, ambiguity, clarification need, Unknown preservation, or lawful stop. This is supported by recurring inquiry, pressure, translation, handoff, and stop discipline, but the repository does not yet provide a first-class `goal` implementation family.

Low confidence for any stronger role, ownership model, lifecycle, execution semantics, scheduling semantics, or autonomous behavior.
