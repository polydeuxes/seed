## Reconciliation

### Recurring constitutional relationships

Constitutional kinds and constitutional question-family recurrence are distinct but mutually intelligible under bounded inquiry discipline.

A constitutional kind is recovered by the kind of answer recurring evidence can lawfully support. A constitutional question form is recovered by the recurring kind of question that can lawfully be asked of evidence without exceeding scope, authority, or confidence. The recurrence of “Purpose answers...”, “Responsibility answers...”, and “Mechanism answers...” is therefore not accidental: each recovered kind is visible as an inquiry surface because bounded inquiry tests what questions the repository can answer and what questions it must refuse.

The relationship is not identity. The answer-kind is not the question-family. The question-family or question-shape does not create the kind. Rather, recurring question forms expose and discipline the recovery of constitutional kinds, while recovered constitutional kinds constrain which answers are lawful and which adjacent questions must be refused.
