# Candidate Movement to Lawful Movement Transition Investigation

Repository authority wins.

## Boundary

This is exactly one bounded Candidate Movement to Lawful Movement Transition Investigation.

It answers only whether recurring repository evidence supports an independently recoverable constitutional transition between candidate movement and lawful movement, and if so what recurring constitutional conditions govern that transition.

It does not recover implementation, constitutional grammar, execution architecture, canonical movement, canonical lawfulness, or a movement framework. The phrases `candidate movement`, `lawful movement`, and `transition` are used only at this investigation resolution.

## Core finding

Recurring repository evidence supports a constitutional transition between candidate movement and lawful movement, but only weakly as a cross-family pattern and not as a canonical law of movement.

The transition is not that movement becomes lawful because it is named, repeated, useful, presented, structurally visible, or desired. The recurring pattern is smaller:

```text
A candidate movement becomes lawful only when
repository evidence is sufficient for a bounded subject,
through a competent local authority boundary,
with provenance/support preserved,
negative authority and refusal conditions respected,
unknowns preserved,
confidence limited,
and a lawful stop available.
```

The evidence supports transition conditions more strongly than it supports any universal movement identity. Lawfulness appears to belong to the evidence-bound relation among subject, authority, provenance, boundary, reliance, unknowns, and stop conditions, not to movement as an isolated thing.

## Independently inspected lawful movements

### Lawful movement as bounded acquisition

Observation evidence supports lawful movement from source report to attributable observation when source, scope, vantage, provenance, confidence limits, and receiving authority are preserved. This lawful movement remains acquisition, not truth or verification.

### Lawful movement as bounded reliance

Warrant and reliance evidence support lawful downstream use when repository evidence entitles a bounded consumer to rely on content in a specific role while preserving authority boundary, negative authority, unknowns, and confidence limits.

### Lawful movement as family-local promotion

Promotion authority evidence supports lawful promotion only where family-local evidence satisfies that family's boundary and stop conditions. No universal promoter or automatic promotion path is recovered.

### Lawful movement as bounded investigation

Investigation grammar evidence supports lawful investigation movement when a bounded question, evidence scope, negative authority, unknown preservation, confidence, and lawful stop govern the artifact.

### Lawful movement as provenance-preserving transition

Lawful transition evidence supports movement across representation and presentation boundaries only when provenance, evidence/support, role, confidence, contradictions, unknowns, and surface locality survive.

## Recurring transition conditions

Across the independent inspections, the recurring conditions are:

1. **Bounded subject.** The movement must concern a bounded claim, inquiry, source report, artifact, representation, responsibility, or transition.
2. **Competent local authority.** The boundary that authorizes the movement must be local to the family or surface and competent to interpret the evidence for that movement.
3. **Repository evidence.** Structural recurrence, useful wording, pressure, candidate presence, presentation, or operator concern is insufficient without repository evidence suitable to the proposed movement.
4. **Provenance and support preservation.** Source, support, role, derivation, timing distinctions, and evidence identity must not be stripped during movement.
5. **Authority boundary and negative authority.** The movement must preserve what it may not do, including non-promotion, non-mutation, non-truth-transfer, non-universalization, and refusal of adjacent work.
6. **Admissibility or eligibility where needed.** Candidate material becomes admissible, verified, earned, or relied upon only where the family-local boundary accepts it under its rules.
7. **Locality.** Lawful movement remains local to its source, family, representation, view, diagnostic, investigation, or responsibility boundary unless separate evidence supports crossing.
8. **Unknown preservation.** Unresolved conditions remain unknown rather than becoming inferred facts, truth, resolved claims, or silent permissions.
9. **Confidence limit.** Movement carries only the confidence or evidence strength repository evidence supports.
10. **Lawful stop.** Insufficient evidence, absent authority, lost provenance, unsupported subject identity, contradiction, or overreach must stop movement rather than force promotion.

## Recurring refusal conditions

Recurring repository evidence refuses movement from candidate to lawful when:

- the subject is unbounded;
- evidence is absent, insufficient, malformed, or outside the competent boundary;
- candidate presence is treated as admission;
- observation is treated as truth, fact, verification, history, projection, or host truth;
- diagnostic or presentation output is treated as cluster truth or repository knowledge;
- provider vocabulary, repeated prose, section labels, or attractive terms are treated as constitutional law;
- provenance, support, role, source, confidence, contradiction, or unknowns would be dropped;
- local responsibility is treated as universal authority;
- promotion would exceed the family-local boundary;
- a consumer would rely beyond warrant;
- stopping conditions are bypassed.

## Rejected orientation hypotheses

The investigation attempted to falsify the orientation statement. Repository evidence rejects or weakens the following hypotheses:

1. **Lawfulness belongs to movement itself.** Rejected. Lawfulness recurs as a relation among bounded subject, evidence, authority, provenance, role, unknowns, confidence, and stop conditions.
2. **Candidate movement always becomes lawful when enough recurring terms appear.** Rejected. Repetition and structural recurrence can make something visible, but the app and prior investigations explicitly refuse prose interpretation and authority inference from structure alone.
3. **Competent authority alone is sufficient.** Rejected. Authority must be evidence-bound and local; authority without evidence/support/provenance does not permit promotion or reliance.
4. **Evidence alone is sufficient.** Rejected. Evidence authorizes movement only through the competent boundary that can interpret it for that family.
5. **Bounded subject plus evidence always produces promotion.** Rejected. Negative authority, admissibility, confidence, unknowns, contradictions, and stop conditions may preserve candidate status.
6. **Unknowns are defects that must be resolved before lawful movement.** Rejected. Repository evidence repeatedly treats preserved unknowns as part of lawful movement and lawful stop.
7. **Produced artifact proves movement lawfulness.** Rejected. Movement and artifact emission are constitutionally distinct at investigation resolution; artifacts may record, expose, or result from movement without being identical to lawfulness.
8. **A single canonical transition has been recovered.** Rejected. The recurring conditions are supported, but canonical transition identity, complete ordering, and universal lawfulness are not recovered.

## Preserved unknowns

- Whether future repository evidence will split candidate-to-lawful transitions into narrower constitutional families remains unknown.
- Whether admissibility deserves a separate recovered artifact at this exact resolution remains unknown because this investigation relied only on admissibility evidence already summarized by reviewed reports.
- Whether `candidate movement` and `lawful movement` should become stable repository vocabulary remains unknown and unsupported here.
- Whether all lawful movements require every listed condition explicitly remains unknown; evidence supports recurring conditions, not a universal checklist.
- Whether some movements can be lawful with intentionally different provenance, record scope, or mutation boundaries remains family-local and unresolved here.

## Confidence

High confidence that candidate status is constitutionally distinct from lawful status across observation, warrant, reliance, promotion, transition, local responsibility, and investigation evidence.

High confidence that movement from candidate to lawful is evidence-gated, boundary-local, provenance-preserving, negative-authority-constrained, unknown-preserving, confidence-limited, and stoppable.

Medium confidence that this should be called a recurring constitutional transition, because the repository supports the pattern but has not recovered canonical movement law or stable transition vocabulary.

Low confidence in any stronger claim that a single universal candidate-to-lawful transition, sequence, grammar, or taxonomy has been recovered.
