# Competency Interrogation Grammar Investigation

Repository authority wins. This investigation treats the proposed sequence `Identity -> Inputs -> Outputs -> Negative Authority` as an investigation subject, not as an assumption, implementation plan, or framework.

## Bounded question

Has the repository already converged on a recurring competency interrogation grammar?

## Evidence reviewed

Representative evidence reviewed:

- `competency_interrogation_001.md` through `competency_interrogation_008.md`.
- `competency_interrogation_methodology_reconciliation.md`.
- `inquiry_competency_district_investigation.md`.

- `inquiry_completion_evaluation_audit.md`.
- App visibility command: `python scripts/seed_local.py --diagnostic-inventory --json`.

The review did not privilege the proposed four-part sequence. It looked for recurring questions that appear independently across competency interrogations, district surveys, neighborhood surveys, bridge investigations, candidate characterizations, and completion/evaluation audits.

## Smallest truthful answer

Yes, but not as the proposed four-step sequence.

Repository evidence supports a recurring interrogation grammar as a review discipline: name the subject, bind claims to evidence, determine what the subject may observe or require, determine what it may produce or hand off, identify granted authority and apparent-but-ungranted authority, locate boundaries and locality, preserve unknowns and stop conditions, state lawful reliance, and calibrate confidence.

Repository evidence does **not** support implementing an interrogation engine, introducing a competency framework, or requiring every investigation to traverse the exact ordered sequence `Identity -> Inputs -> Outputs -> Negative Authority`.

## Recurring interrogations

### 1. Subject identity

#### Identity

This interrogation answers: What is the investigated thing, and what is its smallest repository-supported identity?

#### Recurrence

- Competency interrogations repeatedly use an `Identity` section to separate worker, competency, role, bounded responsibility, incapability, and forbidden expansion.
- The methodology reconciliation classifies `Identity` as earned because it repeatedly separated surface names, workers, competencies, roles, responsibilities, incapabilities, and refusals.
- District and survey artifacts independently use identity questions to characterize recurring competencies or neighborhoods before describing evidence, boundaries, maturity, or confidence.
- Candidate and bridge investigations similarly begin by selecting or characterizing the bounded subject before making supported and unsupported conclusions.

#### Constitutional role

Subject identity prevents treating a public name, presentation label, CLI flag, candidate phrase, or investigation title as an owner or authority. It forces the investigation to recover the smallest repository-supported subject.

#### Ordering

Implementation and repository evidence suggest subject identity naturally appears early, usually before boundaries, reliance, or confidence. This is recurring in the competency interrogation files and in survey formats. The evidence does not prove that identity must always be literally first; several documents begin with scope, executive answer, or selected question before identity-like characterization.

#### Requiredness

Always recurring for competency interrogations; frequently recurring across related investigations.

Confidence: high.

### 2. Evidence ledger / implementation evidence reviewed

#### Identity

This interrogation answers: What repository evidence permits any claim in this investigation?

#### Recurrence

- Competency interrogations repeatedly include `Implementation evidence reviewed` before the interrogation matrix or substantive conclusions.
- The methodology reconciliation classifies this question as earned because it binds claims to files, tests, app commands, diagnostics, or adjacent reports.
- Bridge, candidate, and completion/evaluation audits independently include implementation evidence reviewed before boundary analysis, supported conclusions, unsupported conclusions, or direct answers.
- District and survey artifacts provide evidence subsections for each recurring competency or visible neighborhood.

#### Constitutional role

The evidence ledger prevents philosophizing from vocabulary alone. It makes repository authority concrete by requiring claims to cite implementation, tests, diagnostics, app behavior, or prior repository reports.

#### Ordering

Evidence ledger naturally precedes supported conclusions. It often follows subject selection or scope. Repository evidence supports this as an early grounding move, but not as a strict universal first step.

#### Requiredness

Always recurring in the competency interrogation methodology; frequently recurring across district, bridge, candidate, and completion artifacts.

Confidence: high.

### 3. Inputs / preconditions / eligibility

#### Identity

This interrogation answers: What must already be present before the subject can answer lawfully, and what input evidence can it accept?

#### Recurrence

- Later competency interrogations include `Preconditions` as a recurring section and `Evidence` sections that distinguish observable, preservable, absent, movement-permitting, and stop-causing evidence.
- The methodology reconciliation classifies `Preconditions` as working methodology because it repeatedly distinguished projected state, notes, candidates, inventory facts, selected documents, or diagnostic inputs required before a surface can answer.
- The inquiry competency district investigation independently recovers eligibility and resolution before dispatch, including exact question-family admission, explicit arguments, and unknown-family refusal.
- The typed unknown neighborhood survey independently uses `Inputs` for unresolved investigation conditions and observed evidence gaps.
- Input interpretation candidate characterization examines CLI input, runtime input paths, repository observation paths, and exact question-family paths before drawing conclusions.

#### Constitutional role

This interrogation prevents a competency or artifact from appearing self-grounding. It distinguishes eligible inputs from unsupported operator text, presentation labels, inferred candidates, or missing prerequisites.

#### Ordering

Evidence suggests preconditions/inputs naturally precede movement, output, or reliance. However, repository evidence does not prove that `Inputs` must be a named section in every investigation. Sometimes input questions are folded into `Evidence`, `Eligibility`, `Scope`, or `Current implementation behavior`.

#### Requiredness

Frequently recurring, but not always with the exact label `Inputs`.

Confidence: medium-high.

### 4. Outputs / artifact handoff / preserved evidence

#### Identity

This interrogation answers: What artifact, report, view, record, or compatibility shape leaves the subject, and what provenance or orientation survives that handoff?

#### Recurrence

- Competency interrogations repeatedly include `Artifact Handoff`, preserved evidence, and reliance sections.
- The methodology reconciliation treats `Artifact Handoff` as working methodology because it repeatedly exposed reports, JSON, notes, verification rows, relation records, provenance, support strings, and excluded meaning.
- Typed Unknown Preservation independently characterizes outputs as `TypedUnknownRecord` and public compatibility projection.
- Operational visibility district survey characterizes presentation and JSON output as a visible neighborhood.
- Completion/evaluation audits examine completion artifacts, evaluation conclusions, and whether completed work is consumed by recurring responsibilities.

#### Constitutional role

This interrogation prevents internal state, presentation output, diagnostic visibility, or compatibility payloads from being mistaken for broader repository truth. It asks what survives the boundary and what is intentionally excluded.

#### Ordering

Outputs naturally follow evidence/preconditions when a handoff exists. Repository evidence does not prove every investigation needs a separate output section; small or non-handoff investigations sometimes discuss outputs under evidence, current behavior, or reliance.

#### Requiredness

Frequently recurring for handoff-heavy surfaces; locally recurring where a specific neighborhood or diagnostic surface has explicit output.

Confidence: medium-high.

### 5. Boundaries and locality

#### Identity

This interrogation answers: Where does the subject begin, where does it end, which neighboring responsibilities does it avoid, and why is it local rather than universal?

#### Recurrence

- Competency interrogations repeatedly include `Boundaries` and `Locality`.
- The methodology reconciliation classifies `Boundaries` as earned and `Locality` as working methodology, noting that locality prevents universalizing substrate-local or surface-local behavior.
- Operational visibility district survey repeats boundaries for visible neighborhoods.
- Bridge investigations include neighboring architectural neighborhoods and neighbor boundary analysis.
- Candidate characterization uses safe-stop and counterexample sections to prevent candidate interpretation from expanding into routing, repository observation, or unrelated candidate responsibilities.

#### Constitutional role

Boundaries and locality prevent a local competency from becoming a universal planner, registry, gateway, governance owner, implementation owner, or conceptual framework.

#### Ordering

Boundaries usually follow identity and evidence. Locality often follows boundaries, but repository evidence also shows locality can be embedded in boundary analysis. The evidence does not prove strict ordering between `Boundaries` and `Locality`.

#### Requiredness

Always recurring as boundary work in competency interrogations; frequently recurring across district, bridge, candidate, and audit artifacts. Locality as a separate label is working/frequent rather than always required.

Confidence: high for boundaries; medium for locality as a distinct interrogation.

### 6. Constitutional / granted authority

#### Identity

This interrogation answers: What authority is actually granted, who may invoke or consume the artifact, and what constraints limit lawful use?

#### Recurrence

- Competency interrogations repeatedly include `Constitutional Authority` or equivalent granted-authority analysis.
- The methodology reconciliation classifies constitutional authority as earned because it separated invocation, consumption, trust, granted authority, constraints, and unlawful expansion.
- Constitutional permission bridge investigation directly asks how evidence-producing sides hand off into behavior-producing sides without becoming behavior, planning, recommendation, or execution.
- Inquiry district evidence recovers exact question-family admission and dispatch eligibility, which are authority-like gates.

#### Constitutional role

This interrogation prevents visibility, verification, recommendation-like output, admission, inventory presence, or diagnostic accessibility from becoming permission.

#### Ordering

Authority is usually evaluated after identity/evidence and before reliance or lawful termination. Repository evidence does not require it to precede or follow negative authority in every artifact; some documents separate granted authority first, then authority that merely appears to exist.

#### Requiredness

Frequently recurring; always important in competency interrogations that expose public or diagnostic surfaces.

Confidence: high.

### 7. Negative authority / apparent authority

#### Identity

This interrogation answers: What authority appears to exist but is not granted by implementation evidence?

#### Recurrence

- The methodology reconciliation classifies `Negative authority / apparent authority` as earned, with independent recurrence in later competency interrogations.
- Competency interrogations 006 and 007 explicitly identify negative authority as recurring, and competency interrogation 008 gives it its own major section.
- Constitutional permission bridge investigation provides counterexamples: permission is not behavior, planning, recommendation, or execution.
- Candidate characterization provides counterexamples showing bare raw input does not become candidate interpretations, runtime routes only a single decision, repository observation needs explicit surface selection, question-family maps are exact, and existing candidates belong elsewhere.

#### Constitutional role

Negative authority prevents output appearance from becoming constitutional permission. It is the recurring refusal grammar: visible does not mean authorized; verified does not mean permitted; related does not mean important; candidate does not mean ready; agreement does not mean truth.

#### Ordering

Repository evidence suggests negative authority often follows granted authority, boundary analysis, or evidence review because it depends on knowing what is actually supported. However, strict ordering is not proven; counterexample sections sometimes perform the same work later in the document.

#### Requiredness

Frequently recurring and earned for competency methodology; not proven as a required named section in every repository investigation.

Confidence: high.

### 8. Unknowns / stop conditions / unsupported conclusions

#### Identity

This interrogation answers: What remains unknown, what conclusions are unsupported, and where must the investigation stop?

#### Recurrence

- Competency interrogations repeatedly include `Unknown / Stop`, `Unsupported answers`, `Remaining questions`, and `Lawful termination`.
- The methodology reconciliation classifies `Unknown / Stop`, lawful termination, remaining questions, and typed unknowns as recurring honesty mechanisms.
- Typed Unknown Preservation is a neighborhood dedicated to preserving unresolved conditions without resolving them.
- Inquiry completion/evaluation audit distinguishes explicit stop, gap, pressure, no gap, unknown, and unsupported outcomes.
- Bridge and candidate investigations include lawful unknowns, unsupported conclusions, and safe-stop/counterexample analysis.

#### Constitutional role

This interrogation preserves lawful ignorance. It prevents unsupported unknowns from being converted into plans, recommendations, ownership, authority, maturity, or implementation recovery.

#### Ordering

Unknowns and stop conditions usually appear after evidence, boundaries, and supported/unsupported conclusion analysis. They also appear inside evidence sections as stop-causing evidence. Repository evidence supports a late summarizing role and an embedded guard role, not one strict position.

#### Requiredness

Always recurring in competency interrogations; frequently recurring across bridge, candidate, neighborhood, and completion/evaluation artifacts.

Confidence: high.

### 9. Reliance / lawful use

#### Identity

This interrogation answers: What may later work rely on, and what must it not rely on?

#### Recurrence

- Later competency interrogations repeatedly include `Constitutional reliance`.
- The methodology reconciliation classifies constitutional reliance as earned because it states what Seed may rely on and must not rely on.
- Permission bridge investigation independently includes constitutional reliance for permission handoff artifacts.
- Completion/evaluation audit asks what recurring responsibilities consume completed work and whether evaluation can stop.

#### Constitutional role

Reliance converts characterization into lawful use constraints without recommending implementation. It narrows how truthful artifacts may be consumed.

#### Ordering

Reliance naturally follows identity, evidence, authority, boundaries, and negative authority because it depends on those answers. Repository evidence supports this relative order more strongly than for many other sections.

#### Requiredness

Frequently recurring in later competency and bridge investigations; not always present in early or survey-style artifacts.

Confidence: medium-high.

### 10. Maturity / completion / confidence

#### Identity

This interrogation answers: How strong is the repository evidence, and has the subject reached a local stopping or maturity condition?

#### Recurrence

- Competency interrogations repeatedly end with `Confidence` and often `Lawful termination`.
- The methodology reconciliation classifies confidence as earned because it calibrates certainty.
- Operational visibility district survey uses maturity and confidence for each visible neighborhood.
- Typed Unknown Preservation includes maturity and confidence.
- Completion audits and inquiry completion/evaluation audit examine completion, evaluation, natural stopping points, and confidence.

#### Constitutional role

This interrogation prevents bounded findings from being presented as complete recovery. It distinguishes high-confidence local behavior from medium-confidence district characterization and low-confidence framework or ownership claims.

#### Ordering

Confidence usually comes near the end after claims have been bounded. Maturity may appear earlier inside survey entries. Repository evidence supports confidence as a terminal calibration, but does not require maturity to be terminal.

#### Requiredness

Always recurring as confidence in competency interrogations; frequently recurring as maturity/completion/confidence in surveys and audits.

Confidence: high.

## Emergent grammar

Do recurring interrogations appear to form a lawful grammar?

Yes.

Observed grammar only:

1. Establish the subject and scope without granting it authority from its name.
2. Bind claims to repository evidence.
3. Determine prerequisites, eligible inputs, and observable evidence.
4. Determine preserved evidence, outputs, handoff artifacts, and compatibility shapes where they exist.
5. Locate boundaries, neighbors, locality, and granted authority.
6. Identify apparent authority that implementation does not grant.
7. Preserve unknowns, unsupported conclusions, and lawful stop conditions.
8. State lawful reliance where later work may consume the artifact.
9. Calibrate maturity, completion, and confidence.

This is an observed recurring review grammar, not an implementation design. It is not a recovered engine, framework, workflow owner, campaign, or authorization sequence.

## Answer to the proposed sequence

The repository has not converged specifically on `Identity -> Inputs -> Outputs -> Negative Authority` as a complete or mandatory sequence.

Repository evidence supports all four candidate interrogations in some form, but it also supports additional recurring interrogations that the proposed sequence omits: evidence ledger, boundaries, granted authority, unknown/stop, reliance, and confidence. Repository evidence also weakens strict ordering: inputs can appear as preconditions, evidence, or eligibility; outputs can appear as handoff, preserved evidence, presentation, JSON, or completion artifacts; negative authority can appear as a named section or as counterexamples/unsupported conclusions.

Therefore the four-part sequence is a partial recurring habit with repository support for its parts, not the full lawful grammar by itself.

## Preserved unknowns

- Whether future competency interrogations should compress `Inputs` into `Evidence` or preserve `Preconditions` as a distinct section.
- Whether `Outputs` should remain separate from `Artifact Handoff` and `Constitutional reliance` for non-handoff surfaces.
- Whether `Locality` should remain separate from `Boundaries` in small investigations.
- Whether `Resolution` belongs to competency interrogation generally or only to inquiry/completion districts.
- Whether `Maturity` should be asked of every competency or only survey/completion artifacts.
- Whether negative authority should always be a named section or may remain embedded in boundary/counterexample/unsupported-conclusion analysis.
- Whether the observed grammar is stable enough to preserve as methodology beyond documentation; no implementation evidence supports a runtime owner or engine.

## Confidence

High confidence that the repository has repeatedly asked lawful questions about identity, evidence, boundaries, authority, negative authority, unknowns/stops, reliance, termination, and confidence.

Medium-high confidence that inputs/preconditions and outputs/handoff are recurring grammar elements, because they recur under multiple labels and are stronger for handoff-heavy or dispatch-heavy surfaces.

Medium confidence that locality, adjacency, resolution, and maturity are grammar elements rather than local survey/audit tools. They recur, but repository evidence does not prove all are universally required or independently ordered.

Low confidence in any claim that the repository has converged on the exact ordered sequence `Identity -> Inputs -> Outputs -> Negative Authority` as a complete grammar.
