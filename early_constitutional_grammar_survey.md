# Early Constitutional Grammar Survey

Repository authority wins.

## Boundary

This is exactly one bounded Constitutional Grammar Survey. It determines recurring local grammar for already recovered constitutional participants only. It does not recover implementation, ownership, districts, universal process, runtime architecture, scheduler, planner, registry, or a universal lifecycle.

The survey treats participant names as bounded repository-evidenced participants only where prior recovered evidence supports them. Recurrence is used to compare grammar after independent inspection, not to infer ownership.

## Comparison after independent inspection

### Recurring grammar

Across the recovered participants, the recurring constitutional grammar is:

1. A bounded subject or artifact becomes available only through a repository-visible surface.
2. The artifact is consumed with provenance, boundary, support, and current-status limits.
3. Evidence may permit movement, but only through the competent local boundary for that family.
4. The participant emits a bounded artifact: present material, stop, readiness status, warrant, historical placement, explanation, or orientation constraint.
5. The emitted artifact travels to named neighboring consumers without carrying implementation ownership, runtime command, scheduler authority, or universal process authority.
6. Unknowns, negative authority, and non-promotion boundaries are outputs, not failures.

### Recurring consumed artifacts

- Bounded subject or candidate.
- External representation or observed material.
- Evidence/support relation.
- Provenance.
- Authority boundary.
- Current lawful status.
- Prior preserved history.
- Warrant and confidence.
- Explicit stop or insufficient-evidence condition.

### Recurring emitted artifacts

- Present bounded material.
- Continued Null / unknown / uncommitted status.
- Readiness or current lawful condition statement.
- Warrant profile.
- Local permission/refusal to move.
- Historical placement or biography/explanation path.
- Non-promotion and negative-authority statement.
- Preserved unknowns and confidence.

### Recurring emitters

- Repository-visible evidence surfaces.
- Observation/intake or preservation surfaces.
- Competent family-local admission, readiness, warrant, promotion, characterization, audit, or explanation boundaries.
- Repository orientation artifacts.
- Implementation/tests where behavior is at issue.

No recurring emitter is recovered as a universal owner.

### Recurring consumers

- Readiness review.
- Current lawful condition statements.
- Promotion/admission/selection surfaces.
- Warrant and confidence review.
- History/biography/explanation surfaces.
- Answer composition and future repository-facing investigations.

No recurring consumer receives hidden ownership, scheduling authority, or implementation command merely by consuming artifacts.
