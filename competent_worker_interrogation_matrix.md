# Competent Worker Interrogation Matrix

## 1. Concise model statement

Repository evidence supports the competent-worker model as an audit discipline, not as a new runtime architecture:

```text
Operator
  ↓
Seed organism
  ↓
bounded competent workers
  ↓
bounded artifacts
  ↓
local admission by downstream responsibilities
```

The useful constitutional claim is narrow: Seed coordinates bounded work by asking local workers what they are allowed to see, preserve, translate, hand off, and refuse. Seed should not become Kubernetes, SQL, Linux, Prometheus, repository analysis, diagnostic governance, or answer composition. Repository authority wins when implementation evidence shows a local grammar, local stop condition, or local artifact.

The model improves over universal ownership because it preserves implementation-local authority and avoids turning every recurring label into a universal owner. It remains only a hypothesis wherever repository evidence shows presentation vocabulary, candidate status, report-only artifacts, or unsupported proposals.

Applicability markers used below:

- ✓ Applies
- ○ Not applicable
- ? Unknown
- ✗ Constitutionally impossible

## 2. Interrogation matrix

The matrix is intended for any current or future competent worker before Seed trusts, delegates to, consumes from, or preserves artifacts from it.

| Family | Constitutional questions | Applies when | Stop / refusal signal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Identity | What kind of architectural phenomenon is this? What bounded responsibility owns this? What is explicitly not owned here? What competent worker is this? What local language or grammar does it understand? | A surface, module, adapter, report, or record family claims local competence. | Identity is only metaphor, presentation wording, or not backed by implementation artifacts. |
| Pressure | What architectural pressure is this satisfying? What failure appears if this disappears? Is this pressure local to the worker or shared across workers? | A worker exists because a concrete behavior, audit, output, or preservation need recurs. | Pressure is only recent vocabulary or cannot be connected to repository behavior. |
| Constitution / Lawfulness | What constitutional authority permits this worker to participate? What constitutional constraint limits it? What would make its participation unlawful? What lawful stop conditions can it produce? | The worker can affect trust, evidence, handoff, recording, or operational interpretation. | Participation would infer authority, mutate outside its mandate, or convert diagnostic evidence into truth. |
| Evidence | What evidence can this worker observe? What evidence can it preserve? What evidence can it not observe? What evidence permits movement? What evidence prevents movement? What evidence is intentionally discarded or excluded? | The worker emits, consumes, filters, or preserves evidence-like artifacts. | Evidence is unavailable, malformed, stale, out of grammar, or belongs to another owner. |
| Authority | Who may invoke this worker? Who may consume its artifacts? Who may mutate through it? What authority does it not have? Can evidence increase confidence without increasing authority? | The worker has an invocation path, downstream consumer, or implied trust boundary. | Evidence would be mistaken for execution authority, ownership authority, or policy authority. |
| Boundaries | Where does this worker begin? Where does it end? What neighboring work does it avoid? What local admission rule governs input? What local refusal boundary exists? | The worker has an identifiable input grammar and output artifact. | Input does not satisfy local grammar or output would cross into another responsibility. |
| Handoff | What artifact does this worker produce? What minimum orientation must survive? What provenance must survive? What downstream work can consume the artifact? What must not be included in the handoff? | A downstream responsibility consumes a record, report, view, diagnostic, answer, or candidate. | Handoff omits provenance/orientation or includes authority it does not own. |
| Unknown / Stop | What can remain unknown? What causes lawful stop? What would be overclaiming? Can the worker return unsupported without failure? Can the worker preserve a frontier? | Work is incomplete, exploratory, bounded, read-only, or candidate-producing. | Worker pretends unknowns are resolved or treats unsupported as failure. |
| Locality | Why is this local instead of universal? What would fail if centralized? What local grammar/protocol/evidence rule must remain worker-owned? What global coordination is allowed? | More than one local competency appears similar enough to tempt abstraction. | A shared framework would erase provider/substrate-specific evidence rules. |
| Continuity | What identity survives change? What may change without changing identity? What compatibility contract is preserved? What internal freedom remains? What previous artifacts remain valid? | A worker evolves, gains fields, changes internals, or adds adapters. | Change breaks output compatibility without an admitted boundary. |
| Self-Observation / Drift | How can this worker be observed? How would drift be detected? What expected shape exists? What diagnostics or audits already cover it? What false-positive drift must be avoided? | The worker is operational, diagnostic, registry-backed, answer-surface-backed, or shape-audited. | Drift evidence is only lexical change, report wording, or expected local variation. |
| Evolution | What pressure caused this worker to exist? What future evidence could invalidate boundaries? What new competency might consume artifacts later? What justifies splitting or retiring it? | A worker is candidate, compressed, duplicated, or maturing. | Evolution would promote a candidate without repeated implementation evidence. |

## 5. Gaps discovered

1. **Self-observation is uneven outside diagnostic governance.** Diagnostic Inventory and Diagnostic Shape Audit answer drift questions well; many architectural/documented workers do not expose equally clear shape checks.
2. **Authority gaps cluster around orientation and selection surfaces.** Inquiry Orientation, Selection Path, Current Work Position, and Working-State Activation explain position and safe movement, but explanation must not become command authority.
3. **Handoff gaps cluster around candidate/proposal/report-only workers.** Observation Agreement, Grammar Observation, Attachment, and Repository Participation need clearer artifacts and consumers before implementation.
4. **Provider Language Translation is healthy because it remains local.** The temptation to centralize it into a provider registry, grammar engine, or universal intermediate record is contradicted by dpkg, Python, Prometheus, systemd, and local-host variation.
5. **Presentation vocabulary remains dangerous.** Current Work Position, active edge, continuity, source navigation, projection cache, and similar labels can orient work without becoming preserved repository knowledge.
6. **Repository Artifact Observation Adapter needs a more precise audit pass.** Evidence supports the pattern, but this report did not establish the same explicit boundary, registry, and drift coverage available for documentation structure.
7. **Repository Participation is constitutional, not a worker by default.** It may govern admission of artifacts, but treating it as a single implementation owner would risk re-centralization.
8. **Question dispatch has a strong constitutional shape.** Exact question-family identifiers and bounded surface args are healthier than generalized planning or universal interpretation.

## 7. Risks and anti-goals

- **Risk: metaphor hardening.** The competent-worker model can become a new universal ontology if treated as more than an interrogation discipline.
- **Risk: false locality.** Some surfaces, especially diagnostic governance and question dispatch, legitimately require registry-style coordination; locality must not deny implementation facts.
- **Risk: overfitting recent vocabulary.** Terms like current work position, active edge, continuity, and source navigation may orient humans without being repository knowledge.
- **Risk: authority escalation.** Evidence, confidence, support, orientation, and selection explanation must not silently become execution authority or repository truth.
- **Risk: diagnostic sprawl.** Not every report-only concept deserves a diagnostic or shape audit.
- **Risk: handoff over-inclusion.** Workers should preserve minimum orientation and provenance, not smuggle interpretation, ownership, policy, or execution claims.

Anti-goals:

- Do not implement code from this report.
- Do not add diagnostics, CLI flags, schemas, planners, registries, central coordinators, or universal worker abstractions.
- Do not recover ownership or stabilize candidate vocabulary.
- Do not treat unsupported or frontier status as failure.
- Do not attach diagnostic-only findings directly to cluster truth.

4. Whether Observation Agreement and Grammar Observation have concrete consumers that justify worker status.
5. Whether question-surface shape governance already covers enough non-diagnostic operational surfaces to avoid new diagnostic machinery.
6. Whether future provider work will produce repeated evidence for provider-specific intermediate records without justifying a universal provider record.
7. Whether Repository Participation is a constitutional admission boundary, a report family, or a future implementation concern.

## 9. Explicit answers to required questions

### 1. Does the competent-worker model improve over universal ownership?

Yes, if kept as an interrogation discipline. It improves over universal ownership by asking each local competency what it can see, preserve, translate, hand off, and refuse. It prevents Seed from becoming Kubernetes, SQL, Linux, Prometheus, provider grammar, answer composition, diagnostic governance, and repository participation all at once. It fails if converted into a universal worker registry or ontology.

### 2. Which workers already answer the matrix well?

Best answers today:

- Diagnostic Inventory.
- Diagnostic Shape Audit.
- Documentation Structure Adapter.
- Structure Observation.
- Relationship Observation.
- Provider Language Translation, when kept provider-local.
- Capability Candidate / Capability Verification / Capability Inventory.
- Question Family / Bounded Ask Dispatch.
- Answer Composition and Operational Story, when kept compositional and non-authoritative.

### 3. Which workers expose the largest authority gaps?

Largest authority gaps:

- Current Work Position / Working-State Activation, because orientation can be mistaken for authority.
- Inquiry Orientation and Selection Path / Inquiry Lineage, because explanation can be mistaken for command or planning.
- Operator Intake / Opaque Operator Representation, because operator text can be over-interpreted.
- Repository Participation, because broad admission language can become universal ownership.
- Capability surfaces, if confidence or verification is allowed to imply execution permission.

### 4. Which workers expose the largest handoff gaps?

Largest handoff gaps:

- Attachment / Candidate Attachment.
- Observation Agreement candidate.
- Grammar Observation candidate.
- Repository Participation.
- Repository Artifact Observation Adapter, pending a focused boundary audit.
- Current Work Position, because minimum surviving orientation is useful but not clearly implementation-owned.

### 5. Which workers expose the largest self-observation/drift gaps?

Largest self-observation/drift gaps:

- Relationship Observation.
- Provider Language Translation outside provider-specific tests.
- Structure Observation as an internal owner rather than public surface.
- Answer Composition / Operational Story outside their existing tests and representations.
- Current Work Position / Working-State Activation.
- Candidate/report-only concepts generally.

Diagnostic Inventory and Diagnostic Shape Audit are the counterexamples: they answer drift well.

### 6. Which apparent workers are actually only presentation vocabulary or report-only concepts?

Likely presentation, frontier, candidate, or report-only unless further evidence appears:

- Current Work Position / Working-State Activation.
- Active edge / continuation vocabulary adjacent to current position.
- Attachment / Candidate Attachment.
- Observation Agreement candidate.
- Grammar Observation candidate.
- Repository Participation as a single worker.
- Any “competent worker” registry concept derived from this report.

### 7. What should Codex investigate next?

Codex should investigate, in order:

1. Repository Artifact Observation boundary and drift coverage.
2. Operator Intake exact artifact and handoff boundary.
3. Inquiry Orientation versus movement/selection authority.
4. Provider-local stop conditions without creating a universal provider abstraction.
5. Capability inventory/candidate consumers for accidental authority escalation.
6. Whether existing question-surface inventory already provides enough shape governance for answer/question surfaces.

### 8. What should Codex explicitly avoid building?

Codex should explicitly avoid building:

- A universal competent-worker registry.
- A central planner, coordinator, or progression engine.
- A universal provider grammar engine or universal provider intermediate record.
- New diagnostics or CLI surfaces from this report.
- A repository participation owner without admission evidence.
- Vocabulary stabilization around current work position, active edge, continuation, attachment, or grammar observation.
- Any implementation that promotes candidate/report-only workers into architecture without new repository evidence.

## 10. Counterexample search results

- **The model may be metaphor.** The term “competent worker” itself is not a reviewed implementation owner. This report therefore treats it as an interrogation matrix, not architecture.
- **Some workers are not local.** Diagnostic Inventory and Diagnostic Shape Audit coordinate globally across diagnostic surfaces. Their global scope is evidence-backed and should not be denied.
- **Some surfaces require registry-like coordination.** Exact question-family dispatch and diagnostic inventory are legitimate registries; this does not justify a universal worker registry.
- **Some proposed workers duplicate existing owners.** Observation Agreement and Grammar Observation may duplicate Structure Observation, Relationship Observation, Provider Language Translation, or answer-surface checks unless consumers prove otherwise.
- **The matrix can overfit recent vocabulary.** Current Work Position, active edge, and continuation are useful orientation terms but require implementation evidence before preservation.
- **Action lists can manufacture work.** This report marks many items audit-first, frontier, or do-not-build rather than converting every gap into implementation.
