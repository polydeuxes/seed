# Constitutional Source-of-Law Lineage Boundary Investigation

## Bounded question

Across recurring repository evidence, when a human-provided public-world evidence packet contains legal materials, what boundary, if any, prevents Seed from treating a chain of cases, citations, doctrine, or legal commentary as source-of-law lineage without separately preserving constitutional text, amendment text, ratification/adoption provenance where relevant, jurisdiction, temporal authority, enactment or promulgation path, procedural posture, holding boundary, dependency, negative authority, Unknowns, and lawful stop?

This performs exactly one bounded constitutional investigation: **Source-of-Law Lineage != Precedent Chain**.

This is not legal research, not case evaluation, not implementation, not public-world evidence fetching, not a legal validity engine, not a constitutional validity engine, not a precedent engine, and not a source-of-law graph.

## Source-of-law lineage analysis

Repository evidence does **not** establish a fully implemented source-of-law model. The app-visible reachability audit specifically treats `source-of-law lineage` as an unpreserved presentation label in this context.

The smaller repository-supported meaning is conditional and boundary-shaped:

> source-of-law lineage, if used at all in this investigation, means the preserved path showing why a legal material can support a legal-source or constitutional-source claim within a bounded jurisdiction, time, authority role, source role, procedural/adoption/enactment/promulgation path, and claim form.

For constitutional materials, that candidate lineage may require constitutional text, amendment text, ratification or adoption provenance where relevant, jurisdiction, temporal authority, negative authority, Unknowns, and lawful stop. For statutes or regulations, it may require enacted or promulgated text, issuer, jurisdiction, date/version, procedural path, delegated-authority claim, currentness, negative authority, and claim-form limits. For court opinions, it may require forum, posture, facts before the court, holding boundary, procedural outcome, cited authorities, dependency, negative treatment, jurisdiction, time, and source-attributed limits.

But because Seed does not currently expose a source-of-law graph or legal validity engine, this investigation may only preserve the boundary: **a chain of cases or citations is not enough**.

## Precedent-chain analysis

The exact phrase `precedent chain` is not preserved repository knowledge in the app-visible reachability result. Repository documents nevertheless support a bounded distinction: a precedent chain is a path of court opinions relying on, citing, following, distinguishing, extending, or repeating earlier court opinions or doctrine.

A precedent chain can support narrow source-attributed claims such as:

- this opinion cited that opinion;
- this doctrine was repeated across these opinions;
- this court described a prior case as authority;
- this legal narrative has a visible dependency path.

A precedent chain does not automatically support:

- constitutional text lineage;
- amendment ratification provenance;
- statutory enactment provenance;
- regulatory promulgation provenance;
- legal validity;
- constitutional validity;
- independent constitutional warrant;
- historical truth;
- current controlling authority across jurisdiction and time.

## Citation-chain analysis

A citation chain is weaker and broader than a precedent chain. It may show references among sources: court-to-court, court-to-statute, commentary-to-case, article-to-article, regulation-to-statute, or mixed citation paths.

Repository evidence around source independence, repetition/proof, and authority/volume prevents treating citation count or citation connectivity as independent support. A citation chain is dependency and navigation evidence unless the packet separately preserves source role, source independence, claim form, jurisdiction, time, authority, and support path.

Therefore a citation chain may help locate materials, but it is not source-of-law lineage by itself.

## Doctrine-history analysis

Doctrine history is a narrative or analytic account of how a legal doctrine appeared, changed, was repeated, or was described over time. It may include opinions, commentary, treatises, statutes, regulations, social context, and institutional summaries.

Repository narrative/history boundaries prevent doctrine history from becoming constitutional provenance merely because it is coherent, repeated, or institutionally familiar. Doctrine history can support `this narrative says doctrine developed this way` or `these sources describe a doctrine over time`. It cannot, without more, prove constitutional text meaning, valid amendment adoption, lawful enactment, current authority, independent source-of-law lineage, or constitutional warrant.

## Constitutional-text analysis

Constitutional text is source material with a different role from later judicial interpretation. Repository evidence supports preserving source role and claim form:

- constitutional text can support a narrow claim that the artifact contains certain text, in a stated version, under a stated jurisdiction and temporal condition;
- later judicial interpretation can support a narrow claim that a court interpreted or applied that text in a particular case, posture, and holding boundary.

The text is not automatically self-interpreting for every future claim, and an interpretation is not automatically the constitutional text itself. Seed must preserve the difference between `the constitutional text says X`, `the court said X about the text`, `the holding was X`, and `Seed may rely on X as constitutional warrant`.

## Amendment-text / ratification-provenance analysis

Amendment text and ratification/adoption provenance perform different constitutional work.

Amendment text can support source-attributed claims about represented amendment language and artifact identity. Ratification or adoption provenance concerns the path by which the amendment is claimed to have become authoritative: proposal, adoption, ratification votes or procedures, certification, jurisdictional requirements, dates, versions, and relevant negative authority.

The existence of amendment text does not prove valid ratification. Ratification history does not prove valid ratification merely by narrative or repetition. Proof of valid ratification would require competent support for the legal/procedural requirements and their satisfaction. Seed must not decide that here.

## Statute source-of-law lineage analysis

A statute's source-of-law lineage is not constitutional warrant. Statutory text may support source-attributed claims about enacted language, issuer, jurisdiction, date, codification, and version. Enactment provenance may support claims about legislative path, passage, signature, effective date, repeal, amendment, or codification.

But statutory existence or legal authority does not prove constitutional validity, constitutional warrant, historical truth, enforceability, or current controlling effect. A statute may be a legal source while the constitutional warrant for relying on it remains Unknown unless separately supported.

## Amendment-text / ratification / regulation provenance analysis

Regulation text and promulgation provenance are likewise distinct. Regulation text can support source-attributed claims about agency language. Promulgation provenance concerns delegated authority, notice/comment or other procedure, effective date, version, jurisdiction, currentness, amendments, rescission, and negative authority.

A regulation's publication or repeated citation does not prove statutory delegation, constitutional validity, procedural validity, current enforceability, or constitutional warrant. Missing promulgation path or temporal authority requires Unknown preservation or lawful stop.

## Court-opinion lineage analysis

Court-opinion lineage tracks opinions, courts, procedural posture, parties/record if relevant, holdings if competently identified, reasoning, citations, dependencies, negative treatment, jurisdiction, and time. This is not the same as source-of-law lineage.

A court opinion can support `the court published/stated/decided X` within the source role of that opinion. It can sometimes support a holding claim if the holding boundary is competently identified. It does not automatically support that every sentence is a holding, that the court's reasoning is source-derived constitutional text, or that a court-described boundary is a constitutional boundary.

Court-opinion lineage may be part of legal authority analysis, but it is not automatically constitutional source lineage.

## Repeated-citation analysis

Repeated citation can show recurrence, salience, influence, institutional uptake, visibility, or dependency. Repository evidence on repetition/proof, authority/volume, and source independence blocks the stronger inference.

Repeated citation is not independent source-of-law support unless the repeated sources are shown to have independent support paths and appropriate source roles. A hundred citations to the same unsupported proposition may be one dependency repeated a hundred times, not a hundred constitutional warrants.

## Legal-narrative / constitutional-provenance analysis

Legal narrative organizes materials into an explanatory story. Constitutional provenance preserves the source and authority path for a constitutional claim.

A legal narrative may be useful orientation, but it is not constitutional provenance unless it carries competent source materials and preserves the required path: text/version, jurisdiction, time, adoption or enactment path, support role, dependency, negative authority, claim form, Unknowns, and stop condition.

Doctrine history and legal narrative therefore remain presentation or analysis unless repository evidence preserves them as bounded source/support artifacts for a specific claim.

## Source-derived-boundary / court-described-boundary analysis

A court-described boundary is what a court says the boundary is. A source-derived constitutional boundary would require preserved source support beyond the court's description, such as constitutional text, amendment text, adoption/ratification provenance, jurisdiction, temporal authority, and competent interpretation support.

Repository evidence supports preserving court descriptions as source-attributed legal material. It does not authorize Seed to promote the court's self-description into source-derived constitutional authority. If the packet lacks independent source-of-law support, the source-derived boundary remains Unknown.

## Comparison-readiness analysis

Before Seed can compare a legal source's claimed boundary against a source-derived constitutional boundary, it must preserve at least:

1. the exact claim form being compared;
2. source identity, version, jurisdiction, issuer/forum, and date;
3. constitutional text or amendment text where the claim purports to be constitutional;
4. adoption, ratification, enactment, or promulgation provenance where relevant;
5. procedural posture and holding boundary for court opinions;
6. precedent dependency and negative authority;
7. source independence or dependency status;
8. temporal authority/currentness;
9. the distinction between source text, source narrative, legal commentary, doctrine history, and court holding;
10. confidence/evidence-strength limits;
11. Unknowns;
12. lawful stop.

Without these, Seed may preserve source-attributed content but must not compare as if source-derived constitutional lineage were present.

## Jurisdiction / temporal-provenance analysis

Jurisdiction and temporal provenance are authority boundaries. A source may be authoritative for one jurisdiction, forum, time, procedural posture, or claim form while irrelevant, superseded, non-controlling, repealed, amended, overruled, distinguished, or Unknown elsewhere.

Missing jurisdiction or time blocks strong claims about legal validity, current authority, constitutional warrant, or historical truth. The lawful output is source attribution, Unknown, or stop.

## Authority-boundary analysis

Repository evidence distinguishes legal authority from constitutional warrant. Legal authority is a bounded source-role or domain-role claim about a legal artifact, institution, or source. Constitutional warrant is Seed's evidence-bound lawful reliance under preserved authority boundary.

Thus:

```text
legal authority != constitutional warrant
precedent authority != source-of-law lineage
citation volume != independent support
court-described boundary != source-derived constitutional boundary
statutory text != constitutional validity
regulation text != constitutional validity
legal narrative != constitutional provenance
```

Authority must be role-local, claim-local, time-local, and jurisdiction-local.

## Confidence / claim-strength analysis

Confidence must match the preserved support. High confidence may be lawful for narrow repository claims such as `the app-visible reachability audit did not preserve this phrase` or `this repository investigation says legal authority is not constitutional warrant`. Lower confidence or Unknown is required for legal-domain distinctions not implemented by Seed, including ratification validity, current controlling authority, negative legal treatment, source-of-law lineage, and constitutional validity.

Claim-strength escalation is blocked. Evidence for `source says X` does not become evidence for `X is true`, `X is valid law`, `X is constitutional warrant`, or `X is historically true`.

The following Unknowns must remain when not separately preserved by repository evidence or packet evidence:

- source-of-law lineage;
- constitutional text version and identity;
- amendment text version and identity;
- ratification or adoption provenance;
- valid ratification or valid adoption;
- statutory enactment path;
- regulatory promulgation path;
- jurisdiction;
- temporal authority and currentness;
- source role and authority scope;
- court forum and procedural posture;
- holding boundary;
- facts before the court;
- precedent dependency;
- negative authority or treatment;
- source independence;
- whether repeated citation adds independent support;
- legal validity;
- constitutional validity;
- enforceability;
- constitutional warrant;
- historical truth;
- confidence beyond source-attributed claims.

Unknown preservation is a lawful result, not a failed investigation.

## Lawful stop

The lawful stop condition is:

> Stop when a packet provides legal materials, case chains, citation chains, doctrine history, legal narrative, or commentary without separately preserving the source-of-law lineage and boundary evidence needed for the stronger claim.

At that stop, Seed may preserve bounded source-attributed claims such as `the packet contained this opinion`, `the opinion cited this case`, or `the commentary asserted this doctrine`. Seed must not declare legal validity, legal invalidity, constitutional validity, constitutional invalidity, enforceability, constitutional warrant, independent source-of-law lineage, or historical truth.

## Required-analysis answers

1. **What does repository evidence already mean by source-of-law lineage, if anything?** At most, it means preserved source/provenance path for why a legal material can support a bounded legal-source or constitutional-source claim. The term is not implementation-preserved by the app-visible reachability audit.

2. **What does repository evidence already mean by precedent chain, citation chain, or doctrine history?** It means, at most, source-attributed dependency, reference, recurrence, or narrative history among legal materials. The exact `precedent chain` phrase was not app-preserved in the reachability audit.

3. **What distinguishes source-of-law lineage from precedent dependency?** Source-of-law lineage requires source role, jurisdiction, time, text/provenance, adoption/enactment/promulgation path where relevant, and claim-form authority. Precedent dependency shows reliance on earlier opinions or doctrine.

4. **What distinguishes constitutional text from later judicial interpretation of that text?** Constitutional text is source text; judicial interpretation is a court's source-attributed application or explanation in a case. Interpretation is not the text itself and is not automatically constitutional warrant.

5. **What distinguishes amendment text from ratification or adoption provenance?** Amendment text is represented language. Ratification/adoption provenance is the procedural and temporal path claimed to make the amendment authoritative.

6. **What distinguishes ratification history from proof of valid ratification?** Ratification history may be records or narratives about events. Proof of valid ratification would require competent support that applicable requirements were satisfied. Seed must not infer that from narrative or repetition.

7. **What distinguishes statute or regulation text from constitutional warrant?** Statute/regulation text may show enacted or promulgated language. Constitutional warrant requires evidence-bound lawful reliance under preserved authority boundary and is not supplied by legal text alone.

8. **What distinguishes court opinion lineage from source-of-law lineage?** Court-opinion lineage tracks opinions, holdings, reasoning, citations, posture, dependency, and treatment. Source-of-law lineage requires the underlying source/provenance path for the claimed legal or constitutional authority.

9. **What distinguishes repeated citation from independent source-of-law support?** Repeated citation shows recurrence or dependency. Independent support requires non-circular, source-independent paths with appropriate authority, provenance, and claim-form match.

10. **What distinguishes legal narrative or doctrine history from constitutional provenance?** Narrative organizes or explains. Constitutional provenance preserves the competent source path for a constitutional claim.

11. **What distinguishes source-derived boundary from court-described boundary?** Source-derived boundary is supported by preserved constitutional/source materials. Court-described boundary is what a court says; it remains source-attributed unless independently supported.

12. **What must be preserved before Seed can compare a legal source's claimed boundary against a source-derived constitutional boundary?** Claim form, source identity/version, jurisdiction, temporal authority, constitutional/amendment text, adoption/ratification/enactment/promulgation path where relevant, procedural posture, holding boundary, precedent dependency, negative authority, source independence, confidence, Unknowns, and lawful stop.

13. **What role do provenance, jurisdiction, temporal provenance, adoption/enactment path, holding boundary, procedural posture, source independence, negative authority, claim form, confidence, and Unknown preservation play?** They bound lawful reliance, prevent overpromotion, and determine whether Seed may make a narrow source-attributed claim, a comparison, or must stop.

14. **What Unknowns must remain when source-of-law lineage, ratification provenance, jurisdiction, temporal authority, holding boundary, or precedent dependency are missing?** The missing item itself, legal authority scope, currentness, validity, source independence, negative authority, constitutional warrant, enforceability, historical truth, and confidence beyond source attribution remain Unknown.

15. **What lawful stop condition prevents Seed from declaring source-of-law lineage, validity, invalidity, constitutional warrant, or historical truth beyond supported evidence?** Stop when the packet lacks competent preserved lineage/provenance/boundary evidence for the stronger claim. Preserve only the narrower source-attributed content that support allows.

16. **Does this investigation create implementation pressure?** No. It creates review discipline and a boundary conclusion, not implementation pressure.

17. **Does repository evidence support implementation work yet?** No concrete repository failure, missing schema, failing diagnostic, or current consumer need was found. The app-visible surfaces showed absence/non-preservation rather than an implementation defect.

## Supported conclusions

1. Seed must not treat source-of-law lineage as repository knowledge merely because the operator names it.
2. Seed must not treat a precedent chain, citation chain, doctrine history, legal narrative, repeated citation, or legal commentary as source-of-law lineage without separate boundary support.
3. Constitutional text, amendment text, ratification/adoption provenance, statutory enactment, regulatory promulgation, and court-opinion lineage have distinct source roles.
4. Court opinions may support source-attributed claims about what a court said or decided, but not all opinion text is holding, and a holding is not automatically constitutional warrant.
5. Repetition, citation volume, or doctrine history can show recurrence or dependency but not independent warrant.
6. Missing lineage, jurisdiction, temporal authority, adoption/enactment/promulgation provenance, holding boundary, procedural posture, source independence, negative authority, claim form, confidence, or Unknowns requires narrower claims or lawful stop.

## Unsupported conclusions

This investigation does not support:

- a source-of-law graph;
- a precedent engine;
- a legal research engine;
- a legal validity engine;
- a constitutional validity engine;
- source scoring, authority scoring, credibility scoring, or artifact scoring;
- any declaration that a statute, regulation, court opinion, doctrine, or amendment is valid or invalid;
- any declaration about a real case outcome;
- any declaration that a precedent chain is source-of-law lineage;
- any declaration that repeated citation is independent source-of-law support;
- any declaration that legal narrative is constitutional provenance;
- any implementation work.

## Implementation recommendation

No implementation work is recommended. Repository evidence supports a boundary-preserving investigation result only. Future implementation pressure would require a concrete repository failure, current consumer need, diagnostic visibility gap, schema gap, or behavior that actually collapses precedent/citation/narrative material into source-of-law lineage or constitutional warrant.
