Repository authority wins.


## Method

A candidate survivor is treated as supported only when recurring repository artifacts show it surviving across more than one investigation shape and across more than one possible outcome posture, such as supported answer, unsupported answer, preserved unknown, lawful stop, readiness-with-limits, or refusal of stronger claims.

A recurring word is not enough. A recurring section label is not enough. App-visible structural recurrence is not enough. A candidate must be supported by recurring investigation behavior: bounded evidence review, explicit boundary, outcome-independent validity, and refusal to promote unsupported stronger claims.

## Bounded answer

Successful constitutional investigations consistently preserve a narrow set of constitutional products independent of the specific investigation outcome:

1. the bounded question or bounded subject;
2. the reviewed evidence scope and support/provenance limits;
3. the distinction between what was supported, unsupported, unknown, refused, or lawfully stopped;
4. the authority boundary and negative authority of the answer;
5. preserved unknowns where evidence is insufficient;
6. a lawful stop or termination condition;
7. calibrated confidence tied to the reviewed evidence;
8. when movement, translation, promotion, reliance, or recovery is involved, the local boundary/relationship that was actually recovered or refused.

The recurring survivor is therefore not a proved canonical concept named `invariant`. The supported answer is narrower: constitutional investigations consistently leave behind **bounded constitutional products** that remain valid as products of the investigation even when the substantive answer is yes, no, unknown, insufficient, or stop.

## Recurring investigation products

The following products consistently survive successful constitutional investigation at the level supported by repository evidence:

| Product | What survives | Outcome independence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bounded question / subject | The exact question or subject the investigation lawfully answered. | Survives yes, no, unknown, insufficient evidence, and stop because it defines what was and was not investigated. |
| Evidence scope / support / provenance | Reviewed sources, app visibility, implementation evidence, tests, or prior reports used as authority. | Survives even when evidence is insufficient because insufficiency is itself scoped. |
| Supported conclusion | Claims actually warranted by reviewed evidence. | Survives only to the stated strength and boundary. |
| Unsupported conclusion | Stronger or adjacent claims explicitly refused. | Survives as a negative product preventing later silent promotion. |
| Preserved unknown | Unresolved matter kept visible rather than inferred through. | Survives when the answer is unknown, no, partial, or stopped. |
| Authority boundary | What the answer may authorize, rely on, promote, translate, record, or recover. | Survives by limiting all outcomes, including affirmative ones. |
| Negative authority | What the investigation explicitly does not authorize. | Survives across outcomes because it blocks overreach. |
| Lawful stop / termination | Where the investigation stopped and why. | Survives even when no positive recovery occurred. |
| Confidence | Evidence-calibrated strength of the answer. | Survives by preventing unsupported certainty. |
| Local recovered/refused relationship | The boundary, distinction, handoff, relation, or non-collapse result actually shown by evidence. | Survives only locally and only when the investigation involved such a relationship. |

## Supported constitutional distinctions

1. **Recurring product != canonical vocabulary.** The repository supports recurring bounded products, but does not support promoting the informal term `invariant` as constitutional vocabulary here.

2. **Recurrence != authority.** App-visible or prose-visible recurrence can orient investigation, but repository evidence repeatedly refuses recurrence alone as ontology, implementation ownership, semantic truth, or promotion authority.

3. **Outcome-independent success != positive conclusion.** A successful investigation can produce a supported yes, supported no, preserved unknown, insufficient-evidence boundary, unsupported conclusion, or lawful stop.

4. **Unknown preservation != failure.** Preserved unknowns recur as lawful products when evidence, authority, or scope is insufficient.

5. **Lawful stop != abandonment.** Lawful stop recurs as a valid termination product when further movement would exceed evidence, authority, scope, or family boundary.

6. **Authority boundary != implementation recovery.** Authority limits may be constitutionally supported without authorizing implementation ownership, runtime behavior, diagnostic surfaces, or mutation.

7. **Boundary product != universal framework.** Boundaries, distinctions, and relationships recur, but repository evidence does not support one universal investigation framework, ontology, or ordering.

8. **Implementation evidence != universal abstraction.** Implementation recovery methodology supports local boundaries, handoffs, compatibility, tests, provenance, and negative ownership, but refuses shared abstraction unless implementation evidence independently requires it.
