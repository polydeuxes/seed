## Comparison

### Recurring constitutional properties

Across inspected candidates, a local responsibility is recurring only where the repository shows a **bounded constitutional authority to perform one local movement on specific artifacts while preserving the limits that make that movement lawful**.

The recurring properties are:

1. **Local boundary.** The responsibility is bounded to a family, surface, artifact type, transition, or evidence role.
2. **Artifact specificity.** It consumes particular already-present artifacts and emits particular bounded artifacts or lawful stops.
3. **Movement specificity.** It may perform a named transition: categorize, observe structure, translate, recognize observation, allow reliance, admit/promote, preserve history, project current lawful condition, preserve roadmap pressure, or answer a bounded inquiry.
4. **Warrant dependence.** Its movement is lawful only when evidence, provenance/support, role, authority limit, negative authority, unknowns, and confidence limits authorize downstream reliance.
5. **Non-promotion discipline.** It preserves what the artifact is not: representation is not observation; observation is not truth; evidence is not fact; candidate is not verified; support is not admission; projection is not source-of-truth; inquiry is not implementation authority.
6. **Negative authority.** It is defined as much by refused movements as by permitted movements.
7. **Neighbor awareness without absorption.** Neighboring responsibilities recur, but the local responsibility does not own them by adjacency.
8. **Stop authority.** If artifact, warrant, local competence, or target authority is missing, the lawful movement is preservation, rejection, unknown, non-promotion, or explicit stop.
9. **No implementation inference.** Constitutional locality does not imply class ownership, runtime component ownership, scheduler, planner, dispatcher, registry, lifecycle manager, or autonomous actor.

### Responsibility versus competency

Repository evidence keeps responsibility and competency orthogonal.

A responsibility family is an ownership/boundary-projection concept: it is supported by recoverable local boundaries, owner handoffs, implementation-backed slices, compatibility preservation, natural completion, and local transition seams.

A competency is recurring bounded work and discipline across owners: evidence consumption, provenance preservation, authority declaration, termination before unsupported downstream authority, repeated invariants, and cross-family recurrence.

Therefore:

```text
responsibility != competency
```

A local responsibility may exercise a competency, and a competency may recur across multiple responsibilities, but recurring competency discipline does not define one local responsibility unless a local boundary and authorized movement are independently evidenced.

### Responsibility versus inquiry

Inquiry is not direct responsibility recovery. Current implementation-backed answerability uses exact `QuestionFamily` inventory rows, bounded ask eligibility, authority-boundary fields, dispatch maps, required args, and existing answer surfaces. Grammar observations and responsibility-like records do not become inquiry subjects by themselves.

Therefore:

```text
responsibility != inquiry
```

A local responsibility can become answerable only through an implementation-backed inquiry representation or a bounded documentation investigation. The constitutional characterization of local responsibility does not create a runtime inquiry object.
