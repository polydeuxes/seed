## Topology reconciliation

### Recurring neighboring positions

The recurring topology surrounding continued lawful use of previously supported conclusions contains these neighboring constitutional positions:

1. **Evidence / observation / provider material** — source-attributed material enters only through bounded observation, provider translation, imported report, operator statement, or other supported intake paths.
2. **Support preservation** — evidence, provenance, source, confidence, time, dimensions, intermediate conclusions, and support paths constrain later use.
3. **Warrant** — preserved support gives bounded entitlement and limit for lawful reliance.
4. **Reliance** — downstream use consumes warranted content only for the supported role and scope.
5. **Current lawful condition** — present bounded status determines whether prior support is currently enough, insufficient, unknown, contradicted, or stopped.
6. **Derivation / reasoning path** — conclusions may depend on intermediate support chains that must remain traceable enough to constrain use.
7. **Projection / read view** — derived views expose current/supporting material but are not source truth.
8. **Temporal provenance** — occurrence, sample, observation, claim, projection, and read times remain distinct where evidence supports that distinction.
9. **Unknown preservation** — unresolved boundaries remain part of the reliance boundary.
10. **Contradiction existence/discovery/visibility/explanation/resolution** — incompatibility can challenge or qualify continued use without erasing history.
11. **Lawful movement / lawful stop** — changed or insufficient support may permit bounded transition or require refusal/stop.

### Recurring streets

The recurring streets are not implementation mechanisms. They are constitutional paths repeatedly visible in repository evidence:

- **Evidence street:** material may move from observation/provider/intake into evidence and support only under provenance and authority boundaries.
- **Warrant street:** support may authorize bounded reliance only when evidence, authority, negative authority, unknowns, and confidence limits remain preserved.
- **Current-condition street:** present bounded status mediates whether prior support remains usable, insufficient, contradicted, unknown, or stopped.
- **Derivation street:** derived conclusions remain lawful only through preserved support chains and reasoning-path visibility sufficient for the claim's role.
- **Projection street:** projections and read views expose derived current views while remaining challengeable by history/support and not source truth.
- **Temporal-provenance street:** timestamped material travels as provenance and boundary, not as automatic truth, expiration, or invalidation.
- **Contradiction street:** incompatible support paths can move through existence, discovery, visibility, explanation, and optional resolution without erasing preserved history.
- **Unknown/stop street:** insufficient support, missing authority, unresolved contradiction, or unpreserved provenance lawfully preserves unknown or stop rather than forcing continued use.
