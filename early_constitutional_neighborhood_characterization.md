## Recurring lawful transitions

The lawful transitions recovered at characterization resolution are weak, local, and bounded:

- **Null may remain Null.** Continued non-commitment is lawful when evidence is absent, insufficient, outside authority, or unsupported.
- **Null may encounter bounded presence.** A bounded external representation may become present to an existing local responsibility without becoming observation, evidence, fact, knowledge, repository attachment, or inquiry graph creation.
- **Bounded presence may supply evidence-bearing material.** Observation-level or preserved material can orient later assessment only within provenance and authority limits.
- **Evidence may support warrant.** Warrant can establish lawful reliance limits, confidence, negative authority, and unknown preservation without causing promotion.
- **Evidence plus competent local boundary may support movement.** Promotion / admission may move a bounded subject only through the appropriate family-local boundary.
- **Current lawful condition may be stated.** A present bounded status may become available, including supported, insufficient, unknown, Null, frontier-only, selected, complete, or stopped.
- **Selection may occur locally.** Exact bounded targets or families may be selected, refused, or preserved as non-selected only where local evidence and eligibility support that selection.
- **History may preserve placement.** Preserved material may enter constitutional history with provenance and lineage, without becoming truth or authority by accumulation.
- **Stop may be the correct transition.** Unsupported continuation, unsupported promotion, unsupported interpretation, missing authority, insufficient evidence, or fabricated ownership require stop, refusal, unknown, or non-promotion.
- **Handoff may occur only with boundaries preserved.** A bounded artifact may cross a road only with sender, receiver, artifact, warrant, and non-crossing limits intact.

These transitions do not compose into a global phase model, startup sequence, runtime control model, or canonical sequence. Their ordering is lawful because later claims would overclaim without the required bounded subject, presence, evidence, warrant, authority boundary, or stop condition.
