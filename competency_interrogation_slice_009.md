## Supported answer

According to recurring repository evidence, **Surveyor** is a recoverable implementation-local competency whose lawful ownership is bounded evidence mapping before recovery.

In bounded form:

```text
Surveyor owns evidence-bounded architectural surveying:
identifying visible neighborhoods, districts, bridges, pressures, inputs, outputs,
adjacencies, maturity, confidence, and preserved unknowns from recurring repository evidence,
then stopping before ownership recovery, topology recovery, bridge recovery,
visibility implementation, implementation recommendation, crew installation,
or scout discovery.
```

The Surveyor is therefore not an implementation workflow owner. It is the recurring competency that surveys what is visible enough to orient later work without converting visibility into ownership, topology, bridge characterization, or recommendations.

Recurring repository evidence supports Surveyor as a bounded competency because survey-like artifacts repeatedly perform the same work and repeatedly refuse the same overreach:

- `architectural_city_survey.md` surveys the city at city resolution, identifies visible districts, visible citizens, visible infrastructure, and unknown districts, and explicitly refuses implementation recommendations, campaigns, slices, ownership recovery, district merges, new frameworks, and taxonomy promotion.

- `typed_unknown_preservation_neighborhood_survey.md` surveys an implementation-local neighborhood by identity, evidence, inputs, outputs, internal physiology, neighboring neighborhoods, bridges visible as distinctions, maturity, remaining pressure, preserved unknowns, and confidence; it stops before unknown resolution, methodology, planning, recommendation, or public schema expansion.
- `competency_interrogation_bridge_inspection.md` inspects bridge pressure made visible by competency interrogation grammar, but preserves candidate bridges as hypotheses or adjacencies rather than characterizing bridge responsibilities.
- `constitutional_permission_bridge_investigation.md` distinguishes recurring bridge pressure from a stabilized first-class bridge implementation, refusing planning, execution, routing, recommendation, behavior selection, or proof of a named implementation object.
- `constitutional_grammar_recovery_discipline_characterization.md` states the recurring discipline for grammar recovery: observe recurrence, bind it to evidence and authority, recover only the warranted distinction, refuse unsupported promotion, characterize, preserve unknowns, and stop. Surveyor participates in the earlier evidence-review and recurrence-mapping part of that discipline, but does not own the later recovery result.
- `constitutional_growth_sequence_characterization.md` places observation / repository evidence review and recurring evidence under authority boundary before recovered distinction and characterization. Surveyor owns that pre-recovery survey movement, not the recovered distinction itself.
- `constitutional_pressure_backlog.md` identifies Constitutional Survey Pressure as pressure around surveys, audits, inventories, recurring evidence review, district scans, topology review, and characterization under authority boundary while refusing to convert pressure into implementation work, prioritization, roadmap generation, owners, schemas, runtime mechanisms, registries, diagnostics, or planning surfaces.
- Recent competency interrogations, including `competency_interrogation_007.md` and `competency_interrogation_slice_008.md`, show a different recovery mode: when the repository recovers ownership, it names producer, artifact/helper, consumer, compatibility, and remaining compressed responsibilities. Survey artifacts generally stop before that owner-recovery move.

The recurring difference is visible enough to recover one competency boundary: surveying maps repository-supported visibility and pressure before recovery; it does not itself recover implementation ownership or recommend implementation movement.

## Before

Before this interrogation, recurring repository artifacts showed survey-like behavior across district surveys, neighborhood surveys, bridge inspections, pressure backlog characterization, topology investigations, and recent characterization reports, but **Surveyor** was not named as a bounded competency.

The compressed responsibilities were:

1. surveying visible evidence, neighborhoods, districts, adjacencies, maturity, pressure, confidence, and unknowns;
2. recovering ownership or implementation-local responsibility boundaries;
3. recovering topology or bridge responsibilities;
4. recommending slices, campaigns, implementation movement, or installation work.

Because those activities often appear near each other in repository reports, Surveyor could be mistaken for characterization, topology recovery, bridge recovery, visibility, implementation recommendation, Crew installation, or Scout discovery.
