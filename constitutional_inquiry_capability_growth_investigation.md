## Comparison

Only after the independent inspections above does comparison become admissible.

### Recurring inquiry capability growth

Recurring evidence supports a bounded pattern:

```text
completed constitutional recovery
+ repeated evidence discipline
+ explicit authority boundary
+ preserved unknowns and stop condition
+ non-implementation boundary
    -> a new class of constitutional question may become admissible
```

This is visible in Question Families, question-to-inquiry transition discipline, Inquiry Orientation, Book of Law readiness, Law Projection readiness, and readiness/frontier characterization. In each case, the repository did not merely add vocabulary. It became able to answer a question that was previously unsafe, unsupported, or too broad, because prior recoveries supplied enough evidence, boundaries, prerequisites, and refusal conditions.

The supported growth is therefore not growth by owner creation, implementation creation, or architecture expansion. It is growth by admissibility: new bounded constitutional questions can be asked and answered over already recovered evidence.

### Recurring readiness patterns

Recurring readiness patterns are:

1. A candidate question appears through pressure, recurrence, vocabulary, prior unknowns, or completed recovery.
2. Repository evidence checks whether the candidate is exact enough to answer.
3. Prior recoveries provide evidence classes, authority limits, provenance, contradiction visibility, unknowns, and stop conditions.
4. App-visible or implementation-visible evidence is used only within its authority boundary.
5. The result may be a bounded inquiry capability or readiness conclusion, not a new implementation.
6. The answer preserves what remains unsupported.
