## Stage 1 -- Independent testimony

Each candidate discipline testifies independently. This stage does not compare disciplines or infer a family.

### Bounded investigation

- **Constitutional purpose:** Bound pressure to a specific subject, question, evidence neighborhood, and lawful stop condition so inquiry does not become universal recovery or unsupported movement.
- **Authority granted:** Authority to inspect competent repository evidence inside the bounded subject and preserve what the repository supports, refuses, or leaves Unknown.
- **Authority refused:** No truth creation, implementation recovery beyond the task, universal methodology, framework, scheduler, planner, registry, or authority to treat pressure as conclusion.
- **Upstream dependency:** Pressure, question, mismatch, frontier, or insufficiency must be present enough to bound the investigation.
- **Downstream dependency:** Repository evidence, independent testimony, cross-examination, sufficiency, warrant, reliance, admissibility, stop, or preserved Unknown may depend on the bounded investigation for subject boundary.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Whether all adjacent evidence was exhausted; whether a different bounded investigation would recover a different neighbor; whether the bounded subject is complete outside reviewed evidence.
- **Confidence:** High that bounded investigation recurs as an upstream admission discipline; medium that every instance has the same local shape.

### Repository evidence

- **Constitutional purpose:** Supply competent, inspectable repository support for a bounded conclusion, refusal, dependency, compatibility limit, Unknown, confidence calibration, or stop.
- **Authority granted:** Authority to support only the conclusion or boundary the evidence can competently bear.
- **Authority refused:** No authority from repetition alone, elegance, intuition, investigator confidence, presentation vocabulary, operational visibility, or report volume without competent support.
- **Upstream dependency:** A bounded subject or question that determines what evidence is competent.
- **Downstream dependency:** Independent testimony, cross-examination, sufficiency, warrant, lawful reliance, admissibility, and lawful stop all depend on repository evidence.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Whether additional evidence exists outside the reviewed scope; whether evidence is sufficient for broader generalization; whether evidence belongs to another district.
- **Confidence:** High.

### Independent testimony

- **Constitutional purpose:** Preserve each source, discipline, or evidence-bearing neighbor in its own authority before comparison or reconciliation changes its posture.
- **Authority granted:** Authority to state local purpose, support, limits, refusals, Unknowns, and confidence for that source or discipline.
- **Authority refused:** No comparison, merger, family inference, hierarchy, admission result, or downstream movement by itself.
- **Upstream dependency:** Bounded investigation and repository evidence sufficient to let the witness testify locally.
- **Downstream dependency:** Cross-examination, reconciliation, sufficiency, warrant, Unknown preservation, confidence calibration, and lawful stop depend on testimony that has not been prematurely collapsed.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Whether later cross-examination will find recurrence; whether testimony conflicts; whether the witness belongs to a family.
- **Confidence:** High.

### Cross-examination

- **Constitutional purpose:** Test preserved testimony against recurring ordering, authority changes, admission boundaries, stop boundaries, dependencies, compatibility, refusals, and Unknowns.
- **Authority granted:** Authority to recover the smallest recurring relation supported by independently preserved testimony.
- **Authority refused:** No authority to invent a family, redesign districts, make analogy sufficient, erase negative testimony, or authorize implementation.
- **Upstream dependency:** Independent testimony must already exist.
- **Downstream dependency:** Constitutional sufficiency, admissibility, warrant, reliance, reconciliation, lawful stop, or preserved Unknown may depend on cross-examined relations.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Whether recurrence is local only; whether ordering is mandatory or only recurring in reviewed evidence; whether some neighbors remain parallel rather than familial.
- **Confidence:** High for its boundary after testimony; medium-high for any recovered family relation because negative testimony must remain preserved.

### Reconciliation

- **Constitutional purpose:** Preserve both sides of a comparison while determining whether evidence supports agreement, conflict, boundary, non-equivalence, or unresolved status.
- **Authority granted:** Authority to state evidence-backed reconciliation outcomes and preserve mismatches or non-promotion.
- **Authority refused:** No universal merger, no semantic engine, no implementation mandate, no erasure of independent authority, and no movement beyond the reconciled boundary.
- **Upstream dependency:** Independently preserved sides and a comparison question.
- **Downstream dependency:** Sufficiency, warrant, reliance, admissibility, compatibility, stop, or preserved Unknown may use reconciliation outcomes as bounded evidence.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Whether unresolved sides can later be reconciled; whether reconciliation is local; whether a conflict belongs to a different family.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high. Reconciliation is adjacent to admission but is not always an admission gate.

### Constitutional Sufficiency

- **Constitutional purpose:** Decide whether a bounded constitutional conclusion has been lawfully earned by competent repository evidence under preserved provenance, authority boundary, negative authority, compatibility limits, Unknowns, confidence calibration, and lawful stop.
- **Authority granted:** Authority to carry the bounded conclusion as lawfully admissible for only the next repository-supported movement or stop.
- **Authority refused:** No truth, probability, universal epistemology, standards-of-proof framework, evidence engine, confidence engine, workflow, registry, implementation mandate, mutation, scheduling, global ownership, domain completion, or unsupported promotion.
- **Upstream dependency:** Bounded investigation, repository evidence, and usually independently preserved testimony tested through cross-examination or equivalent authority-bound inspection.
- **Downstream dependency:** Warrant, lawful reliance, admissibility, authorized bounded conclusion, next admissible movement, non-promotion, preserved Unknown, or lawful stop may depend on sufficiency.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Whether broader generalization is supported; whether adjacent movement is authorized; whether a different discipline owns downstream reliance.
- **Confidence:** High that sufficiency recurs; high that its unique admission responsibility is bounded earnedness of a conclusion; medium-high that it is one gate inside a larger admission family.

### Warrant

- **Constitutional purpose:** Preserve evidence-bound lawful reliance under authority boundary so bounded downstream use can occur without converting evidence into universal truth or implementation authority.
- **Authority granted:** Authority for bounded downstream reliance where support, provenance, role/locality, authority boundary, negative authority, Unknowns, and confidence limits remain preserved.
- **Authority refused:** No runtime warrant object, schema, governance mechanism, universal owner, semantic framework, implementation mechanism, formal logic system, translation engine, or generic grammar engine.
- **Upstream dependency:** A sufficient bounded conclusion or evidence-backed support with preserved authority limits.
- **Downstream dependency:** Lawful reliance and next admissible movement depend on warrant when reliance is claimed.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Whether warrant is global or local in every district; whether future artifacts require different warrant carriers; whether warrant alone can authorize any movement without sufficiency.
- **Confidence:** High for recurring warrant as lawful reliance support; bounded for universal scope.

### Lawful reliance

- **Constitutional purpose:** Permit bounded downstream use of a repository-supported conclusion or artifact while preserving provenance, authority boundary, compatibility, Unknowns, and non-mutation.
- **Authority granted:** Authority to rely on a warranted conclusion for the specific downstream purpose supported by evidence.
- **Authority refused:** No truth promotion, mutation, implementation command, ownership transfer, broader generalization, or reliance outside the warrant boundary.
- **Upstream dependency:** Warrant and sufficiency, or another competent admission boundary, must establish that reliance is lawful.
- **Downstream dependency:** Next admissible movement may depend on lawful reliance; stop may result where reliance is not lawful.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Whether reliance remains lawful after context changes; whether downstream use changes the authority boundary; whether reliance is artifact-local or family-local.
- **Confidence:** High for bounded reliance; medium for any claim that reliance is always downstream of sufficiency in the same way.

### Admissibility

- **Constitutional purpose:** Gate whether a candidate, conclusion, testimony, relation, or movement may participate in the next bounded constitutional act.
- **Authority granted:** Authority to admit the supported object only into the bounded downstream context for which repository evidence qualifies it.
- **Authority refused:** No universal admission service, no global gateway, no semantic owner, no automatic promotion from candidate to truth, no implementation mandate, and no participation outside local owner boundaries.
- **Upstream dependency:** Bounded evidence, testimony, cross-examination, sufficiency, warrant, or local owner-specific checks depending on what is being admitted.
- **Downstream dependency:** Authorized bounded conclusion, lawful reliance, next admissible movement, or stop depends on admissibility where a gate is required.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Whether admissibility is one discipline or a relation among local gates; whether every neighbor belongs to the same family; whether some admissions are owner-specific and not constitutional-family-wide.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high. The evidence supports recurring admission gates, but refuses a universal admission service.

### Lawful stop

- **Constitutional purpose:** Preserve a supported end, non-promotion, Null, Unknown, unsupported condition, completion, or frontier boundary when evidence does not authorize further movement.
- **Authority granted:** Authority to stop, preserve Unknown, refuse promotion, or end the bounded investigation without treating insufficiency as failure.
- **Authority refused:** No forced continuation, implementation, recommendation, truth completion, movement by pressure, or silent resolution of gaps.
- **Upstream dependency:** Evidence, sufficiency assessment, Unknown preservation, boundary mismatch, completion, or unsupported status.
- **Downstream dependency:** Later inquiry may depend on the preserved stop boundary; no movement is authorized unless a new bounded investigation supplies evidence.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Whether future evidence will reopen the subject; whether stop is local or family-complete; whether the stopped pressure belongs to another district.
- **Confidence:** High.

### Preserved Unknowns

- **Constitutional purpose:** Keep unresolved states visible as restraint, non-promotion, insufficiency, boundary gap, implementation unknown, relationship unknown, or future inquiry without converting them into fact, authority, or action.
- **Authority granted:** Authority to preserve what is not known and identify the lawful next inquiry or stop condition.
- **Authority refused:** No inferred answer, promotion, implementation, relationship, lifecycle model, runtime state, queue, scheduler, or family equivalence.
- **Upstream dependency:** Evidence gaps, insufficiency, boundary uncertainty, negative testimony, or incomplete authority.
- **Downstream dependency:** Sufficiency, warrant, lawful reliance, admissibility, and stop depend on Unknowns being preserved rather than silently resolved.

- **Confidence:** High for Unknown preservation as admission restraint; low for a unified Unknown ownership topology.

## Stage 2 -- Cross-examination

Only after independent testimony, the evidence supports a recurring admission relation. The smallest supported family is not a new district and not a universal service. It is a recurring constitutional admission family of bounded disciplines that decide whether pressure, testimony, evidence, conclusions, reliance, movement, Unknowns, or stop may lawfully pass into the next bounded constitutional posture.

### Recurring ordering

The strongest recurring order is:

```text
Pressure or question
        ↓
Bounded investigation
        ↓
Repository evidence
        ↓
Independent testimony
        ↓
Cross-examination / reconciliation where comparison is required
        ↓
Constitutional sufficiency
        ↓
Warrant and lawful reliance where downstream use is claimed
        ↓
Admissibility of only the bounded conclusion, movement, Unknown, or stop
        ↓
Next admissible movement or lawful stop
```

This ordering is recurring, not a workflow, scheduler, planner, method, implementation sequence, or mandatory universal procedure. Some local gates may be owner-specific. Some disciplines remain parallel or optional where the repository evidence does not require comparison or downstream reliance.

### Recurring authority changes

- Pressure becomes investigable only after it is bounded.
- Evidence becomes constitutionally usable only inside competent authority boundaries.
- Testimony becomes comparable only after it is independently preserved.
- Comparison becomes admission-relevant only when cross-examination or reconciliation preserves both sides and their negative authority.
- A conclusion becomes lawfully earned only at Constitutional Sufficiency.
- A sufficient conclusion becomes reliably usable only under warrant and lawful reliance boundaries.
- Movement becomes admissible only when the admitted object authorizes the next bounded movement.
- Insufficiency, unresolved conflict, unsupported vocabulary, or boundary gaps become lawful stop or preserved Unknown, not failure.

### Recurring admission boundaries

The family admits only bounded, evidence-supported constitutional posture. It admits a conclusion, relation, reliance, movement, Unknown, or stop only to the exact downstream context supported by repository evidence. It refuses global promotion, universal admission authority, implementation recovery, methodology, proof systems, legal frameworks, registries, planners, schedulers, and new constitutional participants.

### Recurring stop boundaries

The family stops when evidence ends, testimony is not independently preserved, comparison would collapse witnesses, cross-examination does not support recurrence, sufficiency is not met, warrant is absent, reliance would exceed authority, admissibility is local but unsupported, Unknowns remain unresolved, or movement would redesign districts.

- Independent testimony depends on evidence and supplies uncrossed local authority.
- Cross-examination depends on independent testimony.

Repository evidence also refuses these overclaims:

- **Sufficiency owns the family:** Refused. Sufficiency owns only earnedness of a bounded conclusion; testimony, cross-examination, warrant, reliance, admissibility, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop have distinct responsibilities.
- **All candidate disciplines belong automatically:** Refused. Recurrence must be shown for each neighbor; reconciliation and admissibility remain partly local and boundary-specific.
- **Ordering is mandatory workflow:** Refused. The ordering recurs as constitutional relation, not procedure or scheduler.
- **Admission equals truth:** Refused. Admission authorizes bounded participation, reliance, movement, Unknown, or stop; it does not create universal truth.

- **Unknowns are defects to be resolved silently:** Refused. Unknowns are preserved admission restraints.
- **Presentation vocabulary is knowledge:** Refused. Vocabulary does not become repository knowledge without competent evidence.
- **Local owner gates collapse into one gateway:** Refused. The repository repeatedly preserves local owner-specific admission boundaries.
