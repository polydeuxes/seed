## Stage 2 — Cross-examination

### Recurring evidence requirements

A constitutional conclusion becomes sufficiently established only when repository evidence supplies:

1. a bounded subject or question;
2. recurring or direct repository evidence from competent surfaces;
3. provenance/support adequate to inspect why the conclusion is lawful;
4. the authority boundary within which the conclusion may be used;
5. selected bounded content rather than all surrounding vocabulary;
6. negative authority stating what the conclusion does not become;
7. preserved Unknowns and unsupported conclusions;
8. compatibility boundaries where public surfaces, prior conclusions, or adjacent responsibilities must remain unchanged;
9. confidence or evidence-strength calibration where the source carries it;
10. stop or non-promotion when evidence ends.

### Recurring stopping requirements

Stopping is recurring and lawful when evidence no longer exposes a supported adjacent movement, when remaining movement would only wrap or relocate already-owned responsibility, when a subject is unsupported or outside authority, when only presentation vocabulary exists, or when the current condition is Null, unknown, insufficient, unsupported, complete, or frontier-only.

### Recurring compatibility requirements

Compatibility requires preserving public surfaces, read-only boundaries, prior behavior, provenance-bearing handoff, non-mutation, non-truth-promotion, and consumer/producer separation. Compatibility does not by itself prove ownership, truth, readiness, or next movement.

### Recurring Unknown preservation

Unknowns remain visible as evidence gaps, non-promotion status, unresolved ownership, missing implementation, unsupported vocabulary, absent lifecycle proof, incomplete compatibility proof, or insufficient present condition. They are not silently resolved by recurrence, elegance, vocabulary, or confidence.

### Recurring refusal preservation

Conclusions recur with explicit refusals: no truth promotion, no mutation, no universal owner, no framework, no engine, no runtime object, no scheduler, no global selector, no implementation mandate, no methodology independent of evidence, and no domain completion beyond the bounded scope.

### Recurring confidence discipline

Confidence is a boundary marker, not an authorizer. It calibrates reliance to evidence strength and often splits high confidence for local recurrence from low confidence for broader generalization. Investigator confidence never substitutes for evidence, authority boundary, compatibility, Unknown preservation, or stop discipline.

### Recurring authorization boundaries

Constitutional sufficiency authorizes only the next admissible movement named by the evidence: bounded reliance, bounded inquiry, local recovery, completion/stopping, compatibility-preserving handoff, or preserved Unknown. It does not authorize truth, probability, universal methodology, implementation, scheduling, mutation, global ownership, or stronger vocabulary.
