# Constitutional Public Evidence Packet Readiness Investigation

Repository authority wins.

## Bounded question

Across recurring repository evidence, when a human provides Seed with a curated packet of public-world materials, what must the packet expose so Seed can lawfully review it without treating source role as truth, source count as independence, volume as authority, visibility as fact, repetition as proof, confidence as certainty, narrative as history, or operator motivation as evidence?

This document performs exactly one bounded constitutional investigation: **public evidence packet readiness**. It does not implement anything, add a diagnostic surface, create a packet schema, create source scoring, perform legal research, fetch public-world evidence, evaluate any public-world actor, implement ingestion, or promote public-world source categories into ontology.

## Packet analysis

Repository evidence does not currently support a formal Seed public-world packet model. The smaller supported distinction is this:

```text
A human-provided public-world evidence packet is, at most, a bounded review artifact: a curated folder or bundle presented for possible constitutional review, before ingestion, before truth adjudication, before legal analysis, and before implementation pressure.
```

A packet may contain constitutional text, opinions, statutes, contracts, official statements, vendor claims, advocacy reports, journalism, expert commentary, background material, social media, and operator notes. Inclusion in the packet means only that the human put the material within the review boundary. It does not mean the contents are true, authoritative, independent, legally controlling, primary evidence, current, complete, or relevant to implementation.

## Packet-versus-ingestion analysis

A packet differs from ingestion because ingestion is a Seed-side admission or translation path that can create observations, evidence, facts, events, projected state, or other runtime artifacts under implementation-owned authority. Repository evidence around canonical observations shows ingestion-facing structures with subject, predicate, value, source type, observed time, metadata, dimensions, and confidence; observation ingestion can convert observations into provenance evidence and observed or inferred fact events.

A packet, by contrast, is pre-ingestion review material. Lawful packet review may inspect what is present, what provenance is exposed, and what limits must remain. It must not create cluster truth, event-ledger fact truth, projected knowledge, source authority, legal conclusions, or implementation work unless a separate repository-supported boundary admits a narrower artifact for that role.

## Packet-versus-evidence analysis

A packet is not evidence as a whole. A packet may contain candidate materials that could later become bounded evidence for specific claim forms, but only if the material's provenance, source role, authority boundary, temporal provenance, support path, and confidence limits are preserved enough for lawful reliance.

Repository warrant evidence requires evidence binding, preserved authority boundary, provenance/support preservation, selected bounded content, negative authority, Unknown preservation, confidence calibration, and stop discipline. Therefore `packet contains X` is not the same claim as `X supports Y`, and neither is the same claim as `Y is true`.

## Operator-curation analysis

Operator curation is review pressure, not evidence. It can identify the folder, explain why the human assembled it, and bound the requested review. It cannot supply source authority, source independence, legal authority, currentness, factual truth, or proof of harm. Operator motivation may be preserved as operator context or selection rationale, but it must not become evidence for public-world claims.

## Source-role analysis

The external source-role investigation supports source roles only as **warrant-bounding provenance roles**. A role such as official statement, reported claim, primary account, secondary analysis, contextual background, corroborating source, or contradictory source can constrain how a material may be considered. It does not establish that the material is true.

Packet readiness therefore requires source role exposure where available, plus explicit Unknown where missing. The role must travel with negative authority: role is not truth, role is not legal authority for all claims, role is not independence, role is not confidence, and role is not ingestion permission.

## Source-independence analysis

The repetition/proof investigation distinguishes independent corroboration from copied repetition. Source independence requires visible reason to treat support paths as distinct rather than copied, circular, syndicated, derivative, or merely fluent repetition. Useful indicators include distinct source classes, preserved provenance, non-circular support paths, local authority for the claim, contradiction review, and claim-strength matching.

Packet readiness therefore requires an independence basis when the packet asks Seed to treat multiple materials as support. A count of files, pages, reports, posts, citations, or repeated statements is not source independence.

## Source-authority analysis

Source authority is source-local and claim-bounded. An office may have authority to say what it said. A court may have authority to issue an opinion. A statute may be enacted text. A contract may record terms between parties. None of these automatically becomes general truth authority, Seed implementation authority, historical completeness, or proof of every factual assertion inside the document.

Packet readiness requires exposing the claimed authority relation: who authored or issued the material, in what capacity, for what claim form, under what date/version, and with what limits. Missing authority remains Unknown.

## Background-material analysis

Wikipedia and other background material can orient review, define vocabulary as used by a source, point toward leads, or provide context. Repository evidence rejects treating presentation vocabulary or visible material as knowledge merely because it appears. Background material is not primary evidence unless a separate bounded claim concerns the background material itself, such as `this page said this at this preserved time`.

## Legal-source analysis

Legal text, court opinions, statutes, regulations, and contracts have source-local or domain-local authority for bounded legal artifacts: text existed, an issuing body spoke, a contract says particular words, or a court published an opinion. This investigation does not perform legal research. Repository evidence supports the boundary that legal source status is not general truth authority about all factual, historical, technical, vendor, or social claims contained in or surrounding the document.

For lawful review, such materials must expose jurisdiction or issuing body where available, version/date, citation or artifact identity, provenance, and the bounded legal-text role being claimed. Missing legal provenance remains Unknown, and Seed must stop before legal analysis.

## Vendor-claim analysis

Vendor claims are evidence of vendor representation when provenance is preserved. They are not proof of product behavior, deployment, performance, legality, accuracy, or harm. A vendor statement can support the bounded claim `the vendor claimed X at time/context Y` more readily than `X is true in the world`.

Packet readiness requires separating representation claims from behavior claims and preserving temporal provenance, artifact identity, and any independent support path for product behavior.

## Advocacy-report analysis

Advocacy reports may be evidence of what an advocacy organization asserted, collected, interpreted, or emphasized. They are not automatically proof of harm, causation, prevalence, legality, technical operation, or affected-party experience. They may contain primary materials, interviews, public records, analysis, or narrative. Each component requires its own role, provenance, support path, and confidence limit.

## Journalism analysis

Journalism may be evidence that a publication reported a claim, quoted a person, summarized records, or performed analysis. It is not primary evidence for every reported fact. It can sometimes provide a support path to primary records, named interviews, dates, or contradictions, but the reporting itself must remain attributed unless independently supported.

## Social-media repetition analysis

Social media repetition is not independent support. Posts, reposts, screenshots, quote-posts, summaries, and viral repetition can show visibility, spread, narrative salience, or repeated assertion. They do not prove the underlying claim and do not establish independence without provenance showing distinct firsthand sources or distinct support paths.

## Provenance / support-path analysis

For lawful review, a packet must expose enough provenance and support-path information to prevent overpromotion. The repository evidence supports these readiness elements as investigation requirements, not as a new schema:

- packet boundary: what folder or bundle is under review;
- material identity: filename, title, URL or citation if present, stable identifier if available, and preservation source;
- source identity: author, publisher, issuer, speaker, quoted actor, or Unknown;
- source role: official statement, reported claim, primary account, secondary analysis, background, operator note, or Unknown;
- source authority: claimed authority for this material and this claim form, or Unknown;
- claim form: what kind of claim the material could support, such as `source said X`, `document contains text X`, `actor represented X`, `record appears to show X`, `analysis argues X`, or Unknown;
- support path: whether the material directly supports the bounded claim, links to another source, quotes another source, copies another source, summarizes another source, or Unknown;
- independence basis: why support paths are independent, dependent, circular, copied, syndicated, same-origin, or Unknown;
- contradiction and counter-support markers where present;
- confidence or evidence-strength limits if the source supplies them, plus Seed-side refusal to convert confidence into certainty;
- negative authority: what the material must not become;
- review purpose and lawful stop condition.

These are readiness exposures. They are not a packet schema and do not create implementation pressure.

## Temporal-provenance analysis

The temporal authority audit states the relevant boundary: a timestamp is not self-authorizing. Packet review must distinguish occurrence time, observation time, knowledge time, publication time, retrieval time, preservation time, revision time, and operator-curation time where available. If a material says an event happened on one date, was published on another date, retrieved on another date, and added to the packet later, those are different temporal claims with different authority.

Missing or ambiguous temporal provenance remains Unknown. Date volume or repeated timestamps do not prove occurrence.

## Claim-form analysis

Claim form controls lawful reliance. A source may support `the source asserted X` without supporting `X happened`. A court opinion may support `the opinion states X` without supporting general truth of X. A contract may support `the contract contains term X` without proving performance. A news article may support `the article reported X` without proving the reported event. A social-media post may support `the account posted X` without proving X.

Packet readiness requires preserving this form before review. If claim form is missing, Seed must not infer the strongest claim.

## Confidence / evidence-strength analysis

Confidence is not certainty. Repository evidence around warrant and repetition supports calibrated claim strength: evidence may be weak, bounded, source-attributed, contradicted, stale, or sufficient only for a narrower claim. Packet readiness must expose confidence or evidence-strength only when source material or repository-bound review can preserve it. Otherwise confidence remains Unknown rather than being invented from polish, volume, official tone, legal formality, or operator confidence.

## Unknown preservation

The following must remain Unknown when not exposed by packet materials or repository evidence:

- whether Seed has a public-world packet model;
- source role;
- source authority;
- source identity;
- artifact identity;
- temporal provenance;
- claim form;
- support path;
- source independence;
- whether repeated materials are copied, circular, syndicated, same-origin, or independent;
- whether background materials point to primary materials;
- whether legal sources are current, controlling, interpreted correctly, or relevant;
- whether vendor claims reflect product behavior;
- whether advocacy reports prove harm;
- whether journalism proves reported facts;
- whether social-media repetition independently corroborates anything;
- confidence or evidence strength;
- admissibility for ingestion, legal analysis, fact projection, or implementation pressure.

Unknown preservation is a lawful result, not a failure.

## Lawful stop

Lawful packet review stops when review would require any of the following without separate repository-supported authority:

- ingesting packet contents into Seed observations, facts, events, projected state, or ontology;
- adjudicating public-world truth;
- performing legal research or legal analysis;
- treating source role as truth;
- treating source count as independence;
- treating volume as authority;
- treating visibility as fact;
- treating repetition as proof;
- treating confidence as certainty;
- treating narrative as history;
- treating operator motivation as evidence;
- creating source, authority, credibility, corroboration, artifact, truth, or legal scoring;
- recommending implementation work absent a concrete repository failure.

## Supported conclusions

1. Repository evidence supports no formal public-world packet model yet. It supports a smaller pre-ingestion review-artifact distinction.
2. A packet is not ingestion, not evidence as a whole, not authority, not proof, and not implementation pressure.
3. Packet inclusion and operator curation are review boundary and selection context only.
4. Lawful review requires enough exposed provenance, source role, source authority, claim form, support path, temporal provenance, independence basis, confidence limits, negative authority, and Unknown preservation to prevent overpromotion.
5. Public-world source categories can be used only as warrant-bounding roles, not as ontology or truth hierarchy.
6. Wikipedia/background, legal sources, vendor claims, advocacy reports, journalism, and social media each require bounded claim-form separation.
7. Missing provenance, role, authority, temporal information, support path, independence, or confidence remains Unknown.
8. Lawful stop prevents review from becoming ingestion, truth adjudication, legal analysis, or implementation pressure.

## Unsupported conclusions

Repository evidence does not support conclusions that:

- Seed already has a public-world evidence packet schema;
- Seed should implement one now;
- packet inclusion is evidence;
- operator curation is authority;
- source role proves truth;
- source count proves independence;
- volume proves authority;
- legal sources are general truth authority;
- vendor claims prove product behavior;
- advocacy reports prove harm;
- journalism is primary evidence by default;
- social-media repetition is independent corroboration;
- public-world packet readiness should become an operational diagnostic, scoring system, truth engine, legal engine, or world-research workflow.

## Implementation recommendation

No implementation work is supported by this investigation. No concrete repository failure was found. The supported result is constitutional investigation pressure only: preserve the smaller packet-readiness distinction as a bounded review discipline and stop before schema, diagnostic, ingestion, scoring, legal-analysis, or implementation work.
