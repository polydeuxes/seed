# Constitutional Selection Topology Spymaster

## Boundary

This is exactly one bounded Spymaster investigation. It asks only whether recurring repository evidence supports a constitutional relationship topology around candidate, eligibility, selection, and registration in the neighborhood of constitutional selection.

It does not recover implementation, runtime behavior, ownership, scheduling, dispatch, hidden selection engines, implementation recommendations, or implementation slices. It does not promote the orientation sequence into repository architecture. Repository authority wins.

This investigation uses that output only as visibility into already registered public Question Family rows; it does not infer registration ownership or hidden admission machinery from the JSON shape.

### Question

According to recurring repository evidence, what selects Question Shape, Survey Warrant Family, and Question Family?

### Selection boundary observations

1. The three concepts are adjacent selection neighborhoods, not one selector under different names.
2. Question Shape is form/evidence-posture selection at inquiry resolution.
3. Survey Warrant Family is recurrence/warrant selection at investigation-artifact resolution.
4. Question Family is static public answerability selection at inventory/dispatch resolution.
5. The strongest supported relationship is not `Shape -> Warrant -> Family`; it is non-collapse with possible local participation:

```text
Question Shape may support Survey Warrant Family evidence.
Survey Warrant Family may authorize a bounded constitutional investigation artifact.
Question Family requires explicit registration for public answerability.
```

6. Repository evidence preserves a possible future QuestionFamily Admission question, but it remains candidate/future boundary evidence, not current registration machinery.

### Investigation B result

Selection is plural and boundary-specific. Recurring evidence supports separate selectors for ask form, investigation warrant, and public answerable family. No recurring evidence supports one hidden selector that takes candidate constitutional concepts and emits registered Question Families.

## Investigation C — Constitutional Progression

### Question

```text
Candidate
↓
Eligible
↓
Selected
↓
Registered
```

### Evidence tested independently

#### Candidate-like recurrence

Supported in multiple local senses:

- Inquiry Eligibility begins with a candidate inquiry or attractive architectural idea.
- Question Family Registration Boundary Audit discusses recovered-family candidates and non-promotional candidate/admission boundaries as possible future responsibility evidence.
- Bounded ask eligibility treats exact registered rows as selectable subjects and rejects unknown/unsupported rows rather than promoting candidates.
- Survey Warrant Recurrence treats candidate markers as unsupported when recurrence is vocabulary-only or when stronger conclusions would exceed evidence.

Boundary: `candidate` is not one repository-wide status. Candidate may mean an inquiry idea, a warrant marker, a recovered-family possibility, a non-selected alternative, or a bounded ask row under classification. Confidence: medium-high for local recurrence; low for one unified candidate status.

#### Eligibility-like recurrence

Strongly supported:

- Inquiry Eligibility characterizes repository observation, prior-work reload, evidence-strength checks, rejection/defer/open/unsupported outcomes, and narrow authorization before selection/path explanation.
- Bounded ask eligibility classifies exact Question Family rows by immediate eligibility, parameter-required eligibility, diagnostic-only status, not-dispatchable status, and unsupported status.
- Constitutional Condition Progression supports evidence/eligibility assessment as an authority constraint before supported selection or continuation.

Boundary: eligibility is distributed and local; current evidence does not support one implemented subsystem named `InquiryEligibility`. Confidence: high.

#### Selection-like recurrence

Supported but not uniform:

- Constitutional Selection Boundary Scout recovers separate selectors for Question Shape, Survey Warrant Family, and Question Family.
- Selection Path evidence, as summarized by Inquiry Eligibility, explains ordering among candidates once an implemented candidate set exists.
- Bounded ask uses exact Question Family selection followed by deterministic dispatch eligibility.
- Survey Warrant Family selection occurs through recurring evidence across four criteria.

Boundary: selection sometimes follows eligibility; sometimes consumes exact supplied identity; sometimes explains an already selected target; sometimes stops before positive selection. Confidence: high for recurrence, medium for fixed ordering.

#### Registration-like recurrence

Strongly supported only for Question Family public answerability:

- Question Family Registration Boundary Audit says a static implementation edit to `build_question_surface_inventory()` causes a new known QuestionFamily to exist, with bounded dispatch map entries where executable bounded ask participation is supported.
- The app-visible question-surface inventory reported exact registered rows and metadata.
- The same audit says no first-class recovered-family-to-registration responsibility is implemented.

Boundary: registration is not shown for Question Shape or Survey Warrant Family. It is not a universal terminal constitutional status. Confidence: high for QuestionFamily; low for general topology.

### Investigation C result

A recurring constitutional progression resembles only the first half of the orientation sequence. Candidate-to-eligible and eligibility-constrained selection recur strongly. Selected-to-registered is supported only for current QuestionFamily static registration as a terminal public-answerability fact, not as a recovered progression from selected candidate. The full Candidate → Eligible → Selected → Registered sequence is therefore partially supported as an investigative lens and unsupported as a universal constitutional topology.

## Cross Comparison

### Recurring agreements

- All three investigations agree that evidence-proportional eligibility or admission discipline is upstream of lawful selection authority.
- All three agree that unsupported cases are meaningful outcomes: unknown, unsupported, diagnostic-only, not-dispatchable, under-parameterized, insufficient-evidence, or stopped cases are not to be promoted.
- All three agree that selection boundaries are local and concept-specific.
- All three agree that QuestionFamily registration is explicit and static in current evidence, while Question Shape and Survey Warrant Family do not become registered Question Families by recurrence alone.
- All three agree that recurring vocabulary and structural recurrence do not themselves stabilize topology, ownership, implementation, or public answerability.

### Recurring contradictions

- The orientation sequence suggests a single linear progression; Investigation B contradicts this by recovering separate selectors for three neighboring concepts.
- The orientation sequence suggests registration as a natural terminal step; Investigation C contradicts universal registration by finding registration only strongly supported for QuestionFamily public answerability.
- Investigation A finds static registration required for QuestionFamily selection but optional/irrelevant for Question Shape and Survey Warrant Family, contradicting any claim that registration is a general constitutional precondition.
- Constitutional Condition Progression evidence supports real ordering constraints but contradicts the stronger claim that every lawful path traverses every proposed step in order.

### Recurring refinements

- `Candidate` should remain a local descriptive status unless repository evidence names the candidate domain: candidate inquiry, candidate recurrence marker, recovered-family candidate, non-selected alternative, or exact unregistered family candidate.
- `Eligible` is the strongest recurring bridge concept, but it is distributed: inquiry eligibility, bounded ask eligibility, evidence sufficiency, warrant-support criteria, and parameter/diagnostic eligibility are related without one owner.
- `Selected` should be read as local selection under a known boundary: ask-form selection, warrant-family selection, exact QuestionFamily invocation, selection-path explanation, current focus, or bounded continuation.
- `Registered` should be read narrowly as current QuestionFamily public-answerability registration unless future evidence explicitly broadens it.
- A more repository-faithful topology is not a line but a set of local gated transitions constrained by evidence and authority.

### Preserved unknowns

- Whether future repository work will recover a first-class QuestionFamily Admission or Registration responsibility remains unknown.
- Whether future evidence will stabilize `candidate`, `eligible`, `selected`, or `registered` as repository vocabulary remains unknown.
- Whether some recurring Question Shapes will later become registered Question Families remains unknown.
- Whether a stronger bounded-unknown → inquiry → evidence → handoff progression better subsumes this neighborhood remains unknown.
- Whether selection/orientation should remain one compressed neighborhood or split further between exact dispatch, read-only orientation, selection explanation, current work position, and answer composition remains unknown.
- Whether Survey Warrant Family vocabulary remains an investigation handle or becomes broader constitutional vocabulary remains unknown.

## Final answer

According to recurring repository evidence,

what recurring constitutional topology,

if any,

governs the transition between

candidate,

eligibility,

selection,

and

registration?

Recurring repository evidence supports a bounded, plural topology rather than one universal progression:

```text
candidate or attractive surface
↓
evidence-strength / authority-boundary / eligibility assessment
↓
unsupported, deferred, open, stopped, or narrowly authorized
↓
local selection only inside the boundary that has been earned
```

For current QuestionFamily public answerability, the topology then has a separate, narrow registration fact:

```text
explicit static QuestionFamily registration
↓
public answerable identity
↓
bounded ask eligibility, parameter boundary, diagnostic-only status, or refusal
```

The repository does not currently support a general constitutional chain in which every candidate becomes eligible, every eligible surface becomes selected, and every selected surface becomes registered. It supports recurring eligibility-constrained local selection, explicit stop/refusal for unsupported cases, and static QuestionFamily registration as a specific public-answerability boundary.

Spymaster investigation complete.
