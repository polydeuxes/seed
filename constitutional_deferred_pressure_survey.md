# Constitutional Deferred Pressure Survey

## Boundary

This is exactly one Constitutional Deferred Pressure Survey. It surveys only existing repository artifacts and app-visible documentation/inquiry surfaces. It does not inspect implementation, recover ownership, invent constitutional participants, invent constitutional districts, or promote presentation vocabulary into constitutional fact.

Repository authority wins. The listed pressures are investigation candidates only.

## App use

The app was used to orient the survey without interpreting prose as authority:

- `python -m scripts.seed_local --documentation-structure --summary-only` reported 577 documents, all with H1 headings, and a read-only boundary: document structure only; no prose interpretation, grammar interpretation, responsibility recovery, lexicon stabilization, claim extraction, authority inference, shape inference, event-ledger writes, or repository mutation.
- `python -m scripts.seed_local --inquiry-artifacts` reported inquiry artifact visibility as read-only, no recording, no event-ledger writes, no cluster mutation, no inquiry graph creation, no pressure transformation inference, and no workflow or planning behavior. It showed `unknown` and `boundary` as repository-visible, `pressure`, `finding`, and `gap` as partially visible, and `supported_conclusion`, `unsupported_conclusion`, and `open_question` as document-visible.

Therefore the survey treats repository documents as evidence-bearing artifacts, not as automatic constitutional truth.

## Survey method

For each deferred pressure, this survey asks only:

1. Is repository evidence sufficient to support constitutional investigation? Classification: Mature, Emerging, or Premature.
2. Is the pressure foundational, dependent on another pressure, or blocked by missing evidence?
3. Has repository evidence already partially recovered it under another name?
4. What constitutional artifacts currently support it?

Readiness means readiness for a bounded constitutional inquiry, not implementation recovery.

## Cross-pressure answers

### 5. Next constitutional inquiry

**Recommended next inquiry: Constitutional Question Grammar.**

Reason: it is mature, foundational, and directly governs how the repository can ask bounded questions about every other deferred pressure without recovering all of them. It also protects this survey's central discipline: investigation candidates must be admitted through question shape, evidence posture, authority boundary, and stop condition rather than historical momentum.

A suitable next inquiry would be bounded to:

```text
What recurring constitutional question grammar determines whether a deferred pressure may become a lawful constitutional inquiry, remain frontier pressure, or stop as unsupported?
```

### 6. Deferred pressure that unlocks the greatest future recovery

**Relationship Grammar** appears to unlock the greatest amount of future recovery.

Reason: recurring evidence shows many deferred pressures are relationship problems: observation-to-question, pressure-to-inquiry, evidence-to-warrant, artifact-to-consumer, external-grammar-to-local-grammar, family-to-adjacent-family, Unknown-to-resolution, and repository-memory-to-authority. Relationship Grammar can organize these without promoting any one pressure into a participant or implementation owner.

### 7. Least supported deferred pressure

**Survey Districts** is least supported.

Reason: the repository has district-scout files and locality/neighborhood/family evidence, but current evidence does not support `Survey Districts` as a constitutional phenomenon. The safer classification is that district is presently a report/navigation label unless future repository evidence proves otherwise.

### 8. Pressures that are different views of the same constitutional phenomenon

The survey groups the pressures as follows:

1. **Bounded inquiry admission and movement**
   - Constitutional Question Grammar
   - Questions vs Observations
   - Unknowns
   - Constitutional Resolution
   - Constitutional Pressure

   These are different views of how Null becomes observation, observation becomes bounded question, question becomes inquiry, evidence supports or refuses movement, and unresolved material remains Unknown.

2. **Relationship, warrant, and lawful reliance**
   - Relationship Grammar
   - Binding
   - Families
   - Constitutional Competencies

   These are different views of how bounded constitutional artifacts, responsibilities, competencies, and family-local pressures relate without collapsing into ownership, universal architecture, or unsupported authority.

3. **External expression, translation, and comparison**
   - External Grammar
   - The Eye
   - Hurricane

   These are different views of admission and comparison of external or non-native expression. External Grammar is the mature base; The Eye is an emerging orientation around recurring observation/translation/cross-examination responsibilities; Hurricane is an emerging conditions label for when those responsibilities might become inquiry-ready.

4. **Artifact persistence and locality**
   - Repository Memory
   - Survey Districts

   Repository Memory has emerging support as durable repository artifacts under authority limits. Survey Districts remains premature and should not be joined to Repository Memory except as an Unknown about locality labels.

## Highest-priority recommendation

Start the next constitutional inquiry with **Constitutional Question Grammar**, not The Eye or Hurricane.

The Eye and Hurricane are visible pressures, but their readiness depends on bounded question admission, relationship grammar, external grammar, independent testimony, and Unknown preservation. Beginning with The Eye or Hurricane risks turning orientation vocabulary into a constitutional participant. Beginning with Constitutional Question Grammar is more repository-faithful because it determines how such pressures may lawfully become inquiries or stop.
