## Independently surveyed transitions

Each transition is surveyed independently before comparison.

### 1. Unknown / communication / pressure to orientation

```text
Unknown / communication / pressure
↓
Orientation gate
↓
Bounded orientation material
```

**What authorizes movement?**

A preserved note, operator concern, communication, pressure item, or visible disturbance may lawfully orient attention when it is kept as orientation rather than truth. The transition-family characterization identifies `communication / observation / pressure -> orientation` as the smallest recurring constitutional transition.

**What refuses movement?**

Movement is refused when orientation would be promoted into fact, command, ownership, requirement, recommendation, next-safe-move authority, repository knowledge, implementation, or admission without further evidence.

**What remains preserved?**

The original pressure or note, its weak orientation status, absence of truth authority, absence of runtime authority, and any unresolved unknowns.

**Confidence.**

High for orientation as the smallest recurring transition gate; high that it is constitutionally weak.

### 2. Orientation to bounded inquiry

```text
Orientation
↓
Bounded inquiry admissibility gate
↓
Running bounded inquiry or lawful non-dispatch
```

**What authorizes movement?**

Exact Question Family identity, surface-local eligibility, required-argument satisfaction, map-backed selection, dispatch boundary, or a preserved inquiry note invoked by a read-only orientation surface. Question-to-inquiry evidence repeatedly shows that possible questions are not running inquiries until local boundaries admit them.

**What refuses movement?**

Unknown families, diagnostic-only surfaces, not-dispatchable surfaces, missing required arguments, unsupported semantic routing, free-form potential questions, planner-like enumeration, or automatic inquiry spawning refuse movement.

**What remains preserved?**

The orientation material, exact family identity when present, bounded status, required arguments, authority boundary, non-dispatch reason, and stop condition.

**Confidence.**

High for Question Family and bounded-ask surfaces; medium-high for broader inquiry admissibility because it is distributed rather than universal.

**Confidence.**

## Recurring transition gates

Recurring gates recovered across neighboring constitutional states:

1. **Orientation gate** — admits unknown, communication, observation, or pressure only as orientation.
2. **Bounded inquiry admissibility gate** — admits orientation or possible questions into running bounded inquiry only through local identity, eligibility, arguments, authority, and stop conditions.
3. **Evidence / stop gate** — admits bounded inquiry output only as supported evidence-bearing result or explicit lawful stop.
4. **Evidence admission gate** — admits observation into evidence only with provenance, confidence, source role, and competent boundary.
5. **Support / candidate gate** — admits evidence into support or candidate status without verification or admission.
6. **Family-local admissibility gate** — admits candidate/support into stronger status only under the relevant family's evidence, authority, compatibility, test, or projection rules.
7. **Projection gate** — admits preserved evidence, fact support, or history into current belief, read model, or bounded constitutional-reality projection without verification or mutation.
8. **Presentation / read-view gate** — admits projection into answer, diagnostic, explanation, report, or view while preserving read-only and non-truth boundaries.
9. **Record-scope / audit-scope gate** — admits rendered or diagnostic output back toward constitutional record only under explicit record/audit scope.
10. **Frontier stop/admissibility gate** — admits frontier pressure into bounded inquiry or preserves it as unknown.
11. **Implementation-readiness gate** — admits recovery into possible implementation only when a recurring, owned/corridor-bounded, necessary, testable boundary exists.
12. **Smallest-slice recovery gate** — admits implementation readiness into one local compatibility-preserving implementation recovery.
13. **Methodology admissibility gate** — admits working method into repository-supported investigation discipline without runtime authority.

## Recurring similarities

Across gates, movement is authorized by local evidence interpreted inside a competent boundary. Recurring similarities:

- evidence before movement;
- recurrence strengthens but does not alone authorize movement;
- provenance must survive;
- authority must remain local;
- claim strength must not exceed support;
- negative authority must remain visible;
- unknowns must be preserved rather than erased;
- projection is not verification;
- visibility is not authority;
- presentation vocabulary is not repository knowledge;
- diagnostic/read surfaces must not silently mutate cluster truth;
- explicit stop is a lawful result.

## Recurring differences

The gates differ by what changes:

- Orientation changes attention, not knowledge.
- Bounded inquiry changes answerability or routability, not truth.
- Evidence gates change support material, not admission by themselves.
- Candidate/support gates change consideration status, not verification.
- Family-local admissibility changes local constitutional strength.
- Projection changes current read condition, not objective truth.
- Presentation changes consumer-visible form, not source truth.
- Record/audit scope may preserve output but only under explicit mutation and subject boundaries.
- Frontier gates preserve unresolved pressure unless a bounded inquiry is earned.
- Implementation-readiness and smallest-slice gates can authorize code changes only under stricter owner/corridor, necessity, compatibility, and test requirements.
- Methodology gates constrain future inquiry without creating runtime authority.

## Recurring evidence gates

Recurring evidence gates include provenance, source role, support path, confidence, contradiction visibility, recency/currentness where relevant, competent local boundary, and evidence sufficiency. They refuse absent, stale, malformed, unsupported, over-claimed, provenance-erasing, or contradiction-erasing movement.

## Recurring projection gates

Recurring projection gates require preserved material, read/view boundaries, non-mutation, confidence limits, unknown preservation, and refusal of objective-truth collapse. Projection may produce current belief, read model, context, diagnostic, report, or answer surface; it does not create verification, implementation, or source-of-truth authority.

## Recurring implementation gates

Recurring implementation gates are stricter than inquiry or methodology gates. They require independently recurring boundary evidence, stable owner or corridor, implementation necessity, smallest behavior-preserving change, compatibility preservation, and tests or diagnostic shape checks where applicable. They refuse pressure, elegance, accumulated reports, frontier status, convenience, and ownership invention.

## Recurring refusal gates

Movement repeatedly stops when:

- evidence is absent, insufficient, stale, malformed, unsupported, or outside the competent boundary;
- provenance, support, authority, confidence, contradiction, or unknown status would be lost;
- claim strength would exceed evidence;
- presentation vocabulary would be promoted without repository evidence;
- a view would become source truth for itself;
- diagnostic output would become cluster truth without operational authority;
- projection would become verification;
- pressure would become implementation;
- comparison would introduce implementation, ownership, runtime architecture, or a new constitutional office.

## Recurring preservation gates

Movement repeatedly preserves:

- original observation, pressure, note, or evidence;
- source and provenance;
- support path and confidence;
- authority boundary and negative authority;
- local family scope;
- contradiction and unresolved disagreement;
- unknowns and stop conditions;
- read-only versus mutating status;
- event history versus projection versus view;
- implementation compatibility and adjacent non-ownership where implementation recovery is involved.
