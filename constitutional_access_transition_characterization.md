# Constitutional Access Transition Characterization

Repository authority wins.

## Boundary

This is exactly one bounded Constitutional Access Transition Characterization. It determines only the recurring constitutional act, if any, that repository evidence supports as moving an access surface from its initial constitutional state to a lawfully observable state.

It does not recover implementation, runtime authorization, a permission system, security architecture, operator power, or a universal transition mechanism. It does not assume that the operator performs the transition. It does not promote presentation vocabulary beyond what recurring repository evidence has earned.

## Evidence constraints recovered before candidate comparison

The reviewed evidence establishes these constraints:

1. Access surfaces begin constitutionally uncommitted unless a narrower local surface is already characterized as read-only observable.
2. Null or uncommitted condition does not move by vocabulary, arrival, visibility, pressure, operator statement, projection, or candidate status alone.
3. Observation is a source-attributed report or acquisition result, not truth, fact, verification, promotion, projection, or history by itself.
4. Promotion authority is not a universal actor. Evidence authorizes movement only through a family-local boundary competent to interpret that evidence.
5. Warrant is the repository-authorized entitlement and limit for bounded lawful reliance; it preserves provenance, role, authority boundary, negative authority, unknowns, and confidence limits.
6. Permission-like proceed/stop artifacts recur locally, but the repository has not earned a first-class universal bridge identity.
7. Constitutional history and biography preserve provenance-bearing transition and evidence material; they do not convert testimony, agreement, or append-only record existence into objective truth.

## Independently inspected candidate transitions

### Candidate 1: Mere access / arrival / presence

**What constitutional state exists before the transition?**

An access surface is in its initial uncommitted condition: Null, preserved-external-representation only, candidate external representation, provider-representation unaccepted, shape-uncommitted, or authority-uncommitted depending on the local surface.

**What constitutional act occurs?**

Material arrives, is visible, or exists at an access surface.

**What constitutional state exists after the transition?**

At most, the repository may truthfully say that an external representation exists or is available to a bounded surface. It is still not lawfully observable in the stronger constitutional sense unless bounded preservation, inspection, source report, or acceptance occurs.

**Who performs the act, if repository evidence supports that conclusion?**

Repository evidence does not recover a constitutional performer for mere arrival. Sources, files, providers, operators, runtime messages, or local surfaces may be involved, but mere involvement is not a constitutional actor conclusion.

**What authority permits the act?**

No promotion authority is recovered. Existence is not authority.

**What artifact crosses the boundary?**

A raw or external representation may cross: prose, bytes, provider payload, local reading, runtime message, or other access material.

**What explicitly does not cross?**

Truth, fact, verification, source authority, execution authority, repository knowledge, cluster mutation, semantic ownership, and constitutional warrant do not cross merely because the material arrived.

**What explicitly is this act not?**

It is not Observation, Evidence, Fact, admission, verification, promotion, mutation, authorization, or implementation recovery.

**Candidate result:** Rejected as the recurring transition to lawfully observable state. It is a precondition or weak representation-presence state, not the lawful transition itself.

### Candidate 2: Preservation of bounded external representation

**What constitutional state exists before the transition?**

The surface begins at Null or uncommitted access: no repository-visible representation has yet been preserved for the bounded responsibility.

**What constitutional act occurs?**

A bounded local responsibility preserves the external representation without interpreting it as truth. The `.` inquiry-note path is the clean recurring example summarized by prior audits.

**What constitutional state exists after the transition?**

A preserved external representation exists. It can orient bounded inquiry or become available to later inspection, but it remains non-observational or pre-observational unless a further source-attributed observation or accepted shape is lawfully constructed.

**Who performs the act, if repository evidence supports that conclusion?**

The implemented local responsibility that preserves the representation performs the preservation. Repository evidence does not generalize this to the operator or to a universal receiver.

**What authority permits the act?**

Local repository implementation authority permits preserving the representation within its explicit boundary. It does not permit interpretation, promotion, mutation, or truth.

**What artifact crosses the boundary?**

The raw external representation crosses: for example, a non-empty inquiry note, file sample, payload, or message body.

**What explicitly does not cross?**

Observation status, evidence status, fact status, truth, semantic interpretation, goal, command, plan, operator intent, source verification, and cluster mutation do not cross.

**What explicitly is this act not?**

It is not observation construction, not evidence formation, not admission, not verification, not runtime authorization, and not repository knowledge promotion.

**Candidate result:** Survives only as a weak earlier boundary: Null can become preserved external representation. It does not by itself transition an access surface to lawfully observable state.

### Candidate 3: Orientation

**What constitutional state exists before the transition?**

Material is preserved or visible but uncommitted. It may be operator prose, pressure, discussion, note content, or already projected repository material available for bounded orientation.

**What constitutional act occurs?**

A bounded orientation surface inspects or relates material to frame what can be lawfully examined next.

**What constitutional state exists after the transition?**

Orientation exists. The repository can lawfully look at a bounded next question or related material, but no knowledge, authority, fact, admission, execution permission, or implementation boundary is created by orientation alone.

**Who performs the act, if repository evidence supports that conclusion?**

The bounded orientation responsibility performs it where implemented. Repository evidence does not support promoting operator cognition or operator vocabulary as the constitutional performer.

**What authority permits the act?**

Local read-only orientation authority permits inspection and related-material framing. Constitutional transition evidence says this is real but constitutionally weak.

**What artifact crosses the boundary?**

Orientation material crosses: note text, pressure, a prompt concern, a possible target, or a related-material view.

**What explicitly does not cross?**

Repository knowledge, truth, fact, command, ownership, intent, recommendation authority, next-safe-move authority, runtime authority, and cluster mutation do not cross.

**What explicitly is this act not?**

It is not observation itself, not evidence acquisition, not fact projection, not admission, not verification, not implementation recovery, and not authorization.

**Candidate result:** Rejected as the specific answer to access-to-observable transition. It is a recurring constitutional movement, but it changes what can be looked at next rather than making the access material lawfully observable as a source-attributed observation.

### Candidate 4: Provider/source-specific acceptance and observation construction

**What constitutional state exists before the transition?**

The access surface contains unaccepted provider representation, candidate external representation, or shape-uncommitted material. It is not yet an Observation and not evidence or fact.

**What constitutional act occurs?**

A competent bounded source or local responsibility accepts the material under its local contract and constructs or emits a source-attributed observation: source report plus provenance, vantage point, subject-as-reported, time, scope, confidence or metadata when present, and promotion limits.

This act may appear as provider-specific parser selection, response-shape validation, required-field validation, source contract acceptance, safe attributable acquisition, or local observation emission. The recurring constitutional substance is not the parser or implementation. The recurring substance is bounded source-attributed observation under preserved provenance and authority limits.

**What constitutional state exists after the transition?**

The surface is lawfully observable: an attributable observation exists or can be preserved as observation-bearing material. It may later support evidence, facts, projection, audit, explanation, or ambiguity, but none of those later states are automatic.

**Who performs the act, if repository evidence supports that conclusion?**

Repository evidence supports only a family-local performer: the competent source, source adapter, observation responsibility, or bounded local surface that has authority for that observation family. It does not support the operator as the recurring performer and does not recover one universal constitutional actor.

**What authority permits the act?**

Family-local repository authority permits it: the local source contract, observation boundary, admissibility boundary, or competent responsibility that can interpret the material for that surface. Promotion-authority evidence constrains this: evidence authorizes movement only through the local constitutional boundary competent to interpret it, with explicit stop conditions.

**What artifact crosses the boundary?**

A bounded source report crosses with provenance-bearing context: source identity, source type, source-local identifiers, vantage point, subject as reported, observed time, scope, confidence/trust metadata when present, freshness/expiration when present, and collection constraints when present.

**What explicitly does not cross?**

Objective truth, fact status, verification, canonical identity, ownership, runtime state, health, availability, provider management authority, mutation authority, operator intent, cluster truth, and global repository law do not cross.

**What explicitly is this act not?**

It is not implementation recovery, not runtime authorization, not permission architecture, not a security architecture, not fact promotion, not projection, not verification, not provider control, and not a universal bootstrap.

**Candidate result:** Survives. This is the strongest recurring answer for the transition from initial access state to lawfully observable state.

### Candidate 5: Evidence formation / admissible support

**What constitutional state exists before the transition?**

A lawful observation or acquisition result exists, but it is not yet evidence, support, fact, or conclusion.

**What constitutional act occurs?**

Evidence formation consumes an observation as possible support and preserves provenance, source, scope, and limits.

**What constitutional state exists after the transition?**

Evidence-bearing support may exist. It can support claims, contradiction, insufficiency, projection, or stop conditions depending on the family-local rules.

**Who performs the act, if repository evidence supports that conclusion?**

The bounded evidence, claim, audit, or inquiry responsibility performs it where implemented. No universal actor is supported.

**What authority permits the act?**

Local evidence/admissibility authority permits it when the observation is safe, attributable, and useful as evidence.

**What artifact crosses the boundary?**

Observation plus support metadata crosses into evidence-bearing material.

**What explicitly does not cross?**

Truth, fact, verification, mutation authority, and global admission do not cross.

**What explicitly is this act not?**

It is not the first access-to-observable transition. It is downstream of observation.

**Candidate result:** Rejected as the answer to the core question because it begins after lawful observability has already been reached.

### Candidate 6: Candidate/support to admitted, verified, fact, or projected belief

**What constitutional state exists before the transition?**

Candidate, support, evidence, or observation exists, but final admission, verification, fact status, or current belief projection has not been established.

**What constitutional act occurs?**

A family-local admission, verification, promotion, or projection boundary evaluates evidence and records or exposes the stronger state if its local conditions are satisfied.

**What constitutional state exists after the transition?**

The subject may become admitted knowledge, verified capability, supported boundary, fact, FactSupport/current belief, promoted documentation knowledge, or accepted implementation surface within the relevant family.

**Who performs the act, if repository evidence supports that conclusion?**

The local admission or projection boundary performs it. Repository evidence does not support one universal promoter.

**What authority permits the act?**

Family-local evidence and boundary authority permits it.

**What artifact crosses the boundary?**

Evidence, support, verification result, tests, diagnostic shape, implementation behavior, or projection input crosses depending on the family.

**What explicitly does not cross?**

Global runtime authority, universal truth, cross-family admission, and mutation authority outside the local family do not cross.

**What explicitly is this act not?**

It is not the access-surface transition into lawfully observable state. It is a later stronger movement.

**Candidate result:** Rejected as too late and too strong for the core question.

## Comparison

### Surviving candidate constitutional acts

One candidate survives as the recurring access-to-observable transition:

```text
bounded source-attributed observation construction
```

Expanded without implementation vocabulary:

```text
initial uncommitted access representation
↓
family-local acceptance of a bounded source report
with preserved provenance, vantage, scope, subject-as-reported,
time, confidence/metadata where present, and negative authority
↓
lawfully observable source-attributed observation
```

A weaker predecessor also survives, but not as the answer:

```text
Null / no representation
↓
bounded preservation of external representation
↓
preserved external representation only
```

That earlier act can make something repository-visible or orientation-available. It does not make it lawfully observable in the constitutional observation sense.

### Rejected candidates

Rejected as the core answer:

- Mere access, arrival, visibility, or presence.
- Preservation of external representation alone.
- Orientation alone.
- Evidence formation, because it is downstream of observation.
- Admission, verification, fact promotion, projection, or implementation recovery, because they are later and stronger family-local transitions.
- Operator performance, because repository evidence does not support the operator as the recurring constitutional actor.
- A universal permission, authorization, security, or lifecycle mechanism, because repository evidence explicitly supports local boundaries rather than a universal owner.

### Recurring transition grammar

The recurring grammar is:

```text
access surface begins uncommitted
↓
material is preserved or available only as bounded external representation
↓
a competent local source/boundary accepts or reports bounded material
with provenance and authority limits
↓
source-attributed observation exists
↓
observation may later become evidence/support/fact/projection only through separate gates
```

The recurring refusal grammar is:

```text
material appears
↓
repository evidence does not preserve source attribution, shape acceptance,
provenance, scope, or authority boundary
↓
material remains uncommitted, candidate, orientation-only, unsupported, or stopped
```

### Recurring before/after constitutional states

Before the surviving transition, the access surface is one of:

- Null;
- uncommitted;
- preserved-external-representation only;
- candidate external representation;
- provider-representation unaccepted;
- shape-uncommitted;
- authority-uncommitted.

After the surviving transition, the access surface is:

- lawfully observable as a source-attributed observation;
- observation-bearing but not automatically evidence-bearing;
- attributable but not verified;
- usable for bounded downstream consideration only within preserved warrant and authority limits.

### Recurring authority boundaries

The recurring authority boundaries are:

- Repository authority wins.
- Family-local boundaries interpret local evidence.
- Source contracts and observation boundaries can accept bounded reports.
- Stop conditions remain positive authority when evidence is insufficient.
- Warrant permits bounded reliance only when provenance, role, authority boundary, negative authority, unknowns, and confidence limits survive.
- Visibility, diagnostic output, pressure, projection, vocabulary, operator testimony, and candidate status do not supply promotion authority by themselves.

### Preserved unknowns

These remain unknown and must not be inferred through:

- whether a stable canonical name for this transition should be adopted;
- whether all observation families should share one implementation artifact;
- whether future repository work should formalize a common observation-admission grammar;
- whether a future shared import boundary for federated Seed testimony will exist;
- how concurrent or conflicting local observation acceptances should be characterized constitutionally;
- whether a future audit should separate preservation-before-observation into additional subfamilies.

### Confidence

High confidence that mere access, arrival, visibility, preservation, and orientation do not by themselves lawfully transition an access surface into observation.

High confidence that the recurring access-to-observable transition is bounded source-attributed observation construction under family-local acceptance, preserved provenance, vantage, scope, and negative authority.

Medium confidence in the label `bounded source-attributed observation construction`; the constitutional substance is stronger than the wording, and future repository work may choose a different stable phrase.

Low confidence that the repository supports any universal performer, universal transition object, or unified runtime mechanism for this movement.
