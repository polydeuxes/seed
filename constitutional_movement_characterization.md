## Recurring constitutional evidence

### Constitution

`constitution.md` gives the controlling transition discipline for legitimate repository knowledge transition:

```text
Null / unknown / uncommitted
-> observation
```

It also states that a subject remains Null until observable evidence justifies movement, that only evidence may move a subject from Null toward a supported state, that repository authority wins over attractive abstractions or repeated vocabulary, that authority must be explicit, and that stop is a positive result.

This is the strongest constitutional evidence for movement. Movement appears as the change from one lawful constitutional condition to another, but only when evidence and authority justify the transition. The Constitution does not make movement a participant, owner, capability, primitive grammar, or implementation subsystem.

### Constitutional translation

### Constitutional warrant

`constitutional_warrant_characterization.md` recovers warrant as evidence-bound lawful reliance under a preserved authority boundary. It distinguishes content syntax, provider representation, record class, and translation mechanism from the content's repository-supported entitlement to bounded lawful reliance.

This evidence explains why movement can be lawful: movement into later reliance is permitted only where the moved content retains evidence, provenance/support, role, authority limit, negative authority, unknowns, and confidence limits. Movement is not what creates warrant. Warrant is what bounds and authorizes lawful movement.

### Constitutional reliance

`constitutional_reliance_characterization.md` treats reliance as bounded lawful use of warranted content, not truth promotion, mutation, ownership assignment, or unconstrained consumption.

Movement into reliance therefore performs a consumption-side transition: content becomes usable by a downstream consumer only in the role and scope its warrant allows. The recurring role is not reliance itself, and not a new participant between warrant and reliance. Movement names the disciplined transition from preserved warranted content to bounded use.

### Constitutional authority

`constitutional_promotion_authority_characterization.md` says evidence authorizes movement only through the local constitutional boundary competent to interpret that evidence for the relevant promotion family. It rejects universal promoters and automatic movement from candidate, pressure, visibility, observation, or vocabulary.

Authority evidence therefore narrows movement to locally authorized transition. Movement does not carry its own authority. It is prevented, stopped, or reduced when competent authority is absent or negative authority applies.

### Constitutional distinction

`constitutional_distinction_characterization.md` recovers distinctions as evidence-bound constitutional boundary preservation for lawful movement and reliance. Distinctions prevent collapse among observation/evidence, evidence/fact, translation/promotion, visibility/authority, unknown/failure, frontier pressure/implementation readiness, and other adjacent conditions.

This reinforces that movement is not an undifferentiated flow. Movement depends on preserved distinctions. Where distinctions are missing, movement would collapse conditions and over-promote unsupported claims.

### Inquiry orientation and investigation grammar

Inquiry and investigation grammar artifacts repeatedly distinguish orientation from conclusion. Operator prose, recurrence, pressure, and presentation vocabulary may orient inquiry, but they do not become repository truth or implementation readiness.

Movement from question pressure to characterization is therefore lawful only as bounded inquiry: the investigation observes evidence, answers one bounded question, preserves unsupported ideas and unknowns, and stops where evidence ends. ### Lawful stop and Unknown preservation

Valid stops include insufficient implementation evidence, unsupported conclusion, unknown target, adjacent-family pressure, family complete, methodology only, frontier only, and not implementation-backed.

This evidence is essential because it shows movement's negative boundary. Lawful movement includes not moving when support is absent. A stop is not failed movement; it is the constitutional discipline that prevents unsupported transition.
