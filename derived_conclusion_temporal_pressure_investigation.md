# Derived Conclusion Temporal Pressure Investigation

## Status and boundary

This is one bounded Constitutional Pressure Investigation. It does not characterize a candidate, recover implementation, introduce a constitutional participant, design a forecasting system, create scheduling, timers, polling, runtime ownership, planning, cognitive architecture, or new operational surface.

Repository authority wins. Implementation and tests are treated as stronger authority than attractive vocabulary. Prior investigations are used only as evidence of recurring pressure and repository reasoning style.

## Core question

According to recurring repository evidence, what unresolved constitutional pressure is exposed when derived conclusions are themselves relied upon through time?

## Reviewed evidence

### Traceability and derivation evidence

The traceability gap investigation identifies a recurring condition in which a visible conclusion remains only partially explainable when a necessary explanatory layer is missing. For derivation, it says a derivation gap exists when Seed has a conclusion but cannot explain why it exists in terms of source evidence, intermediate conclusions, derived conclusions, consumers, and unknowns. It also records that `reasoning_path` is implementation evidence for that pressure because it carries evidence, intermediate conclusions, derived conclusions, consumers, story impact, unknowns, and a read-only boundary.

The same investigation warns against overgeneralization: not every conclusion already has a complete derivation path, and unsupported targets return unknowns rather than fabricated selection or derivation evidence.

### Derivation frontier evidence

The derivation frontier records that derivation-like results may be claims, relationships, assessments, forecasts, contradiction relations, recommendations, or revisions, but it does not settle derivation as a primary object. It favors treating derivation as an operation or operation family rather than a first-class object.

The frontier also identifies the minimum pressure around derivation-like representation: a result must preserve input support, transformation or method, assumptions, caveats, confidence or strength, scope and authority, creation or knowledge time, lineage to prior claims or relationships, and explanation path. It states that the difference between derivation and fabrication is support discipline: preserved support, explicit scope, declared assumptions, known uncertainty, caveats and confidence, method or comparison boundary, authority and source limitations, and preservation of earlier claims rather than erasure.

This evidence is directly relevant to derived conclusions relied upon through time because it shows that support alone may not be enough when the transforming step, caveats, authority, and lineage are themselves part of the reason a downstream conclusion should not be treated as directly observed truth.

### Temporal reasoning evidence

The temporal reasoning audit says Seed has a narrow temporal model. Current fact selection is latest-current projection logic over the projected latest state, using predicate semantics, confidence, support count, inferred/observed status, observed time, and fact id as deterministic tie-breakers. Expired facts remain stored in projected state, but default support/current/conflict queries exclude them. Staleness is a read-time expiry view plus deterministic refresh recommendations; it does not mutate facts, lower stored confidence, or append refresh events.

The audit identifies a gap: Seed can answer what is true now and why now from latest projection, but cannot answer supported as-of questions such as what was true at event N or timestamp T through a supported API or CLI.

This falsifies a simple answer that the pressure is merely scheduling, polling, or a timer. Existing temporal evidence is about event order, observed time, expiry filtering, current projection, and missing as-of explanation. It does not recover a runtime owner for reconsideration cadence.

### Translation and answer-composition evidence

The translation surfaces investigation says several implemented read-only surfaces convert one reasoning space into another explanatory shape while preserving unknowns, limitations, unsupported domains, authority boundaries, and mutation boundaries. `reasoning_path` turns diagnostic evidence and operational story inputs into derivation explanation. `selection_path`, `reference_selection`, `capability_relationship`, and `operational_story` similarly explain relationships without converting output into cluster truth.

This matters because derived conclusions relied upon through time are not only data rows. They can become explanatory inputs to later answers. Repository evidence therefore pressures answer composition to preserve the difference between observed support, derived support, selected explanation, unknowns, and authority boundaries.

### Typed unknown and unknown-preservation evidence

Typed Unknown Preservation separated unresolved investigation states from public unknown rendering. It records that `build_reasoning_path_audit(...)` detects absent derivation evidence and preserves an unknown rather than inventing an explanation. The recovered producer preserves only an unresolved investigation state and explicitly does not own answer composition, presentation, recommendations, campaign selection, investigation execution, or repository redesign.

This matters because derived conclusions relied upon through time can expose unresolved conditions at multiple layers. Repository evidence supports preserving those unknowns instead of converting them into confidence, freshness, currency, or schedule vocabulary without authority.

### Current lawful condition, warrant, reliance, and movement evidence

Recurring repository evidence named in prior surveys links warrant to evidence-bound lawful reliance under authority boundaries, selected bounded content, negative authority, unknown preservation, confidence calibration, and non-promotion discipline. Current lawful condition evidence is present-tense and bounded: it can include evidence sufficiency, unknowns, lawful stop, unsupported candidates, selected concern, completion status, and current unresolved pressure without collapsing all of those into facts.

This matters because the operator's practical question is not only whether a forecast-like conclusion exists. It is whether current repository authority still warrants relying on a derived conclusion after time has passed, after support may have changed, or after another derivation layer has been added.

## Recurring implementation and constitutional evidence

Across the reviewed evidence, the recurring pattern is:

```text
observed or preserved support
  -> derivation-like transformation
  -> represented conclusion
  -> later explanation, selection, projection, or answer composition relies on that conclusion
  -> repository must still preserve support, method, caveats, unknowns, confidence limits, authority boundaries, and current lawful condition
```

The implementation-backed surfaces do not support replacing this with a new temporal participant. They support visibility and restraint:

- derivation visibility rather than a derivation ontology;
- current/latest projection rather than a full temporal reasoner;
- read-only explanation rather than cluster mutation;
- unknown preservation rather than invented closure;
- authority and mutation boundaries rather than automatic truth promotion;
- evidence-bound warrant rather than unsupported reliance.

## Recurring pressure

The unresolved constitutional pressure is:

```text
How does repository authority preserve and re-evaluate the lawful warrant for relying on a conclusion whose support is itself derivational, layered, caveated, uncertain, and situated in the current lawful condition, without promoting the derived conclusion into direct truth or inventing a new temporal/reliance mechanism?
```

More compactly:

```text
Layered derivation-reliance warrant pressure.
```

This phrase is only a descriptive handle for the pressure in this investigation. It is not a recovered constitutional participant, implementation owner, CLI surface, schema, scheduler, or ontology.

The pressure is exposed because each additional derivation layer can preserve lineage and uncertainty, but repository evidence has not settled how answer composition should determine that current reliance remains lawful when:

- the original support may be old, expired, contradicted, insufficient, or only latest-current;
- the intermediate conclusion was derived rather than observed;
- the downstream conclusion depends on the intermediate derivation;
- confidence, caveats, assumptions, and authority limits may compound or fail to transfer cleanly;
- unknowns must remain unknown rather than becoming fabricated certainty;
- movement may be lawful, stopped, or unsupported depending on current evidence.

The pressure is therefore not primarily time, forecasting, confidence, uncertainty, support lifetime, model quality, projection validity, or scheduling. Those are orientation handles. Repository evidence repeatedly narrows the actual pressure to preserving lawful reliance under layered derivation and current authority constraints.

## Falsified or unsupported explanations

### Not scheduling, timers, polling, or runtime ownership

The temporal audit identifies latest-current projection and missing as-of support, not a scheduler or runtime reconsideration owner. No reviewed evidence recovers a constitutional timer, polling duty, or runtime ownership model for reconsidering derived conclusions.

### Not a forecasting system

The derivation frontier notes that forecasts may be derivation-like, but it does not recover prediction as a constitutional participant or forecasting system. The orientation example exposes derivation pressure; it does not authorize forecast-specific architecture.

### Not confidence decay

Repository evidence uses confidence in fact selection, support summaries, derivation caveats, and explanation limits. It does not show a general rule that confidence decays through time or across derivation layers. Treating the pressure as confidence decay would invent an unsupported mechanism.

### Not support lifetime, freshness, currency, or temporal validity

Recent surveys refused those candidate vocabularies, and the reviewed implementation evidence supports expiry filtering, observed timestamps, current projection, stale views, and unknown preservation without promoting freshness/currency/support-lifetime vocabulary into constitutional knowledge.

### Not projection validity

Projection evidence supports latest-current state and cache behavior. It does not answer whether a layered derived conclusion remains warranted for later reliance. Projection may expose current evidence, but the pressure concerns lawful reliance on derivation through answer composition.

### Not model quality or uncertainty alone

Uncertainty and confidence are preserved as caveats, but the pressure is broader than quality estimation. It includes support lineage, transformation method, assumptions, authority limits, contradiction, unknown preservation, and lawful movement or stop.

## Preserved unknowns

The investigation preserves these unknowns:

- Whether derivation should ever become a first-class represented object remains unsettled.
- Whether support alone can explain every derivation-like result remains unsettled.
- Whether layered derived conclusions require a distinct future representation for method, assumptions, caveats, or lineage remains unsettled.
- How confidence, caveats, contradiction, and unknowns should compose across multiple derivation layers remains unsettled.
- How current lawful condition should be checked for a previously derived conclusion remains unsettled.
- Whether any future implementation should expose this pressure through diagnostics, answer composition, or another surface remains unsettled.
- No scheduler, polling mechanism, timer, support lifetime, forecasting system, or runtime ownership boundary is recovered here.

## Confidence

Confidence is medium-high that repository evidence recognizes a recurring pressure around lawful reliance on layered derived conclusions. The confidence is grounded in implementation-backed reasoning path visibility, current projection behavior, unknown preservation, translation surfaces, and derivation frontier constraints.

Confidence is not high because the repository has not settled a derivation ontology, a complete answer-composition rule for layered derivations, an as-of temporal interface, or a constitutional participant responsible for re-evaluating derived conclusions through time.

## Answer to the bounded question

According to recurring repository evidence, the pressure exposed when derived conclusions are themselves relied upon through time is not a new temporal mechanism. It is the unresolved pressure to preserve and determine current lawful warrant for relying on layered derived conclusions while keeping support lineage, transformation method, caveats, confidence limits, contradictions, unknowns, and authority boundaries visible.

Derived conclusion temporal pressure investigation complete.
