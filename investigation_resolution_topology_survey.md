## Apparent transitions

These are apparent recurring transitions only; they are not canonical ordering:

- Scout evidence often appears before broader survey or characterization artifacts.
- Survey artifacts often inspect multiple participants before a bounded characterization names responsibilities, boundaries, or non-responsibilities.
- Grammar artifacts often neighbor both surveys and characterizations when the recurring concern is language, discipline, transformation, or evidence contract.
- Bridge artifacts often neighbor topology surveys when the recurring concern is crossing, preservation, transformation, or non-crossing between positions.
- Readiness artifacts often neighbor competency, implementation, projection, evaluation, reconciliation, and audit surfaces.
- Pressure artifacts often neighbor audits and characterizations when necessity, evidence boundary, owner locality, or backlog pressure becomes visible.
- Reconciliation artifacts often follow separately recovered statements and preserve alignment without forcing canonical unification.
- Completion artifacts often appear as audits after a family or surface is sufficiently visible to ask whether coverage is complete enough.
- Constitutional artifacts recur across many resolutions rather than occupying a single resolution.

## Incomplete topology

- The evidence does not establish a complete topology of all possible investigation resolutions.
- The evidence does not establish that each visible resolution is canonical.
- The evidence does not establish a universal sequence from scout to survey to characterization to audit.
- The evidence does not establish whether `audit`, `slice`, `assessment`, `inspection`, `backlog`, `roadmap`, `spymaster`, or `frontier` should be treated as independent investigation resolutions under this survey boundary.
- The evidence does not establish whether physiology is broad enough to stand alongside more frequent resolutions; it is visible but sparsely represented.
- The evidence does not establish that constitutional investigations are a resolution in the same sense as survey or characterization; they are a recurring family that operates across resolutions.
- The evidence does not require expansion beyond the requested investigation families to recover implementation, ownership, or canonical taxonomy.
