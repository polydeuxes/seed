```text

appear to lack
```

The observation bundled several assumptions:

Those assumptions are now separable. The original observation remains useful as a visibility concern, but the later evidence does not support the early single-function framing.

## Recovered constitutional understanding

The repository now explains the apparent missing `begin` function as a decomposed constitutional concern, not as a single recovered missing implementation unit.

The recurring explanation is:

```text
Lawful beginning is not one function.
It is constrained movement from Null or uncommitted status
through bounded presence, evidence, warrant, local eligibility,
selection/refusal where supported, and lawful stop or handoff.
```

The repository has recovered several constitutional responsibilities that explain why a single `begin` function was not visible:

- Time contributes temporal authority and provenance separation, but does not own lawful start, scheduling, a Town Clock, or readiness.
- Selection contributes bounded eligibility, local selection, refusal, and selected/non-selected visibility, but does not own a universal start pipeline.
- Warrant contributes evidence-bound lawful reliance under authority limits, not implementation activation.
- Promotion authority contributes local evidence-gated movement, not automatic promotion from pressure, vocabulary, visibility, or candidate status.
- Current lawful condition contributes present bounded status, but the phrase has since been decomposed into multiple recurring families rather than one stable architectural family.
- Null constitutional readiness explains that Null is constitutional non-commitment and evidence gate, not startup or initialization.
- Condition progression explains weak local ordering constraints, not a universal progression.

## Final answer

According to recurring repository evidence, the recovered constitutional understanding is that the original Begin-function concern was not actually a proven need for a single constitutional `begin` function.

What was missing was truthful constitutional visibility into when movement from Null, candidate, unknown, or unsupported status is lawful. Subsequent evidence decomposed that concern into bounded presence, Null/commitment posture, evidence sufficiency, constitutional warrant, local promotion authority, local selection or refusal, temporal authority separation where Time participates, current lawful condition, lawful stop, and preserved unknowns.

The early assumption that Time, Selection, or one callable function should own constitutional beginning is now unsupported. Time explains temporal provenance and authority non-collapse. Selection explains bounded eligibility and local choosing or refusal. Warrant and promotion authority explain why evidence-bound movement must remain local and authority-limited. Null readiness explains why non-commitment is not startup. Condition progression explains local ordering constraints without recovering a universal progression.

Therefore the implementation appeared to lack a constitutional Begin function because the repository had not yet recovered the constitutional responsibilities that make lawful beginning explainable. Those responsibilities are now illuminated enough to permit lawful future slicing, but not to recommend implementation, ownership, runtime activation, scheduling, or slice order.

Reconciliation complete.
