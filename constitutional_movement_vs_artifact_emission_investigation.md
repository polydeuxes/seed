## Core answer

According to recurring repository evidence, performed movement and produced artifact are constitutionally distinct at investigation resolution.

The evidence supports this distinction because:

- the same movement can emit different artifacts depending on local evidence, authority, and family boundary;
- multiple movements can emit, preserve, or expose the same artifact family;
- some movements can terminate, constrain, evaluate, or preserve without emitting a new artifact beyond an already bounded stop, refusal, or continued limit;
- emitted artifacts remain typed by provenance, role, admission status, authority boundary, and family-local continuity, not by the movement that produced or exposed them;
- artifact production itself was only a candidate movement in the act survey, and its independence was weaker than constrain, evaluate, terminate, and preserve because production often remains artifact-family-local.

The distinction is therefore supported, but not as a canonical movement theory or artifact taxonomy.

## Independently inspected movements

### 1. Constrain

```text
What movement
is performed?
```

Constrain limits a subject, claim, inquiry, artifact, output, or downstream use to the boundary repository evidence grants. It moves a broad possible authority into bounded lawful use, bounded non-use, explicit refusal, or stop.

```text
Does it necessarily
emit an artifact?
```

No. Constrain may be expressed as a boundary in another artifact, but the constitutional movement is the limiting of authority. It can also operate by refusing unsupported promotion or by keeping an existing artifact inside its existing boundary.

```text
If an artifact
is emitted,

what artifact?
```

Possible emitted artifacts include bounded questions, evidence scopes, subject identities, admissibility findings, authority limits, negative authority statements, reliance limits, preserved unknowns, and output or handoff limits.

```text
Can multiple
movements emit
the same artifact?
```

Yes. A preserved unknown can be emitted by constrain, evaluate, terminate, preserve, or investigation output/handoff. An authority limit can be emitted by constrain, warrant, reliance, promotion evaluation, or negative authority.

```text
Can one movement
emit different
artifacts?
```

Yes. Constrain can emit a bounded scope, a refusal, a negative authority statement, a reliance limit, a stop condition, or a non-promotion boundary.

```text
Can movement
terminate without
artifact emission?
```

Yes at movement resolution. Constrain can leave an existing artifact unchanged, keep a candidate unpromoted, or refuse expansion. If the investigation records that refusal, a report artifact may exist, but that recording is not identical to the constraining movement.

```text
Movement != ?
```

Constrain is not the boundary artifact, not a policy engine, not a permission system, not proof of impossibility, and not production itself.

## Independently inspected emitted artifacts

### Observations

Observations are source-attributed reports. They can be emitted by observation construction, provider translation, transform, preserve, and constrain. They do not automatically become truth, fact, evidence, change, history, projection, promotion, or verification. Observation may terminate at observation/evidence without fact promotion.

### Classifications

Classifications are bounded status assignments for already-bounded subjects and already-produced evidence. They can be emitted by evaluation, local responsibility status assignment, comparison, and termination. A classification is not the evaluating movement, and classification does not automatically own authority, evidence, truth, rendering, navigation, or mutation.

### Translated representations

Translated representations preserve selected identity, provenance, metadata, dimensions, confidence, and authority limits while changing provider-local expression into Seed-bounded representation. They can be emitted by transform, constrain, warrant-preserving translation, or local provider-language responsibility. They are not the transformation movement itself and do not become truth by changed expression.

### Bounded outputs and handoffs

Bounded outputs and handoffs expose what may leave a subject, report, view, diagnostic, or inquiry. They can be emitted by production, investigation output, constrain, evaluate, terminate, preserve, or reliance. They are not evidence of a universal output architecture.

### Preserved unknowns

Preserved unknowns can be emitted by constrain, evaluate, terminate, preserve, warrant, reliance, investigation grammar, and component roles. They do not resolve the unknown, authorize inference, create backlog commands, or become implementation requirements.

### Confidence statements

Confidence statements calibrate reliance and termination. They can be emitted by evaluation, warrant, reliance, investigation grammar, component roles, and termination. Confidence is not proof, not authority by itself, and not identical to the movement that calibrated it.

### Lawful stops

Lawful stops can be emitted by terminate, constrain, evaluation, promotion refusal, reliance limits, and investigation grammar. A lawful stop is not failure and does not imply a lifecycle. It is a bounded artifact of non-continuation or non-promotion.

### History records

History records preserve what happened or what was recorded without making every observation universally historical or true. They can be emitted by preservation, observation/evidence events where supported, and history characterization. History record emission is not identical to preservation as movement.

### Projected views

Projected views are derived read-model or presentation participants. They can be emitted by projection-local transitions, transform, presentation, orient, and preserve-with-boundary. They are not source truth, not the movement that derives them, and not authority for their own source material.
