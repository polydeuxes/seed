## Investigation A — responsibility unique to finite observation

### Recurring responsibilities

Finite observation uniquely owns bounded intake and provenance. It can preserve what a source, parser, command, provider, file, repo scan, or diagnostic can lawfully observe under its configured permissions and implementation boundaries.

Recurring finite-observation responsibilities:

1. Preserve observed evidence without pretending to exhaust Objective Reality.
2. Preserve provenance, source shape, warning, parser boundary, and confidence where available.
3. Distinguish reachable evidence from unsupported, invalid, unconfigured, unreachable, missing, and unknown domains.
4. Hand evidence to owners that may validate, project, support, derive, or render it.
5. Stop at Unknown, Unsupported, No deterministic attachment, or insufficient evidence when observation cannot support a stronger claim.

### Recurring boundaries

Finite observation does not uniquely own:

- final truth;
- global interpretation;
- fact promotion without implementation owner;
- current lawful condition;
- cluster mutation;
- semantic authority over presentation vocabulary;
- Objective Reality.

### Non-responsibilities

Finite observation is not a constitutional license to infer unseen reality. It does not collapse unknown into false, unobservable, impossible, or not-yet-observed. It does not turn diagnostics into cluster truth or structural recurrence into ontology.

### Preserved unknowns

- Whether a specific unobserved condition is unavailable, unsupported, unconfigured, merely not yet observed, or outside the current observation domain may remain unknown.
- Whether all finite-observation families should share one Unknown taxonomy remains unsupported.

### Confidence

High that finite observation owns bounded intake and provenance. Medium that this is the unique responsibility most analogous to bounded observers in the external grammar, because the repository also emphasizes translation, projection, and lawful current condition.

## Investigation B — pressure test: leaving the Cave means reaching Objective Reality

### Unsupported proposition

The repository does not support:

```text
Leaving finite constitutional visibility = reaching Objective Reality
```

Evidence repeatedly refuses this collapse. Append-only event history is authoritative for repository events but not a truth oracle about the world. Projection builds current State from event history but is derived. Diagnostics and rendered views expose visibility without mutating cluster truth. Presentation labels discovered by reachability audit can remain unpreserved.

### Better-supported alternatives

Repository evidence supports these bounded alternatives:

1. **Increasing visibility:** more surfaces, diagnostics, observations, inventories, and views become available.
2. **Expanding constitutional history:** append-only events preserve more repository-recorded history.
3. **Improving translation:** promotion, projection, registry/audit conformance, explanation, relationship derivation, and current-condition rules become more explicit.
4. **Reducing local uncertainty:** particular unknowns can become supported, unsupported, invalid, unconfigured, or still unknown.
5. **Strengthening warrant:** current lawful condition can become better bounded under repository authority.

### Confidence

High that leaving the Cave is unsupported if it means reaching Objective Reality. High that the repository instead supports bounded increases in visibility, history, translation, and warrant.

## Investigation C — does the boundary between Constitutional Reality and Objective Reality shrink as constitutional history grows?

### Supported evidence

- Append-only history can grow.
- Projection and read models can use more preserved events.
- Diagnostics can expose more surfaces and boundaries.

The repository does not prove that the boundary between Constitutional Reality and Objective Reality shrinks globally. More history can improve constitutional warrant, but event history is still repository history and not a world-truth oracle. Projection remains derived from preserved events and catalogs. Visibility expansion does not equal ontological convergence.

### Preserved unknowns

- Whether any specific domain has a measurable shrinking boundary requires domain-specific observation and audit evidence.
- Whether objective conditions not captured by repository observation are stable, unavailable, or unknowable is not established.

### Confidence

High that local uncertainty can shrink. Low that recurring evidence supports global convergence between Constitutional Reality and Objective Reality.

## Investigation E — Unknown != Unobservable != Not Yet Observed

### Supported distinction

Repository evidence supports separating these states:

| State | Constitutional meaning | Responsibility |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Unknown | Available evidence does not establish a supported classification, conclusion, boundary, implementation, or current status. | Preserve without fabricating closure. |
| Unobservable | Current implementation, permissions, source availability, provider support, or domain boundary prevents observation. | Preserve as a reachability/support/configuration/permission boundary when evidence supports that diagnosis. |
| Not Yet Observed | The repository has not observed something, but available evidence does not prove it is unobservable. | Avoid converting absence into impossibility or falsehood. |

### Recurring boundaries

- Unknown is not false.
- Unsupported is not the same as unobservable.
- Missing evidence is not sufficient to infer absence.
- Presentation vocabulary is not preserved knowledge until reachability/implementation evidence supports it.
- Diagnostic recording does not become cluster mutation.

### Preserved unknowns

- Whether every Unknown instance can be typed as unknown, unobservable, or not-yet-observed remains unsupported.
- Some unknowns may arise from missing evidence, unobserved sources, unsupported domains, unmodeled concepts, or reachability gaps without a visible boundary.

### Confidence

High that the distinction is supported. Medium that current implementation consistently labels all three states uniformly, because Unknown typing remains distributed across surfaces.

## Investigation F — is the Cave finite constitutional visibility rather than ignorance?

After translation, recurring repository evidence supports this interpretation more strongly than an ignorance interpretation:

```text
The Cave
=
finite constitutional visibility
```

The repository repeatedly treats bounded observation, candidate evidence, current facts, diagnostics, read models, projection, and rendered views as lawful but finite visibility. It does not treat finite visibility as mere ignorance. Finite visibility can be useful, warranted, auditable, and current, while still bounded and non-equivalent to Objective Reality.

The better constitutional interpretation is therefore:

```text

↓
```

## Recurring responsibilities recovered

1. **Observation:** collect bounded evidence and provenance.
2. **Preservation:** append repository events and artifacts without rewriting prior history.
3. **Translation/promotion:** move material only through implementation-owned gates.
4. **Projection:** build current State/read models from append-only history and catalogs.
5. **Visibility:** render diagnostics, audits, views, and explanations without silently creating truth.
6. **Current lawful condition:** characterize present bounded status under constitutional authority.
7. **Unknown preservation:** retain unsupported, unresolved, insufficient, unreachable, or not-yet-supported states.
8. **Boundary discipline:** distinguish event writes, diagnostics, cluster mutation, projection, read models, and Objective Reality.

## Recurring boundaries recovered

- Objective Reality is not equated with Constitutional Reality.
- Append-only history is constitutional history, not a world oracle.
- Projection is derived current state, not raw reality.
- Diagnostic visibility is not cluster mutation.
- Presentation vocabulary is not automatically knowledge.
- Observation is evidence-bearing but not all-authoritative.
- Unknown is not false, impossible, or necessarily unobservable.
- More history does not prove global convergence with Objective Reality.
