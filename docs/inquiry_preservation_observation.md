## 1. What The Inquiry Began From

The inquiry began from pressure that can be stated as:

```text
State Summary feels wrong
```

The earliest State Summary pressure was not simply that the surface was broken.
The State Summary authority review observed that the surface mixed inventory,
integrity, observation accounting, knowledge inventory, and operational status.
It then distinguished State Summary from impact and treated it as closest to a
repository-level knowledge inventory / overview surface.

That mattered because the discomfort was not only about formatting or speed. It
exposed a deeper difference between:

```text
what information exists
what that information means as knowledge
what work the participant is actually trying to do with it
```

The performance lineage report supplied a concrete activation chain: State
Summary was slow, profiling showed projection-cache rebuild and source-navigation
cost, a targeted optimization improved performance, and a source-navigation gap
remained. Later State Summary audits converted that initial pressure into
questions about authority, CLI boundaries, top entity selection, endpoint
prominence, filesystem projection, empty operator buckets, inventory versus
knowledge, durable facts versus measurements, and availability authority.

The inquiry therefore began with a practical surface pressure, but the chain did
not remain a UI/surface critique. It became an inquiry into the difference
between available repository information and the work that information supports.

## 2. Concepts Tested

The chain tested these concepts as observations or frontiers rather than as
architectural primitives:

- **Relation of use:** whether meaning appears in how an artifact, fact, or
document is used by later work, not only in what the artifact contains.
- **Participation:** whether repository items participate in work without
necessarily owning the work or being its cause.
- **Interaction temporalness:** whether interaction and evidence become
intelligible through before/after, activation, continuation, and change rather
than through static content alone.
- **Movement preservation:** whether documents preserve safe movement, direction,
sequence, and transition rather than only conclusions.
- **Continuability:** whether enough remains preserved for a later participant to
continue without reconstructing the whole inquiry.
- **Relation preservation:** whether preservation depends on relations among
question, pressure, evidence, boundary, and next move.
- **Purpose and concern:** whether documents preserve what mattered and why an
item was selected.
- **Situation:** whether work depends on why-this, why-now, what-next, what
matters, and what remains active.
- **Orientation bundle:** whether selected concern, boundary, evidence, current
position, and safe movement together carry continuation load.
- **Work shape:** whether different kinds of repository work can be recognized by
their evidence relations and movement constraints.
- **Work recognition:** whether the same information can support different work,
and whether work becomes recognizable through evidence relations rather than
information alone.
- **Pressure precursor and activation:** whether repository history preserves the
pressure or event that activated work, not only the response.

## 3. Concepts That Weakened Into Lenses

Several concepts remained useful but weakened as candidates for primitives:

- **Relation of use** weakened into a lens for reading how artifacts matter in
later work. It did not become an ontology of all repository relations.
- **Participation** weakened into a way to avoid false ownership or causation. It
helps say that a document participates in work without claiming that it caused
or governs that work.
- **Interaction temporalness** weakened into a temporal reading lens. It helps
explain activation, sequencing, continuation, and drift, but repository evidence
does not preserve enough interaction detail to make it a complete model.
- **Movement preservation** weakened into a useful continuation lens. It explains
why next safe move and boundary matter, but not every document needs or preserves
movement.
- **Continuability** weakened into a question about preserved resumption
conditions. It is not the same as correctness, completeness, lineage, or
successful continuation.
- **Relation preservation** weakened into an evidence-reading lens. It remains
important where meaning lives between artifacts, but it is not yet a general
relationship architecture.
- **Purpose and concern** weakened into selection-orientation vocabulary. It helps
explain why material mattered, but it cannot safely infer operator intent.
- **Situation** weakened into a reconstruction aid. It can describe an apparent
work situation from records, but repository-only evidence cannot guarantee the
full situation that existed for participants.
- **Orientation bundle** weakened from possible structure into load-bearing
language for what continuation seems to require. It is not a workflow.
- **Work shape** weakened into a descriptive pattern. It is not a taxonomy.
- **Work recognition** weakened into an observational claim about evidence
relations. It is not a classifier.
- **Pressure precursor** weakened into an evidence category. It is not operator
motivation or intent.

The weakening is not failure. It is the useful reduction from candidate concept
to disciplined lens.

## 4. Findings That Survived Reality Audit

The work-recognition reality audit and subsequent pressure-precursor review left
several findings stronger than the concepts around them:

1. **Available information is not enough to identify work.** The same documents,
facts, or surfaces can support lookup, inventory, audit, reconciliation,
frontier exploration, implementation-facing review, or continuation.
2. **Work becomes recognizable through evidence relations.** Document type,
authority boundary, selected concern, inspected sources, unresolved pressure,
examples, and next safe movement jointly identify the work better than raw
content does.
3. **State Summary pressure was a real historical activator.** It generated
performance work, authority review, surface-boundary audits, and later examples
used by work-recognition and activation documents.
4. **State Summary is closer to a knowledge inventory / repository overview than
to impact, entity interpretation, or an operational dashboard.** This survived as
a useful distinction even though future State Summary evolution remains open.
5. **Inventory is not knowledge.** Counts and categories show the shape of the
projected state, while learned structure and implications require different
surfaces or explanations.
6. **Continuation requires more than available documents.** Handoff availability,
consumption, bootstrap activation, and continuation compliance can diverge.
7. **Working state is different from repository state.** A repository can contain
the correct documents while a participant still lacks the active work-position.
8. **Orientation matters for resumption.** Current work position, active edge,
selected pressure, authority boundary, validation state, and next safe move
explain continuation better than static references alone.
9. **Activation is often weakly preserved.** Repository history more reliably
preserves response documents and boundary interpretations than first activation
events.
10. **Repository evidence can show boundaries without proving intent.** The chain
can observe pressure, response, and missing evidence without converting those
observations into psychological or operator-intent claims.

## 5. Counterexamples And Limits

The theory was limited by several counterexamples:

- **State Summary performance lineage is a strong activation counterexample.** It
shows that some activation chains are well preserved: a report, profiling,
optimization, and residual source-navigation gap are all recoverable.
Therefore, activation is not always weakly preserved.
- **Many documents are intentionally boundary documents.** A reconciliation may
begin from authority and not preserve the original activation event. That does
not make it deficient for its purpose.
- **A document can preserve response better than pressure.** Prometheus, storage,
source-navigation, and handoff clusters often preserve cleanup, boundary, or
response more strongly than first discovery.
- **Successful continuation does not validate a theory.** A future participant
may continue successfully because the documents are rich, because they already
know the context, or because the next task is obvious, not because work
recognition has been correctly theorized.
- **Duplicate work is not always the problem.** It can be a symptom of missing
orientation, selected concern, safe-move context, or activation, but not every
repeat investigation proves an activation failure.
- **Lineage is not activation history.** Cross-references, predecessor documents,
and commit order can suggest movement but do not necessarily preserve the event
that made work become live.
- **Repository-only observation cannot distinguish all cases of missing evidence
from non-existent events.** Some activation pressure may live in prompts,
conversation, operator reports, transient frustration, or runtime experience not
preserved in the repository.

## 6. Relation Between State Summary, Work Recognition, Orientation, And Activation

The preserved relation is:

```text
State Summary pressure
    exposed that available knowledge can feel insufficient or misoriented

work recognition
    asked what kind of work the same available information is supporting

orientation
    named the selected concern, boundary, current position, and safe movement
    that make the work intelligible and continuable

activation
    asked whether the preserved material became live in working state, and what
    pressure or event made a new line of work begin
```

The chain moved from:

```text
State Summary feels wrong
```

to:

```text
available knowledge != recognized work
recognized work != activated work
activation is often weakly preserved
repository-only evidence may be insufficient for the next test
```

State Summary supplied the concrete pressure. Work recognition generalized the
problem: the same repository information can support different work. Orientation
explained why recognizing work requires more than information: the work must be
seen from a selected position with a concern, boundary, validation condition, and
next movement. Activation then exposed another gap: even when information and
orientation are preserved, repository evidence may not show when or whether that
orientation became live for a participant.

## 7. What Repository Evidence Preserves Well

Repository evidence preserves these things well:

- **Responses:** created audits, observations, reconciliations, frontiers, tests,
and implementation-facing read-model documents.
- **Boundary interpretations:** especially distinctions such as State Summary not
being impact, handoff availability not being activation, projection not being
authority, and inventory not being knowledge.
- **Document type and authority posture:** observation, audit, reconciliation,
frontier, implementation characterization, and runtime-facing documents usually
state their scope.
- **Lineage and adjacency:** related sections, maps, indexes, and references make
successor work partially traceable.
- **Selected evidence sets:** inspected documents and search terms often show
what material was used.
- **Surviving pressure after interpretation:** unresolved boundaries, future
questions, non-goals, and next safe movements are often preserved.
- **Implementation-facing surfaces:** State Summary views, state views, context
views, source navigation, projection cache, and tests preserve what runtime
surfaces currently expose or assert.

## 8. What Repository Evidence Preserves Poorly

Repository evidence preserves these things poorly or unevenly:

- **First activation events:** the exact event that made an inquiry live is often
not recorded.
- **Operator experience:** discomfort, urgency, frustration, surprise, or
recognition is usually paraphrased later, if preserved at all.
- **Activation in working state:** documents can exist and be correct without
showing whether they were consumed, activated, or followed.
- **Why-now selection:** later documents often inherit pressure without
reconstructing why that document happened at that moment.
- **Non-selected alternatives:** repository work sometimes preserves selected
paths better than rejected or deferred paths.
- **Degree of confidence in reconstructed situations:** observations can describe
apparent situations but cannot always show whether the reconstruction matches the
original participant state.
- **Interaction-level chronology:** commit order and document lineage are not the
same as detailed interaction temporalness.

## 9. Boundary Of Available Evidence

Repository-only observation appears to hit its boundary at exactly the next
question the chain raises:

```text
Can preserved knowledge, recognized work shape, and orientation reliably become
activated work for a future participant?
```

The repository can show that documents exist, that they distinguish authority,
that they describe pressure, that they include references, and that they propose
next safe movement. It cannot always show:

- whether the participant read them;
- whether the participant selected the intended concern;
- whether the active boundary became part of working state;
- whether a later participant felt oriented;
- whether duplicate work resulted from missing information, missing orientation,
failed activation, or an unrelated choice;
- whether an observed pressure originated in repository evidence or in an
unpreserved prompt, conversation, runtime event, or operator experience.

This is an evidence boundary, not a theory failure.

## Major Findings

1. The inquiry preserved a movement from surface discomfort to evidence-boundary
analysis.
2. The strongest durable distinction is not a new primitive but a chain of
non-equivalences:

```text
available information != knowledge
knowledge inventory != recognized work
recognized work != active orientation
active orientation != activated working state
activation evidence != operator intent
```

3. The repository is strong at preserving responses, boundaries, and lineage; it
is weaker at preserving first activation events and participant working-state
adoption.
4. The reality audit strengthened the work-recognition observation but weakened
its concepts into lenses rather than architecture.
5. The next decisive test cannot be completed by repository-only observation
alone; it likely requires either lint/connectivity review or a bounded
implementation/usage probe.

## Inquiry Preservation Findings

- The chain is preserved as a sequence of pressure decomposition rather than as a
single theory.
- State Summary remains the most concrete starting pressure because it supplied
both practical performance evidence and conceptual authority questions.
- Work recognition is best preserved as an observation about evidence relations,
not a classifier proposal.
- Orientation is best preserved as resumption-relevant relation among concern,
boundary, evidence, validation state, and next safe movement, not a workflow.
- Activation is best preserved as a boundary between existing material and live
working state, not as operator intent.

## Surviving Findings

- State Summary is not impact.
- Inventory is not knowledge.
- Available knowledge is not recognized work.
- Recognized work is not activated work.
- Handoff availability, consumption, activation, and compliance can diverge.
- Working state is not repository state.
- Continuation requires current work-position / orientation, not only references.
- Repository evidence can preserve pressure after interpretation even when first
activation is missing.

## Weakened Findings

- Relation of use, participation, temporalness, movement, continuability,
relation preservation, concern, situation, orientation bundle, work shape, and
work recognition should remain observational lenses unless later authority
promotes them.
- Current work position may become vocabulary inside working state rather than a
separate architectural concept.
- Pressure precursor may remain an evidence-reading practice rather than a
required documentation field.
- Discovery path may partially preserve activation history but should not be
collapsed into it.

## Evidence-Boundary Findings

- Absence of an activation event in repository records is not proof that no event
occurred.
- Preserved response is easier to reconstruct than originating pressure.
- Reconciliations may intentionally preserve authority rather than activation.
- Runtime-facing read models show current surfaces, not participant activation.
- Repository-only observation cannot prove operator intent, motivation,
experience, or activation in working state.

## Deferred Lint / Connectivity Items

- Cross-link the chain if later review finds missing routeability.
- Validate indexes, maps, and related-document lists.
- Check stale or missing references across State Summary, handoff, continuation,
activation, and work-recognition clusters.
- Review naming consistency for working state, current work position, active
edge, orientation, activation, and pressure.
- Confirm implementation-facing read-model documents are discoverable from the
documentation maps.

## Deferred Probe Items

- Compare continuation outcomes with and without explicit orientation/current-
work-position summaries.
- Explore whether State Summary should separate inventory from knowledge signals.
- Explore whether activation can be observed without intent inference.
- Explore whether source navigation can expose inquiry lineage and unresolved
pressure usefully.
- Explore whether duplicate-work review can distinguish missing evidence,
orientation failure, activation failure, and legitimate revalidation.

## Unresolved Observations

- It remains unresolved whether every inquiry needs explicit activation-history
preservation.
- It remains unresolved whether current work position is distinct from working
state or a useful vocabulary slice within it.
- It remains unresolved whether repository maps should route by document type,
pressure lineage, active boundary, or work shape.
- It remains unresolved whether State Summary should evolve into a richer
overview surface or remain a compact inventory/integrity surface.
- It remains unresolved whether activation should ever become observable runtime
state, documentation metadata, probe-only measurement, or remain a conceptual
boundary.

## Conclusion

This inquiry preserved a specific discovery path:

```text
State Summary feels wrong
    -> State Summary mixes inventory, integrity, accounting, knowledge, and status
    -> inventory is not knowledge
    -> available knowledge does not determine work
    -> work is recognized through evidence relations and orientation
    -> orientation can be preserved without becoming activated
    -> activation is often weakly preserved
    -> repository-only evidence may be insufficient for the next test
```

The preserved result is not a reconciled architecture. It is a disciplined
observation boundary for future participants: treat the chain as inquiry
preservation, not canon; use its concepts as lenses unless later authority
promotes them; and defer the next claims to lint/connectivity review or bounded
implementation probe.
