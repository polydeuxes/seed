# Active Edge Frontier

## Purpose

Recent frontier work repeatedly encountered a phenomenon that is not explained
by preserved information alone:

```text
What is currently pulling work forward?
```

The repeated vocabulary differs by document and context:

```text
active question
active gap
active contradiction
active frontier
active relationship
selected tension
active concern
current work position
next safe move
continuation point
```

This document investigates that recurrence directly. It asks:

```text
What is an Active Edge?
```

More specifically:

```text
What causes one concern, question, tension, contradiction, gap, relationship,
or frontier to become the current focus of work?
```

This is exploratory ontology work only. It does not design schedulers, planners,
prioritization systems, workflow engines, attention systems, execution systems,
schemas, runtimes, registries, memory systems, or implementation behavior. It
does not reconcile Seed's ontology and does not supersede authoritative
reconciliations. Repository authority wins over this document.

The document does not assume that Active Edge is an object. It does not assume
that it is a role, operation, relationship, current work position, attention
state, selection result, priority, or inquiry object. It evaluates repository
evidence for and against those possibilities.

## Existing Authority That Constrains This Frontier

### Architectural Status And Next Frontier

`architectural_status_and_next_frontier.md` remains the repository's current
routing authority for active implementation priority. It keeps implementation
attention on bounded Prometheus observation/projection cleanup and treats recent
inquiry, attention, persistence, continuity, current-work-position, relationship,
operation, attribution, derivation, and handoff-lineage documents as exploratory
unless a later reconciliation promotes specific findings.

That constraint matters here because an active edge must not be confused with
repository priority. A frontier may be active as an ontology investigation while
the repository's current implementation priority remains elsewhere. Conversely,
current implementation priority can exist without making every surrounding
ontology concern implementation-ready.

### Foundational Ontology Reconciliation

The foundational reconciliation preserves a bounded object / property / event /
role vocabulary while acknowledging continuation and reasoning planes. It names
handoff as preserving active alignment across time, sessions, and actors, and it
warns against turning context, selection, or explanation into duplicate truth
systems.

This frontier therefore cannot install Active Edge as a new foundational type.
At most, it may expose a pressure that future reconciliation could route through
existing concepts or distinguish more carefully.

### Handoff And Continuation Lineage Frontier

The handoff-lineage frontier found that continuation can fail even when
information is preserved. It also found that continuation improves when handoffs
preserve active investigation, working state, unresolved tensions, live reasoning
branches, selection rationale, navigation intent, and next safe moves.

This is the strongest repository evidence for an active-edge question. It
suggests that a later participant may need more than facts, references, or a
summary. They may need the unresolved edge that was carrying forward movement.

### Current Work Position Frontier

The current-work-position frontier asks what position current work occupies and
what must survive for work to feel continuous. It provisionally characterizes
Current Work Position as selected, bounded, continuation-relevant orientation:
what is active, why it is active, what unresolved pressure it occupies, what
authority and validation boundaries constrain it, and what movement is safe.

Active Edge may be a component inside that position, the pressure that makes the
position current, or merely another name for the selected unresolved portion of
work. This document must keep those possibilities open.

### Continuity Frontier

The continuity frontier distinguishes survival through change from strict
identity, storage, persistence, and lineage. It identifies working-position
continuity, inquiry continuity, handoff continuity, and relationship continuity
as candidate continuity pressures.

Active Edge inherits a continuity question: when work moves from one frontier to
another, what remains active, and what shifts? If nothing active survives,
continuation may become reconstruction rather than continuation.

### Persistence Frontier

The persistence frontier found that questions, gaps, contradictions, findings,
frontiers, inquiry movement, relationships, and working state may survive without
remaining unchanged. It also distinguishes persistence from storage and identity.

Active Edge therefore cannot mean merely retained content. A preserved gap may
be inactive. A contradiction may persist as recorded knowledge without pulling
current work forward.

### Inquiry Frontier

The inquiry frontier characterizes inquiry as active or preserved pursuit of
unresolved understanding. It treats questions, gaps, tensions, findings,
frontiers, open questions, working state, investigation branches, active context,
selection rationale, working knowledge lineage, and handoff lineage as mixed
candidates.

Active Edge is close to inquiry because it appears at the unresolved edge of
understanding. But it may not be identical to inquiry. Some active edges may be
risk, authority boundary, validation state, or next-safe-move pressure rather
than a question alone.

### Selection And Attention Frontiers

The selection-and-attention frontier separates selection, priority, relevance,
active attention, and working state. It states that attention is actual focus,
priority is what should receive focus, and selection explains why a path is
active, preferred, deferred, or excluded. It also observes that frontiers can be
active edges of exploration while future-frontier preservation does not imply
priority or sequencing.

The attention-trigger and attention-target frontiers refine that pressure. A
trigger may draw attention; a target may receive attention; neither necessarily
settles why the work should continue from that edge or what unresolved pressure
survives handoff.

### Relationship Frontier

The relationship frontier treats relationship as a necessary explanatory pressure
signal, not a reconciled primitive. It shows that support, contradiction,
authority, dependency, identity, adoption, recommendation, and working-state
usefulness often require connection semantics that are not reducible to isolated
objects.

Active Edge may be relationship-shaped. It may occur when a connection among a
question, tension, authority boundary, finding, and next safe move becomes the
current pull of work. But the relationship frontier also warns that graph or
relationship implementation remains premature.

### Object, Role, And Operation Frontier

The object / role / operation frontier warns against prematurely classifying a
phenomenon as a thing, contextual participation, or happening. Active Edge has
features of all three: it may point to an unresolved thing, it may name a role a
question or contradiction plays in current work, and it may appear through
activation or continuation movement. This mixed behavior is evidence for caution,
not for immediate categorization.

## Background Observation

The repository repeatedly preserves large amounts of knowledge while only a
small subset exerts current pull. Several patterns recur:

1. Many questions are documented, but only one question may become the next
   investigation.
2. Many gaps persist, but only one gap may block safe movement.
3. Many contradictions can be known, but only one contradiction may force the
   current boundary test.
4. Many frontiers exist, but only one frontier may be the active exploration
   edge.
5. Many relationships are represented, but only some relationships constrain the
   present work.
6. Many findings remain preserved, but only selected findings determine the next
   safe move.

The active-edge question begins at this distinction:

```text
preserved knowledge may be inactive;
some unresolved pressure may be active.
```

The repository evidence does not yet prove whether that pressure is a distinct
ontology concept. It does show that continuation, attention, selection, inquiry,
and current work position repeatedly need vocabulary for the distinction.

## Candidate Participants

The following candidates are evaluated as possible active-edge participants. The
question is not whether each candidate exists in the repository. The question is
whether repository evidence suggests that it can become the currently activated
source of work-forward pressure.

### Question

A question is a strong active-edge candidate. Inquiry work already treats
questions as central to unresolved understanding. A question becomes active when
it does more than remain listed: it frames what evidence must be read, what
boundary must be tested, and what future participant should continue.

Evidence supporting active-edge participation:

- frontier documents commonly begin from a central question;
- continuation succeeds when the current question and its constraints remain
  recoverable;
- a question can focus attention without yet producing a reconciliation.

Falsifying pressure:

- many questions can be preserved without pulling work forward;
- a question alone may be too broad unless paired with tension, authority
  boundary, and next safe move;
- some active work is driven by risk, contradiction, or validation state rather
  than a cleanly phrased question.

Evaluation:

```text
A question can become an active edge when it is the live unresolved pressure
organizing current movement, not merely an archived prompt.
```

### Gap

A gap is a strong but conditional candidate. A gap can pull work forward when it
blocks understanding, navigation, continuation, reconciliation, or safe
implementation.

Evidence supporting active-edge participation:

- persistence and continuity work identify gaps as candidates that may survive
  across revision and continuation;
- handoff work suggests that unresolved gaps need to be preserved when they
  determine what a later participant should inspect;
- current-work-position work treats gaps as possible components of active
  position.

Falsifying pressure:

- not every gap matters now;
- a gap can be known but intentionally deferred;
- a gap may be background incompleteness rather than current work pressure.

Evaluation:

```text
A gap can become an active edge when its absence of understanding blocks the
current safe path.
```

### Contradiction

A contradiction is one of the clearest examples of active versus inactive
preserved concern. Many contradictions may exist simultaneously. Only some force
current work.

Evidence supporting active-edge participation:

- contradiction-discovery work distinguishes visibility from creation and warns
  against treating all surfaced conflicts as resolved truth;
- relationship and continuity work identify contradiction as a relationship-like
  pressure that may persist and constrain work;
- current-work-position work treats active contradiction as belonging only when
  it shapes the current safe path.

Falsifying pressure:

- a contradiction can be historical, stale, resolved, or irrelevant to current
  movement;
- a contradiction may attract attention without being the selected work edge;
- some contradictions require preservation but not immediate investigation.

Evaluation:

```text
A contradiction becomes an active edge when incompatibility constrains what can
safely be claimed, reconciled, or done next.
```

### Frontier

A frontier is a strong candidate because Seed uses frontier documents to preserve
unresolved architecture and ontology pressure without implementation. The phrase
"active frontier" already appears in repository navigation and status language.

Evidence supporting active-edge participation:

- frontier documents intentionally preserve active exploration framing;
- selection-and-attention work explicitly describes frontiers as active edges of
  exploration;
- current-work-position work finds that an active frontier may belong to the
  current position.

Falsifying pressure:

- a frontier can contain many possible edges;
- a frontier can be preserved for future work without being current priority;
- a frontier document may become background authority rather than active work.

Evaluation:

```text
A frontier can be an active edge, but often only at a coarse grain. Active work
may need the selected question, tension, or boundary inside the frontier.
```

### Finding

A finding can become active when it constrains the present move or explains why a
future move is safe or unsafe. This is especially visible in architectural
findings preservation and current-work-position work.

Evidence supporting active-edge participation:

- findings can carry constraints forward;
- findings can explain why an implementation path is rejected;
- selected findings can determine the next safe investigation or cleanup.

Falsifying pressure:

- most findings are preserved knowledge, not active pressure;
- a finding can be authoritative background without being the current edge;
- if its relevance is not preserved, a future participant may not know why it was
  selected.

Evaluation:

```text
A finding can become an active edge when its applicability is live and it changes
what movement is safe now.
```

### Relationship

A relationship can become an active edge when the connection itself is the
unresolved pressure: for example, whether support, contradiction, authority,
dependency, identity, lineage, or continuity connects two represented items.

Evidence supporting active-edge participation:

- the relationship frontier found relationship to be a pressure signal across
  support, contradiction, authority, dependency, identity, recommendation, and
  working-state usefulness;
- continuity work identifies relationship continuity as a candidate;
- active work often depends on how a question relates to evidence, findings,
  authority boundaries, and next safe moves.

Falsifying pressure:

- not every represented relationship is active;
- active edge may move across relationships without being any one relationship;
- relationship implementation remains premature and cannot be assumed.

Evaluation:

```text
A relationship can become an active edge when the unresolved connection is what
pulls work forward, but Active Edge should not be collapsed into graph semantics.
```

### Concern

A concern is a broad candidate. It may name what matters before the repository
has a more precise classification as question, risk, gap, contradiction, or
frontier.

Evidence supporting active-edge participation:

- architectural status and maps organize repository work by concerns;
- current work often begins from a concern that has not yet resolved into a
  precise ontology candidate;
- handoff and active-context work ask what matters right now.

Falsifying pressure:

- concern is too broad to distinguish active from merely important;
- a concern can be durable background rather than current pull;
- without sharper boundaries, concern may hide priority or preference.

Evaluation:

```text
A concern can host an active edge, but usually needs a more specific unresolved
pressure to explain current movement.
```

### Risk

Risk can become an active edge when the possibility of unsafe overclaim,
premature implementation, authority violation, or evidence loss blocks a move.

Evidence supporting active-edge participation:

- repository documents repeatedly preserve non-goals and authority boundaries to
  prevent premature implementation;
- current-work-position work includes risks and validation state among position
  candidates;
- architectural status lists paused/non-current work to prevent unsafe movement.

Falsifying pressure:

- risk may constrain work without being the topic of work;
- risk can be background policy or non-goal rather than active edge;
- treating risk as active priority could over-produce defensive process.

Evaluation:

```text
Risk can become an active edge when avoiding a specific unsafe move is the live
constraint on continuation.
```

### Ambiguity

Ambiguity is a plausible active-edge participant when unclear meaning prevents
safe interpretation, classification, or continuation.

Evidence supporting active-edge participation:

- language, intent, and candidate-meaning documents preserve ambiguity boundaries;
- object/role/operation work exists because classification ambiguity recurs;
- active-edge investigation itself begins from ambiguity around active pressure.

Falsifying pressure:

- many ambiguities are harmless or deferred;
- ambiguity can attract attention while not being selected;
- ambiguity may be a symptom of an underlying question, gap, or contradiction.

Evaluation:

```text
Ambiguity can become an active edge when unresolved meaning blocks safe movement.
```

### Unresolved Tension

Unresolved tension is one of the strongest candidates. Frontier work often
advances by preserving tension rather than resolving it prematurely.

Evidence supporting active-edge participation:

- current-work-position work treats selected tension as a strong candidate;
- inquiry work includes tensions among candidate inquiry objects;
- handoff-lineage work preserves unresolved tensions for continuation.

Falsifying pressure:

- tension without selection rationale becomes noise;
- many tensions are documented but dormant;
- tension may be a relation among other participants rather than an active edge
  itself.

Evaluation:

```text
An unresolved tension becomes an active edge when it is the selected pressure
that must remain unresolved yet carried forward.
```

### Recommendation

Recommendation is a weaker candidate. It can point toward a next move, but it is
not necessarily the source of pressure.

Evidence supporting active-edge participation:

- assessment, recommendation, and decision documents distinguish recommendation
  from decision and authority;
- next-safe-move language often resembles a recommendation;
- a recommendation may preserve where work should continue.

Falsifying pressure:

- a recommendation may be inactive, rejected, superseded, or background advice;
- recommendation can encode priority without preserving the underlying edge;
- treating recommendation as active edge risks confusing guidance with the
  unresolved concern that justified it.

Evaluation:

```text
A recommendation is usually not the active edge. It may carry or point to an
active edge when it preserves the unresolved pressure and rationale behind a
next move.
```

## Critical Examples

### Example 1: Contradiction

Many contradictions may exist. A contradiction becomes current work only when it
is not merely recorded but constrains the present path.

What became active may not be the contradiction object alone. It may be a bundle:

```text
conflicting claims
+ evidence or authority that makes conflict visible
+ reason the conflict matters now
+ unsafe moves blocked by the conflict
+ next safe investigation boundary
```

This suggests Active Edge may be an activated unresolved pressure rather than a
stored contradiction.

### Example 2: Frontier Transition

The sequence:

```text
Persistence Frontier
    -> Continuity Frontier
    -> Current Work Position Frontier
```

shows both survival and shift.

What remained active:

- preserved information is insufficient;
- survival through change is not identity;
- continuation requires more than storage;
- active work needs orientation, constraints, and safe movement.

What shifted:

- persistence asked what survives;
- continuity asked how survival remains intelligible through change;
- current work position asked what position must survive for work to resume.

The active edge appears to have moved from preserved survival, to survival
through change, to selected continuation orientation. The same pressure did not
remain identical, but a lineage of unresolved concern continued to pull work
forward.

### Example 3: Failed Continuation

In failed continuation, information may be preserved while work stalls. The
handoff-lineage and current-work-position frontiers explain this as loss of
active investigation, selection rationale, current frontier, constraints,
validation state, unresolved tension, or next safe move.

Active-edge interpretation:

```text
The repository retained content, but the live unresolved pressure needed for
safe resumption was missing, hidden, or no longer distinguishable from
background information.
```

This supports the active-edge hypothesis.

### Example 4: Successful Continuation

In successful continuation, preserved information is not merely available. A
future participant can tell:

- what is active;
- why it is active;
- what alternatives are deferred;
- what authority boundaries apply;
- what evidence has been read;
- what remains unresolved;
- what move is safe next.

Active-edge interpretation:

```text
The edge of unresolved pressure was preserved well enough to be reactivated.
```

This does not prove Active Edge is a separate object. It supports treating
active-edge preservation as a useful frontier question.

### Example 5: Repository History

Many open questions exist simultaneously across Seed. Only some receive
immediate attention. Repository status documents solve part of this by naming
current implementation priority, while frontier documents solve another part by
preserving unresolved ontology pressure.

What distinguishes active from inactive is not a single factor. Evidence points
to a compound of:

- current relevance;
- unresolved pressure;
- selection rationale;
- authority boundary;
- continuation need;
- risk or blockage;
- attention attraction;
- safe next movement.

This argues against defining Active Edge as simple priority.

## Active Edge Versus Attention

Attention is actual focus. An active edge is the candidate pressure that may
make focus intelligible.

Repository evidence supports three possibilities:

1. Attention can reveal an active edge.
2. Attention can be attracted by an active edge.
3. Attention can land on something that is not the active edge.

A noisy document, surprising phrase, or recent edit may attract attention without
being the work-forward pressure. Conversely, a preserved authority boundary may
remain active even when it is not the sentence currently being read.

Provisional distinction:

```text
attention = realized focus
active edge = unresolved pressure that makes current work need movement
```

Unresolved tension:

```text
Does an active edge exist before attention activates it, or is it only an edge
once attention/working state makes it current?
```

---

## Active Edge Versus Selection

Selection explains why something is included, preferred, ordered, deferred, or
excluded. Active Edge asks what selected or activated pressure is pulling work
forward.

Selection is not sufficient. Something can be selected for citation, navigation,
background, authority, or completeness while remaining inactive. A document can
select a reference without making it the live edge of work.

Something may also appear active without explicit selection when a contradiction,
risk, or ambiguity blocks continuation before anyone has named it as selected.
That does not mean selection is irrelevant. It means explicit selection rationale
may be what makes the active edge preservable.

Provisional distinction:

```text
selection = boundary/order/rationale for inclusion or focus
active edge = the live unresolved pressure among selected or emergent concerns
```

Unresolved tension:

```text
Can active edge be preserved without selection rationale, or is selection
rationale necessary for continuation?
```

## Active Edge Versus Current Work Position

Current Work Position is broader than Active Edge if the repository's
provisional characterization holds. Work position may include:

- active edge;
- selected context;
- authority boundaries;
- validation state;
- risks;
- relevant findings;
- current frontier;
- next safe move;
- non-goals.

Active Edge may be the pressure within that position that makes the position
current rather than archival.

A work position may contain multiple active-edge candidates: an active question,
a selected tension, an authority risk, and a next safe move. It is unresolved
whether one of these is primary or whether the active edge is the relation among
them.

Provisional distinction:

```text
current work position = bounded continuation orientation
active edge = the currently activated unresolved pressure inside or across that
orientation
```

Unresolved tension:

```text
Is Active Edge a part of Current Work Position, a name for its active pressure,
or redundant once Current Work Position is reconciled?
```

---

## Active Edge Versus Inquiry

Inquiry is active or preserved pursuit of unresolved understanding. Active Edge
is close to the point where that pursuit currently presses forward.

Inquiry may require an active edge when it is live rather than merely archived.
However, preserved inquiry can exist without immediate active pressure. A record
of a question, branch, or tension may remain as inquiry lineage while not being
the current edge.

An active edge may also exist without inquiry if the live pressure is a risk,
authority boundary, validation gap, or continuation point in implementation
cleanup. Even then, it often has inquiry-like structure because it asks what is
safe, unresolved, or blocked.

Provisional distinction:

```text
inquiry = pursuit of unresolved understanding
active edge = the live pressure point from which pursuit or continuation moves
```

Unresolved tension:

```text
Is Active Edge merely the active segment of inquiry, or can non-inquiry work
have active edges too?
```

## Active Edge Versus Relationship

Relationships may persist and may exhibit continuity. They may also become the
work-forward pressure when the unresolved issue is how things connect.

Examples:

- Does this evidence support that claim?
- Does this contradiction block that reconciliation?
- Does this handoff preserve that continuation point?
- Does this frontier inherit pressure from a prior frontier?
- Does this role belong to an object or operation?

In those cases, the active edge may be relationship-shaped. It may also move
between relationships as work shifts from support to contradiction, from
contradiction to authority, or from authority to next safe move.

Provisional distinction:

```text
relationship = represented connection or connection-pressure
active edge = currently activated unresolved pressure, which may be a
relationship or may move through relationships
```

Unresolved tension:

```text
Is Active Edge itself relational, or does it only depend on relationships among
question, gap, finding, authority, and next move?
```

---

## Active Edge Versus Priority

Priority names what should receive attention or action under some ordering.
Active Edge names what is pulling current work forward. They can align, but they
can also diverge.

Repository status makes Prometheus cleanup the current implementation priority,
while this document is an exploratory ontology task. That shows priority and
active edge can operate at different scopes. A low-priority future frontier can
contain a sharply preserved active edge for later continuation. A high-priority
cleanup can be blocked by an active contradiction or risk not visible in a
priority list.

Provisional distinction:

```text
priority = normative or recommended ordering
active edge = live unresolved pressure in the current work context
```

Unresolved tension:

```text
When priority and active pressure diverge, which one should a continuation
artifact preserve first?
```

This document does not answer that question by designing a prioritization
system.

## Testing The Potential Finding

Potential finding under test:

```text
Active Edge is the currently activated source of inquiry pressure, attention,
continuation energy, or unresolved concern.
```

### Supporting Evidence

The repository evidence supports parts of this characterization:

- handoff work shows continuation can fail despite preserved information;
- current-work-position work requires knowing what is active and why;
- selection-and-attention work separates actual focus from priority and
  selection rationale;
- inquiry work distinguishes unresolved pursuit from knowledge;
- continuity work shows survival through change without strict sameness;
- relationship work shows connection-pressure can be central to unresolved work.

Together, these findings support the phrase:

```text
currently activated source of unresolved work-forward pressure
```

### Falsifying Evidence

The characterization may be too broad or too metaphorical:

- "continuation energy" is not a settled repository concept;
- attention may be an effect rather than part of Active Edge;
- inquiry pressure may not cover risk, validation, or authority boundaries;
- unresolved concern may be too broad to distinguish active edge from concern;
- current work position may already cover the phenomenon without another term;
- selection rationale may explain activation without needing an Active Edge
  concept;
- relationships may account for edge-like behavior without a new candidate.

### Revised Provisional Characterization

A narrower, safer characterization is:

```text
Active Edge may be the currently activated unresolved pressure that explains why
work continues from this concern rather than from other preserved concerns.
```

This remains exploratory. It does not define an object, role, operation,
relationship, state, engine, or implementation requirement.

## Why Implementation Remains Premature

Implementation remains premature for several reasons:

1. Active Edge has not been reconciled as a distinct ontology concept.
2. It may collapse into Current Work Position, active context, inquiry state,
   selection rationale, attention, relationship semantics, or continuity.
3. Candidate participants are heterogeneous: questions, gaps, contradictions,
   findings, relationships, risks, ambiguities, tensions, and recommendations do
   not share one settled type.
4. The repository has not resolved whether activation is caused by attention,
   selection, continuity need, authority constraint, or unresolved pressure.
5. Priority and active pressure remain distinct, so implementation could
   accidentally create a prioritizer.
6. Handoff and continuation evidence supports preservation of active pressure,
   but not a runtime mechanism for detecting or managing it.
7. Relationship-shaped evidence is strong, but relationship ontology itself is
   not reconciled as a primitive.
8. Any schema, scheduler, planner, workflow engine, attention engine, or
   execution behavior would over-promote exploratory vocabulary into machinery.

This document therefore proposes no system, schema, identifier, detector,
ranking method, lifecycle, runtime behavior, planner, scheduler, workflow,
attention mechanism, or execution change.

## Summary Findings

### What An Active Edge Might Be

Active Edge might be the currently activated unresolved pressure that explains
why work continues from one concern rather than from other preserved concerns.
It may appear as a question, gap, contradiction, frontier, relationship, tension,
risk, ambiguity, selected finding, or next-safe-move pressure.

### Why Active-Edge Pressure Emerged

The pressure emerged because Seed repeatedly found that preservation is not
continuation. Information can persist while the live edge of work is lost.
Handoffs, current work position, continuity, inquiry, selection, and attention
all needed language for what remains active.

### Strongest Findings

1. Preserved information can be inactive.
2. Successful continuation appears to require preservation or reconstruction of
   the active unresolved pressure.
3. Active Edge is not safely reducible to attention, selection, priority,
   inquiry, frontier, relationship, or current work position.
4. Selection rationale is central because it explains why this edge, not another,
   became active.
5. Current Work Position is probably broader than Active Edge.
6. Relationships can become active edges when the unresolved connection itself
   pulls work forward.

### Strongest Falsifying Findings

1. Current Work Position may already cover the phenomenon.
2. Inquiry may cover active unresolved pursuit without a separate Active Edge.
3. Selection rationale may explain activation enough by itself.
4. Attention may be the only needed active/current distinction.
5. Relationship semantics may explain edge-like pressure without another term.
6. The candidate set is heterogeneous enough that Active Edge may be only a
   useful metaphor.

### Attention Findings

Attention is actual focus. Active Edge may attract, justify, or be revealed by
attention, but attention can also focus on inactive or incidental material.

### Selection Findings

Selection is necessary for preserving why an edge is active, but selection is
not sufficient. Selected references, contexts, or findings may remain background.

### Inquiry Findings

Inquiry strongly overlaps with Active Edge. Active Edge may be the active segment
of inquiry, but risk, authority, validation, and continuation pressure suggest
that not every active edge is simply a question.

### Continuation Findings

Continuation is the strongest evidence for Active Edge. Work can stall when
information is preserved but active pressure, rationale, boundary, and next safe
move are lost. Work resumes faster when the edge is preserved or reconstructable.

### Current Work Position Findings

Current Work Position likely contains the active edge plus context, boundaries,
validation state, risks, findings, and safe movement. Active Edge may be the
pressure that makes that position current.

### Unresolved Tensions

The unresolved tensions are active edge versus attention, selection, inquiry,
current work position, frontier, contradiction, relationship, concern, and
priority. These tensions should remain visible for future reconciliation.
