## 4. Recovered presentation-road standing

The surviving implementation witnesses independently warrant these presentation responsibilities:

- `belongs != admitted`: membership evidence may say a candidate belongs while admission may omit it for a requested presentation.
- `admitted != sequenced`: admission only creates an admitted-to-sequencing set; sequencing requires explicit encounter evidence.
- `sequenced != composed`: sequencing orders admitted encounters and preserves unsequenced admitted material; composition consumes exactly one sequencing artifact.
- `composed presentation != completed inquiry`: bounded composition explicitly refuses to imply inquiry closure, operator completion, or prevention of later constraint, expansion, forking, supersession, or reopening.

These are not merely artifacts of the deleted explanation producer because each distinction is preserved by surviving producers, fields, and boundary strings. But their survival does not prove upstream candidate ingress, Answer standing, rendering-after-composition standing, outward Fidelity, or emission.

The former `SharedExplanationRenderingProjection` is best recovered as a compressed boundary among an explanation representation, a rendering result, and presentation-candidate material. Its class name and formatter mark rendering; its fields say it consumes one already-produced stage-local explanation and preserves stage-owned material under display labels; its downstream membership projection consumes it as the candidate. It is not independently evidenced as Answer standing, and it is not simply a presentation candidate by constitutional production because the candidate-ingress warrant is missing.

## 5. Exact current frontier-to-Question gap

The prior goal-to-question survey remains true on current `main`: no current producer was found that consumes `BoundedInquiryFrontier` and produces `BoundedConstitutionalQuestion`, `ConstitutionalQuestionProjection`, or any other Question-standing artifact. The current frontier artifact carries selected need and testimony lineage but explicitly refuses formulation, opening, source/observation selection, admission, authorization, execution, recording, event-ledger writes, mutation, and result knowledge. The current question producer is explicit-caller-input only, and its own default boundaries refuse QuestionProjection production at that production stage.

The gap is therefore not that the repository lacks all question-shaped artifacts. The exact gap is the absent constitutional crossing:

## 8. Rendering, outward Fidelity, and emission boundaries

Rendering is currently distinct in grammar from relevance determination, membership, presentation admission, sequencing, composition, and emission. Implementation, however, compresses parts of that boundary. `SharedExplanationRenderingProjection` is named as rendering but used downstream as candidate material; `format_shared_explanation_bounded_composition()` renders text from a composition but does not produce a separate constitutional rendering artifact or outward Fidelity finding.

Fidelity must be examined in both directions:

1. `external grammar characterization -> internal standing`: current external-grammar grammar supports attributed external material, translation, comparison, and bounded consumption without native promotion.
2. `internal Answer/composition -> external representation`: current communication grammar says representation must preserve source subject, identity, content, provenance, standing, purpose, scope, evidence, uncertainty, conflicts, negative authority, Unknowns, refusals, and stopping/completion limits. But no current producer was found that compares a rendered representation against an internal Answer/composition and emits an outward Fidelity finding for this road.

Therefore external grammar, internal grammar, and Fidelity should not be flattened into three peer families. External grammar is source/representation testimony, internal grammar is constitutional standing, and Fidelity is a constraint/comparison relation that requires a local producer and comparison basis when a specific outward representation is at issue.

Emission remains later than rendering and Fidelity comparison. The Book's communication grammar distinguishes representation formation, emission, delivery, receipt, interpretation, uptake, reliance, responsibility transition, authority transition, and external realization. A current CLI/report can emit text operationally, but the recovered constitutional chain lacks a dedicated outward Fidelity finding and emission occurrence from the shared presentation road.
