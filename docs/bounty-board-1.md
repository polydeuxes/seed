# Memo

This memo preserves emerging investigation pressures that appeared during exploratory discussion but were not yet mature enough for formal observations, audits, frontiers, or reconciliations.

## Claim-Centric Revisit

Pressure:

Why did claim-centric architecture become necessary?

Recent lineage, preservation, and observation-surface investigations repeatedly identify claim-centric architecture as foundational while weakly preserving the failure history that made it necessary.

Candidate future investigation:

- Recover failure signals that preceded claim-centric architecture.
- Distinguish preserved conclusion from preserved necessity.
- Determine whether explanatory-load, preservation-failure, or lens failure pressures contributed.

## Lens And Representation

Pressure:

Repository evidence increasingly suggests a distinction between operator-facing lenses and repository-facing representations.

Examples:

- fact -> claim/support
- learning -> support change / understanding change / derivation

Candidate future investigation:

- What work does a lens perform?
- How do lenses differ from representations?
- When does a lens remain useful?
- When does a lens accumulate excessive explanatory load?
