# Structural Recurrence Visibility Investigation

## Purpose and boundary

This investigation asks whether recurring document structures can become visible without requiring prose interpretation.

This is investigation only.

It does not implement recurrence detection, shape systems, prose interpretation, NLP, ontology work, claim extraction, workflow systems, or automation.

Repository authority remains repository-visible document structure.

## Central finding

Repository evidence supports a distinction between:

```text
structural recurrence
```

and:

```text
meaning recurrence
```

Structural recurrence appears observable without semantic interpretation.

The strongest supported conclusion is:

```text
recurrence visibility
can exist at the structure layer
```

before:

```text
interpretation
```

or:

```text
meaning extraction
```

occur.

## Structural recurrence versus meaning recurrence

Repository evidence supports a meaningful distinction.

### Structural recurrence

Examples:

```text
"Supported Conclusions"
appears N times
```

```text
three-level heading hierarchy
appears M times
```

```text
yaml front matter
appears K times
```

These are observable.

No interpretation is required.

### Meaning recurrence

Examples:

```text
same concept appears repeatedly

same claim appears repeatedly

same distinction appears repeatedly
```

These require interpretation.

Meaning recurrence is outside the scope of this investigation.

## Counterexamples

### Recurrence that is observable but not useful

Examples:

```text
exact punctuation frequency

exact character frequency
```

Visibility exists.

Utility may be limited.

Therefore:

```text
recurrence
    != useful visibility
```

### Recurrence that pressures interpretation

Examples:

```text
heading names

metadata names
```

The structural recurrence is visible.

Meaning remains a separate layer.

### Recurrence mistaken for meaning

Examples:

```text
same section label
```

does not imply:

```text
same content
```

Likewise:

```text
same structure
```

does not imply:

```text

```

```text
    -> recurrence visibility
```


## Supported conclusions

1. Repository documents contain recurring structural elements.
2. Structural recurrence can be observed without semantic interpretation.
3. Useful visibility may emerge from common, rare, and missing structures.
4. Coverage, consistency, inventory, and navigation visibility appear possible without meaning extraction.
5. Structural recurrence is distinct from meaning recurrence.
6. Structural recurrence visibility is compatible with observation-first philosophy.
7. Structural recurrence is a stronger and more conservative claim than shape inference.

## Unsupported conclusions

- Structural recurrence reveals document meaning.
- Structural recurrence requires NLP.
- Structural recurrence requires ontology promotion.
- Structural recurrence automatically implies usefulness.
- Structural recurrence implies shared intent.
- Structural recurrence implies shape discovery.
