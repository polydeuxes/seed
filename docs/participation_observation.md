# Participation Observation

## Purpose

This document observes whether repository evidence supports `participation` as a
useful lens for the boundary between:

```text
answer available
```

and:

```text
answer governing work
```

It asks:

```text
What appears to participate
when knowledge governs work?
```

```text
What appears absent
when knowledge becomes inert?
```

```text
Is participation a useful observation lens,
or merely a compression
of activation,
selection,
current concern,
continuation,
and relation of use?
```

This document does not assume that participation is useful, durable, canonical,
or implementation-relevant. It is not a reconciliation, frontier, runtime
proposal, response proposal, UI proposal, frontend architecture proposal,
workflow proposal, remediation proposal, schema proposal, ontology proposal, or
implementation plan. Repository authority remains with the documents, maps,
frontiers, reconciliations, audits, runtime surfaces, and tests that own their
own scopes.

## Major Findings

### 1. Participation appears strongest as an episode relation, not as stored knowledge

Repository evidence repeatedly distinguishes preserved material from material
that is governing the present work. Interaction-as-evidence, relation-of-use,
working-state activation, situatedness, and preservation-failure documents all
preserve versions of the same negative pattern:

```text
knowledge can survive
and still not govern work
```

When knowledge does govern work, the participating item does not appear to be
knowledge alone. It appears to be a situated configuration containing at least
some of the following:

- a knowledge item, claim, boundary, answer, finding, or document;
- a current concern or active question;
- a participant able to take the item up;
- a present work position in which the item matters;
- a selection or narrowing that makes this item relevant rather than merely
  available;
- pressure, consequence, or risk that explains why the item matters now;
- a continuation or safe-move relation that constrains what can happen next;
- enough orientation for the participant to know how the item bears on work.

That does not prove that participation is a separate architectural concept. It
only suggests that when repository authors say an answer is governing work, they
are observing more than archival availability.

### 2. What appears to participate is often a role-bearing relation

Object/role/operation and relation-cluster work already warns that participation
language often names contextual roles rather than durable objects. In this
review, participation usually appeared as a temporary role or relation assigned
to otherwise durable things:

```text
claim as selected constraint
frontier as current unresolved boundary
document as active orientation source
answer as governing condition
pressure as current reason to continue
concern as work-organizing subject
```

The same object can persist while its participation changes. A document can be
inventory in one episode, orientation in another, a governing boundary in a
third, and inert background in a fourth. That makes participation plausible as an
observation lens for current use, but weak as an identity category.

### 3. Governing knowledge appears to require a current concern, but current concern is not enough

Current-concern, reference-point, pressure, and selection documents make it
plausible that knowledge governs only when it is tied to what presently matters.
However, current concern alone does not explain participation. A concern can be
visible but not selected. It can be selected but not understood. It can be
understood but not safe to continue from. It can be pressure-bearing but not
connected to a specific next move.

Observed governing cases therefore appear to involve a relation among:

```text
knowledge
participant
current concern
current work
```

but the relation is not uniform. Sometimes the knowledge constrains scope.
Sometimes it orients continuation. Sometimes it explains why a pressure exists.
Sometimes it selects among alternatives. Sometimes it prevents duplicate work.
The evidence supports heterogeneity more strongly than a single participation
mechanism.

### 4. Inert knowledge is not merely missing visibility

Operator-surface and preservation documents show that visibility can preserve
inventory without preserving use. Inert knowledge cases often have one or more
of these absences:

- no active concern connecting the item to present work;
- no selection rationale explaining why this item rather than adjacent items;
- no pressure or consequence preserving why it matters now;
- no continuation relation or safe-move boundary;
- no orientation linking the item to current work position;
- no participant uptake, despite availability;
- no currentness signal, or a stale currentness signal;
- no relation of use after preservation has retained the artifact.

The review did not find evidence that all inert cases share one missing
component. The safest observation is plural: knowledge becomes inert in multiple
ways.

### 5. Current Work Position appears to preserve a mixture, not participation alone

Current Work Position asks what position current work occupies and what must
survive for work to feel continuous. In this review it appears to preserve a
mixture of orientation, selected concern, continuation posture, boundary
awareness, and enough relation to resume work. It can include participating
knowledge, but it is not reducible to participation.

Current Work Position therefore looks broader than participation. It can explain
where participation would be evaluated:

```text
from this work position,
which knowledge matters,
for which concern,
and toward what continuation?
```

But the frontier does not by itself settle whether participation is a durable
concept or a compression.

### 6. Active Edge appears narrower than Current Work Position and closer to participating pressure

Active Edge asks what is currently pulling work forward. It appears closer to a
participating concern, selected pressure source, unresolved question, or active
continuation boundary than to knowledge in general. Repository evidence supports
several readings at once:

- a participating concern that organizes work;
- a selected concern among many preserved concerns;
- a pressure source that has not disappeared;
- an unresolved question that keeps work live;
- a continuation edge that marks where safe movement is constrained.

The evidence does not force one of these readings. Active Edge may be the place
where participation language feels strongest, because it names what is active
rather than what is merely preserved. It may also make participation redundant by
already covering the live-pull portion of the phenomenon.

### 7. Participation may be a useful lens precisely because it is compressed

Lens and compression work suggests that broad terms can help group scattered
evidence before later decomposition. Participation appears to function this way.
It usefully holds together activation, selection, current concern,
continuation, relation of use, pressure, and participant uptake long enough to
ask whether preserved knowledge is actually governing work.

The danger is also visible: if participation replaces those component questions,
it can hide the evidence that made the observation useful. The repository gives
stronger support to participation as a temporary diagnostic lens than to
participation as an independent architecture object.

## Participation Findings

Observed participation, when knowledge governs work, appears to involve:

```text
knowledge taken up
by a participant
from a current work position
for a current concern
under selection, pressure, continuation, or safe-move constraints
```

This is not a definition. It is a recurring evidence shape. The repository also
contains weaker or alternative shapes where participation means behavior
evidence, role occupancy, relation endpoint involvement, or interaction with an
artifact. Those meanings should not be collapsed.

Participation appears useful for asking:

- Is the answer merely available, or is it governing this work?
- Is a preserved finding actually constraining the next move?
- Is the participant using the knowledge in the current concern?
- Has pressure survived as a work-organizing force, or only as text?
- Is an artifact visible as inventory but absent from continuation?

Participation appears weak or unsafe for asserting:

- ownership;
- authority;
- truth;
- priority;
- implementation readiness;
- runtime representation;
- stable ontology;
- UI or response behavior.

## Duplicate-Work Findings

This review found substantial overlap with existing documents. The new
observation should not be read as superseding them.

- Relation-of-use documents already ask why knowledge remains useful or becomes
  inert.
- Working-state activation documents already record the `answer exists / answer
  found / answer read / incorrect work still occurs` failure shape.
- Interaction-as-evidence already asks when static artifacts cannot show whether
  knowledge is governing a participant's present work.
- Current Work Position and Active Edge frontiers already own exploratory
  pressure around current orientation and live pull.
- Object/role/operation work already treats participation as often role-like and
  contextual.
- Pressure and preservation observations already distinguish preserved content
  from preserved reason-for-importance.
- Lens/compression work already provides the caution that broad lenses can help
  inquiry and then become unsafe if not decompressed.

The incremental value of this document is therefore narrow: it groups those
findings around the specific question of whether `participation` helps observe
the difference between available knowledge and governing knowledge.

## Boundary Distinctions Reviewed

Repository evidence supports preserving these distinctions as questions, even
where individual cases blur them:

```text
knowledge != participation
activation != participation
selection != participation
continuation != participation
pressure != participation
interaction != participation
use != participation
answer available != answer governing work
```

The distinctions do not all survive as clean separations in every case.
Activation, selection, pressure, continuation, and relation of use often appear
as components of the participation reading. That means participation may be a
compression of those terms rather than an additional phenomenon.

## Evidence Strength

Strong evidence:

- preserved knowledge can fail to govern work;
- visibility and inventory do not guarantee participation;
- working-state activation can fail after discovery and reading;
- current concern and pressure help explain why knowledge matters now;
- Active Edge and Current Work Position are relevant but not identical;
- participation language is already used in role-like and behavior-evidence
  contexts, so authority boundaries are necessary.

Moderate evidence:

- participation is a useful temporary diagnostic lens;
- governing knowledge involves a relation among knowledge, participant, current
  concern, and current work;
- inert knowledge often lacks orientation, currentness, pressure, safe-move, or
  continuation relations.

Weak evidence:

- participation is an independent ontology candidate;
- all governing cases share one participation pattern;
- all inert cases share one missing component;
- participation can be represented without decomposing activation, selection,
  continuation, pressure, and relation of use.

## Unresolved Observations

1. It remains unresolved whether participation survives review after full
   decomposition into activation, selection, current concern, relation of use,
   continuation, pressure, and interaction.
2. It remains unresolved whether participant uptake can be observed adequately
   from repository artifacts alone, or whether interaction evidence is necessary.
3. It remains unresolved whether Active Edge already covers the live portion of
   participation strongly enough to make participation redundant.
4. It remains unresolved whether Current Work Position preserves participating
   knowledge, the conditions for participation, or only the orientation from
   which participation can be judged.
5. It remains unresolved how to distinguish knowledge that is inert because it is
   irrelevant from knowledge that is inert because activation, selection,
   currentness, or continuation failed.
6. It remains unresolved whether participation language adds clarity or merely
   compresses already better-scoped terms.

## Conclusion

Repository evidence supports `participation` as an observation lens for a real
recurring boundary:

```text
preserved knowledge
is not the same as
knowledge governing current work
```

The evidence does not support promoting participation into ontology,
implementation, response behavior, UI, workflow, or architecture. The strongest
reading is cautious: participation names a situated episode in which knowledge,
participant, current concern, current work position, selection, pressure,
continuation, and relation of use combine so that knowledge governs work. The
weakness of that reading is that each component already has more precise
repository treatment. Participation may be useful only while it remains an
explicitly decomposable lens.
