# Claim Shape Typing Observation

Observation

The claim/support side of Seed appears nearly complete compared with newer projection self-description work, but completeness does not remove the need for explicit shape.

Recent work has highlighted two parallel pressures:

```text
projection_shape
    -> describes how projected state is constructed

claim_shape
    -> describes how claims, support, confidence, contradiction, selection,
       lifecycle, and consumption are structured
```

These should remain parallel rather than collapsed.

Claim shape should describe claim reasoning and support structure. Projection shape should describe state construction and projected read-model composition.

Potential claim-shape concerns

A claim-shape description may need to identify:

- claim creation
- evidence attachment
- support aggregation
- confidence evaluation
- contradiction detection
- claim selection
- claim status or lifecycle
- claim consumption
- authority boundaries

Boundary

The observation does not propose claim behavior changes.

It records that an apparently mature claim system can still benefit from typed shape visibility, especially if Seed is expected to reason about its own claim/support/confidence machinery the same way it is beginning to reason about projection and operational surfaces.

Important distinction

Shared mechanics do not imply shared authority.

Claim shape and projection shape may share explanatory vocabulary such as consumes, produces, influences, selected_by, and authority_boundary, but they should not automatically share the same authority model.
