# Handoff Pressure Transition Observation

## Purpose

This document records an observation about handoff preservation.

Recent work suggested that handoffs preserve conclusions more easily than they preserve why inquiry moved. A future participant may receive the right documents and still miss the pressure that made one contradiction, question, or distinction matter.

This document is an observation.

It is not a frontier, reconciliation, template proposal, schema proposal, memory-system proposal, workflow proposal, governance proposal, or implementation design.

## Central Observation

A handoff can preserve:

```text
what was found
```

while weakly preserving:

```text
what broke,
why it mattered,
and why inquiry moved there.
```

Recent repository work repeatedly found that short pressure-transition notes may provide more continuation value than longer summaries when a future participant needs to recover the active edge.

## Handoff Pressure Transition

A pressure transition is a compact statement of inquiry movement:

```text
A -> B

Why:
A failed pressure in this specific way.
```

It is not the full discovery path.

It is not the full finding.

It is not the full audit.

It is the smallest useful orientation for why the active edge moved.

## Distinction From Existing Handoff Content

### Summary

A summary preserves what the session covered.

### Active Work

Active work preserves what is currently in focus.

### Open Questions

Open questions preserve unresolved inquiry.

### Pressure Transition

Pressure transition preserves why inquiry moved from one framing to another.

This distinction matters because a future participant may know the destination but not know why the previous framing became insufficient.

## Critical Examples

### Persistence -> Continuity

Pressure transition:

```text
Persistence -> Continuity

Why:
Persistence kept accumulating storage, identity, and lineage pressure. The question stopped being only what persisted and became what remained recognizable through change.
```

This compact note preserves more continuation orientation than merely listing both documents.

### Working State -> Current Work Position

Pressure transition:

```text
Working State -> Current Work Position

Why:
Working state preserved information, but continuation could still fail. The pressure shifted from stored context to preserved orientation.
```

### Current Work Position -> Active Edge

Pressure transition:

```text
Current Work Position -> Active Edge

Why:
Current work position was broader than the pressure that made the work current. Active edge named the currently activated unresolved pressure pulling work forward.
```

### Documentation Lineage -> Observation Surface / Blind Spots

Pressure transition:

```text
Documentation Lineage -> Observation Surface / Blind Spots

Why:
The lineage observation could see recent metadata-rich artifacts better than older claim-centric discovery paths. The pressure shifted from what documents generated to what the observation surface could see.
```

### Understanding -> Understanding Claim

Pressure transition:

```text
Understanding -> Understanding Claim

Why:
Understanding was being treated like a possessed state, but repository evidence showed it strengthening, weakening, decomposing, and surviving with caveats. The pressure shifted toward understanding as claim-like.
```

## Active Edge Relationship

A pressure transition is closely related to active edge, but not identical.

Active edge asks:

```text
What is currently pulling work forward?
```

Pressure transition asks:

```text
Why did the pull move here?
```

One is present-oriented.

The other preserves the inquiry movement that made the present orientation intelligible.

## Momentum Relationship

Repository participants observed that momentum matters in long-running sessions.

A participant can continue rapidly when the active edge, pressure transition, and current work position survive.

A participant may dissolve into rediscovery when only conclusions survive.

This does not make momentum a canonical concept. It records that handoff quality may depend on preserving enough pressure history for the next participant to avoid re-opening every prior framing.

## Compression Relationship

Documentation compression work observed a recurring pattern:

```text
accepted term
    -> pressure
    -> hidden distinction
    -> expanded terminology
    -> changed understanding
```

Pressure transitions preserve this movement in compact form.

They do not replace the detailed observation or critique.

They give the next participant the key that unlocks the larger document trail.

## Handoff Implication

The optional summary section of a handoff may be a useful place for pressure transitions.

Not as essays.

Not as new authority.

As short orientation lines such as:

```text
Persistence -> Continuity:
Persistence could not explain recognizable survival through change.

Working State -> Current Work Position:
Information survived, but continuation still failed.

Understanding -> Understanding Claim:
Understanding behaved as supportable and challengeable rather than fact-like.
```

These lines can carry high continuation value because they preserve why the active edge moved.

## What Future Participants May Miss

A future participant can often recover:

```text
documents
findings
conclusions
navigation routes
```

They may not recover:

```text
why a contradiction became selected,
why an old framing failed,
why one frontier followed another,
why the active edge moved,
or why the operator could not safely change gears without losing session coherence.
```

Pressure-transition notes directly target that preservation gap.

## Current Characterization

Pressure-transition notes appear to be high-density handoff material.

They preserve inquiry movement, not merely inquiry state.

They appear useful when a future participant needs to understand why the current work position is current.

The repository does not yet show that pressure transitions require a new mandatory handoff section, schema, or document type.

This observation records the preservation pressure only.

## Unresolved Observations

- Whether pressure transitions belong in optional handoff summaries remains unresolved.
- Whether pressure transition is distinct from discovery-path preservation remains unresolved.
- Whether pressure transition is a form of active-edge lineage remains unresolved.
- Whether pressure transitions should be preserved manually, generated from discovery paths, or left as participant judgment remains unresolved.
- Whether momentum is only a participant experience or a preservable continuation concern remains unresolved.
