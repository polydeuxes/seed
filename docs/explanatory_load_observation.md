# Explanatory Load Observation

## Purpose

This observation investigates a recurring repository question:

```text
What appears to happen when repository concepts are asked to explain more than
their original responsibility?
```

It does not assume that explanatory load exists as a canonical repository
concept. It does not assume that explanatory load causes decomposition. It does
not define explanatory load canonically, reconcile ontology, create a
responsibility taxonomy, propose architecture changes, propose runtime changes,
propose schema changes, propose workflow changes, propose governance changes,
propose memory or learning systems, or propose implementation.

The document observes repository evidence only.

## Method And Authority Boundary

The investigation reviewed repository documentation that already preserves
compression, decompression, understanding, knowledge, concept stability,
object / role / operation pressure, persistence, continuity, current work
position, active edge, lineage, preservation failure, observation, claim,
evidence, authority, relationship, and derivation pressure.

Primary evidence included:

- `compression_candidate_observation.md`;
- `documentation_compression_observation.md`;
- `understanding_claim_and_decompression_observation.md`;
- `knowledge_and_understanding_distinction_observation.md`;
- `concept_stability_audit.md`;
- `object_role_operation_pressure_test.md`;
- `persistence_frontier.md`;
- `continuity_frontier.md`;
- `current_work_position_frontier.md`;
- `active_edge_frontier.md`;
- `lineage_distinction_observation.md`;
- `preservation_failure_observation.md`.

Additional evidence was consulted where it materially clarified a case,
especially `derivation_frontier.md`, `observation_interpretation_and_reality_reconciliation.md`,
`reality_fact_and_claim_reconciliation.md`, `claim_support_frontier.md`,
`evidence_trust_and_source_authority_reconciliation.md`, `relationship_frontier.md`,
`documentation_lineage_observation.md`, `discovery_path_preservation_observation.md`,
`preservation_surface_observation.md`, and `bounty_board_and_investigation_selection_observation.md`.

Existing documents retain authority for their own scopes. This observation does
not promote exploratory documents to architecture authority and does not collapse
frontier, observation, audit, reconciliation, vocabulary, or implementation
surfaces into one ontology.

## High-Level Observation

Repository evidence supports a cautious pattern:

```text
some concepts became unstable when later work asked them to explain multiple
questions that their earlier boundary did not safely answer
```

The evidence also supports the counter-pattern:

```text
some concepts remained stable under pressure because they kept a bounded
responsibility and redirected adjacent questions elsewhere
```

This is not a causal law. The repository shows several different outcomes:

1. A concept decomposes into adjacent concepts.
2. A concept survives while a neighboring concept receives new explanatory work.
3. A concept remains broad but bounded because documents repeatedly refuse
   overclaiming.
4. A concept becomes a pressure signal rather than an adopted primitive.
5. A concept remains uncertain because the repository has not preserved enough
   evidence to decide what happened.

The strongest observed relation is not simply:

```text
more pressure -> decomposition
```

It is closer to:

```text
when one concept is repeatedly used to answer reality, representation,
authority, support, continuity, causality, activation, and next-action questions
at once, later documentation often separates those questions or routes them to
more bounded surfaces
```

## Candidate Explanatory-Load Cases

### Fact

`Fact` is one of the strongest candidates.

What `Fact` was being asked to explain:

- what is true in reality;
- what Seed represents;
- what evidence supports;
- what source authority permits;
- how confident a projection should be;
- how contradictions, staleness, and uncertainty should remain visible;
- when a represented proposition may appear in a fact-shaped projection.

What remained inside scope:

- fact-shaped projection remains useful where a claim is sufficiently normalized
  and supported for a particular representation surface;
- facts in reality are not denied by the claim-centric architecture;
- repository usage can still speak compactly about normalized facts when the
  support and authority boundary is preserved.

What moved outside scope:

- truth arbitration moved away from fact-shaped representation;
- support moved to evidence and claim-support surfaces;
- authority moved to source authority, documentation authority, and promotion
  boundaries;
- contradiction and freshness moved to integrity and related claim semantics;
- reality/representation distinction moved to reality/fact/claim and
  observation/interpretation boundaries.

Observed result:

```text
Fact did not disappear, but repository pressure shifted representational
explanation toward Claim, Evidence, support, confidence, authority, and
projection humility.
```

This is a strong load-shift example because the repository preserved ordinary
fact language while refusing to let `Fact` explain representation, truth,
authority, and support alone.

### Working State

`Working State` is another strong candidate.

Responsibilities that accumulated:

- preserved context;
- current activity;
- continuation readiness;
- handoff adequacy;
- live constraints;
- unresolved pressure;
- active frontier;
- selection rationale;
- validation state;
- next safe move;
- why work remains continuous rather than merely reconstructed.

Responsibilities that survived decomposition:

- working-state language still marks the broader state of work;
- continuation documents still use working state as a meaningful handoff concern;
- it remains useful as a container-like signal for activity context and momentum.

Responsibilities that shifted:

- `Current Work Position` received selected, bounded, continuation-relevant
  orientation;
- `Active Edge` received the question of what currently pulls work forward;
- handoff and continuation surfaces received availability, consumption,
  activation, and compliance distinctions;
- preservation surfaces received the question of what survives and what remains
  reactivatable.

Observed result:

```text
Working State appears to have decomposed because stored context could not safely
explain current orientation, activation, selection, live pressure, and next safe
movement by itself.
```

### Lineage

`Lineage` accumulated explanatory responsibility around survival across change.

Responsibilities that accumulated:

- artifact derivation;
- inquiry movement;
- observation surface continuity;
- discovery path;
- causal order;
- prompt or critique influence;
- why a later document exists;
- whether understanding changed;
- whether visible references preserve the pressure that generated them.

Observed result:

```text
Lineage became unstable when visible artifact relationships were asked to
explain inquiry continuity, observation continuity, discovery transition, and
causal history.
```

The repository response was not a canonical lineage taxonomy. It was an
observation that artifact lineage, inquiry lineage, observation lineage, and
discovery-path lineage appear partially overlapping and partially distinct.

### Attention

`Attention` accumulated explanatory responsibility around why work moves.

Responsibilities that accumulated:

- what receives focus;
- why focus moved;
- what should receive focus;
- what is relevant;
- what is selected;
- what pressure is active;
- whether the target, trigger, rationale, or priority is being discussed.

Observed result:

```text
Attention survived as broad language, but pressure moved into selection,
priority, relevance, attention trigger, attention target, and active-edge
questions.
```

The strongest load signal is that saying something has attention does not answer
whether it triggered attention, received attention, deserved attention, became
selected, or carried the active unresolved edge.

### Persistence

`Persistence` accumulated responsibility around survival.

Responsibilities that accumulated:

- storage;
- durable presence;
- identity over time;
- continuity through change;
- participation in future work;
- survival of questions, gaps, contradictions, findings, relationships, and
  working state;
- whether something remains active or merely retained.

Observed result:

```text
Persistence became unstable where survival was asked to explain storage,
identity, continuity, handoff reactivation, and active participation at once.
```

The repository response separated persistence from storage and identity, then
let continuity and current-work-position documents carry adjacent survival and
resumption pressure without turning persistence into all survival semantics.

### Understanding

`Understanding` accumulated responsibility around meaning change.

Responsibilities that accumulated:

- what the repository knows;
- what a participant grasps;
- what changed during decompression;
- why a conclusion matters;
- what hidden assumption was exposed;
- whether a claim, distinction, route, or frontier changes future action;
- how unknown unknowns become visible.

Observed result:

```text
Understanding appears as a pressure signal near claims, knowledge, preservation,
and decompression, but the repository has not promoted it into a canonical
ontology object.
```

The evidence shows load, but also restraint. Knowledge-and-understanding work
observed a distinction without replacing claim-centric knowledge architecture or
creating an understanding system.

### Additional Candidate: Derivation

`Derivation` appears as a candidate that received explanatory pressure from
`Observation`, `Evidence`, and `Claim` rather than simply decomposing from them.

Responsibilities that accumulated nearby:

- whether represented support justifies a new represented proposition;
- whether a conclusion is an observation, interpretation, claim, inference, or
  derived relationship;
- whether the path from evidence to proposition survives;
- whether generated propositions remain support-bound.

Observed result:

```text
Derivation appears to absorb explanation-path pressure that Observation and
Claim should not carry alone.
```

This remains a frontier, not a reconciled reasoning engine.

## Pressure Survivors

### Observation

Pressure accumulated around:

- direct observation;
- interpretation;
- evidence creation;
- claim support;
- observer surface and blind spots;
- documentation observation;
- repository observation;
- mutation boundary;
- observation lineage.

Why it remained bounded:

- observation documents repeatedly distinguish observation from interpretation,
  mutation, truth, and projection authority;
- observation can remain an input or surface without becoming the explanation of
  all meaning generated from it;
- new pressure was routed to interpretation, evidence, claim, derivation,
  observation-surface audit, and blind-spot analysis.

Responsibilities refused, redirected, or routed elsewhere:

- truth moved to claim support and authority boundaries;
- interpretation moved to interpretation-specific reconciliation;
- new proposition generation moved toward derivation frontier questions;
- visibility limits moved to observation surface and blind-spot work.

### Evidence

Pressure accumulated around:

- support;
- trust;
- source authority;
- corroboration;
- promotion;
- explanation;
- whether evidence proves truth.

Why it remained bounded:

- evidence is consistently treated as support material, not truth itself;
- source authority and promotion rules absorb authority questions;
- claim strength and confidence absorb representational strength questions.

Responsibilities refused, redirected, or routed elsewhere:

- truth arbitration is refused;
- source authority goes to evidence trust/source authority;
- promotion goes to corroboration and fact-promotion boundaries;
- contradiction visibility goes to integrity and contradiction documents.

### Claim

Pressure accumulated around:

- represented propositions;
- support;
- confidence;
- contradiction;
- future claims;
- boundary claims;
- behavior, ownership, existence, and relationship claims;
- fact-shaped projection.

Why it remained bounded:

- claim is broad but has a preserved responsibility: represented assertion with
  support, scope, and authority boundaries;
- specialized claim documents route subtypes without requiring `Claim` to settle
  every subtype internally;
- facts in reality, evidence, authority, projection, and contradiction remain
  distinct.

Responsibilities refused, redirected, or routed elsewhere:

- Claim does not become reality;
- Claim does not become evidence;
- Claim does not become authority;
- Claim does not become projection truth;
- specialized documents carry subtype pressure.

### Authority

Pressure accumulated around:

- source authority;
- documentation authority;
- operator authority;
- adoption and decision authority;
- capability authority;
- projection authority;
- whether a document can change architecture.

Why it remained bounded:

- authority repeatedly appears as scoped permission or precedence, not general
  truth;
- navigation surfaces explicitly avoid replacing authoritative documents;
- observations and frontiers preserve their non-authoritative status.

Responsibilities refused, redirected, or routed elsewhere:

- support goes to evidence;
- truth remains distinct from authority;
- navigation goes to maps and indexes without becoming authority;
- implementation authority remains outside exploratory observations unless
  separately reconciled.

### Relationship

Pressure accumulated around:

- support links;
- contradictions;
- identity resemblance;
- dependency;
- authority;
- provenance;
- runtime topology;
- recommendation;
- adoption;
- explanation.

Why it remained bounded:

- relationship has reconciled surfaces for relationship facts, promotion, and
  observation;
- broader relationship ontology remains a frontier and therefore cannot replace
  reconciled claim-centric boundaries;
- relationship pressure is acknowledged without forcing one graph model.

Responsibilities refused, redirected, or routed elsewhere:

- identity equivalence is not overclaimed;
- graph unification is not adopted;
- runtime topology and ontology remain separate from documented relationship
  evidence unless supported.

### Additional Survivor: Boundary

`Boundary` appears to survive pressure because its responsibility is repeatedly
negative and protective: it prevents collapse. It does not need to explain every
thing it separates. It can remain stable while documents use it to mark limits
between observation, interpretation, evidence, claim, authority, projection,
handoff, continuation, and implementation.

## Critical Examples

### Example 1: Observation -> Derivation

The evidence supports a cautious answer:

```text
Observation may have survived partly because new proposition-generation pressure
was routed into Derivation and Claim rather than retained inside Observation.
```

Observation could show what was seen or preserved from a surface. It could not
safely explain how supported propositions are generated from other represented
propositions. Derivation work asks that question without making observation a
reasoning engine. This is a strong responsibility-routing example, but not proof
that derivation exists only because observation was overloaded.

### Example 2: Fact -> Claim

The evidence strongly supports a load shift:

```text
Fact-shaped language lost responsibility for explaining representation truth,
support, uncertainty, contradiction, confidence, freshness, and authority.
Claim received much of the representational responsibility.
```

The shift did not abolish fact language. It bounded it. The repository can still
project normalized fact-like statements while preserving that Seed represents
claims supported by evidence rather than possessing unqualified reality.

### Example 3: Working State -> Current Work Position

The evidence strongly supports a load shift:

```text
Working State could preserve broad activity context, but Current Work Position
received selected, bounded, continuation-relevant orientation.
```

The shifted load includes active question, selected context, live constraints,
validation boundary, unresolved pressure, and safe next movement. This explains
why a handoff can preserve documents and still fail continuation: stored working
state may not preserve the current position from which work should resume.

### Example 4: Persistence -> Continuity

The evidence supports a load shift:

```text
Persistence preserves survival pressure; Continuity receives survival-through-
change pressure.
```

Persistence alone does not explain whether something remains the same, remains
usable, remains active, or survives as a transformed line of work. Continuity
carries the question of relation across change without reducing survival to
storage or strict identity.

### Example 5: Stable Survivor: Authority

Authority appears to survive because it refuses expansion into truth, support,
or navigation.

Documents can ask authority questions repeatedly without decomposing authority
because each authority surface is scoped:

- documentation authority governs which document owns an answer;
- source authority governs what a source can support;
- operator authority governs operator decisions or permissions;
- projection authority governs what projected surfaces may imply.

The concept remains stable because pressure is absorbed by scoping and routing,
not by turning authority into a universal explanation.

## Required Findings

### Strongest Explanatory-Load Candidates

The strongest candidates are:

1. `Fact`, because it was repeatedly asked to explain reality,
   representation, support, authority, confidence, and projection.
2. `Working State`, because it was asked to explain stored context,
   continuation, active orientation, selection, and next safe movement.
3. `Persistence`, because it was asked to explain storage, survival, identity,
   continuity, and active participation.
4. `Lineage`, because artifact traces were asked to explain inquiry movement,
   observation continuity, discovery path, and causal transition.
5. `Understanding`, because it was asked to explain knowledge, grasp,
   decompression, meaning transition, and future action without canonical
   ontology support.

### Strongest Bounded-Responsibility Examples

The strongest examples are:

1. `Authority`, because it remains scoped and refuses to become truth or
   support.
2. `Evidence`, because it remains support material and refuses to become proof
   or authority.
3. `Observation`, because it remains bounded by interpretation, mutation,
   evidence, claim, and derivation boundaries.
4. `Claim`, because it is broad but persistently bounded as representation, not
   reality or evidence.
5. `Relationship`, because reconciled relationship surfaces remain bounded while
   broader relationship questions stay frontier-level.

### Strongest Load-Shift Examples

The strongest shifts are:

1. `Fact -> Claim` for representation and support-aware assertion.
2. `Working State -> Current Work Position` for selected continuation
   orientation.
3. `Working State -> Active Edge` for current unresolved pull.
4. `Persistence -> Continuity` for survival through change.
5. `Observation -> Derivation` for generated proposition paths from represented
   support.
6. `Attention -> Trigger / Target / Selection / Active Edge` for why focus
   moves versus what receives focus.

### Strongest Decomposition Examples

The strongest decomposition examples are:

1. Working state into continuation context, current work position, active edge,
   handoff activation, and preservation concerns.
2. Attention into selection, priority, relevance, trigger, target, and active
   edge.

   observations.

   participation questions.

   confidence, and projection humility.

### Strongest Pressure-Survivor Examples

The strongest survivors are:

1. Authority, because scoping prevents expansion.
2. Evidence, because support remains distinct from truth and authority.
3. Observation, because interpretation and derivation absorb adjacent pressure.
4. Claim, because broad representational responsibility is bounded by support,
   scope, and authority.
5. Boundary, because its function is to prevent collapse rather than explain all
   sides of a distinction.

### Strongest Responsibility-Routing Examples

The strongest routing examples are:

1. Observation pressure routed to interpretation, evidence, claim, derivation,
   and blind-spot surfaces.
2. Fact pressure routed to claim, evidence, support, authority, confidence, and
   projection surfaces.
3. Working-state pressure routed to current work position, active edge,
   continuity, handoff activation, and preservation surfaces.
4. Persistence pressure routed to continuity, current work position, active edge,
   and preservation surfaces.
5. Attention pressure routed to trigger, target, selection, priority, relevance,
   and active edge.
6. Lineage pressure routed to artifact, inquiry, observation, and discovery-path
   lineage observations.

### Strongest Examples Where Pressure Accumulated Without Decomposition

The strongest examples are:

1. Authority, where repeated pressure produced scoped authority surfaces rather
   than one decomposed replacement.
2. Evidence, where trust, support, corroboration, and promotion pressure did not
   erase the support-material boundary.
3. Claim, where many subtype documents appeared but the broad claim boundary
   remained stable.
4. Relationship, where pressure is acknowledged by a frontier while reconciled
   relationship facts and promotion boundaries remain intact.
5. Boundary, where repeated use across documents increased importance without
   requiring canonical taxonomy.

## Tensions Observed

### Responsibility Versus Explanation

A concept can have a responsibility without explaining all adjacent phenomena.
`Observation` can be responsible for what was observed without explaining the
interpretation. `Evidence` can support without proving. `Authority` can govern a
scope without making a statement true.

### Explanatory Load Versus Compression

Compression appears when one term hides distinctions. Explanatory load appears
when one term is asked to answer several questions. They overlap, but they are
not identical. A compressed term may remain useful; a loaded term may remain
bounded if adjacent pressure is routed elsewhere.

### Explanatory Load Versus Abstraction

Abstraction can be legitimate. `Claim` is broad, but repository evidence treats
it as a bounded abstraction. Load becomes visible when abstraction starts doing
unpreserved work: hiding support, authority, change, activation, or causal path.

### Bounded Responsibility Versus Decomposition

Bounded concepts can survive pressure. Decomposition appears more likely when a
concept must answer different kinds of questions that require different evidence
or authority. The repository does not show that all broad concepts should split.

### Pressure Absorption Versus Responsibility Routing

Some concepts absorb pressure by scoping. Authority and evidence are examples.
Other concepts route pressure outward. Observation routes interpretation and
derivation. Working state routes current orientation and active edge.

### Concept Survival Versus Concept Expansion

Survival does not always mean expansion. A concept may survive precisely because
it does not expand. Authority and evidence are strong examples: they remain
important by refusing adjacent responsibilities.

### Stability Versus Explanatory Demand

Stable concepts can face high explanatory demand. Stability appears strongest
where documents preserve what the concept must not explain. Instability appears
where the concept is repeatedly used after those refusal boundaries are unclear.

## Unresolved Observations

Several uncertainties remain:

- The repository does not preserve enough pre-distinction evidence to determine
  whether every decomposition followed explanatory overload, compression,
  preservation failure, or ordinary inquiry refinement.
- `Understanding` shows strong pressure, but its status remains deliberately
  non-canonical.
- `Derivation` may be a load receiver from observation, evidence, and claim, but
  it remains a frontier and not a settled explanation mechanism.
- `Relationship` is both a pressure survivor and a pressure site; reconciled
  relationship surfaces are stable while broader relationship ontology remains
  unsettled.
- `Claim` is both a bounded survivor and a major receiver of load from `Fact`;
  this makes it a stable abstraction rather than a simple decomposed endpoint.
- The evidence is stronger for observed responsibility routing than for causal
  claims about why a concept decomposed.

## Closing Observation

The repository appears to preserve a practical lesson without making it
canonical:

```text
concepts survive pressure best when their responsibility remains bounded and
adjacent explanatory work is either refused, scoped, or routed to another
surface.
```

The same evidence also cautions against overclaiming that lesson. Some concepts
expand safely as abstractions. Some split because new evidence appears. Some
remain unresolved because repository history preserves artifacts better than it
preserves the pressure that produced them. The strongest observation is
therefore not a rule for future design, but a reading of recent repository
history: decomposition often becomes legible when a term was asked to explain
more than its preserved boundary could carry, while pressure survivors remain
legible where the repository preserved what they do not explain.
