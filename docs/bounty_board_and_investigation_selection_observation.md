# Bounty Board And Investigation Selection Observation

## Purpose

This document observes how repository investigations appear to have become
active.

It asks:

```text
How did investigations actually get selected?
```

This is an observation. It is not a frontier, reconciliation, planning document,
prioritization proposal, recommendation engine, governance proposal, workflow
proposal, schema proposal, runtime design, implementation proposal, or roadmap.

Repository authority wins over this document. Existing reconciliations,
frontiers, audits, maps, status documents, and the bounty board retain their own
scoped authority. This document does not promote any frontier, reconcile any
ontology, rank investigations, recommend next work, or propose a mechanism for
future selection.

## Method

The investigation reviewed repository evidence including:

- `future_frontiers.md`;
- `documentation_lineage_observation.md`;
- `discovery_path_preservation_observation.md`;
- `lineage_distinction_observation.md`;
- `concept_stability_audit.md`;
- `architectural_status_and_next_frontier.md`;
- `architectural_knowledge_map.md`;
- adjacent frontier and audit documents referenced by those sources, especially
  inquiry, selection/attention, handoff/continuation lineage, object/role/operation,
  persistence, continuity, current work position, active edge, relationship, and
  observation-surface documents.

The review treated explicit dependencies, `related` links, routing placement,
purpose/method sections, and repeated conceptual pressure as evidence. It did
not treat those signals as causal proof. Absence from a dependency list was
treated as uncertainty, not disproof.

## Scope Boundary

This document distinguishes observed selection from priority.

A document becoming active in repository history does not prove that it was most
important, most valuable, highest priority, planned, ranked, or roadmap-selected.
The repository evidence more often shows inquiry moving when pressure became
hard to ignore, when a distinction failed to hold, when continuation needed a
missing orientation, or when a previous investigation exposed a new unresolved
question.

This observation therefore uses phrases such as `appears to`, `evidence
suggests`, and `observed pattern` deliberately. They preserve uncertainty.

## High-Level Observation

Repository investigation selection appears less like centralized prioritization
and more like pressure-sensitive emergence.

The strongest repeated shape is:

```text
existing document or cluster
    -> unresolved pressure, contradiction, ambiguity, continuation failure, or
       preservation gap becomes visible
        -> a new investigation records that pressure without necessarily ranking it
```

A second repeated shape is:

```text
candidate future frontier preserved but inactive
    -> later active inquiry if adjacent work makes it newly relevant or exposes
       the pressure more sharply
```

The evidence does not show that the bounty board itself selected work. It more
clearly preserves possible work that may or may not later become active.

## Selection Patterns Observed

### Pressure-Driven Investigation

Several investigations appear to have become active because prior work produced
unresolved pressure.

Examples:

- Handoff and continuation lineage exposed that continuation can fail even when
  information is preserved. That pressure fed inquiry, selection/attention,
  persistence, continuity, current work position, and active edge questions.
- Inquiry exposed that preserving questions, gaps, tensions, and branches is not
  the same as preserving accepted knowledge. That pressure fed selection and
  attention questions: among many unresolved possibilities, why did this one
  become active?
- Object / role / operation work appears to have become active because inquiry,
  attention, operations, and frontier documents repeatedly blurred durable
  things, contextual participation, and activities.
- Persistence and continuity appear to have become active because previous
  lineage and role questions could not be answered by storage, identity, or
  document existence alone.
- Discovery-path preservation appears to have become active because conclusion
  preservation did not fully preserve challenge sequences, contradiction
  exposure, compression removal, and understanding transitions.

Observed selection signal:

```text
pressure becomes visible enough that merely routing it is insufficient
```

This is not proof of causality. It is the strongest recurring artifact pattern.

### Contradiction, Ambiguity, And Compression Removal

The repository repeatedly selected investigations when a term or conclusion was
carrying too much compressed meaning.

Examples:

- `persistence` did not simply become a stable answer. It transformed under
  pressure from storage, identity, lineage, continuation, and working-position
  questions.
- `relationship` became a major pressure signal because object/role/operation
  distinctions could not explain several edge-semantics cases by themselves.
- `lineage` split into artifact, inquiry, observation, and discovery-path
  surfaces because one lineage word did not appear to preserve what was
  continuing in all cases.
- `selection` had to be separated from priority, relevance, attention,
  authority, implementation, and truth.

Observed selection signal:

```text
a word or finding remains useful but becomes ambiguous under repeated use
```

### Continuation-Failure Investigation

Several investigations appear selected because future participants would not be
able to resume safely from preserved conclusions alone.

Examples:

- Handoff/continuation lineage focused on what survives across handoffs and what
  can fail despite available information.
- Current work position asked what orientation must survive for work to resume.
- Active edge asked what currently pulls work forward among preserved unresolved
  things.
- Discovery-path preservation asked whether preserving only final findings hides
  how understanding changed.

Observed selection signal:

```text
repository artifacts preserve content, but not enough orientation for safe continuation
```

### Authority And Boundary Investigation

Selection also appears related to authority concerns. Investigations often became
active when a candidate concept risked being mistaken for canonical architecture,
implementation direction, planning authority, or truth authority.

Examples:

- Selection/attention explicitly protects selection from becoming truth,
  authority, policy, decision, recommendation, execution, planner, or ranking
  machinery.
- Concept stability audits recurring concepts without promoting them to
  canonical vocabulary or implementation readiness.
- Documentation lineage observes document-generation patterns without defining
  formal lineage ontology.
- Architectural status routes exploratory frontier work while preserving that it
  is not implementation-ready unless later reconciled.
- The bounty board explicitly denies roadmap, commitment, sequencing,
  implementation-readiness, priority, and canonical-ontology authority.

Observed selection signal:

```text
a useful concept risks being over-promoted beyond its authority boundary
```

### Preservation-Gap Investigation

Some investigations appear selected because repository artifacts preserved one
thing well and another thing poorly.

Examples:

- Documentation lineage preserves artifact generation better than conceptual or
  causal movement.
- Discovery-path preservation observes that final conclusions are often easier
  to recover than the challenge sequence that changed understanding.
- Lineage distinction observes that artifact, inquiry, observation, and
  discovery-path lineage show different survival surfaces.
- Preservation surface work asks what recent investigations appear to preserve
  without defining preservation ontology.

Observed selection signal:

```text
the repository can see something, but the observation surface also reveals its blind spot
```

## Investigation Role Findings

The following findings identify observed roles. They are not rankings and do not
create a priority order.

### Strong Inquiry Generators

Strong inquiry generators are documents or clusters that repeatedly exposed
follow-on questions.

Observed strongest candidates:

- `handoff_and_continuation_lineage_frontier.md` — repeatedly appears upstream of
  inquiry, selection/attention, persistence, continuity, current work position,
  active edge, lineage, and preservation questions.
- `inquiry_frontier.md` — repeatedly appears where questions, tensions, branches,
  and unresolved pursuit need to be distinguished from knowledge artifacts.
- `object_role_and_operation_frontier.md` plus its audit and pressure test —
  generated or sharpened persistence, continuity, relationship, concept
  stability, and active-work questions.
- `persistence_frontier.md` / `continuity_frontier.md` — transformed survival
  questions into followability, resumption, and active-edge questions.
- `documentation_lineage_observation.md` — generated observation-surface,
  discovery-path, lineage-distinction, preservation-surface, and now
  investigation-selection observations.

### Strong Branching Investigations

Branching investigations are observed places where one inquiry surface split
into multiple follow-on directions.

Observed examples:

- Handoff/continuation lineage branched into inquiry lineage, selection/attention,
  persistence, continuity, current work position, and active edge.
- Inquiry branched into selection/attention, attention trigger, attention target,
  object/role/operation, and lineage-distinction questions.
- Object/role/operation branched into consistency audit, pressure test,
  relationship pressure, concept stability, persistence, continuity, and active
  work-position questions.
- Documentation lineage branched into observation-surface/blind-spot,
  discovery-path preservation, lineage distinction, preservation surface, and
  bounty-board/selection observation.

### Strong Bridge Investigations

Bridge investigations connected previously separate areas without necessarily
settling them.

Observed examples:

- Handoff/continuation lineage bridged handoff authority, continuation failure,
  working-state survival, inquiry lineage, and current-position questions.
- Inquiry bridged operator questions, frontiers, gaps, findings, handoffs,
  selection rationale, and investigation lineage.
- Object/role/operation bridged operations, inquiry, attention, relationship,
  persistence, and concept-stability pressure.
- Relationship bridged claim support, contradiction, identity, authority,
  dependency, trust, adoption, recommendation, runtime-topology pressure, and
  active-work explanations.
- Documentation lineage bridged documentation artifacts, navigation routing,
  frontier generation, audit follow-up, and inquiry movement.
- Lineage distinction bridged artifact lineage, inquiry lineage, observation
  lineage, and discovery-path lineage while preserving that they do not cleanly
  collapse.

### Strong Consolidating Investigations

Consolidating investigations gathered multiple pressure lines without turning
that gathering into canonical ontology.

Observed examples:

- `concept_stability_audit.md` consolidated object, role, operation,
  relationship, persistence, continuity, current work position, active edge,
  inquiry, selection/attention, and handoff lineage pressure into stability,
  recurrence, transformation, and load-bearing observations.
- `architectural_status_and_next_frontier.md` consolidated reconciled,
  implemented, exploratory, not-reconciled, and not-implementation-ready states
  while preserving authority boundaries.
- `architectural_knowledge_map.md` consolidated routing across emerging
  meta-architecture frontiers without making the map an authority over their
  conclusions.
- `documentation_lineage_observation.md` consolidated recent follow-on document
  patterns into generative, bridge, branching, consolidating, and terminal roles.

### Apparent Dead Ends Or Low-Follow-On Investigations

The repository evidence is weaker for dead ends than for generators because an
inactive investigation may be preserved for later use, superseded, resolved, or
simply not yet revisited.

Observed low-follow-on or inactive patterns:

- Several bounty-board items remain preserved as uncharacterized, speculative,
  or emerging without visible active descendants in the reviewed cluster.
  Examples include cross-Seed challenge/reevaluation, federated knowledge
  placement, federated inquiry, and some knowledge-burden candidates.
- Some local implementation/audit documents appear important within their scoped
  domain but did not redirect the recent meta-architecture inquiry cluster.
- Some candidate terms were transformed rather than pursued directly. For
  example, persistence appears less terminal than transformed into continuity and
  current-work-position questions.

This does not mean those investigations were unimportant. It only means they
show less visible follow-on movement in the evidence reviewed here.

### Strong Redirections And Direction Changes

Observed examples of inquiry changing direction:

- From knowledge lineage to inquiry lineage: handoff/continuation work made it
  visible that how a claim arose and how an investigation reached its current
  state are not the same question.
- From inquiry to selection/attention: once many unresolved inquiries existed,
  the active question shifted to why one became attended.
- From selection to attention trigger/target: selection pressure split into what
  causes attention to move and what receives attention.
- From operations to object/role/operation: operation language exposed category
  pressure rather than immediately producing operation machinery.
- From persistence to continuity/current work position/active edge: survival
  across change redirected into followability, resumability, and current forward
  pressure.
- From documentation lineage to discovery-path preservation: artifact lineage
  made follow-on documents visible but also exposed that understanding
  transitions were not equally preserved.
- From lineage as one word to lineage distinctions: repeated lineage language
  redirected into artifact, inquiry, observation, and discovery-path surfaces.

## Critical Example Evaluation

### Handoff And Continuation Lineage

Observed role: strong generator, bridge, and redirector.

It connected handoff boundaries to continuation failure, inquiry lineage,
selection, persistence, continuity, current work position, and active edge. It
appears foundational to the recent selection pattern, but this observation does
not promote it to canonical ontology.

### Inquiry

Observed role: strong generator and bridge.

Inquiry became the surface where questions, tensions, gaps, frontiers, branches,
and continuation state could be discussed without collapsing them into accepted
knowledge or documentation artifacts. It generated many descendants and also
exposed the selection question.

### Selection And Attention

Observed role: bridge and boundary protector.

Selection/attention did not appear as a priority system. It appeared because
many unresolved items existed and only some became active. Its strongest role is
separating selected, relevant, attended, prioritized, authorized, true, and
implemented.

### Object / Role / Operation

Observed role: strong branching and pressure-test generator.

It became active because multiple investigations were using concepts that could
be read as durable objects, contextual roles, or operations. It generated audit
and pressure-test follow-up and helped expose relationship and stability
pressure.

### Persistence, Continuity, Current Work Position, And Active Edge

Observed role: transformation chain.

Persistence asked what survives. Continuity sharpened survival into recognizable
followability. Current work position sharpened continuation into resumable
orientation. Active edge sharpened preserved unresolved work into current forward
pressure.

### Relationship

Observed role: pressure signal and bridge.

Relationship repeatedly appears where object/role/operation categories are not
sufficient. It bridges support, contradiction, identity, authority, dependency,
trust, adoption, recommendation, topology, and work-position concerns. The
repository evidence supports relationship as load-bearing pressure, not as a
settled ontology category in this observation.

### Documentation Lineage

Observed role: generator, consolidator, and visibility surface.

Documentation lineage made artifact follow-on patterns visible. It also exposed
limits: artifact lineage can show references and routing more easily than
causal selection, working-memory pressure, or discovery-path transitions.

### Observation Surface

Observed role: boundary and blind-spot detector.

Observation-surface work appears selected because lineage observation could see
some repository movement but not all of it. It redirected from "what generated
what?" toward "what can this observation method see or miss?"

### Discovery-Path Preservation

Observed role: preservation-gap redirector.

It became active because conclusions were more visible than challenge sequences.
Its selection signal appears to be compression removal: the repository preserved
what changed better than how understanding changed.

## Bounty Board Findings

`future_frontiers.md` appears to serve as a voluntary bounty board and candidate
preservation surface.

Observed roles:

- It preserves future inquiry without making that inquiry active.
- It preserves inactive pressure that emerged from previous investigations.
- It preserves deferred attention: a candidate may be worth revisiting without
  being current work.
- It preserves investigations that may never become active.
- It prevents presence on a list from becoming priority, sequencing,
  implementation readiness, commitment, or canonical ontology.

The bounty board therefore appears closer to inquiry preservation than selection
machinery.

Its relationship to actual selection appears indirect:

```text
bounty board preserves possible pressure
adjacent work may later reactivate or sharpen that pressure
active investigation records the sharpened pressure elsewhere
```

The evidence reviewed does not show the bounty board as a roadmap, ranking
system, planning system, or recommendation system.

## Required Tensions Observed

### Selection vs Priority

Selection in the reviewed evidence means a path became active. It does not prove
that the path was highest priority.

### Selection vs Attention

Selection asks why one candidate path became chosen or active. Attention asks
what is currently being considered or pulled forward. The repository repeatedly
preserves the distinction to avoid treating attention as authority.

### Active Inquiry vs Preserved Inquiry

Active inquiry receives current work. Preserved inquiry remains available.
Frontiers, observations, and bounty-board entries may preserve inquiry without
making it active.

### Bounty vs Frontier

A bounty-board item is a candidate future frontier. A frontier document records
an investigated unresolved edge. The bounty board can preserve possibilities that
never become frontier documents.

### Deferred Attention vs Active Edge

Deferred attention is preserved possibility. Active edge is current forward
pressure. The repository evidence suggests many deferred items never become the
active edge, or have not yet done so.

### Future Investigation vs Current Work Position

Future investigation names possible later questions. Current work position
preserves orientation for resuming present work. Confusing them would turn
preservation into planning.

### Planning vs Emergence

The evidence more often shows emergence from pressure, ambiguity, contradiction,
continuation failure, authority concern, preservation gap, or implementation
friction than explicit planning. This observation does not deny planning exists;
it only records that planning is not the strongest visible selection pattern in
this documentation cluster.

## Selection Finding Summary

Repository selection appears related to the following signals, without proving
causality:

- contradiction or category conflict;
- unresolved pressure carried forward from previous documents;
- ambiguity in recurring terms;
- continuation failure or resumability risk;
- authority-boundary risk;
- preservation gaps and observation blind spots;
- documentation gaps or navigation needs;
- implementation friction when exploratory concepts risk being mistaken for
  implementation direction.

The strongest evidence is clustered rather than singular. Investigations appear
selected when several of these signals converge.

## Unresolved Observations

- The repository does not preserve all original prompt pressures, so some
  selection reasons may be invisible.
- Dependency metadata shows use and routing better than causality.
- Navigation placement can show discoverability needs but not why work began.
- Commit grouping can suggest temporal adjacency but not conceptual necessity.
- Dead ends are hard to identify because preserved inactive inquiry may later
  reactivate.
- The bounty board preserves candidates, but the threshold for moving from
  bounty to active investigation is not explicit in repository evidence.
- Investigation selection may involve operator concerns not fully captured in
  documents.

## Conclusion

The repository appears to have selected investigations through visible pressure
rather than explicit ranking.

The most generative movements came from unresolved continuation, inquiry,
category, relationship, persistence, lineage, preservation, and authority
pressures. The bounty board appears to preserve possible future inquiry and
deferred attention without functioning as roadmap authority or selection
machinery.

This observation should help future participants understand how inquiry moved
historically while preserving uncertainty about causality and avoiding any
proposal for how inquiry should move next.
