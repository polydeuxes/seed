# Continuity Frontier

## Purpose

This document investigates a frontier exposed by the Persistence Frontier:

```text
persistence may survive change
```

while also preserving the findings that:

```text
persistence != identity
persistence != storage
```

Those findings suggest a deeper unresolved question:

```text
What is continuity?
```

More specifically:

```text
How can represented understanding exhibit continuity without requiring strict
sameness?
```

This document is ontology discovery only. It does not design identifiers,
storage systems, schemas, lineage engines, continuation mechanisms, runtimes,
registries, object lifecycles, workflow systems, or implementation architecture.
It does not reconcile Seed's ontology and does not supersede repository
authority. Repository authority wins over this document.

The central question is:

```text
What survives change?
```

The document does not assume that continuity is already understood. It tests
whether continuity is distinguishable from identity, persistence, lineage,
inquiry, handoff success, frontier succession, and current work position.

---

## Existing Authority That Constrains This Frontier

### Persistence Frontier

The Persistence Frontier found that persistence is not storage and is not
identity. It also found that claims, relationships, questions, gaps,
contradictions, findings, frontiers, inquiry movement, and working state can show
survival through revision, role change, operation change, and continuation.

This document accepts those findings only as evidence under examination. It does
not treat continuity as solved by persistence. Instead, it asks whether the
persistence findings are examples of continuity, evidence for continuity, or
only symptoms of unresolved ontology.

### Object, Role, And Operation Work

The object / role / operation frontier and its audit distinguished relatively
durable represented things from contextual participation and activity. The
pressure test then found that the lens becomes strained where Seed needs
relationship semantics such as support, contradiction, identity, authority,
lineage, dependency, ownership, and working-state connections.

Continuity therefore cannot be tested only by asking whether an object remained
the same. Some candidates may survive as roles, relationships, tensions,
positions, or inquiry movement rather than as object cores.

### Inquiry Frontier

The Inquiry Frontier characterized inquiry as active or preserved pursuit of
unresolved understanding. It identified questions, tensions, gaps, findings,
frontiers, working state, selection rationale, and handoff lineage as candidates
whose status remains mixed.

Continuity may therefore appear inside inquiry as the survival of a question,
gap, contradiction, direction, working position, or unresolved pursuit. This
document must not collapse inquiry continuity into a workflow process or an
inquiry runtime.

### Handoff And Continuation Lineage Frontier

The handoff-lineage frontier found that continuation can fail even when
information is preserved. It also found that successful continuation appears to
preserve a selected active edge: current frontier, active context, working state,
constraints, unresolved tensions, selection rationale, validation state, and next
safe moves.

That evidence makes continuity harder than retention. If preserved information
is insufficient for continuation, continuity may involve more than stored facts,
references, or summaries.

### Foundational Ontology Reconciliation

The foundational ontology centers claims, facts, relationships, observations,
evidence, events, changes, states, goals, decisions, capabilities, explanations,
assessments, recommendations, and handoffs while preserving boundaries such as
claim != fact, relationship != identity, event != state, projection != authority,
and handoff != architecture.

Continuity must respect those boundaries. A continuity vocabulary that erases
claim/fact, relationship/identity, event/state, or handoff/architecture
boundaries would be a regression.

### Architectural Status

The current architectural status directs active work toward bounded
implementation cleanup and treats broad ontology systems, new engines, and
recursive conceptual audits as non-current unless a concrete operator question
requires them.

This document is therefore deliberately narrow: it records an exploratory
frontier exposed by prior documents. It is not a request to implement continuity
machinery.

## Method

For each continuity candidate, this document asks:

1. What might survive?
2. What clearly changes?
3. What would make a future participant recognize continuity?
4. What would falsify continuity?
5. Is the candidate better explained as identity, persistence, lineage,
   inquiry, relationship, handoff selection, or work-position preservation?

The candidates are:

```text
question continuity
gap continuity
contradiction continuity
finding continuity
frontier continuity
inquiry continuity
relationship continuity
working-position continuity
```

The purpose is not to unify these forms. The purpose is to test whether the word
`continuity` is doing distinct conceptual work or merely renaming older concepts.

---

## Working Distinction: Continuity Is Not Identity

Identity asks:

```text
same thing?
```

Continuity asks:

```text
what survived?
```

The distinction appears useful because repository examples repeatedly show
change without total loss. A question can be refined. A gap can be reframed. A
contradiction can remain active even when the claims around it are clarified. A
frontier can give rise to another frontier. A handoff can preserve enough active
position to continue work without making the next participant identical to the
previous participant.

Strict identity is therefore too narrow for many observed cases. If identity
requires sameness of wording, role, participation, or representation, then many
valid continuations would be classified as discontinuities. But repository work
also warns against making continuity too broad. If any loose resemblance counts
as continuity, then continuity becomes retrospective storytelling rather than an
ontology distinction.

Current finding:

```text
Continuity is weaker than strict identity but stronger than mere resemblance.
```

It asks what survived through change without requiring that every feature remain
the same.

---

## Working Distinction: Continuity Is Not Persistence

Persistence asks whether something endures. Continuity asks what kind of
survival made endurance recognizable across change.

The Persistence Frontier already showed that storage is insufficient and that
identity is not required. Continuity may be the explanatory layer beneath some
persistence judgments:

```text
We recognize persistence because some continuity survived.
```

However, continuity should not simply replace persistence. Persistence can name
the survival of a represented item, relation, pressure, purpose, or work
position. Continuity investigates the pattern by which later states remain
connected to earlier ones despite revision, role change, frontier movement, or
handoff.

Possible distinctions:

| Question | Persistence answer | Continuity answer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Did it endure? | Something persisted. | What survived and how was survival recognized? |
| Is it stored? | Storage is not enough. | Stored material may or may not preserve continuity. |
| Is it the same? | Identity is not required. | Survival can occur through transformation. |
| Can it be resumed? | Some persistent items are irrelevant. | Continuity may require active selected position. |

Continuity may be broader than persistence when it describes the survival of a
pattern across successor artifacts rather than the endurance of one represented
item. Persistence may be evidence of continuity when a stable relation, tension,
or question can be traced through change. Continuity may exist without simple
persistence when the original artifact is superseded but an unresolved pressure,
question, or direction remains active in a successor.

Current finding:

```text
Persistence is evidence that something may have survived; continuity asks what
survived and whether the survival remains intelligible across change.
```

---

## Working Distinction: Continuity Is Not Lineage

Lineage records descent, derivation, inheritance, succession, or provenance.
Continuity asks whether anything meaningful survived across that descent.

Lineage can help explain continuity by showing how a later artifact came from an
earlier one. But lineage alone does not prove continuity. A document may cite a
predecessor while abandoning the predecessor's active question. A finding may be
copied forward while the working position changes so much that the prior inquiry
no longer guides action. A handoff may preserve references but fail to preserve
continuation.

Conversely, continuity might sometimes be recognized before formal lineage is
recorded. A later participant can perceive that the same tension is still being
worked even if the repository has not assigned a lineage mechanism or identifier.
This is not an argument against provenance. It is only a warning that lineage is
not the same concept as continuity.

Current finding:

```text
Lineage can provide evidence for continuity, but continuity requires surviving
meaning, pressure, relation, or work position, not merely a recorded predecessor.
```

## Candidate Continuity Evaluations

### Question Continuity

A question can change wording, scope, and precision while still carrying a
recognizable unresolved concern.

What might survive:

- the unresolved focus;
- the contrast being tested;
- the pressure that made the question necessary;
- the expectation that an answer would reduce uncertainty;
- the connection to findings or next questions.

What clearly changes:

- wording;
- scope;
- assumptions;
- level of abstraction;
- candidate answers;
- active role in an inquiry.

Continuity is plausible when a later question still pursues the unresolved
concern that made the earlier question matter. It is weaker when only vocabulary
is shared. It is falsified when a refined question no longer addresses the same
uncertainty, even if it reuses similar words.

Question continuity is therefore not strict question identity. It may be a
survival of unresolved concern through refinement.

### Gap Continuity

A gap names missing understanding, missing evidence, missing representation, or
missing decision support. A gap can survive after partial answers because the
remaining absence still matters.

What might survive:

- the absence that blocks safe understanding or action;
- the need for more evidence or articulation;
- the reason the gap was visible;
- the relationship between a known area and an unknown area.

What clearly changes:

- the size of the gap;
- the formulation of what is missing;
- the documents that expose it;
- whether it is active, historical, narrowed, or transferred.

Gap continuity is plausible when later work still depends on resolving the same
absence or pressure. It is falsified when the earlier absence has been closed and
new work merely opens a different absence nearby.

Gap continuity appears distinct from question continuity. A question may express
a gap, but a gap can survive multiple questions and can remain even after one
question is answered.

### Contradiction Continuity

A contradiction may survive even when claims are revised, evidence is clarified,
or wording changes. What survives may not be the exact claim pair. It may be the
incompatibility pressure between represented positions.

What might survive:

- the incompatibility;
- the unresolved need to distinguish, reconcile, reject, or scope claims;
- the visibility of conflicting support;
- the caution that both positions cannot be promoted without qualification.

What clearly changes:

- claim wording;
- evidence support;
- severity;
- scope;
- whether the contradiction is active, historical, or resolved;
- whether it becomes an ambiguity, boundary, or rejected finding.

Contradiction continuity is plausible when later work still has to account for
the same incompatibility. It is falsified when the apparent contradiction was
only a wording collision or stale projection and no incompatible pressure
survives.

This is one of the strongest tests against identity. If both endpoint claims are
revised, strict identity may fail, while the contradiction pressure may remain.

### Finding Continuity

A finding can be restated, narrowed, generalized, superseded, or preserved as
background authority. The question is whether the represented discovery survived
or whether only the sentence survived.

What might survive:

- the discovered distinction;
- the evidence-backed caution;
- the non-goal or boundary created by the finding;
- the pressure on future work;
- the reason implementation remains premature.

What clearly changes:

- wording;
- confidence;
- authority status;
- scope;
- relationship to later findings;
- active relevance.

Finding continuity is plausible when later work still relies on the same
discovered distinction even if it expresses it differently. It is falsified when
the later text preserves a statement but no longer preserves the reasoning,
constraint, or boundary that made it a finding.

Finding continuity may depend on relationship continuity: a finding survives
partly through its support, contradiction, authority, and dependency relations.

### Frontier Continuity

A frontier identifies an active boundary of unresolved architectural or
ontological work. A later frontier may inherit pressure from an earlier frontier
without being the same frontier.

What might survive:

- the unresolved tension;
- the active edge of work;
- the operator question;
- the constraints and non-goals;
- the reasons implementation remains premature;
- the successor relationship from one boundary to another.

What clearly changes:

- name;
- scope;
- candidate vocabulary;
- evidence base;
- active examples;
- relation to reconciled authority.

Frontier continuity is plausible when the later frontier exists because an
unresolved pressure in the earlier frontier remained. It is falsified when the
later frontier merely uses similar style or neighboring vocabulary but works a
different pressure.

Frontier continuity is not frontier identity. A frontier can close, become
reconciled, or split while its underlying tension continues elsewhere.

### Inquiry Continuity

Inquiry continuity concerns the survival of unresolved pursuit, not merely the
survival of documents or findings. It is one of the strongest candidates because
Seed's frontier documents often move by carrying questions, tensions, gaps, and
findings forward.

What might survive:

- the unresolved pursuit;
- the selection of what matters next;
- the active tension;
- the working direction;
- the chain of questions and findings;
- the reason a later participant can resume rather than restart.

What clearly changes:

- questions;
- evidence;
- participants;
- documents;
- scope;
- current working state;
- conclusions.

Inquiry continuity is plausible when later work can be understood as continuing
the same pursuit under changed framing. It is falsified when later work merely
uses earlier information but pursues a different problem.

Inquiry continuity may contain question, gap, contradiction, finding, frontier,
and work-position continuities. But those forms should not be assumed identical.

### Relationship Continuity

The pressure test showed that many important semantics are relational: supports,
contradicts, identifies, belongs_to, depends_on, runs_on, authority, lineage, and
working-state connections. Continuity may therefore attach to relations as much
as to objects.

What might survive:

- a support relation;
- a contradiction relation;
- a dependency;
- an authority boundary;
- a successor or inheritance relation;
- the connection that makes a claim, finding, or handoff usable.

What clearly changes:

- endpoints;
- role of the relation;
- strength;
- confidence;
- active relevance;
- whether the relation is asserted, inferred, challenged, or historical.

Relationship continuity is plausible when the connection remains meaningful even
as endpoint descriptions or roles change. It is falsified when only the endpoint
objects remain and the connection no longer holds.

This candidate is important because continuity of understanding may be carried
by edges rather than nodes.

### Working-Position Continuity

Recent work suggests working state may be better understood as a current work
position rather than as a simple durable object. Working-position continuity
asks whether the active position from which safe work can continue survived.

What might survive:

- active context;
- current frontier;
- selected constraints;
- unresolved tensions;
- selection rationale;
- validation state;
- next safe moves;
- known non-goals and authority boundaries.

What clearly changes:

- participant;
- session;
- local context;
- exact summary;
- available evidence ordering;
- actions already completed;
- immediate next step.

Working-position continuity is plausible when a later participant can continue
from the selected active edge without reconstructing the whole inquiry. It is
falsified when information exists but the participant cannot tell what is active,
why it is active, what constraints govern it, or what move is safe next.

This candidate is central to handoffs. It suggests that continuity can fail even
when documents, facts, and references are preserved.

## Critical Example 1: Question Evolution

Example:

```text
Q1: What does it mean for a represented thing to persist?
Q2: What survives across time, inquiry, revision, participation changes, and
    continuation?
Q3: What is continuity?
Q4: How can represented understanding exhibit continuity without requiring
    strict sameness?
```

What remained continuous:

- concern with survival across change;
- suspicion that storage is insufficient;
- suspicion that strict identity is insufficient;
- need to explain recognizable survival without over-promoting implementation.

What changed:

- the target moved from persistence to continuity;
- the level of abstraction increased;
- the candidate answer shifted from endurance to survival pattern;
- the evidence set expanded from object persistence to inquiry and handoff.

Is continuity stronger than identity?

Continuity is not stronger in the sense of stricter. It is stronger in
explanatory reach. Identity can answer whether Q4 is the same question as Q1.
Continuity can explain why Q4 is intelligibly descended from Q1 even if it is
not the same question.

Finding:

```text
Question evolution supports continuity as survival of unresolved concern through
refinement, not as sameness of wording or identity of question object.
```

Falsifying pressure:

If Q4 no longer cared about survival across change and merely reused the word
`continuity`, then no meaningful continuity would survive from Q1.

## Critical Example 2: Inquiry Evolution

An inquiry produces findings and changes direction.

Did the inquiry continue?

It may have continued if the direction change was caused by findings that
transformed the active question while preserving the unresolved pursuit. It did
not continue merely because later work cited earlier work.

What survived:

- the unresolved pursuit;
- accepted findings as constraints;
- rejected assumptions;
- remaining tensions;
- current working position;
- next safe questions.

What changed:

- candidate vocabulary;
- active examples;
- hypothesis under test;
- document scope;
- confidence and authority status.

Finding:

```text
Inquiry continuity appears real when findings redirect rather than terminate the
pursuit.
```

Falsifying pressure:

If a later inquiry ignores the earlier findings' constraints and merely starts a
new subject with old references, then information persisted but inquiry
continuity failed.

## Critical Example 3: Frontier Evolution

Example:

```text
Attention Frontier -> Object / Role / Operation Frontier
```

Did a frontier continue?

Possibly not as the same frontier. The active boundary changed. Attention work
asked how attention is triggered, selected, targeted, or preserved. Object /
role / operation work asked whether repository concepts are durable things,
contextual participations, or activities.

Did a tension continue?

Yes, plausibly. The tension around active selection, target, role, and current
work position appears to survive into object / role / operation language.

Did an inquiry continue?

Possibly. The later frontier can be read as continuing an inquiry into how
active architectural work distinguishes what is attended to, what participates,
and what happens.

Finding:

```text
Frontier succession may preserve tension or inquiry without preserving frontier
identity.
```

Falsifying pressure:

If the later frontier only uses nearby vocabulary and does not inherit the
attention work's active tension, then frontier continuity is a retrospective
construction rather than a repository-supported finding.

---

## Critical Example 4: Continuation Failure

Example:

```text
Information preserved.
Continuation fails.
```

What continuity was missing?

Likely candidates:

- current work position;
- selection rationale;
- active frontier;
- unresolved tensions;
- validation state;
- authority boundaries;
- next safe moves;
- distinction between historical information and active edge.

The failure shows that continuity is not guaranteed by information preservation.
A summary can preserve many facts while losing what made those facts actionable
for the next participant.

Finding:

```text
Continuation failure is one of the strongest falsifications of continuity-as-
storage or continuity-as-information-retention.
```

Falsifying pressure:

The example does not prove that continuity is a separate ontology category. It
could instead show that handoffs require better selection, context, or
activation. Continuity may be the name for that missing active relation, or it
may be an umbrella over several already-known handoff dimensions.

---

## Critical Example 5: Handoff Success

Example:

```text
Information preserved.
Continuation succeeds.
```

What continuity survived?

Likely candidates:

- active edge of work;
- current constraints;
- selected evidence and rationale;
- unresolved tensions;
- enough lineage to explain why this is the next safe move;
- enough authority context to avoid reopening settled boundaries;
- enough non-goal preservation to prevent premature implementation.

A successful handoff does not prove that identity survived. The participant,
local session, wording, and exact mental state changed. It also does not prove
that all persistent information mattered. Success appears tied to preserving the
selected active position rather than the entire archive.

Finding:

```text
Handoff success supports continuity as preserved work-position and inquiry edge,
not as complete information transfer.
```

Falsifying pressure:

A handoff might succeed because the next participant independently reconstructs
the missing position from repository authority. In that case continuity may have
survived in the repository documents rather than in the handoff itself.

## Continuity Versus Identity

The distinction survives initial scrutiny.

Identity asks whether the represented thing is the same thing. Continuity asks
what survived across change. Repository examples need both questions:

- Same wording does not prove same question.
- Different wording does not prove discontinuity.
- Same frontier document can contain changed working position.
- Successor frontier can inherit unresolved tension without being the same
  frontier.
- Handoff continuation can succeed without same participant, session, or exact
  representation.

Strongest finding:

```text
Continuity is not an alternative identity rule. It is a survival question that
can remain meaningful when identity is undecided, weakened, or false.
```

Strongest falsifying finding:

```text
If continuity has no threshold beyond perceived resemblance, it collapses into
interpretive convenience and cannot constrain ontology.
```

Unresolved tension:

Seed still lacks a reconciled vocabulary for distinguishing same object,
refined object, successor object, inherited tension, and merely related object
without designing identifiers.

## Continuity Versus Persistence

Continuity may be broader than persistence, but the distinction remains
provisional.

Evidence that continuity is broader:

- a tension can continue after a frontier closes;
- an inquiry can continue after a question is refined;
- a contradiction pressure can continue after claims are restated;
- a work position can continue across participant change;
- a successor artifact can preserve direction without preserving strict object
  identity.

Evidence that persistence may explain continuity instead:

- continuity judgments often rely on something persisting;
- without persistent relations, findings, or evidence, continuity can become
  unverifiable;
- persistence already includes recognizable survival across change in the
  Persistence Frontier's strongest characterization.

Can continuity exist without persistence?

Possibly, if persistence is limited to a represented item enduring, while
continuity describes survival of unresolved pressure across successor items.
But if persistence already includes relations, pressures, purposes, and
work-positions, then continuity may be a refinement of persistence rather than a
separate concept.

Current finding:

```text
Continuity appears to name the recognizable pattern of survival; persistence
names the endurance being recognized. The boundary is useful but not reconciled.
```

## Continuity Versus Lineage

Does lineage explain continuity?

Partially. Lineage can record how one question, finding, frontier, or handoff
state descends from another. It can preserve evidence that continuity is not an
accidental resemblance.

Does continuity require lineage?

Not necessarily as implemented lineage. A future participant can recognize
continuity through surviving tension, relation, or work position even without a
formal lineage engine. However, without some trace, citation, provenance, or
explanation, continuity claims become difficult to audit.

Current finding:

```text
Lineage is evidence and explanation for continuity, not continuity itself.
```

Unresolved tension:

Seed may need language for continuity before it can safely design lineage, but
lineage may be required to make continuity auditable. That circularity remains
unresolved and is another reason implementation is premature.

## Relationship To Current Work Position

Recent findings suggest working state may be better understood as current work
position. Continuity appears required to preserve that position, but not as an
identity claim.

A current work position includes selected active context, frontier, constraints,
unresolved tensions, selection rationale, validation state, authority boundaries,
and next safe moves. These components can change while the position remains
continuable.

Continuity is required when the participant changes, the session changes, or the
summary changes. What must survive is not the exact representation but enough of
the active edge to let safe work continue.

Current finding:

```text
Working-position continuity is the strongest practical evidence that continuity
matters, because continuation can fail when position does not survive even if
information does.
```

Unresolved tension:

Working-position continuity may be a compound of inquiry continuity, handoff
selection, relationship continuity, authority preservation, and validation
state. It may not be a separate object or concept.

## Potential Finding Under Test

Candidate finding:

```text
Continuity is the recognition of surviving understanding across change.
```

### Evidence Supporting It

The candidate fits multiple examples:

- question refinement preserves unresolved concern;
- inquiry direction changes preserve pursuit;
- frontier succession preserves tension;
- handoff success preserves current work position;
- persistence findings require recognizable survival beyond storage or strict
  identity.

It also explains why future participants matter. Continuity is not only what is
stored. It is what can be recognized as still carrying relevant understanding,
pressure, relation, or position after change.

### Evidence Against It

The phrase `surviving understanding` may be too narrow.

Continuity may involve:

- unresolved gaps, not yet understood;
- contradictions, not yet reconciled;
- authority boundaries, not merely understanding;
- work positions, not only knowledge;
- evidence and provenance, not necessarily understanding;
- relationships, not merely represented content;
- purpose or need pressure, not only conceptual insight.

The word `recognition` is also risky. If recognition is subjective, continuity
may become dependent on a participant's interpretation. If recognition is made
formal, the document risks drifting toward identifiers, lineage machinery, or
implementation design.

### Revised Candidate

A more cautious characterization is:

```text
Continuity is recognizable survival of relevant understanding, pressure,
relation, purpose, or work position across change without requiring strict
sameness.
```

This is not a definition. It is a frontier characterization to be tested by
future work.

## Strongest Continuity Findings

1. Continuity is not strict identity; it asks what survived through change.
2. Continuity is not storage; preserved information can fail to preserve
   continuation.
3. Continuity appears stronger than resemblance because it requires a surviving
   concern, pressure, relation, finding, inquiry edge, or work position.
4. Question continuity is survival of unresolved concern through refinement.
5. Gap continuity is survival of a blocking absence or missing understanding.
6. Contradiction continuity is survival of incompatibility pressure, possibly
   across revised claims.
7. Finding continuity is survival of a discovered distinction, boundary,
   caution, or constraint.
8. Frontier continuity is survival of tension or inquiry edge across active
   boundary changes, not sameness of frontier.
9. Inquiry continuity is survival of unresolved pursuit across questions,
   findings, and direction changes.
10. Relationship continuity is necessary because support, contradiction,
    authority, dependency, and working-state usefulness can survive as edges.
11. Working-position continuity is central to handoff success because the next
    participant needs the active edge, not merely preserved information.
12. Implementation remains premature because continuity may be a compound
    recognition across objects, roles, operations, relationships, inquiry, and
    handoff selection rather than a single ontology category.

## Strongest Falsifying Findings

The strongest falsifications of naive continuity are:

```text
Similar wording does not prove continuity.
Recorded lineage does not prove continuity.
Preserved information does not prove continuation.
A successor artifact does not prove the same frontier continued.
A refined question may replace rather than continue the earlier question.
A gap may close while a different gap opens nearby.
A contradiction may disappear when terms are clarified.
A finding may be quoted while its constraint is forgotten.
A handoff may succeed by reconstruction rather than by preserved handoff
continuity.
Recognition can become subjective storytelling if no surviving pressure,
relation, or position constrains it.
```

These falsifications do not show that continuity is false. They show that
continuity is not yet understood well enough to carry implementation authority.

## Inquiry Findings

Inquiry provides the strongest conceptual setting for continuity because inquiry
already tolerates movement. Questions refine, gaps narrow, contradictions
surface, findings redirect work, and frontiers shift.

Current findings:

1. Inquiry continuity is not identical to question continuity; an inquiry can
   survive through multiple questions.
2. Inquiry continuity is not identical to finding continuity; findings can
   redirect inquiry without ending it.
3. Inquiry continuity is not identical to frontier continuity; frontiers can
   split, close, or become reconciliations while inquiry pressure remains.
4. Inquiry continuity appears to require preservation of unresolved pursuit and
   current working direction.
5. Inquiry continuity can fail if later work preserves facts but loses why those
   facts mattered.

Unresolved question:

```text
Is inquiry continuity a distinct ontology concern, or is it a compound of
question, gap, contradiction, finding, frontier, relationship, and work-position
continuities?
```

## Handoff Findings

Handoff examples are the strongest operational pressure on continuity, even
though this document does not design handoff mechanisms.

Current findings:

1. Handoff success depends on more than information preservation.
2. Handoff failure can occur when active work position, selection rationale,
   authority boundaries, validation state, or next safe moves are missing.
3. Handoff continuity is not participant identity, session identity, or storage
   durability.
4. A successful handoff preserves enough active edge for a later participant to
   continue without unsafe reconstruction.
5. A failed handoff can preserve evidence and findings while losing inquiry or
   work-position continuity.
6. Handoff continuity may be distributed across repository authority, handoff
   content, citations, non-goals, and current task framing.

Unresolved question:

```text
Does handoff preserve continuity, activate continuity already present in the
repository, or require both?
```

## Why Implementation Remains Premature

Implementation remains premature for several reasons:

1. Continuity has not been reconciled as a distinct ontology concept.
2. The boundary between continuity and persistence remains unresolved.
3. The boundary between continuity and lineage remains unresolved.
4. The repository does not yet distinguish same object, refined object,
   successor object, inherited tension, and related object in a settled way.
5. Relationship continuity may be central, but relationship ontology remains a
   pressure point rather than a universal implementation direction.
6. Working-position continuity appears important, but working position itself is
   compound.
7. Handoff continuity may depend on selection and activation rather than a new
   continuity mechanism.
8. Any premature implementation would risk inventing identifiers, storage,
   lineage engines, or continuation systems before the ontology is understood.

This document therefore does not propose schemas, identifiers, persistence
mechanisms, lineage machinery, handoff machinery, continuation engines, runtime
changes, or tests.

## Final Characterization

Continuity matters because the repository repeatedly encounters survival through
change without strict sameness:

```text
questions refine
inquiries redirect
frontiers evolve
contradictions persist as pressure
findings become constraints
relationships carry support and tension
handoffs succeed or fail depending on active position
```

The strongest current characterization is:

```text
Continuity is recognizable survival of relevant understanding, pressure,
relation, purpose, or work position across change without requiring strict
sameness.
```

This characterization remains exploratory. It is useful because it distinguishes
continuity from identity, storage, persistence, lineage, and complete
information transfer. It remains unresolved because the repository has not yet
settled whether continuity is a distinct ontology concept, a pattern within
persistence, a lineage judgment, an inquiry property, a handoff property, or a
compound recognition across several existing concepts.

Future work should preserve the question without implementing it prematurely:

```text
What survives change?
```
