# Projection Self-Description Observation

Observation

Recent projection investigations suggest that the primary limitation is not missing projection machinery but weak projection self-description.

Projection concepts such as supports, conflicts, aliases, relationships, entity type assertions, and graph validation are implemented and participate in a real construction pipeline.

The recurring difficulty is explaining:

- what stage produced a result
- what inputs influenced a result
- what did not influence a result
- which stages are selection-bearing
- which stages are validation-only
- how stages compose into a projected interpretation

Working hypothesis

Projection may be mature at the construction and current-belief layers while remaining immature at the explanatory layer.

This resembles earlier operational work where visibility improved after explicit shape declarations emerged.

Candidate pressure

Projection may benefit from implementation-backed shape visibility describing:

- consumes
- produces
- influences
- does_not_influence
- authority boundary

The observation does not prescribe implementation changes. It records a possible explanation for why projection remains difficult to reason about despite substantial implementation maturity.
