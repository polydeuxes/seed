# Inquiry Artifact Strengthening Investigation

## Central finding

Repository evidence supports the immediate problem as:

```text
artifact visibility
```

not prose visibility.

The current inquiry-artifacts surface already exposes a visibility gradient:

```text
unknown              repository_visible

finding              partially_visible

supported_conclusion document_visible
unsupported_conclusion document_visible
```

This suggests inquiry artifacts can be strengthened before prose becomes observable.

## Can artifacts be strengthened without prose visibility?

Repository evidence supports partial yes.

Artifacts can become stronger when they have:

```text
repeatable visibility
clear visibility class
surface evidence
relationship candidates
lineage candidates
attachment behavior
```

This does not require prose ingestion.

It requires treating visible artifacts as stronger repository-visible units.

## Linguistic branching analogy

The analogy is useful but limited.

Inquiry artifacts appear less like isolated labels and more like units with attachment candidates.

Examples:

```text
boundary
    attaches to
    finding

unknown
    attaches to
    boundary

pressure
    attaches to
    gap

finding
    attaches to
    pressure
```

This resembles branching or attachment behavior.

But repository evidence does not support implementing a grammar, parser, or graph.

## Structural meaning indicators

An artifact appears structurally meaningful when it shows:

```text
repeatable relationships
multiple investigations
multiple surfaces
attachment behavior
lineage behavior
visibility stability
```

Unknown and boundary are strongest by visibility stability.

Pressure, finding, and gap are strongest by repeated investigation use.

That is visibility without strong structure.

## Historical perspective

The current inquiry-artifact surface resembles early observation-domain work.

Pattern:

```text
visibility first
structure later
```

The current surface classifies artifact visibility before describing artifact relationships, attachment, lineage, or branching.

That does not make richer structure inevitable.

It only shows that visibility has appeared before structure.

## Important distinctions

### Artifact

A recurring inquiry unit.

### Artifact visibility

Whether the artifact is repository-visible, partially visible, or document-visible.

### Artifact relationship

A link between artifacts.

### Artifact attachment

A local relation where one artifact qualifies or anchors another.

### Artifact lineage

A longer relation where one artifact leads to another across investigations.

### Artifact structure

Repeatable relationship, attachment, or lineage behavior.

These are related but not equivalent.

## Supported conclusions

1. Inquiry artifact strengthening can be investigated before prose visibility.
2. Unknown and boundary are the strongest artifacts for repository-visible strengthening.
3. Pressure, finding, and gap are strong partially visible candidates.
4. Supported conclusion, unsupported conclusion, and open question remain mostly document-visible.
5. Artifact relationships appear repeatedly, but most are not repository-visible as relationships.
6. Inquiry artifacts appear more like structured units with attachment candidates than isolated labels.
7. The current inquiry-artifacts surface resembles an early observation model where visibility precedes richer structure.
8. Artifact strengthening does not require claiming prose is observable.

## Unsupported conclusions

- Prose ingestion is required now.
- Inquiry graphs should be implemented.
- Artifact relationships are already fully repository-visible.
- Artifact attachment is implemented.
- Artifact lineage is implemented.
- Linguistic branching is more than an analogy.
- Visibility guarantees structure.
- The artifact list is complete or canonical.

## Acceptance answers

### Can inquiry artifacts become structurally meaningful before prose visibility exists?

Partially yes. Unknown, boundary, pressure, finding, and gap already show enough visibility or recurrence to support strengthening as artifacts.

### What relationships already appear between inquiry artifacts?

Gap-to-pressure, pressure-to-finding, finding-to-boundary, boundary-to-unknown, finding-to-supported-conclusion, and supported-conclusion-to-new-pressure are the strongest recurring candidates.

### Does repository evidence support artifact attachment or branching?

It supports attachment as an analogy and candidate pattern, not as implementation-backed structure.

### Can inquiry artifacts be strengthened using repository-visible evidence alone?

Partially. Stronger visibility classes and endpoint evidence can be strengthened without prose visibility, but richer movement still depends on human interpretation.

### Does the current inquiry-artifact surface resemble an early-stage observation model?

Yes. It exposes artifact visibility before artifact relationships, attachment, lineage, or branching.
