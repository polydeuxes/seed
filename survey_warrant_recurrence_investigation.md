# Survey Warrant Recurrence Investigation

## Scout boundary

This is exactly one bounded Scout investigation. It asks only whether recurring survey warrant families appear across previous Scout investigations, Survey reports, Characterizations, neighborhood investigations, constitutional recovery reports, and bounded inquiries.

It does not recover a taxonomy, stabilize vocabulary, recover competencies, recover runtime behavior, recommend implementation, recommend slices, or promote presentation labels into repository knowledge.

Repository authority wins.

## App visibility used

The app was used only as repository visibility:

```text
python scripts/seed_local.py --documentation-structure --recurrence --summary-only --limit 20
```

The app reported 577 documents and structural recurrence across section labels, front matter keys, heading depths, code fence languages, and link target classes. Its boundary explicitly states read-only structural recurrence only, with no prose interpretation, claim extraction, authority inference, ontology promotion, event-ledger writes, or repository mutation. This Scout uses that app output only to confirm that recurrence can be observed structurally; it does not use the output to define warrant families.

## Method

The Scout reviewed recurring report shapes using repository documents and bounded searches for survey, Scout, characterization, investigation, audit, inventory, coverage, comparison, recurrence, implementation evidence, recovered artifact, unsupported conclusions, confidence, and preserved unknowns.

A recurring warrant family is treated as supported only when multiple repository artifacts show the same bounded question shape, implementation-evidence posture, investigation shape, and produced artifact pattern. A candidate marker remains unsupported when recurrence was vocabulary-only or when stronger conclusions would exceed the reviewed evidence.

## Recurring warrant families supported

### 1. Scout / reconnaissance warrant

**Recurring bounded question:** What visible terrain, pressure, landmark, or candidate recurrence is present, without characterizing it, recovering a family, or recommending implementation?

**Recurring implementation evidence:** Scout reports repeatedly begin by bounding the investigation and preserving stronger conclusions as unknown. `constitutional_cadence_scout_audit.md` says it is one bounded Scout audit, searches recurring terrain, and refuses characterization, competencies, implementation recommendations, slices, runtime behavior, and valley connectivity. `constitutional_exchange_scout_audit.md` uses the same one-bounded-Scout posture for exchange currency recurrence. Constitutional and architectural investigations repeatedly use Scout output as a search warrant for later survey or characterization rather than as final recovered knowledge.

**Recurring investigation shape:** Broad repository scans, search markers treated as hypotheses, visible landmarks recorded, pressure noted, unsupported connections preserved, confidence split between recurrence and stronger conclusions.

**Recurring produced artifact:** Scout audit or investigation report containing searched areas, recurring landmarks, visible pressure, preserved unknowns, and confidence.

**Confidence:** High that this warrant recurs. Low that Scout recurrence alone authorizes any recovered taxonomy or competency.

### 2. Survey / neighborhood-resolution warrant

**Recurring bounded question:** What local terrain or implementation neighborhood is visible at survey resolution, without recovering ownership, bridges, topology, competencies, or implementation work?

**Recurring implementation evidence:** `constitutional_valley_survey.md` explicitly uses a completed Scout audit as its search warrant and increases local observational resolution while refusing competency recovery, district recovery, topology recovery, bridge recovery, ownership recovery, runtime recovery, implementation recommendation, and slice recommendation. `visibility_coverage_composition_neighborhood_survey.md` asks what implementation neighborhood Visibility Coverage Composition is and lists visible inputs, outputs, internal activities, neighboring neighborhoods, maturity, pressure, unknowns, and confidence. `typed_unknown_preservation_neighborhood_survey.md` performs the same neighborhood-survey style for Typed Unknown Preservation.

**Recurring investigation shape:** Use a bounded prior warrant or local implementation surface; enumerate recurring implementation evidence, visible inputs and outputs, internal activities, neighboring neighborhoods, maturity or pressure where visible, unsupported conclusions, preserved unknowns, and confidence.

**Recurring produced artifact:** Survey or neighborhood survey report ending in `Survey complete.` or `Neighborhood survey complete.`

**Confidence:** High that survey/neighborhood-resolution warrants recur. Medium that their common shape is stable across all surveys because some surveys are constitutional and others implementation-neighborhood focused.

### 3. Characterization / comparative-distinction warrant

**Recurring bounded question:** Given already surveyed or investigated evidence, what distinguishing characteristics are supported, and what stronger claims remain unsupported?

**Recurring implementation evidence:** `time_neighborhood_characterization.md` states it is exactly one bounded Characterization of already surveyed Time evidence and refuses another survey, new neighborhoods, bridges, topology, ownership, scheduling, runtime behavior, centrality, and implementation recommendations. It reviews surveyed neighborhoods and characterizes recurring distinguishing characteristics, comparative evidence, confidence, and preserved unknowns. `constitutional_commitment_characterization.md` similarly uses the Scout report as a search warrant and reviews only recurring evidence associated with one valley while refusing competencies, districts, topology, bridges, runtime behavior, physiology, implementation recommendations, and slices. `operational_visibility_candidate_characterization.md` follows a characterization posture for candidate operational visibility without implementation recovery or campaign recommendations.

**Recurring investigation shape:** Start from prior survey/Scout evidence; compare only reviewed evidence; list distinguishing characteristics or local meanings; preserve contrasts and unknowns; explicitly reject ownership, topology, runtime, and implementation conclusions.

**Recurring produced artifact:** Characterization report ending in `Characterization complete.` or equivalent bounded conclusion.

**Confidence:** High that characterization is a recurring warrant. Medium-high that its common produced artifact is a distinction-preserving report rather than a recovery artifact.

### 4. Inventory warrant

**Recurring bounded question:** What items, surfaces, sections, artifacts, dependencies, responsibilities, or entries are present in a bounded repository surface?

**Recurring implementation evidence:** The repository contains recurring inventory artifacts such as `implementation_responsibility_family_inventory_audit.md`, `docs/pending_action_lifecycle_inventory.md`, diagnostic inventory implementation and tests, question-surface inventory implementation and tests, observation inventory surfaces, and app-visible documentation structure recurrence output. Inventory work typically enumerates rows or entries and preserves that enumeration is not ownership, authority, or recovered implementation unless separately proved.

**Recurring investigation shape:** Define bounded source set; enumerate discovered entries; often include counts, categories, paths, rows, or registry entries; preserve scope and authority limits.

**Recurring produced artifact:** Inventory report, registry output, inventory audit, or CLI inventory JSON/text surface.

**Confidence:** High that inventory warrants recur. Medium for any single shared inventory semantics, because inventories range from documents to diagnostics to operational surfaces.

### 5. Coverage / visibility-coverage warrant

**Recurring bounded question:** Which discovered surfaces are visible, registered, covered, unchecked, or missing from a declared registry or audit surface?

**Recurring implementation evidence:** `visibility_coverage_composition_neighborhood_survey.md` identifies a neighborhood that composes discovered operational surfaces with CLI-surface classification rows into a coverage audit, reporting discovered, registered, unregistered, unregistered classifications, and diagnostic-inventory invisibility impact. The AGENTS.md operational visibility contract requires diagnostic inventory and shape-audit updates when diagnostic or operational surfaces change. Diagnostic inventory and diagnostic shape audit tests are recurring acceptance evidence for operational visibility surfaces.

**Recurring investigation shape:** Compare discovered surfaces against a registry or declared audit shape; report covered and uncovered surfaces; preserve non-mutation and read-only boundaries; test registration and shape visibility.

**Recurring produced artifact:** Coverage audit, visibility report, diagnostic inventory row, diagnostic shape-audit spec, or tests proving presence in inventory and shape audit.

**Confidence:** High for operational visibility/diagnostic coverage. Medium outside operational surfaces because this Scout did not prove coverage is universal across all repository domains.

### 6. Comparison / contrast warrant

**Recurring bounded question:** How do already visible artifacts, neighborhoods, domains, or concepts differ without collapsing them into one owner, topology, or taxonomy?

**Recurring implementation evidence:** `time_neighborhood_characterization.md` repeatedly uses comparative evidence to distinguish occurrence time, observation time, source sample time, knowledge time, preservation time, diagnostic elapsed timing, progress cadence, and cache visibility time. `cross_domain_architectural_recurrence_investigation.md`, `cross_domain_orientation_territory_recurrence_investigation.md`, `external_artifact_intake_vs_provider_language_translation_investigation.md`, and multiple `*_vs_*` or `*_boundary_*` audits show bounded comparison as a recurring investigation style.

**Recurring investigation shape:** Select already visible comparands; state what each is and is not; use implementation or prior-survey evidence; preserve non-collapse, unknowns, and confidence.

**Recurring produced artifact:** Comparative characterization, boundary audit, reconciliation, or investigation report.

**Confidence:** High that comparison/contrast warrants recur. Medium that all comparison reports share one artifact shape.

### 7. Resolution / bounded-identity warrant

**Recurring bounded question:** What subject, scope, identity, or bounded question is being resolved before or during inquiry, without turning resolution into universal ownership or a competency taxonomy?

**Recurring implementation evidence:** `inquiry_competency_district_investigation.md` explicitly names Scope and Resolution as recurring implementation-supported district evidence around lawful inquiry admission and bounded inquiry identity before evidence-specific work proceeds, while preserving that Scope and Resolution are not proven as isolated family members. `inquiry_subject_resolution_investigation.md`, `question_to_inquiry_transition_characterization.md`, and question-surface inventory evidence recur around bounded ask identity, eligibility, dispatch, and authority boundaries.

**Recurring investigation shape:** Identify the bounded subject or question; verify eligibility/scope/authority; separate admission or identity resolution from evidence-specific execution; preserve unsupported family or competency conclusions.

**Recurring produced artifact:** Inquiry-resolution investigation, district-resolution report, question-surface inventory entry, or characterization of question-to-inquiry transition.

**Confidence:** Medium-high that a resolution warrant recurs. Medium-low for the supplied marker as a named family because repository evidence often treats resolution with scope/admission and explicitly refuses full family recovery.

### 8. Recurrence warrant

**Recurring bounded question:** What repeats across repository artifacts, and is that repetition evidence of visible terrain only or enough for a stronger conclusion?

**Recurring implementation evidence:** The app documentation-structure recurrence output reports structural recurrence while explicitly refusing prose interpretation, authority inference, and ontology promotion. `architectural_naming_recurrence_characterization.md`, `cross_domain_architectural_recurrence_investigation.md`, `cross_domain_orientation_territory_recurrence_investigation.md`, `constitutional_cadence_scout_audit.md`, and `constitutional_valley_survey.md` all treat recurrence as evidence that must be bounded before stronger claims are made.

**Recurring investigation shape:** Search for repeated words, structures, surfaces, or report patterns; separate recurrence from meaning, ownership, competency, or implementation authority; preserve unknowns and confidence.

**Recurring produced artifact:** Recurrence investigation, recurrence characterization, Scout audit, survey section, or app recurrence report.

**Confidence:** High that recurrence warrants recur. High that recurrence alone is repeatedly treated as insufficient for taxonomy or vocabulary stabilization.

### 9. Completion / family-completion audit warrant

**Recurring bounded question:** Is a previously recovered family, slice sequence, or owner sufficiently complete for the reviewed scope, and what remains outside it?

**Recurring implementation evidence:** Files such as `projection_diagnostics_family_completion_audit.md`, `state_build_cache_debug_family_completion_audit.md`, `external_orientation_family_completion_audit.md`, `competency_interrogation_family_completion_audit.md`, `answer_composition_family_completion_audit.md`, and `timing_visibility_current_facts_completion_audit.md` recur as completion audits. They typically verify that specific boundaries or slices are complete while preserving remaining compression, counterexamples, unsupported broader frameworks, and stop conditions.

**Recurring investigation shape:** Review prior slices or recovered boundaries; check remaining compression and counterexamples; distinguish completed local scope from broader unsupported work; state whether to stop or continue only if new evidence appears.

**Recurring produced artifact:** Family completion audit or completion audit report.

**Confidence:** High that completion audits recur as warrants. Medium that this is a survey warrant rather than a post-survey audit warrant; it appears adjacent to survey/recovery cadence rather than one of the supplied markers.

### 10. Boundary / non-collapse warrant

**Recurring bounded question:** Where is the boundary between two adjacent surfaces, claims, responsibilities, or artifacts, and what must not collapse across it?

**Recurring implementation evidence:** `boundary_localization_characterization.md`, `operational_vocabulary_compatibility_boundary_audit.md`, `external_artifact_intake_vs_provider_language_translation_investigation.md`, `pressure_visibility_evidence_classification_boundary_investigation.md`, `docs/boundary_claim_reconciliation.md`, and many `*_boundary_*` audits recur around separating adjacent surfaces. The AGENTS.md instructions also enforce diagnostic-recording boundaries and presentation-vocabulary boundaries.

**Recurring investigation shape:** Identify adjacent terms or surfaces; compare implementation evidence; state permitted and forbidden crossing; preserve unknowns and unsupported ownership or runtime conclusions.

**Recurring produced artifact:** Boundary audit, boundary reconciliation, boundary characterization, or investigation report.

**Confidence:** High that boundary/non-collapse warrants recur. Medium that it should be called a survey warrant family, because many instances are audits or reconciliations rather than surveys.

## Unsupported candidate warrant families

### Discovery

**Conclusion:** Partially supported as a recurring action inside Scout and inventory work, but not independently supported here as a distinct warrant family.

**Evidence:** Scout reports discover recurring landmarks; inventory and operational-surface work discover entries; visibility coverage uses discovered surfaces. The recurring warrant appears to be Scout/reconnaissance or inventory/coverage rather than a separate earned Discovery family.

**Confidence:** Medium.

### Generic Survey

**Conclusion:** Unsupported as a single generic survey warrant that explains all survey-like work.

**Evidence:** `constitutional_valley_survey.md`, `unknown_currency_survey.md`, `visibility_coverage_composition_neighborhood_survey.md`, and `typed_unknown_preservation_neighborhood_survey.md` share survey discipline but ask different bounded questions and produce different local artifacts. Repository evidence supports recurring survey warrants, not one generic survey that owns all recurrence.

**Confidence:** High.

### Stable vocabulary warrant

**Conclusion:** Unsupported.

**Evidence:** Multiple reviewed artifacts explicitly refuse vocabulary stabilization from recurrence alone. App recurrence output explicitly refuses ontology promotion and authority inference. The present Scout therefore preserves warrant labels as investigation handles only.

**Confidence:** High.

### Competency recovery warrant for this task

**Conclusion:** Unsupported and forbidden for this investigation.

**Evidence:** Some repository documents investigate competencies, but the current task forbids competency recovery. The reviewed Scout, Survey, Characterization, and boundary reports also repeatedly distinguish bounded evidence from competency promotion.

**Confidence:** High.
