# Characterization Organizing Principle Recurrence Scout

## Boundary

This is one bounded Scout investigation. It reviews completed Characterization reports as repository evidence and asks only whether Characterization investigations recur in the kinds of organizing principles they produce.

This Scout does not characterize any one district, recover implementation, recover runtime behavior, recommend changes, stabilize new vocabulary, or conclude that Time is representative of the repository.

## Supported recurring organizing principles

### 1. Non-collapse boundaries

**Recurring bounded question.** What must remain distinct while a Characterization report explains a transition, family, surface, or recovery result?

**Recurring implementation evidence.** Reports repeatedly cite implementation-backed boundaries as evidence without reimplementing them: inquiry notes do not become facts or commands; candidate support does not become verified capability; diagnostic visibility does not become cluster mutation; projection remains a read model over admitted evidence.

**Recurring investigative shape.** Characterization commonly starts with a candidate compression, then refuses excess authority by naming what the produced artifact is not. The investigation preserves adjacent classes rather than allowing the answer to collapse them into one owner, engine, authority, or truth source.

**Recurring produced artifact.** Boundary tables, unchanged-artifact lists, negative-authority statements, non-goal sections, and preserved-unknown sections.

**Representative Characterization reports.** `constitutional_conservation_characterization.md`, `constitutional_interpretation_characterization.md`, `constitutional_transition_artifact_delta_characterization.md`, `repository_artifact_runtime_artifact_characterization.md`, `time_higher_order_grouping_characterization.md`, `typed_unknown_characterization.md`.

**Confidence.** High. Recurrence is explicit across conservation, interpretation, transition, artifact/runtime, unknown, and Time reports. The term `non-collapse boundary` remains this Scout's descriptive compression, not a stabilized repository conclusion.

### 2. Authority distinctions

**Recurring bounded question.** What authorizes the claim, transition, output, promotion, rendering, or interpretation, and what does not?

**Recurring implementation evidence.** Characterization reports repeatedly distinguish implementation-backed authority from presentation, operator prose, diagnostic visibility, support rows, candidate rows, pressure, recommendation, and methodology language.

**Recurring investigative shape.** The report asks whether a surface or artifact may promote, interpret, command, mutate, verify, or own something. It then localizes authority to the smallest supported evidence path and leaves broader authority unsupported.

**Recurring produced artifact.** Authority-boundary sections, promotion-family descriptions, warrant descriptions, and matrices separating evidence, support, pressure, readiness, admission, permission, and command.

**Representative Characterization reports.** `constitutional_promotion_authority_characterization.md`, `constitutional_warrant_characterization.md`, `constitutional_conservation_characterization.md`, `constitutional_interpretation_characterization.md`, `provider_language_translation_responsibility_characterization.md`, `docs/uncertainty_and_authority_visibility_characterization.md`, `time_higher_order_grouping_characterization.md`.

**Confidence.** High.

## Unsupported candidate organizing principles

The following supplied search markers were not supported as independent recurring organizing principles beyond the supported patterns above:

- **A single global non-collapse framework.** Recurring boundary preservation is supported; one repository-wide framework named `non-collapse boundaries` is not.
- **A universal producer / consumer registry.** Producer/consumer and transition-path distinctions recur, but the reviewed Characterization evidence preserves consumer and relationship unknowns rather than proving a complete registry.
- **A single compatibility architecture.** Compatibility and continuity boundaries recur, but generic deletion, replacement, supersession, lineage, and compatibility semantics remain unresolved unless local reports prove them.
- **A universal neighborhood topology.** Neighboring/locality distinctions recur, but Characterization reports preserve locality and neighborhood scope rather than proving a single topology for all repository activity.
- **Time as representative of Characterization.** The Time report exhibits authority, visibility, and support/claim boundaries, but this Scout does not use Time as the source of recurrence. Its evidence is representative only where other completed Characterization reports also recover the same pattern.
- **Presentation vocabulary as preserved knowledge.** Reports may use terms such as neighborhood, frontier, pressure, locality, or conservation as investigative compression. This Scout does not promote those terms beyond the evidence of completed reports.

## Representative implementation evidence

Across completed Characterization reports, implementation evidence is used in a recurring bounded way:

- as evidence that a boundary exists;
- as evidence that a surface is read-only, non-mutating, or non-authoritative;
- as evidence that support, candidate, or verification states are separate;
- as evidence that an artifact or output has a local owner or role;
- as evidence that a transition produces one artifact without changing another;
- as evidence that a proposed promotion is unsupported.

The recurring principle is not implementation recovery. It is implementation-backed refusal to overstate characterization outputs.

## Recurring investigative shapes

Completed Characterization reports repeatedly use these shapes:

1. **Candidate compression with authority refusal.** A proposed term or family is treated as a hypothesis, then narrowed by repository authority.
2. **Evidence-reviewed answer.** The report lists implementation-backed and prior-investigation evidence before stating the answer.
3. **Family or distinction recovery.** The report identifies recurring local families rather than one universal mechanism.
4. **Negative boundary preservation.** The report states what the artifact, output, or transition does not do.
5. **Transition or delta accounting.** The report compares changed artifacts with unchanged authorities, supports, facts, or boundaries.
6. **Neighborhood survey.** The report distinguishes local evidence from neighboring adjacency.
7. **Unknown preservation.** The report records unresolved relations, promotion authority, implementation status, or generalization boundaries.
8. **Confidence-limited conclusion.** The report names confidence and avoids turning investigation vocabulary into architecture.

## Preserved unknowns

This Scout preserves these unknowns:

- Whether the supported recurring organizing principles should ever become stable repository vocabulary remains unknown.
- Whether Characterization has a complete taxonomy of organizing principles remains unknown.
- Whether every completed Characterization report exhibits every supported principle remains unknown and unsupported.
- Whether a future implementation-backed surface should inventory Characterization outputs remains unknown; this Scout recommends no changes.
- Whether Time's higher-order groupings should influence other districts remains unknown.
- Whether recurring investigative shapes are manual practice, durable methodology, or future executable behavior remains unknown.
- Whether producer/consumer, compatibility, and neighborhood distinctions have complete repository-wide models remains unknown.

## Answer

According to recurring repository evidence, Characterization investigations recover organizing principles around preserving distinctions: boundaries that prevent collapse, authority, identity, role and responsibility, support versus claim, producer/consumer and transition paths, compatibility and continuity, neighboring/locality, evidence-gated promotion/readiness, and unknown preservation.

The strongest recurring result is not one universal architecture. The recurring result is a bounded investigative habit: Characterization produces evidence-backed distinctions, records what those distinctions authorize, refuses unsupported promotion, and preserves unknowns where repository evidence ends.

Scout investigation complete.
