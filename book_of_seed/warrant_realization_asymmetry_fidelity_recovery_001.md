## Determination summary

1. **Coherent higher-order warrant grammar:** Partly present, but not as a universal warrant ontology or pipeline. The Book preserves a higher-order grammar of bounded subject, support, standing, scope, consumer, authority limit, and preserved Unknown.
2. **Several valid local warrant families:** Yes. The Book more strongly supports several local warrant families than one universal warrant kind.
3. **Unknown conclusion:** The existence of a single higher-order warrant architecture remains Unknown and is not needed for Fidelity examination.
4. **Already sufficient distinctions:** The Book is already sufficient to judge many later implementation witnesses for improper collapse of support, sufficiency, warrant, authority, movement, execution, stopping, and completion.
5. **Compressions preventing expectation formation:** The main compression is not a clause saying all warrants are one thing; rather, it is sparse local grammar in operational realization and stopping/completion, where the Book names warrants without fully enumerating who judges sufficiency, what standing is consumed, and what stronger authority is not created.
6. **Lawful realization asymmetries:** Object fields, SQL predicates, rows, query outputs, serialized handoffs, distributed local assertions, or contrastive comparison surfaces may each lawfully express warrant distinctions if they preserve bounded subject, consumer, support, standing, scope, authority limit, contrary material, and Unknown.
7. **Representation-specific assumptions accidentally promoted:** Existing clauses are mostly resistant to Python-shaped and SQL-shaped promotion. The sparse phrase "an operational realization warrant" could invite mechanism-fitness warrant to sound like the warrant pattern, so a small local repair is warranted.
8. **Selection observability:** Existing grammar is sufficient to evaluate whether an implementation silently equates observable selection artifacts with selection acts, downstream reliance, sufficiency, or authorization. It is not yet sufficient to require one observability surface, trace topology, or preserved artifact form across all realizations.

## Local warrant relations recovered

### 1. Lens assertion warrant

- **Bounded subject:** A lens output as a representation of supplied source material under a declared method.
- **Supported claim:** The supplied sources expose this bounded representation under this lens method.
- **Standing consumed:** Source identity, source availability to the lens, and faithful transformation standing.
- **Evidence/support required:** Source identity and transformation basis sufficient for the declared lens.
- **Who judges sufficiency:** The responsible lens boundary or consumer boundary for the lens purpose.
- **Standing produced:** Bounded representation standing.
- **Scope/purpose limit:** Rendering, projection, selection, or composition according to the declared lens method.
- **Contrary material/Unknown surviving:** Missing provenance, authority, occurrence, and standing remain missing; absence may be made visible but not repaired.
- **Stronger authority not created:** No answer sufficiency, constitutional road, transition warrant, upstream truth, or authority.
- **Relation type:** Warrant plus relation; not a universal uptake stage.

### 2. Consumer uptake / road warrant

- **Bounded subject:** Upstream material accepted by a responsible consumer for a declared purpose.
- **Supported claim:** The consumer may use, narrow, or accept an upstream assertion at that consumer boundary.
- **Standing consumed:** Prior consumer standing to consume, preserved evidence, upstream warrant, identity, scope, authority, confidence, and Unknown limits.
- **Evidence/support required:** Boundary-appropriate identities and invariants, including consumer validation.
- **Who judges sufficiency:** The responsible consumer boundary.
- **Standing produced:** Bounded consumer-local use, standing, or assertion support.
- **Scope/purpose limit:** The declared consumer purpose only.
- **Contrary material/Unknown surviving:** Conflicts, refusal, missing identity, insufficient consumer validation, and Unknown can survive every crossing.
- **Stronger authority not created:** No producer occurrence proof, no re-establishment of upstream standing, no expanded authority.
- **Relation type:** Relation family; constitutional road is the assertion-preserving subfamily.

### 3. Applicability, admission, and consumption standing

- **Bounded subject:** Material presented to a consumer boundary.
- **Supported claim:** The material is applicable, admitted, or consumed within a consumer-local boundary.
- **Standing consumed:** Availability and applicability evidence for applicability; applicability plus admission evidence for admission; admitted material plus responsible use standing for consumption.
- **Evidence/support required:** Boundary-specific evidence rather than mere existence or row/artifact membership.
- **Who judges sufficiency:** The consumer-local applicability, admission, or use boundary.
- **Standing produced:** Applicable, admitted, consumed, unadmitted, inapplicable, conflicting, or Unknown standing.
- **Scope/purpose limit:** The consumer boundary and declared purpose.
- **Contrary material/Unknown surviving:** Applicable material may be unadmitted; admitted material may be unconsumed; consumers may refuse or preserve Unknown.
- **Stronger authority not created:** No re-establishment of upstream standing, producer occurrence proof, or authority expansion.
- **Relation type:** Standing and act family; not one sequential pipeline.

### 4. Evidence support warrant

- **Bounded subject:** Evidence-shaped material used for bounded examination.
- **Supported claim:** The material can serve as applicable represented support for a particular examination.
- **Standing consumed:** Evidence identity distinguished from existence, source attribution, evidence kind, collection context, bounded subject, supported claim, authority boundary, preservation horizon, confidence or uncertainty, and provenance relation.
- **Evidence/support required:** The dimensions required by the examination; absence of dimensions remains absence.
- **Who judges sufficiency:** The examining consumer boundary.
- **Standing produced:** Applicable represented support for further examination.
- **Scope/purpose limit:** Examination of the bounded claim form.
- **Contrary material/Unknown surviving:** Insufficiency, support loss, mismatch, comparison pressure, and Unknown survive as bounded findings.
- **Stronger authority not created:** No factual truth, independent corroboration, current lawful state, execution authorization, universal evidence admission, verified provenance, causation, or responsibility.
- **Relation type:** Support-binding standing; not sufficiency and not authority.

### 5. Selection act warrant

- **Bounded subject:** A bounded candidate set and a selection act choosing zero or more candidates or preserving lawful non-selection.
- **Supported claim:** A lawful selection occurred for that candidate set under the exact evidence, comparison, or policy.
- **Standing consumed:** Candidate-set identity, selection evidence, exact comparison or policy, applicability required by the owner.
- **Evidence/support required:** Enough basis, identity, uncertainty, or refusal information for the intended consumer to distinguish lawful selection from unsupported omission.
- **Who judges sufficiency:** The selection owner and later consumer boundary for its own reliance.
- **Standing produced:** Selected result, non-selection, and bounded selection standing or focus standing where the local road grants it.
- **Scope/purpose limit:** The candidate set, selector kind, owner, and downstream purpose.
- **Contrary material/Unknown surviving:** Non-selected alternatives may or may not need preservation depending on selector; unsupported keys, uncertainty, refusal, and missing basis can survive.
- **Stronger authority not created:** No authorization, execution, inquiry opening, every downstream subject, reusable selection standing, or downstream reliance warrant.
- **Relation type:** Act and artifact; candidate warrant and selection warrant remain distinct.

### 6. Selection-to-consumer reliance warrant

- **Bounded subject:** A consumer's use of a producer selection artifact or selected subject.
- **Supported claim:** The consumer may adopt selected-subject standing, narrow representation selection, or revalidate coherence for a bounded purpose.
- **Standing consumed:** Producer artifact standing plus consumer-side validation and purpose-specific coherence.
- **Evidence/support required:** Identity and invariants sufficient for that consumer's smaller assertion.
- **Who judges sufficiency:** The downstream consumer boundary.
- **Standing produced:** Consumer-local reliance or selected-subject standing, not universal acceptance.
- **Scope/purpose limit:** The consumer's declared reliance form.
- **Contrary material/Unknown surviving:** Producer assertions not accepted by the consumer remain unaccepted; exact-key occurrence may remain unproven; conflicts survive.
- **Stronger authority not created:** No authority, execution, consideration warrant from representation selection, or movement warrant.
- **Relation type:** Reliance relation; not the selection act itself.

### 7. Constraint / precondition warrant

- **Bounded subject:** A proposed act or relevant context governed by a constraint.
- **Supported claim:** A permission, prohibition, requirement, or condition applies to whether or how that act may proceed.
- **Standing consumed:** Applicable policy, bound identity, and current evidence.
- **Evidence/support required:** Policy and context evidence appropriate to the boundary.
- **Who judges sufficiency:** The constraint boundary or policy boundary.
- **Standing produced:** Constraint result or preserved condition.
- **Scope/purpose limit:** The governed act and boundary.
- **Contrary material/Unknown surviving:** Failed, unmet, incomplete, conflicting, or Unknown preconditions may survive.
- **Stronger authority not created:** Passing constraint is not complete authority; access, visible route, capability candidate, catalog entry, or read-only surface does not authorize use, execution, mutation, event-ledger admission, or cluster change.
- **Relation type:** Constraint and judgment; not the governed act.

### 8. Operational realization warrant

- **Bounded subject:** A selected mechanism's fitness for a bounded operational purpose.
- **Supported claim:** The selected mechanism is fit for the contemplated operational role under preserved evidence, authority, policy, and preconditions.
- **Standing consumed:** Selection standing for the mechanism, mechanism-fitness evidence, relevant authority scope, policy and precondition standing.
- **Evidence/support required:** Evidence sufficient to connect selected mechanism and bounded purpose without importing operator or approval authority.
- **Who judges sufficiency:** The responsible operational-realization boundary.
- **Standing produced:** Mechanism-fitness warrant for a bounded proposal or request.
- **Scope/purpose limit:** The selected mechanism and contemplated call or proposal.
- **Contrary material/Unknown surviving:** Failed preconditions, missing authority, refusal, incompatible purpose, and Unknown remain available.
- **Stronger authority not created:** No execution, proof of execution, reusable blanket permission, operator authority, approval authority, or universal realization grammar.
- **Relation type:** Local warrant family; not the master warrant kind.

### 9. Movement warrant

- **Bounded subject:** A transition in lawful position, standing, focus, or advancement posture.
- **Supported claim:** Orientation has become movement through a separately warranted transition.
- **Standing consumed:** Subject, authority, evidence, and limits for the transition claimed.
- **Evidence/support required:** Transition-specific warrant rather than display, association, navigation, runtime write, or projected update alone.
- **Who judges sufficiency:** The responsible movement boundary.
- **Standing produced:** Movement standing or changed lawful position within scope.
- **Scope/purpose limit:** The claimed transition only.
- **Contrary material/Unknown surviving:** Orientation without transition, runtime mutation without warrant, and Unknown remain possible.
- **Stronger authority not created:** No execution authority, mutation authority, or proof of advancement from state change alone.
- **Relation type:** Warranted transition; not mutation.

### 10. Authority-scope / reliance warrant

- **Bounded subject:** Downstream reliance on warranted content, and authority bound to a goal or act.
- **Supported claim:** The consumer may rely only within preserved role, purpose, provenance, evidence, authority, scope, confidence, and Unknown limits.
- **Standing consumed:** Warranted content and separately bound authority where authority is claimed.
- **Evidence/support required:** Operator expressions or explicit approvals after interpretation and scope binding; internal models, selections, records, and handoffs are insufficient to create authority.
- **Who judges sufficiency:** The authority-scope or reliance boundary.
- **Standing produced:** Bounded reliance or authority participation, not standing expansion.
- **Scope/purpose limit:** The exact role, purpose, authority boundary, and negative authority.
- **Contrary material/Unknown surviving:** Non-reliance, negative authority, uncertainty, and Unknown must stop where the warrant stops.
- **Stronger authority not created:** No truth, ownership, implementation authority, mutation authority, Book ratification, reusable approval, or adjudicative authority.
- **Relation type:** Authority boundary and reliance relation.

### 11. Stopping warrant

- **Bounded subject:** A decision not to continue movement or inquiry under current conditions.
- **Supported claim:** Sufficiency, exhaustion, impossibility, refusal, policy block, operator condition, bounded horizon, or explicit status warrants stopping.
- **Standing consumed:** The condition supporting non-movement and the boundary within which it applies.
- **Evidence/support required:** Condition-specific support; no selected movement alone is insufficient.
- **Who judges sufficiency:** Unknown in full ordering; locally, the stopping boundary.
- **Standing produced:** Bounded stopping or non-movement standing.
- **Scope/purpose limit:** The particular inquiry, movement, horizon, or goal condition.
- **Contrary material/Unknown surviving:** Remaining needs, different horizons, policy changes, refusal limits, and Unknown may survive.
- **Stronger authority not created:** No completion, global impossibility, or no remaining need.
- **Relation type:** Judgment or decision; full ordering remains Unknown.

### 12. Completion warrant

- **Bounded subject:** A claim that a bounded goal is complete.
- **Supported claim:** Completion, which is stronger than stopping.
- **Standing consumed:** Unknown in complete constitutional ordering; likely requires more than sufficiency projection, bounded horizon, policy block, or explicit status alone.
- **Evidence/support required:** Remains unclear in the examined Book clauses.
- **Who judges sufficiency:** Unknown.
- **Standing produced:** Completion standing if lawfully warranted.
- **Scope/purpose limit:** Bounded goal only; not global completion.
- **Contrary material/Unknown surviving:** The sufficient warrant for completion remains unresolved.
- **Stronger authority not created:** Stopping does not create completion.
- **Relation type:** Warranted judgment; details Unknown.

## Realization-invariant distinctions

The Book already preserves the following expectation chain without requiring it to be a single pipeline:

```text
available != applicable
applicable != admitted
admitted != consumed
consumed != selected
selected != sufficient
sufficient != warranted
warranted != authorized
authorized != executed
```

The following distinctions are also realization-invariant:

```text
support != sufficiency
sufficiency != warrant
warrant != authority

candidate warrant != selection warrant
selection standing != downstream reliance warrant
producer warrant != consumer-local warrant
movement warrant != execution authority
stopping warrant != completion warrant
```

For later Fidelity examination, the constitutional distinction that must survive every faithful realization is not a shared representation of warrant. It is the survival of bounded subject, supported claim, consumed standing, evidence/support, sufficiency judge, produced standing, scope and purpose, contrary material or Unknown, and the stronger authority explicitly not created.

## Lawful realization asymmetries

A faithful realization may express the same constitutional distinction differently:

- A predominantly Python realization may use objects, fields, constructors, validators, methods, exceptions, or immutable values, provided object state does not silently become authority or constructor success become selection, sufficiency, or warrant.
- A predominantly SQL realization may use tables, rows, predicates, constraints, joins, views, queries, or stored findings, provided row membership does not silently become admission, predicate truth does not become authority, query success does not become sufficiency, and NULL or missing rows do not erase Unknown.
- A composed Python-SQL hybrid may serialize, persist, rehydrate, and hand off values, provided serialization does not strengthen standing, durability does not renew occurrence, rehydration does not reproduce warrant, and compatibility does not become admission.
- A responsibility-distributed or native hybrid may hold local standing at several boundaries, provided local consumer sufficiency does not become universal sufficiency and absence of a central artifact does not become absence of warrant.
- A contrastive realization may expose differences, losses, or compensations among the others, provided the comparison surface does not become constitutional authority and one realization's compensation does not become a universal requirement.

## Possible representation compressions

The Book gives enough grammar to resist these compressions during later implementation examination:

### Python-shaped compression

- Warrant as an object field may lawfully preserve a local warrant, but cannot by itself define who relies, what standing is consumed, or what stronger authority is denied.
- Authority as object state is unfaithful if it bypasses interpretation and scope binding.
- Selection as constructor success is unfaithful if it lacks candidate-set identity and selection basis.
- Responsibility as class ownership is insufficient unless the responsible boundary and consumer purpose are preserved.
- Movement as mutation is unfaithful if the transition warrant, authority, evidence, and limits are absent.

### SQL-shaped compression

- Warrant as predicate truth is insufficient unless predicate scope, subject, consumer, evidence, and limits survive.
- Admission as row membership is unfaithful if applicability, admission, and consumption collapse.
- Unknowns collapsed into NULL or missing rows are unfaithful where Unknown, conflict, refusal, or bounded negative findings must survive.
- Sufficiency as query success is insufficient unless the responsible sufficiency judge and contrary material boundary are preserved.
- Authority as constraint satisfaction is unfaithful unless separate authority grant and scope binding are preserved.

### Composed-hybrid compression

- Serialization may preserve material but not strengthen standing.
- Durability may preserve an artifact but not renew occurrence.
- Rehydration may reconstruct representation but not reproduce producer warrant without preserved warrant dimensions.
- Handoff compatibility may enable transport but not admission, consumption, reliance, or authority.

### Distributed-hybrid compression

- Partial local standing may be faithful locally but not globally.
- Absence of a central artifact does not prove absence of warrant.
- Local consumer sufficiency may support local reliance but not universal sufficiency.

### Contrastive-realization compression

- Implementation difference is not constitutional disagreement by itself.
- A compensation required by one realization is not automatically a constitutional requirement for all.
- Comparison output is a lens or examination surface unless separately warranted as a constitutional road or authority boundary.

## Alternative interpretations considered

### Interpretation A: one universal warrant grammar exists

The Book supports a common coordinate grammar: bounded subject, support, standing consumed, sufficiency judgment, standing produced, scope, limits, and non-authority. This can guide Fidelity questioning.

However, the examined clauses do not authorize a universal `Warrant` ontology, a required warrant pipeline, or one canonical representation. Treating the shared coordinates as a universal object, table, interface, or runtime artifact would exceed the Book.

### Interpretation B: several local warrant families exist

This is the stronger reading. Lens assertions, consumer uptake, evidence support, selection acts, selection-to-consumer reliance, constraints, operational realization, movement, authority scope, stopping, and completion each use warrant-like grammar locally while preserving different subjects, consumers, and limits.

### Interpretation C: warrant is only rhetorical vocabulary

This reading is too weak. The Book repeatedly binds warrant language to constitutional distinctions: support does not create truth, selection does not authorize, movement requires separately warranted transition, reliance stops where warrant stops, and stopping is not completion.

### Interpretation D: operational realization supplies the master warrant grammar

This reading is not supported. Operational realization has a local mechanism-fitness warrant and proposal grammar, but the Book does not move all warrant relations into operational realization and does not make selected mechanism fitness the model for evidence, selection, reliance, movement, or completion.

## Maximum expectation for later implementation witnesses

Later implementation witnesses may be held to the following maximum expectation:

- They must preserve the distinction between availability, applicability, admission, consumption, selection, sufficiency, warrant, authority, and execution wherever they claim those standings or acts.
- They must identify or preserve enough bounded subject, supported claim, consumed standing, evidence/support, sufficiency judge, produced standing, scope/purpose limit, contrary material, and Unknown for the warrant relation they claim.
- They must not treat one realization's artifact, class, row, predicate, serialization boundary, handoff, topology, or comparison surface as Seed's constitution.
- They must not silently upgrade support to sufficiency, sufficiency to warrant, warrant to authority, authority to execution, stopping to completion, or local consumer reliance to universal standing.
- They may express distinctions differently according to realization form, and a difference is lawful if it preserves the constitutional distinction within the claim's bounded scope.

## Bounded Fidelity posture for later implementation examination

### What the constitutional grammar requires

The constitutional grammar requires bounded warrant relations. Each claimed warrant must answer, within scope: what subject is warranted; what assertion, reliance, selection, transition, stopping decision, completion claim, or mechanism fitness is supported; what standing is consumed; what evidence or support is required; who judges sufficiency where the Book identifies a judge; what standing is produced; what scope and purpose limit it; what contrary material, conflict, refusal, or Unknown survives; and what stronger authority is explicitly not created.

### What each realization may lawfully express differently

Each realization may express warrant grammar through its own native forms: objects, functions, rows, predicates, views, constraints, serialized packets, persisted records, distributed local decisions, native components, or contrastive comparison outputs. These expressions are lawful when they remain bounded and do not force another realization's artifacts, classes, rows, handoffs, or topology.

### What no implementation may silently collapse

No implementation may silently collapse:

```text
available into applicable;
applicable into admitted;
admitted into consumed;
consumed into selected;
selected into sufficient;
sufficient into warranted;
warranted into authorized;
authorized into executed;
support into sufficiency;
sufficiency into warrant;
warrant into authority;
candidate warrant into selection warrant;
selection standing into downstream reliance warrant;
producer warrant into consumer-local warrant;
movement warrant into execution authority;
stopping warrant into completion warrant.
```

### What the Book does not yet authorize us to expect

The Book does not yet authorize expecting a universal warrant ontology, a universal warrant pipeline, one canonical realization, a required representation form, a required storage topology, one observability surface for selection, or a complete grammar of completion warrant. It authorizes Fidelity examination of local warrant relations according to their bounded subjects, purposes, supports, standings, limits, and preserved Unknowns.
