## Executive finding

Current Book evidence supports `demand` only as **Compatible / Needs clarification**, not as a canonical named doctrine.

A lawful recovered candidate is:

> A constitutional demand is an established necessity imposed by the relation between current standing and a required condition, where satisfaction of that condition is necessary for a bounded relationship, standing, or possible movement to remain available.

This candidate does not make demand a Seed desire, goal, preference, curiosity, selection, or authority. Demand is not the same as movement. It is better recovered as a **relation-backed standing or constraint result**: an incompatibility has been established between what is presently the case and what must be the case for some already-exposed relation to continue, remain possible, or be judged sufficient.

The Book does not yet canonize a universal `Demand` object, producer, registry, manager, pipeline, or artifact family. The safer result is local grammar:

```text
bounded relation or consumer
+
required condition for that relation/consumer
+
current standing
+
warranted comparison showing incompatibility
=
demand standing at that boundary
```

Demand may be real before Seed recognizes it, because the incompatibility can be imposed by the relationship between present conditions and required conditions. But Seed cannot lawfully use it as Seed-owned standing until a bounded establishment preserves source, scope, required condition, current condition, comparison warrant, Unknowns, and limits.

## Demand: recovered grammar

### What establishes the required condition

A required condition is not necessarily chosen by Seed. It may be established by:

- a bounded consumer's acceptance or sufficiency rule;
- an already-selected bounded goal's movement boundary;
- an authority or scope precondition;
- an operational safety, timing, resource, or policy boundary;
- a communication relation whose continuation requires some shared representation;
- a capability claim whose verification requires behavioral evidence;
- an external environment condition that makes a bounded movement unavailable unless some condition is satisfied.

The required condition must be separately warranted. It cannot be invented from the mere fact that Seed can describe a deficiency.

### Who owns the comparison

The comparison is consumer-local or boundary-local. The owner is the establishment boundary that is entitled to judge the current condition against the required condition for its bounded purpose. The consumer may expose the demand, the environment may expose the condition, and Seed may preserve demand standing, but these are not identical ownership roles.

### Evidence for incompatibility

Incompatibility requires more than naming a gap. Evidence may include:

- current standing that lacks or contradicts a required condition;
- recurrence showing a strategy remains ineffective under bounded conditions;
- a measurement outside an authorized comparison boundary;
- a missing authority grant for a requested movement;
- unavailability of evidence required for inquiry narrowing;
- a capability transformation that must be possible but is not yet supported.

The comparison can remain Unknown. A possible gap is not enough to establish demand.

### Demand before recognition

A demand can exist before Seed recognizes it in the external/reality sense: if a relation already requires a condition and present conditions are incompatible, the necessity is imposed whether or not Seed has represented it. But for Seed movement, the demand must be bounded and preserved; otherwise it remains external, Unknown, or unowned by Seed.

### Demand ending

A demand can disappear when its governing relation ends. If the communication relation stops, the communication-grammar demand may no longer be current, although it may remain historically valid as a previously exposed incompatibility. A demand can also be superseded by a changed requirement, satisfied for one consumer but not another, or preserved as unresolved without authorizing movement.

## Demand, goal, desire, and selection

The following distinctions are supported by current Book grammar:

```text
demand present != Seed desire
demand present != Seed preference
demand present != Seed curiosity
demand present != goal adopted
demand present != movement selected
demand present != continuation authorized
goal adopted != demand resolved
selection made != demand satisfied
```

A demand is what is required by the relation. A goal is a bounded condition adopted as an intended outcome. A demand may exist before a goal is adopted, before response selection, before authority, before capability, and before movement opens.

A demand may be transformed into a Seed goal only by a separate lawful movement that adopts a bounded condition as an intended outcome under authority, scope, and stopping limits. That adoption is not automatic. The demand may instead lead to candidate examination, evidence request, authority request, refusal, stopping, preservation of Unknown, or operator adaptation.

## Need, requirement, gap, and demand

Current Book and implementation vocabulary uses `need`, `demand`, `requirement`, `gap`, `insufficiency`, `missing condition`, `blocking boundary`, and `pressure` in overlapping ways. The best evidence-led distinctions are:

- **Requirement**: a declared condition against which applicability, sufficiency, completion, authorization, safety, or comparison may be judged.
- **Need**: a bounded required condition associated with a purpose, question, consumer, or movement; often already local to an advancement or inquiry route.
- **Gap**: historical umbrella vocabulary for a difference, mismatch, missing support, insufficiency, or comparison result between current and required standing.
- **Demand**: necessity imposed by the incompatibility between present standing and a required condition for some bounded relation to remain possible or sufficient.
- **Pressure**: reason for examination or possible movement; not itself authority, standing, or goal.

The Book supports local inquiry-need standing and capability-demand vocabulary in implementation/report witnesses, but it does not support compressing all such cases into one universal demand pipeline.

## Gap compression

`Gap` is best classified as **Historical shorthand / Needs clarification**. The ordinary word can describe a situation between two things, a difference between standings, a measured separation, or the reason a movement cannot proceed. Current Seed recovery has many local gap families:

- evidence;
- knowledge;
- capability;
- reachability;
- dependency;
- authority;
- representation;
- policy or precondition;
- resource or budget;
- operational realization;
- outcome;
- family coverage.

These do not require one universal `Gap` artifact. They may be independent comparison subjects, different required-condition families, different consumer-local incompatibilities, or coordinates of one bounded comparison. The lawfully preserved grammar is:

```text
gap named != gap measured
gap measured != demand established
demand established != response selected
gap reduced != desired outcome caused
gap absent != movement complete
```

A gap may be a comparison result when current standing is measured against a required condition. It may be a diagnostic description when no measurement or demand is established. It may be a blocking boundary only when a consumer owns a required condition and the comparison shows insufficiency for that consumer. Each gap family can have its own producer, warrant, standing, and consumer.

## Baseline, tolerance, and boundaries

Operational measurement recovery already supports the distinction:

- A measurement is bounded testimony about one operation instance.
- A baseline is retained, scoped, evidence-supported understanding of ordinary behavior under declared conditions.
- A tolerance is a comparison boundary requiring authority, purpose, permitted variation, and an establishment method.
- A deviation is distinct from failure, cause, changed regime, or consequence.

This supports the operator's observation that baseline need not be a single value. Baseline may lawfully be a range, distribution, expected interval, categorical posture, behavior family, temporal regime, or other evidence-supported ordinary standing if scope and conditions are preserved.

Tolerance is not baseline. A tolerance may be one-sided or range-shaped. It may define lower and upper boundaries:

```text
too little / too slow / below minimum
↓
acceptable range
↑
too much / too fast / above maximum
```

But the purpose matters. `Too fast` is not universally a problem; it may matter for race risk, throttling, safety, fairness, operator expectation, external protocol timing, resource spikes, auditability, or another consumer-local condition. Ordinary behavior can be unacceptable if the baseline records common but unsafe behavior. Acceptable behavior can be unusual if the authorized tolerance allows a rare but permitted result. Therefore:

```text
baseline != threshold
baseline != tolerance
baseline != desired state
baseline != requirement automatically
baseline != current value
baseline != one prior sample
outside baseline != unacceptable
inside baseline != sufficient
outside tolerance != causal failure
inside tolerance != goal achieved
```

## Timing witness

Timing measurements expose demand only through a consumer-local comparison. The same duration can be acceptable for one consumer and unacceptable for another. A timing demand may arise from deadline, resource pressure, race risk, throttling, safety, operator expectation, service-level requirement, audit cadence, or another condition. The measurement does not establish the baseline, the tolerance, the cause, the failure, or the demand by itself.

A timing demand is therefore local:

```text
operation instance measured
+
consumer-owned timing requirement or tolerance
+
comparison showing incompatibility
=
timing demand standing for that consumer
```

What becomes possible: candidate explanation, request for more measurement, preservation of deviation testimony, authority request, bounded mitigation, or stopping. What remains unauthorized: execution, mutation, causal conclusion, global performance fact, changed baseline, or goal satisfaction by identity.

## Communication and shared-grammar demand

### Console witness standing

Candidate actual condition:

```text
operator repeatedly supplies free-form material
```

Candidate required condition:

```text
some shared representation or semantic grammar must be available
for bounded operator communication through this console to remain possible
```

Current standing:

```text
shared grammar Unknown or unsupported
```

Possible result:

```text
communication-grammar demand
```

The demand is not established by the literal `hello` alone. One uninterpreted input establishes at most operator-origin material, literal preservation, source role if preserved, representation Unknown, grammar Unknown, meaning Unknown, purpose Unknown, response expectation Unknown, and perhaps communication pressure if the console's purpose is to receive operator material.

The console's declared purpose matters. If the console is intended as operator communication ingress, then repeated free-form operator material plus repeated non-uptake of structured options can expose a boundary-local incompatibility: the available structured channel is not currently producing shared communication standing with this operator interaction. That can warrant a shared-grammar demand without establishing English, operator intention, refusal, preference, authorization to acquire material, or Seed goal.

### What enum non-uptake establishes

Failure to use Seed's enum choices establishes only non-uptake observed under the preserved interaction conditions. It does not establish intentional refusal, inability, preference for prose, English request, rejection of structured communication, or operator goal. Recurrence strengthens the standing from a single unmatched input to a recurrent channel mismatch candidate, but recurrence is not trajectory automatically and trajectory is not baseline automatically.

Supported standings may include:

- repeated free-form material observed;
- structured options exposed;
- no structured uptake observed;
- current strategy ineffective or unsupported for this interaction;
- shared grammar insufficient or Unknown;
- evidence demand for further operator context, correction, or candidate grammar testing.

Unsupported standings include:

- operator intentionally refused enums;
- operator wants English;
- `learn English` was understood as a command;
- English is the only possible shared grammar;
- Seed is authorized to fetch or retain English material;
- Seed has a goal to learn;
- communication must continue.

### At what point shared grammar is warranted

A shared-grammar demand is warranted when all of the following are bounded and preserved:

1. A communication relation is exposed, either by console purpose, operator role, or ingress boundary.
2. The current exchange requires some shared representation for communication to remain possible.
3. Current shared grammar standing is Unknown, unsupported, or insufficient.
4. Evidence shows the existing structured strategy is not yielding uptake for this interaction, especially through recurrence.
5. Unknowns and alternative explanations are preserved: noise, testing, unrelated literals, operator error, channel mismatch, disability, translation layer absence, or implementation limitation.

The demand is symmetric unless a separate boundary assigns responsibility. It may be borne by Seed, the operator, both, an external mediator, or remain Unknown. Lawful responses include asking for bounded context, exposing alternate structured assistance, examining candidate grammars, requesting authority, using an external renderer, preserving Unknown, refusing, or stopping.

## English as candidate grammar

English may become an examinable candidate only as a candidate, not an adopted interpretation, when evidence supports compatibility. Candidate evidence may include:

- literal material such as `hello` and `learn English`;
- preserved source role `operator`;
- material appears language-like;
- candidate English grammar, dictionary, corpus, or lexical reference exists within available authority;
- the phrases are compatible with candidate English vocabulary or grammar;
- bounded candidate interpretation can be tested by operator correction or response.

The lawful standing is:

```text
operator material is compatible with candidate English grammar
```

Not:

```text
operator requested English
operator authorized acquisition
Seed should learn English
Seed should permanently adopt English
candidate interpretation is correct
```

Seed may examine English material only when a separate authority and scope boundary permits that examination. If the English material is already locally available and the examination is read-only within current scope, candidate comparison may be possible. If external material must be fetched or acquired, authority remains unestablished unless separately granted. Even successful bounded exchange is not a communication baseline; it is only evidence toward candidate applicability.

## Communication, evidence, capability, learning, and authority demands

The same reality-exposed incompatibility may expose different local demands at different boundaries without creating one universal demand artifact:

- **Communication demand**: shared exchange requires compatible representation.
- **Evidence demand**: more testimony is required to narrow meaning, grammar, or relation standing.
- **Capability demand**: a transformation must become possible, such as candidate grammar interpretation or rendering.
- **Learning demand**: retained understanding would need revision for later interaction to improve; not automatically created by communication demand.
- **Authority insufficiency**: movement such as external acquisition lacks authority; whether to call this `authority demand` is Needs clarification.

Preserved distinctions:

```text
communication demand != capability demand automatically
demand for capability != demand for learning
learning possible != learning required
learning required != Seed authorized to learn
learning authorized != external acquisition authorized
authority insufficiency != authority grant
```

## Demand satisfaction

Demand satisfaction is consumer-local. The consumer or establishment boundary that owns the required condition judges whether the response satisfies that condition. One response can satisfy one dimension and leave evidence, authority, capability, learning, or communication unresolved. A demand can become inapplicable because the governing relation ends; lawful stopping can satisfy reality by ending the relationship even though the original communication demand was not satisfied.

```text
candidate response completed != demand satisfied
gap reduced != demand resolved
goal reached != every demand resolved
communication succeeds once != baseline established
baseline established != future compatibility guaranteed
```

## Required direct answers

### What is a constitutional demand?

A constitutional demand is best recovered as a bounded necessity: a condition that must be satisfied for an already-exposed relationship, standing, or possible movement to remain available or sufficient. This is a candidate definition, not canonical adoption.

### Who or what produces a demand?

Reality produces the necessity through the relation between current standing and required condition. Seed may preserve or recognize that demand only through a bounded establishment boundary. Consumers establish requirements; comparison boundaries establish incompatibility; environments and operators may expose actual conditions.

### Is demand a Seed intention?

No. Demand is not Seed desire, preference, curiosity, goal, selection, or intent.

### Can a demand exist before Seed recognizes it?

Yes, in the reality-imposed sense. It cannot become Seed-owned standing for later movement until bounded recognition preserves source, scope, comparison warrant, Unknowns, and limits.

### Can a demand exist before a goal is adopted?

Yes. A required condition may constrain possible movement before any goal is adopted. Goal adoption is a later, separate movement.

### Does a demand open movement automatically?

No. Demand may make movement possible to consider; it does not authorize, select, require, or realize movement.

### Is demand itself movement, standing, a constraint result, or a relation?

It is not itself movement. The best recovered classification is relation-backed standing or constraint result: a bounded relation exposes necessity because current standing is incompatible with a required condition.

### Is `gap` one constitutional subject or compressed vocabulary over boundary-local incompatibilities?

Current evidence favors compressed vocabulary. `Gap` names many boundary-local incompatibilities and comparison results, not one canonical constitutional subject.

### Is a gap a measurement, a situation, a difference, or a comparison result?

It can be used as any of these historically. Constitutionally, the safest precise use is a bounded comparison result or diagnostic description whose exact producer, required condition, current condition, and consumer must be named.

### How do gap dimensions relate to a required condition?

A gap dimension identifies the family or coordinate of the required condition being compared: evidence, knowledge, capability, authority, representation, resource, timing, and so on. Dimensions may coexist without forming one universal artifact.

### How do baseline and tolerance differ?

Baseline is evidence-supported ordinary standing under bounded conditions. Tolerance is an authorized comparison boundary for a purpose. Ordinary and acceptable are distinct.

### Can a baseline be a range rather than a value?

Yes. Current operational grammar supports baseline as retained understanding of ordinary behavior, not necessarily a scalar, table, raw timing history, average, or threshold.

### Can a tolerance have lower and upper boundaries?

Yes, if the consumer's purpose establishes both boundaries. A tolerance can also be one-sided.

### Can ordinary behavior fall outside acceptable tolerance?

Yes. Common behavior can be unacceptable for a safety, policy, deadline, or resource consumer.

### Can acceptable behavior fall outside ordinary baseline?

Yes. Unusual behavior can still be acceptable if it remains within authorized tolerance for the purpose.

### How do timing measurements expose demand?

A timing measurement exposes demand only when compared against a consumer-owned timing requirement or tolerance and the comparison establishes incompatibility. Measurement alone does not establish baseline, tolerance, deviation, demand, cause, failure, or execution.

### Does repeated free-form operator input establish a communication demand?

It can, if the console boundary is a communication ingress and recurrence shows current shared representation is insufficient for bounded exchange. It does not by itself establish operator intention, English, authorization, or Seed goal.

### What does failure to use Seed's enums establish?

It establishes non-uptake observed under the interaction conditions. It may support channel-mismatch or strategy-ineffectiveness standing. It does not establish intentional refusal, preference, inability, English request, or meaning.

### At what point is a shared-grammar demand warranted?

When a communication relation is exposed, shared representation is required for that relation, current grammar standing is Unknown or insufficient, and repeated interaction shows the present structured strategy is not producing uptake while alternatives remain preserved as Unknown.

### At what point may English become a candidate grammar?

When preserved material is compatible with English-like lexical or grammatical evidence and examination is bounded as candidate compatibility only. `hello` and `learn English` may support candidate English compatibility, not English applicability or request.

### At what point may Seed examine English material?

Only when authority, scope, source, provenance or provenance Unknown, and representation limits permit examining that material. Local available material may be examined within its existing authority; external fetching requires separate authority.

### Does communication demand establish an operator goal?

No. It establishes at most a communication incompatibility. Operator purpose and goal remain Unknown unless separately testified and bounded.

### Does communication demand establish a Seed goal?

No. Seed goal adoption requires separate bounded adoption. Demand is not selection or intent.

### Does communication demand create evidence, capability, learning, or authority demands at later boundaries?

It may expose them locally. Evidence may be needed to narrow meaning; capability may be needed to interpret or render; learning may be needed for retained improvement; authority may be needed for external acquisition. These are separate boundary-local demands or insufficiencies, not one artifact.

### Who bears the demand to adapt: Seed, operator, both, an external mediator, or Unknown?

Unknown until a responsibility boundary assigns it. Any side may adapt, an external mediator may translate, or the relation may stop.

### Can lawful stopping satisfy reality without satisfying the demand?

Yes. Stopping can end the governing relation, making the demand no longer current. That satisfies reality by ending the relation, not by satisfying the original required condition.

## Required conclusion

### One recovered candidate definition of demand

Demand is an established necessity imposed by the relation between current standing and a required condition, where that condition must be satisfied for a bounded relationship, standing, or possible movement to remain available or sufficient.

### One recovered relation among requirement, gap, and demand

```text
requirement = the bounded condition used for judgment
gap = the characterized or measured incompatibility between current standing and that requirement
demand = the necessity exposed when that incompatibility must be satisfied for the governing relation or movement to remain possible
```

### One exact application to the console witness

If the console is established as operator communication ingress, and the preserved history is:

```text
free-form operator material
→ structured options exposed
→ no structured uptake observed
→ further free-form operator material
→ structured assistance repeated
→ no structured uptake observed
```

then Seed may preserve a boundary-local shared-grammar demand:

```text
some shared communication grammar is required for this operator-console communication relation to remain possible,
and current shared grammar standing is Unknown or insufficient.
```

Seed may also examine English only as a candidate grammar if authority and scope permit, because `hello` and `learn English` are compatible with English-like material. It may not conclude that the operator requested English, that English is correct, that Seed has a goal to learn English, that external acquisition is authorized, that enums were intentionally refused, or that communication must continue.
