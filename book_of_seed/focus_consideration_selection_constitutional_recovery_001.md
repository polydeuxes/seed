## Recovered focus grammar

The mature Book does not establish one universal independent constitutional act named `focus`. It establishes local grammar in which focus can appear as an effect or label inside bounded selection and attention roads.

The strongest Book-backed grammar is:

1. Consideration selection chooses an already-established subject for bounded focus.
2. A consideration selection may establish bounded selection standing or focus standing for that subject.
3. Focus standing, when established in that road, is a bounded effect of the local selection road and not reusable authorization standing.
4. Shared selection vocabulary does not collapse representation selection, consideration selection, work selection, closed-choice binding, and realization selection into one act kind.
5. A constructor or dataclass that can carry focus-like information proves constructability only, not a responsible focus-production occurrence.
6. Testimony can be consumed premise-relatively by a bounded consumer without becoming established fact or proving the source producer occurred.

The Book therefore supports focus in at least two local senses:

- **consideration-selection effect:** bounded focus standing may be established by a consideration selection over an already-established subject;
- **attention/local presentation vocabulary:** attention documents actual activation of focus in exploratory corpus material, but that vocabulary is not a reconciled mature ontology object and must not be promoted without implementation evidence.

The Book does not independently establish focus as durable, universal, pre-selection constitutional standing.

## Recovered consideration grammar

Consideration is not priority, route, primary blocker, authorization, execution, inquiry opening, or next action. In the mature Book, consideration selection chooses an already-established subject for bounded focus. That selection may create selected-subject or focus standing for one local consumer purpose, but it does not authorize consequential movement.

Prior focus-formation recovery is consistent with this grammar but uses stronger local implementation language: it reports an advancement-need road in which explicit focus testimony enters a consideration selector. That report does not override the mature Book grammar; it remains an implementation-road witness.

## Recovered selection grammar

Selection is a bounded act, not merely a record. A lawful selection act consumes a bounded candidate set plus selection evidence, exact comparison, or policy; validates the identity and applicability required by that owner; and chooses zero or more candidates or preserves lawful non-selection.

The Book makes these distinctions explicit:

- selection artifact is not the act of selection;
- candidate eligibility is not selection;
- a unique candidate is not a selected candidate;
- read-only selection can still be a constitutional occurrence;
- selection standing is not authorization standing;
- representation selection is not consideration selection automatically.

For this question, the critical selection grammar is that consideration selection may be the act that changes a subject's standing into selected-for-consideration or bounded-focus standing.

## Admission, applicability, sufficiency, and warrant grammar

The Book supports these boundaries:

- **Applicability or precondition evidence is not the governed act.** A constraint may be checked and consumed without performing the act it governs.
- **Testimony is not established fact.** A consumer may use testimony as premise-relative input, but copied lineage or coherence checks do not establish fact standing or prove producer occurrence.
- **Construction is not production authority.** A public constructor, exported type, or directly built artifact does not prove a lawful producer boundary occurred.
- **Admission is local participation, not ratification.** The nearby corpus characterizes admission as allowing bounded participation in a next posture without turning a claim into truth or transferable authority.
- **Sufficiency is not the whole admission family.** Sufficiency may support admission or stopping, but it is not identical to admission, warrant, or authorization.

## Realization-invariant constitutional topology

The strongest realization-invariant road supported by the Book is:

```text
bounded purpose / candidate set
→ testimony, selection evidence, exact comparison, or policy
→ responsible selection boundary validates owner-local identity and applicability requirements
→ consideration selection act chooses or preserves lawful non-selection
→ selected-for-consideration standing and, where the local road says so, bounded focus standing
→ downstream consumer may rely only on the bounded selected-subject/focus assertion it revalidates
```

For advancement needs, the road is narrower:

```text
advancement-need projections
→ visible advancement-need reference set with selectable metadata
→ caller-supplied focus-named testimony / selection evidence
→ consideration selector identity and selectability boundary
→ one selected advancement-need reference for consideration, or lawful non-selection
→ inquiry-frontier testimony/frontier consumers revalidate selected inquiry-need coherence
```

This topology preserves two negative authorities:

1. Upstream testimony is not established focus standing merely because it is named focus evidence.
2. Downstream selected-for-consideration standing is not priority, blocker, route, inquiry opening, authorization, execution, recording, event-ledger write, or mutation.

## Fidelity cross-examination

### Does focus exist before the consideration-selection act?

As established constitutional standing, not generally. The Book supports testimony or selection evidence before the selection act. It does not establish durable upstream focus standing as a universal prerequisite. In the current advancement road, incoming focus-named artifacts exist before selection, but they are best classified as caller-supplied testimony/selection evidence unless a separate producer occurrence is proven.

### What standing, if any, must be established upstream?

The upstream subject must have candidate standing appropriate to the selector: for advancement needs, visible references exist and established native records are marked selectable. The selector also requires exact identity and lineage testimony. That is weaker than established focus standing.

### What may be supplied merely as testimony or selection evidence?

A focus-named artifact may supply the asserted reference id, need set, selected goal, horizon, family, native projection, native lineage, provenance, unknowns, conflicts, and non-exact states. Supplying those fields does not prove the source producer occurred or that focus standing already exists.

### What does the consideration-selection act itself establish?

A successful act establishes selected-for-consideration standing over one exact selectable reference. Under the mature Book's consideration grammar, it may establish bounded focus standing for that subject in the local road. It does not establish priority, primary blocker, route, inquiry opening, next action, authorization, execution, recording, event-ledger write, mutation, or fact truth.

### Does selected for consideration constitute focus standing?

Within the mature consideration-selection grammar, yes, where the local road uses consideration selection to choose an already-established subject for bounded focus. More cautiously, selected-for-consideration is the stable constitutional term, and `focus standing` is lawful only as its bounded local effect, not as a durable universal property.

### Can focus exist without selection?

Unknown as mature constitutional standing. Exploratory attention grammar says actual focus or activation can precede or diverge from formal selection, but it also warns that attention triggers are not reconciled ontology objects. Therefore non-selection focus may exist as realization-local or presentation/attention vocabulary, not as Book-established durable standing.

### Can selection for consideration occur without prior focus standing?

Yes, in the Book topology. Selection can consume selection evidence, exact comparison, or policy and thereby establish selected-for-consideration/bounded-focus standing. In the current advancement implementation, however, the selector requires focus-named exact testimony; that requirement is a local implementation road, not proof of an upstream constitutional focus-standing producer.

### Is focus purpose-relative rather than durable?

Supported. The Book frames consideration selection as local and bounded by one consumer purpose. Focus standing, where it exists, is not reusable authorization standing or universal state.

### What distinguishes lawful focus from priority or blocker selection?

Lawful focus is bounded selected-subject standing for consideration. Priority or blocker selection would require a separate owner, candidate set, policy/warrant, and artifact that actually asserts priority or blocker standing. The advancement selector explicitly refuses to do that.

## Critical implementation question

The current advancement-need implementation is faithful within its bounded purpose if read as:

```text
visible/selectable references
→ caller-supplied focus-named testimony / selection evidence
→ responsible local selection/admission boundary
→ selected-for-consideration standing
```

It becomes unfaithful if read as:

```text
NeedFocusEvidence exists
→ focus standing already established upstream
→ selector merely validates an existing constitutional focus
```

The implementation avoids several collapses:

- It does not treat visibility as selectability.
- It does not treat uniqueness or sufficiency labels as selection.
- It does not treat selected-for-consideration as priority, blocker, route, inquiry opening, next action, authorization, execution, recording, event-ledger write, or mutation.
- It preserves no-focus, missing identity, ambiguity, conflict, mismatch, absent reference, duplicate lineage, and non-selectable states.

The implementation still has a bounded gap if the intended constitutional claim is stronger than current code:

- No Seed-local producer occurrence was recovered that forms `NeedFocusEvidence` or established upstream focus standing for advancement needs.
- No separate ingress/admission owner was recovered before the selector for externally supplied focus-named testimony.

That gap does not warrant implementing a focus producer, ingress, priority engine, planner, LLM selector, or universal focus abstraction in this pass. It only warrants preserving the distinction in this report.

## Identity validation versus admission

The current selector performs identity and lineage validation as part of its own selection boundary. That local boundary can admit exact focus-named testimony for the purpose of selecting one visible selectable reference for consideration.

It would be a confusion to treat identity validation as:

- proof that an upstream focus producer occurred;
- proof that the testimony content became established fact;
- full admission to inquiry movement;
- sufficiency for answer/result;
- warrant for priority, route, authorization, or execution.

No implementation evidence inspected here shows that the current selector makes those stronger claims.

## Negative authority

This recovery does not authorize:

- implementation of a focus producer;
- implementation of a focus ingress;
- changes to the consideration selector;
- a priority engine;
- ranking advancement needs;
- a universal focus abstraction;
- a planner or autonomous loop;
- an LLM selector;
- Book changes merely to match current implementation.

It also denies these constitutional promotions:

- focus evidence into focus standing without a responsible boundary;
- selection evidence into selection standing without selection act;
- candidate standing into selected standing;
- identity validation into applicability, admission, sufficiency, warrant, fact truth, authorization, or execution;
- consideration into priority, primary blocker, route, next action, inquiry opening, or authority.

## Repository-evidenced finding

The repository-evidenced finding is:

> Focus is testimony or selection evidence consumed by consideration selection, and consideration selection is the local act that establishes selected-for-consideration standing; within that bounded road, selected-for-consideration may constitute focus standing. Focus is not proven as independent standing established before consideration selection. The current advancement implementation is faithful within its bounded purpose when read this way, but it would collapse distinct constitutional roles if `NeedFocusEvidence` were treated as proof of established upstream focus standing.
