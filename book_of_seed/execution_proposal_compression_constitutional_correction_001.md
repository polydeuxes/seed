## Warrant standing

Warrant remains a general bounded support relation for a claim, standing, reliance, mechanism fitness, selection, or other local conclusion. It is not a reusable blanket permission and does not authorize performance by identity.

## Mechanism-fitness standing

An operational realization warrant may carry only bounded mechanism-fitness support: that a candidate or selected mechanism fits a declared operational purpose under stated evidence, conditions, scope, capability standing, constraints, provenance, temporal limits, negative authority, and Unknowns. It does not establish selection, authorization, approval, request formation, emission, invocation, performance, result testimony, recording, or verified external effect.

## Constraint and readiness decomposition

Constraint findings and precondition testimony preserve only the examined condition. Readiness was not preserved as a scalar permission compressing constraint satisfaction, capability standing, reachability, resource sufficiency, authority, approval, representation fitness, recipient availability, and temporal applicability. Passing every known check does not prove complete authority or future performance.

## Request representation boundary

A request-shaped representation may preserve requested subject or act, purpose, target, scope, inputs or arguments, source standing, selection basis where applicable, authority references, constraints, provenance, limits, negative authority, and Unknowns. Request shape is not constitutional request standing by identity. Request formation is not authorization and is not emission.

## Authorization boundary

Authorization standing requires a separately warranted authority boundary preserving authority subject, granting source, recipient or responsible boundary, authorized act or movement, scope, purpose, temporal standing, constraints, evidence, occurrence, negative authority, and Unknowns. Selection, approval testimony, warrant, capability, readiness, constraint result, request representation, emission, invocation, performance report, or result record may contribute evidence only within its own standing and does not grant authority by identity.

## Invocation and performance occurrence boundary

A contemplated act, requested act, request representation, emission, invocation, performance occurrence, refusal, non-occurrence, result testimony, and recording are separate. Emission does not establish invocation. Invocation does not establish completed performance. Performance occurrence requires bounded evidence from the responsible performance boundary; result recording requires separate preservation.
