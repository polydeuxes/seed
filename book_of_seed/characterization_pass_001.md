## Roles characterized

| Role | Consumes | Produces or preserves; assertion | Required warrant and standing effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| act | a bounded subject, applicable evidence/conditions, and act-specific warrant | a result asserting that selection, admission, establishment, recording, or performance occurred | the responsible boundary must validate identity, applicability, and authority; it may change standing, while its artifact alone does not |
| constraint | a proposed act or relevant context | permission, prohibition, requirement, or condition governing possible acts | applicable policy and current bound evidence; does not itself perform the act and can govern several acts |
| testimony | attributed source material or finding | a claim available for possible reliance, with attribution and provenance | source identity and faithful capture; does not by itself establish cluster truth |
| artifact | output/assertion plus identity, provenance, uncertainty, and boundary fields | a durable representation of another responsibility's assertion | standing comes from its lawful producer, not shape; direct construction proves constructibility only |
| constructor | values or representations | a mechanically built representation | input/type invariants prove construction only; occurrence or standing needs a separate authorized boundary |
| lens | existing material and a bounded projection/composition request | what those sources expose under the declared transform | faithful source-bound transformation; read-only use does not change standing |
| road | a producer assertion accepted by a consumer for a declared purpose | a warranted producer-to-consumer relationship, not necessarily a new artifact | compatible identity, provenance, invariants, applicability, and consumer acceptance; adjacency and constructibility are insufficient |

Representative evidence includes exact focus and lineage checks in `select_advancement_need_for_consideration`; admitted-ingress validation in `establish_bounded_operator_goal_from_admitted_interpretation`; inspect-only preconditions and policy evaluation before `ToolExecutor.execute`; read-only constitutional view projection, selection, and composition; and observation/evidence/fact events in `ObservationIngestor`.

## Nearest-neighbor distinctions

- Act versus artifact: `ToolExecutor.execute` can reach a tool-call event boundary; `ExecutionProposal` explicitly cannot execute itself.
- Selection act versus selection artifact: the selector checks exact visible identity and focus evidence; direct `AdvancementNeedConsiderationSelection(...)` construction runs none of those checks.
- Constraint versus act: `PreconditionEvaluator.report` reports readiness without invoking tools, while the executor performs only after validation and policy evaluation.
- Testimony versus fact: an `Observation` preserves attributed material; ingestion separately emits evidence and conditionally promotes a fact.
- Constructor versus establishment: the establishment function validates admitted ingress and preserves lineage; dataclass construction can merely place `"established"` in a field.
- Lens versus road: constitutional view selection/composition exposes registered material read-only; its call order does not establish a universal movement path.
- Road versus adjacency: a road requires a consumer's declared acceptance and validation of the producer's assertion, not an import, neighboring call, or compatible type.
