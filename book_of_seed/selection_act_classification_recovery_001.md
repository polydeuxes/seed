# Selection Act Classification Recovery 001

## Scope

This is a bounded selection-act classification recovery using PR 1829's selection-warrant observability survey as reconnaissance testimony. It does not continue into producer/consumer cross-examination, does not survey observability, does not identify implementation frontiers, does not discuss where to pour concrete, and does not implement anything.

Mechanical names containing `select`, `selection`, `candidate`, `focus`, `choice`, or `policy` are treated as testimony only. The controlling method is:

```text
constitutional grammar
→ expectation
→ witness
→ faithful classification / unfaithful crossing / Unknown
```

## Book grammar consumed

The Book grammar consumed for this pass is bounded and comparative:

```text
A selection act:

- consumes a bounded candidate set;
- consumes selection evidence, exact comparison, or applicable policy;
- validates the identity and applicability required by its local owner;
- chooses zero or more candidates or preserves lawful non-selection;
- produces bounded selection standing for a declared purpose;
- preserves enough basis, uncertainty, conflict, refusal,
  or non-selection information for its intended consumer;
- does not automatically authorize, execute, open inquiry,
  establish downstream reliance, or create broader movement.
```

The Book also preserves `selection artifact != act of selection`, `ranked candidate != selected candidate`, `candidate eligibility != selection`, `unique candidate != selected candidate`, `selection standing != authorization standing`, and `read-only selection != no constitutional occurrence`. Admission is separate from applicability and consumption: applicable material does not thereby become admitted, and a consumer-local admission boundary must establish admitted, unadmitted, Unknown, or conflicting admission standing before responsible consumer use.

For this recovery, the clearer warrant relation is:

```text
selection evidence may warrant the assertion
that a lawful selection occurred

the selection act may then produce
bounded selection standing
```

The warrant is not itself the produced selection standing.

## 1829 claims re-examined

PR 1829's map was useful reconnaissance, but it sometimes populated a common coordinate table in ways that made unlike acts look too uniform. This pass re-examines especially:

- whether an `admission boundary` was over-ascribed from nearby identity validation, lineage validation, registered-name restriction, handoff coherence, or candidate eligibility;
- whether `warrant only as selection-standing` blurred evidence warranting a selection-occurrence assertion with the bounded selection standing produced by the selection act;
- whether audit payloads with selected and non-selected material were mistaken for selector occurrence;
- whether recommendation, ranking, binding, eligibility, or representation matching were compressed into selection.

## Per-neighborhood recovery

### 1. Selection Path Audit

**Claimed act.** The owner answers: what implemented evidence explains the requested selection target? It consumes state-derived pressure-audit and operational-story outputs, then target text such as current focus or pressure category. It produces an audit artifact containing selected value, candidates, factors, non-selected material, evidence, outcome, unknowns, and read-only boundary metadata. It denies arbitrary selector authority, fact recording, event-ledger writes, cluster mutation, execution, and broader downstream reliance.

**Actual selection test.** The module docstring and builder say this is read-only selection trace visibility and explains implemented selection evidence without changing selection behavior. Its selected output for focus/pressure targets is reconstructed from `build_pressure_audit` and `build_operational_story`, while unsupported targets return `selected="unknown"`. The responsible boundary is therefore not proven to perform the originating selection act; it reconstructs, projects, and explains selection evidence for implemented targets.

**Alternative tested.** Alternative reading: it is a selector because it emits `selected` plus non-selected candidates. Rejected. Those fields are audit payloads, and the owner explicitly explains existing implementation behavior rather than establishing original selection standing. Alternative reading: it remains fully Unknown. Rejected for implemented audit targets, because the implementation does faithfully reconstruct and explain bounded selection evidence; Unknown is preserved only for unsupported targets and producer occurrence beyond the audit.

**Classification.** `audit / explanation` and `representation / projection`; partly `Unknown` only for producer occurrence and unsupported targets.

### 2. Constitutional View Selection

**Claimed act.** The owner answers: which registered constitutional read-model view names match exact caller-declared selection keys for one bounded question projection? It consumes bounded-question projection and registered capability projections, validates exact keys and registered view names, and produces selected registered view names plus unsupported-key/no-match uncertainty for composition request formation. It denies semantic matching, evidence discovery, orchestration, mutation, ledger writes, authorization, and downstream reliance beyond the representation purpose.

**Actual selection test.** The implementation says selection consumes deterministic question and capability projections, performs exact comparison, preserves unsupported uncertainty, and produces a `SelectedConstitutionalViews` artifact. Projection helpers explicitly do not themselves perform selection. The responsible selection boundary chooses zero or more registered view names from a bounded registered-view candidate set by exact comparison and preserves lawful non-selection/uncertainty.

**Alternative tested.** Alternative reading: this is only deterministic representation matching. Partly accepted as secondary relation, but not sufficient to displace selection because the owner does choose bounded registered view names and produces a selection artifact for a declared composition purpose. Alternative reading: registered-name restriction is admission. Rejected; registered-name restriction and exact-key validity are identity/applicability constraints, not a consumer-local admission result.

**Classification.** `selection act`; secondary `representation / projection` and exact deterministic matching.

## Misleading mechanical vocabulary

- `Selection Path Audit` contains selected/non-selected fields, but its responsible act is audit/explanation of already implemented behavior.
- `Closed-Choice Selection Binding` contains selection vocabulary, but the recovered primary act is local token binding.
- `Question-Family Selection` vocabulary compresses exact lookup, bounded-work eligibility, dispatch-surface preparation, presentation handoff, and dispatch request construction; only the dispatch-surface choice after eligibility is a witnessed local selection act.
- `Capability Recommendation` and recommendation ranking can look selection-shaped because scores order candidates, but ranked recommendation does not produce selected-provider standing.
- `Operation Selection` is selection-shaped but very narrow: it resolves an already-named operation and must not be read as provider choice or execution authority.

## Places where admission was over-ascribed

The following should not be classified as `admission` on the current witness:

- Constitutional View Selection registered-name restriction: identity/applicability for representation selection, not admitted participation standing.
- Advancement-Need Consideration identity and lineage validation: selector-local validation, not admission.
- Contextual Interpretation Selection refusal of unknown/unwarranted candidate references: selector-local eligibility/standing validation, not admission.
- Closed-Choice token/choice-set coherence: binding validation, not goal-consumer admission.
- Examination-Work handoff, policy, and frontier coherence: selector-local identity and eligibility validation, not admission.
- Operational-Realization Selection handoff and partition coherence: selector-local validation, not admission.
- Operational-Realization Warrant selected-candidate/handoff coherence: warrant validation, not admission.
- QuestionFamily exact inventory lookup and registered/handled restriction: lookup/eligibility unless a consumer-local admitted/unadmitted/Unknown/conflicting participation standing is produced.
- Operation registered-name lookup: narrow operation resolution/validation, not admission into execution.

## Places where warrant and produced standing were blurred

- Constitutional View Selection: exact-key evidence may warrant the assertion that a lawful representation selection occurred; the produced artifact is bounded selected-view standing for composition, not a reusable warrant.
- Advancement-Need Consideration: focus evidence may warrant the assertion that consideration selection occurred; produced standing is selected-for-consideration, not priority or movement warrant.
- Contextual Interpretation: candidate warrant-set production is separate from meaning selection. Candidate warrants may be consumed by selection evidence, but the warrant set is not selected-meaning standing.
- Operational Realization: operational-realization selection produces selected-candidate standing and a future warrant handoff; the separate warrant evaluates reliance. Selection standing is not mechanism-fitness warrant.
- Selection Path Audit: audit evidence may support a claim that an implemented selection behavior occurred, but audit output is not the producer act and not general selection standing.
