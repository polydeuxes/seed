## Central Finding

Current repository evidence supports more than one meaning of “family.” The two explicitly implementation-backed family systems examined here are not peers in one flat constitutional list:

1. `NeedFamily` in goal-advancement need-set code classifies the nature of a stage-owned advancement need projection for one bounded goal/horizon.
2. `QuestionFamily` in question-surface inventory code identifies an inventory-backed public answerability identity and its answering surface or non-dispatchable visibility status.

The candidate topology is therefore **qualified**:

```text
constitutional kind
        ↓
family
        ↓
exact bounded subject
        ↓
eight-dimensional characterization
        ↓
local responsibilities and localities
        ↓
acts, constraints, and relations
```

It is useful as a nesting warning, not as a universal pipeline. Repository evidence supports that families sit below or inside a constitutional kind when that kind has variants. It also supports that exact bounded subjects need identity, scope, provenance, responsibility, standing, authority, content, and occurrence/preservation evidence. It does **not** support requiring every constitutional subject to pass sequentially through every layer or treating every use of the word family as the same layer.

## Why “Family” Is Ambiguous

The repository uses family language for several different organizing purposes:

- subject variants, such as the four `NeedFamily` members;
- implementation-backed public answerability identities, such as `QuestionFamily` rows;
- coordinate variants inside dimensions, such as standing or responsibility families;
- responsibility jurisdictions, such as family ownership of a blocker;
- methodological or shape forms, such as question shape and survey warrant family language;
- implementation neighborhoods, such as socket address family or implementation family;
- historical or contamination vocabulary preserved in reports about deleted model, decision, planning, routing, handoff, execution, operational-realization, parser, or observer districts.

Because these uses organize different things, they cannot be added into one constitutional family count.

## Structural Levels

### Constitutional Kind

A constitutional kind is the larger kind of thing under consideration: Demand/need, Question, evidence, act, relation, authority, projection, event, standing, responsibility, and similar Book-level or implementation-backed kinds. The dimensionality survey treats subject/identity, content, standing, provenance, responsibility, authority, scope/locality, and occurrence/preservation as recurrent macro-dimensional families, but also warns that acts, constraints, movement, roads, lenses, projections, composition, standings, values, relations, and fields must be classified before being called dimensions.

**Edge to family:** supported with qualification. A constitutional kind may have a family system where implementation or Book evidence creates variants, but not every kind has one registry and not every family word marks a subject-family registry.

### Family

A family is a typed variant system under a bounded organizing purpose. For `NeedFamily`, the purpose is classifying stage-owned need projections inside a goal-advancement need set. For `QuestionFamily`, the purpose is exact inventory admission plus answerability/dispatch-surface responsibility.

**Edge to exact bounded subject:** supported with qualification. A family label alone is not the subject. It must be bound to the local identities and evidence needed by that kind. `NeedFamily="inquiry"` is not an exact Inquiry Demand until tied to a candidate resolution, goal establishment, horizon, evidence snapshot, bounded uncertainty component, owning stage, and standing. `QuestionFamily="knowledge reachability"` is not an individual Question until an exact bounded question occurrence/formulation exists.

### Exact Bounded Subject

An exact bounded subject is the specific thing being preserved or examined, not the family label. For current Demand evidence, recurring bindings include candidate resolution, goal establishment, horizon, evidence refs, component refs, scope or transformation refs where applicable, owning stage/responsibility, and standing. For current Question evidence, recurring bindings in active Book clauses include identity, provenance, scope, evidence demand, authority limits, uncertainty, and lawful stop conditions; implementation `QuestionFamily` rows add inventory identity, examples, answer responsibility, authority boundary, surface, dispatch status, and diagnostic relationship, but do not establish a constitutional question occurrence.

**Edge to eight-dimensional characterization:** supported. Exact subjects are characterized by identity, content, standing, provenance, responsibility, authority, scope/locality, and occurrence/preservation. The dimensions are not mandatory fields on one universal class.

### Eight-Dimensional Characterization

The current eight macro-dimensional families are:

1. subject / identity;
2. assertion / content;
3. standing;
4. source / provenance;
5. responsibility;
6. authority / warrant;
7. scope / locality;
8. occurrence / preservation.

They characterize exact subjects and relations. They do not classify all families. Standing is explicitly cross-cutting, occurrence/preservation may later split, and locality contains many bounded coordinate systems.

### Localities

Localities are local coordinates under bounded responsibility. They unfold inside or across dimensions. For example, provenance can open into reference presence, referenced material existence, source identity, source context, attribution, represented lineage, lineage applicability, internal coherence, independent verification, verified causation, producer occurrence, and support independence. These are not twelve new subject families; they are locality-sensitive coordinates for examining provenance in a bounded responsibility.

### Acts, Constraints, and Relations

Acts, constraints, and relations are structurally distinct from subject families:

- Acts such as preserve, examine, compare, select, admit, establish, project, record, and emit are responsible occurrences or operations over subjects and coordinates when locally warranted.
- Constraints such as applicability, sufficiency, warrant, authority, admissibility, fidelity, and scope govern permission, prohibition, requirement, or condition.
- Relations such as supports, applies to, produced by, consumed by, selected from, contradicts, narrows, and revises connect bounded subjects or standings.

The repository evidence supports this distinction, not a canonical exhaustive vocabulary for all listed examples.

## Demand-Family Recovery

### Inventory

Current implementation-backed `NeedFamily` members are exactly four:

```text
clarification
inquiry
authority
operational_realization
```

The implementation symbol remains `NeedFamily`. Constitutionally, these are better described in report prose as Demand-family variants or Demand types because each classifies why something is required for bounded goal advancement:

- Clarification Demand: operator-meaning uncertainty requires clarification.
- Inquiry Demand: repository/world uncertainty requires examination.
- Authority Demand: authority requirement plus insufficient/unavailable authority standing requires authority.
- Realization Demand: required operational transformation lacks available complete realization coverage for the horizon, with the blocker owned by operational realization.

This report does not rename code.

### What kind of thing is being classified?

`NeedFamily` classifies stage-owned need projections assembled for one advancement horizon. The need set preserves supplied family projections without reinterpretation and explicitly says coexisting needs are an unordered set, not a priority order, route, or next action.

### What makes these four siblings?

They are siblings because `GoalAdvancementNeedSet` enumerates them in one `FAMILIES` tuple and assembles records for each with the same dispositions: supplied, absent, or excluded. They share the same bounded horizon identity checks against candidate resolution, goal establishment, and horizon.

They differ by family-local evidence grammar:

- Clarification consumes explicit component-bounded operator-meaning uncertainty testimony.
- Inquiry consumes explicit repository/world uncertainty testimony.
- Authority joins explicit authority requirement testimony with authority standing testimony.
- Operational realization joins explicit operational-realization requirement testimony with realization standing testimony.

### What bindings make one exact Demand bounded?

Recurring implementation bindings include:

- `candidate_resolution_id`;
- `goal_establishment_id`;
- `horizon_id`;
- `evidence_refs` from the horizon evidence snapshot;
- family-local bounded component refs;
- `owning_stage` or family ownership;
- family-local content such as uncertainty, required authority, required transformation, applicable scope, and materiality;
- standing buckets such as established, unsupported, unknown, conflicting, excluded, or unclassified.

Authority and operational realization add paired requirement/standing testimony and join conditions. Clarification and inquiry are currently single-testimony projections.

### Can multiple Demand families coexist?

Supported. The need set accepts a projection per family, creates a record for each member, and states that coexisting needs are an unordered set. No implementation evidence inspected here limits the set to one supplied family.

### Does the current need set order or prioritize them?

No. The dataclass exposes `orders_needs=False` and `prioritizes_needs=False`; the boundary notes state the set is not a priority order, route, or next action. Sorting in JSON output is deterministic presentation, not constitutional priority.

### Does Demand standing open movement?

No, not by itself. The projections and need set carry explicit false booleans for opening inquiry, requesting authority, selecting realization, authorizing work, executing, recording, event-ledger writing, and mutation. Later selection/frontier artifacts may consume need references under separate constraints, but need standing alone does not open movement.

### Does a Demand formulate a Question?

Not automatically. Clarification and inquiry need projections explicitly refuse question wording selection or inquiry opening. The active Book states that operator material or inquiry pressure is not itself an internal constitutional question; Seed must form a bounded question through a Seed-owned act preserving identity, source, scope, evidence demand, authority limits, uncertainty, and lawful stop.

### Does absence of a family projection establish absence of that Demand?

No. The need set distinguishes supplied, absent, and explicitly excluded need families. The horizon also states that included need family means potentially relevant to preserve, not that a need exists. Therefore an absent projection records absence of the supplied projection at assembly, not absence of the constitutional Demand.

## QuestionFamily Recovery

### Inventory and count

Current implementation-backed `QuestionFamily` inventory has **18** rows. Their bounded statuses are:

- **11 dispatchable now / `eligible_now`:** operational pressure; current operational explanation; knowledge reachability; capability pressure; ownership ambiguity; observation domain coverage; observation permission state; authority-constrained container ownership; authority-constrained service ownership; listener endpoint reachability; projection shape visibility.
- **3 dispatchable with parameters / `eligible_with_parameters`:** derivation explanation; selection explanation; constitutional pipeline.
- **2 diagnostic-only:** surface inventory; surface shape validation.
- **2 visible but not dispatchable / `not_dispatchable`:** source definition/import lookup; inquiry orientation.
- **Unknown family:** not an inventory member; `build_question_family_definition()` returns an unknown-status explanation, while exact lookup raises `ValueError`.

Thus the candidate summary is correct if “14 bounded-ask dispatch mappings” means 11 immediately eligible plus 3 parameterized dispatch families. It is more precise to say **14 dispatchable total: 11 now, 3 with parameters; 2 diagnostic-only; 2 visible non-dispatchable; 18 inventory members**.

### What does QuestionFamily identity classify?

It classifies an exact inventory-backed public answerability identity and answering surface relationship. Each row stores the family label, examples, surface, CLI flag, answer responsibility, authority boundary, notes, bounded status, dispatch surface, required args, diagnostic relationship, and implementation reason.

### Is identity exact and inventory-backed?

Yes for the exact lookup path. `_lookup_exact_question_family()` iterates current inventory rows and admits only exact row text; otherwise it raises `ValueError`. The preparation handoff states that it admits only exact inventory-backed question family and does not classify free text.

### Does it classify free text?

No. The code explicitly refuses free-text classification: it looks up exact inventory row text and does not select dispatch, validate args, render, mutate state, or perform semantic routing at the admission boundary.

### Does it establish an individual Question?

No. Active Book evidence separates question-shaped external material, question-family surfaces, and internal bounded question formation. Implementation `QuestionFamily` rows provide examples and answerability identities, not a preserved occurrence of a bounded question.

### Does a registered family prove a current Demand exists?

No. A registered `QuestionFamily` row may exist without any current `NeedFamily` projection or established Demand. The inventory is static and read-only.

### Can an exact bounded Question exist without a registered QuestionFamily?

Supported by Book evidence and qualified by implementation scope. The active Book says bounded questions are formed by Seed with identity, provenance, scope, evidence demand, authority limits, uncertainty, and stop conditions; it also says implementation question family is not a constitutional question taxonomy. Current bounded-ask implementation may refuse unknown family labels, but that refusal is about public dispatch eligibility, not the constitutional possibility of a bounded question.

### Can a QuestionFamily exist without any current Question occurrence?

Yes. The inventory is deterministic static rows. It does not record question occurrences or current Demand standing.

## Demand, Question, and QuestionFamily

Recovered distinction:

```text
Demand:
    why clarification, inquiry, authority, or transformation is required
    for one bounded advancement horizon.

Question:
    the exact bounded uncertainty being asked, formed by Seed under
    identity, provenance, scope, evidence demand, authority, uncertainty,
    and lawful stop conditions.

QuestionFamily:
    implementation-backed answerability identity and existing answering
    surface relationship that may address some bounded questions.
```

Candidate asymmetries:

| Statement | Classification | Evidence and qualification |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Demand can exist without a Question. | supported | Need projections establish or preserve need standing while explicitly not selecting question wording, opening inquiry, or authorizing movement. |
| Question can exist without a registered QuestionFamily. | supported with implementation qualification | Active Book rejects `QuestionFamily` as Seed's constitutional question taxonomy. Bounded ask may reject unknown family labels for dispatch; that is not a constitutional impossibility proof. |
| QuestionFamily can exist without a current Demand. | supported | The inventory is static and read-only; rows are not assembled from current demand projections. |
| Inquiry Demand does not automatically formulate a Question. | supported | Inquiry projection establishes inquiry need standing from repository/world uncertainty testimony and explicitly does not open inquiry or select question wording. |
| Question formulation does not automatically register or select a QuestionFamily. | supported | Book question formation is Seed-owned; implementation family lookup is a separate exact inventory admission/eligibility path. |
| QuestionFamily registration does not establish that an individual Question was asked. | supported | Inventory rows include example questions and answering surfaces but no occurrence record for a current individual question. |

## Locality Relationship

The supported formulation is:

```text
Family says which variant of subject is present.
Dimensions characterize that exact subject.
Localities expose how dimensions vary under a bounded responsibility.
```

Qualified: some family systems are not subject variants. `QuestionFamily` is an answerability identity rather than a constitutional subject-family registry. Some locality coordinates bridge dimensions rather than nesting neatly under one parent.

The dimensionality survey's approximately sixty supported localities are not sixty constitutional subject families and not sixty required modules. They are bounded coordinate distinctions that recur across Python, SQLite, Book clauses, diagnostics, evidence, projection, inquiry, selection, and recording contexts. For example, provenance localities distinguish reference presence, referenced material existence, source attribution, source context, lineage representation, lineage applicability, internal coherence, independent verification, and producer occurrence. Those distinctions prevent false inferences such as “reference exists, therefore source material exists,” or “lineage is represented, therefore producer occurrence is verified.”

### Gap families

The current active Book testimony names outcome, evidence, knowledge, capability, reachability, dependency, authority, representation, and resource/budget gaps. It expressly accepts those distinctions as navigational and not as a closed schema. Therefore they are not currently a canonical required schema. They are locally useful vocabulary when evidence and reference conditions distinguish the gap; some statuses remain unresolved for future implementation or learning.

## Family Topology

Compact supported topology:

```text
constitutional kind
  ├─ may have subject-family variants
  │    └─ family label under one organizing purpose
  │         └─ exact bounded subject
  │              ├─ identity
  │              ├─ content
  │              ├─ standing
  │              ├─ provenance
  │              ├─ responsibility
  │              ├─ authority
  │              ├─ scope/locality
  │              └─ occurrence/preservation
  │                   └─ localities under responsible consumer or producer boundaries
  └─ may instead have answerability, coordinate, method, relation,
     transition, implementation, or historical family vocabulary

acts operate on or preserve bounded subjects and coordinates
constraints govern permission, sufficiency, warrant, scope, and admissibility
relations connect subjects, standings, evidence, producers, and consumers
responsible consumers decide local uptake under preserved warrant and limits
```

Demand / Question / QuestionFamily topology:

```text
Demand / NeedFamily system
  constitutional concern: why bounded advancement cannot lawfully proceed as-is
  family variants: clarification, inquiry, authority, operational_realization
  exact subject: family-local need item bound to goal, horizon, evidence,
                 component/transformation/scope, owner, and standing
  effect: read-only standing/projection; no automatic question, movement,
          authority, execution, recording, event-ledger write, or mutation

Question
  constitutional concern: exact bounded uncertainty being asked
  required Book bindings: identity, provenance, scope, evidence demand,
                          authority limits, uncertainty, lawful stop
  relation to Demand: may be occasioned by demand/pressure but is not automatic

QuestionFamily
  implementation concern: exact public answerability identity and answering surface
  inventory bindings: row label, examples, surface, CLI flag, responsibility,
                      authority boundary, bounded status, dispatch surface,
                      required args, diagnostic relationship
  relation to Question: may address a bounded question; does not create one
  relation to Demand: may answer a question connected to demand; does not prove demand
```

## Unsupported Flat Interpretations

Unsupported interpretations include:

- all family vocabulary belongs to one flat list;
- `NeedFamily` and `QuestionFamily` are peer ontological subtypes;
- `QuestionFamily` is Seed's constitutional question taxonomy;
- a family label is an exact bounded subject;
- demand standing automatically opens inquiry, selects action, formulates a question, or registers/selects a question family;
- the eight dimensions classify family registries rather than characterize exact subjects and relations;
- localities are additional subject families or required modules;
- every supplied act, constraint, or relation term is canonical just because a report uses it;
- deleted architecture vocabulary regains current standing because it used the word family.

## Historical and Contamination Findings

Repository history and existing reports show repeated contamination risk where implementation or agent/workflow neighborhoods used family language. Deleted or quarantined districts around model, decision, planning, routing, handoff, execution, operational-realization, parser, and observer vocabularies must not be rescued merely because reports preserved “family” wording. Current standing must come from active Book clauses and live implementation evidence.

The strongest contamination risks found here are:

- treating implementation `QuestionFamily` as a constitutional ontology despite active Book refusal;
- treating gap-family navigational vocabulary as a closed required schema;
- treating responsibility or standing family vocabulary as subject-family members;
- treating implementation-family vocabulary as constitutional kind evidence;
- flattening deleted architecture residue into active constitutional topology.

## Bounded Orientation

The smallest faithful larger framework currently supported is relational and bounded:

```text
constitutional kind
  → family system only when evidence shows a bounded organizing purpose
  → exact bounded subject bound by identity, content, standing, provenance,
    responsibility, authority, scope/locality, and occurrence/preservation
  → localities activated under the responsible producer/consumer boundary
  → acts, constraints, and relations operating on or between those subjects
    without becoming the same thing as subject families
```

For Demand and Question specifically:

```text
Demand families classify kinds of bounded advancement need.
Questions are exact bounded uncertainties formed under Book constraints.
QuestionFamilies identify public answering responsibilities and surfaces.
```

They may relate in a bounded case, but none automatically creates, registers, selects, or proves the others.
