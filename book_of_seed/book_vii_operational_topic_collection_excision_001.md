# Book VII Operational Topic Collection Excision 001

## Scope and negative authority

This correction is bounded to the active Book district formerly at `book_of_seed/07-operational-realization/`, directly affected active navigation, and this correction testimony. It does not modify implementation, tests, CLI behavior, events, projections, archived documents, historical reports, or `book_of_seed/internal_external_occurrence_grammar_recovery_001.md`. This record is correction testimony; it is not independent constitutional authority apart from the active clauses and navigation corrected.

## PR 1928 governing recovery

PR 1928 recovered that Seed's internal constitutional grammar governs Seed-owned acts over dimension-bearing material: attribute, preserve, record, examine, compare, select, admit, establish, rely, project, compose, emit, express, and refuse. External operational terms such as capability, mechanism, availability, reachability, invocation, performance, external effect, outcome, and causation are ordinarily subjects of testimony, propositions under examination, externally attributed occurrences, bounded findings, or Unknown. They are not automatically Seed-native constitutional kinds, stages, or districts.

The correction applies that recovery more strictly than the report-only suggestion that Book VII may retain a partially coherent subject.

## Preservation burden applied

Book VII was tested for an independent constitutional subject, a distinct responsible act or relation, standing not already governed elsewhere, and need for a dedicated canonical home. Chapter text, useful terminology, implementation artifacts, tests, formatters, diagnostics, historical importance, and recurring operational subject matter were not treated as sufficient warrant.

Book VII was not preserved merely because its chapters contained useful distinctions, implementation vocabulary, or recurring operational subject matter.

Seed's general constitutional grammar is sufficient to preserve, attribute, examine, establish, select, rely upon, project, and express bounded claims about external operations without creating a specialized operational constitution.

## Former Book VII subjects examined

## Capability and mechanism disposition

Capability propositions are ordinary claim subject matter. General testimony, evidence, examination, and claim-standing grammar govern claims that a transformation may be possible under declared conditions. Mechanism existence, availability, reachability, fit, possible transformation, and result-production propositions likewise remain ordinary bounded claims or findings with claim-appropriate evidence.

Mechanism fitness is a local bounded examination or warrant conclusion about a represented mechanism and purpose. It does not require a distinct constitutional family named `CapabilityStanding`, `MechanismFitness`, or `OperationalRealizationWarrant`.

## Execution and invocation disposition

The former execution chapter did not leave a distinct constitutional kind named execution after separating Seed-owned act occurrence, request-shaped representation, emission, invocation occurrence, external mechanism performance, result testimony, and recording. Seed-owned act occurrence belongs to general act grammar. Request formation and emission belong to representation and communication grammar. Invocation claims require responsible occurrence evidence and may be Seed-owned only when the responsible Seed act boundary is separately established.

## Performance and external-occurrence disposition

Seed-owned performance is a general act occurrence. External performance is an externally attributed occurrence or claim subject. A provider response, status object, timing sample, function return, or result object is testimony or evidence within scope, not proof of external performance, successful outcome, verified external effect, or causation by identity.

## Recording disposition

Recording is Seed-owned preservation responsibility, but the active constitutional home is the recording and knowledge-extraction grammar in Book V. The former Book VII recording distinctions were already governed there and by testimony/fact boundaries: record exists is not recorded proposition true, recording is not upstream occurrence, recording is not external-effect verification, recording is not knowledge extraction, recording is not authorization, and recording is not renewed occurrence. No missing recording clause required relocation.

## Selection and authority disposition

Selection remains governed by selection grammar. Authority remains governed by authority grammar. Selection is not availability, mechanism fitness, authority, invocation, performance, recording, or external effect. Authority is not inherited from selection, capability, mechanism fit, request formation, recording, or returned result. The active selection and authority chapters already preserve these distinctions, so Book VII did not need to survive as a cross-reference collection.

## Interaction, outcome, learning, and causation disposition

Movement-linked interaction testimony is a possible relation among preserved subjects, acts, claims, reports, and uncertainty; it is not a universal episode, experience, lifecycle, or trajectory artifact. Outcome evaluation is bounded examination relative to a declared purpose, need, gap, or capability demand. Capability revision and learning establishment are ordinary revisions in bounded standing under evidence, comparison, and establishment grammar. Causal standing is general claim standing with stronger causal evidence requirements, not operational grammar.

## Fidelity-determination disposition

Fidelity determination is constitutional grammar plus bounded expectation plus implementation witness producing a qualitative finding: faithful within scope, unfaithful boundary crossing, crossing or mixed, or Unknown within scope. It is not capability standing, mechanism fitness, operational realization, or execution.

PR 1929 overclaimed prior active ownership when it treated Book I's cross-seam consumption of limits supplied by a Fidelity finding as proof that Book I already owned Fidelity production. Consumption of a Fidelity finding does not establish responsibility for producing that finding. This correction places the general Fidelity-production boundary in Book I's external-versus-constitutional grammar chapter. Cross-seam preservation of limits supplied by a Fidelity finding remains a separate consumer responsibility. No new Fidelity Book, engine, registry, score, diagnostic, or ontology was created.
