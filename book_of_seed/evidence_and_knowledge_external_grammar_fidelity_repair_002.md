# Evidence and Knowledge External Grammar Fidelity Repair 002

## Scope

This repair began with the two explicit unresolved compressions left by PR 1808, then examined `book_of_seed/05-evidence-and-knowledge/` for places where persistence, serialization, database, artifact, diagnostic, or implementation grammar may have been carried into the constitutional result.

The examination did not inspect implementation to supply missing constitutional grammar. It treated Book 5 clauses as constitutional text and asked whether each clause preserved a bounded distinction or accidentally promoted witness nouns such as record, row, artifact, extraction, lineage, retrieval, or availability.

## Initial correction 1: external grammar does not become constitutional grammar

### Exact clause before repair

> External grammar can be identified, compared, and attributed without thereby becoming Seed's constitutional grammar. Realization languages, including programming, database, serialization, persistence, cache, and hybrid implementation grammars, are external grammar until a bounded constitutional warrant states what distinction survived and what remains limited or Unknown. `[UNRESOLVED]` The sufficient promotion warrant is not yet safely established.

### External or mechanical grammar involved

Programming, database, serialization, persistence, cache, and hybrid implementation grammar.

### Compression found

The phrase "external grammar until a bounded constitutional warrant" could be read as making warrant transform the source grammar into constitutional grammar.

### Smallest constitutional distinction recovered

Python grammar remains external grammar. SQL grammar remains external grammar. Provider grammar remains external grammar. A distinction expressed through external grammar may be constitutionally recovered and warranted. The recovered distinction receives standing. The source grammar remains attributed external grammar.

### Alternative reading tested

The clause may have meant only that a bounded constitutional distinction can be recovered from external grammar. That is faithful, but the word "until" left the source grammar's status ambiguous.

### Exact Book edit

Edited `book_of_seed/01-grammar-and-standing/external-and-constitutional-grammar.md` so realization languages "remain external grammar" and only the recovered distinction may receive standing. The unresolved portion now concerns the sufficient warrant for recovering a distinction, not a promotion of the witness grammar itself.

## Initial correction 2: uptake does not collapse distinct consumer acts

### Exact clause before repair

> Consumer uptake is a later bounded act of receipt, reliance, refusal, or use. A consumer that can deserialize or query a representation has not thereby constitutionally adopted it.

### External or mechanical grammar involved

Consumer-success, deserialization, query-result, message-passing, handoff, and convenience-of-use grammar.

### Compression found

The sentence treated receipt, reliance, refusal, and use as one later bounded act. Repository-supported distinctions preserve that these may be distinct consumer-side acts or relations.

### Smallest constitutional distinction recovered

receipt != reliance
reliance != refusal
refusal != use
deserialization != receipt
query availability != reliance

The positive meaning of uptake remains only partly resolved.

### Alternative reading tested

The sentence may have intended compact orientation: uptake does not follow merely from deserialization or query. The second sentence was already faithful. The first sentence still risked replacing several distinct consumer acts with one unified act.

### Exact Book edit

Edited `book_of_seed/realization_language_compression_orientation_001.md` to state that consumer uptake remains only partly resolved and to preserve the warranted non-identities without defining a complete positive theory of uptake.

## Book 5 candidate recurrences

### Candidate 5.1: recording as creation and knowledge extraction as process

#### Exact Book clause before repair

> Recording creates a retrievable representation of attributable events and findings within the preservation horizon supplied by the recorder. Knowledge extraction is a separate process with normalization, evidence, reconciliation, and standing rules; diagnostic recording should remain scoped to its diagnostic run unless explicitly promoted.

#### External or mechanical grammar involved

Recording, retrieval, preservation horizon, extraction-process, diagnostic recording, and establishment/promotion grammar.

#### Bounded distinction recovered

recording != fact establishment
successful extraction != knowledge establishment
record existence != asserted occurrence
diagnostic recording != cluster truth

A recording boundary may preserve attributed material. Examination or extraction from recorded material may occur under a separate constitutional responsibility, but extraction success does not itself establish knowledge.

#### Alternative interpretations tested

One faithful reading is that the clause already distinguishes recording from knowledge extraction. However, "Recording creates" sounded too automatic, and "Knowledge extraction is a separate process with normalization, evidence, reconciliation, and standing rules" could be read as an ordered evidence pipeline where extraction, reconciliation, and establishment are bundled.

#### Exact Book edit

Edited `book_of_seed/05-evidence-and-knowledge/recording-and-knowledge-extraction.md` to say a recording boundary may create a retrievable representation, and that examination or extraction from recorded material is a separate constitutional responsibility with its own evidence, reconciliation, and standing limits. Added that successful extraction does not itself establish knowledge.
