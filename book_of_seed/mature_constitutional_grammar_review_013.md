Question asked: what realization-independent constitutional distinction has become mature in repository evidence but is not yet adequately preserved by the current Book of Seed?

## Pressure observed

The immediate pressure is not that the Book lacks a reliance clause. PR 1756 added the missing consumer-side reliance boundary in Book VIII. The remaining pressure appears where that clause depends on a prior responsibility that the current Book still only implies: comparison across independently bounded testimony or findings.

Book IV currently preserves bounded relevance before movement and positive lawful inactivity. Book V preserves testimony, evidence support binding, provenance applicability, and fact-promotion boundaries. Book VIII now preserves reliance without standing expansion. These clauses together prevent many over-inferences, but they do not explicitly name the cross-examination responsibility that compares independently preserved source-local results without erasing their limits.

That absence matters because recent evidence repeatedly uses comparison, contradiction, agreement, conflict, and source-local limits as constitutional work rather than as operator story or host-language mechanics. Without a Book clause, comparison can still be compressed into local examination, fact establishment, reconciliation, warrant, or reliance.

## Evidence examined

### SQLite friction report 010

Report 010 shows that SQLite joins, counts, views, foreign keys, `COALESCE`, and `CASE` expressions can mechanically compare or select rows while silently compressing constitutional distinctions. It explicitly refuses to treat a join as support, warrant, handoff, or reliance; refuses to treat row counts as source independence or corroboration; and preserves provenance-reference disagreement rather than allowing `COALESCE` to choose a constitutional topology.

Relevant pressure recovered from 010:

- A view can expose examination-shaped standing, but view availability and query evaluation do not record examination occurrence or consumer reliance.
- Multiple rows or distinct source strings do not establish independent corroboration.
- Conflicting assertion-level and evidence-level provenance references must be preserved as conflict, not collapsed by host-language precedence.
- Positive source independence remains unresolved, but the negative boundary is mature.

This supports a comparison boundary, not a source-independence algorithm and not a SQLite-specific rule.

### Dimensionality survey 011

Report 011 classifies standing, source/provenance, responsibility, authority/warrant, scope/locality, roads/handoffs/uptake, and movement as independently varying constitutional families. It treats consumer-relative admissibility, reliance permission, truth status, verification status, Unknown reason, and forbidden inference as local standing coordinates. It also records that host languages compress these coordinates through object identity, status strings, SQL rows, joins, checks, and counts.

Relevant pressure recovered from 011:

- Standing is not a scalar; it varies by consumer, role, authority, source, and scope.
- Responsibility includes producer, examiner, recorder, and consumer boundaries.
- Roads, handoffs, and uptake relate producer, artifact, consumer, purpose, warrant, authority, standing, and scope.
- Host-language comparison mechanisms can hide source, authority, scope, and standing boundaries.

This supports the need to preserve source-local limits before comparison is relied upon downstream.

### Reconciliation report 012

Report 012 distinguished local examination, cross-examination, reconciliation, warrant, and reliance. Its minimal Book change was only the Book VIII reliance clause. It left open whether a later Book should add a dedicated cross-examination chapter or clause.

Independent review accepts part of 012 and narrows another part:

- Accepted: warrant and reliance are distinct, and the new Book VIII clause adequately preserves bounded reliance without standing expansion.
- Accepted: examination and cross-examination are distinct responsibilities.
- Narrowed: a full cross-examination chapter is not warranted. The current pressure requires only a minimal Book IV clause preserving the comparison boundary, because Book IV already owns examination methods and probes.
- Rejected: a universal inquiry pipeline, a mandatory runtime sequence, or a global topology in which city context prescribes local seams.

### Current Book coverage

Current Book IV already says a bounded examination responsibility may treat recorded material as relevant only under explicit support, subject binding, evidence/provenance requirements, and authority boundary; it also preserves lawful inactivity and Unknown. Current Book V already distinguishes testimony from fact and evidence/provenance representation from verification, causation, occurrence, and independent support. Current Book VIII now says downstream reliance consumes warranted content without strengthening standing, creating authority, proving truth or ownership, authorizing implementation, or erasing negative authority.

Those clauses are necessary but incomplete for one independently recurring responsibility: cross-examination as bounded comparison of independently preserved testimony or findings.

## Competing interpretations

### Interpretation A: no Book change is needed because Book IV, V, and VIII jointly imply cross-examination

This is partly true. Existing clauses prevent many bad inferences: testimony is not fact, evidence is not truth, provenance representation is not verification, and reliance does not expand standing. However, implication is not adequate preservation when a responsibility recurs independently and can be compressed into several neighbors. The Book currently has no clause saying that comparison must preserve each input's source-local attribution, provenance, support basis, scope, authority, confidence or uncertainty, Unknowns, contradictions, and standing.

### Interpretation B: the missing distinction is bounded competency declaration

Report 010 identifies general bounded competency declaration as missing or only partly named. That pressure remains real, but this review does not promote it. The current evidence shows a witness-local row shape with responsibility, authority, evidence/provenance requirements, and stop condition. It does not yet show that a general competency declaration is mature enough as Book grammar independent of specific examination, capability, or witness slices.

### Interpretation C: the missing distinction is positive source independence

Report 010 also marks positive source independence as unresolved. The Book adequately preserves the negative boundary that row count, repeated testimony, or distinct source strings do not establish independent corroboration. A positive constitutional relation for source responsibility, collection independence, causal independence, and authority to rely on independence remains Unknown. Promoting it now would design an algorithm rather than preserve mature grammar.

### Interpretation D: the missing distinction is cross-examination without source-local erasure

This interpretation is supported. Cross-examination is not merely local examination, not reconciliation, not warrant, not reliance, not agreement, not truth, and not contradiction-as-failure. It is the bounded comparison of independently preserved testimony or findings, with a duty to keep each input's source-local limits visible and to produce only comparison standing unless a later responsible boundary establishes more.

## Constitutional distinction recovered

The recovered distinction is:

> **Cross-examination is a bounded comparison responsibility over independently preserved testimony or findings. It may produce agreement, disagreement, contradiction, conflict, refinement, insufficiency, or Unknown, but it must preserve each input's source-local attribution, provenance, support basis, subject, scope, authority, confidence or uncertainty, Unknowns, standing, and forbidden inferences. Cross-examination does not by itself establish truth, warrant, reliance, source independence, implementation permission, or reconciliation.**

This distinction is realization-independent. It does not depend on Python, SQLite, Markdown, dataclasses, SQL views, CLI order, or report naming. It is recurrently required to keep comparison from becoming host-language equality, row-count corroboration, source-independent proof, fact establishment, or reliance.

## Minimal Book change warranted

A single Book IV clause is warranted because Book IV already owns examination methods and probes. The change is minimal and does not create a new chapter, runtime design, SQLite witness, universal inquiry pipeline, or Seed language.

Added clause:

- `04.Examination.C — Cross-examination without source-local erasure` in `book_of_seed/04-inquiry-and-examination/examination-methods-and-probes.md`.

The clause states that cross-examination may compare independently preserved testimony or findings only while preserving each input's attribution, provenance, support basis, subject, scope, authority, confidence or uncertainty, Unknowns, standing, and forbidden inferences; that its output is bounded comparison standing; and that agreement, contradiction, or repeated support does not by itself establish truth, warrant, reliance, source independence, implementation permission, or reconciliation.

## Smallest truthful answer

The mature realization-independent constitutional distinction not yet adequately preserved by the current Book was **cross-examination without source-local erasure**: bounded comparison of independently preserved testimony or findings that preserves each input's source, provenance, support, subject, scope, authority, confidence or uncertainty, Unknowns, standing, and forbidden inferences, and that does not by itself establish truth, warrant, reliance, source independence, implementation permission, or reconciliation.
