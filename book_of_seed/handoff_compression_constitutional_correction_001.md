Its constitutional subject, core question, bounded resolution, addressable boundaries, important distinctions, representative anchors, counterexamples, related chapter context, and temporal material were corrected so "handoff" no longer functions as a constitutional primitive.

Clause `08.Handoff.A` became `08.Communication.A — Bounded responsibility transition` and now requires affected subject, prior holder where known, candidate receiver, actual receiver where evidenced, scope, authority applicable to the transition, transition evidence, acceptance or establishment occurrence, surviving limits, and Unknowns.

New clause `08.Communication.D — Authority does not move with material` states the authority non-transfer rule.

New clause `08.Communication.E — External receipt and realization remain separately evidenced` states the external-realization boundary and preserves temporal Unknowns.

Related active clauses in grammar, constraints, realization, authority, and navigation were amended only where they relied on compressed handoff vocabulary as a primitive.

## Communication decomposition
Communication was cross-examined separately from handoff. The corrected Book treats communication as potentially involving representation, emission, possible transport, possible receipt, and possible interpretation, but none of those coordinates follows merely from the others. Communication is not mutual understanding and is not establishment.

## Representation and emission boundary
Seed may form and emit a bounded representation. Formation proves only that a representation was constructed by a responsible method. Emission proves only the occurrence of presentation toward a consumer boundary when evidenced. A representation may receive only the standing directly warranted for its declared purpose.

A bounded representation must preserve source subject, content, provenance, standing, scope, authority limits, confidence or uncertainty, conflicts, negative authority, Unknowns, and stopping or refusal conditions.

## Receipt and interpretation standing
Receipt is Unknown unless evidenced. Interpretation is consumer-local and Unknown unless evidenced. Delivery, acknowledgement, routing, naming a recipient, or producing an operator-visible artifact does not establish interpretation.

## Uptake and reliance boundary
Uptake is consumer-local and separately warranted. Reliance is separately bounded. Interpretation does not establish uptake, and uptake does not establish lawful reliance.

## Responsibility-transition standing
Responsibility can transition, but only through a bounded transition exposing the affected subject, prior responsible holder if known, candidate receiving holder, actual receiving holder if evidenced, scope of responsibility, authority applicable to the transition, evidence for the transition, occurrence of acceptance or establishment, limits that survive the transition, and remaining Unknowns. A named recipient, owner field, routing target, report delivery, adjacent process, or ordinary-language handoff reference is insufficient by itself.

## Authority non-transfer
Material movement is not authority movement. Authority remains absent unless a separately responsible boundary establishes authority subject, granting source, recipient, scope, purpose, duration or temporal standing, constraints, evidence, and occurrence.

## External-realization boundary
External response is not verified external effect. Possible transport, delivery, receipt, provider output, returned testimony, and operator-visible response remain evidentiary subjects only within their bounded support and do not establish external realization without separate competent evidence.

## Historical testimony preserved
Historical root reports, archived documents, investigations, audits, excision reports, and implementation histories were not rewritten. Remaining historical uses outside the amended active chapter district retain historical-testimony standing only.
