## Internal constitutional grammar recovered

The broader Book grammar is not a direct model of objective external reality. It is a bounded repository-governed grammar for making material constitutionally addressable, preserving source attribution and provenance, examining support, recognizing conflict or insufficiency, establishing claim standing within scope, selecting or relying within bounded authority, projecting views without equating projection with standing, and expressing results without strengthening them by presentation.

The central recovered grammar is:

1. Material or testimony is received, observed, formed, recorded, or otherwise preserved.
2. The source, subject, content, provenance, scope, authority, confidence, conflict, temporal standing, and Unknowns remain attached.
3. A responsible boundary may examine, compare, normalize, select, admit, establish, project, emit, or refuse within that preserved boundary.
4. The standing produced is local to the act, subject, evidence, warrant, scope, authority, and consumer.
5. External vocabulary, provider vocabulary, implementation vocabulary, artifact shape, record existence, or projection visibility does not become native constitutional grammar, cluster truth, current standing, producer occurrence, authorization, execution, consumer receipt, or verified external effect by identity.

This grammar is for responsible constitutional treatment of dimension-bearing material: identity, content, standing, provenance, responsibility, authority, scope, occurrence or preservation, and limits.

## Seed-owned acts recovered

The examined chapters consistently warrant these as Seed-owned or Seed-governed constitutional acts when produced by the responsible boundary:

- identifying, attributing, preserving, and translating external material without assimilating its grammar;
- constructing or preserving an artifact while distinguishing artifact shape from standing;
- performing an act such as a choice, admission, standing establishment, or bounded operation when the applicable production boundary is evidenced;
- selecting a candidate for bounded consideration and recording the basis;
- normalizing and establishing a claim as Fact standing within evidence, source authority, scope, and conflict limits;
- recording assertion-bearing material within a preservation horizon;
- examining or extracting from recorded material as a separate responsibility;
- projecting preserved material under declared rules while preserving consumer-local standing limits;
- forming and emitting a representation while distinguishing emission from delivery, receipt, interpretation, uptake, reliance, responsibility transition, authority transition, and external realization;
- establishing bounded support, conflict, insufficiency, or Unknown for claims about external operations.

These are Seed-owned acts over material and standing. They do not, by themselves, become external operation, provider performance, recipient receipt, external causation, or verified external effect.

## External subjects and occurrences recovered

The examined Book VII material uses external operational terms as possible subject matter of claims: mechanisms may exist, be available, be reachable, fit a purpose, be invoked, perform, refuse, produce result testimony, or produce an external effect. But the wider grammar requires each such occurrence to be separately evidenced and attributed. Seed may possess testimony about the occurrence, evidence supporting the occurrence, a bounded finding about the occurrence, a claim with standing, or Unknown. Seed does not internally possess the external occurrence itself merely by preserving, projecting, formatting, or recording material that says it occurred.

## Internal occurrence vs attributed external occurrence

A Seed-owned occurrence is a constitutional occurrence at a responsible Seed boundary: Seed observed, recorded, examined, compared, selected, established, projected, formed a representation, emitted a representation, expressed a bounded result, or refused within scope.

An externally attributed occurrence is subject matter asserted or evidenced by a source or mechanism boundary: a tool ran, a mechanism performed, a provider responded, a recipient received, an external effect occurred, or an outcome followed. The Book's broader grammar supports the distinction:

- Seed preserved testimony that X occurred is not equal to X occurred.
- Seed established bounded support for claim X is not equal to Seed performed or possessed external event X.
- A record exists is not equal to the recorded assertion being true.
- A provider response is not equal to independently verified external effect.
- Selection is not availability, invocation, performance, or causation.

Book VII frequently states these distinctions faithfully, but its title and constitutional-subject wording sometimes retain specialized operational framing that can over-suggest an internal road.

## Capability-standing analysis

Capability standing is not recovered as a distinct constitutional standing family. The active Book VII chapter itself defines it as bounded support that a transformation may be possible under declared conditions and denies that it is mechanism identity, selection, invocation permission, performance occurrence, recording, or external effect. Its listed dimensions are exactly the ordinary dimensions of Seed claim standing: subject, proposition, evidence, provenance, source or producer authority, scope, conditions, temporal standing, confidence or uncertainty, conflicts, and Unknowns.

The phrase may still be lawful shorthand for a recurring subject matter: a proposition of the form “mechanism M may perform transformation T under conditions C.” But the specialized ontology `CapabilityStanding` is not warranted by the examined Book as a separate family. General claim standing can carry the proposition while preserving all limits.

## Mechanism-fitness analysis

Mechanism fitness is recovered as a bounded local conclusion about a represented mechanism and purpose. Book VII's strongest faithful clause says the warrant supporting it is ordinary warrant grammar applied to the local conclusion, not a special primitive that creates a road to external effect. Mechanism fitness may be useful as a local name for the claim under examination, but not as a Seed-native coordinate that itself authorizes selection, invocation, performance, or effect.

The strongest risk is that the chapter title and the operational-realization district make mechanism fitness look like one step in a specialized constitution. The internal grammar does not require that. It requires evidence, comparison, scope, authority, conflicts, and Unknowns.

## Execution and invocation analysis

Execution is currently mixed. The execution chapter distinguishes execution from local implementation function calls, projections, rendering, cache lookups, measurement, and execution-status display. It also separates proposal, authorization, invocation, completed execution, and recorded execution. This is faithful to the internal/external boundary.

However, the chapter's constitutional subject, “the performance of an authorized execution and the separate preservation of what happened,” and the phrase “externally realized operation” leave pressure unresolved. Execution can be a Seed-owned occurrence when Seed performs an authorized constitutional act at a responsible boundary. Invocation can be a Seed-owned act if Seed is the responsible invoking boundary. But invocation may also be an externally attributed occurrence when a mechanism boundary is outside Seed or only reported by provider testimony. The chapter governs the separation, not automatic external truth.

A Python function call, return value, provider response, status object, or result record does not establish every claimed occurrence automatically. Producer function is not occurring road; constructible artifact is not active production; internal field named performance is not external performance occurrence.

## Performance and external-effect analysis

Performance is mixed. Seed may perform internal constitutional acts: observe, record, examine, compare, select, establish, project, express, refuse, or form and emit a representation. External mechanism performance is an attributed occurrence. A performance report is testimony; it is not independently verified external effect. Successful result is not established fact. Outcome observed is not proof that selected movement caused it.

External effect is not an internal constitutional coordinate. It is a possible external occurrence or condition represented through testimony and evidence. Seed can establish bounded support for a claim that an external effect occurred only through claim-appropriate evidence, authority, scope, conflict handling, and Unknown preservation.

## Recording analysis

Recording is Seed-owned when a responsible recording boundary preserves assertion-bearing material within a declared preservation horizon. The occurrence recorded may be a Seed-owned occurrence, an externally attributed occurrence, refusal, non-occurrence, result testimony, lineage, measurement testimony, or already established standing.

Recording produces standing that a record exists and preserves an attributed assertion. It does not produce the upstream occurrence, authorize the act, verify external effect, establish the recorded proposition, extract knowledge, create cluster truth, or prove consumer receipt. Recording is faithful as a constitutional responsibility, but that faithful responsibility cannot justify a foreign operational ontology in which recording completes an external-performance road.

## Selection analysis

Selection is Seed-owned when a responsible selection act narrows a candidate set and records its basis. Selection may identify a represented mechanism, route, representation, or subject for a purpose. It does not establish mechanism availability, reachability, mechanism fitness, authority, request formation, emission, invocation, performance, result recording, successful outcome, external effect, or causation.

The active selection chapter strongly supports the central distinction. Book VII remains faithful where it preserves selection as bounded candidate choice rather than participation in an external operational road.

## Current Book VII orientation

Book VII currently contains both orientations.

### Orientation A — specialized operational constitution

```text
capability standing
→ mechanism fitness
→ selection
→ authority
→ invocation
→ performance
→ external effect
```

This orientation is visible in the directory name, Book VII title, the concentration of capability/mechanism/execution vocabulary, the “operational realization” frame, and coordinate lists that can read as an operational sequence.

### Orientation B — general constitutional grammar over external subjects

```text
external testimony or representation
        ↓
attribution and preservation
        ↓
examination
        ↓
bounded support, conflict, insufficiency, or Unknown
        ↓
possible establishment of claim standing
        ↓
bounded selection, reliance, projection, or expression
```

This orientation is more strongly warranted by the examined Book as a whole. The neighboring grammar chapters require external vocabulary to remain attributed until translated, artifact shape to remain distinct from standing, act artifacts to remain distinct from act occurrence, testimony to remain distinct from established fact, recording to remain distinct from knowledge extraction, events to remain assertions rather than truth, projection to remain distinct from current standing, and emission to remain distinct from receipt, reliance, authority transition, and external realization.

Book VII itself increasingly denies a universal road from need to external effect and decomposes operational realization into separately warranted coordinates. Therefore the active canonical Book warrants Orientation B as the governing constitutional orientation, while retaining Orientation A as partly foreign or over-specialized residual vocabulary.

## Faithful clauses

Faithful clauses include:

- External grammar can be identified, compared, and attributed without becoming constitutional grammar.
- Artifact shape preserves assertions but does not supply warrant.
- Consumer validation does not prove producer occurrence.
- Selection narrows candidates but does not grant authority or prove later movement.
- Testimony, observations, diagnostics, and evidence payloads carry claims that require source, scope, confidence, conflict, and authority limits.
- A recorded claim may be consumed as attributed testimony without establishing testified content as fact.
- Evidence identifiers, foreign keys, citations, coherence, or lineage strings do not prove producer occurrence or independent support.
- Events record assertions; they do not make every asserted occurrence true.
- Representation formation is not emission; emission is not receipt, uptake, reliance, authority transition, or external realization.
- Capability standing is not mechanism, selection, invocation, performance, recording, or external effect.
- Mechanism fitness does not establish selection, authority, invocation, performance, outcome, or external effect.
- Execution record is testimony, not the execution occurrence or verified external effect.

## Mixed clauses

Mixed clauses include:

- Book VII's constitutional subject and title, which combine capability, mechanism, execution, and recording in a way that may imply a specialized operational constitution even while the clauses deny a universal road.
- “Execution is a warranted performance boundary for an authorized act or externally realized operation,” which distinguishes execution from ordinary computation but does not fully settle whether the occurrence is Seed-owned or externally attributed in each case.
- “Interaction,” “outcome evaluation,” “capability revision,” and “learning” passages, which correctly separate selection, occurrence report, effect, outcome, and causation but retain movement-linked operational language that can sound like a foreign workflow.
- Representative implementation anchors, which are carefully limited but still preserve capability, mechanism, operational realization, warrant, and execution-status vocabulary.

## Foreign or over-specialized grammar

The strongest foreign or over-specialized grammar is not a single clause asserting that external effects are Seed truth. It is the residual orientation that makes operational vocabulary look like an internal constitutional road: capability standing to mechanism fitness to selection to authority to invocation to performance to external effect. The examined Book repeatedly decomposes and denies this road, so the foreign element is the specialized framing, not necessarily every concept named within it.

“Capability standing,” “mechanism fitness,” “availability,” “reachability,” “external effect,” and “outcome” appear constitutionally safe when treated as claim subject matter or local findings under general standing grammar. They become foreign if treated as Seed-native coordinates that necessarily advance toward external realization.

## Candidate correction boundary

A future correction, if authorized, should not delete Book VII merely because it contains external operational vocabulary. The justified correction boundary is narrower: preserve Seed-owned acts and general claim-standing grammar; de-specialize terms whose work is only subject-matter naming; separate Seed-owned occurrence from externally attributed occurrence; and keep recording, selection, execution, invocation, performance report, outcome evaluation, external-effect evidence, and causal standing as independent, separately evidenced boundaries.

No new artifacts such as `ExternalEvent`, `PerformanceClaim`, or `CapabilityClaim` are justified by this report. General claim/testimony standing appears sufficient unless a later implementation-backed inquiry identifies a real constitutional responsibility that cannot be expressed through existing subject, proposition, evidence, provenance, scope, authority, confidence, conflict, temporal standing, and Unknown grammar.

## Lawful stopping point

The inquiry stops at recovery of orientation. It does not amend Book VII, correct clauses, rename directories, inspect broad implementation topology, change diagnostics, or introduce new schema. The lawful result is the bounded finding that the active Book's broader grammar warrants general constitutional treatment of external operational claims more strongly than a specialized operational constitution, while Book VII retains mixed residual vocabulary requiring later correction if authorized.
