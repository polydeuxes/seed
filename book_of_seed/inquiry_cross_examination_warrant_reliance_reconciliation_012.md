## 4. Independent examination of each responsibility

### A. Local examination

Local examination is a bounded responsibility applied to a bounded subject. The examined subject may be a question, record, claim, change assertion, evidence item, testimony item, frontier member, or proposed probe only when identity, provenance, subject binding, authority boundary, and applicable evidence requirements are present enough for that examination.

Admissible evidence is not any nearby text or row. Book IV requires bounded questions to have identity, provenance, and an admissible relationship to the concern. Book IV also requires recorded material to support the local responsibility, subject binding, evidence or provenance requirements, and authority boundary before examination may treat it as relevant. Book V requires evidence-shaped material to preserve evidence identity, source attribution, evidence kind, context, subject, supported claim, authority boundary, preservation horizon, confidence or uncertainty label where used, and provenance relation. SQLite Slice 001 independently witnesses the same distinction by producing local postures for applicable, irrelevant, authority-blocked, insufficient, and Unknown cases.

The authority governing examination is the examination responsibility itself, bounded by Book IV and by Book VIII authority scope. Local examination may conclude bounded relevance, permission to examine further, lawful inactivity, insufficiency, authority block, or Unknown. It may not conclude selection, execution, mutation, fact establishment, universal evidence admission, external occurrence, current lawful state, or required action merely from examination standing.

Later work may rely on a local examination only for the standing it actually produced: for example, that material is relevant inside a boundary, that a record exists as recorded material, that testimony may be used as attributed premise-relative input, or that an Unknown or insufficiency must be preserved. Later work must not rely on the local examination as proof of truth, producer occurrence, implementation permission, ownership, global topology, or authority beyond the preserved boundary.

Local examination must stop when evidence is irrelevant, unsupported, insufficiently bound, outside authority, or not bound enough to decide relevance. That stop is lawful examination output, not incomplete execution.

### B. Cross-examination

Cross-examination consumes independently bounded testimony or findings. What may be compared are source-local claims whose source, scope, authority, support, Unknowns, confidence, and standing have not been erased. Before comparison, each testimony item must remain source-local: its attribution, provenance, context, subject, claim, support basis, confidence or uncertainty, authority boundary, conflicts, and forbidden inferences must remain visible.

Constitutionally permitted comparison may test agreement, disagreement, shared support, source independence, contradiction, applicability, scope mismatch, confidence mismatch, and whether a claim from one boundary is admissible to another. It may produce refinement, disproof of a narrower claim, insufficiency, contradiction, preserved disagreement, or Unknown. It may show that two findings agree within a boundary, but agreement does not become truth merely by repetition. It may show contradiction, but contradiction is not failure; it is an integrity-preserving result.

Cross-examination itself does not necessarily establish warrant. It supplies material from which a later establishment or warrant judgment may be made. It may strengthen a support analysis only when source independence, applicability, and sufficiency are themselves established. If those conditions are missing, it must preserve contradiction, insufficiency, or Unknown rather than infer through them.

Cross-examination must stop at the edge of independently preserved testimony. It may not erase source-local limits, force consensus, create authority, promote testimony to fact, or turn comparison into implementation permission.

### C. Reconciliation

Reconciliation consumes bounded examination results, compared testimony, contradictions, insufficiencies, Unknowns, and applicable Book or implementation witnesses. It is not mere comparison. Comparison can show relationships among claims; reconciliation owns the bounded constitutional disposition of those relationships.

Reconciliation may resolve a distinction, distinguish two responsibilities, preserve disagreement, report insufficiency, withhold conclusion, or retain Unknown. It produces a bounded reconciliation result with its own authority as a constitutional report or Book correction only to the extent supported by repository evidence. It does not create truth merely because it reconciles; it may explicitly refuse establishment.

Standing that survives reconciliation includes preserved testimony standing, evidence limits, contradictions, confidence limits, Unknowns, negative authority, and lawful stop conditions. Reliance after reconciliation is lawful only for the bounded result actually warranted: for example, a Book clause correction, a preserved Unknown, a refusal to promote terminology, or a consumer-side reliance boundary.

### D. Warrant

Warrant is produced or established by a responsible boundary that has sufficient subject identity, admissible evidence, provenance, applicable authority, scope, and conditions for the bounded act or reliance at issue. Examination, cross-examination, and reconciliation may contribute to warrant, but none necessarily produces warrant automatically.

A warrant carries both entitlement and limit. It states what bounded consumer or act may use a result, for what purpose, under what authority and evidence boundaries, and where use must stop. Warrant is withheld when source identity is missing, provenance is inapplicable, evidence is insufficient, authority is absent or mismatched, scope is overrun, contradictions defeat sufficiency, confidence is inadequate for the role, or Unknown remains material.

Unknowns and negative authority remain attached to warrant. A warrant is not an authorization for arbitrary execution, not an ownership grant, not a fact promotion by itself, and not a permission to expand the result into neighboring topology. The consumer of a warrant may be a downstream examination, explanation, frontier, selection, communication, or Book correction boundary, but only for its declared role.

### E. Reliance

Reliance remains supported as a constitutional consumption discipline. It is the consumer-side use of warranted content inside preserved source, authority, scope, evidence, provenance, confidence, and Unknown boundaries. The earlier phrase “the lawful downstream use of warranted content within the preserved authority boundary” is substantially supported, but incomplete unless it explicitly preserves consumer role, non-reliance, provenance continuity, confidence limits, negative authority, Unknowns, and the rule that reliance does not strengthen consumed content.

A bounded consumer may rely only for the role warranted: examination, explanation, preservation, communication, selection input, Book correction, or another bounded constitutional use. Reliance does not change the standing of consumed content. It does not promote testimony into truth, create authority, imply ownership, authorize implementation, prove source occurrence, erase Unknowns, or strengthen confidence merely by being consumed.

Non-reliance must be stated when a completed examination would otherwise invite overuse. If a report relies on testimony as source-relative input, it must also say it does not rely on that testimony as fact. If it relies on a local distinction, it must not rely on parent or city topology to prescribe that seam. Reliance stops where warrant, evidence, authority, provenance, confidence, or scope stops.

## 5. Cross-examination

The independently examined responsibilities support these smallest distinctions:

- **Examination != cross-examination.** Examination establishes a bounded local posture over a subject and evidence. Cross-examination compares independently preserved testimony or findings.
- **Comparison != reconciliation.** Comparison identifies relation, agreement, disagreement, contradiction, insufficiency, or Unknown. Reconciliation assigns a bounded constitutional disposition to those relationships.
- **Reconciliation != establishment.** Reconciliation can establish a bounded distinction or Book correction, but it can also preserve disagreement, withhold conclusion, or retain Unknown.
- **Agreement != truth.** Agreement can contribute to support only when independence, applicability, and sufficiency are established.
- **Contradiction != failure.** Contradiction is a preserved integrity result and may be a lawful stopping reason.
- **Sufficiency != warrant automatically.** Sufficiency for one role may be insufficient for another; warrant requires authority, role, provenance, scope, and negative-boundary checks.
- **Warrant != reliance.** Warrant is the entitlement-and-limit produced by a responsible boundary; reliance is downstream consumption under that warrant.
- **Reliance != truth promotion, ownership, authority creation, or implementation permission.** Reliance preserves standing; it does not improve it.
- **Unknown != failed inquiry.** Unknown is a lawful preserved result when evidence cannot decide a bounded question.
- **Lawful stop != incomplete execution.** Stop may be required by insufficiency, Unknown, authority mismatch, contradiction, refusal, or completed sufficiency.

Repository evidence supports **independent examination before cross-examination** as constitutional law for any cross-examination that claims constitutional standing. It is not a universal runtime sequence for every investigation. The constitutional dependency is that comparison may not erase source-local source, scope, authority, support, Unknowns, confidence, and standing. A runtime may realize that dependency in different ways, but a constitutional cross-examination without independently preserved testimony would be overclaiming.

## 6. Smallest supported topology

The smallest supported topology is not a universal inquiry pipeline. It is a set of dependency boundaries:

1. A bounded subject may be examined only under admissible evidence, provenance, authority, scope, confidence, and Unknown boundaries.
2. Independently preserved testimony or findings may be cross-examined only without erasing their source-local limits.
3. Comparison may produce agreement, disagreement, contradiction, refinement, insufficiency, or Unknown.
4. Reconciliation may assign a bounded constitutional disposition: establish a distinction, distinguish claims, preserve disagreement, withhold conclusion, or retain Unknown.
5. Warrant, when established, carries entitlement and limit for a bounded consumer and role.
6. Reliance is consumer-side use under that warrant and must preserve non-reliance where over-inference is possible.
7. Lawful stop occurs wherever evidence, provenance, confidence, authority, warrant, scope, or Unknown boundaries end.

## 8. Reliance recovery

Reliance is missing or compressed in the current Book as a consumer-side discipline. Current clauses mention permitted reliance and forbidden inference in particular places, but the Book does not explicitly preserve the producer/consumer distinction between warrant and reliance.

Recovered Reliance boundaries:

- Reliance is downstream consumption, not producer-side establishment.
- Reliance requires a bounded consumer, role, warrant, evidence, source/provenance continuity, authority, confidence or uncertainty limits, Unknown boundaries, and negative authority.
- Reliance does not strengthen consumed content.
- Reliance does not promote truth, create authority, imply ownership, or authorize implementation.
- A completed examination must state lawful reliance and required non-reliance where silence would invite overclaiming.
