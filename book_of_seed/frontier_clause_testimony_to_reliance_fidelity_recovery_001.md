## Producer-side standing recovered

### What a testimony producer is responsible for

The Book distinguishes an artifact's represented assertions from the warrant for those assertions. An artifact may preserve identity, provenance, result, uncertainty, and boundaries, but that representation does not itself supply the producer-side warrant. Production authority requires a responsible boundary that validates the required identity, provenance, state, and warrant before asserting stronger standings such as selection, establishment, admission, or occurrence.

Therefore a testimony producer is responsible, at most, for the claim form it actually produces: the source-relative assertion, its declared scope, provenance or attribution, uncertainty, and limits. If producer occurrence or producer responsibility is claimed, that stronger claim requires separate preserved support. A witnessed producer return may add observer-held occurrence standing, but an artifact with matching fields does not automatically carry that standing unless it is represented or preserved.

### Must every constitutional assertion have a recognized owner?

The examined Book clauses do not require every preserved assertion-bearing artifact to have a recognized owner before it can be constitutionally visible or examined. The Book explicitly permits recorded assertions and testimony to exist as attributed claims, diagnostic findings, evidence records, or premise-relative inputs. It also explicitly separates attribution from responsibility.

However, if the claim being made is producer responsibility, accountability, original production, or owner-backed warrant, then recognized owner or responsibility standing cannot be inferred from attribution, association, copied lineage, or downstream reliance.

### May testimony remain constitutionally usable while ownership is Unknown?

Yes, for narrower uses. Testimony may be consumed as attributed testimony or premise-relative input for bounded examination, provided attribution, source-relative limits, conflicts, and uncertainty are preserved. Recorded material may also be examined as recorded material. This is constitutional usability, not factual establishment or ownership recognition.

The Book does not thereby authorize every downstream reliance. It permits consideration, preservation, examination, applicability evaluation, and some bounded premise-relative use while ownership remains Unknown. Stronger claims that depend on responsible producer standing remain Unknown or prohibited until separately supported.

### What attribution establishes

Attribution establishes, within its own claim form, that an assertion is represented as coming from, being associated with, or being reported by a source, actor, account, artifact, or boundary. It may preserve provenance or inquiry pressure.

Attribution does not establish responsibility, ownership, fault, verified producer occurrence, truth, independent corroboration, or current lawful state.

### What producer responsibility establishes

Producer responsibility, where separately supported, establishes that a responsible boundary or owner made the relevant assertion or produced the relevant artifact under the claim-appropriate authority and warrant. It may supply standing for that producer-side act. It still does not automatically establish downstream consumer reliance, universal truth, authority expansion, or every later operative relation.

### Can an adapter preserve or translate standing without becoming the original producer?

Yes. The Book permits projection, explanation construction, lensing, handoff, recording, and transformation-like responsibilities to preserve or represent source material under declared methods without changing the standing of represented source material. A consumer, adapter, or lens may produce a bounded representation of what supplied sources expose. That act can have its own local warrant without proving the upstream producer occurred or making the adapter the original producer.

### What missing ownership prohibits

Missing ownership prohibits claims that require recognized owner standing, including:

- the assertion has a recognized producer;
- the producer occurred;
- the producer is responsible for the asserted content;
- attribution is ownership;
- copied lineage is occurrence proof;
- downstream reliance retroactively creates producer standing.

Missing ownership does not by itself prohibit preservation, visibility, source-relative examination, premise-relative use, or a narrower consumer-local assertion if the consumer preserves the ownership Unknown and does not claim the stronger upstream standing.

## Consumer-side standing recovered

### May the consumer consider testimony without accepting it?

Yes. Reachability may create local visibility to a bounded consumer, observer, diagnostic, projection, or inquiry without acceptance, admission, reliance, projection as truth, ledger recording, or mutation authority. A frontier may assemble bounded inquiry possibilities, and membership creates visibility for consideration rather than selection, authorization, examination, or resolution.

### May the consumer preserve testimony without relying upon it?

Yes. A recording boundary may preserve an attributed assertion; diagnostic or examination-scoped recording may preserve bounded availability for examination without cluster truth, universal state, reliance, or receipt. Preservation and retrieval are not receipt, acceptance, reliance, or fact establishment.

### What local act or relation permits reliance?

The Book does not impose a universal admission road. It does, however, require consumer-local responsibility for reliance. A responsible consumer must have prior standing to consume upstream material, and the preserved evidence, warrant, identity, scope, authority, confidence, and Unknown limits must be sufficient for that consumer boundary. Where the consumer accepts the producer assertion for a declared purpose and validates the identities and invariants needed at that boundary, the relation becomes the assertion-preserving subfamily of uptake called a constitutional road.

In authority grammar, downstream reliance on warranted content is lawful only for the bounded role, purpose, provenance, evidence, authority, scope, confidence or uncertainty, and Unknown boundaries preserved by that warrant. Admission or sufficiency findings, where present, allow participation only within their limits and are not ratification.

Thus the prior movement is claim-relative. It may be applicability, admission, sufficiency, warrant, or another local consumer relation where that is the claim. It need not be a universal stage, but the consumer-local reliance standing and its limits must remain attributable.

### Does the consumer require the strongest upstream artifact?

No. Road sufficiency is local: producer-side warrant, artifact preservation, and consumer-side validation are related but not interchangeable. A later consumer may sometimes need only enough standing for its exact assertion. A separately constructed or preserved artifact may satisfy local type, registration, identity-coherence, examination, or premise-relative checks without proving original producer occurrence.

### Can mediated or unowned testimony support a narrower local claim?

Yes, where the narrower claim is framed as consumer-local, purpose-bound, uncertainty-preserving, and non-promoting. The Book's frontier clause is especially direct: frontier establishment is consumer-local and can establish identity coherence, required boundary-clause family coverage, and operative frontier standing from supplied selected-need and testimony artifacts without proving selected-need truth, testimony truth, producer occurrence, inquiry opening, execution, or result knowledge.

The lawful narrower claim is not that the testimony is true, owned, canonical Evidence, admitted for all purposes, or independently verified. It is that the consumer boundary, under its own local standing and preserved limits, may use the testimony as a premise for a bounded assertion.

## Possible movement from testimony to reliance

The examined Book supports no single universal sequence. It does support the following bounded possible movement where each crossing remains observable and non-promoting:

```text
prior consumer standing for a bounded frontier assembly
→ explicitly preserved attributed or unowned clause-boundary testimony
→ preservation of source-relative scope, purpose, uncertainty, conflicts, and Unknown ownership
→ consumer-local validation that the preserved coordinates are sufficient for this frontier assertion
→ bounded reliance on the coordinates as premise-relative testimony
→ frontier-local operative relation
```

This movement permits, at maximum, a consumer assertion such as:

```text
this clause is operative for this frontier assembly
```

It does not permit the consumer to assert, merely from the preserved coordinates, that:

```text
the clause is true universally;
the clause has a recognized producer;
the clause is canonical Evidence for every consumer;
the coordinate standing was independently verified;
the original producer occurred;
the consumer-local operative relation is intrinsic to the testimony.
```

### Temporal orientation

The strongest temporally faithful pattern recovered is:

```text
prior standing: consumer has bounded frontier-assembly standing
→ responsible relation: consumer-local uptake/reliance relation over preserved testimony
→ required support: attribution or unowned status, scope, purpose, evidence currency and availability, standing/disposition coordinates, uncertainty, conflicts, and Unknowns preserved
→ consumer-local reliance standing: coordinates may be used as premise-relative input for this frontier assertion only
→ resulting assertion: frontier-local operativity, not upstream truth
```

Prior standing remains unchanged: attribution remains attribution, unowned remains unowned, represented lineage remains represented, and preservation remains preservation. Stronger standing is not created: truth, ownership, verified provenance, occurrence proof, general admission, sufficiency for other claims, universal warrant, and authority do not arise by reliance. Reverse inference is forbidden: a later established frontier does not prove the upstream testimony was true, owned, admitted, sufficient, or responsibly produced. The relation survives only for the declared consumer purpose and temporal frontier boundary unless a separate standing movement supports reuse.

## Claim-relative standing differences

| Claim made | Minimum Book-supported standing recovered | Stronger standing not created |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `the testimony says X` | Preserved record or testimony with attribution or unowned status and boundaries | Truth, ownership, occurrence, admission |
| `the consumer may consider X` | Reachability/visibility to the bounded consumer, with no promotion | Acceptance, reliance, frontier establishment |
| `the consumer may preserve X` | Recording boundary and preservation horizon | Receipt, reliance, knowledge extraction, fact |
| `X is applicable to this consumer` | Consumer-local applicability evaluation | Admission, consumption, sufficiency |
| `X is admitted to this consumer` | Separate admission standing if claimed by the local grammar | Final-consumer consumption, truth, ratification |
| `the consumer relies on X as a premise` | Consumer-local prior standing plus sufficient preserved evidence, warrant, identity, scope, authority, confidence, and Unknown limits for the exact assertion | Upstream standing expansion, ownership, producer occurrence |
| `the consumer establishes a local relation from X` | Responsible consumer assertion within purpose, scope, uncertainty, and negative authority | Universal truth of X, canonical Evidence, reusable authority |
| `X is established constitutional fact` | Explicit ingestion, support, normalization, reconciliation, and conflict-aware establishment | Not supplied by testimony preservation or reliance alone |

## Alternative interpretations tested

### A. Unowned testimony may be preserved but never relied upon

Rejected as too strong for the examined Book. The Book permits testimony to be consumed as premise-relative input and permits consumer-local frontier establishment from testimony artifacts without proving testimony truth or producer occurrence. However, this interpretation remains correct for claims that require ownership, responsibility, or verified producer occurrence.

### B. Unowned testimony may support bounded consumer-local reliance when attribution, scope, purpose, uncertainty, and limits remain explicit

Supported within scope. This is the strongest positive interpretation recovered for frontier-boundary testimony. It depends on the exact claim remaining consumer-local and non-promoting.

### C. Recognized producer or adapter responsibility must precede reliance

Partially claim-dependent. Recognized producer responsibility must precede claims of producer responsibility or owner-backed warrant. Adapter responsibility may be required where the adapter's transformation is itself the support being relied upon. But the examined Book does not require recognized original producer ownership for every premise-relative or frontier-local consumer reliance claim.

### D. A separate consumer-local acceptance, admission, sufficiency, or warrant relation must precede reliance

Supported in dimensional form, not as one universal layer. Reliance is not created by reachability or preservation alone. A consumer-local relation sufficient for the exact reliance must precede or constitute reliance. The Book allows that relation to be expressed as uptake, road, admission, warrant, sufficiency, or another bounded responsibility depending on the claim; it does not require a universal artifact or stage named Admission or Warrant.

### E. Different constitutional claims require different prior standings

Supported. This is the main recovery. Ownership claims require ownership support; fact claims require fact establishment support; frontier-local operative claims may require only consumer-local frontier standing plus bounded premise-relative testimony support.

### F. The Book does not yet resolve the movement

Partially preserved as Unknown. The Book resolves enough to permit bounded local reliance and to forbid silent promotion. It does not resolve a universal testimony-to-reliance sequence, all possible consumer families, or the exact required artifact shape for every realization.

## Realization-invariant expectations

The Book recovers constitutional invariants, not object shapes. A faithful realization need not contain an ownership object, admission artifact, warrant field, lifecycle enum, or separate function for every movement. It may be Python, SQL, composed hybrid, distributed hybrid, or contrastive if it preserves the same standing distinctions.

Required surviving distinctions:

- testimony preserved != testimony accepted;
- testimony accepted != testimony admitted;
- testimony admitted != testimony relied upon;
- testimony relied upon != testimony established as fact;
- producer ownership != consumer-local reliance standing;
- attribution != ownership;
- lineage != occurrence proof;
- consumer reliance != retroactive producer standing;
- operative relation != intrinsic property of the testimony;
- available != applicable;
- applicable != admitted;
- admitted != consumed;
- consumed != sufficient;
- sufficient != warranted;
- warranted != authorized.

Required movement observability:

- the consumer purpose and boundary for reliance must remain identifiable;
- preserved testimony status, including unowned or attributed status, must remain visible;
- scope, family disposition, standing disposition, evidence currency, evidence availability, uncertainty, conflicts, and Unknowns must survive as reliance limits where they are the coordinates relied upon;
- any claim that the frontier-local relation became operative must remain local to the frontier assembly and time/purpose for which it was formed;
- any stronger claim must be separately supported rather than inferred.

Unconstitutional silent promotions would include:

- treating unowned testimony as owner-backed testimony;
- treating attribution as producer responsibility;
- treating preserved coordinates as independently verified truth;
- treating frontier-local operativity as universal clause truth;
- treating availability as admission or admission as final-consumer consumption;
- treating consumer reliance as producer occurrence;
- erasing uncertainty, scope limits, conflicts, or Unknown ownership while relying on the coordinates.

## Fidelity determination against the recovered witness

Later witness to judge:

```text
caller or upstream stage supplies clause coordinates
→ preservation records those coordinates as attributed or unowned testimony
→ downstream frontier assembly evaluates them
→ the clause reference may become frontier-locally operative
→ the frontier may become established
```

Determination: **faithful within bounded scope, with prohibited stronger readings**.

The Book establishes an expectation strong enough to permit this witness if, and only if, the implementation preserves the coordinates as attributed or unowned testimony, keeps scope/purpose/uncertainty/Unknown limits explicit, and makes the resulting operative relation frontier-local rather than upstream truth. It would become an unfaithful crossing only if the witness silently promoted the coordinates into recognized ownership, verified producer occurrence, canonical fact, universal Evidence, general admission, reusable authority, or operativity intrinsic to the testimony.

## Bounded answer

The maximum standing the Book permits a bounded consumer to derive from explicitly preserved but unowned frontier-boundary testimony is **consumer-local, purpose-limited premise standing sufficient to assert a frontier-local operative relation for that exact frontier assembly**.

The prior movement that must precede or constitute that reliance is **not a universal admission or ownership stage**. It is a bounded consumer-local responsibility: the consumer must have prior standing for the frontier assertion and must preserve and evaluate the testimony's attribution or unowned status, scope, purpose, coordinate family, standing and disposition claims, evidence currency and availability, uncertainty, conflicts, and Unknowns as limits on its own reliance. That movement permits local reliance; it does not establish upstream truth, producer ownership, canonical Evidence, independently verified coordinate standing, or reusable authority.
