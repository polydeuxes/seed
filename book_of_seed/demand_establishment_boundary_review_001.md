## Executive finding

The compression in PR 1907 is recoverable if `demand exists before Seed recognizes it` is decomposed into two standings:

```text
external necessity
=
the governing relation is in fact unsatisfied or unable to continue
unless a condition is satisfied,
whether or not Seed has recognized that condition

Seed-owned demand standing
=
Seed has lawfully established, for a bounded consumer and purpose,
that an applicable warranted requirement is materially unsatisfied
by current standing or by preserved current-Unknown standing,
and that resolving the incompatibility is necessary for that bounded
consumer relation, sufficiency judgment, admissibility boundary,
completion boundary, or proposed movement to remain possible.
```

The first may be real but is not yet Seed standing. The second is Seed-owned standing and may constrain later movement, but it still does not assign responsibility, authorize response selection, authorize execution, prove cause, prove satisfaction, or amend reality.

The responsible boundary is therefore not merely the requirement source, not merely the current-standing producer, and not merely a mechanical comparator. It is the boundary that has standing to assert this exact conclusion:

```text
for this consumer,
under this purpose and scope,
this warranted and currently applicable required condition
is materially unsatisfied by this current standing or surviving Unknown,
and that incompatibility must be addressed for this bounded relation
or movement to remain possible or sufficient.
```

## Recovered distinction: reality versus Seed standing

The following separations must survive:

```text
required condition is unsatisfied in reality
!=
Seed has established that incompatibility

Seed has established incompatibility
!=
Seed has established demand

demand established
!=
responsibility assigned

responsibility assigned
!=
movement authorized

movement authorized
!=
demand satisfied
```

A relation can fail in reality without Seed owning any constitutional standing about that failure. Seed may receive external material, testimony, a measurement, a complaint, a refusal, a missing support result, or a suspected insufficiency. Those may be evidence or pressure. They become Seed-owned demand only when the demand-establishment boundary preserves enough required-side standing, current-side standing, comparison authority, materiality to consumer, Unknowns, and limits.

Thus the lawful vocabulary is posture-sensitive:

| Posture | Safer vocabulary | Standing limit |
|---|---|---|
| Relation is in fact unsatisfied but Seed has not established it | external necessity / external incompatibility | Reality pressure or testimony; not Seed-owned demand standing |
| Requirement is stated but source, authority, scope, applicability, or consumer is unsupported | candidate requirement / possible demand | Cannot establish demand from it |
| Current side is incomplete or unmeasured | current Unknown / suspected insufficiency | Missing evidence is not evidence of absence |
| Current and required sides are compared under a responsible boundary | comparison finding / incompatibility finding | Not automatically material demand |
| Incompatibility is necessary to address for bounded consumer continuation or sufficiency | demand standing / demand incidence | Responsibility and response still downstream |
| A proposed act is consumed by a constraint and refused, narrowed, or blocked | constraint result / blocking standing | Stronger because it is tied to an actual constrained proposal |
| Consumer-local demand is later assigned to an actor or relation role | response responsibility | Not implied by incidence alone |
| A response is chosen | response selection | Not authorized by demand alone |
| Consumer judges requirement satisfied or relation ended | demand satisfaction / supersession / inapplicability | Local to that consumer and purpose |

## What warrants the required side

The required side must have some independent standing before it can support demand establishment. It may come from, for example:

- a consumer acceptance or sufficiency rule;
- a bounded goal condition;
- a question evidence requirement;
- an authority precondition;
- a policy or safety boundary;
- a timing tolerance or deadline;
- a communication relationship that requires shared representation;
- a capability verification rule;
- an external protocol condition.

The requirement need not be a canonical Book term or a universal artifact. It must, however, be sufficiently bounded for the demand claim being made. At minimum, the establishment boundary must preserve or recover:

```text
requirement identity or description
requirement source or source Unknown
consumer
purpose
scope
applicability conditions
current applicability state
warrant or warrant Unknown
required dimension or dimensions
tolerance or comparison method where applicable
surviving Unknowns and conflicts
```

The required distinctions are:

```text
requirement stated
!= requirement authoritative

requirement authoritative
!= currently applicable

requirement applicable
!= currently unsatisfied
```

Seed cannot establish demand from an unsupported requirement. It may preserve a candidate requirement, possible demand, or Unknown. A requirement can remain candidate or Unknown when its source, consumer, purpose, authority, applicability, or comparison method is insufficient. A requirement can be authoritative for one consumer but inapplicable now. A currently applicable requirement can still be satisfied.

## What warrants the current side

The current side must be evidenced at the strength needed by the demand claim. It can be established condition standing, bounded observation testimony, measured operation testimony, preserved non-uptake, admitted premise-relative testimony, or a bounded Unknown that is itself material to a consumer requirement.

The following distinctions remain controlling:

```text
current condition Unknown
!= required condition unsatisfied

missing evidence
!= evidence of absence

one failed attempt
!= persistent incompatibility

one measurement
!= baseline

one observation
!= current standing universally
```

Demand can be established while part of current standing remains Unknown only when the requirement is itself a requirement for known standing, evidence sufficiency, verification, admissibility, communication uptake, or another condition where the surviving Unknown is materially incompatible. Example: if a consumer requires verified capability before selection, current capability Unknown can support a capability demand for that consumer. But if the requirement is `condition absent`, Unknown cannot be promoted into absence.

## Comparison ownership

Comparison is consumer-local or boundary-local. It is not owned automatically by the requirement source, the observer, the recorder, the presentation surface, or the later response selector.

The comparison boundary must answer:

```text
Who has standing to compare?
What exact subject is compared?
For which consumer?
Under which purpose and scope?
What requirement is currently applicable?
What current standing or Unknown is admitted?
What method, tolerance, or evidence rule applies?
What result is produced?
What Unknowns survive?
```

Roles must remain separate:

| Role | May supply | Must not be collapsed into |
|---|---|---|
| Requirement source | Required condition, rule, tolerance, precondition | Current standing, demand, responsibility, response |
| Current-standing producer | Measurement, observation, testimony, current projection | Requirement authority or materiality |
| Comparison boundary | Compatibility or incompatibility finding | Demand unless materiality is also established |
| Consumer | Purpose and sufficiency/admissibility context | Universal owner of all comparison roles |
| Demand establishment boundary | Demand standing and incidence | Responsibility assignment or movement authorization |
| Responsibility bearer | Obligation or standing to respond | Demand existence by identity |
| Response selector | Candidate response selection | Requirement truth or demand satisfaction |

## Comparison result versus demand

A comparison finding is not yet demand. It says, in bounded form:

```text
current standing is incompatible with the required condition
```

Demand additionally says:

```text
that incompatibility must be addressed for this bounded consumer relation,
sufficiency judgment, admissibility boundary, completion boundary,
or proposed movement to remain possible or sufficient
```

The additional relation is materiality to a consumer. More precisely, demand needs a relation such as:

- necessity for continuation of a communication, protocol, or operational relation;
- necessity for sufficiency of an inquiry, answer, candidate, or coverage judgment;
- necessity for admissibility under an authority, evidence, policy, or safety boundary;
- necessity for completion under a bounded goal or movement boundary;
- necessity for a proposed act to pass a constraint.

Thus a safer local distinction is:

```text
requirement = bounded condition used for judgment
incompatibility finding = bounded comparison result showing current standing does not meet it
demand = established standing that resolving the incompatibility is materially necessary for this consumer relation
```

This does not require a universal `Gap` object. `Gap` remains historical shorthand or a local description of difference, missing support, mismatch, or comparison result.

## Demand and constraint result

`Demand as relation-backed standing` and `demand as constraint result` are related but not identical.

A demand is an insufficiency standing when the establishment boundary concludes that a materially necessary required condition is unsatisfied for a consumer, without consuming a specific proposed act. Example: current shared grammar is insufficient for the operator-console communication relation to remain possible.

A demand is a constraint result when a constraint boundary consumes a proposed act or relevant context and produces an outcome such as blocked, refused, narrowed, unsupported, insufficient, or unavailable because the required condition is not met. Example: a proposed operational realization is blocked because required authority is unavailable, or a timing-sensitive act is inadmissible because measured current behavior is outside an applicable tolerance.

The same incompatibility can support both standings at different boundaries:

```text
authority unavailable for candidate action
→ authority insufficiency demand for the action-selection consumer
→ constraint result when the warrant or execution boundary consumes the proposed action and blocks it
```

Constraint-result demand carries stronger warrant about a particular proposed movement because the act, consumer, constraint, context, and result have been joined. It still does not prove cause, assign responsibility, authorize an alternative, or satisfy the demand.

## Demand dimensions

Demand dimensions need not be fields on one universal artifact. They may belong to different parts of the establishment:

| Dimension family | Where it may belong |
|---|---|
| Temporal | requirement tolerance, measurement context, current standing, comparison method |
| Communicative | consumer relation, required shared representation, current uptake standing |
| Representational | requirement for representation, current artifact shape, comparison method |
| Semantic | required meaning boundary, candidate grammar, current Unknowns |
| Authority | requirement source, admissibility condition, constraint result |
| Evidence | question or verification requirement, current evidence standing |
| Capability | required transformation, current reachability, candidate realization standing |
| Resource | policy/tolerance, current resource testimony, materiality to consumer |
| Dependency | candidate basis, current availability, constraint result |
| Safety | policy boundary, current observation, admissibility comparison |

One consumer may receive several independently established demands. Same consumer does not imply one demand. Same incompatibility does not imply one dimension. Several dimensions do not imply a universal demand vector. A communication relation can expose a shared-grammar demand, an evidence demand, and a capability demand at separate boundaries, each with its own warrant, Unknowns, incidence, and downstream responsibility question.

## Responsibility incidence

PR 1907's symmetric-demand shorthand should be corrected. Relation-wide incidence does not imply symmetric responsibility.

```text
relation-wide incompatibility
!= equal responsibility

demand affects both parties
!= both parties must adapt

one party can respond
!= that party owns the obligation

responsibility Unknown
!= symmetric responsibility
```

A recovered distinction is:

```text
demand incidence
=
which relation, consumer, candidate movement, or boundary is constrained

response responsibility
=
who has standing, duty, permission, or obligation to respond
```

Responsibility assignment is downstream from demand establishment unless the same constitutional boundary explicitly establishes both and has warrant for both. Demand can constrain a relation while responsibility remains Unknown, unilateral, shared, external, operator-owned, Seed-owned, policy-owned, or mediated. The ability of Seed or the operator to adapt is evidence of possible response capacity, not obligation.

## Console witness

Bounded interaction:

```text
operator supplies free-form material
Seed exposes structured enum options
no structured uptake is observed
operator supplies further free-form material
Seed repeats structured assistance
no structured uptake is observed
```

Strongest lawful standing:

```text
free-form operator material observed
structured enum options exposed
structured-channel uptake not observed in this bounded interaction
current communication success not established
current shared grammar Unknown or insufficient for this interaction
structured assistance strategy not shown sufficient here
```

Preserved exclusions:

```text
no successful uptake observed
!= enum strategy incapable of success

non-uptake
!= intentional refusal

recurrence
!= operator preference

free-form recurrence
!= English request

communication incompatibility
!= responsibility assigned to Seed
```

The console witness supports at least candidate communication demand. It establishes shared-grammar demand only if an additional warrant is present: the console boundary must already have standing as an operator communication ingress whose continuation or sufficiency requires shared representation, and the demand boundary must be allowed to treat repeated non-uptake as materially incompatible with that relation while preserving alternatives such as noise, testing, operator error, inaccessible UI, translation absence, or unrelated literals.

Without that additional warrant, the result is:

```text
current communication success: not established
current structured-channel uptake: not observed
shared grammar: Unknown or insufficient as candidate standing
communication demand: candidate, not established
```

With that warrant, the result may be:

```text
communication demand: established incidence on the operator-console communication relation
responsibility: Unknown
response authorization: not established
English request: not established
```

## Timing witness

Bounded timing example:

```text
measured duration
+
consumer timing requirement or tolerance
+
comparison
```

Timing is a clearer demand-establishment witness than communication when the requirement, measurement method, tolerance, consumer, and comparison authority are already explicit. It illustrates the boundary cleanly:

```text
measurement outside tolerance
!= demand automatically

deviation material to consumer
!= responsibility assigned

timing demand established
!= cause known

timing demand established
!= mitigation authorized
```

A measured duration outside an applicable tolerance establishes an incompatibility finding. Demand requires the additional standing that the timing incompatibility is materially necessary to resolve for the consumer's continuation, sufficiency, admissibility, or completion. Cause, persistence, baseline transition, changed ordinary behavior, and mitigation authority remain separate.

Timing is not clearer because numbers are inherently constitutional. It is clearer only when the comparison boundary already exists. If there is one measurement but no applicable requirement, baseline, tolerance, consumer, or materiality, timing has the same insufficiency as communication: possible pressure, not demand standing.

## Demand before recognition

The phrase `demand exists before Seed recognizes it` should be decomposed.

`External necessity` is constitutionally meaningful only as external material, antecedent, testimony, pressure, or Unknown until Seed establishes it. It may describe reality faithfully: a protocol may require a token, a user relation may require shared representation, a deadline may be missed, a safety limit may be exceeded. But Seed cannot rely on it as Seed-owned demand standing without a lawful boundary.

`Seed demand standing` is the internal established posture. It preserves the governing relation or consumer, required condition, current standing or material Unknown, comparison result, materiality, scope, evidence, authority, conflicts, and remaining limits.

Therefore:

```text
external necessity may be real before recognition
but Seed-owned demand does not exist before establishment
```

## Required direct answers

### Can a demand exist outside Seed before Seed recognizes it?

Yes, if `demand` means external necessity: reality may contain an unsatisfied governing relation before Seed recognizes it. No, if `demand` means Seed-owned demand standing: that exists only after lawful establishment.

### What is the difference between external necessity and Seed-owned demand standing?

External necessity is reality pressure or external incompatibility. Seed-owned demand standing is a bounded established claim that an applicable warranted requirement is materially unsatisfied for a specific consumer and purpose.

### What minimum standings must exist before demand can be established?

Minimum standings are: a bounded consumer or relation, a warranted and currently applicable requirement, current standing or material Unknown sufficient for the claim, comparison authority, an incompatibility finding, materiality to the consumer, and preserved scope, evidence, conflicts, Unknowns, and limits.

### Must the requirement itself already be warranted and applicable?

Yes. A merely stated requirement can support candidate demand or possible demand, but not established demand. Authoritative does not mean currently applicable, so current applicability must also be established.

### Can demand be established while current standing remains Unknown?

Only when Unknown is itself materially incompatible with the requirement, such as a requirement for verified evidence, capability, admissibility, or shared communication standing. Unknown cannot be promoted into absence when the requirement depends on absence.

### Is every established incompatibility a demand?

No. Incompatibility becomes demand only when resolving it is materially necessary for a bounded consumer relation, sufficiency judgment, admissibility boundary, completion boundary, or proposed movement.

### What additional relation makes an incompatibility materially necessary?

The additional relation is consumer materiality: the incompatibility must bear on continuation, sufficiency, admissibility, completion, or constraint passage for the named consumer or boundary.

### Who owns the comparison?

The consumer-local or boundary-local comparer owns the comparison. The requirement source, current-standing producer, recorder, presentation surface, responsibility bearer, and response selector do not own it by identity.

### Who establishes demand standing?

The demand-establishment boundary establishes demand standing. It may be the same as the comparison boundary only if that boundary also has authority to establish materiality to the consumer and preserve the required limits.

### Is demand a standing, relation, finding, constraint result, or boundary-local combination?

Best recovery: demand is a boundary-local combination that produces standing. It joins a relation/consumer, requirement, current standing, comparison finding, and materiality. In constraint settings it may also be represented as a constraint result.

### When is demand a constraint result?

Demand is a constraint result when a responsible constraint boundary consumes a proposed act or context and produces blocked, refused, narrowed, unsupported, insufficient, or unavailable standing because a materially necessary requirement is unmet.

### Can one consumer receive several independent demands across different dimensions?

Yes. The same consumer may have separate temporal, evidence, authority, capability, communicative, representational, semantic, resource, dependency, or safety demands. Same consumer does not mean one demand.

### Does relation-wide demand imply symmetric responsibility?

No. Relation-wide incidence means the relation is constrained. It does not assign equal responsibility to all parties.

### Is responsibility assignment downstream from demand establishment?

Yes, unless a single warranted boundary explicitly establishes both demand and responsibility. In ordinary recovery, responsibility is downstream and separately warranted.

### What does repeated enum non-uptake establish?

It establishes repeated free-form material, structured enum exposure, and no observed structured uptake under the bounded interaction. It may support current strategy insufficiency or channel-mismatch candidate standing.

### Does the console witness establish shared-grammar demand or only candidate demand?

By itself, only candidate demand. It establishes shared-grammar demand only with an additional warrant that the console is a communication ingress requiring shared representation and that repeated non-uptake is materially incompatible with that bounded relation.

### Does timing provide a clearer demand-establishment witness?

Yes, when the timing requirement, tolerance, consumer, measurement method, and comparison authority are explicit. Otherwise timing is only another possible pressure.

### What later movement remains unauthorized after demand is established?

Responsibility assignment, response selection, execution, mutation, external acquisition, cause attribution, changed-baseline claims, satisfaction claims, and future guarantees remain unauthorized unless separately established.

## Required stop recovery

- Reality may impose external necessity before Seed recognition.
- Seed must establish a bounded consumer/relation, warranted applicable requirement, current standing or material Unknown, comparison authority, incompatibility, materiality, scope, evidence, conflicts, Unknowns, and limits before demand becomes standing.
- Incompatibility differs from demand because incompatibility is a comparison result; demand adds consumer materiality and necessity for continuation, sufficiency, admissibility, completion, or constraint passage.
- Demand is a constraint result only when a constraint boundary consumes a proposed act or context and blocks, refuses, narrows, or otherwise constrains it because a materially necessary condition is unmet.
- Demand dimensions belong wherever the warrant places them: requirement, current standing, comparison method, consumer, incompatibility, or constraint result; they need not be fields on one universal artifact.
- Incidence identifies the constrained relation or consumer; responsibility identifies who has standing or obligation to respond.
- The console witness establishes repeated non-uptake and candidate shared-grammar demand unless communication-ingress materiality is separately warranted; the timing witness establishes demand more cleanly only where tolerance and comparison boundaries are explicit.
- The single remaining boundary is unresolved: what canonical Book boundary, if any, establishes consumer materiality strongly enough to convert an established incompatibility into Seed-owned demand standing without also assigning responsibility or authorizing response?
