# Two-cycle constitutional bootstrap recovery 001

## Scope and restraint

This pass asks whether Seed currently preserves enough constitutional grammar for one bounded inquiry result to orient a later inquiry that was not fully specified in advance. It does not design final intelligence, mandate a universal inquiry pipeline, build alternate realizations, synthesize machine code, or complete future deployment paths.

The answer is bounded by the Book of Seed and implementation witnesses available in this repository. Where evidence is insufficient, this report preserves Unknown rather than completing the grammar by assertion.

## 1. Corrected question-lens-evidence distinctions

The recovered distinction is local and non-collapsing:

- A question binds an inquiry identity, provenance, scope, evidence demand, uncertainty, and lawful stop conditions. It does not establish the answer, authorize unbounded investigation, or convert the operator wording into repository knowledge. See `book_of_seed/04-inquiry-and-examination/questions-and-inquiry.md` and `seed_runtime/bounded_constitutional_question.py`.
- A question may constrain a family of applicable lenses or methods by naming required dimensions and observation boundaries. It does not always force one lens, and some inquiries may require no new lens when already-preserved evidence suffices or lawfully stops the inquiry.
- A lens consumes existing material and exposes, selects, projects, or composes a bounded representation without changing the standing of the represented source material. See `book_of_seed/01-grammar-and-standing/lenses-views-and-roads.md`.
- Evidence identifies source and collection context. It is not fact, answer, sufficiency, or provenance verification merely because it is present, citable, projected, or internally coherent. See `book_of_seed/05-evidence-and-knowledge/evidence-provenance-and-explanation.md`.
- Lens output is neither answer nor evidence sufficiency by default. It may become answer-supporting only under a separate establishment, support, or consumer-local boundary.
- Question applicability, lens applicability, and evidence sufficiency are separate standings.
- Absence within examined evidence is not global nonexistence.

The preserved candidate distinctions therefore stand unless later implementation evidence disproves them:

```text
question != lens
lens != evidence
lens output != answer
lens output != evidence sufficiency
question applicability != lens applicability
lens applicability != evidence sufficiency
evidence absence within a boundary != global absence
```

## 2. How missing required dimensions can produce bounded new evidence

If a question asks about provenance and the examined evidence has no preserved provenance dimensions, the original answer remains Unknown. No lens can recover provenance merely by rearranging material that does not preserve provenance.

But the inquiry has not produced nothing. It may produce a bounded negative finding:

```text
within this examined evidence,
under this question and observation boundary,
the required provenance dimensions are absent or insufficient,
and the answer is not recoverable here
```

That finding is new evidence about the examination boundary and its insufficiency. It can lawfully support later questions about:

- which provenance dimensions were required;
- which responsibility could observe them;
- which act could produce them;
- how they must be preserved;
- which consumer may examine them;
- what standing they would have;
- what remains Unknown.

It must not become the missing provenance, a global absence claim, or an answer to the original provenance question.

## 3. Answer failure versus constitutional inquiry progress

Answer failure means the question's requested answer was not recovered under the preserved evidence and method. Constitutional inquiry progress can still occur when the cycle preserves why recovery failed, what boundary was examined, which dimensions were required, which were absent or insufficient, and what lawful stop follows.

The constitutional difference is:

- `Unknown` can preserve the answer boundary without erasing the inquiry result.
- `insufficient evidence` can preserve a support failure without asserting falsehood.
- `bounded negative finding` can become evidence for a later inquiry without becoming the missing answer.
- `refusal`, `conflict`, `deferment`, `no-selection`, and `lawful inactivity` may also be valid outputs when their standing and limits are preserved.

## 4. Evidence currently supporting a first inquiry cycle

Repository evidence supports a first bounded inquiry cycle in several local forms:

1. Bounded question production exists. `BoundedConstitutionalQuestion` preserves question identity, operator inquiry, inquiry provenance, bounded question, constitutional intent, scope status, uncertainty, Unknowns, caller-supplied fields, testimony status, and read-only/no-ledger/no-mutation boundaries. `produce_bounded_constitutional_question` deterministically constructs that artifact from explicit inputs.
2. Question projection for selection exists, but is narrow. `project_constitutional_question` carries bounded-question identity, exact caller-declared selection keys, and uncertainty/Unknowns into selection. It explicitly performs no semantic matching, natural-language inference, capability discovery, persistence, ledger write, or mutation.
3. Lens-like view selection exists. `select_constitutional_views` compares exact question selection keys with capability keys and preserves unsupported keys and uncertainty, but does not prove semantic bestness, completeness, authority, or downstream sufficiency.
4. Constitutional view composition exists as a read-only composition surface. The Book already warns that composition compatibility is not general selection standing.
5. Examination frontier projection exists. `project_examination_frontier` accepts a bounded inquiry, supplied corpus members, and supplied candidate work, then classifies work as eligible, examined, blocked, unsupported, deferred, failed, Unknown, or conflict. Its boundary notes reject discovery, scheduling, execution, semantic interpretation, recurrence, and mutation claims.
6. Evidence/provenance grammar exists. The Book preserves evidence support binding, provenance applicability, bounded negative findings, correlation/causation separation, attribution/responsibility separation, and observability/provenance separation.
7. Occurrence evidence has been surveyed. The occurrence survey distinguishes live producer-boundary occurrence, artifact construction, event recording, and knowledge extraction, preventing direct construction from masquerading as proof of responsible production.

This supports first-cycle recovery: Seed can apply already-preserved grammar to bounded evidence and produce a local answer, refusal, conflict, bounded Unknown, unsupported classification, or insufficiency finding.

## 5. Evidence currently supporting a second inquiry cycle

Repository evidence supports parts of a second cycle, but does not fully automate it.

Supported second-cycle material:

- Bounded negative findings may preserve insufficiency, support loss, mismatch, comparison pressure, or Unknown standing for further examination.
- Question graph relations allow re-entry as new pressure without mutating the prior artifact, provided the local relation preserves source standing, target standing, crossing evidence or warrant, authority boundary, and surviving limits.
- Inquiry frontiers can preserve bounded, evidenced inquiry possibilities and visible evidence territory without selecting, executing, or answering them.
- Advancement-need selection roads preserve selected subject identity and enough lineage for later inquiry-frontier consumers in the surveyed local road.
- Realization comparison evidence shows that implementation differences can expose compression families without making a realization's native grammar Seed ontology.

What is missing or compressed:

- The current bounded-question producer preserves explicit caller fields only; it does not infer a newly exposed question from a first-cycle insufficiency finding.
- The current question projection selects only exact caller-declared selection keys; it does not derive required dimensions from a finding.
- The examination frontier projects supplied candidate work; it does not discover corpus members, compatible capabilities, or newly required observations.
- The Book preserves re-entry and further examination grammatically, but it does not yet show a general responsible act that converts a first-cycle finding into a later bounded question with preserved required dimensions and standing.

Therefore, second-cycle recurrence is present as constitutional pressure and local relation grammar, but compressed at the point where Seed itself would recognize and form the next question from first-cycle output.

## 7. First-cycle outputs preserving enough orientation for later inquiry

A first-cycle output preserves enough orientation for a lawful later inquiry when it carries at least:

- source inquiry identity;
- provenance or explicit provenance Unknown;
- examined corpus/surface/evidence boundary;
- question form or evidence demand;
- required dimensions for answer recovery;
- method/lens/examination boundary used, or explicit statement that no new lens was used;
- present answer state: answer, refusal, conflict, bounded Unknown, no-selection, deferment, or insufficiency;
- missing, unsupported, unmatched, or conflicting dimensions;
- authority and lawful stop limits;
- whether the output is evidence about insufficiency rather than the missing evidence itself;
- consumer-local limits for reuse;
- standing reason for re-entry or later applicability.

Outputs with those fields can orient later inquiry. Outputs that contain only prose conclusion, display status, answer text, or a generic Unknown do not preserve enough bootstrap leverage.

## 9. How the Book currently preserves or compresses recursive inquiry

Preserved:

- The Book distinguishes question standing from answer standing.
- The Book preserves plural question graph relations and re-entry without requiring one universal sequence.
- The Book distinguishes lenses, roads, uptake, selection, and consumer-local warrant.
- The Book preserves bounded negative findings and Unknowns.
- The Book treats projection and explanation as bounded acts rather than upstream truth.
- The Book treats realization plurality as contrastive Fidelity evidence, not ontology.

Compressed:

- Lens selection can appear answer-engine-like unless lens output, answer, and evidence sufficiency remain separated.
- Projection can appear conclusion-like unless projection output remains distinct from current constitutional standing.
- Iteration can look like lifecycle repetition unless the first result preserves standing, reason, scope, and missing-dimension evidence for the next cycle.

## 12. Smallest evidenced bootstrap threshold

The smallest evidenced threshold is not final intelligence. It is this preservation requirement:

```text
A first-cycle result must preserve the source question identity,
its evidence demand, examined evidence boundary, method or no-method boundary,
required dimensions, missing or insufficient dimensions, provenance/Unknown,
authority limits, answer state, lawful stop, and the standing reason why
that result may orient a later inquiry, while distinguishing the new
insufficiency finding from the missing evidence it still needs.
```

### Explicit answers

#### What must survive the first cycle so that the second cycle can begin lawfully?

The surviving material must include question identity, provenance or provenance Unknown, bounded scope, examined evidence territory, required dimensions, lens/method boundary or no-lens boundary, answer state, missing or insufficient dimensions, authority limits, lawful stop, and a standing reason for later applicability.

#### Can Seed currently preserve that material?

Partially. Seed currently preserves many required pieces in bounded question artifacts, evidence/provenance grammar, bounded negative finding grammar, inquiry-frontier artifacts, selection artifacts, and realization-comparison reports. It does not yet show a general structured artifact that preserves all pieces as a recursive-bootstrap result.

#### Can Seed recognize the next question from it?

Not generally. The Book allows re-entry as new pressure and later questions, but implementation witnesses examined here still rely on caller-supplied bounded fields, selection keys, corpus members, and candidate work. The next-question recognition boundary remains compressed or Unknown.

#### Can Seed distinguish newly created insufficiency evidence from the missing evidence it still needs?

Constitutionally, yes: the Book's bounded negative finding grammar supports the distinction, and this pass made that distinction explicit for provenance absence. Operationally, the distinction is not yet shown as a general two-cycle bootstrap artifact across all inquiry surfaces.

#### What operator knowledge is still being supplied that Seed cannot yet recover for itself?

The operator still supplies the newly exposed distinction, the later question or question form, required-dimension salience, candidate lens/method family, evidence territory, and the judgment that a first-cycle insufficiency finding should seed a later inquiry rather than merely close the first one.

## Final finding

Both cycles are partially recoverable. First-cycle recovery is well supported for bounded questions, read-only projections, frontiers, Unknowns, conflicts, refusals, and bounded negative findings. Second-cycle recurrence is constitutionally present but compressed: Seed preserves grammar by which first-cycle insufficiency may orient later inquiry, but the repository evidence examined here does not yet prove that Seed can generally recognize and form the next question from that result without operator-supplied distinctions.
