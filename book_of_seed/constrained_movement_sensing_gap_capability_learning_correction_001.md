# Constrained Movement Sensing, Gap, Capability, and Learning Correction 001

## Status

Canonical Book correction from merged `main` after PR 1900. This operation changes constitutional orientation only. It does not modify production code, CLI behavior, persistence, projections, selectors, observation sources, capability implementations, handoff artifacts, or runtime behavior.

## Central Finding

PR 1900's distinctions are preserved, but their constitutional grammar is rotated from a topology of possessed things toward constrained movement: a warranted transition in lawful position, standing, or advancement posture. Sensing, remembering, trajectory, need, gap, capability demand, capability standing, interaction outcome, gap revision, capability revision, learning establishment, adaptive reliance, and causal standing are not primarily classes or owned objects. Each names a subject, standing, transition, constraint result, consumer relation, or combination only as warranted by its boundary.

## PR 1900 findings preserved

PR 1900 correctly recovered distinct boundaries for sensing, remembering, observation history, trajectory standing, need, gap, capability demand, capability standing, interaction outcome, gap revision, capability revision, learning establishment, adaptive reliance, and causal standing. This correction preserves those distinctions while refusing to treat the names as a universal object hierarchy.

## Object-oriented bias identified

The remaining risk is reading constitutional subjects as things Seed possesses rather than asking which movement is being constrained. The correction therefore preserves that constitutional subject is not class, standing is not durable object by identity, relation is not wrapper artifact by identity, constraint result is not pipeline stage, movement is not method call, learning is not a `Learning` object, gap family is not subclass hierarchy, interaction is not `Episode` or `Experience` object, and capability standing is not capability object ownership.

Direct answer: Is every constitutional standing best understood as an object? No.

Direct answer: Does every named constitutional subject require a dedicated artifact? No.

## Constitutional movement grammar

Constitutional movement is a warranted transition in lawful position, standing, or advancement posture. Movement may be conceptual, evidentiary, temporal, responsibility-bearing, selection-bearing, authorization-bearing, projection-bound, or externally realized. Movement is not mutation. A changed record, projection, cache, external system, runtime value, or displayed label is not constitutional movement unless the responsible transition preserves warrant, subject, evidence, authority, scope, and limits.

Direct answer: Does constitutional movement require runtime mutation? No.

Direct answer: Does changed standing automatically open later movement? No.

## Constraint grammar

A constraint is not the governed act or movement. A constraint result is not performance of governed movement. A constraint is not a sequential pipeline stage. A constraint may determine whether movement may occur, which movement remains admissible, how far movement may proceed, under which scope movement remains lawful, which standing may be relied upon, and when movement must stop. Passing one constraint does not establish complete authority or complete movement warrant.

Direct answer: Does a constraint result perform the movement it governs? No.

## Sensing and evidentiary movement

1. Current standing: external material may exist outside Seed; Observation testimony may exist only after acquisition and interpretation.
2. Transition considered: external material to bounded Observation testimony, and later testimony to admission, examination, comparison, establishment, projection, or inquiry continuation.
3. Constraint: acquisition method, interpretation boundary, source authority, temporal scope, conflict, and uncertainty.
4. Evidence consumed: provider material, acquisition record, interpretation method, source metadata, and preserved observation dimensions.
5. Lawful result: bounded testimony that may be considered by later evidentiary consumers.
6. Later movement: may admit, narrow, redirect, defer, or leave unchanged later evidentiary movement.
7. Not performed: sensing is not advancement, establishment, admission, current standing, or permission to rely.
8. Consumers: admission, examination, comparison, establishment, current projection, and inquiry-continuation boundaries.
9. Unknowns: which future domains require stronger producer-specific sensing methods.

## Remembering and recoverable movement

1. Current standing: prior testimony or standing may or may not remain recoverable.
2. Transition considered: later reliance, revision, explanation, or movement recovering its constitutive warrant across time.
3. Constraint: preservation sufficiency, provenance, scope, temporal standing, authority, and rebuildability or honest compression.
4. Evidence consumed: retained raw testimony, history, standing, summaries, provenance, and preservation limits.
5. Lawful result: recoverability, non-recoverability, or Unknown.
6. Later movement: may enable, constrain, or refuse later reliance, revision, explanation, stopping, or inquiry.
7. Not performed: remembering is not later reliance, establishment, projection, or automatic warrant survival.
8. Consumers: explanation, revision, selection, stopping, audit, and inquiry consumers.
9. Unknowns: preservation minimum by learning family remains future work.

## Trajectory establishment as constrained movement

Repeated testimony may support trajectory examination, but observation history is not trajectory establishment. Trajectory establishment is constrained movement from occurrence history to bounded temporal understanding when series identity, temporal authority, scope, comparison method, continuity, missing testimony, conflict, sufficiency, transition vocabulary, and uncertainty warrant the transition. Count increased is not temporal understanding moved, and trajectory standing is not causal standing.

Direct answer: Can trajectory establishment be understood as movement in temporal understanding? Yes, when bounded by the temporal examination conditions above.

## Need and gap movement constraints

Need and gap discipline possible advancement without opening it. A need may characterize what advancement may require. A scoped gap may characterize boundary-local insufficiency or a constraint result relative to a declared reference condition. Neither admits movement by itself.

Direct answer: Does an established gap itself perform movement? No.

## Gap-family boundary cross-examination

The named gap families are not one required subclass hierarchy. They are boundary-local insufficiency or constraint results when warranted:

- Evidence gap: constrains establishment movement at the evidence boundary; consumes testimony/support standing; may require more evidence or preserve Unknown.
- Knowledge gap: constrains answer or explanation movement at the inquiry boundary; consumes question standing and admitted evidence; may continue inquiry or refuse an answer.
- Outcome gap: constrains advancement relative to purpose at the outcome boundary; consumes purpose/gap/demand and resulting testimony; may support later gap revision or stopping.
- Capability gap: constrains possible-realization movement at the capability boundary; consumes capability demand and capability standing; may require capability inquiry.
- Reachability gap: constrains present route movement at the route boundary; consumes route, dependency, authority, and environment standing; may redirect or defer.
- Dependency gap: constrains prerequisite-satisfied movement at the dependency boundary; consumes prerequisite standing; may block or narrow candidate realization.
- Authority gap: constrains lawful-use movement at the authority boundary; consumes scope and authorization standing; may refuse use despite technical possibility.
- Representation gap: constrains translation or handoff movement at the representation boundary; consumes grammar and compatibility testimony; may require translation inquiry.
- Resource or budget gap: constrains feasible movement within budget at the resource boundary; consumes budget, cost, and resource testimony; may narrow or stop.

Direct answer: Is gap primarily one universal object family? Not by default; the Book treats named gaps as distinct boundary-local insufficiency or constraint results unless later evidence warrants a shared implementation family.

## Capability demand and constrained candidate formation

Capability demand constrains the transformation that may be sought. Established insufficiency may support a bounded transformation requirement, but capability demand established is not capability exists, not mechanism selected, and not movement authorized. It narrows candidate formation, realization inquiry, representation inquiry, and dependency inquiry.

## Capability standing and possible movement

Capability standing constrains possible realization movement by asking which transformations remain supportably possible under which conditions and scope. It may admit or exclude a candidate, narrow applicability, preserve Unknown, expose conflict, redirect inquiry, or require further evidence. It does not select, authorize, hand off, realize, or warrant reliance by itself.

Direct answer: Does capability standing itself select movement? No.

## Gap revision as movement in insufficiency standing

Gap revision is constrained movement in insufficiency standing when prior scoped insufficiency, new evidence, and comparison against the declared reference condition warrant a revised result: remains, narrows, widens, changes family, becomes unsupported, becomes Unknown, or resolves within scope. It is not movement authorization, and resolution within scope is not advancement complete.

Direct answer: Can gap revision be understood as movement in insufficiency standing? Yes, within the bounded evidence, reference condition, comparison method, scope, and Unknowns.

## Capability revision as movement in realization standing

Capability revision is constrained movement in possible-realization standing when prior capability standing, new behavioral, observational, measurement, or interaction evidence, and bounded examination warrant a revised realization standing. One success is not universal capability standing and one failure is not capability absence.

Direct answer: Can capability revision be understood as movement in realization standing? Yes, as bounded movement in possible-realization standing, not as mechanism selection or authorization.

## Interaction as an evidentiary road

Interaction standing is best understood as an evidentiary road or relation among separately preserved standings: prior lawful position, selected movement, selection warrant, authority, handoff, reported realization/refusal/non-occurrence, resulting testimony, and outcome comparison. It need not be forced into an Episode or Experience object. Selection is not realization, handoff is not realization, realization report is not verified effect, and after-observation is not attributed result.

## Outcome evaluation and later standing movement

Outcome evaluation constrains later standing movement. Resulting testimony plus declared purpose, gap, or capability demand plus comparison method may establish a bounded outcome finding. That finding may constrain gap standing, capability standing, stopping, further inquiry, or later candidate consideration. It does not automatically revise every standing, prove causal standing, or make completed identical to successful movement.

## Learning establishment as movement in understanding

Learning establishment can be understood as constrained constitutional movement in retained understanding when prior standing, admitted prior evidence, bounded examination or comparison, sufficiency, and warrant establish revised bounded standing. The revised subject may concern condition, trajectory, gap, capability, reachability, dependency, authority, representation compatibility, resource feasibility, expectation, or strategy. This is not storage mutation, projection replacement, new current value, adaptive reliance, or a universal Learning object.

Direct answer: Can learning establishment be understood as constrained movement in retained understanding? Yes, when it is the warranted establishment of revised retained understanding for a bounded subject; no universal learning artifact or slogan is required.

## Adaptive reliance as movement constrained by learned standing

Adaptive reliance is later movement constrained by the result of earlier movement in understanding. The topology is prior evidence to constrained movement in understanding to revised standing to later reliance warrant to later movement narrowed or redirected. It may constrain inquiry continuation, candidate consideration, selection, handoff, stopping, resource allocation, or explanation. Revised standing exists does not mean later movement occurred, later selection changed does not prove learned standing was consumed, and adaptive reliance is not automatic execution.

Direct answer: Is adaptive reliance identical to learning establishment? No.

Direct answer: Does adaptive reliance describe later movement constrained by revised standing? Yes, when the consumer exposes the relied-upon standing, warrant, applicable purpose, scope, and remaining Unknowns.

## Causal establishment as constrained movement

Causal establishment is constrained movement from sequence or association toward bounded intervention standing. It is stronger than movement followed by outcome, gap reduced, capability standing revised, or association repeated. It consumes intervention identity, before/after binding, alternative-cause examination, temporal relation, criteria, counterevidence, repeatability, scope, and uncertainty. It may warrant later intervention, explanation, selection, or stopping that association alone may not.

## Stopping implications

Evidence gap, capability Unknown, authority gap, resource insufficiency, unresolved causation, or preservation failure may require stop, defer, narrow, return to inquiry, expose insufficiency, or refuse reliance. Stopping is not failure, and Unknown is not permission to invent movement.
