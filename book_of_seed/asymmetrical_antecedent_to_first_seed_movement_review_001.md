# Asymmetrical Antecedent to First Seed Movement Review 001

## Status

Report-only canonical investigation. No production code, tests, CLI behavior, persistence, projections, selectors, concordance, unresolved questions, or canonical Book chapters are amended.

## Question

What constitutional relation connects an antecedent occurrence outside Seed to Seed's first lawful uptake without making Seed responsible for the antecedent, its production, its complete history, or its meaning?

The supporting correction question is whether the historical sentence:

```text
There is always a prior observation.
```

was preserving a required prior Seed `Observation`, or a weaker asymmetrical condition: Seed's first owned movement responds to something not Seed-owned.

## Evidence basis

This review reads the Book as already separating external material, testimony, addressability, question formation, observation production, evidence, Fact standing, movement, projection, capability, handoff, refusal, and Unknowns.

Key recovered evidence:

- External material may become constitutionally addressable without becoming Seed constitutional grammar, adopted law, repository authority, or truth. Addressability must preserve source role or Unknown source role, provenance or Unknown provenance, source grammar boundary, referenced scope, uncertainty, confidence limits, and authority limits.
- Cross-seam consumption is lawful only when the limits that made the material addressable travel with the consumer boundary.
- Question-shaped external material is not an internal question. Operator material may become attributed testimony, pressure, goals, constraints, corrections, or responses; Seed owns the internal bounded question-forming act.
- Constitutional movement is a warranted transition in lawful position, standing, or advancement posture, not runtime mutation by identity.
- Testimony and Fact standing remain distinct. A recorded claim or evidence record can be consumed as premise-relative input without proving the testified content, producer occurrence, or stronger standing.
- Provenance represented, provenance verified, producer occurrence, causation, responsibility, and complete lineage are separate standings.
- Sensing produces Seed-native testimony after acquisition and interpretation; provider material is not itself an Observation.
- PR 1905's report recovered `Getting off Null` as historical shorthand for the first warranted transition from no presently bounded subject to bounded addressable external material whose Unknowns are preserved.

## Finding

The Book does not support a doctrine that every first Seed-owned movement requires a prior Seed `Observation`. It supports a weaker asymmetrical relation:

```text
antecedent outside Seed
↓
possible external carrier, operator act, or environment route
↓
bounded crossing or present condition at a Seed-handled boundary
↓
Seed-owned preservation sufficient for addressability
↓
possible Seed-owned question formation
```

The antecedent is constitutionally relevant because first addressability cannot be self-originating at `Null`: Seed must have some material, condition, reference, pressure, or seam condition to which a Seed-owned boundary can respond. But the antecedent is not imported as Seed responsibility, Seed knowledge, Seed Observation, full provenance, authorization, or meaning. The relation is responsive and asymmetric, not causal-origin ownership.

## Operator roles without collapse

The operator may be several roles at once or only one role in a given crossing:

| Operator role | What it may contribute | What it does not establish |
|---|---|---|
| Observer | Testimony that the operator observed or encountered X | X is true, complete, or Seed-observed |
| Interpreter | Attributed interpretation, vocabulary, uncertainty, or context | Seed inherits the interpretation or grammar |
| Inquirer | Inquiry pressure, goals, corrections, historical motivation | Seed's internal question already exists |
| Experimenter | Artifacts, results, claims about trials, uncertainty | Full prehistory, validity, or causal explanation |
| Capability supplier | A supplied mechanism or route as external/implementation material | Seed-native capability, selection, authority, or execution |
| Authority source | Local permission or scope statement | Universal authority or mandatory continuation |
| Material carrier | File, literal, transcript, command text, or reference | Ownership of later Seed inquiry |
| External mover | The external party whose act supplies a condition to which Seed may respond | Seed responsibility for the act or its causes |
| Testimony producer | Bounded source-relative assertions | Fact standing unless separately warranted |

At Seed ingress, operator-supplied material can establish no more than its bounded presence, attribution where preserved, scope, available provenance or provenance Unknown, source grammar boundary, uncertainty, authority limits, and stop conditions. It can also supply inquiry pressure, but only after Seed-side bounded translation can an internal question be formed.

## Immediate crossing versus complete prehistory

The proposed sufficiency boundary is Book-compatible when stated narrowly:

```text
operator or environment supplied material M
at boundary B
under scope S
with provenance P or provenance Unknown
```

That immediate crossing can be sufficient for first addressability if `M`, `B`, and `S` are bounded enough to prevent invention or erasure. The following may remain Unknown without blocking addressability by identity:

- why the operator selected `M`;
- what earlier observation motivated it;
- whether `M` was copied, generated, transformed, or selected from a larger set;
- whether the operator interpreted it correctly;
- whether `M` is complete;
- what external occurrence ultimately caused it.

Those Unknowns may block stronger standing, such as truth, provenance verification, causation, responsibility, Fact standing, capability standing, or answer formation. They do not automatically block first addressability if the immediate crossing is honest and bounded.

## Getting off Null relation

PR 1905's candidate remains intact if `Getting off Null` is kept narrow:

```text
Getting off Null
=
first Seed-owned movement into addressable material
```

The antecedent occurs before that movement as a non-Seed-owned condition, occurrence, carrier act, or crossing pressure. The crossing is part of `Getting off Null` only at the Seed-owned preservation/addressability side, not as the external production or full route. Addressability does not require testimony that explains the whole crossing. It requires enough preserved standing to say, without invention:

```text
material M is now present or referenced at boundary B
under scope S
with source/provenance P or Unknown
and with authority, uncertainty, grammar, completeness, and stop limits preserved
```

Seed can get off Null when that is the only supported claim, provided the material identity and received scope are bounded. If even those cannot be bounded, Seed remains at Null or stops without failure.

## Event, occurrence, and observation distinctions

| Term | Distinction |
|---|---|
| occurrence | Something happened or obtained, possibly outside Seed and possibly unrecorded |
| event artifact | A record object that asserts an occurrence or claim |
| event recording | The act or result of preserving an event artifact |
| external event | An outside occurrence or outside record, not Seed-owned by identity |
| operator-observed occurrence | What the operator reports or is understood to have observed |
| operator-reported occurrence | Testimony that the operator reported an occurrence |
| Seed Observation | Seed-native testimony formed after acquisition and interpretation under preserved source, method, scope, time, conflict, authority, and uncertainty limits |

`There is always a prior event` is safer than `prior observation` only if `event` means informal occurrence. It is too strong if it implies an event artifact, event recording, verified occurrence, event-ledger row, or canonical Event standing. The narrower phrase should avoid both `Observation` and `Event` as required constitutional kinds.

## Constitutional asymmetry

The Book supports these claims when bounded:

```text
the world may already be in motion before Seed participates
```

and:

```text
Seed's first movement is responsive, not self-originating
```

But the following do not follow:

```text
responsive != causally determined
antecedent != authorization
external pressure != opened Seed movement
material supplied != material admitted
first response != mandatory continuation
```

The asymmetry is constitutional rather than metaphysical: Seed does not produce its own first subject from Null, but Seed also does not inherit responsibility for what preceded the boundary.

## Inquiry inheritance

Operator inquiry may lawfully cross as question-shaped testimony, observations, conclusions, uncertainty, goals, corrections, artifacts, or pressure. It crosses as attributed external material and limits, not by identity as Seed's internal inquiry. The lawful topology remains:

```text
operator inquiry pressure
↓
Seed-side bounded interpretation
↓
Seed-owned question formation
```

The operator's earlier inquiry can motivate, supply, or shape material without owning Seed's later inquiry, selecting its answer, or granting unbounded authority.

## Direct answers

### Does every first Seed-owned movement require a prior Seed Observation?

No. A prior Seed `Observation` is not required. Seed Observation is downstream of acquisition and interpretation; first movement may be addressability of external material before any Seed Observation exists.

### What was `there is always a prior observation` most likely trying to preserve?

It most likely tried to preserve asymmetry: Seed's first owned movement is responsive to a non-Seed-owned antecedent rather than self-originating at Null. It also likely preserved that the operator's historical inquiry included observation, but not that a Seed Observation is constitutionally prerequisite.

### Is `observation` too strong for the antecedent?

Yes. `Observation` is too strong if it means Seed-native Observation, and `observation` is still too strong if it implies that every antecedent must have been observed rather than supplied, typed, generated, handed off, started, or merely present as a bounded condition.

### Is `event` also too strong?

Yes, if `event` implies an event artifact, ledger recording, verified occurrence, or canonical Event standing. It is tolerable only as loose historical prose for an occurrence, and even then `condition` or `crossing` is narrower.

### What is the narrowest constitutional description of the antecedent?

A non-Seed-owned antecedent condition or crossing that supplies bounded material or pressure to which Seed can respond by preserving an addressable subject with limits.

### Must the antecedent be claim-bearing?

No. Raw external material can become addressable without claim-bearing testimony. Claim-bearing standing begins only where a source-relative assertion, observation, diagnostic finding, or claim payload is bounded.

### Must the antecedent's source or full history be known?

No. Source role and provenance may be preserved as Unknown. Complete prehistory is not required and must not be invented.

### Can the immediate crossing be sufficient while deeper provenance remains Unknown?

Yes. If material identity, boundary, scope, and limits are preserved honestly, first addressability can occur while motive, generation route, complete lineage, ultimate cause, and correctness remain Unknown.

### Can the operator act as the first external observer without their observation becoming a Seed Observation?

Yes. The operator can report observing X, and Seed may preserve that as attributed testimony. It becomes Seed Observation only if Seed later produces Observation testimony through its own acquisition and interpretation boundary.

### Can the operator's prior inquiry supply pressure without becoming Seed's internal inquiry?

Yes. Operator inquiry pressure may be consumed through bounded translation. Seed owns any internal question formed from it.

### Is Seed's first movement necessarily responsive to an external antecedent?

Yes, under the narrow `Getting off Null` reading: the first Seed-owned addressability movement responds to a non-Seed-owned antecedent condition or crossing. This does not establish causal determination, authorization, or mandatory continuation.

### Does a Seed process exist constitutionally before addressability?

A process may physically run before addressability, but runtime existence is not constitutional movement or a bounded addressed subject by identity. Constitutionally, the first clear movement is addressability establishment.

### How do physical birth, material arrival, and constitutional movement differ?

Physical birth is process start. Material arrival is material becoming available at a seam. Constitutional movement is the warranted transition that preserves material and limits sufficiently for addressability or later inquiry. None collapses into the others.

### Is the first external movement always outside Seed?

The antecedent's production is outside Seed by definition. The Seed-owned response begins only at the preservation/addressability boundary. The exact status of physical ingress remains the unresolved boundary.

### What exact standing must be preserved at the first crossing?

At minimum: bounded literal or reference identity of `M`, boundary `B`, received scope `S`, source role or Unknown, provenance or Unknown, source/external grammar boundary, uncertainty, confidence and authority limits, completeness limits or Unknown, temporal standing if preserved, and lawful stop conditions.

### Does this finding alter PR 1905's definition of Getting off Null?

No. It clarifies the antecedent side. `Getting off Null` remains first Seed-owned movement into addressable material; the antecedent is before it and not owned by Seed.
