# Fidelity Production Ownership Correction 001

## Scope and negative authority

This correction is bounded to the active Book of Seed and to the production-ownership defect left after PR 1929. It changes only the active Book I external-versus-constitutional grammar chapter, this correction record, and PR 1929 correction testimony. It does not modify implementation, tests, CLI behavior, events, projections, archived documents, historical reports, unrelated active clauses, Book numbering, PolicyGate or policy/precondition anchoring, operational-realization implementation families, `seed_runtime/execution_proposals.py::ExecutionProposal`, or other stale implementation anchors.

This record is correction testimony. It does not create a Fidelity ontology, engine, schema, artifact family, registry, score, diagnostic, runtime behavior, public audit, automatic correction authority, or proof of external effect.

## Established producer-versus-consumer defect

PR 1929 correctly excised Book VII, but its testimony overclaimed when it said Book I already owned general Fidelity determination because Book I required cross-seam consumers to preserve limits supplied by a Fidelity finding.

Consumption of a Fidelity finding does not establish responsibility for producing that finding.

The preserved defect is:

```text
consumer preserves limits supplied by Fidelity finding
!=
bounded comparison produces Fidelity finding
```

The correction preserves:

```text
consumer accepts or preserves an artifact
!=
responsibility for producing its standing
```

## Candidate owners examined

### External and constitutional grammar

Book I's external-versus-constitutional grammar chapter governs the boundary where external or implementation grammar is compared against Seed constitutional grammar before external distinctions may be constitutionally relied upon. Fidelity production has this more specific constitutional subject: bounded comparison between a constitutional grammar expectation and an implementation witness, with source, scope, authority, evidence, provenance, invariants, conflicts, and Unknowns preserved. This chapter is therefore the coherent active owner for the general production boundary.

### Examination methods and probes

Book IV's examination chapter governs bounded examination, relevance, lawful inactivity, and cross-examination. Fidelity production uses bounded examination and comparison discipline, but its subject is not merely method selection or generic probe output. Its subject is the constitutional comparison of an implementation witness against constitutional grammar and bounded expectation. Book IV remains a related examination discipline, not the owner of the general Fidelity-production boundary.

### Constitutional kinds and artifact standing

Book I's artifact-standing chapter distinguishes represented shape from lawfully produced standing. It preserves that a Fidelity-shaped artifact may represent a finding without proving that the required comparison occurred. A Fidelity-shaped artifact does not prove that the bounded comparison responsibility occurred. This chapter prevents artifact/occurrence confusion but is not the production owner merely because a finding can be represented as an artifact.

## Fidelity constitutional subject

The constitutional subject of Fidelity production is the bounded comparison between Seed constitutional grammar and an implementation witness under a bounded expectation. The comparison asks whether, inside the examined seam or scope, the witness preserves or crosses the constitutional boundary being tested.

## Production boundary

The active production boundary is now `01.External.D — Fidelity production through bounded constitutional comparison` in Book I's external-versus-constitutional grammar chapter. It requires a bounded comparison that brings constitutional grammar, bounded expectation, and implementation witness under a declared examined seam or scope, preserves the required evidence and authority limits, and stops at a qualitative finding inside that scope.

## Consumer boundary

The active consumer boundary remains `01.External.C — Cross-seam consumption preserves refusal conditions`. It governs a different responsibility: a downstream constitutional responsibility consuming addressable or translated cross-seam material must carry forward the limits supplied by an already-produced Fidelity finding. It does not produce the finding, does not establish the finding's occurrence, and does not become the authority owner of the finding.

## Selected owner or preserved Unknown

The selected owner is Book I's external-versus-constitutional grammar chapter because Fidelity production specifically compares implementation witness against Seed constitutional grammar under bounded expectation. Production ownership is no longer Unknown at the general boundary. Particular implementation-specific Fidelity remains Unknown until separately examined under the new production boundary.

5. **Is Fidelity production identical to bounded examination?** No. It uses bounded examination discipline, but its subject is the specific external-versus-constitutional comparison of implementation witness against constitutional grammar.
6. **Is Fidelity production identical to external-grammar translation?** No. Translation can make external grammar addressable for use; Fidelity production compares implementation witness against constitutional grammar under a bounded expectation.
7. **Is consuming a Fidelity finding the same responsibility as producing it?** No. Consumption preserves limits supplied by an already-produced finding; production creates the finding through bounded comparison.
8. **Did Book I already own Fidelity production before this correction?** No. Before this correction, Book I evidenced cross-seam consumption of Fidelity limits, not explicit production ownership.
9. **Where does Fidelity production now belong?** It belongs in Book I's external-versus-constitutional grammar chapter at `01.External.D`.
10. **Was a new active clause required?** Yes. A new active production clause was required to avoid treating consumer responsibility as producer ownership.
11. **If no owner was warranted, what remains Unknown?** An owner was warranted for the general production boundary; particular implementation-specific Fidelity findings remain Unknown until separately examined.
12. **Was Book VII restored?** No.
13. **Was any implementation changed?** No.
14. **Was any Fidelity engine, registry, score, or diagnostic introduced?** No.
15. **What unrelated residues remain outside scope?** `seed_runtime/execution_proposals.py::ExecutionProposal`, PolicyGate or policy/precondition anchoring, operational-realization implementation families, other stale implementation anchors, and Book numbering remain outside scope.
16. **Where must this correction stop?** It must stop at the active Book I production clause, PR 1929 testimony correction, this correction record, and verification that Book VII remains absent.
