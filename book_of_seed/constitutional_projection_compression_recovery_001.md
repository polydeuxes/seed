## Existing grammar recovered

The Book already distributes most of the required grammar across several districts rather than assigning it to one chapter.

### Projection, view, and lens standing

Recovered grammar:

- A lens consumes existing material and exposes, selects, projects, or composes a bounded representation without changing source standing.
- A lens assertion is about what supplied sources expose under a declared method, warranted by source identity and faithful transformation.
- A projection is a recoverable view shaped by replay scope and projection rules.
- Projected material may support later standing only through a responsible consumer boundary.
- A view or cache is not a new constitutional source of law and is not standing by identity.
- Projection, diagnostic, audit, and inventory surfaces expose visibility only inside recorded scope, inputs, freshness, shape, and mutation boundary.
- Existence and output of a surface can evidence that the bounded surface exists or reported what it reported, but cannot by identity establish source truth, Book law, complete corpus coverage, implementation readiness, ownership, current standing, or mutation authority.

This preserves:

```text
source standing != projected standing
projection != source duplication
view != producer occurrence proof
view != reusable authority
projection output != evidence sufficiency
```

### Evidence, testimony, and preserved limits

Recovered grammar:

- Observations and specialized testimony preserve attributed claims.
- Testimony may be consumed as attributed or premise-relative input for bounded examination.
- That consumption preserves attribution, source-relative limits, conflicts, and uncertainty.
- Testimony does not establish the testified content as fact, prove source producer occurrence, or strengthen standing through repetition or copied lineage.
- Evidence support binding requires the consumer to distinguish evidence identity from material existence and to represent source attribution, evidence kind, collection context, bounded subject, supported claim, applicable authority boundary, preservation horizon, confidence or uncertainty where used, and required provenance relation.
- Provenance representation, applicability, internal coherence, independent verification, verified causation, and producer occurrence are separate standings.
- A missing, unmatched, unsupported, or unpreserved support result may be stated only as a bounded negative finding inside the surface, corpus, query or claim form, temporal boundary, authority boundary, and limitations that produced it.

This preserves:

```text
no testimony != negative testimony
not preserved != false
not found in a bounded surface != does not exist
producer-local standing != preserved producer occurrence proof
represented lineage != verified provenance
```

### Applicability, admission, consumption, and reliance

Recovered grammar:

- Applicability, admission, and consumption are distinct constitutional standings.
- Available upstream material may be applicable, inapplicable, Unknown, or conflicting for a consumer boundary.
- Applicable material does not automatically become admitted.
- Admission-boundary consumption does not mean the final target consumer consumed the material.
- Responsible consumer use of admitted material may produce a new consumer-local assertion or standing without re-establishing upstream standing, proving upstream producer occurrence, or expanding authority.
- A consumer may refuse or preserve Unknown at a crossing.
- Handoff structure is not automatically assertion uptake or a constitutional road.
- Uptake begins only where a consumer gives transported material bounded constitutional use or standing, or makes an assertion materially warranted by it.

This preserves:

```text
producer-local standing != handoff testimony
handoff testimony != consumer-local standing
consumer-local standing != renewed producer standing
consumer acceptance != upstream admission renewal
consumer-local reliance != reusable authority
```

### Compression, representation, and handoff

Recovered grammar:

- Egress representation can make a bounded result operator-facing or external only by preserving result identity, source inquiry or subject, evidence territory, provenance, authority scope, confidence or uncertainty, Unknowns, conflicts, negative authority, refusal conditions, neighboring-inquiry boundaries, and stop/completion limits.
- The outward representation receives presentation or communication standing only.
- Compression, formatting, translation, citation, report inclusion, API or CLI emission, and explanation are lawful only while the limits that made the result lawful remain visible or explicitly preserved.
- Cross-seam consumption preserves refusal conditions and must not erase limits, invent missing source or authority, mutate external material into Seed truth, or relocate authority.

This already implies that compact representation need not duplicate every source detail, but may not erase the distinctions that condition lawful use.

## Places where view was compressed into presentation

The Book mostly prevents this collapse, especially through `06.Projection.A` and `08.Handoff.C`. Still, the recovery pressure exposed sparse wording around Seed-consuming views. The existing projection clause emphasizes visibility and diagnostic surfaces. The handoff clause emphasizes operator-facing or external representation. Together they cover much of the question, but neither clause previously stated in one compact place that a projection may be an internally consumed bounded artifact whose lawful compression is purpose-relative.

The risk was not that the Book declared views presentation-only. It did not. The risk was that the available compact clauses could be read as over-weighting presentation, visibility, cache, and diagnostic display while leaving internal consumer reliance and package-formation standing implicit.

## Lossless-compression principle recovered

The Book warrants a purpose-relative principle, with a minor correction added below:

```text
A constitutional projection is lossless for a declared bounded purpose when it preserves the distinctions needed by its lawful consumer to recover the exact bounded standing the consumer is permitted to rely on, without reopening the producer, inventing missing standing, strengthening the upstream claim, or erasing limits that condition lawful use.
```

This is not a universal field list and not source duplication. Losslessness is constitutional, not byte-level or object-level. It is relative to the consumer, purpose, source standing, authority boundary, evidence demand, and lawful stop conditions.

### Limits of the principle

A projection can be lossless for one bounded purpose and insufficient for another. A view that preserves enough material for a display summary may be lossy for a downstream responsibility that must distinguish unavailable testimony from empty testimony. A handoff that preserves enough to prove that a report said something may be lossy for reliance on the reported content. A read model that preserves current standing for one subject may be lossy for reconstructing producer occurrence or full provenance depth.

Losslessness does not mean:

```text
preserves every implementation detail
preserves every upstream detail
preserves private helper structure
preserves transport mechanics
proves producer occurrence
supplies universal authority
makes all consumers safe
```

Losslessness does mean that the consumer-required distinctions survive in a form the consumer may lawfully use without reopening or re-performing the producer.

## Purpose-relative preservation

The Book supports purpose-relative preservation rather than mandatory universal fields.

A projection may lawfully omit, when irrelevant to the declared consumer purpose:

- implementation-local object shape;
- private helper structure;
- irrelevant candidate details;
- unused provenance depth;
- transport mechanics;
- display or presentation details;
- source details not required by the consumer's lawful reliance.

A projection may need to preserve some bounded subset of:

- subject identity;
- scope or purpose;
- produced standing;
- basis sufficient for the consumer's reliance;
- source attribution;
- evidence boundary;
- provenance or provenance Unknown;
- authority scope;
- confidence or uncertainty;
- Unknowns;
- conflicts;
- refusal or non-performance conditions;
- known loss;
- package existence, availability, cardinality, and completeness standing;
- negative authority;
- freshness and mutation boundary;
- lawful stop or completion limits.

Which subset is required is determined by the lawful consumer boundary and declared purpose, not by a universal projection schema.

## Package formation standing

Existing Book grammar clearly distinguishes many adjacent standings, but it did not compactly name package formation standing before this pass. The added clause recovers the minimal constitutional boundary without inventing a universal `Package` ontology.

Recovered standing:

- The responsibility that forms a bounded package, projection, view, set, handoff, or selected collection owns the standing of that formed input within its declared boundary.
- That responsibility establishes, where the declared purpose requires it, whether the bounded input exists, is available, is populated or empty, has known cardinality, is complete enough, is incomplete, preserves Unknown standing, preserves conflict standing, or preserves known loss.
- This may be realized through an object, query, relation, view, serialized handoff, distributed testimony, shell/process output, or another lawful form.
- The constitutional distinction is not the form. The distinction is whether formation standing and its limits survive for the consumer that relies on the formed input.

This preserves:

```text
package standing != package contents
package existence != package completeness
package cardinality != aggregate result
empty != unavailable
empty != incomplete
empty != Unknown
empty != conflict
```

## Package contents and cardinality standing

The Book supports separating contents from the standing of the container or formed input.

Recovered grammar:

- Contents are the supplied items that a later responsibility may inspect or aggregate.
- Cardinality is standing about how many contents were supplied or known under the formation boundary.
- Population is standing that the package has one or more contents under the declared boundary.
- Empty is standing that the formation responsibility produced no contents within its declared boundary.
- Empty is not unavailability, incompleteness, Unknown, null, omission, conflict, or negative testimony by identity.
- Omitted, empty, explicit null, missing, Unknown, unavailable, delayed, failed, partial, and conflicting are distinct unless a local warranted rule intentionally relates them.

The Book does not establish a universal list of package states, field names, or lifecycle transitions. It establishes that distinctions needed for downstream lawful reliance must not be collapsed by formation or handoff.

## Aggregation standing

The Book supports the following bounded grammar, now made compact in `06.Projection.B`:

```text
package formation
→ package standing

content collection
→ bounded content set

aggregation
→ judgment over that content set
```

These may be separate local responsibilities even if one implementation expression performs them together.

An aggregate claim generally requires both:

1. standing of the input package or formed content set; and
2. the aggregation result over the contents actually supplied.

Recovered contrastive outcomes:

- A populated package where every contribution says `No` may support aggregate `No`, if the local aggregate rule is warranted and the input package standing is sufficient.
- A populated package with mixed testimony may support Unknown, conflict, or another locally defined result, if the rule preserves its basis and limits.
- An empty package supports empty-package standing. It does not automatically support aggregate `No`.
- An unavailable package supports unavailable standing. It is not empty.
- An incomplete package supports incomplete standing. It is not a complete aggregate result.

The Book does not prescribe a universal aggregate state machine. It requires the local responsibility not to manufacture package standing from iteration behavior or erase the formation boundary.

## Empty / unavailable / incomplete / Unknown / conflict distinctions

The Book already provides adjacent authority through Unknown preservation, bounded negative findings, refusal/non-performance, applicability/admission/consumption, evidence support binding, and handoff preservation. The recovery clarifies their projection/package consequence.

Distinctions preserved:

```text
empty != unavailable
empty != incomplete
empty != Unknown
empty != conflict
empty != null automatically
empty != omitted
null != Unknown automatically
missing message != negative message
no matching row != no event globally
successful command with empty output != populated negative testimony
```

An empty input can be a legitimate result of a formation responsibility, but only when that responsibility has standing to say the input exists and contains no items under its boundary. An empty iteration in an implementation language is not enough by itself.

## No testimony / negative testimony distinction

Existing Book grammar strongly preserves this distinction.

- `05.Evidence.C` prevents not-found, unsupported, or unpreserved results from becoming falsehood, disproof, global nonexistence, operational absence, no-authority, or fact without separate authority.
- `05.Testimony.A` prevents testimony repetition or copied lineage from strengthening standing.
- The lens chapter prevents a lens from manufacturing missing provenance, authority, occurrence, or standing.

Recovered distinction:

```text
no testimony != negative testimony
no supporting item != all items oppose
all known items say No != no known items exist
bounded absence != global absence
not recoverable from this material != false
```

A negative aggregate requires negative authority in the contents, plus sufficient package standing for the question being answered. Absence of contents is only absence or empty standing unless a separate local rule, with warrant and limits, makes a stronger negative conclusion lawful.

## Producer / handoff / consumer standing

Recovered grammar:

- Producer-local standing is what the producing responsibility lawfully established within its own boundary.
- A projection or package may carry handoff testimony about that producer-local standing, but handoff testimony is not automatically proof that the producer occurred or that the producer's standing is renewed.
- Consumer-local reliance begins only when the consumer has prior standing to consume the upstream material and the preserved evidence, warrant, identity, scope, authority, confidence, and Unknown limits are sufficient for that consumer boundary.
- The consumer may produce a new local assertion or standing, but it must not strengthen upstream claims, expand authority, or silently adopt producer standing beyond the declared purpose.

This preserves:

```text
producer-local standing != handoff testimony
handoff testimony != consumer-local standing
consumer-local standing != renewed producer standing
consumer use != producer standing reproduction
```

## Realization-asymmetry findings

These findings are contrastive only. They do not canonize any realization language.

### Python

Compression risks:

- `all([]) == True` can turn no supplied contents into an apparent universal affirmative or universal negative, depending on predicate framing.
- Empty iterables can be indistinguishable from unavailable or uncomputed inputs if formation standing is not preserved separately.
- `None`, empty lists, empty strings, omitted optional values, and Unknown labels can collapse unless explicitly represented.

Constitutional distinction tested: aggregation over supplied contents must not manufacture package existence, completeness, or negative testimony from iteration behavior.

### SQL

Compression risks:

- No rows, `NULL`, `COUNT = 0`, aggregates returning `NULL`, and vacuous `NOT EXISTS` predicates can each express different realization conditions.
- Joins can erase dangling references or unavailable related material if inner joins are used where absence must remain visible.
- Referential integrity can prove row existence without proving applicability, support, provenance verification, producer occurrence, or truth.

Constitutional distinction tested: row absence, null, empty result set, dangling reference, and negative predicate result are not constitutional equivalents unless local warrant says so.

### Serialized JSON or similar representation

Compression risks:

- Missing field, explicit `null`, empty array, omitted default, schema-version mismatch, and stale encoded shape can be accidentally normalized.
- A compact payload can preserve presentation while losing uncertainty, refusal, known loss, negative authority, or formation completeness.
- Consumer defaults can invent standing not present in the serialized handoff.

Constitutional distinction tested: transport compactness is lawful only if purpose-required limits and formation standing survive in the representation or are explicitly preserved by the handoff boundary.

### Distributed/message-based realization

Compression risks:

- No message, delayed message, empty response, unavailable producer, partial response set, timeout, duplicate message, and conflicting message can collapse into the same consumer observation.
- A consumer may mistake delivery, acknowledgment, or absence of a message for receipt, understanding, reliance, nonexistence, refusal, or negative testimony.
- Partial response aggregation can silently appear complete.

Constitutional distinction tested: message behavior cannot replace package availability, completeness, producer occurrence, or consumer-local reliance standing.

### Shell/process realization

Compression risks:

- Empty stdout, successful exit status, failed command, no matching rows, pipeline exit semantics, truncation, and stderr-only diagnostics can be conflated.
- A command that exits successfully with no output may mean successful empty package, unsupported query, filtered-out material, hidden failure, or nothing relevant, depending on the command's declared contract.
- Pipeline behavior can lose an upstream failure or unavailable producer.

Constitutional distinction tested: process success, output shape, and stream emptiness are external mechanics; they do not by themselves establish populated, empty, complete, unavailable, Unknown, conflict, or aggregate negative standing.

## Closing questions

### What does lossless constitutional compression mean?

Lossless constitutional compression means purpose-relative preservation of every distinction a lawful consumer needs to recover the exact bounded standing it may rely upon, without reopening or re-performing the producer, inventing missing standing, strengthening the upstream claim, erasing authority or evidence limits, or treating compactness as fidelity by itself.

### What may a projection lawfully omit?

A projection may omit implementation-local object shape, private helper structure, irrelevant candidate details, unused provenance depth, transport mechanics, display details, and other upstream material not required by the declared consumer purpose, provided the omission does not erase a distinction needed for lawful reliance.

### What distinctions must survive for its bounded consumer?

The surviving distinctions are consumer- and purpose-relative, but may include subject identity, scope or purpose, produced standing, sufficient basis for reliance, attribution, evidence boundary, provenance or provenance Unknown, authority scope, confidence or uncertainty, Unknowns, conflicts, refusal conditions, known loss, negative authority, freshness, mutation boundary, package existence, availability, cardinality, completeness, and lawful stop or completion limits.

### Who owns package existence, cardinality, availability, and completeness standing?

The bounded responsibility that forms the package, projection, view, set, handoff, or supplied content boundary owns package existence, cardinality, availability, and completeness standing for its declared purpose. A later aggregator may rely on that standing only if it is preserved and sufficient for the aggregator's local responsibility; the aggregator must not manufacture it from iteration behavior.

### What may an aggregator conclude from an empty package?

An aggregator may conclude empty-package standing only if the input formation responsibility preserved that standing. It may not automatically conclude aggregate `No`, negative testimony, complete opposition, unavailability, incompleteness, Unknown, or conflict merely from zero contents. Stronger conclusions require a separate local aggregate rule with preserved warrant, authority, and limits.

### How can realization language create different lossy-compression failure modes?

Python can make empty iteration look like a universal result; SQL can collapse no rows, `NULL`, zero counts, vacuous predicates, and join loss; serialized payloads can collapse missing, null, empty, defaulted, stale, or schema-shifted fields; distributed systems can collapse no message, delayed message, empty response, unavailable producer, partial response, and conflict; shell/process realizations can collapse empty stdout, exit status, command failure, no matches, truncation, stderr, and pipeline behavior. These mechanics are contrastive risks, not constitutional grammar.

### What compact Book correction, if any, is warranted?

A compact correction is warranted in the projection chapter: add `06.Projection.B — Purpose-relative lossless projection and package standing`, stating that a projection is lossless only relative to a declared consumer purpose and that package formation standing is distinct from contents and aggregate judgment.

### What remains Unknown before implementation survey?

It remains Unknown which implementation surfaces currently preserve or collapse formation standing, cardinality, availability, completeness, Unknown, conflict, refusal, and negative authority; which aggregate rules are faithful for their local purposes; and whether any runtime diagnostics, shape audits, serialized handoffs, SQL views, shell/process outputs, or distributed interactions need implementation changes in a later cross-language survey.
