## Stage 1 — Independent Characterization Recovery

This stage does not compare artifacts. Each artifact testifies independently.

### Typed Unknown

**Characterization fields recovered:** unresolved state, evidence gap, meaning or state description, lawful next inquiry or resolution condition, status, confidence, preserved unknowns, and explicit non-promotion limits.

**Constitutional purpose:** preserve an unresolved constitutional condition without pretending the repository knows more than it knows.

**Constitutional authority:** evidence-bound unknown preservation. Its authority is to keep uncertainty visible and actionable as unknown, not to resolve it.

**Constitutional boundaries:** Typed Unknown does not become truth, fact, implementation, runtime state, owner, type hierarchy, or universal artifact class.

**Preservation requirements:** preserve the unknown condition, evidence basis or gap, boundary, resolution condition where supported, and confidence limit.

**Negative authority:** unresolved material may not be silently promoted into repository knowledge, implementation readiness, admission, selection authority, or architectural law.

**Confidence:** high that unknown preservation recurs; lower for any claim that Typed Unknown is part of a universal artifact system.

### Competency Interrogation

**Constitutional purpose:** recover what a competency may lawfully do, emit, consume, refuse, or stop under repository evidence.

**Constitutional boundaries:** Competency Interrogation is not a runtime engine, registry, owner, framework, generalized renderer, public surface, schema field, CLI behavior, JSON behavior, or implementation plan by itself.

**Preservation requirements:** preserve reviewed evidence, local competency identity, authority limits, handoff boundaries, unknowns/stops, negative authority, and confidence.

**Confidence:** high for recurring methodology shape; medium for broad labels that are not implementation-preserved.

## Stage 2 — Grammar Comparison

Only after the independent recoveries above, the survey compares recurring characterization behavior.

### Recurring characterization fields

The smallest recurring fields supported across the reviewed artifacts are:

1. **Artifact or subject identity:** the bounded thing being characterized, selected, preserved, offered, relied on, observed, moved, or refused.
2. **Constitutional purpose:** what the artifact is for locally.
3. **Evidence or support basis:** the repository evidence, provenance, support, or prior artifact relation that permits any claim.
4. **Constitutional authority:** what the artifact can lawfully authorize, preserve, expose, or permit.
5. **Authority boundary / locality:** where that authority stops and which family, role, surface, or local condition bounds it.
6. **Preservation requirements:** what must remain visible for the artifact to remain lawful as that artifact.
7. **Negative authority:** what the artifact explicitly cannot do or become.
8. **Unknowns / stop conditions:** what remains unresolved and where lawful refusal or termination occurs.
9. **Confidence:** calibrated strength of the characterization and its unsupported extensions.

This is a characterization grammar, not an ontology. It describes how repository reports characterize bounded artifacts; it does not assert one shared artifact type, registry, runtime schema, or model.

### Recurring omissions

The reviewed artifacts repeatedly omit or refuse:

- universal artifact ontology;
- implementation authority by characterization alone;
- hidden owner or engine;
- runtime architecture;
- type hierarchy;
- automatic promotion from visibility to truth;
- automatic movement from support to admission;
- automatic registration or dispatch;
- vocabulary stabilization from recurrence alone.

### Recurring local variations

The grammar varies by artifact purpose:

- Typed Unknown emphasizes unresolved state, resolution condition, and non-promotion.
- Competency Interrogation emphasizes competency identity, handoff, locality, and termination.
- Warrant emphasizes role-bounded reliance under preserved authority.
- Admissibility emphasizes family-local consideration or refusal.
- Observation emphasizes provenance-preserving bounded presence and non-interpretation.
- History emphasizes evidence-supported past condition and separation from current reality.
- Movement emphasizes lawful transition or stop.
- Selection emphasizes eligibility, constrained object, selector basis, and refused crossing.
- Survey Warrant emphasizes prior artifact dependency and bounded investigation warrant.

### Recurring preservation fields

Preservation most often attaches to:

- bounded identity or subject;
- provenance, evidence, support, or prior artifact relation;
- role or purpose;
- authority boundary and locality;
- confidence or evidence-strength limit;
- unknowns and unresolved conditions;
- stop/refusal condition;
- non-promotion boundary.

### Recurring authority fields

Authority recurs as local and bounded:

- unknown-preservation authority;
- competency-local inquiry or handoff authority;
- evidence-bound reliance authority;
- family-local admissibility authority;
- observation/preservation authority;
- history-preservation authority;
- transition authority only under lawful movement conditions;
- selection authority only for constrained objects under eligibility/evidence limits;
- bounded investigation warrant from prior artifacts.

### Recurring negative-authority fields

Negative authority is not incidental. It is a core recurring field. The artifacts repeatedly say what they do not authorize: truth, facthood, ownership, implementation, runtime machinery, mutation, registration, promotion, universal law, automatic reliance, automatic movement, or public dispatch.

### Recurring confidence behavior

Confidence repeatedly behaves conservatively:

- high where recurring evidence supports a local artifact role or boundary;
- medium where a broad label compresses several local families;
- low or unsupported where a label would become canonical, implementation-preserved, universal, owned, runtime, or architectural;
- preserved unknown where evidence supports recurrence but not promotion.

## Stage 3 — Distinction Recovery

The smallest supported distinction is:

```text
Shared characterization grammar

Different artifact purpose
```

The repository supports a recurring way of characterizing artifacts, not a universal artifact model. The reviewed evidence supports shared fields for bounded characterization while preserving artifact-specific purposes, authorities, boundaries, preservation duties, refusals, and confidence limits.

Supported distinctions:

- **Characterization grammar != constitutional law.** Recurring fields are evidence of report grammar unless independently supported as law.
- **Shared fields != shared type.** Similar characterization dimensions do not create an artifact class, registry, schema, or type hierarchy.
- **Purpose differs by artifact.** Unknown preserves uncertainty; warrant authorizes bounded reliance; admissibility gates consideration; observation preserves bounded presence; history preserves past condition; movement characterizes lawful transition; selection narrows under eligibility and authority.
- **Authority remains local.** Authority is bounded by family, role, evidence, locality, and negative authority.
- **Preservation prevents promotion.** The grammar preserves limits at least as much as it preserves positive authority.

**High confidence** that this recurrence does not authorize a universal artifact ontology, type hierarchy, implementation, runtime architecture, ownership, registry, or engine.
