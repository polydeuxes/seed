## Lawful inquiry movement

Lawful inquiry can now move through the city in these repository-supported ways:

1. It can begin with external or operator material only after the relevant intake surface bounds and prepares that material.
2. It can enter exact question-family eligibility when the question family is inventory-backed and exact.
3. It can move from eligibility into bounded selection, dispatch request, and dispatch only where the implementation permits that path.
4. It can use inquiry orientation or source navigation to find related material from projected state without turning that orientation into authorization.
5. It can inspect operational and diagnostic visibility through inventory, classification, coverage, and shape-audit surfaces.
6. It can observe pressure as ranked read-only visibility without treating pressure as instruction.
7. It can carry unresolved conditions through Typed Unknown Preservation where the current implementation supports typed records.
8. It can stop lawfully at unknown, unsupported, ambiguous, incomplete, blocked, diagnostic-only, not-dispatchable, outside-authority, or candidate states.

Movement stops where:

- no exact question-family eligibility exists;
- a surface is diagnostic-only;
- implementation evidence is absent;
- authority is unavailable or outside scope;
- a finding would require truth promotion, mutation, provider acquisition, execution, planning, or ownership assignment beyond the surface's authority;
- an unknown is preserved rather than resolved;
- presentation vocabulary lacks implementation support.

Preserved Unknowns route future inquiry by keeping unresolved areas visible without compressing them into truth. Today, those routes are especially visible through Typed Unknown Preservation, inquiry orientation uncertainty, unknown-family eligibility stops, pressure-audit unknown counts, responsibility evaluation classifications, and investigation artifacts that preserve constitutional unknowns.

## Changed architectural orientation

Compared to the beginning of the recent investigation sequence, the city now offers these newly available orientations:

1. **External Orientation is no longer isolated.** It now visibly forms a set of preparation edges connecting inquiry orientation, source navigation, question eligibility, operational visibility, and diagnostic inventory while preserving downstream ownership.

2. **Operational Visibility is now a city district rather than a loose set of diagnostics.** It exposes, discovers, classifies, registers, audits, and renders operational visibility through visible neighborhoods.

3. **Diagnostic Visibility is now recognizable as a guardhouse system.** Diagnostic inventory and shape audit make record scope, ledger writes, mutation boundaries, JSON behavior, and implementation conformance visible.

4. **Typed Unknown Preservation is now a visible checkpoint.** Unknowns can be preserved internally with type-bearing identity while public compatibility remains unchanged.

5. **Recurring competencies are now visible as lawful-movement physiology.** Scope, Resolution, Eligibility, Orientation / Preparation, Declaration, and Verification recur across implementation and methodology, but only some are implementation citizens.

6. **The city now has clearer walls.** Pressure is not command, verification is not permission, diagnostics are not cluster truth, orientation is not authorization, and candidate agreement is not promotion.

7. **Movement can now be described without planning.** Lawful inquiry can move through gates, guardhouses, bridges, roads, and stop conditions without authorizing any construction campaign.

8. **Repository authority has become the map constraint.** Completed recoveries can show connections, but they do not merge districts, invent frameworks, or resolve preserved unknowns without implementation evidence.
