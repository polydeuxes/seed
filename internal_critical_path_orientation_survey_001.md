# Internal Critical-Path Orientation Survey 001

## Bounded constitutional question

Can Seed project and consume, for one bounded purpose, its current constitutional dependency standing well enough to select its next lawful inquiry, where the intended constitutional consumer is Seed itself rather than an operator report, Codex handoff, roadmap, or backlog?

## Book grammar consumed

This survey used current Book grammar as authority before recovering implementation witnesses.

- Artifact shape and reachability do not establish constitutional standing. Standing depends on the kind-specific production or establishment boundary, and public visibility does not become admission, reliance, projected truth, recording, or authority.
- Constructors and public helpers prove constructability, not responsible production occurrence. A downstream consumer may sometimes obtain consumer-local admissibility, but that is not proof that the upstream production boundary occurred.
- A lens can expose or project bounded representations without changing source standing. A constitutional road requires consumer-side authority to consume upstream material, preserved identity and limits, and local sufficiency for the declared consumer boundary.
- Available, applicable, admitted, and consumed material are distinct. Applicable material is not automatically admitted; admitted material is not automatically consumed by the final target consumer.
- Act occurrence is not identical to constructing an act-shaped artifact. Read-only status does not erase constitutional occurrence, and a read-only selector may still make a bounded selection.

## Recovered bounded-purpose standing

The strongest repository-supported bounded purpose is not “choose Seed’s global next task.” It is narrower:

> For one already-selected bounded goal and bounded advancement horizon, preserve stage-owned advancement needs, expose exact references to native need records, and optionally select one exact established need for present consideration when explicit focus evidence names it.

That bounded purpose is evidenced by the `GoalAdvancementNeedSet` boundary notes: it preserves supplied stage-owned projections without reinterpretation, treats coexisting needs as unordered rather than priority/order/blocker/route/next action, and explicitly refuses sufficiency judgment, inquiry opening, authority request, realization selection, execution, recording, event-ledger writing, and mutation.

The next narrower inquiry road is evidenced only after exact focus evidence selects an established inquiry need. `BoundedInquiryFrontier` can then assemble an inquiry frontier from preserved boundary testimony for that selected inquiry need, but it refuses to formulate the question, open inquiry, select sources or observations, authorize access, execute, record, write the event ledger, mutate cluster state, or know the result.

## Current Seed-local consumer surfaces

### Recovered consumers

1. **Advancement need consideration selection** consumes a reference set plus explicit focus evidence naming exact visible advancement-need references. It can select one exact selectable need for consideration, while preserving missing identity, ambiguity, conflict, mismatch, absence, duplicate lineage conflicts, non-selectable references, unknowns, conflicts, and non-selected references. This is Seed-local consumption of projected dependency material, but only under an externally supplied exact focus-evidence boundary. It expressly refuses primary blocker, resolution, next action, inquiry opening, authority request, authorization, execution, recording, event-ledger write, and mutation.

2. **Bounded inquiry frontier assembly** consumes one selected inquiry need and independent frontier-boundary testimony. It can establish whether a frontier boundary is coherent for that selected need, and it preserves missing required clause families or material conflicts. It does not select the inquiry need; it depends on the prior selection.

3. **Examination work selection** consumes an examination frontier plus an examination policy projection and selector handoff. This is an internal consumer road for selecting examination work after an inquiry/examination frontier exists. It is not the same as the central road’s initial selection of the next lawful inquiry from current constitutional dependency standing.

### Consumer absence preserved as Unknown / absent

No inspected current responsibility consumes the whole projected standing set to determine:

- which downstream constitutional act is next across need families;
- which upstream standing is a smallest unresolved dominating dependency;
- whether known lawful alternative roads are exhausted or bypassed;
- which bounded next inquiry Seed should select without exact focus evidence already naming a selectable need.

The strongest current consumer (`select_advancement_need_for_consideration`) selects from exact focus evidence; it does not infer focus from the need set, family order, sufficiency reasons, severity, uniqueness, wording, presentation order, or number of selectable records.

## Producer → consumer → inquiry → reprojection topology

### Road A: advancement need visibility to exact consideration selection

```text
family need projections
→ GoalAdvancementNeedSet
→ AdvancementNeedReferenceSet
→ AdvancementNeedConsiderationSelection
```

- Bounded purpose: select one exact advancement need for present consideration.
- Established standing: native established records become selectable references.
- Required standing: exact focus evidence that names the same need set, goal, horizon, family, projection, and native lineage.
- Blocking condition: no focus evidence, missing identity, ambiguity, conflict, mismatch, absent reference, duplicate lineage conflict, or non-selectable reference.
- Alternatives: non-selected references remain visible, but are not compared as lawful roads.
- Dominance: missing exact focus evidence dominates this selector because the selector explicitly refuses all implicit selection grounds.
- Internal consumer: yes, but only for exact-reference consideration selection; not for autonomous next-inquiry orientation.
- Reprojection trigger: a new native projection or new exact focus evidence can create a new reference set/selection. No current owner independently initiates that reprojection as self-orientation.

### Road B: selected inquiry need to frontier boundary

```text
AdvancementNeedConsiderationSelection(selected inquiry need)
+ InquiryFrontierBoundaryTestimony
→ BoundedInquiryFrontier
```

- Bounded purpose: determine whether already-preserved frontier clauses coherently establish a bounded inquiry frontier for the selected inquiry need.
- Established standing: only if the selected need is an inquiry need and all required operative clause families are present with no material conflicts.
- Blocking condition: not selected inquiry need, material binding conflict, or missing required clause family.
- Alternatives: adjacent, mixed, unsupported, unknown, conflicting, stale, unavailable, and out-of-scope clauses are preserved; alternative frontier roads are not selected.
- Dominance: missing required clause family dominates frontier establishment for this specific selected need; it does not prove global critical path.
- Internal consumer: yes for frontier coherence, not for choosing which inquiry need to select.
- Reprojection trigger: new frontier-boundary testimony or a new selected inquiry need.

### Road C: examination frontier to selected work and probe request

- Alternatives: non-selected eligible work and unchanged blocked/unsupported/deferred/examined/failed/conflict-unknown references remain preserved.
- Internal consumer: yes for examination work selection after a frontier exists.
- Relation to central question: downstream and adjacent. It does not prove Seed can select the next lawful inquiry from current constitutional dependency standing.

## Critical-path discipline finding

Repository evidence supports a purpose-relative pressure relation only in bounded local roads, not a repository-wide critical-path engine.

- In Road A, missing exact focus evidence dominates selection because the selector’s boundary rejects all other selection bases.
- In Road B, missing required frontier clauses dominate frontier establishment because all required clause families are needed and no material conflict may exist.
- In Road C, an unresolved tie under sufficient no-order policy prevents work selection because the selector refuses arbitrary tie-breaks.

A dependency is therefore not critical merely because it is missing. It becomes locally dominating when a downstream constitutional act requires upstream standing, the act’s owner refuses lawful movement without it, and no represented lawful bypass within that owner is available. Current implementation does not generalize that into a current constitutional dependency view consumed by Seed for next-inquiry selection.

## Fidelity crossings and responsibility collapses found

- **Visibility to uptake collapse risk:** need sets and reference sets expose dependency ingredients, but their boundary notes explicitly refuse prioritization, routing, and next action.
- **Selection evidence to self-orientation collapse risk:** exact focus evidence can select a need for consideration; that does not mean Seed can derive the focus evidence itself.
- **Frontier establishment to inquiry execution collapse risk:** a bounded frontier can be established without opening inquiry, selecting observations, authorizing access, executing, recording, or knowing the result.
- **Diagnostic report to constitutional consumer collapse risk:** pipeline diagnostics and inquiry orientation can show status or related material but explicitly refuse authority and next safe move.
- **External handoff to execution permission collapse risk:** probe requests and operational-realization handoffs preserve request meaning but do not identify a provider/tool/arguments or authorize execution.

## Finding

**Seed projects the required standing but lacks a Seed-local constitutional consumer** for the central road as stated.

More precisely: Seed currently projects many ingredients of constitutional dependency standing and consumes some of them in local selectors after exact focus, frontier, or policy evidence has already been supplied. It does not currently recover an internal consumer road that takes the distributed current standing, identifies a smallest unresolved dominating dependency across lawful alternatives, and selects Seed’s next lawful inquiry for the bounded constitutional purpose. The ingredients are stronger than mere operator visibility, but the central self-orientation road remains incomplete at the consumer-selection crossing.
