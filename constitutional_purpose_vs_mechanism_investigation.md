## Independent purpose interrogation

This section interrogates `Purpose` without reference to mechanism.

### What recurring repository evidence supports it?

Recurring evidence supports constitutional purpose as the answer to what constitutional work a recovered district, artifact, role, or responsibility serves under repository authority.

The district characterization explicitly used purpose as a characterization dimension after recovering district identity. It described the district as serving lawful constitutional use without allowing visible material to overpromote itself, preserving enough relation for lawful work while refusing unsupported collapse.

The district purpose cross-examination then treated purpose as the bounded question of what recurring constitutional purpose the previously characterized district actually serves. It tested candidate purpose descriptions, rejected unsupported descriptions, and concluded only at district-purpose resolution.

The topology survey repeatedly identifies recovered recurring responsibilities for constitutional citizens. These responsibilities describe what each citizen does constitutionally: observation preserves bounded presence and provenance before promotion; evidence supports, constrains, challenges, or refuses claims without becoming truth; warrant carries bounded entitlement to rely; reliance consumes only what warrant authorizes; admissibility gates consideration; promotion authority permits or refuses promotion under boundary; lawful transition preserves continuity and stop; local responsibility performs or refuses bounded roles.

The sentence investigation supports purpose as a lawful answer-shape at investigation resolution. Candidate constitutional sentences are lawful only when bounded by evidence, authority, provenance, warrant, unknowns, confidence, and stop. This supports purpose as an evidence-bound constitutional question, not as mere label preference.

Warrant and reliance characterizations support purpose because each recovers a recurring constitutional role. Warrant makes reliance lawful under preserved authority boundary. Reliance makes warrant operative as bounded downstream use while refusing overuse.

History and constitutional reality evidence supports purpose as recurring responsibility rather than objective truth: append-only history preserves durable event-supported transition material so current projections and explanations can be rebuilt and challenged.

Local responsibility evidence supports purpose because local responsibility is recovered where the repository shows bounded authority to perform one local movement on specific artifacts while preserving lawful limits. This is purpose-like because it answers what local constitutional work the responsibility may perform and what it must refuse.

### What recurring repository evidence contradicts it?

Recurring evidence contradicts purpose when purpose is asserted before identity, evidence, authority, and boundary have been recovered.

The district purpose cross-examination refused to promote orientation to district purpose at the same resolution, even though orientation survived as a recurring effect at another resolution.

The topology survey preserves low confidence in exhaustive topology, single ordering, universal sequence, universal relation model, or complete crossing map. This contradicts any purpose claim that becomes totalizing.

Observation evidence contradicts treating observation's purpose as truth, fact, verification, promotion, projection, history, implementation recovery, or runtime authority.

Evidence, warrant, reliance, movement, grammar transformation, history, and local responsibility evidence all contradict unbounded purpose claims that ignore provenance, negative authority, unknowns, confidence limits, local competence, or lawful stop.

### What constitutional questions does it answer?

Purpose answers bounded constitutional questions such as:

- What constitutional work does the recovered subject serve?
- What recurring responsibility makes the subject constitutionally intelligible?
- What lawful use, preservation, refusal, or boundary does the subject exist to protect?
- At what resolution is this purpose supported?
- What weaker purposes or effects must not be promoted to the primary purpose at that resolution?

### What constitutional questions does it refuse to answer?

Purpose refuses to answer:

- How implementation performs the work.
- Which runtime component, owner, sequence, algorithm, or execution structure carries the work.
- Whether a presentation term is repository knowledge.
- Whether all neighboring responsibilities must be unified.
- Whether purpose alone authorizes promotion, mutation, truth, implementation, or downstream reliance.

### What recurring unknowns remain?

Recurring unknowns remain:

- Whether a recovered purpose should become stable repository vocabulary.
- Whether future evidence will split a purpose into narrower purposes.
- Whether future evidence will merge a purpose with adjacent constitutional work.
- Whether purpose at one resolution becomes only an effect or sub-purpose at another resolution.
- Whether all recurring limits associated with a purpose are necessary in every local instance.

## Independent mechanism interrogation

This section interrogates `Mechanism` without reference to purpose.

### What recurring repository evidence supports it?

Recurring repository evidence supports mechanism only in a weakened constitutional sense: a recurring bounded way, discipline, relation, transition, or local movement by which constitutional work becomes lawful without becoming implementation.

The movement investigations distinguish movement from artifact emission and candidate movement from lawful movement. Lawful movement requires enough recurring participants: bounded subject, evidence or support, provenance, competent authority, boundary, negative authority, locality, eligibility or admissibility where applicable, unknown preservation, confidence limits, and stop or refusal conditions. This is evidence for recurring constitutional operation, not for an implementation model.

The topology survey supports mechanism-like recurrence by showing recurring adjacencies, relationships, non-adjacencies, and crossing boundaries among observation, evidence, warrant, reliance, admissibility, promotion authority, lawful transition, movement participation, artifact emission, constitutional sentence, grammar transformation, local responsibility, history, and projection. It also explicitly refuses a universal relation model, single ordering, and execution mechanism.

Grammar transformation evidence supports a recurring local discipline: bounded producer, bounded artifact, bounded consumer. The recurring local responsibility is often `consume -> transform -> emit`, with explicit refusal boundaries around promotion, mutation, semantic interpretation, and ownership. That evidence supports local artifact-handoff discipline, not a universal pipeline.

Local responsibility evidence supports mechanism-like operation as bounded authority to perform one local movement on specific artifacts while preserving the limits that make the movement lawful. It requires consumed artifacts, emitted artifacts, permitted movement, refused movement, warrant, negative authority, non-promotion, and lawful stop.

Observation evidence supports mechanism-like recurrence by showing an acquisition discipline: observation consumes reported or selected material and emits bounded observation while preserving subject-as-reported, source, vantage, provenance, and pre-justification limits.

Warrant and reliance evidence supports mechanism-like recurrence where warrant is produced by recurring repository evidence under bounded authority, and reliance consumes only what warrant authorizes.

History and constitutional reality evidence supports mechanism-like recurrence where durable history preserves event-supported transition material, and projections or explanations can be rebuilt and challenged without treating presentation state as source-of-truth.

### What recurring repository evidence contradicts it?

Recurring evidence strongly contradicts mechanism when mechanism means implementation machinery, universal pipeline, owner, runtime object, canonical ontology, or formal architecture.

The district characterization says the district is not a hierarchy, framework, ontology, grammar, or implementation design.

The purpose cross-examination rejects any totalizing structure, runtime owner, grammar authority, or mechanism as the district-purpose description.

The topology survey explicitly records no universal physiology in movement participation evidence, and low confidence in any universal relation model or single ordering.

The sentence investigation refuses to promote constitutional sentence into a new repository mechanism.

Warrant is not syntax, schema, runtime object, translation engine, semantic framework, implementation mechanism, or universal owner.

Reliance is not a universal mechanism, trust framework, dependency system, planning authority, mutation authority, or implementation permission.

Grammar transformation evidence refuses a universal implemented owner and records counterexamples where substrate is lawfully consumed directly at adapter or owner boundaries.

Local responsibility is not a universal observer, mechanism, bootstrap tool, provider controller, or permission system.

### What constitutional questions does it answer?

Mechanism, in its only supported constitutional sense, answers bounded questions such as:

- By what recurring lawful discipline does a subject move, transform, cross, emit, or become usable?
- What participants, boundaries, and refusal conditions are required for a movement or transformation to be lawful?
- What artifact is consumed, what artifact is emitted, and what boundary must survive?
- What local authority performs or refuses a bounded movement?
- What stops movement when evidence, authority, provenance, confidence, eligibility, or unknown preservation is insufficient?

### What constitutional questions does it refuse to answer?

Mechanism refuses to answer:

- Why a district, artifact, or responsibility exists constitutionally.
- What primary constitutional purpose a recovered district serves.
- Which implementation performs the operation.
- Whether there is a universal ordering, pipeline, or owner.
- Whether local recurring discipline authorizes semantic promotion, truth, mutation, or repository-wide architecture.

### What recurring unknowns remain?

Recurring unknowns remain:

- Whether the repository should preserve the word `mechanism` at all, given its repeated implementation risk.
- Whether recurring operation should instead be described only through movement, transition, transformation, local responsibility, and relation.
- Whether every lawful movement requires every currently recurring participant explicitly.
- Whether some local disciplines should be split into narrower constitutional kinds.
- Whether future evidence will support a more precise non-implementation term for recurring lawful operation.
