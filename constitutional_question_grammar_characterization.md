## Characterization

Constitutional Question Grammar is the recurring admission discipline that decides whether a repository pressure, uncertainty, prompt, or candidate question may become a bounded constitutional inquiry.

It is not the inquiry itself. It is not Orientation. It is not Recovery. It is not a dispatcher, planner, semantic parser, ownership owner, or implementation surface.

Its recurring constitutional work is:

```text
pressure / possible question
  -> bounded question shape
  -> warrant and dependency check
  -> inquiry admission, deferral, Unknown preservation, or lawful stop
```

The repository evidence supports Question Grammar as mature and foundational because multiple recovered artifacts already use its pieces under other names: bounded question discipline, question shape, question family boundary, survey warrant family, inquiry dependency, bounded work invocation, admissibility record, frontier prioritization, and deferred pressure classification.

## 1. Recurring constitutional question properties

A lawful constitutional question recurs with these properties.

### 1. Bounded subject identity

A lawful question identifies the bounded subject, surface, family, pressure, artifact, target, domain, note, or relation under review. The question dependency survey repeatedly finds that later questions depend on exact identity, target, bounded endpoints, or question family before a relation or answer may be tested.

Unlawful form:

```text
What is this really about?
```

Lawful form:

```text
Across the named constitutional artifacts, what recurring admission discipline governs whether a deferred pressure may become a bounded constitutional inquiry?
```

### 2. Evidence posture

A lawful question states the evidence it can use and preserves where evidence is absent. Survey warrant recurrence requires multiple artifacts showing the same bounded question shape, implementation-evidence or constitutional-evidence posture, investigation shape, and produced artifact pattern.

A question is unlawful when it asks the repository to answer from historical momentum, intuition, attractive vocabulary, analogy, or presentation labels alone.

### 3. Authority boundary

A lawful question says what the inquiry is authorized to decide and what it is not authorized to decide. The recurring grammar requires negative authority: no implementation recovery, no ownership recovery, no topology, no mutation, no planner, no semantic routing, no new participant, no promotion from vocabulary to fact.

### 4. Dependency visibility

A lawful question shows which earlier recovered questions made it askable: scout before survey, survey before characterization, identity before bridge, readiness before proceed/stop, prior frontier before next bounded inquiry, and endpoint identity before topology or relationship testing.

A question is unlawful, or at least premature, when it asks a later question without the earlier identity, evidence, warrant, endpoint, or readiness question having supplied support.

### 5. Inquiry shape and produced artifact

A lawful question names or implies the artifact it can produce: Scout, Survey, Characterization, audit, inventory, reconciliation, admissibility record, frontier prioritization, bounded slice report, or lawful stop. Question Grammar does not permit a question to pretend to produce implementation, ownership, runtime behavior, or constitutional participants when the bounded inquiry is only a characterization.

### 6. Unknown preservation

A lawful question admits that missing support may become a preserved Unknown rather than a forced answer. Unknown is not false, failure, or completion by implication. The question-family and inquiry-generation evidence repeatedly preserves unknown family, missing derivation, missing selection logic, unsupported relation, and unresolved admission relationship as Unknowns.

### 7. Stop condition

A lawful question carries a visible stop grammar: stop when identity is absent, required evidence is missing, the next move would require implementation or ownership recovery, the candidate is vocabulary-only, the surface is diagnostic-only, the relation lacks bounded endpoints, or confidence cannot support movement.

### 8. Confidence calibration

A lawful question produces bounded confidence. It may recover high confidence for the admission discipline, medium confidence for applying it across future inquiries, and low confidence for any unsupported topology, engine, or universal model.

## 2. Recurring movement from Question to Inquiry

The recurring movement is not automatic. Repository questions become lawful constitutional inquiries through an admission sequence.

```text
Question
  -> bounded identity
  -> evidence posture
  -> warrant family / dependency check
  -> authority and negative-authority boundary
  -> artifact expectation
  -> Unknown / stop visibility
  -> Inquiry
```

### Movement 1: preserve the possible question without promoting it

A possible question first appears as pressure, frontier, deferred pressure, operator prompt, candidate question, or open question. Question Grammar preserves it as a candidate only. It does not treat pressure as command authority.

### Movement 2: bind the question to a bounded subject

The question must name what is being asked about. In bridge and topology questions, both sides must be visible before a relation can be tested. In question-family work, exact question-family identity precedes eligibility and dispatch. In constitutional work, the bounded constitutional subject precedes characterization.

### Movement 3: select admissible evidence

The inquiry states which artifacts are responsive and which areas are forbidden. This prevents a constitutional characterization from silently becoming implementation inspection, ownership recovery, or participant recovery.

### Movement 4: test warrant and dependency

The question asks whether prior repository artifacts already support this inquiry shape. Survey warrant evidence asks whether recurrence exists across multiple artifacts. Question dependency evidence asks whether earlier questions have supplied identity, evidence, scope, authority, endpoint, readiness, or stop criteria.

### Movement 5: define negative authority

Before inquiry begins, the grammar states what the inquiry may not produce. This is not rhetorical caution; it is the constitutional boundary preventing a characterization from becoming implementation, ownership, topology, runtime, planner, or semantic parser recovery.

### Movement 6: admit the inquiry or refuse it

If bounded identity, admissible evidence, warrant, dependency, authority, artifact shape, and stop condition are present, the question becomes a lawful inquiry. If not, it becomes deferred pressure, preserved Unknown, or lawful stop.

### Movement 7: produce only the admitted artifact

The inquiry produces the artifact its grammar admitted. A Characterization produces a characterization. A Survey produces a survey. A bounded work invocation slice produces a slice report. Question Grammar does not produce the downstream recovery itself.

## 3. Question Grammar, Orientation, and Recovery

Repository evidence supports distinguishing the sequence:

```text
Question Grammar

↓

Orientation

↓

Recovery
```

### Question Grammar

Question Grammar asks whether a possible question has the constitutional properties needed to become a lawful inquiry at all:

- bounded subject;
- evidence posture;
- warrant;
- dependency;
- authority boundary;
- negative authority;
- artifact expectation;
- Unknown and stop grammar;
- confidence boundary.

Its responsibility is admission discipline.

### Orientation

Orientation asks where lawful attention may rest before recovery. The orientation recovery survey characterizes Orientation as bounded survey discipline before recovery: preserve current explanation, locate validated domain and limits, search for adjacent evidence, distinguish visibility/presentation/compatibility/ownership, preserve Unknowns, and select the smallest lawful candidate or stop.

Orientation receives an admitted inquiry question and orients the inquiry inside repository evidence. It does not decide all possible question admission by itself.

### Recovery

Recovery changes constitutional or implementation visibility only when the admitted and oriented evidence exposes a lawful recoverable boundary. Implementation recovery grammar moves one adjacent implementation-local ownership boundary at a time and stops when remaining movement would wrap orchestration, relocate already-owned material, or exceed evidence.

Recovery is downstream of Question Grammar and Orientation. Question Grammar does not recover; Orientation does not recover; Recovery cannot lawfully begin without the earlier admission and orientation conditions.

## 4. Responsibilities belonging to Question Grammar rather than Orientation

Question Grammar owns these responsibilities:

1. **Question admissibility.** It determines whether a possible question may become a lawful constitutional inquiry.
2. **Question shape discipline.** It requires bounded wording, bounded subject, resolution, evidence posture, and forbidden overreach.
3. **Inquiry dependency discipline.** It checks whether earlier questions supplied identity, evidence, scope, authority, readiness, endpoints, or stop conditions.
4. **Survey warrant admission.** It asks whether recurrence supports the inquiry shape and produced artifact pattern.
5. **Pressure classification.** It classifies pressures as ready, emerging, premature, deferred, Unknown, or stop according to evidence.
6. **Artifact expectation.** It identifies what kind of artifact the admitted inquiry may produce.
7. **Negative authority before inquiry.** It states what the question may not become.
8. **Lawful refusal.** It authorizes non-admission without treating refusal as failure.

Orientation owns adjacent but different responsibilities:

- locating the current bounded explanation;
- selecting the evidence corpus for an admitted inquiry;
- exposing current limits, validated domains, candidate boundaries, and Unknowns;
- distinguishing visibility, presentation, compatibility, ownership, and consumption;
- preparing a candidate for recovery or stop.

Orientation can help answer admissibility questions, but it is not identical to Question Grammar.

## 7. Inquiry-admission grammar

The recurring admission grammar is:

```text
A constitutional pressure may become a lawful inquiry only when:

1. the subject is bounded;
2. the proposed inquiry has a recognizable question shape;
3. admissible repository evidence exists;
4. prior questions supply required dependencies;
5. the inquiry has a warrant family or local constitutional support;
6. authority and negative authority are explicit;
7. the produced artifact is bounded;
8. Unknowns and stop conditions are preserved;
9. confidence can be stated without overclaiming.
```

If those conditions hold, the pressure may become a lawful inquiry.

If some conditions are emerging but incomplete, the pressure remains deferred.

If the missing relation itself is material and cannot be resolved, it becomes preserved Unknown.

If the question would require forbidden movement, unsupported promotion, or absent evidence, the lawful result is stop.

## 8. Recurring stop grammar

Question Grammar stops inquiry when any of these conditions recur:

- vocabulary is presentation-only, metaphor-only, or historically attractive but not repository-supported;
- bounded subject identity is absent;
- exact target, endpoint, family, or required argument is missing;
- evidence belongs to another local family;
- the question would require implementation recovery when implementation is forbidden;
- the question would require ownership recovery when ownership is forbidden;
- the question would invent a participant, topology, universal grammar, planner, router, scheduler, or runtime system;
- the question would promote natural-language parsing into constitutional fact;
- the question would collapse Question Shape, Question Family, Survey Warrant Family, Orientation, and Recovery;
- the question would treat diagnostic, orientation, or visibility output as cluster truth;
- confidence would exceed repository evidence.

Stop is a positive constitutional result. It preserves repository authority by refusing unlawful motion.

## 9. Determining lawful inquiry, deferred pressure, Unknown, or lawful stop

### Lawful inquiry

A pressure becomes a lawful inquiry when repository evidence supplies bounded identity, admitted evidence, dependency, warrant, authority boundary, artifact shape, Unknown preservation, stop grammar, and sufficient confidence.

Example: `constitutional_deferred_pressure_survey.md` classified Constitutional Question Grammar as mature, foundational, partially recovered under several names, and ready. That classification made this characterization admissible without recovering The Eye, Hurricane, Relationship Grammar, ownership, or implementation.

### Deferred pressure

A pressure remains deferred when it has evidence or recurrence but lacks enough bounded support for the requested inquiry. Deferred pressure is not rejected as meaningless; it is preserved as not-yet-admitted.

Example: The Eye and Hurricane were treated as emerging and dependent, not as first recovery subjects or constitutional participants.

### Preserved Unknown

A pressure becomes preserved Unknown when the repository can identify a material relation or admission question but cannot answer it with current evidence.

Examples include whether question-shape recurrence should ever become implementation-backed Question Family admission, whether bounded inquiry admissibility is standalone or part of Inquiry Navigation, whether local Unknown artifacts are identical or only analogous, and whether Question Grammar should ever be implemented as a public inventory.

### Lawful stop

A pressure stops when continuing would overrun authority: implementation recovery, ownership recovery, participant invention, topology, semantic parsing, planner behavior, mutation, or truth promotion without evidence.

Lawful stop is not failure. It is the correct artifact when admission cannot be supported.

## 10. Dependencies on Unknowns, Relationship Grammar, and External Grammar

### Unknowns

Question Grammar depends on Unknown preservation because non-admission must not silently become falsehood, failure, or ignored evidence. Unknown lets the grammar preserve unsupported but material candidate relations without promoting them.

Unknown also protects future inquiry: a deferred relation can remain visible until evidence matures, rather than being forced into premature recovery.

### Relationship Grammar

Question Grammar depends on Relationship Grammar only where a question seeks a relation, bridge, dependency, exchange, compatibility, or non-collapse between bounded subjects. Relationship Grammar supplies the discipline that relation requires visible endpoints, evidence of relation, and non-collapse.

Question Grammar is prior at the admission boundary: it asks whether a relationship question may be asked. Relationship Grammar governs how the admitted relationship inquiry can characterize the relation without collapsing the sides.

### External Grammar

Question Grammar depends on External Grammar where a question involves outside testimony, provider language, repository-external vocabulary, or external-facing translation. External Grammar supplies structural recovery and admission boundaries for external forms.

Question Grammar must refuse to treat external vocabulary or natural-language form as constitutional fact unless External Grammar evidence has lawfully admitted the structure. External Grammar is therefore a dependency when the question's evidence crosses repository-internal/external-language boundaries.

This characterization preserves these Unknowns:

- Whether the grammar has a finite list of admissibility fields stable across all future constitutional investigations.
- Whether Question Grammar and Inquiry Navigation are distinct constitutional competencies or overlapping descriptions of the same admission frontier.
- Whether recurring Question Shapes can ever be admitted into Question Families without a future implementation recovery.
- Whether a single admissibility record exists across constitutional, diagnostic, and implementation-backed inquiry surfaces.
- Whether local Unknown artifacts across question admission, inquiry movement, current condition, and family completion are identical, compatible, exchanged, or only analogous.
- Whether Relationship Grammar supplies a reusable dependency for all relation-shaped questions or only for already recovered bridge/topology inquiries.
- Whether External Grammar admission can be generalized beyond the already recovered external-structure cases.
- Whether future constitutional pressure surveys will continue to classify Question Grammar as foundational after additional participants are recovered.

## 13. Confidence

**High** that repository evidence supports a recurring Constitutional Question Grammar as an admission discipline for lawful constitutional inquiry.

**High** that the grammar distinguishes lawful inquiry from deferred pressure, preserved Unknown, and lawful stop through bounded identity, evidence posture, warrant, dependency, authority boundary, negative authority, artifact expectation, and confidence.

**Medium-high** that `Question Grammar -> Orientation -> Recovery` is the safest current distinction, because orientation recovery evidence explicitly separates pre-recovery orientation from recovery, and question evidence supports a prior admission discipline.

**Medium** that dependencies on Relationship Grammar and External Grammar are stable, because those dependencies are strongest only when a question seeks relation or external-grammar admission.

**Low** for any stronger claim that Question Grammar is an implemented component, universal logic system, planner, router, participant, topology, or ownership authority.
