## Independent neighborhood testimony

The neighborhoods testify independently in this section. No comparison is made until reconciliation.

### Neighborhood A — Constitutional State

Recovered only: constitutional state, identity, continuity, preserved conditions, bounded locality, confidence.

#### Testimony

Recurring repository evidence supports constitutional state most safely as present bounded lawful condition, not as implementation state, runtime state, object state, or a universal state layer.

`current_lawful_condition_characterization.md` is the clearest testimony. It separates durable constitutional rule from present lawful condition. A durable rule may say only evidence may move Null; a present lawful condition says a specific subject is currently Null, currently supported, currently insufficient, currently stopped, currently unresolved, or currently frontier-only.

Identity is local. The state belongs to a bounded subject, question, family, pressure item, evidence relation, intake, or current work position. Repository evidence does not support an unbounded global constitutional state for all subjects.

Continuity is preserved by keeping the present condition under law visible. Continuity does not mean permanent truth. A condition may change if evidence, authority, boundary, confidence, or support changes.

Preserved conditions include Null, unknown, insufficient evidence, supported condition, candidate condition, current completion outcome, current stop condition, bounded readiness, and frontier-only status.

Bounded locality is central. Constitutional state is lawful only inside the subject and authority boundary that supports it.

#### Confidence

High confidence that recurring evidence supports present bounded lawful conditions. Medium confidence for calling the whole family `constitutional state`, because repository evidence repeatedly warns against promoting attractive state vocabulary beyond the locality proven by evidence.

### Neighborhood B — Constrained Movement

Recovered only: lawful movement, lawful refusal, lawful stop, transition boundaries, authority, confidence.

No artifact ontology is referenced here.

#### Testimony

Recurring repository evidence does not support one generic constitutional movement. It supports family-local constrained movement.

`constitutional_transition_family_characterization.md` states the negative rule directly: repository evidence does not support one generic constitutional movement, and instead supports several transition families with different effects and authority boundaries.

`candidate_movement_to_lawful_movement_transition_investigation.md` gives the strongest constrained-movement testimony: candidate movement becomes lawful only when repository evidence is sufficient for a bounded subject, through competent local authority, with provenance/support preserved, negative authority respected, unknowns preserved, confidence limited, and a lawful stop available.

Lawful movement is therefore not motion, sequence, desire, pressure, presentation, repeated wording, or usefulness. It is evidence-bound, authority-bound, locality-bound, preservation-bound, and confidence-bound change in constitutional condition or use-status.

Lawful refusal recurs when support is missing, provenance would be stripped, authority would be broadened, unknowns would be resolved by assertion, confidence would be inflated, presentation vocabulary would become knowledge, or diagnostic output would become truth without authority.

Lawful stop is positive, not failure. It preserves the current limit when further movement would cross evidence, authority, boundary, unknown, reliance, negative-authority, or confidence limits.

Transition boundaries are family-local. Observation, inquiry, warrant, reliance, admission, projection, completion, and competency judgment do not share one universal transition law.

#### Confidence

High confidence that constrained constitutional movement is family-local and authority-bound. Low confidence for any universal movement sequence.

### Neighborhood C — Artifact Emission

Recovered only: emitted artifacts, emitted constitutional profiles, producer boundaries, preservation behavior, confidence.

Later consumers are not referenced here.

#### Testimony

Recurring repository evidence supports artifact emission as bounded preservation by a competent local producer. It does not support the claim that every movement emits an artifact or that every emitted artifact is authoritative.

`constitutional_movement_vs_artifact_emission_investigation.md` is the key boundary testimony: performed movement and produced artifact are constitutionally distinct at investigation resolution. A report artifact may preserve a movement result, but the movement is not identical to the report.

`artifact_bounded_lawful_reliance_characterization.md` adds that a repository-supported artifact may carry preserved evidence, provenance, role, authority limit, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, and stop information. That carried profile is what makes bounded later reliance inspectable when the artifact is otherwise competent.

Producer boundaries are local. The recurring producer is not a global producer role. It is the competent local producer for the bounded artifact: a characterization writer, investigation report, diagnostic declaration, observation path, inspection path, or recovery-slice producer as already recovered in prior evidence.

Preservation behavior is the constitutional feature. Emitted artifacts preserve bounded profiles: subject identity, evidence/support, provenance, role, authority boundary, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, stop conditions, and non-promotion limits.

Artifact emission alone does not promote content to truth, create global authority, create implementation, prove consumption, or require further movement.

#### Confidence

High confidence that some artifacts carry bounded constitutional profiles. High confidence that artifact emission remains distinct from movement. Medium confidence about any generic artifact category, because the task forbids and evidence does not require a universal artifact ontology.

### Neighborhood D — Artifact Consumption

Recovered only: consumed artifacts, required constitutional profile, local authority, bounded consumption, refused consumption, confidence.

Producers are not referenced here.

#### Testimony

Recurring repository evidence supports artifact consumption only where the consumed artifact carries a sufficient constitutional profile for the consumer's local role.

`constitutional_reliance_characterization.md` and `artifact_bounded_lawful_reliance_characterization.md` support the consumption boundary: lawful reliance is a bounded downstream use-status, not a free transfer of truth. A consumer may use preserved content only for the specific role supported by warrant and only within the preserved authority boundary.

The required constitutional profile includes bounded subject, evidence/support, provenance, role, authority limit, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, and stop conditions. Where that profile is absent or insufficient, consumption remains candidate or is refused.

Local authority is decisive. A consumer may have authority to read, explain, orient, inspect, diagnose, compare, preserve unknowns, or state reliance, but that does not imply authority to promote to truth, mutate cluster state, implement, assign ownership, or create universal architecture.

Bounded consumption is lawful when it preserves the artifact's limits and uses the content only inside the consumer's supported role.

Refused consumption recurs when the consumer exceeds the artifact's role, drops support, treats presentation vocabulary as repository knowledge, treats diagnostic findings as cluster truth, converts candidate agreement into proof, inflates confidence, or resolves unknowns without evidence.

#### Confidence

High confidence that artifact consumption is local, profile-bound, and refusal-bearing. Medium confidence about the exact minimum profile in every family, because different families preserve different fields with different names.

## Reconciliation

Reconciliation begins only after the independent testimony above.

### Recurring constitutional sequence

The evidence supports a recurring **possible relation**, not a mandatory sequence:

```text
present bounded lawful condition
  may permit or refuse
family-local constrained movement
  may emit or preserve
bounded artifact with a constitutional profile
  may permit, limit, or refuse
later local bounded consumption or movement
```

The important word is `may`. Repository evidence does not support inevitability at any arrow.

### Recurring non-sequence

Repository evidence refuses this as a universal sequence:

```text
State
↓
Movement
↓
Artifact
↓
Movement
```

That sequence may describe some local cases, but it is too strong as constitutional law.

Unsupported assumptions:

- that constitutional state always initiates movement;
- that movement always emits an artifact;
- that an artifact always produces later movement;
- that artifact consumption always changes constitutional state;
- that the same authority governs state, movement, emission, and consumption;
- that a single runtime pipeline or state machine exists.

### Recurring producer/consumer locality

Producer and consumer locality is recurring, but not as ownership or universal architecture.

Smallest supported distinction:

- an emitter may be competent to preserve a bounded profile;
- a later consumer may be competent to rely on that profile for one bounded role;
- producer competence does not grant consumer competence;
- consumer desire does not backfill producer authority;
- the artifact mediates only what it preserves and only inside the authority boundary carried or otherwise supported by repository evidence.

### Recurring movement constraints

Movement is constrained by:

- bounded subject identity;
- evidence/support sufficiency;
- provenance preservation;
- competent local authority;
- role preservation;
- negative authority;
- unknown preservation;
- confidence calibration;
- stop availability;
- family-local transition rules.

### Recurring preservation constraints

Artifacts and later uses must preserve:

- subject boundary;
- evidence/support path;
- provenance;
- role;
- authority boundary;
- negative authority;
- unknowns;
- confidence;
- stop conditions;
- non-promotion limits.

### Recurring stop conditions

Movement or consumption must stop when:

- evidence is insufficient;
- provenance would be lost;
- authority would be widened;
- a consumer lacks local competence;
- an artifact lacks the required profile;
- unknowns would be silently resolved;
- confidence would be inflated;
- presentation vocabulary would become knowledge;
- diagnostic or report output would become truth or mutation without authority;
- a proposed relation would require implementation, runtime architecture, universal artifact ontology, ownership, or a universal movement model.

### Preserved unknowns

- Whether constitutional state is canonical vocabulary or only a useful characterization for present lawful conditions.
- Whether every family uses the same minimum constitutional profile.
- Whether all artifact-carried profiles can be described by one stable vocabulary without creating a universal artifact ontology.
- Whether some local `State -> Movement -> Artifact -> Movement` instances exist with stronger evidence.
- Whether later bounded movement changes state, emits another artifact, stops, or merely relies without further output in a given family.
- Whether admissibility deserves a separate recovered artifact at this resolution.

### Confidence

High confidence:

- constitutional state is safest as present bounded lawful condition;
- constrained movement is family-local and authority-bound;
- movement and artifact emission are distinct;
- artifacts can carry constitutional profiles;
- later consumption is lawful only when local authority and preserved profile support the role;
- the universal `State -> Movement -> Artifact -> Movement` sequence is unsupported.

Medium confidence:

- the phrase `artifact-carried constitutional profile` accurately names the recurring profile without becoming ontology;
- the recurring relation can be summarized as state-conditioned, movement-constrained, profile-preserving, consumption-bounded lawful relation.

Low confidence:

- any stronger canonical sequence;
- any universal producer/consumer model;
- any runtime or implementation architecture.

## Smallest supported constitutional distinctions

1. **Constitutional state is not movement.** It is a present bounded lawful condition under constitutional rule and repository evidence.
2. **Constrained movement is not state.** It is a family-local lawful change or refusal governed by evidence, authority, preservation, confidence, unknowns, and stop conditions.
3. **Artifact emission is not movement itself.** It may preserve a movement result or profile, but emission does not equal constitutional movement and does not automatically grant authority.
4. **Artifact-carried profiles are not universal ontology.** They are bounded preserved conditions that may include subject, support, provenance, role, authority, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, and stop limits.
5. **Artifact consumption is not automatic movement.** It becomes lawful later bounded movement only where the consumer has local authority and the artifact profile supports the role.
6. **Lawful refusal and lawful stop are first-class constitutional outcomes.** They preserve boundaries rather than failing the investigation.

## Final answer

According to recurring
repository evidence,

what constitutional
relationship,

if any,

exists between

constitutional state,

constrained movement,

artifact emission,

artifact-carried constitutional
profiles,

and

later bounded movement?

A present bounded lawful condition may permit, refuse, or stop a family-local constrained movement. A constrained movement may, but need not, emit or preserve a bounded artifact. An emitted artifact may carry a constitutional profile: subject, support, provenance, role, authority boundary, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, stop conditions, and non-promotion limits where the local evidence supports them. A later participant may consume that artifact only within local authority and only to the extent the carried profile supports the bounded role. Later bounded movement is therefore profile-supported and authority-local, not automatic.

The repository does not support a universal:

```text
State
↓
Movement
↓
Artifact
↓
Movement
```

It supports the smaller relation:

```text
bounded lawful condition
  may constrain
local lawful movement or stop
  may preserve
bounded artifact-carried constitutional profile
  may authorize, limit, or refuse
later local bounded reliance or movement
```

Constitutional constrained movement reconciliation complete.
