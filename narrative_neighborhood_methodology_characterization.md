## Central answer

Stable narrative neighborhoods recur during architectural recovery as **temporary relational inquiry fixtures**.

That phrase is intentionally smaller than storytelling, metaphor generation, communication, architecture, implementation, competency, projection, or inquiry result.

A narrative neighborhood supplies a stable identity-rich environment in which a relationship can be held still, compared, stressed, and translated without merging the narrative identity into architecture. When the relationship is recovered, the neighborhood is released. What survives is not the story. What survives is the relationship, boundary, contrast, refusal, or question shape that repository evidence can continue to support.

The recurring role is therefore:

```text
stable narrative identity
  -> bounded relational comparison surface
  -> relationship testing / compression / orientation
  -> recovered relationship retained only if repository evidence supports it
  -> narrative neighborhood released
```

## Temporary inquiry surface analysis

The recurring pattern is supported as methodology, not architecture:

```text
Neighborhood A
+
Neighborhood B
↓
Temporary shared inquiry surface
↓
Relationship testing
↓
Identity preserved
↓
Recovered relationship retained if repository evidence supports it
↓
Neighborhoods separate
```

Examples of the pattern:

- Constitution + courtroom creates a temporary surface for admissibility, authority, evidence, and judgment. The retained relationship is evidence-gated authority, not a legal subsystem.
- Stars + navigation creates a temporary surface for topology and orientation. The retained relationship is remembered adjacency, not astronomical architecture.
- Scout + systems administration creates a temporary surface for read-only probing and reporting. The retained relationship is diagnostic observation without mutation, not a scout component.
- Bridge + implementation-recovery slices creates a temporary surface for owner handoff. The retained relationship is bounded handoff, not a universal bridge object.
- Halloween + architectural vocabulary creates a temporary surface for testing presentation identity. The retained relationship is vocabulary caution, not a costume ontology.

The pattern does not always complete. Some stories recur only as communication aids or memory aids. The lawful characterization is narrower: when a story materially changes investigation direction, it does so by making a relationship testable without requiring architectural merger.

## Relationship compression analysis

Narrative neighborhoods repeatedly function as relational compression, but compression alone is insufficient.

They compress:

- identity under repeated use;
- boundary and authority distinctions;
- adjacency and topology;
- probe/report/mutation differences;
- handoff and bridge structure;
- candidate/unknown/refusal discipline;
- presentation-vs-knowledge distinctions.

They orient by giving the investigator a stable local neighborhood in which to ask smaller questions:

```text
What is the owner?
What changed?
What did not change?
What crosses the boundary?
What remains only presentation?
What survives translation?
```

They scaffold inquiry by preventing premature collapse. A constellation permits relation without merging stars. A courtroom permits evidence testing without making every report a verdict. A scout permits observation without command. A bridge permits crossing while preserving banks.

They are also communication and memory aids, but communication alone does not explain recurrence. The same neighborhoods recur because they preserve relationship shape across investigations. If they were only prose, no relationship would consistently survive after release.

## Neighbor analysis

The reviewed recurrence supports these non-identities:

```text
Narrative Neighborhood != Architecture
```

A narrative neighborhood can illuminate an architectural relationship. It is not itself recovered architecture.

```text
Narrative Neighborhood != Implementation
```

A story never proves code, runtime behavior, CLI behavior, event mutation, projection behavior, or diagnostic shape. Implementation-backed evidence remains required.

```text
Narrative Neighborhood != Competency
```

A recurring story is not a Seed capability, evaluator, planner, or constitutional office. At most it is a methodological aid used by an investigator.

```text
Narrative Neighborhood != Projection
```

Projection is an implemented event-to-read-model concern. Narrative projection of relationships into a story is only an inquiry act unless implementation evidence says otherwise.

```text
Narrative Neighborhood != Inquiry Result
```

The story is not the answer. It is released after the result is recovered.

```text
Narrative Neighborhood != Recovered Relationship
```

The relationship may survive translation; the mythic or narrative identity should not.

These distinctions recur because repository work repeatedly rejects promotion from vocabulary, presentation, recurrence, pressure, or explanatory usefulness alone.

## Smallest truthful answer

The smallest recurring methodological role performed by stable narrative neighborhoods during architectural recovery is:

```text
temporary relationship-preserving inquiry fixture
```

A stable narrative neighborhood holds identity and relation long enough for comparison. It lets the investigator test whether a relationship is real, transferable, bounded, and separable. It does not itself become architecture.

What survives after the story is gone:

```text
the recovered relationship, boundary, contrast, refusal, or unknown
that repository evidence can still support without the story
```
