## 2. Legal-advice analysis

Repository evidence also does not currently preserve `legal advice` as an implemented model. The lawful recovery must therefore remain narrow and negative.

For this investigation, `legal advice` is a stronger advice-like claim form that would tell a person, entity, or audience what legal position to take, what legal consequence applies, what legal action to pursue or avoid, what compliance posture to adopt, or what personal legal reliance is warranted under a jurisdiction, time, professional role, source authority, and audience-specific context.

Current repository evidence does not authorize Seed to make that move. It supports analysis, explanation, support-gap preservation, constitutional pressure, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop. It does not support legal counsel, litigation strategy, compliance commands, personal legal reliance, or professional legal judgment.

## 3. What distinguishes bounded legal-source analysis from legal advice?

Bounded legal-source analysis is evidence-facing and non-promotional:

```text
source S says X;
source role R is preserved or Unknown;
support path P is preserved or missing;
claim form C is supported only to strength K;
legal effect/advice remains Unknown;
Seed stops.
```

Legal advice is audience-facing and action/effect-facing:

```text
you should do A;
you may rely on X legally;
this is your legal obligation;
this is the correct legal position for your circumstances.
```

The constitutional work is the same non-promotion boundary recovered in analytical competence, warrant, public packet readiness, and legal authority investigations: useful analysis does not become authority for a stronger claim unless the stronger claim's authority path is separately preserved.

## 4. What distinguishes source-gap reporting from recommended legal action?

A source gap reports that the repository-visible material lacks sufficient support for a specified claim form. It can preserve missing constitutional text, amendment text, adoption or enactment path, holding boundary, procedural posture, jurisdiction, temporal authority, source independence, negative authority review, or currentness.

Recommended legal action tells someone what to file, sue, appeal, comply with, refuse, ignore, challenge, preserve, disclose, or rely upon. Repository evidence blocks that conversion. A missing support path may justify only:

```text
support for claim C is absent, insufficient, dependent, stale, mismatched, or Unknown within the reviewed boundary.
```

It does not justify:

```text
take legal action A because of the gap.
```

## 5. What distinguishes constitutional pressure from litigation instruction?

Constitutional pressure is pressure on Seed's own lawful reasoning boundary. It tells Seed to preserve mismatch, source-of-law gaps, negative authority, confidence limits, Unknowns, and lawful stop.

Litigation instruction is an external action directive about courts, claims, pleadings, remedies, procedural steps, evidence use, strategy, timing, or party conduct. Repository evidence does not give Seed litigation authority merely because Seed detects constitutional pressure. The lawful form is:

```text
constitutional pressure is preserved; litigation consequences remain Unknown.
```

## 6. What distinguishes lawful Seed reliance from personal legal reliance?

Seed-lawful reliance is internal bounded use of warranted repository material. It depends on evidence binding, provenance/support, role, authority boundary, negative authority, Unknowns, confidence, and stop discipline.

Personal legal reliance is a human or entity relying on a conclusion for rights, duties, litigation, compliance, risk, or real-world action. That requires audience, jurisdiction, facts, professional role, legal-source support, temporal authority, and advice authority that repository evidence does not currently preserve.

Seed may rely on an artifact for the narrow proposition that `this support gap was preserved in this bounded investigation`. It may not convert that into `a person may legally rely on this as counsel`.

## 7. What distinguishes explanation from professional legal judgment?

Explanation is a bounded account of evidence, source roles, reasoning path, confidence, Unknowns, and stop conditions. It can explain why a claim is unsupported inside Seed.

Professional legal judgment would require a professional role, jurisdictional competence, client or audience context, facts, duties, current law, negative authority, legal-source support, and authority to apply legal training to a person's circumstances. Repository evidence does not preserve those conditions. Therefore an explanation may be useful and still remain non-professional, non-adjudicative, non-binding, and non-advice.

## 8. What distinguishes confidence in analysis from advice authority?

Confidence calibrates support for the bounded analytical statement. Advice authority is permission or competence to give advice-like legal guidance to an audience.

High confidence that a source-of-law gap exists does not establish that a user should sue, comply, refuse, file, appeal, ignore, or rely. High confidence in a boundary mismatch does not establish legal invalidity. High confidence in an explanation does not create attorney-client context or professional role.

Confidence can strengthen only the claim it is attached to. It cannot upgrade the claim form from analysis to advice.

## 9. What distinguishes general public-world packet review from user-specific legal counsel?

General public-world packet review is pre-ingestion discipline. It may inspect packet boundaries, material identity, source identity, source role, source authority, claim form, support path, independence basis, contradiction markers, temporal provenance, confidence limits, Unknowns, and lawful stop.

User-specific legal counsel applies law to a particular person's facts, rights, duties, risks, goals, jurisdiction, deadlines, procedural posture, and available remedies. Repository evidence supports packet readiness as bounded review material, not counsel. Operator curation and packet inclusion are review pressure, not evidence of truth, legal authority, or personal reliance entitlement.

## 10. What distinguishes legal-source analysis from compliance instruction?

Legal-source analysis preserves what a legal source says, what role it appears to have, what support path is visible, what source-of-law gaps remain, and what claim forms are or are not supported.

Compliance instruction tells an audience what the law requires them to do, not do, document, report, disclose, retain, change, or certify. That requires current applicable law, jurisdiction, regulated party identity, facts, exemptions, enforcement context, competent professional role, and advice authority. None is supplied merely by source analysis.

The lawful output is `the source analysis does not support a compliance instruction`. The unlawful promotion is `therefore comply or do not comply in this way`.

## 11. What distinguishes identifying Unknowns from advising someone what to do?

Identifying Unknowns preserves missing evidence as missing. It prevents Seed from inventing source roles, currentness, jurisdiction, authority, independence, legal effect, or advice.

Advising someone what to do resolves or operationalizes those Unknowns into action despite missing authority. Repository evidence treats Unknown preservation as a lawful result, not a defect to infer through. Therefore Unknowns support stop, not advice.

## 14. Role of audience, jurisdiction, professional role, source authority, claim form, temporal provenance, confidence, negative authority, and Unknown preservation

- **Audience** bounds whether a statement is general analysis or directed guidance. Missing audience blocks advice-like claims.
- **Jurisdiction** bounds legal applicability. Missing jurisdiction leaves legal effect Unknown.
- **Professional role** bounds whether the speaker has advice competence or duties. Seed has no preserved professional legal role.
- **Source authority** bounds what a source can support. Source-local authority is not general truth or advice authority.
- **Claim form** controls strength. `source says X`, `support for X is missing`, `X is law`, and `you should do X` are different claims.
- **Temporal provenance** controls currentness and applicable time. A timestamp, publication date, retrieval date, occurrence date, and effective date are not interchangeable.
- **Confidence** calibrates the bounded analytical claim only. It does not grant advice authority.
- **Negative authority** states what the artifact must not become: truth oracle, validity ruling, compliance instruction, litigation instruction, personal reliance, or professional judgment.
- **Unknown preservation** keeps missing authority and missing facts visible, preventing inference into advice.

## 15. Lawful stop

The lawful stop condition is triggered whenever the artifact would need to move from:

```text
analysis / explanation / source-role preservation / support-gap reporting / constitutional pressure / Unknown preservation
```

to:

```text
legal advice / litigation instruction / compliance instruction / recommended legal action / personal legal reliance / professional legal judgment
```

without separately preserved audience, jurisdiction, attorney-client or non-attorney boundary, professional role, source authority, legal-source support, claim form, temporal authority, confidence limits, negative authority, Unknowns, and output-surface authority.

At that point Seed may preserve the useful analysis and must stop before advice.
