## Recurring prerequisite relationships

Across the independent testimony, recurring prerequisite relationships are:

1. **Bounded question before inquiry movement.** A lawful inquiry commonly requires a bounded question, selected subject, visible terrain, exact family, or concrete ask before proceeding.

2. **Prior evidence before answer.** Inquiry responsibilities recur around already-present evidence: prior artifacts, implementation-backed surfaces, projected material, candidates, source and consumer sides, preserved notes, or existing audit outputs.

3. **Prior warrant or authorization before stronger resolution.** Scout, Survey, Characterization, Survey Warrant, Question Family, bounded ask, Selection, and bridge inquiries recur in requiring some scoped warrant, exact registration, eligibility, selected artifact, or bounded authority before moving.

4. **Prior artifact before crossing.** Bridge, bounded ask, Question Family, Orientation, and Selection evidence recur that a specific artifact or answer surface must exist before neighboring responsibility can proceed.

5. **Prior boundary before lawful use.** Inquiry responsibilities repeatedly require an already visible boundary excluding overreach: no ownership inference, no implementation recovery, no semantic routing, no runtime authority, no authority grant, no promotion from presentation vocabulary to knowledge, and no unsupported crossing.

6. **Prior visibility before confidence.** Confidence recurs only after evidence, candidates, warrant, boundary, or artifact visibility is sufficient; otherwise the result preserves unknowns.

7. **Prior unknown-preservation path before termination.** Lawful inquiry requires the ability to stop honestly when evidence does not support movement.

## Recurring differences

- Scout requires visible terrain and bounded search pressure, while Survey more often requires prior warrant or local surface.
- Characterization requires already surveyed or investigated comparative evidence, not merely first sightings.
- Question Shape can proceed from recurring form-level evidence, while Question Family requires exact public answerability evidence.
- Survey Warrant inquiry depends on recurrence across prior investigation artifacts, while bounded ask invocation depends on exact selected family and supplied parameters.
- Orientation depends on preserved prose and related existing material, while Bridge inquiry depends on independently visible source-side and consumer-side artifact evidence.
- Selection depends on candidates and criteria, while Competency Interrogation depends on an evidence-backed reason to interrogate a selected competency.
- Some confidence is high where implementation-backed public surfaces or audited bridges exist; confidence is lower where evidence is methodological, form-level, or explicitly incomplete.

## Recurring similarities

- Each surveyed inquiry requires something already visible before lawful movement.
- Each surveyed inquiry treats evidence as prior to stronger claims.

- Each surveyed inquiry preserves unknowns or stop conditions.
- Each surveyed inquiry refuses to let vocabulary alone create authority.
- Each surveyed inquiry supports the constitutional progression in which Scout locates terrain, Survey increases bounded resolution, and Characterization compares already recovered evidence.
