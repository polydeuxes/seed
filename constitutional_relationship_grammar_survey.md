## Stage 2 — Independent Relationship Characterizations

Each relationship is characterized independently. This section does not compare relationships.

### constrains

- **Constitutional purpose:** Prevents a bounded participant from being used, moved, admitted, selected, projected, or relied on beyond the support that repository evidence and authority allow.
- **Constitutional authority:** Repository evidence, authority boundaries, evidence-strength limits, eligibility status, negative authority, confidence limits, and preserved unknowns.
- **Constitutional boundaries:** Local and participant-specific. Constraint does not create ownership, implementation, or universal ordering.
- **Required preservation:** The constraining boundary, the reason for the constraint, any unknowns, confidence, and stop/refusal status.
- **Negative authority:** Does not authorize movement; does not promote vocabulary; does not infer missing evidence.
- **Confidence:** High.

### preserves

- **Constitutional purpose:** Carries constitutionally relevant limits and support across local use, transition, translation, projection, answer composition, or handoff.
- **Constitutional authority:** Prior evidence and warrant requiring provenance, source, support, role, authority boundary, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, and stop limits to remain attached.
- **Constitutional boundaries:** Preservation is not proof of truth, registration, implementation, or mutation. Preserved material may remain unknown or limited.
- **Required preservation:** Evidence, provenance, support relation, authority boundary, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, locality, and stop conditions where applicable.
- **Negative authority:** Does not resolve unknowns by carrying them; does not turn diagnostic findings into cluster truth; does not make emitted artifacts lawful movement by itself.
- **Confidence:** High.

### authorizes

- **Constitutional purpose:** Grants or recognizes bounded lawful permission for use, reliance, admission, movement, selection, promotion, or answer within a defined role.
- **Constitutional authority:** Evidence-bound warrant, competent local authority, explicit registered surface metadata where applicable, and preserved authority boundaries.
- **Constitutional boundaries:** Authorization is role-local and evidence-bounded. It is not global permission, ownership, runtime control, or universal authority.
- **Required preservation:** Source of authority, role/scope, evidence support, boundary, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, and stop/refusal limits.
- **Negative authority:** Does not authorize stronger claims than evidence supports; does not authorize hidden engines or universal pipelines.
- **Confidence:** High.

### limits

- **Constitutional purpose:** Narrows claim strength, role, scope, downstream reliance, projection, admission, and movement to the smallest repository-supported use.
- **Constitutional authority:** Evidence-strength discipline, current lawful condition boundaries, admissibility and eligibility status, confidence, unknowns, and negative authority.
- **Constitutional boundaries:** Limits are not participants and do not define a sequence. They may coexist with authorization or support.
- **Required preservation:** The limiting condition and its effect on allowed claim/use strength.
- **Negative authority:** Does not silently become refusal unless the evidence requires stop; does not become implementation policy.
- **Confidence:** High.

### refuses

- **Constitutional purpose:** Lawfully stops unsupported movement, overreach, inference, admission, selection, reliance, projection, or vocabulary promotion.
- **Constitutional authority:** Negative authority, inadmissibility, insufficient evidence, missing required identity/arguments, unsupported candidate status, boundary mismatch, contradiction, or preserved unknown.
- **Constitutional boundaries:** Refusal is a lawful result, not a failure to answer. It does not decide unrelated participants.
- **Required preservation:** Refusal reason, unsupported claim or candidate, boundary violated, unknowns, confidence, and any permitted narrower answer.
- **Negative authority:** Does not imply the opposite claim is true; does not infer a missing relationship; does not produce a universal stop rule.
- **Confidence:** High.

### admits

- **Constitutional purpose:** Allows bounded material through a competent local boundary after required checks, so downstream local use may proceed under preserved limits.
- **Constitutional authority:** Admissibility, lawful acceptance, warrant, compatibility, evidence, local boundary, and eligibility evidence.
- **Constitutional boundaries:** Admission is local and role-specific. It is not identical to authorization, reliance, mutation, or implementation.
- **Required preservation:** Admitted role, boundary, evidence/warrant, compatibility limits, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, and stop conditions.
- **Negative authority:** Does not admit unknown or unsupported material by attraction; does not grant global downstream use.
- **Confidence:** High.

### supports

- **Constitutional purpose:** Supplies bounded evidence or recurrence sufficient to justify a claim, warrant, selection, transition, answer, or refusal at a calibrated strength.
- **Constitutional authority:** Repository evidence, source/provenance, fact support, implementation-visible evidence where already recovered, recurrence across bounded artifacts, and confidence calibration.
- **Constitutional boundaries:** Support is not truth by itself and not authority by itself where authority is separately required.
- **Required preservation:** Evidence identity, provenance, support relation, claim strength, confidence, contradictions, unknowns, and limits.
- **Negative authority:** Does not promote presentation vocabulary; does not fill gaps; does not prove universal grammar.
- **Confidence:** High.

### relies

- **Constitutional purpose:** Allows a downstream bounded participant to use warranted or admitted content for a specific role.
- **Constitutional authority:** Warrant and admission under preserved evidence, role, authority boundary, confidence, and unknown limits.
- **Constitutional boundaries:** Reliance is downstream use, not the evidence itself, not warrant itself, and not mutation.
- **Required preservation:** Warrant profile, relied-on content, downstream role, authority boundary, unknowns, confidence, and stop/refusal conditions.
- **Negative authority:** Does not use content outside warranted role; does not continue if contradiction or boundary loss defeats reliance.
- **Confidence:** High.

### projects

- **Constitutional purpose:** Presents or derives a bounded current view, answer, or projection from preserved history/support without claiming direct source truth.
- **Constitutional authority:** Preserved history, projection/read-model boundaries, derivation support, lineage, confidence, and current-lawful-condition limits.
- **Constitutional boundaries:** Projection is not source observation, verification, mutation, complete interpretation, or universal state.
- **Required preservation:** Lineage, source/support, derivation boundary, confidence, unknowns, currentness or temporal limits, and non-mutation limits.
- **Negative authority:** Does not promote projected vocabulary into knowledge without evidence; does not make diagnostic output cluster truth.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high.

### selects

- **Constitutional purpose:** Chooses a bounded identity, surface, candidate, warrant family, or answerable form under local eligibility and evidence constraints.
- **Constitutional authority:** Exact identity, registered metadata where applicable, bounded ask eligibility, evidence strength, selection path evidence, and local authority boundary.
- **Constitutional boundaries:** Selection is plural and boundary-specific. It does not imply a hidden selector or candidate-to-registration pipeline.
- **Required preservation:** Candidate/target identity, eligibility basis, selected and non-selected status where available, authority boundary, unknowns, confidence, and refusal reasons.
- **Negative authority:** Does not create registration; does not infer unknown families; does not select from prose alone.
- **Confidence:** High.

### reconciles

- **Constitutional purpose:** Resolves pressure between candidate distinctions by preserving supported distinctions, refusing unsupported collapse, and carrying unknowns.
- **Constitutional authority:** Prior bounded investigations, recurrence, contradiction evidence, compression/reconciliation discipline, negative authority, and confidence calibration.
- **Constitutional boundaries:** Reconciliation is not ontology creation, implementation, or final vocabulary ratification.
- **Required preservation:** Compared candidates, supported distinctions, refused collapses, preserved unknowns, confidence, and remaining boundaries.
- **Negative authority:** Does not force one umbrella; does not erase local differences; does not infer transitive closure.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high.

### consumes

- **Constitutional purpose:** Describes downstream bounded use of admitted/warranted material by an inquiry, reliance, transition, projection, answer, or diagnostic surface.
- **Constitutional authority:** The consumer's local boundary plus the warrant/admission profile of the consumed material.
- **Constitutional boundaries:** Consumption is not ownership and not stronger authority than the consumed material carries.
- **Required preservation:** Consumer role, consumed material, warrant/admission limits, source/provenance, unknowns, confidence, and stop limits.
- **Negative authority:** Does not consume unsupported material; does not erase provenance; does not broaden role.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high.

### emits

- **Constitutional purpose:** Produces a bounded artifact, output, report, handoff, answer, diagnostic row, or profile that may carry constitutional limits.
- **Constitutional authority:** Local surface authority, investigation boundary, diagnostic/reporting boundary, or artifact-emission evidence.
- **Constitutional boundaries:** Emission is not identical to lawful movement, admission, truth, mutation, or cluster knowledge.
- **Required preservation:** Emitting surface, output boundary, recordability or non-recordability where relevant, provenance, authority limits, unknowns, confidence, and mutation boundary.
- **Negative authority:** Does not prove the emitted material may be relied on unless warrant/admission supports reliance.
- **Confidence:** Medium.

## Stage 3 — Relationship Topology

This topology records only recurring neighbors, incompatibilities, local sequencing, independent relationships, and preserved unknowns. It does not recover a universal sequence.

### Recurring neighboring relationships

- **supports** often neighbors **authorizes**, **limits**, **constrains**, and **refuses** because evidence strength can warrant bounded use, narrow it, or stop it.
- **authorizes** often neighbors **relies**, **admits**, **selects**, **consumes**, and **limits** because lawful use generally requires preserved authority boundaries.
- **preserves** often neighbors all other relationships because evidence, provenance, unknowns, confidence, and negative authority must remain attached across local movement or output.
- **refuses** often neighbors **limits**, **constrains**, **preserves**, and **reconciles** because unsupported material is stopped while the reason and unknowns are preserved.
- **admits** often neighbors **authorizes**, **supports**, **limits**, **relies**, and **consumes** because admitted material may be locally used only under preserved warrant.
- **selects** often neighbors **supports**, **constrains**, **admits**, and **refuses** because selection depends on identity, eligibility, authority, and sufficient support.
- **projects** often neighbors **preserves**, **supports**, **limits**, and **refuses** because projection depends on preserved lineage and refuses source-truth or mutation claims.
- **emits** often neighbors **preserves**, **limits**, **supports**, and sometimes **admits** or **relies**, but emission alone does not establish those latter relationships.
- **reconciles** often neighbors **preserves**, **refuses**, **limits**, and **supports** because reconciliation keeps supported distinctions and refuses unsupported collapse.

### Recurring incompatibilities

- **emits** is incompatible with being treated as automatic lawful movement.
- **supports** is incompatible with automatic truth, global authority, or participant promotion.
- **authorizes** is incompatible with global permission where the evidence is local.
- **admits** is incompatible with unbounded downstream reliance.
- **selects** is incompatible with hidden universal selection machinery or automatic registration.
- **projects** is incompatible with source observation, verification, mutation, or complete preserved interpretation.
- **relies** is incompatible with use beyond the warranted role, confidence, unknowns, and authority boundary.
- **reconciles** is incompatible with erasing preserved distinctions or forcing a universal umbrella.
- **preserves** is incompatible with silently resolving unknowns.
- **refuses** is incompatible with treating unsupported material as negatively proven opposite truth.

### Recurring lawful sequencing where directly supported

The evidence supports only local order constraints:

- Evidence/support commonly precedes warrant-backed **authorizes** or **relies**.
- Identity/eligibility commonly precedes local **selects** or **admits**.
- Boundary and negative authority commonly precede or accompany **limits**, **constrains**, and **refuses**.
- **preserves** must accompany movement, handoff, projection, translation, reliance, or emitted outputs where constitutional limits matter.
- Insufficient support, missing authority, inadmissibility, contradiction, or unresolved unknown can lawfully sequence to **refuses** or stop.
- **emits** may occur without **admits** or **relies**; if later use occurs, separate warrant/admission is required.

No reviewed evidence supports one universal relationship sequence.

### Recurring independent relationships

The following relationships recur independently enough not to collapse into each other:

- **supports** is not **authorizes**; evidence support may still lack competent authority.
- **authorizes** is not **admits**; authorization may define permission while admission is local boundary passage.
- **admits** is not **relies**; admission permits local entry/use status while reliance is downstream bounded use.
- **preserves** is not **projects**; preservation carries limits, projection derives or presents a view.
- **emits** is not **moves** or **admits**; emitted artifacts may carry profiles without proving lawful movement.
- **selects** is not **registers**; current evidence supports explicit registration only in specific public answerability contexts.
- **refuses** is not **limits**; a limit may narrow use, while refusal stops unsupported use.
- **reconciles** is not **collapses**; reconciliation preserves supported distinctions.

### Preserved unknowns

- Whether the recovered relationship vocabulary is complete remains unknown.
- Whether some relationships require different names in narrower families remains unknown.
- Whether every recovered participant participates in every recovered relationship is unsupported.
- Whether any relationship should become canonical constitutional vocabulary is not decided by this survey.
- Whether projection, emission, selection, and consumption share one deeper connective family remains unsupported.
- Whether future repository evidence will split or merge any relationship remains open.
- Whether any implementation surface should expose this relationship grammar is outside this survey and unsupported.

## Stage 4 — Reconciliation

### Recurring constitutional relationship grammar

Recurring repository evidence supports a smallest bounded relationship grammar:

```text
bounded participant
  may be supported by evidence,
  constrained and limited by authority/boundary/unknown/confidence,
  authorized only for a local role,
  admitted only through a competent local boundary,
  relied on only within warrant,
  selected only under local identity/eligibility/evidence,
  projected only from preserved support/history under projection limits,
  consumed only by a permitted downstream role,
  emitted only as bounded output,
  reconciled only by preserving supported distinctions,
  refused when support, authority, admissibility, identity, or boundary is insufficient,
  and preserved with evidence, provenance, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, and stop conditions.
```

This is grammar in the limited sense of recurring constitutional connective work. It is not an implementation grammar, runtime grammar, workflow, object model, or state machine.

### Recurring distinctions between participants and relationships

- Participants are bounded constitutional materials or positions already recovered: present bounded lawful condition, constrained movement, bounded constitutional artifacts, artifact-carried profiles, lawful reliance, admissibility, and bounded local authority.
- Relationships are connective work between participants: support, constraint, preservation, authorization, admission, reliance, projection, selection, reconciliation, consumption, emission, limitation, and refusal.
- A relationship can attach to different participants without becoming a participant itself.
- A participant can participate in several relationships without those relationships collapsing into the participant.
- Relationship recurrence does not authorize a new owner, surface, engine, object, state, workflow, or universal sequence.

### Recurring preservation rules

When a recovered relationship is used, recurring evidence requires preservation of whichever limits make the relationship lawful:

- evidence and source/provenance;
- bounded subject or target identity;
- authority boundary and local role;
- negative authority and refused overreach;
- eligibility/admissibility where relevant;
- support strength, confidence, and contradiction state;
- unknowns and unresolved questions;
- recordability, event-ledger, and mutation boundaries where output or diagnostics are involved;
- stop/refusal conditions.

### Recurring stop conditions

Repository evidence supports stopping or refusing when:

- evidence is insufficient for the claim strength;
- authority is missing or local authority is exceeded;
- subject, target, question family, or required arguments are unknown or unsupported;
- admission or eligibility fails;
- projection would be mistaken for source truth or mutation;
- emission would be mistaken for lawful movement;
- reliance would exceed warrant;
- selection would infer an unknown family or registration;
- reconciliation would collapse preserved distinctions;
- unknowns would be silently resolved.

### Confidence

Confidence is **high** that recurring repository evidence supports constitutional relationships as distinct connective work between already recovered participants.

Confidence is **high** for the smallest relationships **constrains**, **preserves**, **authorizes**, **limits**, **refuses**, **admits**, **supports**, **relies**, and **selects**.

Confidence is **medium-high** for **projects**, **reconciles**, and **consumes** as recurring relationships because the evidence is strong in multiple neighborhoods but more dependent on local surface boundaries.

Confidence is **medium** for **emits** as a constitutional relationship because movement-versus-artifact-emission evidence strongly distinguishes emission from movement, while emission's constitutional role depends on local output, diagnostic, handoff, or artifact boundaries.

Confidence is **low** for any stronger claim that these relationships form a complete vocabulary, universal order, object model, runtime grammar, or implementation architecture.
