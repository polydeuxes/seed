## Stage 1 -- Independent Family Testimony

Each family testifies independently in this section. No family is used to interpret another family in Stage 1.

### 1. Provider grammar

- **Testimony source:** Provider output, provider-local syntax, provider contract evidence, and provider-language translation investigations.
- **Preserved testimony artifact:** Source-attributed provider material, provider-language candidate, parsed or translated observation candidate, provenance, validation boundary, unsupported provider vocabulary, and confidence where preserved.
- **Translation boundary:** Provider syntax can become Seed-local observation or evidence only through a bounded translation that preserves provenance and authority limits. Provider form does not become Seed truth.
- **Comparison boundary:** Provider testimony is comparable only after its own translation boundary is preserved. Raw provider vocabulary is not directly comparable as constitutional content.
- **Reconciliation boundary:** Reconciliation can classify provider assumptions, unsupported terms, preserved observations, or contradictions only within repository evidence. It cannot treat provider fluency as authority.
- **Warrant produced or preserved:** Warrant is limited to evidence-bound reliance on translated provider material under preserved provenance, validation, support, and negative authority.
- **Lawful downstream reliance:** Downstream work may use provider-derived evidence only as provider-scoped, translation-bounded material. It may not rely on provider syntax as repository knowledge.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Provider intent, complete semantics, source clocks, latent provider assumptions, unsupported provider vocabulary, and the completeness of any provider contract.
- **Lawful stop:** Stop when provider material lacks provenance, translation support, authority, confidence, or a repository-supported acceptance boundary.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high for the provider translation boundary; medium for completeness of provider-family recurrence.

### 2. Prose / report grammar

- **Testimony source:** Operator prose, constitutional reports, audits, surveys, characterizations, report sections, reviewed-evidence lists, supported/unsupported conclusions, preserved Unknowns, and confidence sections.
- **Preserved testimony artifact:** Carrier text, bounded claim, report section role, cited evidence, negative authority, preserved Unknowns, lawful stop, and confidence where the report preserves them.
- **Translation boundary:** Prose is expression and carrier before it is warrant. Report vocabulary must be translated through its evidence, boundary, authority, Unknown, stop, and confidence profile.
- **Comparison boundary:** Prose artifacts can be compared only after each report's own evidence and boundary are inspected. Repeated labels or fluent wording do not by themselves become comparison evidence.
- **Reconciliation boundary:** Reconciliation may compare report claims, assumptions, contradictions, and unsupported conclusions, but may not collapse narrative similarity into repository truth.
- **Warrant produced or preserved:** Report warrant is bounded by reviewed evidence, explicit authority, negative authority, and confidence. A report may preserve constitutional recovery without making an implementation-visible surface.
- **Lawful downstream reliance:** Later investigations may rely on report conclusions only in the scope the report supports and must preserve report-local Unknowns and stops.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Whether presentation vocabulary is preserved knowledge, whether a report is stale, whether vocabulary recurs as structure or only style, and whether report-only recovery has implementation visibility.
- **Lawful stop:** Stop when prose lacks evidence, confuses presentation with knowledge, lacks authority, or leaves the claim constitutionally/report-only.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high for prose as a bounded carrier; medium for prose recurrence as implementation-visible preservation.

### 3. Competing testimony grammar

- **Testimony source:** Reconciliation artifacts, competing reports, assumption audits, contradiction characterizations, district/kind cross-examinations, and constitutional comparison artifacts.
- **Preserved testimony artifact:** Scoped testimony, independent claim, competing assumption, contradiction, support path, assumption classification, preserved negative authority, Unknown, confidence, and stop.
- **Translation boundary:** Competing testimony enters as source-attributed testimony or report evidence, not as settled truth. Each testimony must retain its own source, scope, authority, and confidence before cross-examination.
- **Comparison boundary:** Comparison is lawful only after independent testimony. Premature collapse of testimony into agreement or contradiction is refused.
- **Reconciliation boundary:** Reconciliation can classify supported overlap, contradiction, refinement, disproof, and Unknown. It cannot erase disagreement or promote unsupported consensus.
- **Warrant produced or preserved:** Warrant is produced when recurring evidence survives comparison and contradiction handling under authority limits. Otherwise the artifact preserves Unknown or negative authority.
- **Lawful downstream reliance:** Downstream work may rely on reconciled conclusions only within the reconciliation's evidence and authority boundary.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Whether all relevant competing testimony was reviewed, whether contradictions are complete, whether unresolved disagreement is locally resolvable, and whether implementation-visible reconciliation exists.
- **Lawful stop:** Stop at unresolved contradiction, insufficient independent testimony, missing evidence, unsupported authority, or a comparison boundary failure.
- **Confidence:** High that independent-before-comparison is constitutionally preserved; medium for implementation-visible recurrence of competing-testimony reconciliation.

### 4. Repository grammar

- **Testimony source:** Repository files, instructions, committed reports, tests, app-visible read-only inspection, current git state, and repository-local evidence lists.
- **Preserved testimony artifact:** File content, committed artifact, local instruction, reviewed evidence, app output, tests, support path, and repository-authority boundary.
- **Translation boundary:** Repository material testifies through inspection and evidence binding. It does not become true merely because it is in the repository, and prompt vocabulary must yield to implementation evidence.
- **Comparison boundary:** Repository claims can be compared after file-level and artifact-level evidence is independently inspected. Repository-wide completeness is not presumed.
- **Reconciliation boundary:** Conflicting repository artifacts are reconciled by scope, date only where relevant, implementation evidence, tests, authority, Unknowns, and negative authority. Repository authority wins over unsupported metaphor.
- **Warrant produced or preserved:** Repository warrant is bounded by files, tests, instructions, app output, and recurring evidence. It can authorize lawful reliance on existing artifacts within their scope.
- **Lawful downstream reliance:** Later work may rely on inspected repository evidence and instructions, especially direct repository authority and implementation-backed boundaries.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Repository completeness, stale artifacts, unreviewed files, conflicts among reports, and unsupported presentation labels.
- **Lawful stop:** Stop when repository evidence is absent, stale, conflicting without reconciliation, presentation-only, or not implementation-visible.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high for reviewed repository evidence; low for unreviewed repository completeness.

### 5. Implementation grammar

- **Testimony source:** Code, tests, CLI diagnostics, JSON payloads, diagnostic inventory, diagnostic shape audit, implementation recovery slices, local ownership boundaries, and compatibility assertions.
- **Preserved testimony artifact:** Local behavior, record shapes, tested output, local ownership boundary, diagnostic declaration, observed field, record scope, mutation boundary, compatibility handoff, and pressure evidence.
- **Translation boundary:** Implementation behavior is evidence, not self-authorizing constitutional law. Code may inform constitutional pressure through local, tested, repository-visible behavior.
- **Comparison boundary:** Implementation grammar can be compared to constitutional expectations only after the local surface, owner, tests, and boundaries are inspected.
- **Reconciliation boundary:** Reconciliation separates implemented local behavior from constitutional report-only recovery, compressed boundaries, untested paths, and unsupported architecture.
- **Warrant produced or preserved:** Implementation warrant is strongest where tests and diagnostics prove a surface exists and carries its declared boundary. It remains local.
- **Lawful downstream reliance:** Downstream work may rely on tested implementation behavior and diagnostics within their declared surface and compatibility boundary.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Hidden coupling, untested paths, design intent, global ownership, universal architecture, and implementation visibility for report-only constitutional distinctions.
- **Lawful stop:** Stop when code lacks tests, behavior is not visible, ownership is compressed, or a report conclusion lacks implementation artifact support.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high for tested/local implementation evidence; low for universal implementation claims.

### 6. Diagnostic / projection grammar

- **Testimony source:** Diagnostic inventory, diagnostic shape audit, knowledge reachability audit, documentation structure recurrence, projected state/read models, projection diagnostics, and tests around diagnostic visibility.
- **Preserved testimony artifact:** Diagnostic names, CLI flags, JSON support, record support, record scope, diagnostic fact emission, event-ledger write boundary, repository-file use, projected-state use, `mutates_cluster`, shape-audit declared/observed/status rows, reachability loss stages, and read-only structural recurrence output.
- **Translation boundary:** Diagnostic and projection output is bounded visibility. It may expose shape, reachability, recurrence, or consistency but does not become cluster truth, repository knowledge, or constitutional authority by display.
- **Comparison boundary:** Diagnostic surfaces can compare declared and observed fields or expose structural recurrence only within their implemented audit boundary.
- **Reconciliation boundary:** Diagnostic reconciliation is local to declared-versus-observed fields, reachability stages, projection boundaries, or structural recurrence. It does not reconcile all constitutional testimony.
- **Warrant produced or preserved:** Warrant is implementation-visible for the diagnostic surface's own shape and declared boundary, not for broader semantic claims.
- **Lawful downstream reliance:** Downstream work may rely on diagnostic output to know whether a surface is visible, read-only, recordable, event-writing, projected-state-dependent, repository-file-dependent, or non-mutating.
- **Preserved Unknowns:** Semantic meaning of diagnostic labels, completeness of projected state, public compatibility compression, and whether a report-only term is preserved knowledge.
- **Lawful stop:** Stop when a term is only a presentation label, when a surface is not preserved/projected/rendered, or when diagnostic output exposes only structure rather than meaning.
- **Confidence:** High for diagnostic shape boundaries; medium for projection completeness.

## Stage 2 -- Cross-Examination Shape

Only after the independent family testimony above is comparison made.

### Recurring inputs

The recurring inputs are bounded testimony sources: provider output, report/prose artifacts, competing testimony, repository files, implementation behavior, and diagnostic/projection outputs. Each enters as source-attributed material, not as truth.

### Recurring preserved artifacts

The recurring artifact is not a universal testimony object. It is a bounded carrier that preserves some local combination of source, evidence, provenance, claim or candidate, support, authority boundary, negative authority, contradiction or assumption status, Unknowns, stop condition, and confidence.

### Recurring translation discipline

Translation recurs as movement from external expression or local surface vocabulary into repository-bounded evidence while preserving provenance and refusing imported authority. Provider syntax, prose, report vocabulary, repository labels, implementation objects, and diagnostic output all require this boundary.

### Recurring comparison discipline

Comparison recurs only after independent testimony or independent inspection. Families repeatedly refuse direct comparison of raw vocabulary, presentation labels, fluency, or implementation names before each testimony's source, artifact, translation boundary, and authority are preserved.

### Recurring contradiction handling

Contradiction recurs as preserved pressure, not failure. Disagreement, unsupported assumptions, negative authority, diagnostic mismatch, implementation compression, and report conflicts are preserved or classified rather than erased by consensus.

### Recurring reconciliation outputs

The smallest recurring reconciliation outputs are: supported overlap, locally bounded refinement, contradiction preserved, unsupported/disproven portion, implementation-visible local behavior, report-only constitutional recovery, preserved Unknown, and lawful stop.

### Recurring warrant boundaries

Warrant recurs as evidence-bound lawful reliance under preserved authority boundary. It is not provider syntax, prose fluency, repository authorship, implementation existence, diagnostic visibility, or repetition.

### Recurring reliance boundaries

Reliance recurs downstream and bounded. A consumer may rely only on the artifact's preserved warrant, scope, provenance, authority, negative authority, Unknowns, confidence, and stop condition.

### Recurring stop conditions

Stop recurs when provenance, evidence, translation support, authority, comparison independence, contradiction handling, implementation visibility, confidence, or Unknown preservation is insufficient.

### Preserved Unknowns

Recurring Unknowns include completeness of each family, whether presentation vocabulary is preserved knowledge, whether report-only recovery is implementation-visible, whether all contradictions were surfaced, whether implementation behavior is fully tested, whether diagnostics expose meaning or only shape, and whether future local surfaces will preserve narrower boundaries.

### Confidence

Confidence is high that these disciplines recur constitutionally across reviewed families. Confidence is medium that they are implementation-visible as one recurring cross-examination boundary, because current implementation-visible surfaces preserve local agreement, grammar observation, diagnostics, and reachability, but not a reusable cross-examination surface.
