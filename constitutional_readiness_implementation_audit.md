## Audit standard

For each recovered responsibility, this audit asks only:

```text
Has constitutional uncertainty fallen below the point where implementation visibility is now the dominant remaining source of knowledge?
```

A responsibility is classified as one of:

- `Ready for implementation slicing`
- `Requires additional constitutional recovery`
- `Preserve as unknown`

Readiness here means constitutional maturity only. It is not an implementation recommendation.

## Audited constitutional responsibilities

### 1. Observation

#### Constitutional visibility

**Recovered constitutional identity:** Observation orients lawful inquiry and preserves bounded signals without automatically producing fact, truth, ownership, semantic interpretation, implementation authority, or mutation. Observation can expose material that later participates in evidence, warrant, selection, or stop discipline, but observation alone is not admission.

**Confidence:** High.

#### Boundary stability

**Recovered non-responsibilities:** Observation does not own fact creation, semantic interpretation, repository truth, implementation beginning, runtime activation, promotion, scheduling, or global authority.

**Preserved constitutional boundaries:** Observation remains distinct from evidence sufficiency, fact/admission, selection, warrant, Null movement, and visible behavior. The Null-first transition evidence especially preserves raw note presence without promoting it to Observation, Evidence, Fact, attachment, semantic interpretation, ownership, recommendation, plan, or inquiry graph.

**Confidence:** High.

#### Implementation visibility

Recurring repository evidence exposes bounded implementation-visible seams around preservation, orientation, read-only explanation, and non-promotion of observed material. Ownership appears sufficiently localized only where existing surfaces already preserve or render observed material under explicit read-only limits. The broader constitutional role of observation remains distributed across local surfaces rather than centralized.

#### Remaining constitutional uncertainty

Unresolved questions remain about which future surfaces, if any, should make observation boundaries more explicit. Further constitutional recovery is unlikely to materially change the basic observation boundary that observation is orientation and preservation, not truth or authority.

#### Disposition

**Ready for implementation slicing.**

Observation's constitutional identity and non-responsibilities recur strongly enough that implementation visibility is now the dominant remaining source of knowledge for any future bounded work.
