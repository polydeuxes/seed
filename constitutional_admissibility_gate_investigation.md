## Comparison

### Recurring admissibility gates

Recurring repository evidence supports admissibility gates in these forms:

- **Question admissibility:** a possible question becomes eligible only through exact Question Family identity, required arguments, bounded status, and dispatch boundary.
- **Inquiry admissibility:** orientation or pressure becomes bounded inquiry only when reduced to a question with evidence posture, authority boundary, and stop conditions.
- **Implementation admissibility:** recovery becomes implementation only when a smallest local owner/corridor, necessity, compatibility, and testable preservation surface are established.
- **Projection admissibility:** a candidate law or constitutional answer becomes projectable only with evidence, provenance, confidence, unknown preservation, contradiction visibility, and non-projection boundaries.
- **Methodology admissibility:** repeated investigative form becomes repository-supported discipline only with implementation-backed use and explicit negative authority.
- **Transition admissibility:** candidate/support becomes admission only under family-local evidence and authority gates.

### Recurring non-admissibility

Recurring evidence refuses admissibility where there is only:

- presentation vocabulary;
- recurrence without evidence;
- pressure without bounded responsibility;
- operator excitement;
- broad constitutional recovery without local implementation necessity;
- readiness without implementation authorization;
- visibility without authority;
- orientation without dispatch;
- support without admission;
- projection capability without canonical artifact;
- methodology stability without runtime capability.

### Recurring stop conditions

Recurring stop conditions include:

- unknown family;
- missing required parameters;
- diagnostic-only or not-dispatchable surface;
- evidence gap;
- implementation unknown;
- unsupported ownership;
- insufficient compatibility proof;
- unresolved adjacent boundaries;
- local authority absence;
- projection uncertainty or contradiction that must remain visible.

### Recurring implementation refusals

The reviewed progressions repeatedly refuse:

- new owners by recurrence alone;
- new implementation by readiness alone;
- new runtime architecture by answerability;
- canonical vocabulary by presentation label;
- new artifact by projection capability;
- general mechanism by investigation pattern;
- automatic conversion from possible question to running inquiry;
- universal transition, promotion, or readiness engines.

### Recurring projection approvals

Projection is approved only as read-only, bounded, evidence-bearing presentation over already recovered material. Approved projection patterns preserve:

- evidence and provenance;
- confidence;
- uncertainty and unknowns;
- contradiction visibility;
- non-mutation;
- non-ownership;
- non-implementation;
- source/projection distinctions.

Projection approval therefore strengthens admissibility evidence, because projection repeatedly requires a gate and repeatedly refuses implementation.

### Preserved unknowns

- Whether future repository evidence will preserve `Admissibility` as canonical vocabulary remains unknown.
- Whether future implementation will expose an admissibility-related diagnostic or inquiry surface remains unknown.
- Whether every transition family should be described with admissibility vocabulary remains unsupported.
- Whether some implementation recovery cases bypass readiness vocabulary while still satisfying equivalent local gates remains only partially inspected.
- Whether future Question Shapes will be admitted into Question Families remains unknown.
- Whether future Book of Law or Law Projection work will become implementation remains unknown and is not authorized here.

## Determination

Implementation does not consistently follow constitutional recovery directly.

Recurring repository evidence shows an intervening constitutional question before movement: whether the recovered material is admissible to the next bounded state. The next bounded state may be inquiry, dispatch, projection, methodology preservation, admission, or a smallest implementation slice. The gate is local, evidence-bound, negative-authority-preserving, and stop-capable.

The evidence does not support Admissibility as a new owner, office, engine, runtime gate, registry, lifecycle framework, or implementation architecture. It supports Admissibility as a recurring constitutional transition boundary: a question the repository repeatedly asks between recovery and any stronger movement, especially implementation.

Confidence: medium-high.
