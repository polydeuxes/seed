# Constitutional Grammar Topology Survey

## Constitutional Question Grammar Topology

### Constitutional responsibilities

- Determines whether a repository pressure, uncertainty, prompt, possible question, candidate question, or open question may become a lawful bounded constitutional inquiry.
- Requires bounded subject identity before inquiry admission.
- Requires an evidence posture that names admissible repository evidence and preserves absent support.
- Requires explicit authority and negative-authority boundaries before inquiry begins.
- Tests dependencies supplied by earlier questions, including identity, evidence, scope, authority, endpoint, readiness, and stop prerequisites.
- Determines whether recurrence and warrant support the proposed inquiry shape and produced artifact pattern.
- Classifies pressures as lawful inquiry, deferred pressure, preserved Unknown, or lawful stop.
- Identifies the artifact an admitted inquiry may produce without performing downstream recovery.
- Authorizes lawful refusal without treating non-admission as failure.

### Constitutional movements

- Possible question is preserved as candidate pressure without being promoted into command authority.
- Candidate pressure is bound to a bounded subject, target, family, endpoint, artifact, domain, or relation under review.
- Admissible evidence is selected while forbidden evidence domains remain outside scope.
- Prior warrant and dependency are checked before admission.
- Negative authority is made explicit before inquiry begins.
- The candidate is admitted as lawful inquiry, deferred, preserved as Unknown, or stopped.
- The admitted inquiry produces only its admitted artifact.
- Missing support can move into preserved Unknown rather than forced conclusion.
- Unsupported overreach can move into lawful stop.

- Handoff to Orientation when inquiry is admitted.

### Constitutional boundaries

- Question Grammar is not the inquiry itself.
- Question Grammar is not Orientation.
- Question Grammar is not Recovery.
- Question Grammar is not implementation recovery, ownership recovery, runtime behavior, diagnostic inventory, event-ledger write, cluster mutation, natural-language semantic routing, universal question object, universal inquiry engine, planner, scheduler, workflow, topology, responsibility assignment, or participant invention.
- Pressure is not command authority.
- Presentation vocabulary, metaphor, historical attraction, and natural-language form are not constitutional fact without repository support.
- A question may not pretend to produce implementation, ownership, runtime behavior, topology, or participants when the admitted inquiry can only produce a characterization, survey, audit, inventory, reconciliation, admissibility record, slice report, or lawful stop.
- Stop is a positive constitutional result when admission cannot be supported.

### Constitutional dependencies

- Depends on bounded subject identity.
- Depends on admissible repository evidence.
- Depends on warrant, recurrence, and question-shape support.
- Depends on prior questions supplying required identity, evidence, scope, authority, endpoint, readiness, or stop conditions.
- Depends on explicit authority and negative authority.

### Excluded responsibilities

- Does not recover implementation.
- Does not recover ownership.
- Does not create new constitutional participants.
- Does not recover runtime behavior.

- Does not mutate repository or cluster state.

- Does not assign responsibility.
- Does not produce topology.
- Does not prove that presentation vocabulary is repository knowledge.
- Does not create a universal question engine, semantic parser, planner, router, scheduler, workflow, or inquiry object.

Medium-high confidence that the safest current topology is admission before orientation before recovery.

Medium confidence in dependencies that apply only within the admitted question's own bounded evidence and authority posture.

Low confidence for any stronger claim that Question Grammar is an implemented component, universal logic system, planner, router, participant, topology, ownership authority, or runtime system.

## Relationship Grammar Topology

### Constitutional responsibilities

- Characterizes recurring constitutional connective work between already recovered constitutional participants.
- Preserves relationship work as bounded connective operation rather than participant, implementation object, engine, workflow, or runtime grammar.
- Names the smallest supported relationship vocabulary currently visible in repository evidence: supports, constrains, limits, authorizes, admits, relies, preserves, refuses, selects, projects, reconciles, consumes, and emits.
- Requires every relationship use to remain local, evidence-bound, authority-bound, boundary-bound, confidence-bound, Unknown-preserving, and stop-aware.
- Distinguishes relationship recurrence from ownership, implementation, routing, scheduling, execution, orchestration, universal causation, equality, object model, state machine, or transitive closure.
- Records lawful stop conditions when support, authority, admissibility, identity, or boundary is insufficient.

### Constitutional movements

- Evidence may support a bounded claim, warrant, selection, answer, transition, or refusal at calibrated strength.
- Boundary, authority, evidence strength, eligibility, confidence, and Unknowns may constrain or limit participant use.
- Evidence-bound warrant or competent local authority may authorize bounded use within preserved role and scope.
- Competent local boundaries may admit bounded material after checks.
- Downstream participants may rely on warranted or admitted content only within preserved limits.
- Local identity, eligibility, or evidence may select among bounded candidates or surfaces.
- Preserved support or history may project a bounded view while refusing source-truth, verification, mutation, or complete interpretation claims.
- A downstream role may consume only material permitted by its warrant or admission profile.
- A local surface may emit bounded output without emission itself proving lawful movement, admission, truth, mutation, or reliance.
- Reconciliation may preserve supported distinctions, refuse unsupported collapse, and carry Unknowns.
- Unsupported movement, overreach, inference, admission, selection, reliance, projection, or vocabulary promotion may be refused.
- Relevant evidence, provenance, authority boundary, negative authority, Unknowns, confidence, and stop conditions must be preserved across relationship use.
