# Constitutional Act Characterization 001

Repository authority wins.

## Boundary

This characterization answers only the requested question:

```text
How do independently recurring constitutional acts differ by the constitutional effect they perform?
```

It does not change implementation or the Book. It does not promote a final act canon, topology, hierarchy, taxonomy, workflow, pipeline, action engine, mutation policy, cache topology, or universal constitutional lifecycle.

The review began from these locators:

- `constitutional_action_mutation_frontier_survey.md`
- `constitutional_acts_constraints_topology_audit_001.md`
- `constitutional_act_survey.md`

Those reports were treated as evidence locators and prior bounded findings, not as proof that their candidate lists are complete, correctly divided, or canonically named. Repository implementation evidence was inspected separately where the prior reports pointed to live producers, callers, boundary flags, or stop behavior.

## Method

A distinction is characterized here only when repository evidence supports a difference in constitutional effect. Names are therefore secondary. The same implementation operation can realize more than one broader act, and the same broader act can appear through more than one concrete operation.

For each supported distinction this characterization asks:

- what standing or condition is consumed;
- what constitutional effect is performed;
- what standing or condition results;
- what authority and evidence are required;
- what negative or stop result is lawful;
- how constraints relate to the act;
- how concrete implementation operations realize, overlap with, or fail to prove the broader act.

## Core answer

The repository supports distinct constitutional effects, but not a complete list of independent constitutional acts.

The strongest supported characterization is:

```text
Independently recurring constitutional acts differ by the
standing-changing effect they lawfully perform,
not by implementation verb, artifact emission, cache behavior,
action vocabulary, mutation vocabulary, or position in a pipeline.
```

The repository supports:

1. distinct recurring acts where the effect is independently visible;
2. overlapping act families where one surface performs multiple effects or where one effect appears across several surfaces;
3. concrete implementation operations that realize narrower instances of broader acts;
4. preserved Unknowns where topology, independence, or act identity is not proven.

## Supported distinctions by constitutional effect

### 1. Observe / receive

**Consumed standing or condition:** Source material, operator expression, runtime input, repository condition, diagnostic condition, or external material is present to a local surface but is not yet truth, fact, authority, warrant, or mutation permission.

**Constitutional effect:** Observation makes material available as bounded, attributed, inspectable material. It preserves source, scope, and boundary rather than promoting the material.

**Resulting standing or condition:** The result is an observation, representation, or visibility artifact that may later be evidence only through a separate evidence-binding or admissibility surface.

**Authority and evidence required:** Authority is local receipt or inspection authority. The observed material must carry enough identity, provenance, source, or boundary information to avoid silent promotion.

**Lawful negative or stop result:** If the material cannot be safely attributed or bounded, the lawful result is non-promotion, Unknown, malformed/unsupported material, or stop.

**Relation to constraints:** Provenance, source identity, scope, read-only boundary, and non-mutation flags are not optional decoration. They are the condition that keeps observation from becoming truth.

**Concrete operations:** Repository observation surfaces expose repository state with `writes_event_ledger=False` and `mutates_cluster=False`; diagnostic and selection-path surfaces similarly preserve read-only visibility. Observation ingestion can turn an `Observation` into `Evidence`, but that is a separate operation and fact production can be suppressed.

**Counterexample:** Runtime input recording, repository observation, diagnostic inventory, and presentation labels do not by themselves create constitutional truth or mutation authority.

### 2. Attribute / translate / represent

**Consumed standing or condition:** Material exists in a source carrier, source vocabulary, exact operator text, external representation, or presentation form.

**Constitutional effect:** Translation or attribution changes carrier, identity, or representation while preserving the original boundary. It makes the material legible to a Seed-local consumer without expanding authority.

**Resulting standing or condition:** The result is an attributed expression, bounded representation, translated content, normalized identity, or read-only projection whose provenance and limits remain attached.

**Authority and evidence required:** Evidence must support the exact material being translated and the carrier change. Translation authority is not authority to alter standing, verify content, execute work, or mutate state.

**Lawful negative or stop result:** If provenance, authority boundary, confidence, Unknowns, or negative authority would be lost, the lawful result is no translation, partial/limited representation, residual unsupported spans, Unknown, or stop.

**Relation to constraints:** Authority, provenance, scope, Unknown preservation, and confidence limits travel with the translated material. They are orthogonal constraints and also part of the act's success condition.

**Concrete operations:** `attribute_operator_expression()` normalizes exact operator text and assigns stable identity. `interpret_operator_expression()` consumes that attributed expression and can parse bounded forms, but it still treats authority-bearing language as language rather than an authority grant.

**Counterexample:** A normalized expression or provider handoff suggestion is not execution authority. A formatter or JSON renderer is a communication lens, not a constitutional producer.

### 3. Bind identity, scope, or choice

**Consumed standing or condition:** A candidate subject, question, option token, expression, workspace/session reference, event identity, or scope field exists but is not yet bound to the specific local meaning required by a consumer.

**Constitutional effect:** Binding fixes a local identity, scope, or choice relation so that a later consumer can tell exactly what was selected, referred to, or bounded.

**Resulting standing or condition:** The result is a stable bounded identity, closed-choice binding, scoped question, event identity, or local reference. The result is usually eligible for later validation or consumption, not automatically authoritative beyond the local boundary.

**Authority and evidence required:** The binding must be supported by exact presented choices, explicit caller fields, stable identity rules, or event uniqueness rules. Caller-controlled assertions remain testimony unless another act evaluates them.

**Lawful negative or stop result:** Duplicate identity, unsupported token, ambiguous reference, unbound scope, or missing presented option lawfully yields refusal, unsupported binding, Unknown, or stop.

**Relation to constraints:** Identity and scope are constraints that recur across many acts. They can also be the immediate effect of a binding operation.

**Concrete operations:** Closed-choice selection binding binds captured token to the exact presented option set. `produce_bounded_constitutional_question()` establishes a bounded question from explicit caller-supplied fields and stable identity over supplied payload.

**Counterexample:** Establishing a bounded question does not prove the caller's semantic fields true and does not require prior interpretation.

### 4. Establish a bounded constitutional question or inquiry object

**Consumed standing or condition:** An operator inquiry, caller-supplied fields, provenance, scope status, uncertainty, unknowns, and selection keys exist as testimony or explicit inputs.

**Constitutional effect:** Establishment turns explicit inputs into one bounded constitutional question artifact. It localizes inquiry without resolving the inquiry.

**Resulting standing or condition:** The result is a bounded question eligible for projection, selection, or composition in the surfaces that consume it.

**Authority and evidence required:** Authority comes from explicit caller construction and artifact rules, not from truth of the semantic content. Evidence is the supplied payload, provenance, uncertainty, and unknowns.

**Lawful negative or stop result:** Missing required fields, unstable identity, overbroad scope, or unsupported construction yields refusal, invalid artifact, Unknown, or stop.

**Relation to constraints:** Scope, provenance, uncertainty, unknowns, and caller-control remain preserved constraints on downstream use.

**Concrete operations:** `produce_bounded_constitutional_question()` can be consumed by `project_constitutional_question()` and by the constitutional pipeline helper, but the topology audit found this road only where that helper is invoked.

**Counterexample:** Establishment is not interpretation, selection, composition, recording, or mutation. Bounded question production can bypass interpretation.

### 5. Evaluate / judge admissibility, sufficiency, or compatibility

**Consumed standing or condition:** A candidate claim, key set, observation metadata, grammar applicability package, requested view, operation contract, policy context, or handoff stands as possible but not yet admitted for a specific local use.

**Constitutional effect:** Evaluation tests whether local evidence, identity, authority, applicability, compatibility, or sufficiency supports a stronger local standing.

**Resulting standing or condition:** The result may be supported, unsupported, ambiguous, incomplete, admissible, inadmissible, authority-blocked, outside-authority, confidence-calibrated, selected, suppressed, or stopped.

**Authority and evidence required:** The evaluating surface needs competent local rules and evidence for the exact question being evaluated. A valid shape does not equal policy authorization; policy authorization does not equal execution.

**Lawful negative or stop result:** Unsupported keys, incompatible metadata, failed applicability, refused authority, missing policy allowance, or insufficient evidence lawfully yield no selection, no fact, non-allow, Unknown, or stop.

**Relation to constraints:** Constraints such as identity, authority, provenance, scope, read-only/mutation boundary, confidence, and compatibility usually govern evaluation orthogonally; sometimes the validation or refusal is itself the evaluated effect.

**Concrete operations:** `select_constitutional_views()` evaluates projected question keys against capability keys. Observation ingestion evaluates fact-promotion suppression. `tool_execution_policy.py` separates operation validation from policy authorization and does not execute tools, append events, or create pending actions.

**Counterexample:** Evaluation output is not mutation authority unless a separate local mutation surface consumes it under its own authority.

### 6. Warrant / authorize bounded reliance

**Consumed standing or condition:** Evidence-bound content, translated material, preserved provenance, support, role, authority boundary, negative authority, Unknowns, and confidence limits exist but have not yet been lawfully consumed by a downstream role.

**Constitutional effect:** Warrant authorizes bounded lawful reliance. It changes content from merely expressed or observed to usable within a preserved authority boundary.

**Resulting standing or condition:** The result is warranted content or a reliance boundary that a downstream consumer may use only within the authorized role, scope, confidence, and negative authority.

**Authority and evidence required:** Repository evidence must bind content to support, provenance, authority boundary, negative authority, Unknowns, role/consumer boundary, and stop discipline.

**Lawful negative or stop result:** Where evidence or authority is insufficient, the lawful result is no warrant, weaker confidence, bounded non-use, preserved Unknown, or stop.

**Relation to constraints:** Warrant is one place where constraints become consumable. It does not erase them; it carries them into reliance.

**Concrete operations:** Operational realization warrant artifacts preserve candidate, mechanism, grammar, dependency, authority, limitations, unknowns, conflicts, and read-only/non-mutation boundaries. Translation evidence similarly preserves warrant rather than creating new rights.

**Counterexample:** Reliance on warranted content is not execution, state mutation, or truth creation.

### 7. Rely / consume within warrant

**Consumed standing or condition:** Warranted content or an eligible handoff exists with preserved support and limits.

**Constitutional effect:** Reliance consumes that content for a specific downstream role while staying inside the warrant.

**Resulting standing or condition:** A consumer may evaluate, render, compare, select, proceed, or refuse within the reliance boundary. The consumed content remains bounded by its support and negative authority.

**Authority and evidence required:** The consumer must have a role and authority boundary compatible with the warrant. Reliance requires preserved provenance, identity, scope, support, Unknowns, confidence, and stop conditions.

**Lawful negative or stop result:** If the desired use exceeds warrant, the lawful result is refusal, non-use, narrower conclusion, Unknown, or stop.

**Relation to constraints:** Reliance is almost entirely constraint-governed. Its distinguishing effect is lawful use, not production or mutation.

**Concrete operations:** Constitutional view composition consumes selected registered view names and view builders within an explicit composition request; downstream rendering consumes the composition artifact. These are reliance-like consumption surfaces only inside their request and registration boundaries.

**Counterexample:** A consumer becoming eligible to use an artifact does not prove that the artifact may be relied on by every other consumer.

### 8. Select / admit a local handoff

**Consumed standing or condition:** Multiple eligible or possible candidates, requested keys, registered views, capability contracts, handoff artifacts, or operation candidates exist.

**Constitutional effect:** Selection/admission chooses or admits a subset for a specific local consumer after matching local criteria.

**Resulting standing or condition:** The selected or admitted item becomes eligible input to the next local surface. Unsupported alternatives remain preserved as unsupported, unknown, or unselected.

**Authority and evidence required:** Selection requires exact local matching rules, registration, support, policy, or compatibility evidence. It does not require or prove a universal selection act.

**Lawful negative or stop result:** No matching key, unsupported requested view, invalid operation contract, or non-allow policy lawfully yields no selection, refusal, compatibility answer, Unknown, or stop.

**Relation to constraints:** Selection is governed by compatibility, identity, scope, registration, authority, and read-only/mutation boundaries.

**Concrete operations:** `select_constitutional_views()` selects registered view names. `selected_constitutional_views_to_composition_request()` adapts that selection into a request, but the audit identifies it as wiring rather than an independent judgment.

**Counterexample:** Direct composition request construction can bypass selection, proving selection is not universally mandatory.

### 9. Compose / project / present a read-only view

**Consumed standing or condition:** Bounded questions, selected view names, registered contracts/builders, event history, or read-model inputs exist.

**Constitutional effect:** Projection or composition derives a representation from bounded inputs without creating new authority beyond the input boundaries.

**Resulting standing or condition:** The result is a projection artifact, view composition artifact, read model, JSON/text output, or derived state representation.

**Authority and evidence required:** The surface needs registered-view admission, explicit request authority, event-ledger input, projection rules, or caller-supplied builder contracts. It must preserve contributing evidence and unknowns.

**Lawful negative or stop result:** Unsupported requested views, missing builders, inconsistent contracts, or projection boundary violations yield refusal, unsupported view, Unknown, or stop.

**Relation to constraints:** Read-only flags, provenance, contributing evidence, unknowns, and unsupported request accounting keep projection/composition from becoming truth creation.

**Concrete operations:** `build_constitutional_view_composition()` composes explicitly requested registered views. `StateProjector` derives state from event history. Formatters and JSON functions communicate artifacts but do not produce authority.

**Counterexample:** Composition is not mandatory for recording or state projection; recording and projection have separate roads.

### 10. Record / preserve history

**Consumed standing or condition:** An event, observation/evidence/fact event batch, runtime input, tool lifecycle event, state patch event, or recordable diagnostic output exists as something to preserve.

**Constitutional effect:** Recording preserves durable or in-memory history with order, identity, payload, workspace, actor, causation, and correlation so later projection, audit, reconstruction, or accountability is possible.

**Resulting standing or condition:** The result is stored event history or diagnostic-run scoped record. Depending on event kind and downstream projector rules, it may or may not affect projected state.

**Authority and evidence required:** Recording requires event identity, ledger validation, batch uniqueness, allowed record scope, and any surface-specific recording boundary.

**Lawful negative or stop result:** Duplicate event identity, invalid event shape, unsupported record scope, or forbidden mutation boundary lawfully yields refusal, no record, or diagnostic-only record.

**Relation to constraints:** Recording is constrained by identity, append-only history, causation/correlation, record scope, event-ledger status, and mutation flags.

**Concrete operations:** `EventLedger.append()` and `append_many()` record events. `ObservationIngestor.ingest_many()` creates observation and evidence events and possibly fact events before ledger append. Diagnostic recording must remain `diagnostic_run` scoped unless intentionally different.

**Counterexample:** Event-ledger writes are not automatically cluster mutation or projected/domain truth. Some durable events are ignored by projection; diagnostic record writes remain diagnostic evidence.

### 11. Mutate / change a local domain surface

**Consumed standing or condition:** A proposed write, patch, execution result, repository file edit, pending-action transition, projection-store write, or other concrete mutation request has passed whatever local authority and validation apply.

**Constitutional effect:** Mutation changes a concrete local surface: event history, projected/domain state, repository files, operational state, pending-action status, or persisted projection storage.

**Resulting standing or condition:** The changed surface exists, but the constitutional meaning of the change still requires observation, replay, audit, tests, or reconstruction. Mutation occurrence is not proof of lawful effect.

**Authority and evidence required:** Mutation requires separate local implementation-backed authority. Observation, pressure, warrant, reliance, translation, diagnostic visibility, provider handoff metadata, and selection explanation are insufficient by themselves.

**Lawful negative or stop result:** Missing authority, policy non-allow, invalid operation, compatibility risk, or unknown mutation boundary lawfully yields refusal, no-op, preservation of Unknown, or stop.

**Relation to constraints:** Mutation is where compatibility, authority, read/write boundary, event-ledger behavior, and cluster mutation flags are decisive. They do not define the whole topology, but they constrain whether a local write is lawful.

**Concrete operations:** Event appends, state patch acceptance, repository file changes, projection-store persistence, and pending-action transitions are concrete write behaviors when implementation-backed. Tool policy deliberately does not execute or append; it only validates/authorizes for an outside caller.

**Counterexample:** Diagnostic output, pressure visibility, selection audit, repository observation, and read-only operational realization projections are explicitly not cluster mutation.

### 12. Terminate / refuse / preserve Unknown

**Consumed standing or condition:** A proposed movement, claim, reliance, selection, admission, promotion, mutation, topology claim, or act identity lacks sufficient evidence or exceeds authority.

**Constitutional effect:** Termination stops unsupported movement while preserving the reason, boundary, negative authority, Unknown, or confidence limit.

**Resulting standing or condition:** The result is unsupported, inadmissible, authority-blocked, incomplete, stopped, refused, preserved Unknown, no known realization, no fact, no selection, or no mutation.

**Authority and evidence required:** Stop authority follows from evidence insufficiency, boundary excess, contradiction, unsupported vocabulary, missing producer/consumer proof, compatibility failure, or absence of local mutation authority.

**Lawful negative or stop result:** The negative result is itself lawful; it is not failure, backlog creation, or proof that future evidence can never change the standing.

**Relation to constraints:** Negative authority, Unknown preservation, confidence calibration, compatibility, scope, and evidence sufficiency are the recurring constraints that make stop constitutional rather than accidental.

**Concrete operations:** Interpretation branches preserve unsupported residual spans, unknown references, and conflicts. Selection records unsupported keys. Observation ingestion may suppress Fact while still recording observation/evidence. Runtime free-text input records a refusal event rather than model-routed movement.

**Counterexample:** Pressure, recurring vocabulary, or goal-shaped prose cannot force continuation through a lawful stop.

## Overlaps and act families

The repository supports overlapping families rather than one-to-one names.

### Evaluation overlaps with selection, admission, warrant, and termination

Evaluation can produce selection, no selection, warrant, no warrant, admission, suppression, non-allow, or stop. It is independently recurring as evidence-testing, but concrete surfaces often realize it together with another local effect.

### Translation overlaps with preservation but differs from preservation

Translation preserves provenance and authority limits while changing representation. Preservation can occur without representation change, for example event history or unknown retention. Translation therefore realizes preservation constraints but is not identical to preservation.

### Recording overlaps with preservation but differs from mutation and projection

Recording preserves history and can be a write. However, repository evidence distinguishes event append, projection, cluster mutation, and diagnostic-run recording. Recording is not automatically domain mutation, and projection is not automatically event recording.

### Selection overlaps with composition but remains bypassable

The constitutional pipeline helper orders question projection, capability projection, selection, request creation, and composition, but direct composition request construction can bypass selection subject to composition admission checks. The ordered helper is therefore a composite road, not universal topology.

### Warrant and reliance are adjacent but not identical

Warrant authorizes bounded use. Reliance is the downstream consumption of that authorization. A report, view, or handoff may carry warrant-like limits without proving that every downstream use is lawful.

### Mutation overlaps with recording only in local write behavior

An event append is a write to history. A state patch may project into domain state. A diagnostic record may write diagnostic evidence. These are different local write effects and do not prove one generic constitutional mutation act.

## Counterexamples to overbroad divisions

The repository contradicts these overbroad divisions:

1. **Every implementation verb is an act.** Formatters, JSON renderers, constructors, adapters, and caller-supplied builders can be lenses or wiring rather than constitutional judgments.
2. **Every act emits an artifact.** Evaluation, refusal, suppression, non-promotion, and stop can be constitutional effects even when the artifact is only a record of the effect or no stronger artifact is emitted.
3. **Acts form one pipeline.** The repository supports separate question/composition, event, observation, and grammar roads. Their vocabularies can touch without proving universal order.
4. **Mutation is defined by cache or projection behavior.** Projection, event append, diagnostic recording, repository file change, and cluster mutation are separable local write or representation behaviors.
5. **Action or mutation vocabulary names a constitutional participant.** The action/mutation frontier survey did not support `Action` or `Mutation` as standalone constitutional participants.
6. **Presentation vocabulary is knowledge.** Presentation labels require knowledge reachability evidence before promotion.
7. **Selection proves composition or composition proves selection.** Direct composition request construction is a live bypass; selection is not universally mandatory.
8. **Observation implies fact.** Observation ingestion can preserve observation/evidence while suppressing fact production.
9. **Recording implies projected truth.** Some event records do not project into domain state; diagnostic recording remains diagnostic evidence.
10. **Policy authorization executes.** Tool execution policy validates and authorizes but intentionally leaves execution and event routing elsewhere.

## Exact stopping boundary

This characterization stops at repository-supported effect distinctions. It does not:

- rename repository law;
- promote a canonical act list;
- complete candidate inventories;
- impose a hierarchy, taxonomy, workflow, or pipeline;
- infer independence from implementation verbs;
- infer topology from action, mutation, compatibility, cache, projection, or presentation vocabulary;
- classify every module in the repository;
- change implementation or tests;
- change the Book;
- decide future acceptance, realization, examination, publication, or mutation-role characterizations.

The lawful final standing is:

```text
Distinct effects are supported.
Overlapping families are supported.
Concrete operations realize narrower instances of broader effects.
Several topology and independence questions remain Unknown.
```
