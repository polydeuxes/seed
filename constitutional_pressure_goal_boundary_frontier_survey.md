# Constitutional Pressure / Goal Boundary Frontier Survey

## Bounded question

Across recurring constitutional recoveries, what constitutional boundary, if any, separates diagnostic pressure from priority, course selection, and goal authority?

## Investigation scope

This is exactly one Constitutional Frontier Survey. It reviews recurring repository evidence about pressure, diagnostics, inquiry orientation, reasoning paths, selection paths, capability and verification surfaces, authority boundaries, bounded lawful reliance, Unknown preservation, lawful stop, responsibility recovery, and implementation recovery methodology.

The survey treats the terms `informative pressure`, `priority`, `navigation`, `course selection`, and `goal authority` as investigation pressure only. It does not assume that any of them are constitutional participants.

Out of scope:

- implementing planning, goal management, operator policy, prioritization, schedulers, planners, mission systems, or autonomous behavior;
- creating runtime goal selection;
- promoting presentation vocabulary into repository knowledge;
- treating diagnostic output as cluster truth;
- treating a pressure score, inventory row, reasoning path, or selection explanation as an implementation mandate.

## Recurring constitutional evidence

### Pressure is visibility before command

Recurring constitutional evidence supports pressure as an attention or inspection signal, not as purpose or authority. `repository_purpose_pressure_and_inquiry_characterization.md` states that pressure begins when existing evidence shows that something deserves attention, and that implementation-backed pressure is built from bounded category, score, evidence, reason, and recommended inspection command. It also states that pressure stops at attention and inspection, not purpose, planning, mutation, or automatic inquiry execution.

`constitutional_self_governance_competency_investigation.md` identifies the pressure audit as read-only operational pressure over existing visibility surfaces and says that pressure can become visible without command authority. Its recurring constitutional frame is evidence, jurisdiction, authority, refusal boundary, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop before a bounded capability may proceed.

`null_constitutional_readiness_characterization.md` preserves the same distinction: pressure may orient inquiry but does not command work, recover ownership, prove implementation readiness, or select priority. It treats pressure sensing as a visible capacity whose lawful result may be stop rather than action.

### Bounded inquiry refuses goal and plan promotion

Inquiry orientation evidence repeatedly refuses to treat operator prose or related material as fact, goal, tool need, decision, proposal, plan, authorization, command, or runtime instruction. This matters because it separates possible inquiry pressure from operator intent and goal authority.

`bounded_inquiry_recovery_characterization.md` preserves an unresolved question about whether pressure is feeling, operator preference, priority engine, or implementation condition. Its preservation of this as pressure rather than conclusion supports the boundary: recurrence is not enough to promote pressure into a priority or goal mechanism.

`inquiry_dependency_survey.md` says inquiry responsibilities require a prior visible boundary excluding ownership inference, implementation recovery, semantic routing, runtime authority, authority grants, promotion from presentation vocabulary to knowledge, and unsupported crossing. That is a recurring constitutional pattern: orientation may help locate evidence, but does not authorize course selection.

### Authority and reliance require evidence-bound entitlement

Recurring authority evidence separates a diagnostic condition from lawful movement. `constitutional_authority_characterization.md`, `constitutional_movement_characterization.md`, and `constitutional_movement_participation_survey.md` repeatedly treat authority boundary, negative authority, evidence/support/provenance, locality, confidence limits, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop as required constraints before movement.

`constitutional_movement_characterization.md` is especially direct for this survey: provider translation, inquiry orientation, source navigation, diagnostic inventory, and shape-audit behavior can expose, translate, orient, or audit within their boundaries without promoting output into truth or cluster mutation. Movement is not automatic; unsupported movement terminates or remains unsupported.

Bounded lawful reliance evidence similarly does not equate pressure with entitlement. Reliance requires a warrant, evidence boundary, and negative authority. A diagnostic pressure may contribute evidence to a later warranted inquiry, but the pressure itself is not the warrant unless a repository-supported authority says so.

### Unknown preservation and lawful stop are positive outcomes

Unknown preservation recurs as a lawful result rather than a defect to be resolved by action. `constitutional_self_governance_competency_investigation.md` says unknowns are preserved in question-family definitions, inquiry orientation, authority slices, pressure audit counts, and responsibility evaluations. `constitutional_movement_participation_survey.md` treats preserved unknowns, confidence limits, and lawful stop/refusal conditions as recurring movement constraints.

For the bounded question, this means an exposed diagnostic condition may end in `Unknown`, `frontier only`, `unsupported conclusion`, or `lawful stop`. It does not have to become priority, navigation, course selection, or goal authority.

### Implementation recovery is evidence-gated, not pressure-gated

Recurring implementation recovery methodology distinguishes pressure from implementation readiness. `architectural_frontier_characterization.md` says a frontier preserves important pressure that is not yet implementation-recoverable and does not create a planner, priority, schedule, runtime behavior, or implementation mandate. `repository_purpose_pressure_and_inquiry_characterization.md` says pressure informs what deserves attention but does not define purpose.

This supports a boundary between diagnostic pressure and implementation course selection: implementation recovery requires a bounded failing command, behavior, or visibility gap plus the smallest implementation-backed change and tests. Pressure alone is insufficient.

## Recurring implementation evidence

### Pressure audit

`seed_runtime/pressure_audit.py` defines `PressureItem` as category, score, evidence, reason, and recommended command. Its builder explicitly ranks operational pressure without planning, recording, or mutating state. It sorts pressure items by score and category and formats them as recommended inspections, not selected goals or plans.

This is implementation evidence for: pressure can rank inspection attention inside the audit surface, but the code does not create goals, mutate projected state, write events, or select a course of action.

### Diagnostic inventory and diagnostic shape audit

`seed_runtime/diagnostic_inventory.py` declares diagnostic surfaces with explicit boundary fields including `supports_record`, `record_scope`, `emits_diagnostic_facts`, `emits_cluster_facts`, `writes_event_ledger`, and `mutates_cluster`. The repository-level operational visibility contract requires diagnostic surfaces to appear in diagnostic inventory and shape audit, and requires recording boundaries such as `record_scope=diagnostic_run` and `mutates_cluster=false` unless intentionally different.

This is implementation evidence for a diagnostic boundary: surfaced findings have declared shape, record scope, ledger behavior, and mutation status. Diagnostic visibility is governed as visibility, not as goal authority.

### Inquiry orientation

`seed_runtime/inquiry_orientation.py` states that inquiry notes are preserved outside the event ledger and never mutate projected state, append events, call providers, execute tools, or create facts, goals, tool needs, decisions, proposals, or plans. Its authority boundary says related material is deterministic lexical overlap only and does not assert importance, ownership, intent, concern, recommended action, or next safe move.

This implementation evidence separates navigation-like orientation from priority, intent, and course selection.

### Reasoning path and selection path audits

`seed_runtime/reasoning_path_audit.py` declares a `read_only_reasoning_audit` boundary with `records_facts=false`, `writes_event_ledger=false`, and `mutates_cluster=false`; its builder derives paths only from implemented diagnostic surfaces. `seed_runtime/selection_path_audit.py` declares a `read_only_selection_audit` boundary with the same non-recording and non-mutation fields and explains implemented selection evidence without changing selection behavior.

These surfaces expose lineage and selection evidence, but their own boundaries prevent them from becoming planners, goal managers, or mutation authorities.

### Existing goal implementation is operational state, not constitutional authorization

The repository has operational goal records in state-patch and context code, but this survey found no recurring evidence that constitutional diagnostic pressure itself owns runtime goal creation or goal selection. Where goals exist operationally, they are created through explicit state-patch operations and projected into context; that is separate from diagnostic pressure surfaces.

These pressures recur without becoming goal authority.

## Supported constitutional distinctions

### 1. Diagnostic pressure is informative, not authoritative

Supported. Pressure exposes evidence-bearing conditions and recommended inspections. It does not itself authorize cluster mutation, event-ledger writes, fact creation, goal creation, plan creation, or course execution.

### 2. Pressure can rank inspection attention, but that is not global priority

Supported. The pressure audit sorts its own pressure items. That local ranking is an audit presentation rule. Recurring evidence does not support treating it as repository-wide priority, operator priority, implementation priority, or constitutional priority.

### 3. Instrumentation can orient without navigating the repository's next move

Supported. Inquiry orientation, source/navigation-adjacent surfaces, reasoning path audit, selection path audit, diagnostic inventory, and shape audit expose relationships, paths, or boundaries. They do not decide the next safe move unless a separate implemented authority supports that decision.

### 4. Evidence exposure is not course selection

Supported. Diagnostic surfaces expose evidence. Reasoning and selection path audits explain derivation or implemented selection evidence. They do not create new selection behavior or choose a future implementation course.

### 5. Diagnostic findings are not goal authority

Supported. Diagnostic findings may be scoped to `diagnostic_run` and declared non-mutating. Recurring evidence does not support silently attaching diagnostic-only findings to runtime entities or converting them into goals.

### 6. Implementation readiness requires more than pressure

Supported. Frontier and implementation recovery evidence distinguish pressure from implementation readiness. A pressure may justify a next bounded investigation or an inspection, but not a patch, architecture, planner, mission, scheduler, or autonomous behavior without separate implementation evidence and tests.

## Answer to the acceptance question

Yes, recurring repository evidence supports a constitutional boundary separating diagnostic pressure from priority, course selection, and goal authority.

The supported boundary is:

> Diagnostic pressure may expose, rank, and explain bounded evidence-bearing conditions for attention or inspection, but it does not by itself create priority, select a course, establish operator intent, create or select goals, authorize mutation, promote diagnostic findings into cluster truth, or prove implementation readiness.

The boundary is enforced by recurring constitutional constraints: evidence/support/provenance, authority boundary, negative authority, locality, bounded lawful reliance, Unknown preservation, confidence limits, and lawful stop. It is reinforced by implementation surfaces whose boundaries are read-only, non-recording or diagnostically scoped, non-mutating, and explicitly separated from goals, plans, commands, and next-safe-move claims.

## Confidence

High confidence that the repository supports the negative boundary: pressure is not priority, course selection, or goal authority.

Medium confidence on the exact positive wording, because the repository supports several adjacent formulations: pressure as attention, inspection, visibility, boundary localization, and frontier preservation. The smallest common supported formulation is attention/inspection pressure under explicit authority and mutation boundaries.

Low confidence that any positive constitutional participant named `priority`, `navigation`, `course selection`, or `goal authority` has been recovered. The reviewed evidence supports preserving those as Unknown rather than promoting them.
