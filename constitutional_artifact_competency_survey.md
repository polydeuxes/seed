# Constitutional Artifact Competency Survey

## Boundary

This is exactly one bounded Constitutional Artifact Competency Survey. It asks whether recurring repository evidence supports constitutionally typed artifacts as the recurring constitutional medium exchanged between constitutional competencies.

It does not recover implementation, runtime architecture, ownership, a universal artifact system, or a general artifact model. Repository authority wins.

## App use

The app was used before the survey determination:

- `python -m scripts.seed_local --documentation-structure --summary-only` reported a read-only corpus structure surface over 577 documents, with no prose interpretation, authority inference, shape inference, event-ledger writes, or repository mutation.
- `python -m scripts.seed_local --inquiry-artifacts` reported inquiry-artifact visibility as read-only and exposed unknown, boundary, pressure, finding, supported conclusion, unsupported conclusion, open question, and gap as repository-visible, partially visible, or document-visible inquiry artifacts with explicit limitations.
- `python -m scripts.seed_local --knowledge-reachability-audit --knowledge-reachability-audit-subject "constitutionally typed artifacts" --candidate-kind presentation_label --knowledge-reachability-audit-limit 10` reported `constitutionally typed artifacts` as a presentation label with first loss at `not_preserved`.

The app therefore supports corpus visibility, inquiry-artifact visibility, and caution against promoting the survey phrase itself into preserved knowledge. The constitutional conclusions below are recovered from recurring repository artifacts, not from app-inferred meaning.

## 2. Survey

### Recurring artifact neighborhoods

- Unknown preservation neighborhood: preserved unknowns, evidence gaps, implementation unknowns, boundary unknowns, relationship unknowns, methodological unknowns, locality unknowns, constitutional unknowns, implementation-readiness unknowns, and future investigation states.
- Competency interrogation neighborhood: selected competency, evidence reviewed, identity, authority, preconditions, evidence, boundaries, handoff, unknown/stop, locality, continuity, negative authority, reliance, termination, remaining questions, and confidence.
- Operator grammar neighborhood: bounded subject, evidence, authority, negative authority, unknown preservation, artifact handoff, and stop discipline.
- Warrant/reliance neighborhood: evidence-bound lawful reliance, preserved authority boundary, provenance/support, role, selected content, negative authority, unknown preservation, confidence, and non-promotion.
- Question/admissibility neighborhood: prior Scout, Survey, Frontier, Readiness, Resolution, admissibility, dependency, selected question shape, produced investigation artifact, and stop boundary.
- Movement/emission neighborhood: constitutional movement, artifact emission, bounded transition, emitted artifact, consumed artifact, and non-equivalence boundary.

### Recurring producers

- Bounded investigations produce reviewed-evidence ledgers, supported conclusions, unsupported conclusions, preserved unknowns, confidence, and stop conditions.
- Competency interrogations produce boundary recoveries, artifact handoffs, reliance statements, negative authority, and typed unknowns/refusals.
- Unknown-preservation work produces preserved Unknown states or records.
- Warrant work produces lawful reliance boundaries rather than truth or permission.
- Selection/admissibility work produces constrained investigation artifacts and admissibility boundaries.
- Observation/History/Time/Warrant/Movement/Selection witnesses produce local constitutional artifacts only inside their own authority.

### Recurring consumers

- Later constitutional investigations consume prior Scout, Survey, Frontier, Readiness, Resolution, characterization, topology, warrant, reliance, authority, and Unknown artifacts as evidence or boundary constraints.
- Competency interrogation consumes files, tests, app commands, diagnostics, adjacent reports, public output, and prior methodology reports.
- Compatibility consumers consume public unknown dictionaries while implementation-local typed unknown records remain bounded.
- Warrant and reliance consumers consume evidence/support/provenance only within the preserved authority boundary.
- Selection/admissibility consumers consume prior warrant and question-shape evidence to determine whether a bounded investigation can proceed.

### Recurring artifact movement

- Artifact movement is usually local and boundary-preserving: producer -> bounded artifact -> consumer.
- Movement carries evidence, provenance, authority limits, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, and stop conditions when those fields are supported.
- Compatibility movement may project a richer local record into an unchanged public shape without expanding public authority.
- Inquiry movement may remain document-visible rather than implementation-backed.
- Artifact emission alone does not equal constitutional movement.

### Recurring boundaries

- No universal producer, consumer, registry, engine, planner, scheduler, or runtime path is supported.
- Public vocabulary is not preserved knowledge merely because it recurs.
- Diagnostic or inquiry output does not become cluster truth by being visible or recordable.
- Evidence may support a bounded artifact without supporting architecture, implementation, promotion, ownership, or action.
- Unknowns must remain visible where evidence does not support resolution.

## 4. Characterization

### Preserved Unknown / Typed Unknown

- Constitutional purpose: preserve unresolved constitutional state without converting it into fact, authority, implementation, relationship, or action.
- Constitutional authority: authority to keep a known unresolved state visible within the bounded evidence surface.
- Constitutional boundaries: does not resolve the unknown, infer missing evidence, promote vocabulary, or authorize implementation.
- Constitutional profile: unknown type or state, area, reason, evidence exposure, meaning of what is not known, lawful next inquiry, resolution condition, status, confidence, preserved unknowns.
- Required preservation: unresolved condition, type/state distinction where supported, reason, area/locality, confidence, stop.
- Negative authority: no truth-making, no cluster mutation, no schema expansion unless independently supported, no universal Unknown taxonomy.
- Confidence: high that Typed Unknowns are preserved constitutional Unknown states; medium that all instances are artifacts in the same sense.

Typed Unknown determination: recurring evidence supports Typed Unknown as a preserved unknown state that can be carried by a bounded artifact in at least one implementation-local slice. It does not yet support collapsing Typed Unknown into only an artifact type or only a general artifact state across all repository uses.

### Competency interrogation output artifact

- Constitutional purpose: recover the smallest truthful competency boundary, evidence basis, authority, negative authority, reliance, Unknowns, stop, and confidence.
- Constitutional authority: methodology-bound authority to document what reviewed evidence supports.
- Constitutional boundaries: not an implemented capability, not constitutional law by itself, not runtime physiology, not new authority.
- Constitutional profile: selected competency, evidence reviewed, identity, authority, preconditions, evidence, boundaries, handoff, Unknown/Stop, locality, negative authority, reliance, termination, remaining questions, confidence.
- Required preservation: evidence ledger, locality, authority boundary, handoff where present, Unknowns, confidence, lawful stop.
- Negative authority: no universal ownership, no planner, no redesign, no unsupported consumer map.
- Confidence: high for recurring methodology shape; medium for Artifact Handoff as a permanent separate field because repository evidence marks it working methodology.

### Warrant / lawful reliance artifact

- Constitutional purpose: define what may be lawfully relied on and what must not be relied on under a preserved authority boundary.
- Constitutional authority: evidence-bound lawful reliance, not permission or truth by itself.
- Constitutional boundaries: no promotion, no mutation, no action activation, no generalized transition.
- Constitutional profile: evidence/support, provenance, authority boundary, role, selected bounded content, negative authority, Unknowns, confidence, stop/non-promotion limit.
- Required preservation: support path, authority boundary, reliance and non-reliance, confidence, Unknowns, stop.
- Negative authority: no implementation, no selection by itself, no current truth mutation.
- Confidence: high.

### Admissibility / question-shape artifact

- Constitutional purpose: allow or constrain a bounded investigation by preserving prior warrant, question shape, evidence posture, investigation shape, and produced artifact pattern.
- Constitutional authority: admissibility within the bounded investigation family.
- Constitutional boundaries: does not create runtime behavior, select public question families universally, or authorize implementation.
- Constitutional profile: selected question or family, prior Scout/Survey/Frontier/Readiness/Resolution artifact, admissibility basis, evidence posture, output shape, negative authority, Unknowns, confidence, stop.
- Required preservation: source artifact, admissibility reason, locality, refused expansions, confidence.
- Negative authority: no universal selector, no workflow, no schedule, no automatic progression.
- Confidence: medium-high.

### Observation / history / movement / selection output artifact

- Constitutional purpose: make local constitutional change or posture visible without collapsing adjacent competencies.
- Constitutional authority: only the local witness authority supported by evidence.
- Constitutional boundaries: observation is not truth; history is not a truth engine; movement is not artifact emission alone; selection is not a universal selector.
- Constitutional profile: produced local artifact, provenance/support, authority boundary, unchanged state, refused authority, produced unknowns, confidence.
- Required preservation: locality, provenance, support relation, non-promotion boundary, Unknowns, confidence.
- Negative authority: no activation, scheduling, ownership, runtime action, or promotion by appearance.
- Confidence: medium-high because exact artifact forms vary by witness and evidence surface.

## 6. Reconciliation

### Recurring constitutional artifact structure

Repository evidence supports a recurring structure in which bounded artifacts preserve evidence, provenance, authority boundary, negative authority, Unknowns, confidence, stop conditions, and lawful reliance or compatibility boundaries where supported.

The structure recurs across Typed Unknowns, competency interrogations, warrant/reliance, admissibility/question-shape work, topology/movement distinctions, and witness-produced constitutional artifacts.

### Recurring competency relationships

Competencies recur as bounded producers and consumers of such artifacts:

- Unknown preservation produces preserved unresolved-state artifacts consumed by public unknown surfaces and later inquiry.
- Competency interrogation produces boundary/reliance/handoff/unknown/confidence artifacts consumed by later methodology and constitutional investigations.
- Warrant/reliance produces lawful reliance artifacts consumed by admissibility, selection, and later bounded inquiry.
- Observation, history, movement, and selection produce local constitutional artifacts consumed by later current-condition, topology, readiness, and bounded inquiry work.

### Recurring constitutional movement

Constitutional movement recurs as boundary-preserving handoff or reliance over bounded artifacts. The movement is not guaranteed by emission alone and does not imply runtime architecture.

### Recurring distinctions

The survey preserves these distinctions:

- artifact emission is not constitutional movement;
- artifact type is not automatically artifact state;
- public shape is not necessarily local type-bearing record;
- lawful reliance is not permission, truth, or action;
- recurrence is not preservation;
- document-visible inquiry state is not implementation-backed runtime behavior;
- Unknown preservation is not Unknown resolution.

### Preserved unknowns

- Whether `constitutionally typed artifacts` is or should become preserved repository vocabulary remains unknown.
- Whether all constitutional competencies are primarily connected through bounded artifacts remains partly unknown because not every competency relation has complete consumer evidence.
- Whether Typed Unknown should be finally classified as an artifact type, artifact state, preserved unknown, or another constitutional structure remains only partially resolved.
- Whether artifact handoff and lawful reliance should remain distinct in all smaller reports remains unknown.
- Whether consumer governance can be made complete remains unknown.
- Whether future evidence will identify additional artifact profiles remains unknown.

### Confidence

High confidence: recurring repository evidence supports bounded artifacts carrying constitutional profiles across multiple independent neighborhoods.

Medium-high confidence: constitutional competencies are often connected through these bounded artifacts as producer/artifact/consumer or warrant/reliance relationships.

Medium confidence: the word `primarily` is stronger than current evidence can fully prove, because consumer governance remains incomplete and some inquiry states are document-visible rather than implementation-backed.

Medium confidence: Typed Unknowns fit within the broader recurring artifact behavior when a bounded artifact carries them, but repository evidence does not support a clean universal subset claim.

### Final determination

According to recurring repository evidence,

The stronger claim that constitutional competencies are primarily connected through constitutionally typed artifacts is supported as a bounded, medium-confidence constitutional explanation, not as preserved vocabulary, implementation, runtime architecture, or universal artifact system.
