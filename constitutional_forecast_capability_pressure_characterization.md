## Independent scenario examination

### Scenario A: low-confidence derived storage forecast

```text
Seed derives a low-confidence forecast that a storage device may become full in
approximately three to five months. The forecast is supported, confidence is
limited, and the growth estimate is itself derived.
```

**Constitutional participants that appear:**

- Observation appears as the source-attributed storage evidence from which growth
  may be estimated.
- Derivation appears as the growth estimate and future-oriented forecast.
- Warrant appears only to the extent support, method, scope, caveats, confidence,
  and authority limits remain preserved.
- Reliance appears as possible operator orientation, not as truth or execution.
- Current lawful condition appears as the present evidentiary assessment: a
  supported but low-confidence orientation exists.
- Unknown appears because future storage use and forecast accuracy remain
  unresolved.

**Constitutional pressure that appears:**

The pressure is to make a future-oriented derived conclusion useful for future
observation without allowing it to become direct truth, automatic priority,
implementation ownership, or action authority.

**Constitutional responsibility that remains:**

The remaining responsibility is to preserve support lineage, derivation method,
confidence limits, caveats, unknowns, and authority boundary so downstream use
knows that the forecast orients observation only.

**Constitutional movement that appears:**

The movement is:

```text
observed storage evidence
-> derived growth estimate
-> bounded forecast/orientation
-> possible future observation focus
```

The movement is lawful only if warrant and negative authority remain visible.

**Constitutional capability that seems absent:**

A forecasting engine, scheduler, timer, alarm, polling loop, autonomy boundary,
self-preservation owner, or runtime planning system remains absent.

**Unsupported:**

It is unsupported that the forecast should mutate cluster truth, authorize
migration, create an alarm, select a runtime plan, or become a constitutional
participant.

**Unknown:**

It remains unknown whether the device will become full, whether the growth model
will remain applicable, whether future observations will confirm the trend, and
whether any operational act will later be warranted.

### Scenario B: observation boundary for disk remaining below 10%

```text
Seed establishes a simple observation boundary such as Disk remaining < 10%.
The forecast provides orientation while the observation boundary becomes the
operational mechanism for reacquiring reality.
```

**Constitutional participants that appear:**

- Observation appears more strongly than forecast: the threshold is a boundary
  for reacquiring reported current storage condition.
- Derivation appears only as orientation that helped select where future
  observation should be useful.
- Warrant and reliance constrain the threshold's role: it may support bounded
  future observation, not universal action.
- Current lawful condition appears as the future present-tense assessment when a
  new observation exists.
- Responsibility movement appears if responsibility-bearing material moves from
  derivation/orientation into an observation boundary without overclaiming.

**Constitutional pressure that appears:**

The pressure is to distinguish orientation from reality reacquisition. A derived
forecast may improve where Seed looks later, but observation must reacquire the
current condition before stronger lawful movement.

**Constitutional responsibility that remains:**

The responsibility remains to keep the threshold as an observation boundary, not
an alarm/scheduler/action rule. If later observation reports disk remaining below
10%, separate evidence and authority are still required for any next act.

**Constitutional movement that appears:**

```text
bounded forecast orientation
-> selected observation boundary
-> future attributable observation
-> current evidentiary assessment
```

**Constitutional capability that seems absent:**

Adaptive thresholds, polling, timers, alarms, automatic remediation, runtime
planning, and threshold-driven mutation remain absent.

**Unsupported:**

It is unsupported that `Disk remaining < 10%` becomes a constitutional alarm,
execution trigger, autonomous plan, or direct migration authorization.

**Unknown:**

It remains unknown whether the threshold will ever be crossed, whether the
threshold is the best operator boundary, and what future evidence would authorize
beyond observation.

### Scenario C: SMART diagnostics and possible migration

```text
SMART diagnostics indicate increasing probability of disk failure. Seed has
evidence, may possess access to another machine, and may or may not possess
privilege to migrate.
```

**Constitutional participants that appear:**

- Observation appears as SMART diagnostic reports and access/privilege evidence.
- Evidence appears as support for risk, access, and privilege claims.
- Derivation may appear as probability of failure or interpretation of SMART
  trend evidence.
- Warrant appears as the lawful basis and limit for relying on diagnostic and
  privilege evidence.
- Reliance appears as bounded downstream use of the SMART evidence.
- Current lawful condition appears as the present assessment of risk, access,
  authority, and unresolved unknowns.
- Constitutional responsibility movement appears if risk evidence, access
  evidence, or privilege evidence must cross boundaries before a lawful act.

**Constitutional pressure that appears:**

The pressure is not technical possibility. It is whether current repository
evidence authorizes the next constitutional act after future-oriented risk
orientation has exposed where observation and authority checks matter.

**Constitutional responsibility that remains:**

The remaining responsibility is to preserve the distinction among risk evidence,
access evidence, privilege evidence, migration authority, and unknowns. If
privilege or authorization is missing, lawful stop remains required.

**Constitutional movement that appears:**

```text
SMART observation
-> diagnostic evidence
-> derived risk orientation
-> current authority/privilege assessment
-> lawful movement or lawful stop
```

**Constitutional capability that seems absent:**

An autonomous migration system, self-preservation executor, planner, timer,
policy engine, or technical-possibility-to-authority converter remains absent.

**Unsupported:**

It is unsupported that increasing failure probability authorizes migration,
cluster mutation, privilege escalation, or autonomous execution.

**Unknown:**

It remains unknown whether failure will occur, whether another machine is a
lawful target, whether migration privilege exists, and whether the current
operator/repository authority authorizes any next act.

- Reliance appears as bounded use of test/validation evidence before promotion.
- Current lawful condition appears as the present status of pre-update, recovery,

- Lawful transition appears as promotion only after invariants survive.
- Unknown appears where forecast cannot prove update success.

**Constitutional pressure that appears:**

The pressure is to prevent forecast confidence from substituting for
reacquired evidence. Future-oriented reasoning may orient preparation, but
verification and invariant-preserving promotion must reacquire reality.

**Constitutional responsibility that remains:**

The remaining responsibility is to preserve recovery ability, verification
support, test evidence, validation results, invariant boundaries, and lawful stop
before promotion.

**Constitutional movement that appears:**

```text
forecasted success orientation
-> recovery-state preparation
-> verification/testing/validation evidence
-> current lawful promotion assessment
-> promote or stop
```

**Constitutional capability that seems absent:**

A forecast-based updater, autonomous self-modification owner, runtime planner,
scheduler, or prediction-to-promotion mechanism remains absent.

**Unsupported:**

It is unsupported that forecasted success authorizes promotion, that recovery

**Unknown:**

It remains unknown whether future update attempts will pass, which invariants may

### Scenario E: backups and periodic restoration

```text
Seed creates backups. Forecasts suggest restoration will succeed. Seed
periodically restores those backups to reacquire reality rather than permanently
relying upon prediction.
```

**Constitutional participants that appear:**

- Observation appears through restore-test results and backup artifacts as
  reported/preserved material.
- Derivation/forecast appears as confidence that restoration should succeed.
- Evidence appears when restoration is actually tested.
- Warrant appears only to the extent restore evidence supports bounded reliance
  on a backup.
- Reliance appears as using backup-restoration evidence within its supported
  scope.
- Current lawful condition appears as the present backup/restore assessment.
- Responsibility movement appears when backup evidence moves into current
  readiness or recovery confidence without losing provenance and limits.

**Constitutional pressure that appears:**

The pressure is to use prediction only as orientation while requiring
reacquisition of restoration reality. The constitutional question is not whether
success is forecast, but whether current restore evidence warrants reliance.

**Constitutional responsibility that remains:**

The remaining responsibility is to preserve restore-test evidence, scope,
provenance, limitations, unknowns, and lawful stop if restore evidence is absent
or fails.

**Constitutional movement that appears:**

```text
backup existence / forecasted restorability
-> selected restore observation boundary
-> restore-test observation/evidence
-> current lawful reliance assessment
```

**Constitutional capability that seems absent:**

A backup scheduler, restore timer, polling system, alarm, autonomous continuity
system, or forecast-based recovery executor remains absent.

**Unsupported:**

It is unsupported that predicted restorability equals current recovery warrant,
that backups are reliable without restore evidence, or that periodic testing is
recovered here as runtime scheduling behavior.

**Unknown:**

It remains unknown whether any untested backup will restore, how long restore
evidence remains sufficient, and what future authority would govern operational
recovery.

## Cross examination

### Recurring constitutional patterns

Across all five scenarios, the recurring pattern is:

```text
current observation/evidence
-> derived or future-oriented orientation
-> selected future observation, verification, or authority-check boundary
-> reacquired current evidence
-> bounded lawful reliance, lawful transition, or lawful stop
```

Forecasts and derived conclusions improve the direction, priority, or shape of
future observation. They do not replace future observation, verification,
current lawful condition, authority assessment, or warrant.

### Recurring constitutional movements

The recurring movements are:

1. **Observation to derivation:** existing observations can support derived
   orientation if support, method, scope, caveats, and confidence remain visible.
2. **Derivation to observation boundary:** future-oriented reasoning may help
   select what should be observed, verified, or authority-checked later.
3. **Observation boundary to current lawful condition:** later evidence
   reacquires reality and supports present-tense assessment.
4. **Current lawful condition to reliance/transition/stop:** only current,
   evidence-bound warrant permits bounded reliance or lawful movement; otherwise
   stop and unknown preservation remain lawful.

### Recurring constitutional invariants

The recurring invariants are:

- Observation is not fact, truth, verification, or promotion.
- Derivation is not observation.
- Forecast is not authority.
- Confidence is not warrant by itself.
- Warrant requires preserved evidence, provenance, role, authority boundary,
  negative authority, unknowns, and confidence limits.
- Reliance remains bounded to the role and evidence that warrant permits.
- Current lawful condition must be assessed from current evidence and bounded
  authority, not from predicted future success alone.
- Responsibility movement requires evidence-gated local boundary crossing.
- Unknowns remain visible instead of becoming inferred certainty.
- Lawful stop remains a positive constitutional outcome.
- Presentation vocabulary and operator pressure do not become repository
  knowledge without implementation/repository evidence.

### Recurring capability pressures

The actually exposed constitutional capability pressure is:

```text
Forecast-oriented observation reacquisition pressure.
```

In bounded form:

```text
When forecasts, derived conclusions, and future-oriented constitutional reasoning
are used only to improve future constitutional observation, repository evidence
exposes pressure for Seed to preserve the lawful path from derived orientation to
future observation/verification/authority-check boundaries, then reacquire
current evidence before warrant, reliance, transition, or stop.
```

This is a capability pressure, not a recovered capability implementation. It is
constitutional because it concerns what must be preserved for future movement to
remain lawful: support lineage, derivation method, confidence limits, caveats,
unknowns, authority boundaries, selected observation boundary, current evidence,
and lawful stop.

The pressure is adjacent to observation, derivation, warrant, reliance, current
lawful condition, and responsibility movement. It does not introduce a new
constitutional participant. The nearest already-supported formulation from prior
evidence is layered derivation-reliance warrant pressure; the forecast-specific
scenario narrows that pressure toward reacquiring reality through future
observation.
