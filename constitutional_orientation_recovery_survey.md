## Recurring orientation movements

### 1. Start from an existing bounded explanation or limitation

Before successful campaigns, work did not begin from a blank slate or broad theory. The artifacts first preserved the current bounded explanation:

- implementation grammar began from already completed implementation-family artifacts;
- ownership grammar began from implementation grammar and completion audits as limiting evidence;
- the non-implementation family survey began from the prior ownership limitation that implementation recovery was the only validated grammar;
- repository-artifact ownership slices began from the non-implementation family survey and adjacent repository-artifact/runtime-artifact evidence;
- orientation-guided methodology explicitly describes preserving a current bounded explanation before incoming evidence exposes an exact insufficiency.

Orientation movement: preserve the current bounded explanation before trying to move.

### 2. Locate the validated domain and its limits

Each pre-recovery orientation separated what had been validated from what remained unsupported:

- implementation grammar validated implementation-local recovery only;
- ownership recovery grammar identified ownership-shaped observations but refused to generalize beyond implementation validation;
- the non-implementation family survey treated the prior limitation as controlling, then looked only for independent constitutional evidence;
- repository-artifact ownership slices selected one adjacent repository-artifact boundary without absorbing runtime, navigation, status, roadmap, vocabulary, lifecycle, or implementation authority.

Orientation movement: identify the lawful domain before selecting a recovery subject.

### 3. Search for adjacent constitutional evidence, not analogy

Successful campaigns began by looking for immediately adjacent repository evidence:

- implementation recovery looked for adjacent implementation-local compressed boundaries;
- the ownership grammar characterization asked which observations required implementation and which were merely ownership-shaped;
- the non-implementation family survey looked for independent constitutional evidence of non-implementation ownership rather than applying the implementation grammar by analogy;
- Repository Artifact Ownership Slice 001 started from repository-artifact/runtime-artifact evidence immediately adjacent to the first viable non-implementation family.

Orientation movement: require adjacency and refuse analogy as a substitute for evidence.

### 4. Distinguish visibility, presentation, compatibility, and ownership

Before recovery began, artifacts repeatedly asked whether visible material was actually ownership pressure:

- implementation grammar says visibility is not ownership pressure;
- ownership grammar says compatibility surfaces are not automatically ownership surfaces;
- the non-implementation family survey distinguished presentation preservation from primary ownership recovery;
- repository-artifact slices preserved compatibility and consumer use without transferring ownership.

Orientation movement: test whether a visible surface is merely visible, presentational, compatible, or consumable before treating it as recoverable ownership.

### 5. Preserve Unknowns and lawful stops before selecting recovery

Each pre-recovery orientation preserved Unknowns rather than filling them:

- implementation grammar preserved Unknowns about whether the grammar applies outside implementation;
- ownership grammar preserved Unknowns blocking broader constitutional ownership recovery;
- the non-implementation family survey preserved that only one viable first non-implementation ownership family was found and recurrence remained unproven;
- repository-artifact slices left remaining compressed repository responsibilities unrecovered;
- orientation-guided methodology preserved typed unknowns around global orientation participants, automated repair, semantic question formation, universal methodology engines, and future implementation readiness.

Orientation movement: make non-recovery and Unknowns visible before any recovery action.

### 6. Select the smallest lawful candidate or stop

Before recovery, the artifacts narrowed candidates:

- implementation recovery narrowed to one adjacent implementation-local boundary at a time;
- non-implementation family survey selected Repository Artifact Ownership as the only viable first non-implementation ownership candidate;
- Repository Artifact Ownership Slice 001 selected preservation authority as the first adjacent boundary and left status, roadmap, navigation, lifecycle, vocabulary, and other authorities unrecovered;
- Slices 002 and 003 similarly selected current status/frontier and roadmap authority without treating the whole repository-artifact space as recovered.

Orientation movement: produce a candidate recovery target only where evidence selects one; otherwise stop.

## Recurring constitutional questions

The same pre-recovery questions recur across the surveyed artifacts:

1. **What is the current bounded explanation or limitation?**
   - Example: implementation recovery grammar was validated only in implementation; the non-implementation survey treated that as a limit, not a mandate.

2. **What evidence is admissible for this question?**
   - Example: the non-implementation family survey reviewed constitutional or constitutional-characterization artifacts and avoided implementation slices.

3. **What domain has already been validated?**
   - Example: ownership grammar found high confidence for implementation recovery and low confidence for broader constitutional ownership recovery.

4. **What remains Unknown?**
   - Example: ownership grammar preserved Unknowns about non-implementation families, lawful equivalents of producers/helpers/artifacts, completion conditions, and visibility-versus-ownership recurrence.

5. **Is there independent recurrence, or only analogy from another domain?**
   - Example: non-implementation family survey required independent constitutional evidence and found only one viable first candidate.

6. **Is the visible surface ownership pressure, compatibility, presentation, orientation, or consumption?**
   - Example: repository-artifact slices repeatedly preserved consumer use without transfer of ownership.

7. **Which adjacent boundary is directly observable?**
   - Example: repository-artifact slices selected preservation authority, status/frontier authority, and roadmap authority one at a time.

8. **Who owns the recovered responsibility, what artifact carries it, and who consumes it?**
   - This is a recovery question, but orientation repeatedly prepares it by determining whether enough evidence exists to ask it lawfully.

9. **Would further movement recover ownership, or merely wrap, rename, relocate, or generalize already-owned material?**
   - Example: implementation completion grammar stops when remaining movement is lawful orchestration or compatibility handoff.

10. **Does repository authority permit recovery now, or require lawful stop?**
    - Example: non-implementation survey stopped short of proving a general non-implementation ownership grammar.

These are evidence products of orientation. They are not ownership recovery by themselves.

## Recurring human judgment points

These remain human judgment today:

1. **Selecting the survey corpus.** Repository artifacts can constrain scope, but a human surveyor still decides which recovered constitutional artifacts are responsive to the orientation question.

2. **Recognizing natural expansion.** The prompt permits expansion only where constitutional evidence naturally leads. Determining whether a lead is natural remains judgment-bound.

3. **Distinguishing analogy from independent recurrence.** The repository can preserve the distinction, but a surveyor still judges whether a proposed connection is evidence or merely resemblance.

4. **Classifying candidate readiness.** The non-implementation survey classified one candidate as ready for recovery, but that classification was a constitutional judgment over evidence, not an automatic computation.

5. **Calibrating confidence.** Confidence is repeatedly preserved, but no reviewed artifact recovers an automated confidence calculator for orientation recovery.

6. **Choosing lawful stop.** The repository preserves stop conditions, but stopping before recovery remains a surveyor act when evidence is insufficient.

7. **Maintaining forbidden boundaries.** Avoiding implementation recovery, repository-artifact recovery, and automation design requires human discipline under repository authority.
