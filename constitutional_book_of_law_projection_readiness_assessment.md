# Constitutional Book of Law Projection Readiness Assessment

## Boundary

This is exactly one bounded Repository Readiness Assessment. It determines whether already recovered repository evidence is sufficient to support projection of a constitutional **Book of Law** view.

It does not recover a Book of Law. It does not create a Book of Law. It does not recover implementation. It does not introduce a Law Engine, Constitutional Rule Engine, Runtime Law Manager, Registry, Framework, Planner, Scheduler, or Universal Evaluator.

Repository authority wins.

## Comparison

Comparison begins only after the independent assessments above.

### Independently supported laws

The following laws are independently supported strongly enough to be projected as a bounded read-only view if the repository chooses to project one later:

- Visibility is not Authority.
- Observation is not Fact.
- Fact is not Objective Truth.
- Projection is not Mutation.
- Unknowns are preserved.
- Lawful movement is evidence-gated, with family-local gates.
- Repeated behavior does not imply shared ownership.

The following are independently supported but require cautious projection language:

- Translation preserves provenance, because provider translation exists but exact implementation staging is uneven.
- Constitutional layer characterization is supported, but not as a single owner or runtime layer.
- Bridge topology is locally inspectable, not universally complete.

### Insufficiently supported laws

Insufficiently supported as laws for projection:

- Any claim that a Book of Law already exists.
- Any claim that a Book of Law has a constitutional owner.
- Any claim that one constitutional office owns all laws.
- Any claim that a universal evaluator, law engine, planner, scheduler, or registry is warranted.
- Any claim that recurrence alone stabilizes ownership.
- Any claim that current evidence proves complete constitutional topology.

### Active investigations

The following remain active or bounded by preserved uncertainty:

- Whether `constitutional history` should become formal repository vocabulary.
- Whether future multi-Seed exchange produces shared history, multiple histories, or imported evidence records.
- Whether provider translation contracts become uniformly staged.
- Whether typed unknown taxonomy stabilizes beyond preservation discipline.
- Whether every bridge has the same source-consumer sufficiency pattern.
- Whether future repository evidence stabilizes a new constitutional office.
- Whether a future read-only projection should group laws by family, evidence class, transition family, or another repository-earned shape.

### Contradictory evidence

No reviewed evidence contradicts the core negative laws. The strongest contrary pressures are not contradictions; they are projection limits:

- Recurring structure exists, but the app visibility boundary refuses authority inference.
- Local implementation registries exist, but their ownership is surface-local.
- Some transitions do admit, verify, promote, or record, but only under family-local gates.
- Bridge and topology evidence exists, but not complete topology ownership.
- Constitutional vocabulary recurs, but vocabulary is not automatically repository knowledge.

### Projection readiness

The repository has earned enough independent constitutional evidence to support a bounded, read-only projection of a constitutional Book of Law **as a view over already recovered evidence**.

That readiness is conditional:

- The projection must not create laws.
- The projection must not create ownership.
- The projection must not recover implementation.
- The projection must preserve evidence counts, contradictions, active investigations, and unknowns.
- The projection must remain subordinate to repository authority and implementation-backed evidence.
- The projection must not imply a law engine, runtime manager, registry, framework, planner, scheduler, or universal evaluator.

The repository has **not** earned implementation of such a projection, nor has it earned a canonical Book of Law artifact in this assessment. It has earned the ability to project such a view if a later bounded task asks for that projection under the same boundaries.

### Preserved unknowns

- Whether the future projection should exist as documentation, diagnostic visibility, or another read-only surface remains unanswered.
- Whether a future projection would require diagnostic inventory and shape-audit updates depends on whether it becomes an operational diagnostic, audit, CLI flag, view, or recordable output.
- Whether all candidate constitutional laws have been exhausted is unknown.
- Whether all recoveries reviewed here are sufficient for canonical vocabulary is unknown.
- Whether future evidence will contradict or refine any assessed law is unknown.
- Whether a Book of Law projection should include only high-confidence laws or also active investigations is unknown.

### Confidence

Overall confidence: **Medium-high**.

Confidence is **High** that multiple core negative laws are independently supported and stable.

Confidence is **Medium-high** that the repository has earned bounded read-only projection readiness.

Confidence is **Low** for any claim that projection readiness authorizes implementation, ownership, runtime management, universal evaluation, or canonical completeness.
