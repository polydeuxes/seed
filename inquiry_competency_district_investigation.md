# Inquiry Competency District Investigation

## Scope

This is one district discovery investigation. It does not recover implementation, recommend slices, introduce an Inquiry framework, introduce a Survey framework, or characterize implementation ownership boundaries.

The bounded question is:

```text
Do Scope and Resolution
appear to belong
to a larger recurring competency district?

If so,

what implementation evidence
makes that district visible?
```

Repository authority wins.

## Answer

Yes, with bounded confidence.

Scope and Resolution appear to belong to a larger recurring implementation-supported competency district that operates around **lawful inquiry admission and bounded inquiry identity before evidence-specific inquiry work proceeds**.

The present evidence does not establish an architectural family, a shared owner, a runtime framework, or a complete district recovery. It does establish a recurring district-level pattern across multiple investigations and implementation surfaces:

```text
candidate / external / operator-supplied inquiry material
↓
constitutional admission, scope, exact identity, or explicit target resolution
↓
lawful inquiry / evidence selection / answer composition may proceed only within that bounded identity
```

The district is visible because multiple implementation investigations independently encountered pre-inquiry competencies before they could lawfully recover, audit, or compose downstream work:

- exact question-family eligibility before bounded ask dispatch;
- explicit subject, target, domain, query, or note identity before evidence selection;
- external material preparation before bounded owners consume it;
- diagnostic and operational surface declarations/specifications before visibility claims can be audited;
- eligibility filtering before methodological selection or recovery continuation.

This answer does not prove that Scope and Resolution are isolated. It also does not prove that they are members of a recovered architectural family. The supported answer is that they appear to inhabit a **larger recurring competency district**, presently visible at district resolution only.

## District evidence

### 1. Exact inquiry family admission appears before bounded ask dispatch

The implementation statically maps known question families to dispatch surfaces and declares required explicit surface arguments for parameterized families. Unknown families are not inferred. `bounded_status_for_question_family(...)` derives whether a question family is eligible now, eligible with parameters, diagnostic-only, or not dispatchable from those implementation maps.

Implementation evidence:

- `BOUNDED_ASK_DISPATCH_SURFACES` maps exact question-family strings to existing answer surfaces.
- `BOUNDED_ASK_REQUIRED_SURFACE_ARGS` declares explicit parameters for `derivation explanation` and `selection explanation`.
- `_QuestionFamilyEligibilityInput` is a narrow prepared exact-text handoff.
- `_prepare_question_family_eligibility_input(...)` admits only an exact inventory-backed family and explicitly refuses free-text classification, dispatch selection, argument validation, presentation, rendering, or mutation.
- `_bounded_work_eligibility_for_prepared_question_family(...)` turns the exact admitted family into a permission result.

This evidence makes **Eligibility**, **Scope**, and **Resolution** visible before lawful bounded ask execution.

### 2. App dispatch preserves the same pre-inquiry ordering

The app-level bounded ask path requires the literal `ask --question-family <exact-question-family>` shape, prepares eligibility input, evaluates eligibility, requires exact explicit parameter counts when needed, and only then constructs and executes a dispatch request.

Implementation evidence:

- `apply_bounded_ask_dispatch(...)` rejects `--question-family` outside the exact `ask` shape.
- It prepares exact-family eligibility before dispatch.
- It rejects missing or extra explicit surface arguments for parameterized families.
- It routes presentation separately from execution.
- It dispatches only after eligibility, parameter validation, selection, and dispatch-request construction.

This makes the district visible as an ordering discipline: lawful inquiry does not begin from attractive prose alone; it begins after exact admission and explicit identity resolution.

### 3. External Orientation exposed recurring preparation before bounded owners

The External Orientation completion audit recovered multiple edges where external or operator-supplied material is prepared into narrow implementation-local handoffs before existing bounded owners consume it. That audit did not promote a generic orientation framework, but it showed recurring pre-inquiry preparation across inquiry orientation, source navigation, question-family eligibility, operational surface classification, and diagnostic inventory composition.

Implementation evidence includes:

- `_InquiryOrientationCompositionRequest`, built from a preserved inquiry note and note tokens before orientation composition.
- `_PreparedSourceNavigationQuery`, built from external query intake before source-navigation composition.
- `_QuestionFamilyEligibilityInput`, built from exact question-family text before bounded eligibility.
- `_CliSurfaceClassificationInput`, built from parser material before operational surface classification.
- `_DiagnosticInventoryCompositionInput`, built from diagnostic declarations before inventory output.

This evidence makes **Orientation/Preparation** visible as a recurring adjacent competency in the same district, but only at district resolution. It does not prove a common owner.

### 4. Operational Visibility investigations exposed declaration and verification before visibility claims

Operational Visibility is not the subject of this investigation, but its investigations exposed the same constitutional pattern in a different district edge: implementation-discovered operational surfaces, diagnostic registry declarations, and diagnostic shape specs must be declared and checked before visibility claims can be trusted.

Implementation evidence includes:

- CLI flags exposing operational visibility surfaces.
- operational surface discovery rows and classification rows.
- coverage composition that joins discovered surfaces and classifications.
- diagnostic inventory entries declaring read-only shape for operational visibility surfaces.
- diagnostic shape-audit specs and audit rows checking declarations against implementation evidence.

This makes **Declaration** and **Verification** visible as competencies that can occur before or during lawful inquiry depending on the surface. They belong in the same larger district only to the extent that they govern whether a question, surface, or claim can be lawfully relied on.

### 5. Prior methodology investigations required constitutional interrogation before recovery

Previous methodology and eligibility investigations repeatedly refused to move from interesting vocabulary or pressure into recovery until implementation evidence supported the claim strength. The architectural recovery methodology describes recovery as beginning from implementation compression, recovering one boundary at a time, preserving compatibility, proving behavior, and stopping when remaining pressure changes ownership family. Inquiry Eligibility characterizes a pre-selection discipline that rejects, defers, preserves, or narrowly authorizes candidate inquiries based on evidence strength.

This evidence makes **Eligibility** visible outside any single runtime module. It is strongest as repository practice and implementation-adjacent discipline, not as a named runtime subsystem.

## Recurring competencies

### 1. Scope

#### Identity

Scope bounds the claim, inquiry, or operational surface to what implementation evidence can support. Its constitutional role is to prevent a candidate inquiry from exceeding evidence, ownership, visibility, or authority boundaries.

#### Evidence

Scope appears in multiple implementation investigations:

- External Orientation completion stopped at narrow preparation handoffs and refused a shared orientation framework.
- Operational Visibility boundary investigation found multiple possible footings rather than exactly one recoverable boundary.
- Visibility Coverage Composition survey stayed at neighborhood resolution and preserved unknowns rather than recovering ownership.
- Architectural recovery methodology requires one compressed owner at a time and stops when pressure changes ownership family.
- Inquiry Eligibility requires claim strength to remain proportional to evidence strength.

Implementation-adjacent code evidence includes exact eligibility handoffs and negative authority clauses in `question_surface_inventory.py`: `_QuestionFamilyEligibilityInput` carries only exact question-family text, and `_prepare_question_family_eligibility_input(...)` refuses free-text classification, dispatch selection, argument validation, presentation, rendering, or mutation.

#### Phase

Before lawful inquiry.

Scope can also constrain inquiry during and after execution, but the recurring district evidence here is strongest before lawful inquiry proceeds.

#### Recurrence

Yes.

Scope recurs independently across External Orientation, Operational Visibility, Visibility Coverage Composition, Inquiry Eligibility, and architectural recovery methodology.

#### Confidence

High.

### 2. Resolution

#### Identity

Resolution supplies or validates the exact identity required before inquiry-specific evidence selection can begin: question family, dispatch surface, explicit subject, target, domain, query, note id, diagnostic name, or operational surface flag.

Its constitutional role is not semantic understanding. It is the lawful conversion of candidate inquiry material into an explicit implementation-backed identity.

#### Evidence

Resolution appears in multiple implementation investigations:

- Inquiry Subject Resolution found that public bounded inquiry starts from exact question-family identity and that subject-like values are often explicit operator-provided parameters.
- Bounded ask dispatch validates exact question-family strings and required surface arguments before routing.
- Source Navigation prepares a normalized query before source-navigation composition.
- Inquiry Orientation selects a recorded note and prepares note tokens before related-material orientation.
- Operational Visibility resolves parser flags into discovered surfaces and classification rows before coverage composition.

Implementation evidence includes exact dispatch maps and required argument maps in `question_surface_inventory.py`, the exact-family and exact-argument checks in `apply_bounded_ask_dispatch(...)`, `_PreparedSourceNavigationQuery` in `source_navigation.py`, and `_InquiryOrientationCompositionRequest` in `inquiry_orientation.py`.

#### Phase

Before lawful inquiry.

Resolution may be refined during surface-local work, but the recurring district evidence here is strongest before evidence selection and answer composition.

#### Recurrence

Yes.

Resolution recurs independently across Inquiry Subject Resolution, External Orientation, Source Navigation, Inquiry Orientation, bounded ask dispatch, and Operational Visibility investigations.

#### Confidence

High.

### 3. Eligibility

#### Identity

Eligibility determines whether a candidate inquiry, question family, boundary, vocabulary, or implementation direction is constitutionally permitted at the claimed strength.

#### Evidence

Eligibility appears in multiple implementation investigations:

- Inquiry Eligibility characterizes the recurring pre-selection discipline of comparing claim strength to evidence strength.
- Bounded ask eligibility returns `eligible_now`, `eligible_with_parameters`, `diagnostic_only`, or `not_dispatchable` from implementation maps.
- Operational Visibility boundary investigation concluded that evidence exposed multiple possible candidates but not exactly one lawful recovery footing.
- External Orientation completion audit concluded that no additional same-family seam was supported and preserved neighboring unknowns.
- Architectural recovery methodology describes stopping when remaining pressure changes ownership family.

Implementation evidence is strongest in `question_surface_inventory.py`, where exact question-family admission and eligibility are computed before selection or dispatch.

#### Phase

Before lawful inquiry.

#### Recurrence

Yes.

Eligibility appears independently in bounded ask implementation and in multiple methodology/recovery investigations.

#### Confidence

High.

### 4. Orientation / Preparation

#### Identity

Orientation or Preparation takes admitted external/operator/parser/declaration material and makes it usable by a bounded implementation owner without promoting it into broader truth, ownership, or action authority.

Its constitutional role is to preserve incoming material while preparing only the narrow facts needed for the next lawful owner.

#### Evidence

Orientation/Preparation appears in multiple implementation investigations:

- External Orientation completion audit identified recurring private preparation/handoff artifacts across inquiry orientation, source navigation, question-family eligibility, operational surface classification, and diagnostic inventory composition.
- Inquiry Orientation preserves raw notes outside the event ledger and prepares note tokens for read-only orientation.
- Source Navigation prepares normalized query and projected source rows before composition.
- Operational surface classification prepares parser material before classification.
- Diagnostic inventory prepares diagnostic declarations before inventory JSON/text composition.

Implementation evidence includes `_InquiryOrientationCompositionRequest`, `_PreparedSourceNavigationQuery`, `_QuestionFamilyEligibilityInput`, `_CliSurfaceClassificationInput`, and `_DiagnosticInventoryCompositionInput`.

#### Phase

Before lawful inquiry, sometimes at the boundary into lawful inquiry.

#### Recurrence

Yes.

The External Orientation family alone shows multiple independent preparation edges, and Operational Visibility/Diagnostic Visibility add adjacent examples.

#### Confidence

Medium-high.

The recurrence is strong, but the term `Orientation / Preparation` must remain district-level. Current evidence does not prove a single owner or framework.

#### Evidence

- Diagnostic Inventory declares operational shape for diagnostic and diagnostic-like surfaces.

#### Phase

Before lawful inquiry.

#### Recurrence

#### Confidence

Medium-high.

The evidence is direct, but this investigation does not recover whether declaration is one competency or several neighboring competencies.

### 6. Verification / Audit

#### Identity

Verification checks declared or composed inquiry/visibility shape against implementation evidence before stronger claims are trusted.

Its constitutional role is consistency enforcement: declared surfaces, shapes, and behaviors must remain aligned with implementation evidence.

#### Evidence

Verification appears in multiple implementation investigations:

- Diagnostic shape audit checks declared diagnostic fields against implementation-observed fields.
- Operational Visibility investigations rely on diagnostic shape verification for operational visibility surfaces.
- Visibility Coverage Composition uses implementation-discovered surfaces and classifications to report registration coverage.
- Architectural recovery methodology requires tests and audits to preserve recovered boundaries where executable behavior is involved.

#### Phase

During lawful inquiry.

It can also occur after a surface is declared, but the recurring implementation role is checking claims while inquiry/audit work proceeds.

#### Recurrence

Yes.

Verification recurs across diagnostic shape audit, operational visibility coverage, and recovery methodology tests/audits.

#### Confidence

Medium.

The evidence supports verification as district-adjacent. It is less certain whether it belongs in the same pre-inquiry district as Scope and Resolution or forms a neighboring audit district.

## Confidence

Overall confidence: **medium-high**.

Confidence is high that Scope and Resolution are not merely isolated one-off labels: exact scope bounding and identity resolution recur across bounded ask, subject resolution, external orientation, source navigation, inquiry orientation, operational visibility, and recovery methodology.

Confidence is medium that the larger recurring district should be characterized as a single district, because the evidence is distributed and implementation-local rather than represented by one owner, API, schema, runtime framework, or public surface.

Confidence is low for any stronger claim: current evidence does not support a recovered architectural family, implementation campaign, shared framework, or ownership boundary.

## Acceptance answer

Scope and Resolution have revealed a larger recurring implementation-supported competency district at district resolution.

They are not presently proven to be isolated implementation competencies. They are also not presently proven to be members of a recovered architectural family.

The district is made visible by recurring implementation evidence that lawful inquiry requires bounded scope, exact identity resolution, eligibility/admission, narrow preparation, explicit declaration, and sometimes verification before evidence selection, answer composition, recovery, or visibility claims can proceed.

District investigation complete.
