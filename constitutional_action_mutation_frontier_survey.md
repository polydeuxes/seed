# Constitutional Action / Mutation Frontier Survey

Repository authority wins.

## Investigation scope

This is exactly one Constitutional Frontier Survey.

It answers only this bounded question:

```text
Across recurring constitutional
recoveries,

what constitutional work,

if any,

separates lawful observation,

lawful reliance,

and lawful mutation?
```

The words `action` and `mutation` are treated only as investigation pressure. This survey does not assume that either term is already a constitutional participant. It does not implement an action engine, autonomous behavior, planning, scheduling, execution management, orchestration, mutation policy, or runtime behavior.

The investigation reviewed recurring constitutional evidence around observation, warrant, reliance, authority, translation, handoffs, implementation recovery, compatibility preservation, responsibility recovery, inquiry admissibility, pressure, goal characterization, operator grammar recovery, event-ledger behavior, diagnostic mutation boundaries, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop. Implementation evidence is used only where it reinforces recurring constitutional behavior.

## Recurring constitutional evidence

### 1. Observation does not itself authorize truth, reliance, or mutation

Recurring constitutional work repeatedly begins with a bounded receipt, observation, or representation, then refuses to let that receipt become truth or authority by mere presence.

The lawful transition survey states that source-side material may cross into Seed only as observed source material, provider output, operator statement, file content, external sample, imported report, raw input, or candidate claim. What crosses is not the external condition itself, but source-attributed material capable of preservation, inspection, translation, or representation.

The same survey explicitly refuses truth transfer: source existence, provider vocabulary, operator concern, foreign output, presentation label, imported report, repeated phrase, or diagnostic visibility cannot cross as Seed truth, verified fact, repository knowledge, mutation authority, or universal law by itself.

This supports a first separation:

```text
observation
!= truth
!= reliance
!= mutation authority
```

Observation may create material that can be inspected. It does not complete the constitutional work needed for downstream use or state change.

### 2. Warrant and reliance mediate lawful use without collapsing into mutation

`constitutional_reliance_characterization.md` recovers Reliance as a constitutional consumption discipline. Its supported answer is that reliance is the lawful downstream use of warranted content within a preserved authority boundary.

That document separates expression, warrant, and reliance:

```text
Expression carries candidate content.
Constitutional Warrant authorizes bounded lawful reliance.
Constitutional Reliance is the bounded lawful use that warrant permits.
```

It also states that lawful reliance requires repository evidence binding, preserved warrant, bounded identity, provenance/support preservation, role and consumer boundary, preserved authority boundary, negative authority, Unknown preservation, confidence calibration where supported, and stop/non-promotion discipline.

This supports a second separation:

```text
observation may expose content;
warrant may authorize bounded use;
reliance consumes only inside that authorization;
mutation remains excluded unless separately supported.
```

Reliance is therefore not mutation. Reliance is lawful use by a downstream consumer, constrained by what the evidence and authority boundary permit. Reliance may support a later move only if an additional local authority permits that move.

### 3. Authority performs boundary work around lawful movement and stop

`constitutional_authority_characterization.md` recovers authority as a boundary condition for lawful movement. Its conclusion says authority bounds what evidence permits, what translation may preserve, what warrant may authorize, what reliance may consume, what inquiry may conclude, what responsibility recovery may assign, what pressure may become, and when the repository must stop or preserve Unknown.

The same characterization explicitly denies that authority itself supplies truth, ownership, implementation, ontology, command, or universal grammar.

This supports a third separation:

```text
authority can permit or forbid a movement;
authority is not the evidence itself;
authority is not mutation itself;
authority does not become an action engine.
```

Authority is the recurring constitutional boundary that prevents unsupported promotion while allowing only repository-warranted movement.

### 4. Translation preserves warrant; it does not create new mutation rights

`constitutional_translation_invariant_investigation.md` is repeatedly cited by reliance and transition surveys as evidence for role-bounded, provenance-preserving, authority-reduced translation. Translation can move content across carrier, vocabulary, report shape, or consumer surface only when warrant, provenance, authority limits, confidence, and Unknowns survive.

This matters for action/mutation pressure because translation is a recurring intermediary act. It may make observed or warranted material consumable by a downstream surface, but it does not itself authorize mutation. Translation changes representation. It does not silently change cluster truth, implementation state, repository knowledge, or operational authority.

### 5. Producer → artifact → consumer handoffs are eligibility boundaries, not automatic action

Responsibility recovery evidence repeatedly treats handoffs as boundary-crossing artifacts. `responsibility_recovery_evaluation_readiness_investigation.md` says downstream responsibilities commonly become eligible only after consuming explicit evidence, identity, validation, assessment, construction result, durable record, payload, request, result, or compatibility handoff produced by an upstream boundary.

The same report says a proposed recovery is stronger when it has a concrete crossing artifact such as an event id, event payload, fact, state object, request/result dataclass, diagnostic row, inventory row, compatibility object, query/target/domain identity, cache lookup result, construction result, or support record.

This supports a fourth separation:

```text
producer completion
-> boundary-crossing artifact
-> consumer eligibility
```

That sequence is not the same as autonomous action. It is a lawful handoff discipline. A consumer may become eligible to rely, evaluate, render, validate, or proceed only within the authority of the artifact and the consumer's role.

### 6. Compatibility preservation constrains mutation-like recovery

Implementation recovery methodology and responsibility recovery evidence repeatedly require compatibility preservation. `responsibility_recovery_evaluation_readiness_investigation.md` states that completed recoveries preserved event kinds, ordering, causation, correlation, result shapes, report accessors, CLI/JSON output, diagnostic inventory, diagnostic shape audit, capability ordering, cache semantics, and answer/report surfaces.

This does not create mutation policy. It does show that where implementation change occurs, lawful recovery is not just “doing.” It must preserve existing contracts unless repository evidence supports a break. Compatibility is therefore a constitutional constraint around admissible change.

### 7. Pressure and goal-shaped prose do not become action authority

Pressure investigations consistently distinguish pressure from priority, truth, or automatic work selection. `pressure_visibility_competency_frontier.md` says Pressure Visibility is read-only and must not continue into recovery, slicing, ranking, architecture design, abstraction, planning, compatibility change, runtime modification, or autonomous work selection.

`operator_methodological_selection_characterization.md` similarly states that pressure artifacts inform selection but do not own the choice of which pressure to act on next. Its implementation evidence describes selection-path and reference-selection outputs as read-only, no-recording, no-event-ledger, no-cluster-mutation surfaces.

This supports another separation:

```text
pressure
!= priority
!= goal
!= action authority
!= mutation authority
```

Goal-shaped prose is likewise insufficient unless a local implementation or constitutional evidence path supports it.

### 8. Inquiry admissibility and lawful stop protect against forced continuation

Bounded inquiry recovery evidence says broad questions, possible observations, hypotheses, and testimony reduce to exact implemented families, evidence-strength checks, orientation, or explicit stop. Unsupported families remain Unknown. Interesting observations do not become recovered artifacts merely because they are fluent or recurring.

`constitutional_lawful_transition_survey.md` says movement terminates when evidence is insufficient, source material is malformed or unsupported, authority does not reach the proposed claim, provenance would be lost, claim strength would be exceeded, contradiction cannot lawfully be resolved, the subject remains Unknown, or movement would silently promote candidate material into truth.

This supports an intermediary act between reliance and mutation:

```text
admissibility / evaluation / stop
```

The repository repeatedly evaluates whether a move is lawful before allowing movement. If not, it preserves Unknown or stops. That is constitutional work, not an implementation scheduler.

### 9. Unknown preservation is part of the boundary, not a gap to infer through

Reliance characterization makes Unknown preservation a condition of lawful reliance. Continued reliance topology says Unknowns and confidence limits constrain reliance rather than being inferred through. Transition evidence says unresolved Unknowns must remain preserved during movement.

This prevents observation or reliance from becoming mutation through missing evidence. Unknowns are not TODOs, automatic actions, or implied permissions. They are preserved limits.

### 10. Lawful movement is distinct from artifact emission

`constitutional_movement_vs_artifact_emission_investigation.md` distinguishes constitutional movements such as constrain, evaluate, terminate, and preserve from the artifacts that may record them. Evaluation can move a possibility into supported, unsupported, ambiguous, incomplete, authority-blocked, outside-authority, candidate, admissible, inadmissible, or confidence-calibrated condition. It need not be identical to a report artifact.

This is relevant because mutation pressure can mistake an emitted artifact for completed action, or a movement for a mutation. Recurring evidence supports finer distinctions:

- constrain;
- evaluate;
- authorize or refuse bounded reliance;
- preserve Unknown;
- terminate/lawfully stop;
- emit an artifact recording the result;
- only where separately supported, mutate a domain surface.

## Recurring implementation evidence where applicable

The local AGENTS instructions require diagnostic surfaces that support `--record` to prove `record_scope=diagnostic_run`, and if they write the event ledger, to prove `mutates_cluster=false` unless intentionally operational. The app authority check used `--diagnostic-inventory` and `--diagnostic-shape-audit` to confirm that this visibility boundary remains implemented.

This implementation evidence supports:

```text
diagnostic observation
may be recorded as diagnostic_run evidence;
recording diagnostic evidence
!= cluster mutation;
cluster mutation requires separate authority.
```

### Repository observation is read-only by implementation shape

`seed_runtime/repository_observation.py` defines `RepositoryObservation` with `writes_event_ledger=False` and `mutates_cluster=False` by default. Its Git provider observes repository path, VCS, HEAD, branch, dirty status, counts, and remote presence without writing events or mutating cluster state.

This is a direct implementation example of lawful observation separated from mutation.

### Selection-path visibility is explicitly read-only

`seed_runtime/selection_path_audit.py` defines `SelectionPathAudit` with a default boundary of `read_only_selection_audit`, `records_facts=False`, `writes_event_ledger=False`, and `mutates_cluster=False`. Its formatter states the surface is read-only, with no recording, event ledger writes, or cluster mutation.

This reinforces that selection explanation or pressure ranking visibility is not action authority.

### Tool execution policy separates validation, authorization, execution, and event routing

`seed_runtime/tool_execution_policy.py` says registered operation validation and policy authorization are intentionally separate steps. Validation proves that the selected operation contract accepts the call; policy decides whether that valid call may execute now. The service intentionally does not execute tools, append events, create pending actions, or collapse non-allow policy outcomes.

This is one of the strongest implementation examples near action pressure. It separates:

1. selected operation contract validation;
2. policy authorization;
3. execution by a caller outside the policy service;
4. event/pending-action routing by existing runtime behavior.

That supports intermediary constitutional acts rather than a single concept of “action.”

### Capability resolution separates advisory handoff metadata from execution authority

`seed_runtime/tool_needs.py` says capability resolution returns read-only metadata. Catalog entries describe known capabilities and non-executable provider/handoff suggestions, while registered operation candidates come from registry lookup. The method does not execute tools, authorize actions, create pending actions, or mutate registry/catalog state.

This implementation evidence reinforces that accepting or exposing an external/provider handoff candidate is not execution and not mutation.

### Event ledger behavior distinguishes durable evidence from projected/domain mutation

`docs/runtime_event_ledger_work_reconstruction_reconciliation.md` characterizes the event ledger as append-only runtime history. The event coverage matrix distinguishes events that are durable but ignored by projection, events that project into state, read-only responses, tool lifecycle events, pending-action events, and state patch domain mutations.

Most importantly for this survey, the report distinguishes:

- operator input recorded as `input.user_message` without direct projected domain state impact;
- model decisions recorded without direct projection;
- response events recorded without projected domain state impact;
- tool lifecycle events recorded without direct projection;
- state patch accepted events that project into entities, evidence, facts, or goals;
- projection finalization as in-memory derived indexes rather than a ledger event.

This supports the implementation-side distinction between:

```text
recording that work happened
!= projecting domain state
!= mutating cluster truth
```

## Recovered constitutional role, if any

The recurring evidence does **not** yet support `Action` or `Mutation` as standalone constitutional participants.

It does support a constitutional frontier adjacent to action/mutation pressure: a **lawful change boundary** composed of recurring intermediary disciplines that separate observation, reliance, acceptance, mutation, and post-mutation observation.

The supported role is not an engine. It is not autonomy. It is not a scheduler. It is not a general mutation policy.

The recovered constitutional work is:

```text
Repository evidence may be observed.
Observed material may become evidence only within provenance and authority limits.
Warrant may authorize bounded reliance.
Reliance may consume warranted content only inside role, scope, negative authority, Unknowns, and confidence limits.
A proposed movement or change must then pass local admissibility, authority, compatibility, and handoff conditions.
Only surfaces with separate implementation-backed authority may mutate domain state, event history, repository files, or operational state.
After mutation, the result must be observed or reconstructed as evidence rather than presumed.
```

The constitutional frontier is therefore the boundary work that prevents this collapse:

```text
observation
-> reliance
-> mutation
```

into an unsupported shortcut.

The repository instead supports this more disciplined chain:

```text
observation / receipt
-> evidence binding or candidate preservation
-> warrant
-> bounded reliance
-> admissibility / authority / compatibility evaluation
-> acceptance or refusal
-> authorized local mutation, if any
-> post-mutation observation / reconstruction / projection
-> renewed reliance only within preserved support
```

## Supported constitutional distinctions

### Observing

Observing receives, preserves, inspects, or represents material with source/provenance limits. It does not by itself prove truth, authorize reliance, admit mutation, or change cluster state.

### Relying

Relying is bounded downstream use of warranted content. It requires preserved evidence, provenance, authority boundary, negative authority, Unknowns, confidence limits, and stop conditions. Reliance is consumption discipline, not mutation.

### Accepting

Accepting is supported only as a local admissibility or boundary-crossing act where evidence, identity, validation, authority, compatibility, and consumer role make a handoff usable. Acceptance can make a downstream consumer eligible. It does not necessarily mutate domain state.

The strongest supported forms of acceptance are:

- accepting content as a candidate;
- accepting evidence as sufficient for a bounded claim;
- accepting a handoff artifact as eligible input;
- accepting an operation contract as valid;
- accepting policy authorization for a specific execution;
- accepting a state patch into event-producing operations where implementation supports it.

These are not one universal acceptance primitive.

### Mutating

Mutation is not constitutionally generalized in the reviewed evidence. Implementation evidence shows local mutations only where a surface appends events, projects state, applies state patches, changes pending action status, writes repository files, or otherwise performs a concrete write. Lawful mutation requires separate local authority. Diagnostic recording, read-only visibility, pressure ranking, provider recommendations, and selection explanation are not mutation authority.

### Observing the result

Post-mutation observation is separate from mutation. Event-ledger reconstruction, projection replay, diagnostic audit, repository observation, and compatibility tests provide evidence of what changed or what was preserved. A mutation's occurrence is not the same as a lawful conclusion about its result.

## Answer to the bounded question

Across recurring constitutional recoveries, the constitutional work separating lawful observation, lawful reliance, and lawful mutation is **boundary-preserving lawful movement discipline**.

More concretely:

1. **Observation is source/provenance-bound receipt or representation.** It can expose material but cannot silently become truth, reliance, acceptance, or mutation authority.
2. **Evidence binding and warrant mediate movement from observation to lawful use.** Material becomes usable only when support, provenance, role, authority, negative authority, Unknowns, and confidence limits are preserved.
3. **Reliance is bounded consumption, not change.** A downstream consumer may use warranted content only within its authorized role.
4. **Acceptance is an intermediary local act when supported.** It may admit a candidate, evidence item, handoff artifact, validation result, policy result, or state patch into the next local boundary. Acceptance is not universal and not always mutating.
5. **Mutation requires separate local authority and implementation-backed write behavior.** Neither observation, pressure, goal-shaped prose, translation, diagnostic visibility, advisory handoff metadata, nor reliance supplies mutation authority by itself.
6. **After mutation, the repository must observe, replay, audit, or reconstruct the result.** Post-mutation observation renews evidence; it does not retroactively make the original mutation lawful.
7. **Unknown preservation and lawful stop prevent forced action.** Where evidence, authority, compatibility, identity, or admissibility is insufficient, the lawful result is Unknown, refusal, non-promotion, or stop.

In compact form:

```text
lawful observation = bounded source-aware seeing
lawful reliance = warranted bounded consuming
lawful mutation = separately authorized local write/change, if implementation-backed

The separator is not autonomy.
The separator is evidence, warrant, authority, admissibility,
compatibility, handoff, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop.
```

Confidence is **low** that the repository currently supports a general action/mutation architecture.
