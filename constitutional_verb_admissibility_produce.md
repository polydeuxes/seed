### Is `produce` independently repository-supported?

`produce` appears in immediately adjacent evidence:

- `constitutional_relationship_slice_001.md` explicitly names `Warrant produces/carries bounded entitlement-and-limit`.
- The same slice states `Warrant production owns the entitlement-and-limit that makes reliance lawful`.
- `constitutional_warrant_characterization.md` asks what produces constitutional warrant and answers that recurring repository evidence under bounded authority does so.
- `constitutional_warrant_global_characterization.md` asks what recurring constitutional conditions produce constitutional warrant.
- Cross-neighborhood evidence recognizes bounded producer -> bounded artifact -> bounded consumer as a recurring interaction shape.

This supports `produce` as repository wording adjacent to the boundary. It does not fully support `produce` as the smallest independent constitutional verb for the boundary, because the strongest recurring warrant characterizations define the constitutional property through preserved entitlement/limit, carried support, and lawful reliance. The source/input uses of `produce` mostly describe evidence or conditions giving rise to warrant, or local producer/artifact/consumer interaction grammar, not a separately recovered constitutional verb that must name the warrant boundary.

### Does another already recovered constitutional verb better describe the boundary?

The recurring adjacent warrant/reliance evidence already uses `carry` for the warrant side and `consume` for the reliance side:

```text

Reliance consumes within that entitlement-and-limit.
```

`carry` is repeatedly attached to preserved evidence/support, provenance, role, authority limit, negative authority, Unknowns, confidence, stop/non-promotion limits, and lawful downstream reliance. It also preserves the repository's negative authority: the artifact or content may move across forms without becoming implementation, runtime machinery, ownership, mutation, or stronger truth.

`consume` is already recovered for the receiving side and is not in doubt here.

### Do multiple verbs remain constitutionally distinguishable?

Yes, but only in a bounded way.

- `carry` remains distinguishable as preserving the entitlement-and-limit profile across forms and into lawful reliance.
- `consume` remains distinguishable as the reliance-side use-status that stays inside what warrant authorizes.
- `produce` remains visible in local source/input and producer/artifact/consumer wording, but the reviewed evidence does not require it as the smallest constitutional verb for the warrant boundary.
- `emit` remains artifact-output vocabulary in adjacent movement/admissibility/handoff grammar and is not the warrant boundary verb.

### Should the boundary remain lexically Unknown?

No, not entirely. The boundary need not remain lexically Unknown because recurring repository evidence already supports `carry` and `consume` around warrant/reliance:


However, the admissibility of `produce` as the constitutional verb remains only partially supported and should not replace the smaller recurring vocabulary.
