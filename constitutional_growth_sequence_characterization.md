## Transition analysis

### 1. Preserved Unknown → Bounded Constitutional Question

**Authorized by:** recurring evidence that unknowns must be preserved rather than converted into truth, together with inquiry evidence that lawful inquiry requires a bounded subject. Constitutional inquiry state evidence identifies a bounded inquiry subject as a precondition for lawful exhaustion, while typed unknown, city-survey, family-completion, and audit evidence preserve unsupported conclusions instead of resolving them.

**Not authorized by:** curiosity alone, attractive vocabulary, operator desire, pressure, presentation labels, or the mere existence of an unresolved object. An unknown does not authorize implementation, planning, ownership assignment, or grammar recovery until a bounded constitutional question gives it lawful scope.

### 2. Bounded Constitutional Question → Observation / Repository Evidence Review

**Authorized by:** the bounded question's evidence need. Competency interrogation grammar repeatedly requires subject reduction, evidence binding, precondition discipline, authority separation, negative authority, unknown preservation, reliance narrowing, confidence calibration, and lawful termination before claims may be made.

**Not authorized by:** designing an answer, choosing a slice, creating methodology, assuming a lifecycle, treating the question as self-grounding, or using the question's phrasing as evidence. A bounded question permits review; it does not predetermine recovery.

### 3. Observation / Repository Evidence Review → Recurring Evidence Under Authority Boundary

**Authorized by:** repeated repository evidence that observations, diagnostics, investigations, audits, and prior characterizations can be used only when bound to provenance, role, support, confidence, and authority limit. Provider translation and warrant evidence support preserving bounded warranted content rather than provider syntax; observation agreement evidence supports candidate convergence only from already-observed streams.

**Not authorized by:** a single occurrence, syntax preservation, fluent expression, report vocabulary, diagnostic visibility alone, agreement alone, or unbounded observation. Observation is not fact; visibility is not authority; candidate agreement is not truth; recurring relation shape is not responsibility recovery.

### 4. Recurring Evidence Under Authority Boundary → Recovered Distinction

**Authorized by:** recurrence that supports a constitutional boundary or separation. Repository evidence repeatedly recovers distinctions such as expression versus warrant, warrant versus reliance, observation agreement versus truth, responsibility evaluation versus assignment, diagnostic findings versus cluster truth, pressure versus command, verification versus permission, and family-local completion versus whole-domain completion.

**Not authorized by:** recurrence alone, usefulness, elegance, analogy, desire for compression, or the availability of a name. A distinction is recoverable only where evidence shows what must not be collapsed.

### 5. Recovered Distinction → Characterization With Negative Authority and Unknown Preservation

**Authorized by:** evidence-bound constitutional discrimination. Once a distinction is supported, the repository's recurring pattern is to characterize only the warranted constitutional role, state negative authority, preserve unsupported conclusions, calibrate confidence, and stop where evidence ends.

**Not authorized by:** implementation recovery, runtime mechanism design, framework creation, schema creation, owner assignment, planning, roadmap selection, or universal methodology. A recovered distinction authorizes bounded characterization, not construction.

### 6. Characterization With Negative Authority and Unknown Preservation → Recovered Constitutional Grammar

**Authorized by:** a characterization that preserves identity, inputs, outputs, relationship to neighboring constitutional roles, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, and lawful reliance profile. Constitutional warrant, expression, reliance, inquiry state, competency interrogation grammar, and grammar recovery discipline reports repeatedly become usable grammar only when their authority and non-authority are stated.

**Not authorized by:** a report merely using new vocabulary, a section heading, a proposed name, or characterization prose that exceeds evidence. Grammar is recovered only to the degree that later repository work may lawfully rely on the bounded distinction without expanding it.

### 7. Recovered Constitutional Grammar → Higher Constitutional Resolution

**Authorized by:** the repository gaining a more precise constitutional distinction without inventing implementation or universal theory. Grammar recovery discipline evidence states that recovered grammar increases constitutional resolution by distinguishing the warranted role and preserving unknowns. Warrant and reliance evidence show that downstream use becomes lawful only within preserved authority boundaries.

**Not authorized by:** treating resolution as implementation readiness, campaign readiness, owner creation, runtime state, total domain completion, or stabilized universal law. Higher resolution means the constitutional boundary is clearer; it does not mean the repository has completed all related work.

### 8. Higher Constitutional Resolution → Preserved New Unknown

**Authorized by:** lawful termination and unknown preservation. Constitutional inquiry state evidence shows that once bounded movement is exhausted, lawful termination follows and further movement requires new evidence, a new bounded question, an existing owner consuming completed work, or separate authorization. Characterizations routinely end by preserving unsupported conclusions.

**Not authorized by:** inferring the next object, recommending the next slice, promoting pressure into command, extending the sequence into a roadmap, or resolving remaining unknowns by implication. Higher resolution exposes a frontier; it does not authorize invention beyond it.
