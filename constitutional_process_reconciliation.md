## Directly supported movements

The following movements are directly supported:

1. **Pressure → Lawful Question.** A pressure may become a lawful inquiry only after bounded identity, evidence posture, warrant/dependency, authority boundary, negative authority, artifact expectation, Unknown preservation, stop condition, and confidence are present.
2. **Lawful Question → Orientation.** Question Grammar artifacts include handoff to Orientation when inquiry is admitted; Orientation receives an admitted inquiry question rather than deciding all question admission by itself.
3. **Orientation → Recovery.** Orientation prepares lawful attention before recovery; Recovery begins only when admitted and oriented evidence exposes a recoverable boundary.
4. **Recovery → Cross-Examination.** Recovered neighborhoods can be cross-examined for dependency, artifact exchange, identical responsibility, presentation-level similarity, required distinction, ownership compression, and collapse.
5. **Cross-Examination → Completion Audit.** District completion relied on characterization, topology, and cross-examination to determine whether the current district was complete for visible evidence.
6. **Completion Audit → Lawful Stop.** Completion Audit lawfully stops current district recovery when reviewed evidence is complete for visible support and remaining material is pressure rather than recoverable neighborhood.

## Inferred movements

The following remain inferred rather than directly recovered as universal transitions:

2. **Mandatory Cross-Examination after every Recovery.** Cross-examination is strongly supported for recovered constitutional neighborhoods and district completion, but the repository does not prove every recovery artifact must be followed by a separate cross-examination artifact.

### 1. Are these the same constitutional responsibility?

**No, not on current evidence.**

Question Admission is directly recovered as Question Grammar's responsibility: deciding whether a possible question may become a lawful bounded constitutional inquiry, deferred pressure, preserved Unknown, or lawful stop.

Asymmetrical Question Construction is visible as a pressure around how a question may be shaped when one side, dependency, endpoint, evidence posture, or authority boundary is not symmetrical. Current reviewed evidence supports bounded question shape, endpoint identity, dependency visibility, authority boundaries, and admissible evidence; it does not separately recover `Asymmetrical Question Construction` as the same owner as Question Admission.

### 2. Are they adjacent responsibilities?

**Yes, with medium confidence.**

They are adjacent because a constructed question shape supplies the possible bounded question that admission can test. Question Grammar already requires bounded subject identity, evidence posture, dependencies, authority, negative authority, artifact expectation, Unknowns, stop grammar, and confidence. If asymmetry changes any of those properties, it matters immediately before admission.

### 3. Does one produce artifacts consumed by the other?

**Partially supported.**

A bounded question statement, scope/non-goals, evidence corpus boundary, question shape, dependency record, negative-authority boundary, artifact expectation, Unknown list, and confidence calibration are Question Grammar artifacts. Current evidence supports those artifacts as consumed by admission and by handoff to Orientation. It does not yet prove a separate artifact producer named `Asymmetrical Question Construction`.

Therefore: constructed question material can be consumed by Question Admission, but a distinct producer remains unrecovered.

### 4. Would separating them reduce constitutional ownership compression?

**Unknown.**

Separating them might reduce compression if future repository evidence shows that asymmetrical construction has its own stable responsibility, artifact, consumer, stop conditions, and boundary before admission.

Current reviewed evidence does not prove that. Separating now would risk recovering ownership from pressure rather than evidence.

### 5. Or would separating them merely rename existing pressure?

**On current evidence, separating them would more likely rename existing pressure.**

The currently supported responsibility is Question Admission / Constitutional Question Grammar. Asymmetry is best preserved as unresolved pressure inside or adjacent to question shape and dependency discipline until repository evidence proves an independently consumed construction artifact or responsibility.
