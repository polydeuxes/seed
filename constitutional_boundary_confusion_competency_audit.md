# Constitutional Boundary-Confusion Competency Audit

Repository authority wins.

## Bounded question

Across current repository evidence, is Seed already capable of examining whether a produced artifact preserves the distinctions between:

- Evidence != Vibes
- Authority != Volume
- Visibility != Truth
- Repetition != Proof
- Confidence != Certainty
- Narrative != History

This is exactly one bounded competency audit. It does not implement the competency, add a diagnostic surface, create a framework, create a world-research workflow, implement the Eye, implement a truth engine, evaluate Flock cameras, perform legal research, or promote the motivation into constitutional law.

## App-visible evidence used

The app was used as a bounded, read-only evidence surface, not as an oracle.

- `python scripts/seed_local.py --help` exposed existing surfaces for diagnostic inventory, diagnostic shape audit, knowledge reachability, reasoning path, selection path, evidence, fact support, source navigation, contradictions, confidence facts, stale facts, current facts, and observation/projection surfaces.
- `python scripts/seed_local.py --diagnostic-inventory` showed current diagnostic and operational surfaces and their declared boundaries, including whether they use repository files, use projected state, support JSON or record, emit diagnostic facts, write the event ledger, and mutate cluster state.
- `python scripts/seed_local.py --diagnostic-shape-audit` showed the registry-vs-implementation checks for diagnostic shape declarations, including record scope, event-ledger writes, and `mutates_cluster` boundaries.
- `python scripts/seed_local.py --knowledge-reachability-audit --knowledge-reachability-audit-subject "source navigation" --knowledge-reachability-audit-limit 5` showed that a phrase may be treated as a presentation label and not preserved/projected/read/rendered unless implementation evidence supports it.

These outputs support only app-visible surface existence and boundary behavior. They do not decide the constitutional answer by themselves.

Reviewed evidence includes:

- `inquiry_eligibility_characterization.md`, especially bounded inquiry admissibility, evidence-strength checking, refusal of unsupported claim promotion, and projection/presentation-not-truth authority.

## Distinction-by-distinction analysis

### 1. Evidence != Vibes

1. **Already supported by recurring repository evidence?**
   Yes. The distinction is strongly supported.

2. **Supporting surfaces or grammar**
   - Core constitutional grammar starts with observation, accumulates evidence, and normalizes justified claims rather than beginning with truth or intuition.
   - Evidence-strength reconciliation distinguishes related evidence from sufficient evidence.
   - Constitutional Warrant requires evidence binding and provenance/support preservation before lawful reliance.
   - Inquiry Eligibility rejects pressure, roadmap, frontier, or presentation language as authorization without implementation evidence.

3. **What the repository already knows how to preserve**
   Seed can preserve the provenance, support, claim strength, authority boundary, and non-promotion limits that keep an artifact from treating motivation, fluency, pressure, or vibe-like language as evidence.

4. **What the repository does not yet know how to preserve**
   The repository does not expose a dedicated artifact-scoring or public-world evidentiary-quality evaluator that labels material as `vibes`. It can examine whether a produced artifact preserved evidence and support boundaries, but it does not implement a generalized vibe detector.

5. **Unknowns remain**
   Whether every produced artifact carries enough normalized support metadata to audit this distinction uniformly remains Unknown. Whether `vibes` should become repository vocabulary remains unsupported.

6. **Lawful stop condition**
   Stop when the artifact's claim exceeds repository-visible support or when the only support is motivation, repetition, narrative pressure, or presentation wording without evidence binding.

### 2. Authority != Volume

1. **Already supported by recurring repository evidence?**
   Yes, with a bounded form. Authority boundaries are strongly supported; `volume` as a named repository category is not.

2. **Supporting surfaces or grammar**
   - Constitutional Warrant requires preserved granted authority and negative authority.
   - Seed escalates to the operator when authority is required; capabilities, recommendations, projections, and visibility do not imply authorization.
   - Diagnostic inventory and shape audit preserve read-only, record, event-ledger, and mutation boundaries.
   - Responsibility Evaluation classifies authority-blocked or outside-authority conclusions rather than assigning ownership or responsibility by attractive evidence mass.

3. **What the repository already knows how to preserve**
   Seed can preserve who or what has authority for a claim, what authority is absent, and where a surface must not promote visibility or repeated support into mutation, truth, responsibility assignment, or execution authority.

4. **What the repository does not yet know how to preserve**
   The repository does not implement a general public-source-volume model, consensus counter, popularity model, or authority-vs-volume scoring engine.

5. **Unknowns remain**
   What counts as `volume` in a particular artifact remains local and often Unknown unless the artifact explicitly carries counts, source classes, or corroboration boundaries. Whether volume can ever be relevant evidence depends on local claim and authority grammar.

6. **Lawful stop condition**
   Stop when many instances, repeated sources, or high-volume visibility are offered as authority without a preserved authority path, role, provenance, or local admissibility rule.

### 3. Visibility != Truth

1. **Already supported by recurring repository evidence?**
   Yes. This is one of the strongest recurring boundaries.

2. **Supporting surfaces or grammar**
   - `docs/seed.md` states Seed does not begin with truth, observations are not automatically true, and projections communicate preserved knowledge without becoming authority.
   - Evidence-strength reconciliation treats partial visibility as support only for visible portions, not whole-artifact replacement or stronger truth claims.
   - App-visible diagnostic inventory and shape audit are visibility surfaces with explicit boundaries; they do not mutate cluster state or become truth engines.
   - Knowledge reachability auditing can show that presentation labels are not preserved knowledge.

3. **What the repository already knows how to preserve**
   Seed can preserve that an observation, projection, diagnostic row, app output, or rendered surface is visible evidence with boundary metadata, not truth by existence.

4. **What the repository does not yet know how to preserve**
   The repository does not provide a universal truth adjudicator. It also does not guarantee every rendered artifact has a normalized visibility-vs-truth marker unless the local surface carries the boundary.

5. **Unknowns remain**
   Whether a particular visible artifact is true remains Unknown unless supported by evidence, warrant, authority, and local claim rules. Whether all presentation labels should be audited by knowledge reachability remains local.

6. **Lawful stop condition**
   Stop when visibility is the only basis for truth, when a projection is treated as source authority, or when app output is used as an oracle rather than bounded evidence.

### 4. Repetition != Proof

1. **Already supported by recurring repository evidence?**
   Yes in principle through evidence binding, support strength, recurrence boundaries, and warrant. Less directly as a named `repetition` distinction.

2. **Supporting surfaces or grammar**
   - Constitutional Warrant says unsupported vocabulary, aspiration, or repeated prose cannot substitute for evidence.
   - Evidence-strength reconciliation blocks escalation from related evidence to stronger claims.
   - Inquiry Eligibility rejects presentation vocabulary or repeated pressure when implementation evidence is insufficient.
   - Continued-reliance topology distinguishes contradiction discovery, support revision, and history preservation from mere repetition.

3. **What the repository already knows how to preserve**
   Seed can preserve that recurrence may be evidence of a pattern, but proof of a stronger claim requires local support, provenance, authority, and claim-strength matching.

4. **What the repository does not yet know how to preserve**
   There is no general proof system, no repetition-count-to-proof rule, and no public-world corroboration engine. Repetition can be examined only as repository-visible recurrence or artifact content under local evidence rules.

5. **Unknowns remain**
   Whether repeated statements are independent, copied, authoritative, stale, contradictory, or merely fluent remains Unknown without source/provenance analysis. The repository does not currently normalize independence of repeated public-world claims.

6. **Lawful stop condition**
   Stop when repeated wording, recurring narrative, or high count is used as proof without independent evidence binding and a local rule that makes recurrence admissible for the claim.

### 5. Confidence != Certainty

1. **Already supported by recurring repository evidence?**
   Yes. The distinction is strongly supported as confidence/evidence-strength calibration and Unknown preservation, not certainty modeling.

2. **Supporting surfaces or grammar**
   - Constitutional Warrant preserves confidence or evidence-strength limits where the source carries them.
   - Evidence-strength reconciliation prevents support strength from being overclaimed.
   - Responsibility Evaluation preserves candidate labels, confidence, ambiguity, insufficient evidence, blocked authority, and outside-authority results.
   - Continued-reliance topology preserves confidence limits, stale support concerns, and lawful stop when support becomes insufficient.

3. **What the repository already knows how to preserve**
   Seed can preserve confidence as bounded, support-relative, and constrained by Unknowns and authority. It can prevent high confidence from becoming certainty or truth authority.

4. **What the repository does not yet know how to preserve**
   The repository does not implement a universal certainty calculus, probabilistic truth engine, or global confidence calibration standard for every artifact.

5. **Unknowns remain**
   Whether every artifact must expose confidence explicitly remains Unknown. How to compare confidence across heterogeneous surfaces remains Unknown unless a local implementation defines it.

6. **Lawful stop condition**
   Stop when confidence is treated as certainty, when uncertainty is hidden, or when a high-confidence candidate is promoted beyond its preserved support and authority boundary.

### 6. Narrative != History

1. **Already supported by recurring repository evidence?**
   Partly yes. History/current-state distinctions are supported; `narrative` as a separate repository competency is less developed and must remain partially Unknown.

2. **Supporting surfaces or grammar**
   - `docs/seed.md` separates historical claims from facts, relationships, assessments, decisions, and other claim forms; it also says events, changes, state, and projections are distinct and that history survives current-state change.
   - Continued-reliance topology preserves history through contradiction handling, support revision, projection/read-view boundaries, and current lawful condition.
   - Natural-language interpretation grammar treats language as evidence of communicative acts while remaining weak or irrelevant as direct support for environment truth.

3. **What the repository already knows how to preserve**
   Seed can preserve that a produced account, explanation, or narrative-like rendering is not automatically historical fact. It can preserve event/history/projection/current-state boundaries where the artifact carries support, time, provenance, and claim form.

4. **What the repository does not yet know how to preserve**
   The repository does not implement a general narrative-analysis engine, historiography model, world-history verifier, or public-world chronology reconciler. It does not normalize `narrative` as a first-class repository claim form distinct from language interpretation and historical claims.

5. **Unknowns remain**
   Whether a specific narrative accurately preserves history remains Unknown without bounded evidence, temporal provenance, support paths, and authority. Whether the repository should introduce a named narrative/history competency remains unsupported by current evidence.

6. **Lawful stop condition**
   Stop when a coherent story, explanation, or public motivation is treated as historical fact without preserved event evidence, temporal provenance, support chain, and claim-form boundary.

## Supported competencies

Current repository evidence supports a bounded pre-Eye competency examination. Seed can review a produced artifact for whether it preserves boundary distinctions using existing disciplines:

1. **Evidence binding and claim-strength matching** — prevents evidence/vibes, repetition/proof, confidence/certainty, and visibility/truth collapse.
2. **Constitutional Warrant** — preserves evidence-bound lawful reliance under authority boundary, negative authority, Unknowns, confidence limits, and stop discipline.
3. **Inquiry Eligibility / bounded inquiry admissibility** — determines whether a candidate question, vocabulary, boundary, or implementation direction has been earned by repository evidence.
4. **Responsibility Evaluation** — classifies claims as supported, unsupported, ambiguous, incomplete, authority-blocked, outside-authority, or candidate without overpromotion.
5. **Diagnostic inventory and diagnostic shape audit discipline** — preserves visibility/record/mutation boundaries for operational surfaces.
6. **Knowledge reachability / presentation-vocabulary caution** — helps prevent visible terms and labels from becoming preserved knowledge without implementation evidence.
7. **Temporal/history/projection boundaries** — preserve distinctions among event, change, state, projection, current condition, and history where evidence supports them.

## Unsupported competencies

Current repository evidence does not support:

- an Eye implementation;
- a world-research engine;
- a truth engine;
- artifact scoring;
- a public-world source authority model;
- a legal or Fourth Amendment analysis;
- a Flock-camera evaluation;
- a universal proof system;
- a general vibe detector;
- a volume/consensus authority calculator;
- a narrative-analysis or historiography engine;
- promotion of the six phrases into a new constitutional ontology.

## Smallest missing competency or implementation pressure

The smallest missing competency is not implementation. The missing pressure is an artifact-discipline gap:

```text
Produced artifacts are not uniformly required to expose, in one normalized place, which claim-form boundary, support path, authority boundary, confidence/evidence-strength limit, Unknowns, and lawful stop condition they used for each boundary-confusion risk.
```

This does not justify a new diagnostic surface yet. Existing repository evidence supports a next bounded investigation only if a future task asks whether current artifact forms already carry enough fields or sections to make this review repeatable.

## Preserved Unknowns

- Whether `vibes`, `volume`, `proof`, `certainty`, and `narrative` should become stable repository vocabulary remains Unknown.
- Whether every produced artifact carries enough support metadata to audit all six distinctions uniformly remains Unknown.
- Whether a normalized artifact-level boundary-confusion checklist is warranted remains Unknown.
- Whether public-world source independence, source authority, and source volume can be evaluated without world research remains Unknown.
- Whether confidence should be mandatory on every artifact remains Unknown.
- Whether narrative/history requires a dedicated competency or is sufficiently covered by existing language-interpretation and historical-claim boundaries remains Unknown.

## Lawful stop

This audit stops at competency examination. It concludes that Seed can examine these distinctions before the Eye using existing repository evidence, but only as a bounded artifact review grounded in evidence binding, warrant, inquiry eligibility, responsibility evaluation, diagnostic visibility boundaries, knowledge reachability caution, confidence limits, Unknown preservation, and temporal/history boundaries.

It does not implement the competency. It does not create a diagnostic surface. It does not promote public-world motivation into constitutional law. It does not claim Seed can determine external truth.

## Recommendation for next bounded investigation

Only if repository evidence is needed for repeatability, the supported next bounded investigation would be:

```text
Determine whether existing produced-artifact formats already expose support path, claim form, authority boundary, confidence/evidence-strength limit, Unknowns, and lawful stop condition consistently enough to support repeatable boundary-confusion review without adding a new diagnostic surface.
```

No implementation work is recommended by this audit.
