## 1. Analytical-competence analysis

Repository evidence does not currently establish `analytical competence` as a canonical implemented entity. The app-visible reachability result treated the exact breadcrumb as not preserved. The lawful recovery is therefore smaller: analytical competence means a repository-supported capacity to perform bounded comparison, source-role preservation, source/support review, claim-strength calibration, confidence limitation, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop within a stated evidence scope.

That competence is methodological and evidentiary. It can say that a source packet has identified roles, missing lineage, precedent dependency, mismatch, contradiction, stale support, non-independent support, temporal uncertainty, claim-form mismatch, or unresolved Unknowns. It cannot by itself change the legal status of a statute, regulation, court opinion, doctrine, amendment, factual event, or public-world actor.

## 2. Adjudicative-authority analysis

Repository evidence also does not establish a Seed adjudicative-authority model. The nearest prior boundary states that adjudicative authority is an external or legal-institutional capacity to decide legal effect, and that Seed does not acquire it by detecting pressure, observing repetition, running diagnostics, finding source-derived gaps, or composing constitutional investigations.

For this investigation, adjudicative authority means competent authority to decide a legal consequence for an exact claim form under a jurisdiction, temporal condition, procedural posture, source role, source authority, and source-of-law path. It would require separately preserved support for the authority to decide, not merely competence to analyze.

## 3. Competence / authority distinction

Analytical competence is capacity to inspect, compare, preserve, explain, and stop. Adjudicative authority is capacity to bind, decide effect, or determine legal status.

The constitutional work is a non-promotion boundary:

```text
competent analysis of evidence
→ bounded explanation / pressure / support gap / Unknown / lawful stop
≠ binding legal effect
```

Seed may become better at analysis without acquiring any authority to adjudicate. Better parsing, better source-role recovery, clearer comparison, or higher confidence only improves the bounded explanation unless a separate authority path is preserved.

## 4. Support-gap / legal-effect distinction

A support gap means the preserved evidence is insufficient for the proposed claim form. It can arise from missing constitutional text, amendment text, enactment path, promulgation path, holding boundary, source role, source independence, negative authority, jurisdiction, procedural posture, temporal authority, or currentness.

Deciding legal effect says what follows legally from that gap. Repository evidence blocks that step. Missing support is not disproof; insufficiency is not adjudication; absence in Seed is not absence in law; a diagnostic gap is not legal invalidity.

Lawful form:

```text
Source packet does not preserve enough support for claim C under boundary B.
Legal effect remains Unknown.
```

Unsupported form:

```text
Claim C is legally invalid, unenforceable, unconstitutional, or void.
```

## 5. Boundary-mismatch / legal-conclusion distinction

Boundary mismatch is comparison pressure: two preserved boundary descriptions do not align for the same bounded claim form, source role, jurisdiction, time, posture, and authority path. It can support a careful statement that the compared boundaries are mismatched, insufficiently linked, differently scoped, or Unknown.

A legal conclusion requires more than tension. It requires a legal claim form plus competent legal authority or a preserved legal-source pathway sufficient for that exact conclusion. Seed may preserve mismatch and stop; it may not convert mismatch into illegality, constitutional invalidity, unenforceability, or historical truth.

## 6. Constitutional-pressure / constitutional-validity distinction

Constitutional pressure is pressure on Seed's lawful reasoning boundary. It signals that Seed should avoid unsupported promotion, preserve Unknowns, calibrate confidence and claim strength, and stop before overclaiming.

Constitutional validity is a stronger status claim about a law, source, doctrine, amendment, practice, holding, or institutional action. It would require competent constitutional authority, source-of-law support, temporal and jurisdictional authority, procedural posture, claim-form precision, negative authority review, confidence limits, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop.

Therefore constitutional pressure may be preserved as pressure only. It is not a constitutional holding.

## 7. Comparison-competence / jurisdictional-authority distinction

Comparison competence lets Seed compare artifacts or claims within repository-evidenced boundaries. It may distinguish text from interpretation, precedent dependency from source-of-law lineage, source-attributed assertion from warranted reliance, and repetition from independent corroboration.

Jurisdictional authority is not comparison skill. It is a legal/institutional authority boundary defining who may decide, where, for whom, under what law, at what time, in what posture, and with what effect. Seed can notice that jurisdiction is missing or mismatched. It cannot fill jurisdiction by comparing well.

## 8. Source-of-law-analysis / source-of-law-authority distinction

Source-of-law analysis preserves the path by which a legal material might support a bounded legal-source or constitutional-source claim: text, version, issuer, adoption/enactment/promulgation path, forum, holding boundary, dependency, negative authority, jurisdiction, time, source role, procedural posture, and claim form.

Source-of-law authority is the authority of the source itself, or of a competent institution, to define legal effect. Repository evidence supports a boundary that a chain of cases, citations, commentary, or doctrine history is not source-of-law lineage by itself. Analysis can identify missing lineage; it cannot create lineage authority.

## 9. Explanation / binding-determination distinction

A competent explanation is a bounded account of evidence, support, confidence, authority limits, Unknowns, and stop conditions. It can be useful inside Seed for lawful reliance on repository evidence.

A binding determination changes or declares legal status. Repository evidence does not authorize Seed investigations, diagnostics, source navigation, or shape audits to bind courts, agencies, legal institutions, parties, public-world sources, or legal sources.

## 10. Confidence / authority distinction

Confidence is a support-calibration signal. It estimates how strongly the preserved evidence supports the bounded analytical statement.

Authority is permission or competence to define a type of claim. Claim-strength evidence already separates confidence, trust, corroboration, and authority. High confidence that evidence is missing does not authorize a stronger legal conclusion. High confidence in a comparison does not establish jurisdiction, adjudicative authority, enforceability authority, validity authority, or constitutional authority.

## 11. Seed-lawful-reliance / legal-institutional-reliance distinction

Seed-lawful reliance is internal bounded use of warranted repository material within a preserved role and authority boundary. It depends on evidence, provenance, role, authority limit, negative authority, Unknowns, confidence, and stop conditions.

Legal-institutional reliance is reliance by a court, agency, lawyer, litigant, legislature, regulator, or other legal institution under legal rules and institutional authority. Seed's lawful reliance can justify preserving or using an analytical result inside Seed; it does not create legal reliance in a legal institution.

## 14. Provenance / jurisdiction / procedural-posture analysis

Provenance identifies why a claim is available for consideration and where it came from. Source-of-law provenance identifies the legal-source path that might support a legal claim. Temporal provenance identifies whose clock or legal time supplies the time claim and what kind of time is asserted.

Jurisdiction bounds where a source or authority can matter. Procedural posture bounds what an opinion, filing, agency action, legislative act, or institutional source decided or could decide. Source role distinguishes text, interpretation, commentary, precedent, narrative, packet evidence, diagnostic output, and repository analysis. Source authority asks whether that source is allowed to define this kind of claim.

Claim form controls strength: `a source says X`, `Seed has support for X`, `X is current law`, `X is valid`, and `X is enforceable` are different claims. Confidence limits how strongly Seed states its bounded analysis. Negative authority prevents one-sided promotion. Unknown preservation prevents missing evidence from becoming invented authority.

## 15. Authority-boundary analysis and lawful stop

The lawful stop condition is triggered whenever analytical output would need to become adjudicative output without separately preserved adjudicative authority and support.

Stop when a statement would move from:

```text
analyzed / compared / support gap detected / pressure preserved / Unknown preserved
```

to:

```text
valid / invalid / unconstitutional / illegal / enforceable / unenforceable / binding / decided
```

unless the stronger legal authority, jurisdiction, posture, source role, source authority, source-of-law support, temporal authority, claim form, confidence boundary, negative authority, and Unknown handling are separately preserved.
