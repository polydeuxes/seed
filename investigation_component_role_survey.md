## Independently surveyed components

Each recurring component is inspected independently before comparison. `Role != ?` names unsupported expansions the evidence does not authorize.

### 1. Bounded question

What recurring responsibility does it serve?

- Narrows an unbounded possibility, pressure, recurrence, candidate, readiness concern, bridge concern, or competency concern into one repository-answerable subject.
- Prevents broad interest from becoming unrestricted recovery, implementation, workflow, or constitutional promotion.

Does it appear to provide input?

- Yes. It supplies the selected inquiry subject or question boundary that later claims must answer.

Does it appear to constrain movement?

- Yes. It excludes adjacent questions and prevents expansion beyond the present bounded work.

Does it appear to transform understanding?

- Yes. It transforms broad or attractive concern into an evidence-answerable repository question.

Does it appear to produce output?

- Sometimes. It can produce a bounded question statement, but its recurring responsibility is not primarily final output.

Does it appear to evaluate?

- Partly. It evaluates whether the broad formulation has been reduced enough to be answerable, but it does not by itself evaluate the answer.

Does it appear to terminate?

- Partly. It can stop unsupported broad questions by refusing unbounded movement.

Must its role remain unknown?

- Not wholly. Input, constraint, and transformation roles are visible. Exact ordering and universal requiredness remain unknown.

Role != ?

- Bounded question != canonical first step.
- Bounded question != planner.
- Bounded question != implementation authorization.
- Bounded question != investigation grammar.

Does it appear to terminate?

## Components whose role remains unclear

No independently recurring component surveyed here is wholly role-unknown. However, several role boundaries remain unclear:

- Whether bounded question is always an initial input or sometimes an output of reduction remains locally variable.
- Whether evidence scope primarily functions as input, constraint, or evaluation depends on the report shape.
- Whether independent inspection is mandatory for all investigation forms remains unsupported.
- Whether output/handoff terminates or merely transfers bounded material remains local.
- Whether confidence is part of evaluation, termination, reliance, or output remains overlapping rather than singular.
- Whether locality should be separated from boundary in every investigation remains unsupported.
- Whether recurring role labels are stable repository vocabulary remains unsupported.

## Incomplete topology

The topology remains incomplete.

Repository evidence presently supports recurring role neighborhoods and overlapping responsibilities, not a complete component map. The evidence also does not prove that every lawful repository investigation must contain every component as a named section. Several components recur as responsibilities that may be folded into evidence, scope, boundary, reliance, unknowns, or confidence depending on the local investigation.
