# Constitutional Selection Boundary Scout

## Scout boundary

This is exactly one bounded Scout investigation. It asks only where recurring repository evidence supports constitutional selection boundaries relating to Question Shape, Survey Warrant Family, and Question Family.

It does not recover implementation, runtime behavior, ownership, dispatch implementation, implementation recommendations, slices, hidden selection engines, or new stabilized vocabulary. Repository authority wins.

## Concept-by-concept selection recovery

### 1. Question Shape

#### What selects a Question Shape?

**Recurring selection trigger:** A bounded inquiry or recovery artifact needs a repeatable form of asking, at a particular resolution, under an evidence posture.

**Recurring selection evidence:** Prior relationship evidence reports Question Shape recurring in observation question templates, bounded self-questions, Scout/Survey/Characterization report forms, narrative neighborhoods, and QuestionFamily Admission candidate discussion. Survey Warrant Family evidence treats bounded question shape as one support criterion, but not as the whole warrant.

**Recurring selector, if supported:** No single selector is supported. Repository evidence supports local artifacts and bounded inquiries preserving or recognizing a question shape when evidence recurs. It does not support a centralized Question Shape selector, registry, or owner.

**Recurring constrained artifact:** The constrained artifact is the inquiry form itself: what kind of question may be asked, what resolution it may occupy, and what evidence posture must remain attached. In Survey Warrant evidence, it also constrains whether an investigation pattern can be counted as part of a warrant family.

**Recurring permitted behavior:** A supported Question Shape permits bounded characterization of the ask form, reuse of the form as methodological evidence, preservation through narrative or constitutional investigation, and possible later consideration by an admission boundary.

**Recurring refused behavior:** It refuses promotion into a public Question Family without explicit registration/admission evidence; refuses treating all bounded asks as the same shape; refuses a universal Question abstraction; refuses semantic routing, ownership recovery, or implementation recovery by shape vocabulary alone.

**Confidence:** Medium that Question Shape is selected by recurring bounded ask form plus evidence posture; low that any distinct selector exists.

#### What does a Question Shape subsequently select, permit, or constrain?

**Subsequent selection supported:** It can participate in selecting or supporting a Survey Warrant Family only as one criterion among bounded question shape, implementation-evidence posture, investigation shape, and produced artifact pattern. It can also be a candidate input to QuestionFamily Admission discussion where the question becomes whether a shape is registered or handled.

**Subsequent selection unsupported:** It does not itself select a Question Family, dispatch surface, owner, implementation slice, answer surface, or constitutional warrant. It does not prove admission.

**Subsequent constrained artifact:** It constrains the inquiry's ask-form boundaries: scope, resolution, evidence posture, and non-collapse with adjacent forms.

**Subsequent permitted behavior:** It permits bounded inquiry to proceed in that form, permits comparison with recurring warrant evidence, and permits preservation of unknowns about registration or admission.

**Subsequent refused behavior:** It refuses using presentation vocabulary or recurring phrasing as repository knowledge without implementation or recurring constitutional evidence.

**Confidence:** Medium.

### 2. Survey Warrant Family

#### What selects a Survey Warrant Family?

**Recurring selection trigger:** Multiple prior artifacts show the same bounded constitutional investigation pattern recurring with stable enough shape to warrant family-level description.

**Recurring selection evidence:** `survey_warrant_recurrence_investigation.md` is the primary evidence. It requires recurrence across bounded question shape, implementation-evidence posture, investigation shape, and produced artifact pattern. Its supported families include Scout/reconnaissance, Survey/neighborhood-resolution, Characterization/comparative-distinction, Inventory/coverage, Implementation-boundary, Completion/audit, and Boundary/non-collapse-like warrants with varying confidence.

**Recurring selector, if supported:** The selector is recurring repository evidence evaluated by a bounded Scout/Survey-style investigation. No separate constitutional warrant registry, runtime selector, or implementation owner is supported.

**Recurring constrained artifact:** The constrained artifact is the investigation artifact and its conclusion strength: Scout, Survey, Characterization, Inventory, audit, completion, or boundary report.

**Recurring permitted behavior:** A selected warrant family permits the investigation to ask a bounded kind of constitutional question, bind evidence, state supported distinctions, preserve unsupported conclusions, calibrate confidence, and stop lawfully.

**Recurring refused behavior:** It refuses implementation authority, runtime ownership, public bounded ask dispatch identity, taxonomy stabilization beyond evidence, and conversion of recurrence into universal law.

**Confidence:** Medium-high that Survey Warrant Family selection occurs through recurring evidence across the four criteria; low that any implemented selector exists.

#### What does a Survey Warrant Family subsequently select, permit, or constrain?

**Subsequent selection supported:** It selects or authorizes the form and strength of a constitutional investigation artifact: whether the work is Scout-like, Survey-like, Characterization-like, Inventory-like, Completion/audit-like, or Boundary/non-collapse-like.

**Subsequent selection unsupported:** It does not select Question Families, bounded ask dispatch targets, answer responsibilities, implementation owners, or runtime behavior.

**Subsequent constrained artifact:** It constrains the permissible investigation method, evidence threshold, produced artifact pattern, conclusion strength, confidence, negative authority, and lawful stop condition.

**Subsequent permitted behavior:** It permits bounded methodological use of recurring constitutional evidence and permits preserving unknowns where recurrence is insufficient.

**Subsequent refused behavior:** It refuses recovering implementation, recommending slices, collapsing adjacent concepts, or treating survey warrant authority as public app-routing authority.

**Confidence:** Medium-high.

### 3. Question Family

#### What selects a Question Family?

**Recurring selection trigger:** A public, answerable inquiry identity exists when implementation explicitly registers an exact Question Family row and, where bounded ask participation is supported, provides bounded ask metadata pointing to an existing surface.

**Recurring selection evidence:** `question_family_registration_boundary_audit.md`, `docs/bounded_ask_question_family_eligibility_audit.md`, `seed_runtime/question_surface_inventory.py`, `scripts/seed_local.py`, and tests all show exact family names, static inventory rows, bounded status, required surface args, diagnostic-only declarations, map-backed dispatch surfaces, unknown-family rejection, and free-text rejection.

**Recurring selector, if supported:** The supported selector is explicit implementation registration in Question Surface Inventory plus bounded ask eligibility/dispatch metadata. A separate recovered-family-to-registration owner is not supported. Exact operator selection of a registered family selects among already registered families at invocation time; it does not create the family.

**Recurring constrained artifact:** The constrained artifact is the public inquiry surface: exact Question Family identity, definition/explanation payloads, bounded ask eligibility, required arguments, diagnostic-only status, not-dispatchable status, and mapping to an existing answer surface.

**Recurring permitted behavior:** A supported Question Family permits inventory visibility, definition/explanation composition, diagnostic registration/audit visibility, exact bounded ask eligibility when mapped, parameterized eligibility when required args are supplied, and deterministic forwarding to an existing surface.

**Recurring refused behavior:** It refuses unknown family inference, free-text semantic routing, diagnostic-only execution as inquiry answer, missing parameter inference, public answerability without static registration, and promotion from recovered concept to Question Family without implementation edits.

**Confidence:** High for implementation-backed selection by explicit registration and exact invocation; high that no separate registration responsibility is currently implemented.

#### What does a Question Family subsequently select, permit, or constrain?

**Subsequent selection supported:** At invocation, exact Question Family text selects bounded ask eligibility status. If permitted, it selects the existing dispatch surface through static maps and may require explicit surface args.

**Subsequent selection unsupported:** It does not select Survey Warrant Family, constitutional warrant authority, ownership, implementation readiness, or semantic interpretation of operator prose.

**Subsequent constrained artifact:** It constrains bounded ask invocation, required parameters, answer surface routing, explanation/definition content, diagnostic visibility, and refusal modes.

**Subsequent permitted behavior:** It permits read-only inquiry-answer participation for eligible rows, parameterized participation for eligible-with-parameters rows, and diagnostic-only visibility for diagnostic-only rows.

**Subsequent refused behavior:** It refuses dispatch for unknown, not-dispatchable, diagnostic-only, or under-parameterized families; refuses treating the inventory as a route to arbitrary runtime behavior; refuses mutation by bounded ask identity alone.

**Confidence:** High.

## Comparative summary

According to recurring repository evidence, constitutional selection decisions occur in different places for the three concepts.

A Question Shape is selected locally by recurring bounded ask form under evidence posture. It subsequently constrains the form, scope, resolution, and evidence discipline of an inquiry, but it does not itself select implementation registration, dispatch, ownership, or warrant authority.

A Survey Warrant Family is selected by recurring constitutional evidence across bounded question shape, implementation-evidence posture, investigation shape, and produced artifact pattern. It subsequently constrains the investigation artifact's method, evidence threshold, conclusion strength, confidence, negative authority, and lawful stop condition, but it does not select public Question Families or runtime behavior.

A Question Family is selected into public answerability by explicit implementation registration in Question Surface Inventory and bounded ask metadata. Exact operator invocation can then select among already registered Question Families and, when eligible, select an existing dispatch surface. It subsequently constrains bounded ask eligibility, required parameters, diagnostic-only or not-dispatchable status, explanation content, and refusal behavior, but it does not select constitutional warrant authority.

Scout investigation complete.
