## Governance boundaries

### Directional relationships

- **Question Grammar → Constitutional Process:** directional. Admission supplies the Lawful Question boundary consumed by the process; Process does not retroactively create question admission authority.
- **Question Grammar → Orientation/Recovery inside Process:** directional where reviewed Process evidence says Orientation receives an admitted inquiry question and Recovery is downstream of admission and orientation.
- **Relationship Grammar → this governance classification:** directional. The investigation may use recovered connective relationships; it may not invent new governance relationships.
- **External Grammar → later evidence use:** directional. External representation must be translated and bounded before later constitutional work may consume it.
- **Constitutional Process → lawful movement/stop:** directional. Process-level recurrence constrains movement from pressure toward stop, without proving a universal runtime sequence.
- **Constitutional Fidelity → lawful realization:** directional. Fidelity tests preservation of authority during realization; it does not create the authority it preserves.

### Reciprocal relationships

- **Question Grammar and Relationship Grammar:** reciprocal only at the governance boundary. Question Grammar needs relationship/dependency evidence to admit relation questions; Relationship Grammar needs bounded admitted participants/endpoints before relationship testing is lawful. The authorities remain independent.
- **Relationship Grammar and Process:** reciprocal in bounded use. Process consumes relationships for cross-examination, completion, refusal, and non-collapse; Relationship Grammar consumes process-produced or process-bounded artifacts as relationship endpoints. Neither owns the other.
- **External Grammar and Relationship Grammar:** reciprocal in bounded evidence families. External Grammar can emit relationship facts from source or documentation metadata; Relationship Grammar governs how those facts may be consumed, preserved, limited, or refused. Neither becomes a common owner.
- **Process and Fidelity:** reciprocal only as preservation pressure. Process supplies recovered stage authority and readiness boundaries; Fidelity tests whether realization preserves those boundaries. Fidelity does not authorize Process, and Process does not create Fidelity's preservation criterion.
