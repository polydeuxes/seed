   - Preserved boundary: readiness is constitutional status, not behavior.

5. **Relationship / bridge road**
   - Lawful sender: bridge, relationship, and topology investigations.
   - Lawful receiver: readiness topology.
   - Artifact crossing: sender / receiver / artifact / warrant / non-crossing boundary discipline.
   - Warrant crossing: observational topology only.
   - Does not cross: invented endpoints, first-class bridge identity, ownership, or implementation-local responsibility.
   - Preserved boundary: roads are recovered only where recurring evidence supports them.

## Competing hypothesis comparison

### Hypothesis A: readiness emerges from already recovered constitutional neighborhoods

Recurring evidence supports much of Hypothesis A:

- Time neighborhoods and higher-order Time groupings explain the necessary temporal non-collapse and support / claim boundaries.
- Constitutional warrant explains lawful reliance.
- Promotion authority and admission evidence explain family-local movement rather than universal promotion.
- Current lawful condition and Null readiness explain present status and lawful stop.
- Relationship and bridge investigations explain sender / receiver / artifact / warrant discipline.

Hypothesis A survives strongly as a constitutional topology explanation: readiness behaves like an intersection of already recovered constitutional neighborhoods.

However, Hypothesis A does not fully eliminate the preserved gap, because `time_constitutional_readiness_characterization.md` explicitly preserved a missing bounded constitutional readiness responsibility: the pre-work determination and preservation that a specific Time-related movement has a current lawful condition under constitutional warrant.

### Hypothesis B: readiness requires an independent implementation-local responsibility

Recurring evidence does not support Hypothesis B as an existing requirement.

The repository preserves that no implementation-local owner has been recovered, no Time neighborhood owns the pre-work readiness decision, and no Town Clock implementation has been recovered. The conditional implementation topology can be described only as roads a hypothetical responsibility would have to consume and produce. Those roads do not prove the responsibility exists.

Hypothesis B therefore does not survive as a required implementation-local explanation. It remains a preserved possibility only because the constitutional readiness gap has not been satisfied by a recovered owner or implementation-local surface.

### Pressure test

The statement:

```text
Readiness is fully explained by already recovered constitutional neighborhoods.
```

is too strong if "fully explained" means the repository has already recovered a satisfying readiness responsibility. The repository still preserves a bounded constitutional readiness gap.

The statement:

```text
Readiness cannot be explained without recovering an independent implementation-local responsibility.
```

is also too strong. Recurring evidence explains the topology of readiness through already recovered constitutional neighborhoods and does not require an implementation-local owner.

Therefore neither hypothesis eliminates the other completely. The repository supports constitutional emergence as the stronger current behavior while preserving the implementation-local possibility as unresolved and unsupported.
