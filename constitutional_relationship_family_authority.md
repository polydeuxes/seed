## Stage 1 — Independent Interrogations

### Relationship A — `authorizes`

No comparison is made in this testimony.

#### Constitutional purpose

`authorizes` names a bounded constitutional permission relation: repository-visible evidence can make a specific movement, use, or conclusion lawful when the competent local boundary is able to interpret that evidence for the relevant constitutional purpose.

Its purpose is not to create general permission. Its purpose is to prevent movement from occurring merely because content is visible, named, repeated, desired, projected, or attractive.

#### Constitutional movement

The movement is from unsupported possibility toward a lawful next status or lawful downstream use, but only where the evidence remains attached to the role, subject, scope, boundary, negative limits, unknowns, and confidence that made the movement supportable.

The movement is local and bounded. It is not automatic transition, global promotion, mutation, execution, implementation readiness, or truth creation.

#### Constitutional authority

The authority comes from repository-visible evidence interpreted through a competent local constitutional boundary. Recovered authority evidence includes preserved evidence, tests, diagnostics, observations, compatibility behavior, reports, handoff artifacts, and family-specific support where those materials are competent for the bounded question.

The relation therefore carries a source-and-boundary requirement: evidence alone is insufficient unless a competent boundary can lawfully interpret it for the move at issue.

#### Constitutional boundaries

The boundaries are:

- local constitutional competence;
- explicit evidence requirement;
- bounded subject and role;
- preserved support/provenance;
- preservation of negative limits;
- preservation of unknowns;
- confidence calibration;
- no automatic expansion from candidate, pressure, vocabulary, visibility, or presentation.

#### Constitutional preservation

What must be preserved is the evidence that made the lawful movement possible, the local authority boundary, the reason for the permitted movement, the role/scope of the permitted movement, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, and the stop condition when evidence ends.

#### Negative authority

This relation does not grant truth, ownership, implementation recovery, runtime architecture, mutation authority, planning authority, universal promotion, or generalized permission. It does not make operator concern, repeated language, projection output, diagnostics, presentation labels, or architectural preference sufficient by themselves.

#### Lawful stop

Stop when evidence is absent, local competence is absent, scope is unclear, negative authority would be erased, unknowns would be resolved by implication, confidence would be overstated, or the requested movement would exceed the boundary that made the permission lawful.

#### Confidence

Confidence is **high** that repository evidence supports `authorizes` as a bounded evidence-and-boundary permission relation. Confidence is **low** for any claim that it is canonical implementation vocabulary, a universal relation, or a mechanism.

### Relationship B — `admits`

No comparison is made in this testimony.

#### Constitutional purpose

`admits` names a bounded constitutional entry relation: a candidate, observation, pressure, evidence item, output, view, recordable result, or recovery may enter a narrower lawful status only through the gate competent for that status.

Its purpose is to distinguish being present or visible from being lawfully allowed into a specified next constitutional position.

#### Constitutional movement

The movement is entry into a bounded status, role, view, inquiry condition, record scope, projected surface, support condition, or stronger candidate condition. The movement remains constrained by the gate's evidence, authority, eligibility, compatibility, test, projection, record-scope, and stop requirements.

Entry may also stop, leaving material as orientation, unknown, unsupported, candidate, read-only view, or insufficient evidence.

#### Constitutional authority

The authority comes from the competent local gate and its required evidence. Repository evidence shows admission-like gates for orientation, bounded inquiry, evidence, support/candidate status, family-local stronger status, projection, presentation/read-view, record/audit scope, frontier stop, implementation-readiness, smallest-slice recovery, and methodology discipline.

The gate is competent only for its local role.

#### Constitutional boundaries

The boundaries are:

- gate-local competence;
- eligibility for the target status;
- required arguments or identity where applicable;
- evidence/provenance/confidence/source-role requirements;
- compatibility or test requirements where applicable;
- read-only and non-truth limits for views;
- explicit record/audit scope for recordable results;
- preservation of unknown or stop where entry is not lawful.

#### Constitutional preservation

What must be preserved is the admitted status, the status boundary, the evidence and reason for entry, the gate's scope, the source/provenance where required, confidence, read-only or non-truth limits where applicable, and the fact that non-entry remains lawful when conditions are not met.

#### Negative authority

This relation does not make presence into truth, candidate status into verification, view into mutation, recordable output into cluster truth, orientation into implementation, or vocabulary into preserved knowledge. It does not imply universal admission or a shared owner of all gates.

#### Lawful stop

Stop when the local gate cannot identify the target, evidence is missing, eligibility is absent, provenance/confidence/source role is insufficient, compatibility or tests are missing where required, record scope is absent for recording, or admission would promote beyond the gate's competence.

#### Confidence

### Relationship C — `relies`

No comparison is made in this testimony.

#### Constitutional purpose

`relies` names a bounded constitutional consumption relation: a downstream surface may use warranted content only within the preserved authority boundary, role, evidence, unknowns, negative limits, and confidence that make the use lawful.

Its purpose is to keep downstream use from becoming unsupported truth, promotion, generalization, mutation, or stronger commitment.

#### Constitutional movement

The movement is downstream use of warranted content by a bounded consumer. The content is not strengthened by being used. The use remains inside the preserved warrant, role, scope, authority boundary, unknowns, and confidence.

When those limits do not survive, the movement weakens or stops.

#### Constitutional authority

The authority comes from warrant: evidence-bound lawful reliance under preserved authority boundary. The downstream consumer does not create the entitlement; it consumes what the preserved warrant permits.

The authority is therefore carried by preserved evidence/support, provenance, role, authority limit, negative authority, unknowns, and confidence.

#### Constitutional boundaries

The boundaries are:

- warranted content only;
- preserved authority boundary;
- bounded consumer role;
- bounded subject and scope;
- evidence/support and provenance continuity;
- negative authority preservation;
- unknown preservation;
- confidence preservation;
- refusal of stronger use when warrant is absent, ambiguous, outside-authority, or overextended.

#### Constitutional preservation

What must be preserved is the entitlement and limit being consumed: evidence, support/provenance, role, authority boundary, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, and any stop/non-promotion limits.

#### Negative authority

This relation is not truth, ownership, implementation readiness, promotion authority, mutation authority, planning authority, trust framework, dependency system, universal mechanism, or permission to generalize beyond evidence.

#### Lawful stop

Stop when warrant is absent, evidence or provenance is lost, authority boundary is missing, the consumer role exceeds the support, unknowns would be erased, negative authority would be ignored, confidence would be overstated, or downstream use would become a stronger claim than the preserved warrant permits.

#### Confidence

Confidence is **high** that repository evidence supports `relies` as bounded lawful downstream consumption of warranted content. Confidence is **low** for any claim that it is a runtime object, universal owner, canonical schema, or implementation mechanism.

