## Comparison after independent inspection

Only after inspecting the recurring postures independently, the following shared constitutional properties are supported.

## Recurring constitutional properties

1. **Bounded subject.** A recurring investigation viewpoint has a subject that repository evidence can bound. The subject may be a question, responsibility, boundary, relation, pressure, readiness condition, unknown, or completion state, but it cannot be only an operator label.
2. **Question-shaped identity.** The viewpoint is recognizable by the recurring question it asks, not by a canonical name.
3. **Evidence binding.** Claims must remain tied to reviewed repository evidence. Recurrence, vocabulary, app-visible structure, public output, or operator usefulness is not enough by itself.
4. **Visibility recovery.** Each viewpoint recovers a specific kind of visibility: recurrence, boundary, grammar, evidence/classification, readiness, relation, coexistence, completion, or constitutional identity.
5. **Authority separation.** Each viewpoint distinguishes what evidence permits from what it merely makes visible.
6. **Negative authority.** Each viewpoint states what it is not allowed to become.
7. **Unknown preservation.** Each viewpoint leaves unsupported conclusions visible rather than resolving them by analogy, compression, recurrence, or elegance.
8. **Neighbor discipline.** Each viewpoint recognizes adjacent viewpoints without collapsing into them.
9. **Stop discipline.** Each viewpoint can end with answer, unknown, insufficient evidence, unsupported conclusion, no-pressure, residual, adjacent pressure, or boundary-preserving stop.
10. **Confidence calibration.** Each viewpoint states the strength and boundary of the claim rather than treating characterization as complete recovery.

## Recurring investigation responsibilities

A recurring investigation viewpoint constitutionally owns one local investigative responsibility:

- recognize recurrence without ontology promotion;
- reduce broad concern to bounded question;
- recover grammar without imposing framework;
- interrogate competency without granting unsupported authority;
- assess readiness without implementing;
- classify pressure without authorizing recovery;
- identify relation without collapse;
- reconcile distinctions without universal resolver;

- characterize constitutional property without taxonomy or implementation.

These responsibilities recur as bounded constitutional work, not as a mandatory sequence.
