### Recurring evaluation patterns

Recurring evidence shows prerequisite evaluation repeatedly occurs before lawful movement. The recurring pattern is not one owner. It is a repeated discipline:

1. identify a bounded prerequisite artifact;
2. evaluate whether that artifact is sufficient for the local office's movement;
3. consume neighboring evidence only within the receiving office's boundary;
4. refuse stronger responsibilities not authorized by the evidence;
5. preserve unknowns and confidence limits.

This pattern recurs across Scout, Survey, Characterization, Survey Warrant, Question Family, Selection, Bridge Inspection, and Competency Interrogation.

### Recurring local evaluation

Local evaluation recurs strongly:

- Scout evaluates bounded reconnaissance sufficiency.
- Survey evaluates scoped recurrence and neighborhood-resolution sufficiency.
- Characterization evaluates comparative-distinction readiness.
- Survey Warrant evaluates recurrence across prior artifacts.
- Question Family evaluates exact public answerability and bounded ask eligibility through implementation-backed local metadata.
- Selection evaluates candidates, criteria, target, alternatives, and outcome inside bounded selection surfaces or operator-supplied tasks.

- Competency Interrogation evaluates interrogability and boundary sufficiency for a selected competency.

### Recurring consumed evaluation

Consumed evaluation also recurs, but as bounded input rather than transferred ownership:

- Survey consumes Scout findings or warrant evidence.
- Characterization consumes surveyed or investigated evidence.
- Survey Warrant consumes prior artifacts.
- Question Family consumes registration, diagnostic, answer-surface, and bounded ask metadata.
- Selection consumes candidate and pressure artifacts.
- Bridge Inspection consumes independently produced source-side and consumer-side evidence.
- Competency Interrogation consumes reports, commands, diagnostics, tests, app visibility, and adjacent artifacts.

Consumption does not collapse the consuming office into the producing office and does not prove a shared evaluator.

### Recurring independent evaluation

Independent evaluation recurs where the same words appear differently in neighboring offices. Bounded question, evidence, warrant, artifact, boundary, visibility, and unknown-preservation recur, but each office asks whether those prerequisites are sufficient for its own lawful movement.

The strongest repository pattern is therefore independent local evaluation under shared constitutional discipline, not one recurring constitutional office performing all prerequisite evaluation.
