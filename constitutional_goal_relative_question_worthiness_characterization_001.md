# Constitutional Goal-Relative Question-Worthiness Characterization 001

Repository authority wins.

## Boundary and stopping rule

This characterization investigates only this transition:

```text
visible goal-relative condition
→ question-worthiness standing
```

It stops before candidate-question formation. It does not generate question text, determine formation cardinality, bind or establish a question, admit a question to a frontier, select a question, examine a question, authorize work, schedule work, or create a question judge/producer/generator/selector/frontier/planner/scheduler/queue.

The controlling distinction recovered from implementation is:

```text
visible unresolved pressure
!= information deficit
!= question-worthy condition
!= candidate-question material
```

Current implementation preserves many local pressures, Unknowns, conflicts, insufficiencies, exclusions, refusals, and stop boundaries. It does not implement a general goal-relative question-worthiness judge. Therefore this report records supported local distinctions and the missing realization required before any condition could lawfully proceed to candidate-question formation.

## Core answer

A goal-relative condition may lawfully become candidate-question material only after a separate local judgment establishes more than unresolvedness. Repository evidence supports, at most, permission for a future question-worthiness standing when the condition is exactly bounded to a current goal/horizon or equivalent local position, remains unresolved after fulfilled work and available evidence are accounted for, cannot be resolved by already admitted non-question movement, is an information deficit rather than an authority/action/binding/execution/capability deficit, lies within remaining authority, has bounded evidence territory that may lawfully be sought, is not stopped, refused, duplicated, or already resolved, and preserves Unknowns/conflicts without silently promoting them.

No implementation-backed constitutional rule was found that requires question formation from any goal-relative condition. The strongest implemented rule is negative: many visible conditions must remain pressure, Unknown, refusal, provisional standing, sufficiency result, authority boundary, or stop rather than becoming question material.

## Source-condition standings

### 1. Established or known scope

- **Consumed standing:** A bounded goal establishment may contain `known_scope`, `establishment_state`, `intended_outcome`, and ingress lineage.
- **Deficit or pressure kind:** No deficit by itself. Known scope is orientation or already bounded material.
- **Available non-question movement:** Continue reversible work inside the established/provisional orientation; inspect the preserved ingress lineage; use the goal as bounded context where a downstream local consumer accepts it.
- **Forbidden movement:** Treating known scope as unresolved, opening inquiry, authorizing work, executing, recording, or judging satisfaction merely because a goal exists.
- **Requires new information:** No, not by itself.
- **Can existing evidence resolve it:** Often yes; the known scope is itself the resolved orientation evidence.
- **Worthiness standing:** `no question warranted` or `condition already resolved`, locally supported by goal establishment.
- **Evidence and authority:** Exact ingress artifact, establishment state, known scope, sufficiency and stop fields, and negative-effect flags.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Bounded goal establishment explicitly records that goal establishment is not inquiry opening, resource observation, constraint satisfaction, work authorization, or goal satisfaction.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported and implemented locally for goal establishment; unrealized as a general question-worthiness outcome.

### 2. Unresolved scope or unsupported residual material

- **Consumed standing:** `unresolved_scope` entries from closed-choice, expression interpretation, authority/scope, or admitted-interpretation ingress.
- **Deficit or pressure kind:** Visible unresolved pressure. It may be unsupported selection evidence, unresolved references, excluded scope, applicable-but-unadmitted material, or residual spans.
- **Available non-question movement:** Inspect existing ingress, source snapshots, residual spans, admission evidence, or authority/scope binding; preserve unresolved material; refuse goal establishment where the local owner cannot support orientation.
- **Forbidden movement:** Inferring question-worthiness from the field name `unresolved_scope` or from unresolvedness alone.
- **Requires new information:** Sometimes, but not necessarily. The unresolved condition may instead require binding, admission, authority, comparison, or refusal.
- **Can existing evidence resolve it:** Yes where already available evidence has not been inspected or where a local binding/admission evaluation remains pending.
- **Worthiness standing:** Usually `question formation unsupported`; possibly `question formation premature` if local prerequisites are missing; not implemented as permitted material.
- **Evidence and authority:** Exact source condition, owner family, unresolved reason, available evidence territory, authority boundary, and proof that no admitted non-question movement can resolve it.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Authority/scope binding can preserve unresolved scope and also state that it does not produce a bounded constitutional question.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported as pressure and implemented locally; question-worthiness unrealized.

### 3. Absent advancement need

- **Consumed standing:** A goal advancement need set can record a family as `absent` when no projection is supplied and no exclusion applies.
- **Deficit or pressure kind:** Absence of supplied native need evidence, not an information request.
- **Available non-question movement:** Preserve absence; evaluate sufficiency against coverage; remain Unknown if sufficiency cannot be established.
- **Forbidden movement:** Treating absence as a generated question, priority, or next action.
- **Requires new information:** Not necessarily; the lawful result may be Unknown or sufficient/insufficient for now depending on other coverage.
- **Can existing evidence resolve it:** Yes where coverage and supplied need projections establish sufficient-for-now or insufficient-for-now.
- **Worthiness standing:** `no question warranted` or `question-worthiness Unknown` unless a separate rule proves an information deficit.
- **Evidence and authority:** Selected goal, horizon, family assembly records, native projection identity, and coverage set.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Sufficiency projection says coverage gaps may prevent sufficiency but are not promoted into native advancement needs.
- **Classification:** Implemented locally as need/coverage standing; question-worthiness unsupported.

### 4. Insufficient-for-now standing

- **Consumed standing:** `GoalAdvancementSufficiencyProjection.conclusion == "insufficient_for_now"`.
- **Deficit or pressure kind:** Advancement sufficiency deficit for the present horizon.
- **Available non-question movement:** Preserve unordered reasons; inspect family reasons and coverage; continue only within lawful non-authorizing boundaries.
- **Forbidden movement:** Route selection, next-action selection, inquiry opening, authority request, realization selection, work authorization, execution, recording, event-ledger writing, or cluster mutation.
- **Requires new information:** Sometimes, but an insufficiency may arise from unresolved native need, material conflict, or coverage gap rather than a bounded information deficit.
- **Can existing evidence resolve it:** Existing family/coverage evidence can establish the insufficiency without a question.
- **Worthiness standing:** `question formation unsupported` unless a later local judgment identifies a bounded information deficit; not required.
- **Evidence and authority:** Need set, coverage set, horizon, family reasons, and explicit non-selection/non-authorization flags.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** The projection preserves reasons as unordered and not as priority, route, next action, or authorization.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported and implemented locally; question-worthiness unrealized.

### 5. Unknown current state

- **Consumed standing:** Preserved `unknowns` in goal establishment, authority/scope binding, need assembly, sufficiency, or caller-supplied question testimony.
- **Deficit or pressure kind:** Unknown preservation. Unknown is not automatically an information deficit suitable for question formation.
- **Available non-question movement:** Preserve Unknown, refuse local establishment/admission where required evidence is absent, or remain Unknown in sufficiency projection.
- **Forbidden movement:** Promoting Unknown into fact, authority, native need, question material, or cluster truth.
- **Requires new information:** Often, but the repository does not prove that the new information must be sought through a question.
- **Can existing evidence resolve it:** Sometimes by inspecting already preserved lineage or by completing pending binding/evaluation.
- **Worthiness standing:** `question-worthiness Unknown` or `question formation premature` depending on the local owner.
- **Evidence and authority:** Exact Unknown source, affected local standing, current owner, available evidence, and stop/authority boundary.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Bounded constitutional question production preserves caller unknowns only as testimony and explicitly avoids fact, authority, view-selection, event-ledger, and mutation effects.
- **Classification:** Unknown preservation implemented locally; general question-worthiness Unknown is constitutionally supported but unrealized.

### 6. Conflicting evidence

- **Consumed standing:** Preserved `conflicts`, identity conflicts, conflicting scope expressions, or material conflict reasons.
- **Deficit or pressure kind:** Conflict requiring comparison, reconciliation, refusal, or changed request; not an information deficit by default.
- **Available non-question movement:** Compare conflicting evidence where a comparison owner exists; refuse establishment or binding; preserve conflict; stop or require changed request.
- **Forbidden movement:** Treating conflict as a question simply because it is unresolved.
- **Requires new information:** Not necessarily; the next lawful movement may be comparison of existing evidence.
- **Can existing evidence resolve it:** Yes if comparison or local conflict evaluation can decide support/refusal from present evidence.
- **Worthiness standing:** `question formation premature`, `unsupported`, or `blocked` until conflict handling is complete.
- **Evidence and authority:** Conflict source, exact identities/scopes, local comparison/evaluation authority, and conflict result.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Authority/scope explanation says an authority/scope or stated-constraint conflict may require the request or constraint to change before reconsideration.
- **Classification:** Conflict handling implemented locally; question-worthiness unsupported without additional judgment.

### 7. Unavailable, stale, excluded, or missing evidence

- **Consumed standing:** Missing prerequisites, unavailable authority, excluded scope, absent family projections, coverage gaps, or omitted caller/testimony fields.
- **Deficit or pressure kind:** Evidence availability or admissibility boundary. It may be missing evidence, excluded evidence, missing authority, missing capability, or merely uninspected available evidence.
- **Available non-question movement:** Inspect available evidence; preserve exclusion; refuse use; remain Unknown; wait for prerequisite; or stop.
- **Forbidden movement:** Treating all missing/unavailable evidence as a lawful question target.
- **Requires new information:** Only if the evidence truly does not exist in the current lawful record and may lawfully be sought.
- **Can existing evidence resolve it:** Yes when the issue is unexamined evidence rather than information absence.
- **Worthiness standing:** Usually `question formation premature` or `unsupported`; possible future `question formation permitted` only if a bounded, authorized information deficit remains after evidence inspection.
- **Evidence and authority:** Evidence inventory, exclusion reason, prerequisite status, authority to seek evidence, and stop boundary.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Advancement sufficiency refuses to promote coverage gaps into native needs, showing that missing coverage is not itself a generated information request.
- **Classification:** Implemented locally as absence/exclusion/coverage; positive worthiness unrealized.

### 8. Blocked, deferred, failed, or refused movement

- **Consumed standing:** Local states such as `blocked`, `refused`, failed prerequisite records, deferred work, or ineligible work.
- **Deficit or pressure kind:** Movement deficit, not necessarily information deficit.
- **Available non-question movement:** Preserve refusal reason; await prerequisite; repair authority/scope; change request; keep inactive; or stop.
- **Forbidden movement:** Using refusal or blocked movement as automatic inquiry generation.
- **Requires new information:** Sometimes no; the required change may be authority, capability, execution success, or request revision.
- **Can existing evidence resolve it:** Sometimes by completing pending binding, satisfying prerequisite, or recognizing duplicate/fulfilled work.
- **Worthiness standing:** `question formation blocked by authority`, `premature`, `unsupported`, or `no question warranted` depending on local reason.
- **Evidence and authority:** Source state, refusal reason, missing boundary, resolvability, preserved Unknowns/conflicts, and prohibited downstream movement.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Minimum lawful advancement explanation can identify authority/scope boundaries while prohibiting bounded-question origination, realization selection, and authorization/execution.
- **Classification:** Implemented locally; general worthiness not implemented.

### 9. Unmet dependency or prerequisite

- **Consumed standing:** Required admission evidence, dispatch prerequisites, exact consumer/purpose matching, identity/handoff consistency, or required authority class.
- **Deficit or pressure kind:** Prerequisite deficit. It may require a prior binding/evaluation or external authority, not new question material.
- **Available non-question movement:** Complete the prerequisite, refuse the mismatch, await required evidence, or preserve Unknown.
- **Forbidden movement:** Skipping the prerequisite by forming a question.
- **Requires new information:** Not always; identity mismatch can be decided from existing refs.
- **Can existing evidence resolve it:** Yes when exact refs prove mismatch or satisfaction.
- **Worthiness standing:** `question formation premature` or `blocked by authority`.
- **Evidence and authority:** Required consumer/purpose, handoff identity, admission evidence, prerequisite list, and source lineage.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Bounded goal establishment from admitted interpretation refuses admissions for another consumer or purpose without revising selection.
- **Classification:** Implemented locally; question-worthiness unrealized.

### 10. Authority or scope exclusion

- **Consumed standing:** Excluded scope refs, prohibited scope, missing grant, blocked requested activity, operator constraints, or stop conditions.
- **Deficit or pressure kind:** Authority deficit or negative authority.
- **Available non-question movement:** Refuse movement, preserve excluded scope, explain minimum lawful advancement, seek exact authority through an authorized road if one exists, or stop.
- **Forbidden movement:** Treating authority-bearing language as authority grant; treating question formation as a bypass for negative authority.
- **Requires new information:** Sometimes authority evidence; sometimes an actual authority grant or changed request, which is not merely information.
- **Can existing evidence resolve it:** Yes where current authority context proves excluded scope or missing grant.
- **Worthiness standing:** `question formation blocked by authority` or `question formation premature`.
- **Evidence and authority:** Operator identity, workspace/session authority, scope binding, requested activity class, required authority class, excluded/permitted scopes, restrictions, and stop conditions.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Authority/scope binding states that authority-bearing language is not itself an authority grant and that the artifact does not produce a bounded constitutional question.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported and implemented locally; question-worthiness blocked only by negative authority, not by a positive judge.

### 11. Unsatisfied sufficiency condition

- **Consumed standing:** Provisional or unsupported sufficiency state in bounded goal establishment or advancement sufficiency.
- **Deficit or pressure kind:** Sufficiency judgment deficit.
- **Available non-question movement:** Preserve provisional standing; evaluate supplied need/coverage; continue reversible work if local rules allow; stop where sufficiency is required.
- **Forbidden movement:** Assuming provisionality requires question formation.
- **Requires new information:** Not necessarily; sufficiency may be resolved by existing coverage or local rule.
- **Can existing evidence resolve it:** Yes where need/coverage evidence is complete.
- **Worthiness standing:** `question formation unsupported` or `question-worthiness Unknown` unless a later local rule proves bounded information deficit.
- **Evidence and authority:** Sufficiency conditions, sufficiency state/conclusion, family reasons, local continuation boundary.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** A provisional bounded goal may be enough orientation for reversible continuation without perfect goal resolution.
- **Classification:** Implemented locally; positive worthiness unrealized.

### 12. Explicit stop condition

- **Consumed standing:** `stop_conditions`, boundary notes, prohibited downstream movement, read-only/no-ledger/no-mutation flags, or local stop/refusal results.
- **Deficit or pressure kind:** Stop boundary, not information deficit.
- **Available non-question movement:** Stop, remain inactive, preserve state, refuse downstream use, or render/report read-only evidence.
- **Forbidden movement:** Inquiry opening, question formation, execution, recording, mutation, or any downstream movement named by the stop.
- **Requires new information:** No, unless a later lawful transition changes the stop condition.
- **Can existing evidence resolve it:** Yes; the stop condition itself controls current movement.
- **Worthiness standing:** `question formation blocked by authority` or `no question warranted`.
- **Evidence and authority:** Stop condition text, source artifact, boundary flags, and local negative-effect fields.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Goal establishment preserves stop conditions while also keeping `inquiry_opened`, `work_authorized`, `execution_started`, `recording_started`, and `satisfaction_judged` false.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported and implemented locally.

### 13. Potentially duplicated pressure or already fulfilled work

- **Consumed standing:** Known scope, selected refs, admitted snapshots, already examined/answered testimony, duplicate identities, or overlapping unresolved entries.
- **Deficit or pressure kind:** Identity/overlap/fulfillment standing. It is not a new information deficit until duplicate/fulfilled status is rejected by evidence.
- **Available non-question movement:** Compare identity and overlap; recognize fulfillment; preserve duplicate pressure; refuse duplicate movement; or remain Unknown if no duplicate evaluator exists.
- **Forbidden movement:** Generating a new question where evidence already resolves or duplicates the pressure.
- **Requires new information:** Usually no if identity/lineage evidence is already available; sometimes Unknown because general duplicate/answered-question evaluation is not implemented.
- **Can existing evidence resolve it:** Locally yes for stable ids and exact refs; generally Unknown for semantic duplicate/answered status.
- **Worthiness standing:** `condition already resolved`, `condition duplicates existing work`, or `question-worthiness Unknown`.
- **Evidence and authority:** Exact identity, lineage, fulfilled work evidence, admitted snapshots, and overlap comparison authority.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** The bounded question constructor can create one artifact from caller fields but does not compare it to current goal conditions or retire duplicates.
- **Classification:** Identity binding implemented locally; general duplicate/answered question-worthiness unrealized.

## Positive question-worthiness standing

The repository does not implement a positive goal-relative question-worthiness judgment. A future local implementation would need repository-backed evidence for at least these properties before a condition could be treated as lawful candidate-question material:

1. The condition belongs to an exact bounded goal, horizon, or equivalent current local position.
2. The condition remains unresolved after known scope, fulfilled work, duplicate pressure, and already available evidence are accounted for.
3. No already admitted non-question movement can resolve it directly.
4. The deficit is informational rather than a missing authority grant, missing capability, incomplete binding, pending execution, unresolved comparison, failed prerequisite, or stop condition.
5. The missing information can be bounded without generating question text in this characterization step.
6. Relevant evidence territory exists or may lawfully be sought.
7. Questioning lies within remaining authority and outside excluded scope.
8. No stop condition, refusal reason, or negative authority forbids inquiry.
9. The condition is not already answered, fulfilled, or duplicated.
10. Preserving the condition without formation would prevent lawful advancement, if and only if an implementation-backed local rule establishes that consequence.

These are not asserted as a universal mandatory checklist. They are the minimum families of evidence implied by the repository's existing negative boundaries. The missing realization is the local act that would consume those families and emit a worthiness standing without forming a candidate question.

## Non-question roads recovered

The repository gives concrete counterexamples to questioning as the default response:

- **Information deficit != evidence already available but unexamined.** Existing ingress lineage, snapshots, authority contexts, and coverage records may be inspected or evaluated without asking.
- **Information deficit != missing authority.** A missing activity grant or excluded scope requires authority/scope handling, refusal, exact grant, or changed request, not question formation.
- **Information deficit != missing capability.** A missing capability or realization is not supplied by a question; capability reachability and operational realization have their own warrant/selection boundaries.
- **Information deficit != incomplete binding.** Unresolved scope, identity mismatch, or consumer/purpose mismatch must be bound or refused before question-worthiness can be assessed.
- **Information deficit != pending execution.** Failed or pending work may require execution movement, repair, or stop, not inquiry.
- **Information deficit != conflict requiring comparison.** Conflicts may require comparison or request change before any question standing is lawful.
- **Information deficit != lawful stop.** Stop conditions and prohibited downstream movement defeat formation.
- **Information deficit != mere uncertainty preservation.** Unknown can remain Unknown without being turned into a question.

Therefore question-worthiness, if implemented, would depend on the constitutional kind of deficit rather than unresolvedness alone.

## Participating acts

Question-worthiness is not implemented as one standalone act and is not proven to have one universal owner. Repository evidence instead shows local acts that would have to participate in any lawful judgment:

- **Recognition / observation:** makes a visible condition available without promoting it.
- **Evaluation / sufficiency judgment:** can decide sufficient, insufficient, unsupported, conflicting, or Unknown standings.
- **Comparison:** distinguishes conflict, duplicate, overlap, fulfilled, or already answered conditions where local comparison authority exists.
- **Binding:** fixes exact goal, horizon, scope, choice, source, consumer, purpose, and identity before a condition can be assessed.
- **Warrant / reliance:** controls whether evidence may be consumed for the worthiness purpose.
- **Admission / refusal:** admits a local handoff or refuses mismatched/unsupported material before later movement.
- **Termination / stop:** blocks formation where authority, stop conditions, or negative movement boundaries control.
- **Unknown preservation:** prevents pressure from becoming question material when evidence is incomplete.

The best-supported characterization is that question-worthiness is currently an unrealized composition/effect that would be produced inside local responsibility families, not a repository-wide universal act.

## Unsupported assumptions

Repository evidence does not support these assumptions:

- Every unresolved goal-relative condition warrants a question.
- Every Unknown warrants a question.
- Every insufficiency or absent need warrants a question.
- Every conflict warrants a question rather than comparison/refusal/changed request.
- Every missing evidence condition is an information deficit.
- Question formation is ever mandatory as a general constitutional rule.
- Field names such as `unknown`, `unresolved`, `open_question`, `inquiry`, `need`, or `insufficient` establish question-worthiness.
- A caller-supplied bounded question proves that a live goal-relative condition was question-worthy.
- Frontier membership, selection, examination, execution, recording, or mutation can be inferred from question-shaped pressure.

## Exact stopping boundary

This characterization ends at question-worthiness standing. It does not form candidate questions, choose zero/one/many candidates, establish bounded questions, admit a frontier, select movement, examine, authorize, execute, record, mutate, or retire questions.

Goal-relative question-worthiness characterization 001 complete.
