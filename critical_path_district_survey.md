# Critical Path District Survey

## Scope

This survey answers one bounded question:

```text
After the completed
constitutional inquiry work,
which unresolved district
now has the strongest
repository-supported pressure?
```

It does not recommend implementation, slices, campaigns, architecture, future framework design, or operator preference. It surveys only recurring repository evidence from completed investigations, audits, bridge inspections, neighborhood surveys, district surveys, family completion audits, typed unknown preservation work, hit lists, and architectural recovery records.

Repository authority wins.

This survey treats prior markdown investigations as repository evidence only where they recur, preserve boundaries, cite implementation-backed surfaces, or explicitly stop at unsupported conclusions. It does not treat attractive vocabulary as repository knowledge by itself.

## Candidate district survey

### Candidate 1: Competency Self-Governance

#### Identity

```text
What recurring district
is becoming visible?
```

A district around **Competency Self-Governance** is becoming visible: the constitutional capacity to ask whether a named capability, inquiry, or bounded movement has sufficient identity, evidence, authority, jurisdiction, negative authority, unknown preservation, and lawful stop conditions to proceed.

The recurring question is not “what should Seed do next?” It is:

```text
May this bounded capability lawfully continue?
```

#### Repository Pressure

```text
What recurring repository evidence
creates pressure
toward this district?
```

Repository pressure is strong and recurring because many completed investigations now end at the same constitutional boundary:

- Competency interrogation methodology repeatedly asks for identity, inputs, boundaries, authority, negative authority, unknowns, reliance, termination, and confidence.
- Constitutional self-governance investigation directly names recurring pressure around deciding whether a bounded capability has identity, evidence, jurisdiction, authority, refusal boundaries, unknown preservation, and lawful stop conditions sufficient for movement.
- Constitutional inquiry state investigation recovers bounded constitutional exhaustion as the condition where every repository-supported lawful move has been consumed and further movement would cross evidence, authority, boundary, unknown, reliance, negative-authority, or confidence limits.
- Constitutional inquiry self-awareness investigation says the repository supports mixed constitutional self-awareness only through artifact-level, audit-level, methodology-level, and local implementation-level evidence, while rejecting a universal runtime self-awareness mechanism.
- Question eligibility / bounded ask evidence shows one local implementation-backed form of “may this exact family proceed?” through exact question-family admission, bounded status, required arguments, and refusal of unknown or diagnostic-only families.
- Responsibility evaluation evidence repeatedly classifies claims as supported, unsupported, ambiguous, incomplete, blocked, outside authority, or candidate, then stops before assignment, promotion, execution, mutation, or truth changes.
- Observation agreement and grammar observation repeatedly preserve candidate relation evidence without truth promotion or responsibility recovery.
- Pressure visibility repeatedly exposes pressure without command authority.
- Typed unknown preservation repeatedly preserves unsupported conclusions instead of converting them into cluster truth or architectural truth.

The pressure is constitutional rather than operational: after the repository has recovered inquiry state and inquiry self-awareness, the unresolved recurring pressure is whether Seed can evaluate the lawfulness of a bounded capability's continuation without becoming a planner, recovery engine, or self-observer framework.

- **Responsibility Evaluation / Responsibility Selection**, which supplies support/unsupported/blocked/candidate classifications and refusal to promote claims into action.

- **Constitutional Inquiry Self-Awareness**, which bounds self-awareness to artifact/audit/methodology/local implementation evidence and rejects runtime self-awareness overclaim.

#### Criticality

```text

```

Classification: **Critical Path**.

Support: this district carries the strongest repository-supported pressure because it is the unresolved crossing point shared by the completed constitutional inquiry work. The repository has already recovered bounded inquiry state and bounded inquiry self-awareness, but the remaining recurring question is whether a bounded capability is lawfully permitted to continue. Multiple recovered districts already provide local pieces of that question, while the repository repeatedly rejects promoting those pieces into a universal framework.

This is critical path not because implementation should occur, but because the repository's next constitutional question naturally asks about lawful continuation of bounded competencies after inquiry exhaustion and self-awareness have been characterized.

#### Blocking Relationship

```text

depends upon

```

Evidence-bound future work that depends on this district includes:

- any future use of competency interrogation as internal physiology rather than external Codex methodology;
- any future attempt to bind question eligibility, responsibility evaluation, authority boundaries, unknown preservation, and lawful stop into a single constitutional movement check;
- any future distinction between pressure visibility and authority to continue;
- any future treatment of inquiry self-awareness as more than artifact-level/audit-level/methodology-level/local implementation evidence.

These dependencies are not implementation recommendations. They are preserved blocking relationships visible in recurring repository evidence.

#### Unknowns

Unsupported conclusions preserved:

#### Confidence

**High** that this district has the strongest recurring repository pressure.

**Medium** that it should be named `Competency Self-Governance`, because the phrase is supported by a recent investigation but not necessarily stabilized as repository vocabulary.

**Low** that the district has implementation ownership or runtime form today.

## Synthesis

```text
What district

```

The city itself would survey **Competency Self-Governance** next.

This answer follows repository pressure rather than architectural preference:

1. Completed constitutional inquiry work has already characterized bounded constitutional exhaustion and mixed constitutional self-awareness.
2. Those conclusions repeatedly stop before claiming a runtime state machine, self-observer, lifecycle, planner, recovery engine, or generic framework.
3. The remaining unresolved pressure is nevertheless recurring and constitutional: whether a bounded capability, inquiry, or competency may lawfully continue given identity, evidence, authority, negative authority, unknown preservation, and lawful stop conditions.

5. Other candidates are either dependencies already surveyed, narrower bridges, future review frontiers, adjacent deferred boundaries, local preservation checkpoints, or unsupported framework temptations.

Therefore the strongest repository-supported pressure is not implementation, not a slice, not future architecture, and not the deferred Operator Visibility Tool / Seed Observation Organ bridge. It is the unresolved constitutional district of **Competency Self-Governance**: the district asking how Seed can know whether a bounded capability may lawfully continue without exceeding evidence, authority, unknown, boundary, confidence, or stop conditions.
