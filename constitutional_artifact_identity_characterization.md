# Constitutional Artifact Identity Characterization

## Scope and method

This document uses `identity` only as the prompt's working label. It does not introduce an Identity component, subsystem, registry, or philosophical identity theory. The review treats implementation-backed evidence as authority and treats prior characterization vocabulary as evidence only where it remains consistent with code, tests, app surfaces, and recorded repository boundaries.

The investigation used the app surfaces `seed --diagnostic-inventory`, `seed --diagnostic-shape-audit`, and `seed --question-surface-inventory` as current visibility checks, then compared those outputs with recurring repository evidence about transition families, artifact deltas, interpretation, promotion authority, implementation readiness, inquiry orientation, capability candidates, verification evidence, capability verification, diagnostic boundaries, and projection boundaries.

## Evidence reviewed

Recurring evidence came from these implementation-backed families:

- Transition family and artifact-delta characterizations: transitions are distinguished by what becomes visible, supported, admitted, projected, or authority-bearing, and by what explicitly remains unchanged.
- Inquiry orientation: operator prose can be preserved and rendered as read-only orientation while explicitly not becoming fact, claim, goal, requirement, ownership, intent, recommendation, command, authorization, plan, observation, event-ledger entry, or cluster truth.
- Question surface inventory and bounded ask behavior: possible questions become runnable only through exact implemented surfaces with eligibility, required-argument, dispatch, diagnostic-only, and non-dispatchable boundaries.
- Domain model and projection behavior: observations, immutable evidence, interpreted facts, support aggregates, current projected facts, and projection caches remain distinct participants.
- Capability candidate, verification evidence, capability verification, inventory, and promotion-readiness paths: package-derived candidates, acquired local support, verified inventory facts, and readiness conclusions are different constitutional participants.
- Diagnostic inventory and shape-audit paths: visibility rows and shape checks expose diagnostic surfaces without granting domain authority, event-ledger writes, or cluster mutation unless declared.
- Projection diagnostics and state-build/current-facts diagnostics: projection payloads, selections, diagnostic consumers, and presentation surfaces are implementation-local handoffs, not source truth.
- Provider/source and observation-agreement families: file-format candidates, provider-specific parser acceptance, candidate agreement, grammar recurrence, and architectural truth are separately admitted or explicitly refused.

## Recurring artifact families

### 1. Operator communication and inquiry orientation

**Before transition:** an operator communication, prompt, pressure, or note may exist outside repository truth.

**After transition:** an inquiry-note or orientation view may exist as preserved operator prose plus deterministic related-material visibility.

**Continuity answer:** the same communication can remain the same preserved note by retaining its note record, provenance, and orientation boundary. It is not the same participant as a fact, observation, requirement, plan, command, recommendation, or authority-bearing artifact.

**Constitutional role:** presentation/orientation support. It is not admission.

### 2. Question surfaces and bounded inquiry

**Before transition:** a possible question or concern exists as orientation or operator intent.

**After transition:** if it matches an exact implemented family, the app can represent it as eligible, eligible with parameters, diagnostic-only, not dispatchable, or executable through a bounded dispatch surface.

**Continuity answer:** the question does not remain identical by vocabulary. It remains lawful only if the implementation preserves a matching surface identity: family, diagnostic surface, required arguments, dispatch mapping, formatter, and authority boundary.

**Constitutional role:** routable bounded work when eligible; otherwise orientation, diagnostic-only visibility, or stop.

### 3. Observation, evidence, fact, support, and projection

**Before transition:** an observation or raw input may exist as source material.

**After transition:** it may become immutable evidence, an interpreted fact, fact support, a current projected belief, or projection-cache output, depending on the implemented path.

**Continuity answer:** evidence remains evidence by preserving provenance and immutability. A fact derived from evidence is a derived participant, not the same participant as the evidence. A projection is a presentation/read-model participant derived from facts/support, not source truth.

**Constitutional role:** source/evidence/admission/projection roles are family-local and not interchangeable.

### 4. Capability candidates, verification evidence, verified capability, and readiness

**Before transition:** package facts or observed environment facts may imply a capability candidate.

**After transition:** local PATH metadata can become acquired verification evidence; existing `capability_verified` facts can contribute inventory verification; promotion-readiness can report supported or unsupported readiness.

**Continuity answer:** a candidate remains the same candidate only inside the candidate surface by preserving the candidate subject, source evidence, and non-permission boundary. Verification evidence is support about the candidate, not the candidate itself. A verified inventory fact is an admitted participant. Readiness is an evaluation participant and still not execution permission or mutation.

**Constitutional role:** candidate, support, admission, and readiness are distinct.

### 5. Diagnostic inventory, shape audit, and diagnostic records

**Before transition:** implementation surfaces may exist as code, tests, CLI flags, formatters, and builders.

**After transition:** diagnostic inventory exposes declared shape, record scope, JSON support, repo-file/projected-state usage, event-ledger behavior, and mutation boundaries; shape audit checks implementation consistency.

**Continuity answer:** a diagnostic surface remains the same surface only through its registered name, CLI flags, builder/formatter/JSON shape, record behavior, event-ledger declaration, mutation declaration, and audited implementation contract. A diagnostic row is visibility about the surface, not the surface's domain authority.

**Constitutional role:** visibility/audit participant. If recorded, the record should remain diagnostic-run scoped unless intentionally different.

### 6. Projection payloads, selections, read models, and presentations

**Before transition:** projection execution can produce diagnostics and projected state from event/fact inputs.

**After transition:** payload snapshots, consumer-specific selections, current-facts views, state-build cache reports, and formatted presentations can exist.

**Continuity answer:** payload continuity is local to the payload snapshot and compatibility surface. A selection is derived from a payload. A presentation is derived from selected/read-model material. None of those becomes the source ledger or repository truth.

**Constitutional role:** derived support and presentation, not source authority.

### 7. Provider/source candidates and provider-specific acceptance

**Before transition:** unknown file bytes or external source output may be only a language or shape candidate.

**After transition:** a provider-specific source may accept the candidate through declared purpose, parser selection, shape checks, and provider-specific extraction.

**Continuity answer:** the candidate remains a candidate until a provider-local implementation admits it. Parser acceptance creates provider-specific observation material; it does not prove a global provider-contract subsystem.

**Constitutional role:** candidate support progressing through local admission.

## Continuity analysis

Repository evidence supports several different relationships rather than one global artifact-identity law:

| Relationship | Implementation-backed meaning | Recurring examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Artifact continuity | The same participant remains within the same family because its defining record/subject/provenance/boundary remains stable across a lawful transition. | Inquiry note as preserved operator prose; diagnostic surface as the same registered surface; evidence as immutable evidence. |
| Artifact replacement | A participant is superseded or no longer current under a local projection/read-model rule. | Current projected facts and support selection can change without making projections source truth. |
| Artifact derivation | A new participant is produced from another participant with provenance/support. | Fact from evidence; projection from facts/support; capability readiness from candidate plus verification support; diagnostic presentation from payload/selection. |
| Artifact presentation | A read-only view renders or explains existing material without becoming the underlying authority. | Inquiry orientation view; diagnostic inventory row; selection-path output; current-facts timing report. |
| Artifact promotion | A local admission gate changes status or authority inside a family. | Candidate/support to verified or admitted fact when implemented criteria are satisfied; provider candidate to provider-specific observations after local parser acceptance. |

The recurring pattern is not that an artifact has one global identity token. The pattern is that lawful transitions preserve or change typed roles under local authority boundaries.

## Distinction analysis

### Support != Admission

**What remains distinct:** support preserves reasons, evidence, or acquired metadata. Admission changes the artifact's status inside an owning family.

**What changes:** a candidate can gain support without gaining verified, admitted, executable, or authoritative status.

**What never changes:** support does not self-promote. It requires a family-local admitting implementation.

**Defining property:** the artifact is defined by the status its owning implementation assigns, not by the mere presence of support.

### Presentation != Authority

**What remains distinct:** rendered views, rows, reports, diagnostics, and explanations expose existing material. Authority remains with source records, implementation owners, registries, tests, or admission gates.

**What changes:** visibility and navigability can improve.

**What never changes:** presentation does not become source truth merely by being rendered.

**Defining property:** a presentation artifact is defined by derivation and boundary, not by the domain content it displays.

### Orientation != Truth

**What remains distinct:** orientation frames lawful attention and related material. Truth requires evidence/admission under repository authority.

**What changes:** a future inquiry can be better aimed.

**What never changes:** preserved operator prose remains operator prose unless another implemented path consumes and admits it.

**Defining property:** orientation is defined by preserved communication plus non-authorizing related-material visibility.

### Candidate != Verified

**What remains distinct:** a candidate is eligible for consideration; verified status is admitted by inventory/fact/verification semantics.

**What changes:** verification evidence and support may accumulate.

**What never changes:** candidate status does not grant capability permission, execution authority, or policy approval.

**Defining property:** candidate artifacts are defined by candidate source and non-permission boundary; verified artifacts are defined by admitted verification facts or inventory semantics.

### Projection != Source truth

**What remains distinct:** projection is rebuilt from source/event/fact material and can cache or present current beliefs. Source truth remains in the ledger/evidence/facts and repository implementation.

**What changes:** current views, support aggregation, and cache contents can change.

**What never changes:** a projection cache is not the authority for the facts it projects.

**Defining property:** projection artifacts are defined by derivation, rebuildability, and compatibility surface.

### Operator testimony != Repository truth

**What remains distinct:** testimony can orient, motivate, or supply prose. Repository truth requires implementation-backed evidence and lawful admission.

**What changes:** testimony can become preserved material or a target for inquiry.

**What never changes:** testimony alone does not override implementation evidence.

**Defining property:** testimony remains a communication artifact unless an implemented repository path admits derived evidence.

## Negative analysis

Recurring failures arise when one participant is treated as another:

- **Support becoming admission:** capability support or readiness can be mistaken for `capability_verified` admission or execution permission. Implementation keeps support, verification facts, readiness, policy, and execution separate.
- **Presentation becoming authority:** diagnostic inventory rows, inquiry orientation views, projection reports, and selection explanations can be mistaken for the domain authority they describe. Implementation treats them as read-only views unless explicit record/mutation semantics say otherwise.
- **Operator testimony becoming truth:** inquiry notes and prompt vocabulary can be mistaken for facts, requirements, plans, or repository knowledge. Inquiry orientation explicitly preserves operator prose as non-authorizing material.
- **Recommendation becoming command:** answer, pressure, or operational story surfaces may expose next concerns, but repository evidence preserves bounded authority and does not convert recommendations into runtime instructions without execution authorization.
- **Projection becoming source:** current-fact views, projection caches, and diagnostic payloads can be mistaken for source truth. Implementation keeps projections derived and rebuildable.
- **Candidate becoming verified:** package-derived or source-derived candidates can be mistaken for verified capabilities or provider contracts. Implementation requires local verification/admission.

## Locality

Artifact continuity is not currently recovered as globally constitutional.

The evidence supports this narrower locality:

- **Family-local:** strongest. Candidate, evidence, fact, projection, inquiry note, diagnostic surface, provider candidate, and capability verification each preserve what they are through family-owned fields, boundaries, and admission rules.
- **Boundary-local:** strong. The recurring constitutional work is preserving boundaries such as support/admission, presentation/authority, orientation/truth, candidate/verified, projection/source.
- **Implementation-local:** strong. Many continuities depend on concrete code paths, record shapes, registry names, tests, CLI flags, cache compatibility surfaces, and parser/admission functions.
- **Globally constitutional:** weak to unsupported. The repository repeatedly recovers preserved distinctions, not a single global property that all artifacts share across all lawful transitions.

## Typed unknowns

The following remain unknown or under-earned and should not be stabilized as repository doctrine:

- **Artifact substitution:** insufficient evidence for a general rule saying when one artifact can stand in for another.
- **Artifact equivalence:** no global equivalence relation is implemented across families.
- **Artifact merging:** no recurring implementation-backed rule was reviewed for merging constitutional participants.
- **Artifact splitting:** some families derive multiple artifacts from one source, but no general splitting law is recovered.
- **Artifact lineage:** provenance exists in several families, but not as a universal lineage subsystem.
- **Artifact retirement:** current projection and replacement behaviors exist locally, but no general retirement model is recovered.
- **Artifact identity:** the word remains only a working label; implementation evidence supports local participant continuity and preserved distinctions, not a repository Identity subsystem.

## Smallest truthful answer

The smallest recurring property currently earned is not global identity.

The smallest recurring property is:

```text
A constitutional artifact remains what it is only while its owning implementation preserves
its typed role, provenance or source relation, admission status, and authority boundary.
```

That property is recurring but local. It says less than a conservation framework and less than an Identity subsystem. It explains why an inquiry note can remain preserved operator prose, evidence can remain evidence, a candidate can remain a candidate, a diagnostic surface can remain a registered read-only surface, and a projection can remain a derived read model. It also explains why derived artifacts are not the same participants as their sources.

If the question requires one global constitutional property that preserves every artifact as the same participant across all lawful transitions, the answer is: **no such property has been earned**. The repository has more strongly recovered preserved constitutional distinctions than artifact identity.

## Confidence

**High confidence** that the repository repeatedly preserves constitutional distinctions such as orientation/truth, presentation/authority, support/admission, candidate/verified, projection/source, and operator testimony/repository truth.

**High confidence** that continuity is family-local, boundary-local, and implementation-local in the reviewed evidence.

**Low confidence** in any claim that the repository has recovered a global artifact-identity property.

**Final answer:** the repository does recover a recurring local property: preserved typed role plus provenance/source relation plus admission status plus authority boundary. It does **not** recover a global conservation or Identity framework. The recent work mostly recovered preserved distinctions, and treating those preserved distinctions as artifact identity would overclaim the evidence.
