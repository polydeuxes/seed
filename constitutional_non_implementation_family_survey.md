# Constitutional Non-Implementation Family Survey

Repository authority wins.

## Scope

This performs exactly one Non-Implementation Constitutional Family Survey.

The survey asks whether repository constitutional evidence already contains at least one family whose primary recoverable ownership is **not implementation**. It does not recover implementation, inspect implementation, recover implementation slices, prove broader ownership grammar, introduce a framework, introduce a methodology, introduce a registry, introduce an engine, introduce a planner, introduce a workflow, or generalize beyond recovered constitutional evidence.

The survey treats the prior ownership recovery characterization as controlling boundary evidence:

```text
Recovered grammar currently characterizes implementation ownership recovery only.
```

That statement is not treated as a rejection of broader ownership recovery. It is treated as a limitation on current validation and as a warrant to look only for independent constitutional evidence.

The survey reviewed only constitutional or constitutional-characterization artifacts already present in the repository, plus one repository-artifact characterization where constitutional artifacts naturally led because it directly characterizes non-runtime repository ownership.

## Evidence reviewed

### Ownership Recovery Grammar

The prior characterization is the limiting authority for ownership-recovery generalization. It found strong recurrence for implementation ownership recovery, but preserved that every validating family was implementation-based. It also identified the missing evidence needed before broader application: independent non-implementation ownership families, non-implementation boundaries, non-implementation completion evidence, compatibility or presentation boundaries, and recurrence across multiple non-implementation families.

Relevant evidence:

- Recovery was evidence-selected and local in the implementation families.
- The strongest completion condition was expressly implementation-local.
- Ownership-shaped observations such as local responsibility, compatibility not backfilling ownership, visibility not being ownership pressure, bounded Unknowns, and stopping when further movement merely relocates responsibility were broader in vocabulary but validated only in implementation.
- The characterization expressly preserved the Unknown of whether constitutional characterization, architectural investigation, or operational diagnostics have independently recovered the same grammar.

Assessment: this family does not itself provide a non-implementation ownership family. It supplies the boundary conditions for recognizing one without overgeneralizing.

### Repository Artifact / Runtime Artifact responsibility boundary

This characterization is the strongest surveyed candidate for non-implementation ownership. It explicitly recovers a responsibility boundary between repository artifacts and runtime artifacts. Repository artifacts preserve, route, explain, reconcile, audit, characterize, sequence, and hand off repository knowledge. Runtime artifacts implement bounded behavior.

Relevant evidence:

- The distinction is responsibility-based rather than merely docs-versus-code.
- Repository artifacts have documentation-scoped authority: orientation, vocabulary, lifecycle, status, finding preservation, scoped decision provenance, and observed evidence.
- Repository-owned responsibilities include constitutional thesis and invariants, stable vocabulary and boundary reference, navigation and answer routing, documentation authority and lifecycle control, current status and frontier ownership, completed findings, negative findings, audit memory, investigations, gap analyses, slice reports, pressure artifacts, and methodology reconciliations.
- The characterization states that many responsibilities intentionally remain documentation-owned and are not implementation-owned because they support repository competency, provenance, uncertainty preservation, duplicate-authority avoidance, and architecture-drift prevention.
- Future consumption by Seed, if ever implemented, would not automatically transfer artifact ownership; ownership would change only if later implementation-backed repository decision changed ownership.

Ownership assessment: this family primarily recovers **repository artifact ownership**, not implementation ownership. Its boundaries are constitutional/documentary, not code-local. It is therefore a viable non-implementation ownership candidate.

### Constitutional Reality Layer responsibility boundary

This characterization distinguishes source-side material, Seed's constitutional representation, and consumer-specific presentation or reports. It treats names such as Objective Reality, Constitutional Reality, and Subjective Reality as investigative labels rather than canonical constitutional law.

Relevant evidence:

- Objective/source-side material can provide source assertions but remains external to Seed's constitutional representation.
- Constitutional representation preserves and normalizes evidence-backed claims under scope and authority without equating representation with truth.
- Consumer-specific reports, projections, diagnostics, explanations, and views render or select from preserved constitutional representation while preserving boundaries.
- The characterization refuses runtime layer, processing pipeline, trust system, ontology layer, or new constitutional-family conclusions.
- It preserves that exact ownership of every constitutional responsibility remains Unknown.

Ownership assessment: this family contains adjacent responsibility boundaries and presentation-preservation boundaries, but it does not yet primarily recover ownership. It is constitutional orientation around representation and consumption, with ownership deliberately left unresolved in places.

### Constitutional Transition Families

This characterization rejects one generic constitutional movement and separates transition families such as orientation, bounded inquiry eligibility, evidence-bearing inquiry, candidate/support, promotion/admission, fact projection, implementation recovery, and earned methodology.

Relevant evidence:

- Communication, observation, or pressure can change orientation without changing repository knowledge, authority, runtime state, fact projection, implementation, or admission status.
- Orientation can lead to bounded inquiry eligibility only through local eligibility and dispatch boundaries.
- Evidence-bearing inquiry can preserve support or insufficiency without automatic promotion.
- The characterization repeatedly asks what changed and whether the change is authority-bearing.

Ownership assessment: this family primarily recovers transition boundaries, not ownership. It exhibits adjacent movement and authority-boundary discipline, but it does not identify a non-implementation owner comparable to implementation-local recovered owners.

### Constitutional Commitment

This characterization surveys Constitutional Commitment as a valley associated with uncommitted, eligible, ready, current, active, completed, and exhausted vocabulary.

Relevant evidence:

- Null, unknown, and uncommitted recur as default commitment posture until evidence justifies movement.
- Evidence sufficiency gates promotion or non-promotion.
- Current or active status distinguishes preserved material from presently operative status.
- Completion, exhaustion, stop, frontier-only status, and insufficient evidence appear as bounded status outcomes.
- The characterization explicitly refuses a commitment model, state machine, district, ownership, topology, runtime behavior, physiology, implementation recommendations, or slices.
- It states that commitment terrain often has status without ownership.

Ownership assessment: this family primarily recovers constitutional status discipline, not ownership. It has completion-like status terms, but it intentionally does not recover an owner.

### Constitutional Readiness / lawful beginning reconciliation

This reconciliation explains why a single constitutional `begin` function was not recovered. It decomposes lawful beginning into Null or uncommitted status, bounded presence, evidence, warrant, local eligibility, selection/refusal where supported, and lawful stop or handoff.

Relevant evidence:

- Lawful beginning is not one function.
- Time, Selection, Warrant, Promotion Authority, Current Lawful Condition, Null readiness, and Condition Progression each contribute bounded constitutional constraints without owning universal beginning.
- Readiness is not runtime activation, scheduling, lifecycle, workflow, behavior, or implementation readiness.
- The reconciliation preserves Unknowns about whether a future implementation object should expose a begin-like surface and which local responsibility, if any, could own it.

Ownership assessment: this family recovers constitutional decomposition and non-ownership boundaries. It does not itself identify a primary non-implementation owner.

## Required question assessment

### 1. Which constitutional families were surveyed?

Surveyed families were:

- Ownership Recovery Grammar.
- Repository Artifact / Runtime Artifact responsibility boundary.
- Constitutional Reality Layer responsibility boundary.
- Constitutional Transition Families.
- Constitutional Commitment.
- Constitutional Readiness / lawful beginning reconciliation.

### 2. Which surveyed families primarily recover ownership rather than implementation?

Only the **Repository Artifact / Runtime Artifact responsibility boundary** primarily recovers ownership rather than implementation.

Its primary recovered ownership is repository-artifact ownership: constitutional thesis, vocabulary, routing, documentation authority, lifecycle control, current status/frontier ownership, preservation of completed and negative findings, investigations, gap analyses, pressure artifacts, and methodology reconciliations. This ownership is documentation-scoped and repository-competency-oriented rather than implementation-local.

The Ownership Recovery Grammar surveys ownership but validates it only in implementation. The Reality Layer, Transition, Commitment, and Readiness families contain responsibility or boundary evidence but do not primarily recover ownership.

### 3. Which surveyed families, if any, exhibit adjacent ownership recovery?

The Repository Artifact / Runtime Artifact responsibility boundary exhibits the clearest adjacent non-implementation ownership recovery:

```text
mature constitutional / repository artifacts
remain repository-owned
rather than becoming runtime-owned merely because they are mature,
current, imperative, readable, or consumable.
```

The adjacency is between repository artifact responsibility and runtime artifact responsibility. Ownership is recovered by preserving the boundary rather than by moving artifacts into implementation.

The Reality Layer family exhibits adjacent responsibility boundaries between source-side material, constitutional representation, and consumer-specific presentation, but it does not recover ownership strongly enough to count as an ownership family by itself.

The Transition, Commitment, and Readiness families exhibit adjacent status or movement boundaries, not adjacent ownership recovery.

### 4. Which surveyed families, if any, exhibit bounded compatibility or presentation preservation?

Several surveyed families exhibit bounded compatibility or presentation preservation:

- **Repository Artifact / Runtime Artifact responsibility boundary:** future consumers may consume repository artifacts without transferring their ownership to runtime. Documentation may be observed, cited, interpreted, compared, routed, accepted, rejected, superseded, scoped, or preserved without becoming an executable command path.
- **Constitutional Reality Layer:** consumer-specific reports, diagnostics, read models, projections, explanations, and context views may present preserved constitutional representation without creating warrant or truth.
- **Constitutional Transition Families:** orientation can expose what may be looked at next without changing knowledge, authority, admission, fact projection, or implementation.
- **Constitutional Commitment:** visible status words such as current, active, completed, exhausted, eligible, and ready do not promote notes, pressure, observations, candidates, presentation labels, diagnostics, or reports into truth, ownership, authority, implementation, or runtime behavior.
- **Constitutional Readiness:** readiness vocabulary does not become activation, scheduling, workflow, behavior, implementation readiness, or a universal begin function.

These are compatibility or presentation boundaries. Only the Repository Artifact / Runtime Artifact family combines them with primary non-implementation ownership.

### 5. Which surveyed families, if any, exhibit completion conditions comparable to implementation recovery?

No surveyed non-implementation family exhibits completion conditions fully comparable to implementation ownership recovery.

The closest analogues are:

- **Repository Artifact / Runtime Artifact:** preservation of completed findings, negative findings, audit memory, supersession, archival roles, and documentation lifecycle responsibilities. These are durable repository-artifact completion and preservation responsibilities, but they are not equivalent to the implementation recovery completion condition of no adjacent implementation-local compressed owner remaining.
- **Constitutional Commitment:** complete, exhausted, stopped, frontier-only, and insufficient-evidence statuses constrain movement, but the characterization explicitly avoids recovering ownership or a lifecycle.
- **Constitutional Readiness:** lawful stop or handoff is part of readiness decomposition, but no begin owner or completion owner is recovered.
- **Constitutional Transition Families:** inquiry can end in evidence-bearing result or explicit stop, but this is transition discipline rather than ownership completion.

Therefore comparable completion-like evidence exists, but not enough to prove the broader ownership grammar across non-implementation families.

### 6. Does repository evidence currently identify a viable first non-implementation ownership family?

Yes.

Repository evidence currently identifies **Repository Artifact / Runtime Artifact responsibility boundary** as a viable first non-implementation ownership family. It is viable because it independently recovers repository-artifact responsibilities that are not implementation-owned, preserves an ownership boundary against runtime absorption, identifies consumers, preserves compatibility under future consumption, and explains why maturity does not imply implementation backlog.

It is only a first candidate. It does not prove a general non-implementation ownership grammar. Recurrence across multiple independent non-implementation ownership families remains unproven.

## Implementation independence assessment

The viable candidate is implementation-independent in the limited sense required by this survey:

- Its primary ownership is repository-artifact responsibility, not code responsibility.
- Its authority form is documentation-scoped authority, not runtime behavior.
- Its consumers include humans, agents, documentation maintainers, implementation workers, and future investigations, without transferring ownership to implementation.
- Its boundary survives possible future runtime consumption of prose.
- It does not depend on implementation slices, implementation recovery campaigns, implementation-local builders, implementation-local helpers, or implementation completion audits.

This does not mean implementation is irrelevant to the repository. It means the candidate family is not primarily recovered as implementation ownership.
