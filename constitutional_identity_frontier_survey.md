# Constitutional Identity Frontier Survey

Repository authority wins.

## Bounded question

```text
Across recurring constitutional
recoveries,

what constitutional role,
if any,

does identity
consistently perform?
```

This survey treats `identity` only as investigation pressure. It does not assume a constitutional Identity capability, import identity theory, introduce ontology, introduce type systems, or recover implementation ownership.

## Bounded answer

Recurring repository evidence does **not** support identity as a single constitutional participant, universal property, ontology, subsystem, or fundamental grammar.

The recurring constitutional role consistently performed by identity-pressure is narrower:

```text
identity preserves the bounded subject or participant of lawful reliance
so evidence, authority, translation, inquiry, observation, Unknown,
and stop discipline remain attached to the correct local thing
without collapsing it into an adjacent role.
```

In the strongest recurring form, identity functions as a **boundary-preserving attachment condition**. It answers: *which subject, participant, artifact, claim, question family, observation, unknown, or local surface is this evidence and authority about?* It prevents evidence, warrant, translation, reliance, current-fact visibility, responsibility claims, and inquiry movement from drifting to a neighboring subject or from promoting one constitutional role into another.

That role is usually local:

- subject-local in inquiry, warrant, responsibility, and observation;
- artifact-local in handoff, translation, and presentation boundaries;
- implementation-local in question-family dispatch, provider translation, subject convergence, diagnostics, and projection/read-model behavior;
- boundary-local in distinctions such as observation/fact, support/admission, candidate/verified, presentation/authority, projection/source truth, and Unknown/resolved fact.

The repository evidence therefore supports identity as a recurring **constraint on constitutional movement and reliance**, not as an independent constitutional capability.

## Recurring constitutional evidence

### Constitution and transition discipline

`constitution.md` sets the general rule that subjects remain Unknown / uncommitted until observable evidence justifies movement, that observation creates orientation rather than truth, that evidence alone moves Null, and that explicit authority distinctions must be preserved. Its artifact handoff rule says competencies exchange bounded artifacts rather than shared internal state, with observation, evidence, conclusion, authority, and stop condition visible enough for lawful continuation.

Identity-pressure appears here as a subject-preservation requirement: a claim, observation, owner, pressure, inquiry, classification, authority, compatibility, or recovery must not move merely because adjacent vocabulary exists. The repository must know *which subject* evidence moves.

### Constitutional translation and warrant

`constitutional_translation_invariant_investigation.md` says lawful translation preserves evidence-bound, authority-reduced, provenance-preserving, role-bounded constitutional content. It specifically names selected identity, provenance/support, confidence or evidence strength where applicable, locality/dimensions where applicable, authority, negative authority, and preserved unknowns as the residue that survives lawful translation while external form may change.

`constitutional_warrant_characterization.md` characterizes warrant as evidence-bound lawful reliance under a preserved authority boundary. It says warrant may preserve subject identity or bounded object identity, selected relation/value/claim/unknown/inquiry condition, provenance/support, authority, negative authority, locality, confidence limits, unknowns, and non-promotion boundaries. It also says warrant is weakened or destroyed if translation changes subject identity, relation, value, agreement, unknown, or inquiry condition beyond what evidence supports.

Identity-pressure is therefore not the whole warrant. It is one attachment condition inside warrant: lawful reliance must remain about the same bounded subject or participant at the resolution supported by evidence.

### Constitutional reliance

`constitutional_reliance_characterization.md` says expression may vary without changing reliance when the same lawful reliance profile survives, but expression changes or destroys reliance when it changes subject identity, relation, value, provenance, authority, negative authority, unknowns, confidence, consumer role, or non-promotion boundary.

This repeats the same pattern: identity is a relied-upon boundary inside a larger lawful-reliance profile. It is not a free-standing source of authority.

### Inquiry orientation and subject resolution

`responsibility_to_inquiry_boundary_audit.md` finds that inquiry dispatch consumes exact `QuestionFamily` identifiers backed by inventory rows and bounded ask dispatch maps, not recovered responsibility objects. It treats `QuestionFamily` as the current implementation-backed answerable-subject identity for bounded ask, while rejecting a direct Responsibility Recovery -> Subject Recovery -> Inquiry bridge.

`inquiry_subject_resolution_investigation.md` similarly distinguishes how subject identity is acquired for different answer surfaces and rejects the idea that Question Families alone own complete subject identity for every answer. It preserves unsupported claims about projection/current facts/capability inventory determining what an inquiry is about.

Identity-pressure here is admission and routing discipline: inquiry can lawfully move only when the answerable subject has an exact implemented identity or explicit required arguments; otherwise it stops, remains diagnostic-only, or remains orientation.

### Observation, evidence, current facts, and projection

`constitutional_artifact_identity_characterization.md` distinguishes operator communication, question surfaces, observations/evidence/facts/support/projection, capability candidates, diagnostics, projection payloads, provider candidates, and provider-specific acceptance. Its smallest truthful answer says an artifact remains what it is only while its owning implementation preserves typed role, provenance/source relation, admission status, and authority boundary. It explicitly rejects a global Identity framework.

`representation_transformation_discipline_investigation.md` and `expression_identity_convergence_investigation.md` show implementation reinforcement for that constitutional pattern. Observations, facts, fact support, current fact views, aliases, and projections transform representation while preserving local subject strings, support, provenance, and projection-specific convergence rules. Subject identity is sometimes assigned at fact creation, sometimes projected by alias resolution, and sometimes deliberately endpoint-scoped rather than globally unified.

Identity-pressure here prevents current fact visibility, support aggregation, observation ingestion, and projection from collapsing into source truth or from treating one expression as the same subject without implementation-backed convergence.

### Responsibility recovery and implementation recovery methodology

Responsibility and methodology reports recurrently bind findings to bounded subjects, evidence, authority, negative authority, locality, unknowns, and stop conditions. `investigation_grammar_topology_survey.md` says bounded question commonly neighbors evidence scope, subject identity, boundary, and termination; competency interrogation uses bounded subject, evidence ledger, subject identity, inputs/preconditions, boundaries, authority, negative authority, handoff, unknowns, and confidence.

Identity-pressure here keeps recovery from becoming abstraction search. A responsibility-like pattern is not enough; the repository must preserve the correct subject, evidence scope, owner or non-owner boundary, and lawful stop.

### Unknown preservation and lawful stop

`typed_unknown_characterization.md` describes Unknown states as preserving what kind of unresolved state exists, what evidence exposed it, what remains unknown, and what inquiry would be lawful next, without promoting unsupported conclusions into fact, implementation, authority, relationship, or action.

`unknown_currency_survey.md` repeatedly treats Unknown as local output, non-promotion status, preserved restraint, bounded unknown-family handling, or present-condition uncertainty. It explicitly refuses to decide whether local Unknown artifacts are identical, compatible, exchanged, connected, owned, or topologically related.

`constitutional_inquiry_state_investigation.md` finds lawful termination follows when bounded constitutional movement is exhausted at evidence, authority, boundary, unknown, reliance, negative-authority, or confidence limits. Identity-pressure participates by identifying the subject whose movement is exhausted; it does not itself authorize continuation.

### Frontier surveys and constitutional characterizations

Frontier and characterization reports repeatedly preserve pressure without overpromoting it. `constitutional_artifact_identity_characterization.md` is especially controlling because it already reviewed artifact identity pressure and concluded that the repository recovers family-local participant continuity and preserved distinctions, not a global identity property.

This survey does not expand beyond that unless recurring evidence requires it. The broader constitutional record confirms a recurring role for bounded subject/participant preservation, but it does not require a new constitutional identity capability.

## Recurring implementation evidence where applicable

Implementation evidence reinforces, but does not independently constitutionalize, identity-pressure:

1. **QuestionFamily dispatch** uses exact question-family strings, inventory rows, bounded status, required args, dispatch maps, and authority boundaries. Unknown, diagnostic-only, not-dispatchable, and incorrectly parameterized families are rejected rather than inferred.
2. **Provider translation** shapes provider-local subjects, predicates, metadata, confidence, dimensions, and canonical or bounded records while discarding unsupported provider excess. Identity is selected and shaped under provider-local authority; provider syntax is not preserved as identity.
3. **Observation/fact/support projection** preserves source observations into facts and support structures, with provenance and support attached. Current fact views present projected support rather than raw source truth.
4. **Alias and subject convergence** show no universal identity object. Some subject identity is exact at fact creation; some projected subject convergence occurs through alias resolution; endpoint-scoped paths preserve separation.
5. **Diagnostic inventory and shape audit** preserve diagnostic surface names, CLI flags, record scope, JSON shape, ledger behavior, and mutation declarations. A diagnostic row is visibility about a surface, not the surface's domain authority.
6. **Typed Unknown implementation** preserves unknown type, area, and reason locally, then projects to compatibility output without resolving the unknown.

These implementation examples all support locality and boundary preservation. None supports a repository-wide Identity subsystem.

## Recovered recurring pressures

The recurring pressures are real, but narrower than a constitutional identity doctrine:

- preserve the bounded subject of evidence during inquiry and translation;
- bind evidence and provenance to the correct subject or participant;
- preserve artifact role across lawful handoff without turning handoff form into authority;
- keep producer/source/provenance attached where downstream reliance depends on it;
- prevent support from becoming admission;
- prevent presentation from becoming authority;
- prevent observation from becoming fact;
- prevent candidate from becoming verified;
- prevent projection/current view from becoming source truth;
- prevent Unknown from becoming resolved fact;
- prevent question text or responsibility vocabulary from bypassing exact answerable-subject registration;
- preserve locality where implementation converges identity only in a specific family or projection path;
- stop when the subject, authority, or evidence boundary is insufficient for the proposed movement.

## Existing recovered capabilities that overlap this investigation

Identity-pressure is mostly explained by already recovered capabilities:

- **Constitutional warrant:** preserves lawful reliance profile, including subject/object identity, evidence, authority, negative authority, unknowns, and confidence limits.
- **Constitutional translation:** preserves warranted content while allowing external form to change.
- **Constitutional reliance:** constrains downstream use to the same supported subject, relation, authority, and non-promotion boundary.
- **Boundary preservation:** keeps roles such as observation, evidence, fact, support, projection, candidate, verified, diagnostic, presentation, and authority distinct.
- **Inquiry orientation / subject resolution:** admits only exact or locally supplied answerable subjects into bounded work.
- **Unknown preservation:** preserves unresolved states without converting them into truth.
- **Lawful stop:** terminates movement when identity, evidence, authority, reliance, or unknown boundaries would be crossed.
- **Implementation recovery methodology:** requires bounded subject, evidence scope, authority, negative authority, locality, handoff, unknowns, confidence, and stop conditions.

Because those capabilities already explain most observed pressure, this survey does not recover identity as a new constitutional capability.

## Supported constitutional distinctions

Supported distinctions:

1. **Identity-pressure is not authority.** Knowing the apparent subject does not authorize truth, mutation, admission, or implementation.
2. **Identity-pressure is not evidence.** It tells what evidence is about; it does not create evidence.
3. **Identity-pressure is not warrant.** Warrant includes evidence-bound lawful reliance under authority; identity is one condition warrant must preserve.
4. **Identity-pressure is not translation.** Translation may reshape identity-bearing content; lawful translation must not shift the supported subject beyond evidence.
5. **Identity-pressure is not ownership.** Ownership recovery requires recurring implementation evidence; identity vocabulary cannot recover an owner.
6. **Identity-pressure is not subject ontology.** Different families preserve subjects, artifacts, producers, observations, unknowns, and projections differently.
7. **Identity-pressure is not continuity in every sense.** Derivation, presentation, promotion, replacement, and support can create adjacent participants rather than the same participant continuing.
8. **Identity-pressure is strongest at boundaries.** Its recurring work is preventing collapse between neighboring constitutional roles.

## Unsupported candidate ideas

The following are not supported by the reviewed recurring evidence:

- a constitutional Identity subsystem;
- a universal identity registry;
- a global identity token for all artifacts;
- a formal identity theory or ontology;
- a type-system account of identity;
- a universal sameness rule across translation;
- a global equivalence relation for subjects or artifacts;
- a general artifact substitution rule;
- a general artifact merging/splitting/retirement model;
- an automated Subject Recovery -> Inquiry bridge;
- a claim that repeated identity vocabulary is repository knowledge;
- a claim that presentation labels such as current work position or source navigation create stable repository identity;
- a claim that implementation subject convergence in one path generalizes to all paths.

## Preserved unknowns

Preserved unknowns:

1. Whether future implementation evidence will recover a specific local identity owner in one family.
2. Whether any family implements artifact substitution, merging, splitting, retirement, or equivalence strongly enough for a later bounded characterization.
3. Whether cross-family lineage is only recurring provenance/support or a future implementation-backed need.
4. Whether all lawful claim-bearing movements require a separately named bounded subject, or whether some families preserve the subject implicitly through local records.
5. Whether diagnostic records can ever lawfully influence later repository reasoning without becoming cluster truth.
6. Whether current facts, current status, current lawful condition, current inquiry, current work, current selection, and current unknowns share any deeper local identity relation beyond currently reviewed projection and presentation boundaries.
7. Whether a future survey should distinguish subject identity, artifact identity, producer identity, observation identity, translation identity, and current-fact identity as separate local families.

## Confidence

**High confidence** that recurring constitutional evidence requires subject/participant preservation as a boundary condition for lawful reliance, translation, inquiry, observation, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop.

**High confidence** that existing recovered capabilities already explain most identity-pressure: warrant, translation, reliance, boundary preservation, inquiry subject resolution, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop.

**Medium confidence** in the phrase `boundary-preserving attachment condition`, because it is a survey compression rather than stabilized repository vocabulary.

**Low confidence** in any stronger claim that identity is a constitutional participant, global property, ontology, implementation owner, or fundamental grammar.

## Recommended next investigation

No implementation change is recommended.

If a future investigation is needed, the smallest bounded next investigation should be:

```text
Across subject identity, artifact identity, producer identity,
observation identity, translation identity, and current-fact identity,
which are separate local families and which are only vocabulary
for warrant/boundary preservation?
```

That future investigation should use implementation evidence only where a specific local path exists, and it should preserve unsupported conclusions as Unknowns.

## Final answer to the bounded question

Identity consistently performs a constitutional **boundary-preserving attachment role**: it keeps evidence, provenance, authority, warrant, reliance, inquiry movement, translation, observation, Unknown status, and lawful stop attached to the correct bounded subject or participant at the resolution repository evidence supports.

It does not consistently perform as an independent constitutional capability. Existing constitutional anatomy already explains most of the pressure: identity is a recurring condition inside warrant, translation, reliance, boundary preservation, inquiry subject resolution, Unknown preservation, and lawful stop.
