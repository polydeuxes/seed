## Comparison after independent inspection

### Recurring investigation responsibilities

Recurring repository evidence supports these investigation responsibilities:

1. Select one bounded question or subject.
2. Declare the evidence scope before making claims.
3. Inspect evidence independently before comparison.
4. Bind each claim to repository evidence or prior bounded artifact authority.
5. Distinguish structural recurrence, vocabulary recurrence, implementation evidence, and constitutional authority.
6. Identify the smallest supported identity, district, boundary, transition, pressure, or warrant.
7. State negative authority as part of the result.
8. Preserve unknowns rather than inferring through them.
9. Calibrate confidence to evidence strength.
10. Stop when further movement would require implementation, framework invention, ownership recovery, runtime architecture, or unsupported promotion.

These responsibilities are investigative. They do not constitute a prompt/runtime mechanism, planning mechanism, scheduling mechanism, registry mechanism, or implementation architecture.

### Recurring investigation transitions

The recurring transitions are:

```text
unbounded possibility / pressure / recurrence / candidate term
↓
bounded question or subject
↓
declared evidence scope
↓
independent evidence inspection
↓
comparison only after inspection
↓
supported local characterization, survey result, readiness answer, bridge boundary, pressure conclusion, competency district, or refusal
↓
unknown preservation and confidence
↓
explicit stop
```

The transition changes investigative warrant. It does not mutate runtime state, create a new owner, or promote recurring prose into implementation.

The reviewed investigations repeatedly enforce these boundaries:

- evidence before conclusion;

- survey resolution before district/family/owner recovery;
- readiness in principle before implementation;
