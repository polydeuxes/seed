The competency is **constitutional local grammar recovery by observation**.

Recurring repository evidence supports these inputs:

1. **An unknown external expression.** The expression must remain initially unknown rather than silently treated as already understood.

3. **Observation access to expression evidence.** The expression must be observable as repository evidence, app behavior, diagnostic output, prior investigation material, provider material, document structure, implementation evidence, or another bounded evidence surface.
4. **Recurring structural evidence.** One occurrence, attractive vocabulary, or fluent expression is insufficient. Recovery depends on repeated evidence-bearing structure.
5. **Recoverable boundaries.** The evidence must show where local units, surfaces, subjects, relations, values, sections, carriers, or record shapes begin and end.
6. **Recoverable roles.** The evidence must show local functions such as carrier, subject, predicate, value, provenance, confidence, dimension, agreement, unknown, authority limit, or refusal.

8. **Recoverable distinctions.** The evidence must support distinctions such as expression versus warrant, agreement versus truth, observation versus mutation, visibility versus authority, provider grammar versus Seed-owned structural evidence, and local completion versus whole-domain completion.
9. **Authority boundary.** Recovery must preserve what the evidence authorizes and what it refuses.
10. **Negative authority.** The inquiry must state what tempting conclusions are not authorized.
11. **Unknown preservation.** Unsupported meaning, implementation, universality, ownership, parser behavior, semantic interpretation, or future capability remains unknown.
12. **Confidence and lawful stop.** Recovery stops when further movement would require implementation design, semantic inference, parser construction, universalization, or evidence-free promotion.

## Recovered competency sequence

### 1. Unknown External Expression → Bounded Constitutional Question

An unknown expression does not authorize recovery by itself. The repository repeatedly treats unknowns as preserved conditions until a bounded question can discriminate evidence without invention.

Local grammar recovery begins when the unknown expression is placed under a question such as: what recurring structure, boundary, role, carrier, or distinction does this expression make available to constitutional observation?

### 2. Bounded Constitutional Question → Observation Under Repository Authority

The bounded question authorizes evidence review, not construction. Observation may inspect expression surfaces, prior investigations, diagnostics, app output, implementation evidence, document structure, provider material, or already-observed records only within the evidence boundary.

Observation is not truth. It is a disciplined intake of evidence capable of preserving provenance, support, role, authority, unknowns, and confidence.

### 3. Observation Under Repository Authority → Recurring Structural Evidence

The expression becomes recoverable only when observation finds recurring evidence-bearing structure. This recurrence may be a repeated relation shape, repeated subject/predicate/value shape, repeated provider-to-observation transition, repeated boundary/refusal pattern, repeated diagnostic row shape, repeated audit section grammar, or repeated characterization pattern.

Recurrence alone is not enough. The recurrence must be bound to repository evidence and must preserve authority limits.

### 4. Recurring Structural Evidence → Recovered Local Grammar

Recovered local grammar is the bounded account of the recurring structure. It may identify units, boundaries, roles, carriers, relations, values, provenance fields, authority limits, negative authority, confidence limits, or stop conditions.

The recovery is local because it applies only to the expression region and evidence pattern reviewed. It is not a universal grammar of the external expression's whole language.

### 5. Recovered Local Grammar → Bounded Characterization

The local grammar becomes constitutional only when characterized with identity, inputs, recovered sequence, structural recovery, relationship to expression, relationship to warrant, relationship to existing observers, negative authority, resolution, unknowns, and confidence.

A recovered pattern that is merely named, repeated, or useful is not yet constitutional grammar unless the characterization preserves its authority and non-authority.

### 6. Bounded Characterization → Constitutional Warrant

Constitutional warrant is the evidence-bound lawful reliance preserved under authority boundary. A local grammar carries warrant only to the extent that evidence, provenance, bounded content, role, authority limit, negative authority, unknown preservation, confidence, and non-promotion survive.

The warrant belongs to the bounded recovery, not to the external expression as such.

### 7. Constitutional Warrant → Lawful Reliance

Lawful reliance permits later work to use the recovered grammar only within its supported role. Later work may rely on the local structure as locally recovered, not as semantic truth, parser behavior, implementation design, universal grammar, or mutation authority.

## Relationship to constitutional warrant

Local grammar becomes lawful only when it carries constitutional warrant.

Constitutional warrant is not the external syntax, provider representation, record class, or translation mechanism. It is the repository-supported entitlement to bounded lawful reliance. Therefore local grammar recovery succeeds only when the recovery preserves:
