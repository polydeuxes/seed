## Recurring constitutional property

The recurring constitutional property preserved under the name **warrant** is:

```text
evidence-bound lawful reliance under a preserved authority boundary
```

More fully:

```text
Constitutional warrant is the recurring property by which a bounded subject,
relation, value, claim, agreement, unknown, or inquiry condition may still be
lawfully relied upon after translation because its repository evidence,
provenance/support, role, authority limit, negative authority, unknowns, and
confidence limit have been preserved.
```

The property is not the content's syntax. It is not the provider representation. It is not the record class. It is not a translation mechanism. It is the content's repository-supported entitlement to bounded lawful reliance.

The strongest recurring evidence is the prior constitutional translation invariant investigation, which found that lawful translation preserves bounded warranted content with provenance, role, authority limit, negative authority, unknown preservation, confidence-calibrated non-promotion, and boundary discipline rather than preserving external form.

## Synthesis

```text
Why can

constitutional warrant

survive translation

when syntax cannot?
```

Constitutional warrant can survive translation when syntax cannot because warrant is the repository-authorized entitlement to bounded lawful reliance, while syntax is only one external or local carrier of content.

Lawful translation may replace provider grammar, parser shape, record class, report form, or presentation wording. It remains lawful only if it preserves the evidence that supports the content, the provenance needed to inspect that support, the bounded subject or relation/value/agreement/unknown/inquiry condition, the role in which the content may be consumed, the authority boundary, the negative authority that blocks overpromotion, the unresolved unknowns, and the confidence or evidence-strength limit where supported.

Syntax fails as the invariant because recurring repository evidence shows many lawful forms: provider records, relationship facts, repository observations, canonical observations, agreement records, grammar observations, typed unknowns, responsibility evaluations, inquiry states, bounded-continuation judgments, and family completion audits. Constitutional warrant survives because each lawful form preserves the same repository-bounded reason for reliance and the same boundary against relying on more than the repository supports.
