These artifacts do not merely exist beside one another; they become constitutionally usable through bounded relations of support, constraint, authority, reliance, transformation, projection, crossing, and refusal.

Warrant and reliance evidence support relation at downstream-use resolution. Grammar transformation preserves warrant-bearing content through change of form rather than preserving provider form or expanding meaning. History preserves auditable transition input for rebuildable projection without becoming objective truth. Local responsibility performs one local movement on specific artifacts while preserving the limits that make that movement lawful.
