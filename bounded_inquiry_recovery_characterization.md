This is a repository characterization, not a constitutional competency, runtime planner, recovery engine, routing framework, or recommendation to implement one.

What recurring behavior transforms an unbounded architectural possibility into a bounded repository investigation?

## Investigation review

### Competency interrogation methodology

**What triggered the investigation?**

Repeated competency reports raised a methodological possibility: perhaps Competency Interrogation had become a stable recovery discipline.

**What was initially unknown?**

Whether the repeated report shape was a constitutional competency, an implemented Seed capability, a source of new authority, or only a useful documentation discipline.

**What made the initial question unrecoverable?**

The broad question could have promoted repeated prose into architecture. The reconciliation explicitly limited itself to the methodology, not individual competencies, runtime physiology, implementation recovery, or constitutional law.

**How was it reduced?**

The report reduced the question to stable sections and recurring mistakes prevented by evidence, identity, authority, boundaries, unknowns, reliance, negative authority, neighbor contrast, termination, and confidence.

**What bounded question reached the repository?**

Which interrogation questions had repeated implementation-backed use sufficient to classify as earned, working, or insufficient?

**Did implementation answer the original question, or the reduced one?**

The reduced one. The answer was that Competency Interrogation earned reconciliation as a boundary-recovery discipline, not as an implemented Seed capability or new authority.

### Implementation readiness

**What triggered the investigation?**

Accumulated architectural understanding and pressure raised the question of when discovery should become implementation.

**What was initially unknown?**

Whether recurrence, pressure, operator excitement, vocabulary, methodology stability, or accumulated reports were enough to authorize implementation.

**What made the initial question unrecoverable?**

A global rule like `understanding accumulated -> implement` would overclaim across families and confuse architectural appeal with implementation necessity.

**How was it reduced?**

The investigation reduced readiness to a convergence of independently recurring boundary, stable owner or corridor, constitutional or implementation necessity, smallest behavior-preserving change, and testable preservation surface.

**What bounded question reached the repository?**

When has repository evidence reduced an architectural question to a bounded, owned, evidence-backed change whose behavior can be preserved or checked?

**Did implementation answer the original question, or the reduced one?**

The reduced one. It did not authorize a backlog of accumulated architecture; it characterized conditions under which a smallest implementation slice is earned.

### Constitutional promotion authority

**What triggered the investigation?**

Repeated transitions among null, candidate, supported, bounded, verified, trusted, promoted, and implemented states raised the question of who may promote artifacts.

**What was initially unknown?**

Whether promotion authority belonged to an operator, artifact, pressure item, methodology, universal governance owner, or something else.

**What made the initial question unrecoverable?**

A universal promoter would erase family-local admission rules and stop conditions.

**How was it reduced?**

The investigation reduced promotion to evidence-gated, boundary-local permission interpreted by the family competent to handle that movement.

**What bounded question reached the repository?**

For each promotion family, what evidence, boundary, owner/corridor, stop condition, and preservation surface permit movement?

**Did implementation answer the original question, or the reduced one?**

The reduced one. No universal promoter was recovered.

### Transition families

**How was it reduced?**

## Reduction analysis

Recurring reductions were found, but not every example is equally supported.

| Candidate reduction | Evidence status | Characterization |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Global -> family-local | Strong | Promotion authority, transition families, implementation readiness, pressure, and artifact identity repeatedly avoid universal owners and recover family-local boundaries. |
| Universal -> bounded | Strong | Question surfaces, bounded ask, inquiry eligibility, and methodology-as-subject all require exact implemented families, dispatch status, arguments, and authority boundaries. |
| Philosophical -> implementation-backed | Strong | Identity, artifact delta, constitutional promotion, and pressure investigations repeatedly refuse philosophical or vocabulary-only conclusions. |
| Interesting observation -> recoverable repository question | Strong | Inquiry eligibility, inquiry orientation, and question responsibility reduce attractive ideas or operator prose to evidence-strength checks, orientation, or exact registered surfaces. |
| Operator testimony -> repository characterization | Strong as a distinction; weak as automatic transformation | Inquiry orientation and artifact identity support preserving testimony separately from fact or authority. They do not show automatic conversion into characterization. |
| Pressure -> implementation | Bounded / conditional | Pressure can trigger investigation, but implementation readiness requires stable owner/corridor, necessity, smallest slice, and preservation checks. |
| Recurrence -> authority | Rejected | Recurrence helps, but repeated vocabulary or reports do not by themselves authorize implementation, registration, facthood, or constitutional promotion. |

The recurring reduction behavior is therefore not a named engine. It is a repeated methodological discipline:

```text
possible observation / broad question / pressure / hypothesis
    -> preserve it without promotion
    -> reload repository evidence and prior work
    -> identify the strongest local implemented family, owner, or surface
    -> compare claim strength with evidence strength
    -> narrow to the exact bounded question that implementation can answer
    -> answer only that reduced question
    -> stop with typed unknowns where evidence ends
```

## Distinction analysis

### Possible observation != recovered artifact

Supported. Inquiry orientation preserves operator prose as a note and can orient it, but explicitly denies that the note is a fact, claim, goal, requirement, decision, command, ownership assertion, recommendation, plan, runtime instruction, or next safe move. Artifact identity likewise preserves operator testimony as distinct from repository truth.

### Interesting hypothesis != recoverable investigation

Supported. Inquiry eligibility distinguishes conceptual appeal from justified inquiry. A hypothesis becomes recoverable only when evidence strength matches the claim strength and a supported slice can be authorized, deferred, rejected, or preserved as unknown.

### Operator testimony != repository characterization

Supported. Operator testimony may trigger or orient investigation, but repository characterization requires implementation-backed evidence, app surfaces, tests, reports, or lawful admission. Testimony does not itself become fact.

### Broad question != bounded inquiry

Strongly supported. Question surfaces and bounded ask require exact family registration, required arguments, dispatchability, and authority boundaries. Unknown families remain unknown. Diagnostic-only and non-dispatchable surfaces do not become executable merely because a question is broad or interesting.

### Recurring method != Seed runtime behavior

Supported. Competency interrogation methodology and methodology-as-inquiry-subject reports recover a documentation/investigation discipline and current app expressiveness. They do not recover a runtime methodology planner, automatic recovery engine, or implemented `InquiryEligibility` subsystem.

## Locality

The reduction behavior appears primarily **methodology-local** and **operator/repository-workflow-local**.

It is repository-backed because the reduction repeatedly uses implementation evidence, app surfaces, tests, and preserved reports. It is not currently repository-local as a single implemented subsystem.

It is not constitutional in the sense of a named constitutional competency or universal authority. Constitutional rules constrain it: evidence before promotion, observation before truth, bounded handoff, authority boundaries, and stop conditions. Those constraints do not prove an implemented constitutional reduction faculty.

Current best locality:

```text
methodology-local discipline
supported by repository evidence
constrained by constitutional evidence/promotion rules
not implemented as a Seed runtime planner or universal inquiry reducer
```

## Typed unknowns

The following remain unknown or under-earned:

- **Automatic inquiry reduction**: no reviewed implementation shows Seed automatically reducing arbitrary unbounded architectural questions into bounded investigations.
- **Self-bounding inquiries**: no evidence proves inquiries autonomously refine their own scope at runtime.
- **Runtime inquiry refinement**: bounded ask dispatches exact known surfaces; it does not demonstrate iterative runtime refinement from broad prose.
- **Cross-family reductions**: global-to-family-local reduction recurs in reports, but no shared cross-family reducer is implemented.
- **Reduction failure taxonomy**: reports preserve stops and insufficiency, but no stable taxonomy of reduction failure has been recovered.
- **Methodology question registration**: methodology-oriented inquiry may fit current Inquiry shape, but it is not currently a registered question family.
- **Operator-local vs team-local recurrence**: the repository shows repeated artifacts, but does not prove whether recurrence belongs to one operator's working style, a team convention, or a formal repository mechanism.

## Smallest truthful answer

Yes, the repository repeatedly demonstrates a behavior that reduces unbounded possibilities into bounded investigations.

The smallest recurring behavior is:

```text
Before an architectural question becomes repository recoverable,
the possible observation is preserved without promotion,
then reduced to the smallest implementation-backed question
that has a local evidence source, authority boundary, and stop condition.
```

More compressed:

```text
evidence-gated bounding before answer
```

This behavior belongs to the current recovery methodology and operator/repository workflow, not to Seed as an implemented runtime competency. It is constrained by repository authority and constitutional evidence rules. It should not be named as a new engine, planner, or automatic Seed behavior.
