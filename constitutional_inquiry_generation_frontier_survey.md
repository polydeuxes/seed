# Constitutional Inquiry Generation Frontier Survey

## Bounded question

Given one bounded observation, what recurring constitutional questions, if any, become lawfully admissible without promoting any interpretation into truth?

## Answer

Recurring repository evidence supports an adjacent constitutional competency only in a narrow, frontier form: **bounded inquiry admissibility**. The supported competency is not a question engine, curiosity, planning, semantic interpretation, or autonomous reasoning. It is the ability to decide whether a next question is admissible because it stays inside an implemented or constitutionally evidenced boundary, preserves the observation as evidence rather than truth, and stops when no such boundary exists.

The evidence does **not** support a general mechanism that generates questions from every observation. It supports a smaller pattern:

1. preserve the bounded observation and its identity/provenance as evidence;
2. ask only questions about support, authority, identity, boundary, unknowns, derivation, selection, admissible surface, or stop condition when those questions map to existing repository surfaces or recurring constitutional reports;
3. refuse semantic meaning-making, action planning, ontology creation, or unsupported promotion;
4. preserve unsupported candidate questions as Unknowns.

Therefore, after a bounded observation, the recurring lawfully admissible question families are not “what does it mean?” but smaller questions such as:

- What exactly is the observed subject, target, domain, source, or question-family identity?
- What evidence supports this observation, and what is still missing?
- What authority does the observation have, and what authority does it lack?
- Is there an implemented surface that can answer a bounded follow-up without semantic paraphrase or new abstraction?
- Is there derivation or selection evidence, or must that relation remain Unknown?
- Does the current evidence authorize reliance, only orientation, or lawful stop?

These are admissible only when the repository already has an implemented surface, exact inventory row, syntactic lookup, diagnostic/audit boundary, or recurring constitutional investigation supporting them.

## Recurring constitutional evidence

### Inquiry navigation exists as a constrained frontier neighbor

`seed_competency_roadmap_v2.md` names **Inquiry Navigation** as the competency whose bounded question is: given interpreted evidence, what is the next evidence-bearing question or authority-bearing surface, and when is there no supported next step. It says the work is limited to bounded implemented surfaces and explicitly says to determine the next question needed to expose evidence, not a plan of action, with stop required when no implemented surface can answer.

The same roadmap refuses the exact forbidden expansions relevant to this survey: no planner, no natural-language routing, no repository-neutral inquiry controller, no automated next-question engine, and no target-repository question inventory. It also excludes planner behavior, autonomous review, semantic question understanding, creation of new question families, direct dispatch from recovered responsibilities, grammar-observation routing, and recommendations about next safe moves.

This is the strongest recurring evidence for the answer: repository evidence supports bounded next-question admissibility, but not question generation as autonomous behavior.

### Existing inquiry disciplines already supply pieces

The recurring evidence identifies these adjacent capabilities:

- exact inquiry/ask dispatch: exact question-family identity, exact argument counts, static dispatch maps, and refusal of unknown or unsupported families;
- inquiry orientation: preserved operator prose, deterministic lexical overlap, and refusal of intent, planning, ownership, commands, requirements, and next-move authority;
- source navigation: syntactic lookup over projected source fact supports, not semantic relevance or responsibility inference;
- diagnostic and visibility surfaces: boundaries for read-only status, recordability, event-ledger behavior, mutation, and shape specs;
- observation/evidence/fact boundaries: state projection, grammar observation, observation agreement, fact support, and explanations preserve non-promotion;
- reasoning and selection surfaces: derivation and selection visibility with Unknown preservation;
- bounded-question discipline: bounded question -> bounded work -> bounded answer -> next responsibility, while refusing broader adjacent questions.

Together these support a constitutional pressure: a bounded observation may make some **evidence-exposing questions** admissible. They do not support arbitrary question generation.

## Recurring implementation evidence

### Inquiry orientation preserves observation-like prose without truth promotion

`seed_runtime/inquiry_orientation.py` states that the orientation is read-only and that the inquiry note is preserved operator prose, not a fact, claim, goal, tool need, requirement, capability, decision, proposal, plan, authorization, command, or runtime instruction. It also limits matches to deterministic lexical overlap and denies importance, ownership, intent, concern, recommended action, or next safe move.

Its builder prepares inquiry material, composes answer material, collects evidence, and returns related material with uncertainty and authority boundary. This demonstrates existing bounded orientation behavior: it can expose related material for a bounded note/observation without promoting the note into truth or a next action.

### Inquiry artifacts explicitly refuse inquiry movement inference

`seed_runtime/inquiry_artifacts.py` exposes a read-only visibility surface with `infers_inquiry_movement=False`, `creates_inquiry_graph=False`, `infers_pressure_transformation=False`, and `performs_workflow_or_planning=False`. Its human-readable boundary says there is no recording, no event-ledger write, no cluster mutation, no inquiry graph creation, no pressure transformation inference, and no workflow or planning behavior.

This implementation evidence matters because it blocks a stronger reading: repository-visible inquiry artifacts do not themselves authorize a generated graph of follow-up questions.

### Exact question-family behavior admits only registered bounded questions

`tests/test_question_surface_inventory.py` verifies that exact question-family lookup is separate from eligibility and dispatch, and that unknown text is rejected before eligibility. The same test suite verifies the unknown-family result shape: unknown dispatch surface, unknown answer responsibility, unknown relationship status, and an unknown authority boundary when no inventory row exists.

This supports the admissibility rule: a next question can be lawfully asked through the exact question surface only if it has implementation-backed identity and eligibility. Unknown question-family text remains Unknown rather than becoming an interpreted question.

### Reasoning and selection path audits preserve gaps instead of inventing paths

`seed_runtime/reasoning_path_audit.py` preserves a typed Unknown with area `derivation` and reason `no derivation evidence currently available` when no evidence, intermediate conclusion, derived conclusion, consumer, or story impact exists. Its formatter renders Unknowns rather than filling the path.

`seed_runtime/selection_path_audit.py` is read-only, records no facts, writes no event ledger, and mutates no cluster. When the target is not an implemented selection surface, it returns `selected="unknown"`, says the target is not implemented, and preserves an `Implementation Unknown` for `selection_logic`.

This demonstrates a recurring pattern: when a bounded observation pressures derivation or selection questions, the implementation can admit the question only as far as implemented evidence reaches; otherwise the lawful answer is Unknown/stop.

## Recovered recurring pressures

The recurring pressures lawfully adjacent to a bounded observation are:

1. **Identity pressure** — What exact subject, target, domain, query, note, or question family has been supplied?
2. **Evidence pressure** — What evidence supports the observation, and what support is absent?
3. **Authority pressure** — What authority does the observation/source/surface have, and what authority is explicitly absent?
4. **Boundary pressure** — Is the follow-up read-only, diagnostic-only, recordable, event-ledger-writing, mutating, or answer-responsible?
5. **Surface admissibility pressure** — Does an exact implemented question family, audit, orientation, source lookup, reasoning path, selection path, fact support, explanation, or diagnostic surface exist for the next question?
6. **Unknown pressure** — Which relation, support, authority, or identity must remain Unknown?
7. **Reliance pressure** — Is the observation sufficient for bounded reliance, only orientation, or no reliance?
8. **Stop pressure** — Does the next step require planning, semantic routing, new ontology, unsupported responsibility recovery, or unregistered dispatch?

These are not generated meanings. They are constitutional checks around admissible inquiry.

## Existing constitutional capabilities that already answer part of the question

- **Target / identity orientation** answers what exact identity was supplied, but not what it means.
- **Evidence visibility** exposes observations, supports, diagnostics, and records without promoting them.
- **Evidence interpretation** can produce bounded support/status/relationship/readiness outputs with authority preserved.
- **Exact question-surface inventory and bounded ask** decide whether a named question family is registered, eligible, diagnostic-only, not dispatchable, or unknown.
- **Inquiry orientation** can surface deterministic related material while refusing intent, planning, recommendation, or next-move authority.
- **Reasoning path audit** can expose available derivation evidence and preserve derivation Unknowns.
- **Selection path audit** can expose implemented selection evidence and preserve selection Unknowns.
- **Diagnostic inventory and shape audit** can determine whether operational visibility surfaces are registered and checked.
- **Stop discipline** is already present where unknown, diagnostic-only, non-dispatchable, unsupported, or authority-blocked conditions refuse movement.

## Supported constitutional distinctions

1. **Observation is not interpretation.** A bounded observation may authorize preservation and evidence questions, not semantic truth.
2. **Inquiry orientation is not planning.** Related material can be exposed without producing a plan, recommendation, or next move.
3. **Admissible question is not generated question.** A question is admissible only when it maps to an implemented or constitutionally supported surface/boundary.
4. **Question-family identity is not eligibility or dispatch.** Exact lookup can succeed or fail before eligibility and dispatch are considered.
5. **Unknown is a lawful answer.** Missing derivation, missing selection logic, missing question-family row, and missing authority boundary remain Unknown.
6. **Diagnostic visibility is not cluster truth.** Read-only diagnostics and artifacts do not mutate projected truth merely by being observed.
7. **Next evidence-bearing question is not next action.** The supported frontier is evidence exposure, not work selection or autonomous behavior.

## Unsupported candidate ideas

The survey does not find support for:

- a general lawful inquiry generation engine;
- autonomous curiosity;
- automatic question generation from every observation;
- semantic interpretation of natural-language observations;
- ontology or grammar recovery from examples;
- LLM prompting behavior;
- planning or next safe move recommendation;
- repository-neutral inquiry control over arbitrary repositories;
- a universal mapping from observation types to question families;
- direct promotion from bounded observation to truth, fact, requirement, goal, command, or implementation work.

## Preserved Unknowns

- Whether the phrase “lawful inquiry generation” should become repository vocabulary remains Unknown.
- Whether bounded inquiry admissibility is a standalone competency or a sub-boundary of Inquiry Navigation remains Unknown.
- Whether there is a stable finite set of constitutionally admissible question families after an observation remains Unknown.
- Whether observations can be typed in a way that lawfully narrows admissible next questions remains Unknown.
- Whether future implementation should expose a registry of admissible inquiry pressures remains Unknown.
- Whether any observation without exact surface identity can lawfully yield more than orientation remains Unknown.
- Whether diagnostic question families and question-family investigations share a single authority boundary remains Unknown.

## Confidence

**Medium.** Confidence is high that the repository supports bounded manual navigation to exact implemented evidence-bearing questions and stop conditions. Confidence is low that the repository supports a new standalone competency named “lawful inquiry generation,” because implementation evidence explicitly refuses planner behavior, natural-language routing, automated next-question engines, inquiry graph creation, and semantic next-move authority.

## Final acceptance answer

Recurring repository evidence supports a constitutional competency adjacent to lawful inquiry generation only as **bounded inquiry admissibility / Inquiry Navigation**: determining whether a next bounded, evidence-bearing question or authority-bearing surface is lawfully available after a bounded observation, while preserving the observation as evidence and stopping when no implemented or constitutionally supported surface exists.

It does not support autonomous question generation, semantic interpretation, planning, curiosity, ontology, or automatic questions for every observation. Unsupported conclusions remain Unknown.
