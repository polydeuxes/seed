## Boundary-mismatch analysis

Repository evidence does not currently establish a general validity model or implemented mismatch model. The app-visible reachability result treats this investigation's exact phrase as an unpreserved presentation label.

The smaller repository-supported meaning is:

```text
boundary mismatch = a comparison pressure or constitutional pressure state in which two preserved boundary descriptions do not yet align for the same bounded claim form, source role, jurisdiction, time, posture, and authority path.
```

A mismatch can mean any of the following weaker states:

- the legal source claims a boundary that Seed has not independently derived from source-of-law lineage;
- a court-described boundary exceeds, narrows, or differs from a separately preserved source-derived boundary;
- a precedent-derived boundary depends on cases, citation chains, or doctrine history without preserved constitutional-source support;
- source support is missing, stale, non-independent, jurisdiction-limited, temporally uncertain, procedurally posture-bound, negatively treated, or Unknown;
- the claim forms being compared are not the same.

A mismatch does not mean invalidity. It is a cue to preserve the compared claims, the support gap, the authority boundary, and the lawful stop.

## Mismatch / legal-conclusion analysis

A legal conclusion requires more than a detected tension. It requires a claim form that is legal in character and support from a competent legal authority or preserved legal-source pathway sufficient for that exact claim, jurisdiction, time, posture, and authority role.

Repository evidence repeatedly refuses promotion from visibility to truth or authority. Documentation-structure visibility does not interpret prose or infer authority. Knowledge-reachability visibility can show non-preservation without declaring that the source is wrong. Source-role evidence can preserve a source-attributed assertion without proving the assertion.

Therefore a mismatch can support only source-attributed and comparison-local statements such as:

```text
Source A claims boundary X.
Source-derived materials presently support only boundary Y, or do not yet support X.
The relation between X and Y remains Unknown / insufficient / conflicted / mismatched under the preserved evidence.
```

It cannot lawfully become:

```text
X is legally wrong.
X is invalid.
The court reached an illegal conclusion.
The doctrine is unconstitutional.
```

## Mismatch / legal-invalidity analysis

Legal invalidity is a stronger validity-related claim. Repository evidence around legal authority and source-of-law lineage blocks the inference from mismatch to invalidity because legal sources can have different roles from constitutional warrant, and a chain of cases or citations is not source-of-law lineage by itself.

A boundary mismatch may indicate:

- missing enactment, promulgation, ratification, holding, or authority path;
- negative authority not yet reviewed;
- jurisdictional or temporal mismatch;
- claim-form mismatch;
- insufficient source independence;
- unresolved contradiction or conflict.

Those are pressure states. They do not prove legal invalidity. Missing support is not disproof, and insufficiency is not an adjudication.

## Mismatch / constitutional-invalidity analysis

Constitutional invalidity and unconstitutionality require still stronger support than legal invalidity because they assert a constitutional status, not merely a source-boundary gap. Repository evidence distinguishes legal authority from constitutional warrant and court holding boundary from constitutional authority boundary.

A court-described boundary can be preserved as what the court said. A source-derived constitutional boundary would require preserved constitutional text, amendment text, source-of-law provenance, jurisdiction, temporal authority, procedural posture, holding boundary, dependency and negative authority, confidence, Unknowns, and lawful stop. A mismatch between the two can create constitutional pressure, but constitutional pressure is not a constitutional holding.

Seed therefore must not say that a doctrine, statute, regulation, opinion, or legal source is unconstitutional merely because its claimed boundary is not yet supported by a source-derived constitutional boundary.

## Mismatch / unenforceability analysis

Unenforceability is not the same as mismatch. It can depend on forum, remedy, posture, party, waiver, severability, statute, injunction, preclusion, current law, administrative action, or adjudicative order. Repository evidence does not implement or preserve an enforceability model.

A detected mismatch may support a lawful note that enforceability is Unknown for the bounded claim. It may not support a declaration that a source is unenforceable. Without competent adjudicative authority or separately preserved enforceability authority, Seed must stop at comparison pressure, Unknown, or insufficiency.

## Mismatch / historical-truth analysis

Historical truth is also stronger than mismatch. Narrative/history boundary evidence distinguishes narrative coherence, repetition, legal history, doctrine history, and source-attributed accounts from supported historical provenance or truth.

A mismatch may show that two sources narrate the boundary differently or that a claimed historical authority path is not preserved. That does not prove what historically happened. It does not prove ratification, non-ratification, enactment, non-enactment, promulgation, invalid promulgation, or the historical meaning of a constitutional source. The lawful result is source-attributed history plus Unknowns.

## Comparison-pressure / adjudicative-authority analysis

Comparison pressure is an internal review condition: preserved evidence suggests two boundaries should be compared or that a claimed boundary lacks matching support. It can warrant more careful preservation of claim form, source identity, jurisdiction, time, posture, lineage, dependency, negative authority, confidence, and Unknowns.

Adjudicative authority is an external or legal-institutional capacity to decide legal effect. Seed does not acquire adjudicative authority by detecting pressure, by observing repetition, by running diagnostics, by finding a source-derived gap, or by composing a constitutional investigation.

The distinction is:

```text
comparison pressure = reason to preserve and bound the mismatch
adjudicative authority = competent authority to decide legal consequence
```

Seed has evidence for the former in this investigation, not the latter.

## Constitutional-pressure / implementation-pressure analysis

Constitutional pressure is pressure on Seed's lawful reasoning boundary. It says Seed should avoid unsupported promotion, preserve Unknowns, calibrate claim strength, and stop before overclaiming.

Implementation pressure requires a concrete repository failure, missing diagnostic surface, incompatible output, test failure, or operational visibility gap. This investigation found no such concrete failure. It adds a report only and does not add or modify diagnostic, audit, probe, view, CLI flag, recordable output, event-ledger behavior, or mutation behavior.

Therefore constitutional pressure exists as a reasoning discipline; implementation pressure is not supported.

## Source-derived-boundary-gap analysis

A source-derived boundary gap means Seed has not preserved enough source support for the claimed boundary. It may arise from missing constitutional text, amendment text, ratification/adoption provenance, statute text, regulation promulgation path, holding boundary, procedural posture, jurisdiction, temporal authority, dependency, negative authority, source independence, claim form, or confidence.

It is not proof that the source is wrong. Repository evidence on public evidence packets, source roles, repetition/proof, and source independence all preserve this discipline: absence of sufficient support blocks stronger reliance but does not become contrary proof.

## Missing-lineage / invalid-lineage analysis

Missing source-of-law lineage means the required lineage evidence is absent, not found, not preserved, not current, or not linked to the claim form. Invalid source-of-law lineage would require a separate stronger claim that the lineage is legally defective under competent authority and preserved support.

Seed may preserve:

```text
lineage missing / lineage not preserved / lineage insufficient for this claim
```

Seed may not infer:

```text
lineage invalid / source invalid / law invalid
```

unless the invalidity claim itself has preserved authority, jurisdiction, temporal provenance, procedural posture, source-of-law support, negative authority, confidence limits, and lawful stop.

## Unknown / contradiction / insufficiency / conflict / mismatch analysis

These states must remain distinct:

- **Unknown**: the repository or packet does not preserve enough information to answer.
- **Insufficiency**: some evidence exists, but not enough for the stronger claim form.
- **Contradiction**: two preserved claims cannot both be true in the same respect, time, scope, and claim form.
- **Conflict**: sources or authorities point in different directions, but the relation may be jurisdictional, temporal, procedural, hierarchical, factual, or otherwise unresolved.
- **Mismatch**: two boundary descriptions do not align under the current comparison frame.

Unknown is not contradiction. Insufficiency is not disproof. Conflict is not invalidity. Mismatch is not legal conclusion. Contradiction, even if present, is still not invalidity without competent authority and preserved support for that stronger legal claim.

## Provenance / jurisdiction / temporal-provenance analysis

Provenance identifies where the boundary claim came from, what artifact carries it, what version was used, and what path links it to the bounded claim. Jurisdiction identifies where a source or authority can matter. Temporal provenance identifies when text, authority, observation, publication, retrieval, amendment, repeal, overruling, or preservation occurred.

Without these, a mismatch may simply reflect comparing the wrong version, wrong jurisdiction, wrong time, wrong posture, or wrong claim form. Jurisdiction and time therefore prevent overclaiming: they turn many apparent contradictions into Unknown, insufficiency, conflict, or local mismatch.
