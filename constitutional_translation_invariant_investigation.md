# Constitutional Translation Invariant Investigation

## Question

```text
When external material
is lawfully translated,

what constitutional substance
must survive
every translation?
```

## Scope discipline

This investigation does not recover implementation, recommend a slice, recommend a campaign, introduce translation engines, introduce semantic frameworks, introduce language models, introduce canonical runtime schemas, or infer implementation beyond repository evidence.

It asks only what recurring constitutional content remains invariant after lawful translation across provider languages, operator prose, question-family text, diagnostic and operational surfaces, candidate artifacts, observations, agreements, responsibility evidence, competency interrogations, and constitutional inquiry artifacts.

Repository authority wins.

## Evidence reviewed

Recurring evidence was reviewed from repository investigations and audits covering:

- Provider Language Translation and provider-grammar-to-observation-grammar investigations.
- External Artifact Intake versus Provider Language Translation.
- Observation Grammar, Relationship Observation, Repository Artifact Observation, and Observation Agreement audits.
- External Orientation and Inquiry Orientation recovery and family completion work.
- Question Eligibility / Bounded Ask and Inquiry Eligibility characterizations.
- Responsibility-family completion, Responsibility-to-Inquiry, and Responsibility Selection materials.
- Competency Interrogation grammar, methodology reconciliation, and bridge inspection materials.
- Constitutional Inquiry State and Constitutional Inquiry Self-Awareness investigations.
- Constitutional Artifact Identity characterization and transition-family characterizations.
- Bridge inspections, district surveys, and family completion audits.

The investigation uses only recurring repository evidence and preserves unsupported conclusions.

## Bounded answer

When lawful translation has completed, the recurring constitutional substance that survives is:

```text
evidence-bound, authority-reduced, provenance-preserving, role-bounded constitutional content
that remains lawful for its downstream consumer and refuses unsupported promotion.
```

In shorter form:

```text
bounded warranted content with provenance, role, authority limit, and preserved non-promotion.
```

This is not a runtime schema, not a universal object, not a semantic engine, not a prompt representation, not a codec, and not a provider implementation pattern.

It is the constitutional residue repeatedly visible when repository material crosses lawful boundaries: selected identity, selected relation or value, provenance/support, confidence or evidence strength where applicable, dimensions or locality where applicable, granted authority, negative authority, and preserved unknowns. External form may change; constitutional warrant must not.

## Candidate characterization

### Identity

```text
What constitutional substance
is recurring?
```

The recurring substance is not external wording, provider syntax, parser shape, record class, presentation label, or implementation artifact. It is the warranted constitutional content that a lawful downstream surface may still consume after translation.

Across provider translation, the recurring work selects provider vocabulary, validates or decodes provider grammar, shapes identity, assigns Seed predicates or bounded record fields, constructs a canonical `Observation` where applicable, or constructs another bounded record where canonical observation is not the path. The supported identity is therefore not the provider syntax but the selected bounded content that can lawfully survive into Seed output.

Across observation grammar, authority decreases from provider-native expressiveness to bounded structural evidence and then, where applicable, to canonical observations. The material that survives is selected identity, bounded structural relation, selected measured or declared value, time/provenance, confidence, and dimensions.

Across external orientation, externally supplied or externally declared material is admitted only into private, narrow preparation artifacts and then consumed by existing bounded owners. The surviving substance is not operator command or truth; it is preserved orientation or prepared eligibility material with the public compatibility surface unchanged.

Across question eligibility and bounded ask, possible or attractive question text survives only if it is translated into exact implemented question-family identity, eligibility, required arguments, dispatchability, diagnostic-only status, or bounded stop. The invariant is not prose similarity; it is exact lawful surface identity plus authority boundary.

Across responsibility and competency investigations, the recurring substance is subject identity bound to evidence, inputs/preconditions, outputs/handoffs, boundaries/locality, granted authority, negative authority, preserved unknowns, lawful reliance, stop conditions, and confidence calibration.

Across constitutional inquiry, the recurring substance is the inquiry's evidence-bound, authority-bound, boundary-disciplined, unknown-preserving, confidence-calibrated lawful condition. When further movement would exceed that content, lawful termination follows.

### Translation Boundary

```text
What may change
during lawful translation?

What must not change?
```

#### What may change

Lawful translation may change:

- external wording;
- provider syntax;
- parser-specific grammar;
- field names;
- record shape;
- presentation format;
- private handoff artifact;
- diagnostic or table rendering;
- JSON arrangement where compatibility permits;
- candidate phrasing;
- local implementation vocabulary;
- compression level between decoding and output construction;
- whether the output is canonical `Observation` or another bounded record.

Repository evidence repeatedly shows that provider-native forms can become `PackageRecord`, `RelationshipFact`, `RepositoryArtifactFact`, `RepositoryObservation`, canonical `Observation`, diagnostic rows, orientation views, eligibility inputs, or completion/audit artifacts. Those forms differ. Form sameness is not the invariant.

#### What must not change

Lawful translation must not change:

- the evidence actually supporting the claim;
- the selected subject or local identity at the supported resolution;
- the role of the artifact or content inside its owning family;
- provenance/support needed to know why the content is warranted;
- the authority boundary that limits use;
- negative authority refusing unsupported promotion;
- unknowns that evidence did not resolve;
- confidence or evidence-strength calibration where the surface carries it;
- locality and dimensions where they are part of the supported content;
- read-only or non-mutating boundaries where declared;
- whether the content is presentation, orientation, support, admission, diagnostic visibility, observation, evidence, fact, projection, candidate, verification, readiness, agreement, or constitutional inquiry result.

The invariant is therefore constitutional warrant under boundary, not representation.

### Negative Authority

```text
What does lawful translation
explicitly refuse
to preserve?
```

Recurring negative authority is strong.

Lawful translation refuses to preserve external authority merely because material was external, visible, repeated, or well-formed. It refuses:

- provider-native excess meaning not selected by the adapter;
- malformed or unsupported provider content;
- arbitrary fields, raw syntax, raw stdout, filenames, exact status pairs, remote details, stderr, and return-code details after availability decisions where current paths intentionally discard them;
- behavior, calls, routes, boundaries, ownership, invocation, reachability, capability authority, runtime ownership, repository scanning, graph building, LLM reconciliation, and runtime/tool integration from Python relationship extraction;
- content interpretation, responsibility recovery, lexicon ownership, event-ledger writes, repository mutation, and cluster mutation from repository artifact observation;
- service health, desired state, operator intent, and ownership from systemd unit observations;
- arbitrary PromQL, non-vector Prometheus responses, malformed samples, missing instances, and unsupported endpoint-scoped fact promotion;
- operator prose becoming fact, claim, goal, requirement, ownership, intent, recommendation, command, authorization, plan, observation, event-ledger entry, or cluster truth;
- question vocabulary becoming runnable work unless exact implemented question-family eligibility and required arguments are present;
- observation agreement becoming truth, grammar, responsibility, lexicon, semantic alignment, event, ledger, repository mutation, runtime mutation, or cluster mutation;
- visibility, inventory presence, verification-like output, recommendation-like output, diagnostic accessibility, or recurrence becoming permission;
- presentation vocabulary becoming repository knowledge without implementation evidence;
- unsupported gaps becoming plans, recommendations, owners, schemas, frameworks, or implementation recoveries.

The refusal is part of what survives. Lawful translation preserves not only positive content but also the boundary of what was not carried forward.

### Resolution

```text
Determine whether the recurring substance exists primarily at:

Implementation Resolution
Neighborhood Resolution
District Resolution
Constitutional Resolution
Unsupported
```

The recurring translation invariant exists primarily at **Constitutional Resolution**.

Support:

- Implementation evidence shows many local translations, but they are uneven and use different forms. Some are separated by intermediate records; some are compressed; some produce canonical observations; some produce non-observation records. That variation prevents the invariant from being primarily an implementation-resolution object.
- Neighborhood evidence shows repeated local physiologies such as External Orientation preparation, Observation Agreement convergence, Question Eligibility admission, and diagnostic inventory composition. These neighborhoods preserve the pattern but do not contain the whole invariant.
- District evidence shows broader pressure around lawful inquiry admission, competency self-governance, responsibility-to-inquiry answerability, and operational/diagnostic visibility. These districts explain why lawful translation matters but still do not reduce the invariant to one district owner.
- Constitutional inquiry evidence repeatedly composes the same safeguards: evidence binding, subject identity, authority boundary, boundary discipline, unknown preservation, negative authority, lawful reliance, stop conditions, and confidence calibration. Those safeguards recur across implementation, neighborhood, district, audit, bridge, and inquiry artifacts.

Therefore the supported resolution is Constitutional Resolution: the invariant is the constitutional warrant that survives local translations, not a shared runtime type, implementation owner, universal grammar, or district-specific framework.

In one sentence:

That is what is still the same thing after every lawful translation: not the language, not the syntax, not the presentation, not the record class, not the operator's wording, and not a canonical runtime schema, but the repository-authorized constitutional content that remains lawful to rely on and unlawful to overpromote.

**High confidence** that external form, provider grammar, operator wording, presentation labels, intermediate record shape, and canonical runtime schema are not the invariant.
