# Roadmap Responsibility Characterization

## Executive answer

Across the recovered roadmap artifacts, Roadmap consistently owns **preserved orientation for future work under repository authority**: it gathers already-earned evidence, unresolved pressure, candidate next slices, sequencing constraints, non-goals, and authority boundaries so future operators can resume work without re-deriving the whole local context from memory.

That responsibility has remained stable, but its vocabulary has evolved. Earlier roadmap artifacts enumerate candidate work and boundaries; later reconciliations classify that work into knowledge acquisition, integrity, selection, and methodology-adjacent pressure. The stable core is not planning authority. The stable core is **continuity of justified direction**: preserving what work is plausible next, why it is plausible, what evidence supports it, what must not be inferred, and what separate artifact or implementation surface must authorize actual execution.

Repository evidence does **not** justify a new Roadmap responsibility. The existing Roadmap family has already recovered its constitutional responsibility as a documentation and orientation artifact. If today's conversation disappeared, existing artifacts would still recover Roadmap's purpose: preserve candidate future work and boundaries without becoming a planner, approval gate, scheduler, workflow engine, or implementation authority.

## Roadmap history

### Reasoning Roadmap

`docs/reasoning_roadmap.md` presents the accepted runtime chain from observations through facts, relationships, explanations, tool needs, capability resolution, operation candidates, and provider/handoff recommendations. It explicitly states that runtime ownership remains canonical, `ToolExecutor` is the only registered-operation executor, `ProjectionStore` owns cached projected state snapshots, and `EventLedger` owns append-only events.

Its Roadmap role is not to execute that chain. Its role is to preserve future foundation areas without adding runtime behavior, planning orchestration, workflow execution, host mutation, network calls, shell execution, or LLM-driven projection logic.

The recurring responsibility visible here is: preserve future work by knowledge concern while protecting architecture boundaries.

### Local Observation Roadmap Audit

`docs/local_observation_roadmap_audit.md` reviews the existing local observation implementation, catalogs current facts, identifies candidate local observation domains, and records boundaries and non-goals. It preserves candidate future observation domains such as mounts, kernel, CPU, memory, users, groups, environment, certificates, storage topology, schedules, systemd units, listening ports, processes, packages, and services.

Its Roadmap role is not to implement those observations. It is to externalize the operator's candidate-domain memory and the negative-space claims needed to keep later implementation narrow.

### Local Observation Roadmap Reconciliation

`docs/local_observation_roadmap_reconciliation.md` revises the order of candidate local observation work. It moves storage topology and listening ports ahead of packages and systemd units, introduces hostname/identity as a possible first slice, and preserves why machine-descriptive knowledge should precede implementation-detail inventories.

This shows evolution in ordering and classification, not evolution into planner authority. Roadmap may preserve relative sequence arguments, but the document remains documentation-only and does not implement behavior.

### Roadmap Reconciliation

`docs/roadmap_reconciliation.md` consolidates roadmap pressure around capability verification, reasoning expansion, context composition, and later work. It aligns future work with already-recovered architecture and distinguishes current implementation status from candidate follow-on work.

The recurring responsibility is preservation of unresolved direction after reconciliation, not creation of a stronger authority than the implementation and architecture already provide.

### Roadmap and Methodology Reconciliation

`docs/roadmap_and_methodology_reconciliation.md` makes the boundary explicit: roadmap/status can preserve implementation pressure, audit/reconciliation, vocabulary stabilization, and narrower implementation guidance, but it should not become governance, mandatory process, review gate, approval workflow, planner, or project-management machinery.

This artifact is the strongest evidence that Roadmap has already recovered its constitutional discipline. It can host methodology notes and candidate sequencing, but it does not authorize work by itself.

### Seed Competency Roadmaps

`seed_competency_roadmap.md` and `seed_competency_roadmap_v2.md` preserve competency-oriented future work and transitions. They participate in the same responsibility: externalize current candidate directions and staged competency maturation so they do not remain only in operator memory.

They do not by themselves prove implementation conformance or authorize execution.

## Recurring responsibility

Every roadmap artifact attempts to preserve some version of this responsibility:

```text
current repository evidence and unresolved pressure
↓
candidate future work or ordering
↓
boundaries, non-goals, and authority limits
↓
recoverable operator orientation for the next bounded inquiry or slice
```

More compactly:

```text
Roadmap preserves justified future orientation without becoming implementation authority.
```

Roadmap preserves information no other artifact preserves as a whole:

- candidate future work grouped across multiple adjacent families;
- why a candidate is plausible now;
- relative ordering or triage arguments;
- the negative-space boundaries that future implementation must respect;
- the distinction between work that is ready for a slice, work that needs reconciliation, and work that remains candidate pressure;
- continuity between completed audits and future attention.

If all roadmap documents were deleted, the operator would immediately have to reload into working memory:

- which candidate observation, reasoning, selection, and integrity slices had already been identified;
- why some candidates should precede others;
- which interpretations were explicitly forbidden;
- which completed audits feed future work;
- where future work should remain documentation-only until implementation evidence exists;
- how to avoid re-inventing planner/governance/project-management structures already rejected by repository evidence.

## Has the responsibility remained stable?

Yes. The responsibility has remained stable at the level of authority and purpose.

It has evolved in expression:

1. **Enumeration stage** — Roadmap listed candidate domains and future slices.
2. **Boundary stage** — Roadmap preserved non-goals and negative-space claims.
3. **Triage stage** — Roadmap grouped future work by knowledge concern, such as acquisition, integrity, and selection.
4. **Methodology-adjacent stage** — Roadmap recognized a recurring audit/reconciliation/vocabulary-before-implementation pattern while explicitly refusing governance or planner authority.

This is maturation, not responsibility replacement.

## Constitutional authority

Roadmap possesses documentation authority only:

- It may preserve candidate future work.
- It may preserve ordering rationale.
- It may preserve unresolved pressure and cross-links to completed audits.
- It may preserve boundaries and non-goals.
- It may preserve methodology notes that explain why the repository often pauses for audit, reconciliation, or vocabulary stabilization.
- It may point to implementation, tests, audits, Constitution, Ontology, Frontier, Hit List, Slice, and Completion Audit as authorities for their own domains.

Roadmap must explicitly not possess:

- implementation authority;
- execution authority;
- cluster mutation authority;
- fact-promotion authority;
- projection authority;
- planner authority;
- scheduler authority;
- goal-engine authority;
- workflow approval authority;
- governance/review-gate authority;
- authority to make presentation vocabulary into repository knowledge without implementation evidence.

## Roadmap compared with key families

### Roadmap vs Frontier

Frontier records unresolved edges and pressure. Roadmap preserves recoverable future orientation across those edges after enough evidence exists to describe plausible candidate work, ordering, and boundaries. Frontier can be more tentative and pressure-shaped; Roadmap is more stabilized and continuity-shaped.

Frontier does not own Roadmap's responsibility because frontier artifacts preserve open edges, while roadmap artifacts preserve candidate future paths and non-goals across multiple edges.

### Roadmap vs Hit List

Hit List names concrete recovery targets. Roadmap preserves the broader reason those targets matter, candidate sequencing among them, and boundaries that apply across work families. Hit List can be an input to Roadmap, but it does not preserve the same cross-family continuity.

### Roadmap vs Investigation

Investigation answers a bounded question. Roadmap preserves what future work remains after multiple investigations, audits, and reconciliations. Investigation can produce Roadmap evidence, but Roadmap does not replace the investigation's evidentiary burden.

### Roadmap vs Slice

Slice is implementation-backed, code/test-producing work. Roadmap is documentation-backed orientation toward possible slices. A roadmap item becomes a slice only when a narrow implementation seam, acceptance behavior, and testable boundary exist.

### Roadmap vs Completion Audit

Completion Audit decides whether a bounded family is complete or where residual pressure belongs. Roadmap preserves future orientation after completion audits, especially when remaining pressure crosses families or should not be forgotten.

## Relationship to Methodological Selection

Methodological Selection has been operating **beside Roadmap and partially through Roadmap**, not as the same responsibility.

Roadmap preserves candidate inquiries and sequencing rationale. It does not select the next inquiry with implementation authority. `operator_methodological_selection_characterization.md` already finds that the operator performs decisive pre-inquiry selection: deciding which unresolved pressure becomes the next bounded investigation, choosing the subject, judging readiness, and mapping the subject into an exact ask. Existing implementation begins after the choice is supplied or operates inside a bounded domain.

Therefore the supported relationship is not:

```text
Roadmap
↓
Methodological Selection
↓
Investigation
```

as an implementation-owned pipeline.

The supported relationship is:

```text
Pressure / Frontier / Completion Audit / Roadmap / Methodology notes
↓
operator methodological selection
↓
bounded Inquiry or Slice
↓
Investigation, implementation, or audit result
↓
Roadmap / Frontier / Hit List / Completion Audit update when warranted
```

Roadmap participates by preserving candidate inquiries, evidence, ordering, and boundaries. Methodological Selection is the operator-side act of deciding which candidate or pressure becomes the next bounded ask. They are two interacting responsibilities, not one recovered owner.

## Promotion paths

Roadmap promotion paths observed or supported by repository evidence:

1. **Investigation to Roadmap** — A bounded investigation identifies durable future work, ordering, or boundary constraints broader than one slice.
2. **Completion Audit to Roadmap** — A completion audit closes a family but leaves adjacent-family or future-phase pressure that should remain recoverable.
3. **Frontier to Roadmap** — A frontier edge stabilizes into recurring candidate work with enough evidence and non-goals to preserve as future orientation.
4. **Hit List to Roadmap** — A concrete target reveals broader recurring work or sequencing beyond one immediate recovery item.
5. **Roadmap to Slice** — A candidate becomes implementation work only after a narrow code seam, acceptance criteria, and tests exist.
6. **Roadmap to Investigation** — A candidate remains uncertain and needs bounded evidence before implementation.
7. **Roadmap to Ontology** — Repeated vocabulary may become ontological only when implementation or cross-document evidence supports the distinction.

## Retirement paths

Roadmap items retire when:

- implemented and verified by a slice and tests;
- closed by a completion audit;
- reclassified as Frontier pressure because evidence is still too unstable;
- reclassified as Hit List because a concrete recovery target exists;
- reclassified as Investigation because the next step is evidentiary, not implementational;
- rejected as unsupported by counterexample evidence;
- superseded by stronger implementation evidence or constitutional clarification.

Roadmap itself does not retire items by executing them. It records why the item is no longer a roadmap concern once another authority has resolved or reclassified it.

## Counterexamples searched

### Counterexample: Roadmap already functions as a planner

Not supported. Roadmap artifacts may preserve candidate ordering and triage, but they repeatedly disclaim planner, workflow, governance, orchestration, and runtime authority. Ordering rationale is recoverable orientation, not an executing planner.

### Counterexample: Roadmap already authorizes implementation

Not supported. Roadmap can identify candidate slices and constraints, but implementation authority appears only through code changes, tests, runtime surfaces, and explicitly authorized execution paths. Roadmap documents commonly state they are documentation-only.

### Counterexample: Frontier already owns Roadmap's responsibility

Not supported. Frontier preserves open edges and pressure; Roadmap preserves stabilized future orientation, candidate sequencing, and non-goals. The two overlap as inputs and outputs, but they do not collapse.

### Counterexample: Hit List already preserves Roadmap information

Partially but insufficiently supported. Hit Lists preserve targets, but not the full cross-family rationale, ordering, negative-space boundaries, or candidate maturity preserved by Roadmap.

### Counterexample: existing roadmap artifacts do not share a recurring responsibility

Not supported. The artifacts consistently preserve future-oriented continuity under boundary discipline, even when their local subject differs.

### Counterexample: Roadmap and Methodological Selection are one responsibility

Not supported. Roadmap preserves candidates and rationale; Methodological Selection chooses the next bounded inquiry, currently as an operator activity informed by many artifacts and only partially supported by implementation-visible selection surfaces.

## Answers to recovery questions

1. **What responsibility has Roadmap consistently owned?** Roadmap owns recoverable, evidence-constrained future orientation: candidate work, sequencing rationale, unresolved pressure, and non-goals.

2. **Has that responsibility evolved?** Yes in vocabulary and maturity, from candidate enumeration to boundary-preserving triage and methodology-adjacent explanation. No in authority: it remains documentation/orientation, not implementation/planning authority.

3. **What constitutional authority does Roadmap possess?** It possesses documentation authority to preserve justified candidate direction and boundaries under repository evidence.

4. **What constitutional authority must it explicitly not possess?** It must not authorize implementation, mutate state, promote facts, select truth, schedule work, approve work, execute plans, or convert presentation vocabulary into knowledge without evidence.

5. **What recurring operator local state does Roadmap externalize?** It externalizes the operator's memory of what future work is plausible, why, in what rough order, under which constraints, after which audits, and with which non-goals.

6. **How does Roadmap differ from Frontier, Hit List, Investigation, Slice, and Completion Audit?** Frontier preserves open edges; Hit List preserves concrete targets; Investigation answers bounded questions; Slice changes implementation; Completion Audit closes or reassigns scoped work. Roadmap preserves durable future orientation across those artifacts.

7. **Has Methodological Selection been operating through Roadmap or beside it?** Beside it, with Roadmap as one important input. Roadmap can preserve candidate inquiries and rationale, but it does not own decisive next-inquiry selection.

8. **Does repository evidence support `Roadmap -> Methodological Selection -> Investigation`?** Not as an implementation-owned pipeline. It supports a broader operator-mediated loop where Roadmap, Frontier, Pressure, Completion Audit, and Methodology notes inform operator selection, which then invokes Investigation or Slice.

9. **Did the repository already recover this responsibility and current operator local state simply failed to reload it?** Yes. Existing roadmap and methodology reconciliation artifacts already recover the responsibility and explicitly reject stronger planner/governance authority.

10. **Is there sufficient repository evidence to justify any new Roadmap responsibility?** Insufficient repository evidence.

## Supported conclusions

- Roadmap is a documentation artifact with constitutional discipline, not an implementation authority.
- Roadmap preserves future orientation that would otherwise live in operator working memory.
- Roadmap's stable responsibility is continuity of justified direction under boundaries.
- Roadmap has matured from lists of candidate work toward triaged, boundary-preserving, methodology-aware orientation.
- Methodological Selection interacts with Roadmap but is not owned by Roadmap.
- No new artifact family or new Roadmap responsibility is justified by the reviewed evidence.

## Unsupported conclusions

- Roadmap is a planner.
- Roadmap authorizes implementation.
- Roadmap selects the next inquiry with implementation authority.
- Frontier or Hit List fully subsume Roadmap.
- Methodological Selection has already become an implementation-owned Roadmap subresponsibility.
- Presentation labels such as current work position or active edge are automatically repository knowledge.
- A new roadmap format, workflow engine, goal engine, scheduler, priority system, or artifact family is justified.
