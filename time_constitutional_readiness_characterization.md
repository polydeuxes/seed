## Recurring constitutional observations

### 1. Time evidence is organized by boundary, not by owner

The recovered Time evidence repeatedly separates temporal meanings by authority boundary:

- external occurrence is not Seed observation;
- Seed observation is not upstream source assertion;
- source sample timestamp is not automatically occurrence time;
- knowledge / justification time is not ledger preservation;
- preservation time is not external occurrence;
- a temporal claim is supported and revisable rather than a raw timestamp.

The higher-order Time reports make this recurrence visible, but they do not recover a Time district owner, universal timing framework, scheduler, bridge, dispatch surface, or central clock.

### 2. Operational visibility timing is not constitutional readiness

Diagnostic elapsed timing, progress cadence, and cache visibility recur as Seed-facing visibility surfaces. They expose local durations, transient status emission, and cache path explanation. They do not decide whether work is constitutionally lawful to begin.

This matters because an operational surface can say that work was measured, displayed, or replayed without proving that the work had a constitutional readiness condition before it began.

### 3. Temporal support does not become temporal authority by appearing

Claim/support separation inside Time reasoning shows that observation timestamps, source sample timestamps, knowledge timing, and preservation events may support a temporal claim. They do not automatically become the claim, the external occurrence, or the authority that starts lawful work.

### 4. Characterization recurrence expects missing responsibilities to remain visible

Across Characterization reports, missing ownership, unsupported promotion, and authority gaps are not repaired by vocabulary. They are preserved as unknowns or negative authority until evidence supplies a competent local boundary.

The absence of implementation-local ownership therefore can be a legitimate characterized result, not a failure of the investigation.

### 5. Constitutional readiness requires a present lawful condition, not just time evidence

The reviewed constitutional warrant and readiness evidence repeatedly distinguishes:

- evidence from movement;
- pressure from command;
- readiness from authority;
- candidate from admission;
- visibility from mutation;
- present lawful condition from behavior;
- current status from implementation runtime state.

Before lawful work may begin, recurring evidence requires a present, bounded, warranted condition that says movement is lawful for this subject now, under preserved authority limits, or else a lawful stop / continued Null condition.

## Responsibilities explicitly not owned by existing neighborhoods

### Occurrence / external-reality time

Does not own readiness. It names or concerns when an external condition happened. It is often unavailable directly to Seed and cannot authorize work merely by being the target of a claim.

### Observation / collection time

Does not own readiness. It records when evidence was observed, sampled, read, reported, ingested, or accepted. It is provenance, not occurrence authority and not permission to begin work.

### Source sample / source-asserted time

Does not own readiness. It supplies upstream timestamp evidence and clock-authority questions. It may support claims but does not itself authorize movement or implementation.

### Knowledge / justification time

Does not own readiness. It concerns when Seed became justified in using support. It is close to evidence entitlement, but reviewed evidence does not make it a pre-work gate for every Time movement or a universal readiness owner.

### Preservation / event-ledger time

Does not own readiness. It records Seed's preservation act in append-only history. It is not external occurrence authority and does not decide whether future work may begin.

### Temporal claim / revision time

Does not own readiness. It organizes support into revisable statements about time. It may be the subject of readiness review, but it is not recovered as the owner of readiness, movement authorization, scheduling, or implementation.

### Diagnostic elapsed / phase timing

Does not own readiness. It measures local diagnostic or projection work after or during execution. It is not a constitutional precondition for lawful work.

### Progress cadence / status timing

Does not own readiness. It gates transient status visibility by elapsed wall-clock cadence. It is not dispatch, scheduling, cadence implementation, movement authorization, or lawful-work selection.

### Cache visibility time

Does not own readiness. It explains cache path, snapshot identity, hit/miss, incremental replay, and events applied. It is not a universal Time topology, readiness gate, or implementation owner.
