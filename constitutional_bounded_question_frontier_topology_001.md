# Constitutional Bounded Question Frontier Topology 001

## Question and stopping boundary

This investigation asks what constitutional standings separate question-worthy uncertainty, question formation, question establishment, question-frontier membership, selection for present movement, examination eligibility, and examination performance.

Repository authority supports a bounded topology, not a universal question engine. The exact stopping boundary is:

> Stop before converting unresolved pressure, vocabulary similarity, caller-authored fields, frontier membership, or eligibility into a produced question, priority order, execution queue, authorization, active examination, knowledge admission, or retirement unless an implementation-backed artifact performs that exact transition.

No implementation, tests, Book content, circulation maps, or existing reports were modified. This report is testimony over existing implementation and tests.

## Executive topology

The repository supports the following compressed but distinguishable roads:

1. **Unresolved pressure / advancement evidence** exists in bounded-goal and horizon families, but is not automatically question-worthy uncertainty.
2. **Explicit bounded-question formation / preservation** exists through `BoundedConstitutionalQuestion`, but it preserves caller material as testimony rather than proving goal-relative origination.
3. **Established bounded question as local artifact** exists only in the limited sense that explicit fields are bound to deterministic identity and read-only boundaries; goal-relative question standing remains unrealized.
4. **Registered-view routing and composition** exists as a separate selected-view road driven by exact caller-supplied selection keys; it is not examination admission and not recovered evidence demand.
5. **Bounded inquiry frontier** exists only after a selected inquiry need and boundary testimony; it explicitly does not formulate questions, open inquiry, select sources, authorize access, or start execution.
6. **Examination frontier** exists as a read-only classification of supplied corpus/work visibility. It can classify work as eligible, examined, blocked, unsupported, deferred, failed, unknown, or conflict, but it is not a scheduler, queue, priority ranking, or campaign-completion verdict.
7. **Examination policy projection** preserves policy testimony over an immutable frontier and method-applicability projection; it does not select work.
8. **Examination work selection** may select at most one frontier-eligible work item for present movement, preserves non-selected alternatives, and refuses arbitrary tie-breaking. Selection still does not authorize, schedule, execute, or mark examined.
9. **Examination probe request binding** is an additional boundary after selection. It requires exactly one selected item, matching frontier/candidate/method/applicability/capability evidence, and applicable method standing. It remains read-only and still does not choose a provider, operation, authorization decision, pending action, or execution state.
10. **Examination performance** is not established by the above roads. Existing completed results may cause a frontier item to be classified as examined, but the frontier projection itself does not perform examination or admit the result as knowledge.

## Supported standings and transitions

### 1. Unresolved pressure visibility

- **Consumed standing:** Bounded-goal, horizon, need, sufficiency, or other local unresolved evidence.
- **Constitutional effect:** Preserves Unknowns, conflicts, gaps, insufficiency, excluded scope, stale/unavailable evidence, and stop conditions as local evidence.
- **Resulting standing:** Pressure or uncertainty visibility, not question standing.
- **Required evidence and authority:** Exact artifact-local fields and lineage; repository reports identify distributed bounded-goal owners rather than one canonical goal object.
- **Lawful negative, deferment, or stop:** Unknown, conflicting, insufficient-for-now, refused horizon, missing identity, or local stop without question production.
- **Implementation witness:** Bridge report identifies unresolveds preserved in `BoundedOperatorGoalEstablishment`, `GoalOrientationInventory`, `GoalInquiryConsiderationSelection`, `BoundedAdvancementHorizon`, `GoalAdvancementNeedSet`, and `GoalAdvancementSufficiencyProjection`.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** The same bridge report says this proves unresolved pressure can be represented but does not prove any unresolved pressure is question-worthy uncertainty or bounded-question standing.
- **Remaining Unknown:** What implemented future act, if any, could evaluate pressure into question-worthy uncertainty.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported but unrealized as a question-producing transition.

### 2. Explicit bounded-question formation and preservation

- **Consumed standing:** Explicit caller-supplied operator inquiry, provenance, bounded question text, constitutional intent, scope status, uncertainty, Unknowns, and optional caller fields.
- **Constitutional effect:** Produces an immutable deterministic `BoundedConstitutionalQuestion` artifact preserving supplied material as evidence/testimony.
- **Resulting standing:** Locally formed and identity-bound bounded-question artifact.
- **Required evidence and authority:** Explicit caller inputs only; no natural-language classification or authority discovery.
- **Lawful negative, deferment, or stop:** Refuses fact promotion, verified claim promotion, constitutional authority creation, repository truth, durable knowledge, capability creation, view selection, projection production, event-ledger writes, and cluster mutation.
- **Implementation witness:** `seed_runtime/bounded_constitutional_question.py` defines immutable fields, read-only boundaries, deterministic identity payload, and `produce_bounded_constitutional_question()`.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** The road-standing audit says tests directly construct bounded questions and prove preservation/identity, but do not prove lawful origination from a bounded goal.
- **Remaining Unknown:** Whether a goal-relative source can lawfully establish the question rather than preserving caller testimony.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported and implemented as explicit preservation; constitutionally over-promoted if treated as goal-relative establishment.

### 3. Goal-relative bounded-question establishment

- **Consumed standing:** Would require exact bounded-goal identity, current position, fulfilled/blocked/deferred work, unresolved pressure, evidence territory, remaining/negative authority, Unknowns/conflicts, and stop conditions.
- **Constitutional effect:** Would distinguish question-worthy uncertainty from pressure and establish question standing for a goal.
- **Resulting standing:** Not implemented.
- **Required evidence and authority:** The bridge report lists exact goal identity, horizon/current-position lineage, fulfilled-work lineage, evidence territory, provenance, and authority/stop evidence as necessary.
- **Lawful negative, deferment, or stop:** Refuse or preserve Unknown when asked to treat bounded-goal pressure, current position, unresolved work, Unknowns, sufficiency reasons, or caller-authored selection keys as an established next bounded question.
- **Implementation witness:** Negative witness: the bridge report found no live consumer that produces a `BoundedConstitutionalQuestion` from bounded-goal state.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** `selection_key` is caller-authored registered-view routing, not evidence demand or question origination.
- **Remaining Unknown:** Whether such an establishment act should exist, and what owner would implement it.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported but unrealized.

### 4. Registered-view projection, selection, adapter, and composition road

- **Consumed standing:** A supplied bounded-question artifact and exact caller-supplied fields named `selection_key` or `selection_key:*`.
- **Constitutional effect:** Projects exact keys, selects registered constitutional views by exact set intersection, adapts selected names to composition, and composes registered read-model views.
- **Resulting standing:** Selected registered-view names and a composition request/result, not examination standing.
- **Required evidence and authority:** Registered capability keys and explicit selected names; unsupported keys are preserved as uncertainty.
- **Lawful negative, deferment, or stop:** Empty selection, unsupported keys, Unknowns, composition failure, no mutation.
- **Implementation witness:** Road-standing audit describes `project_constitutional_question()`, `select_constitutional_views()`, `selected_constitutional_views_to_composition_request()`, and composition as a deterministic exact-key handoff road.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** The audit states projection does not read question text, recover pressure, inspect current work, derive method, discover territory, or infer evidence demand.
- **Remaining Unknown:** Whether selected views answer the bounded constitutional question.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported and implemented locally; over-promoted if treated as question origination or examination admission.

### 5. Bounded inquiry frontier establishment

- **Consumed standing:** Selected inquiry need plus preserved boundary testimony clauses.
- **Constitutional effect:** Establishes a bounded inquiry frontier only when required clause families are present and no material binding conflict defeats it.
- **Resulting standing:** Established frontier, missing-required-clause state, or material-binding-conflict state.
- **Required evidence and authority:** Exact selected need identity, native item/goal/horizon lineage, testimony identity, preserved/operative clause refs, and required clause families.
- **Lawful negative, deferment, or stop:** Missing required clause family, material conflict, unsupported/unknown/mixed/adjacent/stale/unavailable/out-of-scope clauses preserved as non-operative or classified references.
- **Implementation witness:** `tests/test_bounded_inquiry_frontier.py` asserts exact identity binding, missing clause refusal, conflict handling, preservation of non-operative clauses, and read-only/no-execution boundaries.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** The same tests assert the frontier does not formulate questions, open inquiry, select sources, select observations, authorize access, start execution, start recording, write the event ledger, mutate the cluster, or know the result.
- **Remaining Unknown:** Whether this frontier can admit multiple bounded constitutional questions; current implementation is selected-need/frontier specific, not a multi-question frontier registry.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported and implemented for selected inquiry-need frontier; not a question producer, backlog, or queue.

### 6. Candidate examination work visibility

- **Consumed standing:** Bounded corpus members, examination work contracts, and caller-supplied contract visibility.
- **Constitutional effect:** Preserves candidate work visibility.
- **Resulting standing:** Candidate work records suitable for later frontier projection.
- **Required evidence and authority:** Corpus identity and contract/provenance visibility.
- **Lawful negative, deferment, or stop:** Observation-only output; no frontier classification or authorization.
- **Implementation witness:** `tests/test_candidate_examination_work.py` checks candidate observations are not frontier classifications or authorization and forbids eligible/examined/blocked/deferred/failed/selected/priority/authorized vocabulary in that output.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Candidate work can adapt to frontier input, but is not itself frontier classification.
- **Remaining Unknown:** Whether candidate generation is complete for any corpus.
- **Classification:** Implemented locally; constitutionally over-promoted if treated as eligibility or selection.

### 7. Examination frontier membership and eligibility

- **Consumed standing:** A structurally valid bounded inquiry, explicit corpus members, and explicit candidate work records.
- **Constitutional effect:** Projects a read-only frontier and classifies each work item as eligible, examined, blocked, unsupported, deferred, failed, unknown, or conflict.
- **Resulting standing:** Frontier-admitted classified work item; eligibility is one classification among others.
- **Required evidence and authority:** Matching corpus member ids, unique candidate ids, capability id for compatible work, existing-result consistency, compatibility status, authorization status, blockers, deferral testimony, failure references, supplied status, and Unknowns.
- **Lawful negative, deferment, or stop:** Duplicate ids, unknown corpus member, existing-result mismatch, unsupported classification, blocked classification, deferred classification, failed classification, unknown classification, conflict classification.
- **Implementation witness:** `seed_runtime/examination_frontier.py` implements classifications and boundary notes; tests assert eligible items lack `priority_rank` and `selected`, classify blocked/unsupported/deferred/unknown/conflict separately, preserve read-only/no-mutation behavior, and forbid queue/scheduler/priority fields.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** The frontier notes say eligible work has not been selected, authorized, or executed and that the frontier is not a scheduler, work queue, priority ranking, or completion verdict.
- **Remaining Unknown:** Whether a frontier item should later be selected; membership alone refuses that inference.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported and implemented.

### 8. Examination method applicability and policy projection

- **Consumed standing:** Bounded question, candidate work/frontier visibility, method-applicability testimony, and resolution-policy testimony.
- **Constitutional effect:** Preserves method applicability and projects policy sufficiency over a frontier without selecting work.
- **Resulting standing:** Applicable/inapplicable/unknown/conflict method or policy projection; possible selector handoff.
- **Required evidence and authority:** Matching inquiry, frontier, applicability projection references; supported policy kind; explicit work identity, all-eligible-no-order, prerequisite-first, or no-selection testimony; tie treatment and prerequisites when relevant.
- **Lawful negative, deferment, or stop:** Absent testimony -> unknown; conflicting testimony -> conflict; mismatched frontier/applicability -> inapplicable; missing explicit work id -> unknown; multiple eligible without lawful tie-break -> insufficient; explicit no-selection -> no selection.
- **Implementation witness:** `seed_runtime/examination_policy_projection.py` defines policy kinds, states, sufficiency states, tie treatments, and boundary notes refusing selection, authorization, scheduling, execution, semantic truth, priority scores, or knowledge admission.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Policy projection can be applicable but insufficient for a unique lawful selection.
- **Remaining Unknown:** Whether a policy is normatively best; implementation only preserves supported policy testimony.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported and implemented as pre-selection policy projection.

### 9. Selection for present examination movement

- **Consumed standing:** Examination frontier, examination policy projection, and exact selector handoff.
- **Constitutional effect:** Selects at most one eligible work item when policy is applicable and sufficient, or produces lawful no-selection/unknown/conflict/invalid states.
- **Resulting standing:** `ExaminationWorkSelection` with selected work reference or no selected reference, preserved non-selected eligible alternatives, excluded work, unchanged blocked/unsupported/deferred/examined/failed/conflict/unknown references, and optional future probe-request handoff.
- **Required evidence and authority:** Handoff must match policy/frontier ids, policy kind/sufficiency/tie treatment, eligible references, prerequisites, and no-selection conditions; selected item must be frontier-eligible and policy in-scope.
- **Lawful negative, deferment, or stop:** Unknown policy, conflict, inapplicable policy, explicit no-selection, insufficient policy, no eligible work, unresolved ties, non-eligible named work.
- **Implementation witness:** `seed_runtime/examination_work_selection.py` and `tests/test_examination_work_selection.py` prove deterministic identity, mismatch refusal, no-selection states, explicit-work selection, refusal to select non-eligible classifications, no arbitrary tie-break, prerequisite-first selection, preservation of non-selected alternatives, and read-only/no-mutation behavior.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Non-selected eligible alternatives remain preserved and are not rejected; selection does not authorize, schedule, execute, or mark examined.
- **Remaining Unknown:** Whether selection chooses a “question” rather than work: current implementation selects examination work, not a question object. It may carry inquiry reference but does not establish question selection semantics generally.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported and implemented for examination work present movement; overlapping with but not identical to question selection.

### 10. Examination probe request binding

- **Consumed standing:** Exactly one selected work item plus future handoff, matching frontier, candidate-work set, and method-applicability projection.
- **Constitutional effect:** Binds a read-only probe request for the selected examination work and output representation.
- **Resulting standing:** Bound `ExaminationProbeRequest` and operational-realization handoff.
- **Required evidence and authority:** One selected item; handoff belongs to selection; frontier contains selected work; candidate-work record matches corpus/artifact hash/contract/capability; method-applicability record is present, applicable, and non-conflicting; constraints are preserved.
- **Lawful negative, deferment, or stop:** No selection, conflict selection, mismatched handoff/frontier/candidate/artifact/capability, missing method record, inapplicable/unknown/conflicting method applicability.
- **Implementation witness:** `tests/test_examination_probe_request.py` asserts matching artifacts produce an immutable bound request, mismatches fail deterministically, non-applicable/unknown methods cannot bind, conflicting applicability cannot bind, and output remains read-only/no-mutation.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** The request and handoff forbid provider choice, selected provider, registered operation, operation arguments, authorization decision, pending action, execution state, and tool name.
- **Remaining Unknown:** What later artifact authorizes or performs the probe. Probe request binding is not execution.
- **Classification:** Constitutionally supported and implemented as a post-selection/pre-execution boundary.

### 11. Examination performance and examined state

- **Consumed standing:** Existing result references supplied into frontier projection, not produced by frontier itself.
- **Constitutional effect:** Marks matching work item classification as examined when a completed matching result exists.
- **Resulting standing:** Examined classification in the frontier.
- **Required evidence and authority:** Existing result reference must match corpus member, artifact hash, work kind, capability id, and convention; result state must be completed.
- **Lawful negative, deferment, or stop:** Existing-result mismatch raises an error; examined classification prevents eligibility in the same frontier.
- **Implementation witness:** `seed_runtime/examination_frontier.py` checks existing result matching and sets `examined`; tests assert matching completed results classify examined and mismatches fail.
- **Counterexample preventing compression:** Boundary notes state examined work is not necessarily understood, admitted as knowledge, or complete.
- **Remaining Unknown:** The examined result's epistemic admission and retirement effect.
- **Classification:** Implemented locally as result visibility, not examination performance by this road.

## Frontier characterization

The repository does establish standings under which bounded constitutional material or examination work may remain valid across extended work and coexist with alternatives, but the support is family-local:

- `BoundedConstitutionalQuestion` preserves question identity, provenance, uncertainty, Unknowns, and caller fields without mutation.
- Registered-view selection preserves unsupported keys as uncertainty and does not infer evidence demand.
- Bounded inquiry frontier preserves selected need, native lineage, goal/horizon references, testimony identity, operative/non-operative clauses, Unknowns, conflicts, dependencies, and stop boundaries.
- Examination frontier preserves multiple work items with distinct classifications and explicit reasons.
- Examination work selection preserves non-selected eligible work and unchanged blocked/unsupported/deferred/examined/failed/conflict/unknown references.

The repository refuses to infer from mere frontier membership:

- that an item is selected now;
- that it is prioritized;
- that it is authorized for examination;
- that examination is being performed;
- that non-selection invalidates it;
- that deferral is refusal;
- that an answer/examined result retires the question or work;
- that a frontier is a backlog, queue, scheduler, or priority order.

A bounded constitutional question may remain valid as preserved testimony and may become newly selectable only when an implemented selector consumes the necessary frontier/policy/handoff evidence. It may become ineligible, superseded, answered, refused, or retired only through explicit local evidence; no reviewed artifact performs general question retirement.

## Selection characterization

The implemented selection road changes the standing of **one examination work item**, not the general standing of a bounded constitutional question. Selection consumes a frontier classification and policy sufficiency; it creates a selected-work reference and optional future probe-request handoff. It does not create eligibility, evidence territory, capability availability, authorization, execution, or completion.

Selection evidence includes:

- current frontier position through `frontier_id` and classified work items;
- eligibility and unchanged negative classifications;
- method-applicability reference via policy;
- prerequisites and tie treatment when policy kind requires them;
- no-selection, continuation, resource, supporting, contradicting, Unknown, and conflict testimony through policy;
- exact handoff consistency.

Selection does not invent ranking criteria. `all_eligible_no_order` with multiple eligible items and no lawful tie-break yields no selection. `prerequisite_first` can select only when one unique prerequisite-permitted item remains. Explicit work identity can select one eligible item. Explicit no-selection produces no selected work.

Selection may choose a work item for present examination movement and carry an inquiry reference. It does not choose an evidence territory globally, does not choose a provider/capability operation, and does not authorize examination performance. Capability/method constraints remain upstream or downstream depending on the local artifact.

## Examination boundary

The repository preserves these distinct standings:

```text
question selected / work selected
!= evidence territory admitted
!= capability selected
!= reliance warranted
!= examination authorized
!= examination performed
```

Current implementation supports:

- **Selected work:** `ExaminationWorkSelection` selects at most one eligible work item.
- **Evidence/work territory admitted:** `ExaminationFrontier` admits supplied corpus/work visibility and classifies it; `ExaminationProbeRequest` re-checks artifact identity/version and candidate contract.
- **Capability/method constraints preserved:** Candidate contracts and method-applicability projections preserve capability/method visibility; probe request binds constraints.
- **Reliance warranted:** Not generally established. Probe request preserves fidelity, attribution, and claim-treatment constraints, including not promoting results to Evidence or Fact.
- **Examination authorized:** Not established; authorization decision is explicitly forbidden in selection/probe request output.
- **Examination performed:** Not performed by these artifacts; only pre-existing completed results can be represented as examined.

## Compression map

- Formation and local establishment are compressed in `produce_bounded_constitutional_question()` only for explicit caller payload identity. They must not be expanded into goal-relative establishment.
- Frontier membership and eligibility are compressed inside `project_examination_frontier()` as classifications over supplied work, but selection is separate.
- Policy projection and selection are separate: policy can be applicable but insufficient; selector handoff must match exact policy/frontier evidence.
- Selection and probe-request binding are separate: selection creates a future handoff, while probe binding validates selected work against candidate/method/capability constraints.
- Examined classification and examination performance are separated: completed results can be represented, but frontier projection does not perform examination.

## Missing boundaries and Unknowns

1. No implemented producer evaluates unresolved pressure into question-worthy uncertainty.
2. No implemented goal-relative bounded-question establishment act consumes bounded-goal current position and produces a lawful question.
3. No general multi-question constitutional frontier registry was found.
4. No general question selection artifact was found; implemented selection is examination-work selection.
5. No artifact proves that registered-view selected output answers or retires a bounded question.
6. No general question retirement/supersession/deferment lifecycle was found beyond local deferred/examined/unsupported/refused states.
7. No current road authorizes or performs examination after probe-request binding.

## Final classification

The supported topology is a set of local constitutional roads with strong negative authority. The repository supports preserving many lawful bounded uncertainties and candidate/examination work items without selecting them. It supports present-movement selection only where exact frontier, policy, and handoff evidence make one eligible item selectable. It refuses backlog, queue, priority, scheduler, hidden producer, and execution-engine interpretations.

Bounded constitutional question frontier topology 001 complete.
