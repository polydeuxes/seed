## Recurring implementation evidence

### 1. Null / unknown / uncommitted as commitment posture

`constitutional_state_reconciliation.md` distinguishes a timeless constitutional rule from a current status statement. The rule is that Null persists until evidence justifies transition; the current status is that a subject may be `currently Null / unknown / uncommitted`.

The same reconciliation preserves the constitutional statement that `Null / unknown / uncommitted is the default commitment posture` and separately says unsupported evidence must leave a subject uncommitted.

`null_constitutional_readiness_characterization.md` sharpens the same recurrence. It characterizes Null as the default constitutional commitment posture for subjects not moved by evidence, and lists default non-commitment, evidence gate, stop legitimacy, boundary preservation, and current lawful condition as recurring responsibilities of Null. This report is evidence for the observable pattern, not permission to recover a runtime organism.

Observable characteristic recovered:

- Commitment terrain repeatedly contains a **posture of non-promotion**: a bounded subject may remain Null, unknown, unsupported, or uncommitted until evidence justifies movement.

### 2. Evidence sufficiency as the visible gate around commitment

`constitutional_state_reconciliation.md` says `Only evidence may move Null` is constitutional, while `Evidence is insufficient for this claim` and `evidence supports bounded transition` are current evaluations. It also treats insufficient evidence as a successful stop rather than a failure.

`current_lawful_condition_compression_reconciliation.md` preserves the narrower recurrence named `Commitment posture / Null condition`, including examples such as `Subject currently Null`, `Candidate currently unsupported`, and `Unsupported evidence leaves the subject uncommitted`. It distinguishes these from raw presence, selection, and completion.

Observable characteristic recovered:

- Commitment terrain is marked by **evidence-gated promotion or non-promotion**. It appears where the repository asks whether support is sufficient to move a subject, candidate, claim, responsibility, or status out of non-commitment.

### 3. Active/current language as visible operative status, not preserved knowledge alone

`docs/active_edge_frontier.md` says preserved knowledge alone does not explain what is currently pulling work forward. It distinguishes retained or preserved material from an active question, gap, contradiction, frontier, relationship, selected tension, active concern, current work position, next safe move, or continuation point.

The same document warns that Active Edge must not be confused with repository priority, implementation readiness, planner behavior, scheduler behavior, workflow behavior, or a new foundational type.

`docs/current_work_position_frontier.md` similarly says continuation may require preserving active context, current frontier, selected constraints, unresolved tensions, selection rationale, validation state, next safe moves, known non-goals, and authority boundaries. It emphasizes that information may be preserved while a later participant cannot tell what is active, why it is active, what constraints govern it, or what move is safe next.

Observable characteristic recovered:

- Commitment terrain includes **operative currentness**: something may be preserved but inactive, or current/active in a bounded sense. The recurring evidence does not determine ownership, lifecycle, scheduler, or topology; it only shows a visible contrast between retained material and presently operative status.

### 4. Completion, exhaustion, and stop as bounded status outcomes

`constitutional_state_reconciliation.md` treats completion audit outcomes such as `complete`, `continue`, `reassign`, `frontier`, and `insufficient evidence` as current family status after evaluation, not physiological response or runtime machinery.

`current_lawful_condition_compression_reconciliation.md` separates `Frontier / termination / completion condition` from commitment posture, but the Scout's commitment warrant included `completed` and `exhausted` because these words recur near status language. The evidence therefore supports only a visible adjacency: completion and exhaustion can appear as bounded statuses that constrain whether something remains open, stopped, complete, unresolved, or eligible for further movement.

Observable characteristic recovered:

- Commitment terrain sometimes appears through **bounded terminal or exhausted status**. That status can limit further commitment or continuation, but this characterization does not recover a lifecycle or state machine.

### 5. Eligibility and readiness as visible boundary words, not opportunity recovery

The Scout's commitment vocabulary included `eligible` and `ready`, while the neighboring Opportunity valley also used eligibility, authority, permission, and lawfulness terms. The recurring evidence supports a narrow observable distinction only.

Within commitment terrain, `eligible` and `ready` appear as status-like words: whether something is sufficiently bounded, supported, or current enough to be considered for movement. In Opportunity terrain, the same words can appear as permission or authority vocabulary. This characterization does not decide whether eligibility belongs to commitment, opportunity, or both.

Observable characteristic recovered:

- Commitment terrain can include **readiness-like status vocabulary**, but only when the evidence associates readiness with current support, non-commitment, bounded movement, completion, or exhaustion. Readiness does not by itself recover opportunity, permission, implementation readiness, or authority.

## Observable characteristics

According to recurring repository evidence, Constitutional Commitment is visibly distinguished by these characteristics:

1. **Default non-commitment vocabulary**: `Null`, `unknown`, `uncommitted`, `unsupported`, and candidate status recur around subjects that must not be promoted without evidence.
2. **Evidence-gated movement language**: commitment terrain appears where evidence is sufficient, insufficient, bounded, unsupported, or able to justify transition.
3. **Current or active status language**: `current`, `active`, `selected`, `operative`, and related words recur where the repository distinguishes preserved material from what is presently carrying work or status.
4. **Bounded terminal status language**: `completed`, `exhausted`, `stopped`, `frontier-only`, and `insufficient evidence` recur as visible outcomes that can constrain further movement without implying runtime lifecycle.
5. **Boundary-preserving refusals**: commitment evidence repeatedly refuses to convert notes, pressure, observations, candidates, presentation labels, frontiers, diagnostics, or reports into repository truth, ownership, authority, implementation, or runtime behavior merely because they are visible.
6. **Status without ownership**: the evidence often says that a subject, candidate, concern, family, or pressure is currently uncommitted, active, complete, exhausted, unsupported, or bounded; it does not identify a universal owner of those statuses.

## Preserved contrasts

These contrasts are preserved only where directly supported by recurring evidence. They are not architectural boundaries.

### Commitment != Opportunity

Commitment terrain asks whether a subject, candidate, family, concern, or pressure is uncommitted, current, supported, active, complete, exhausted, or otherwise status-bounded.

Opportunity terrain asks whether something may move, is permitted, lawful, allowed, eligible, or authorized. The word `eligible` can appear in both terrains, so this contrast is not a clean vocabulary boundary.

Supported contrast: commitment-like evidence emphasizes **status and non-promotion under evidence discipline**; opportunity-like evidence emphasizes **permission or authority to proceed**.

Unsupported: a recovered boundary, bridge, owner, district, or topology between Commitment and Opportunity.

### Commitment != Current Condition

Commitment terrain includes current status statements such as `Subject currently Null / unknown / uncommitted` and current evaluations such as insufficient evidence. Current Condition is broader: it can also include raw presence, selected concern, active work position, completion status, and other present-tense lawful conditions.

Supported contrast: commitment is one recurring kind of current status, especially around non-commitment and evidence-gated promotion. Current Condition is a broader descriptive umbrella in the reviewed reports.

Unsupported: a new layer named State, an implementation state engine, or a complete taxonomy of current conditions.

### Commitment != Cadence

Cadence terrain uses begin, continue, pause, resume, complete, next, and settle language. Commitment terrain may include completion or readiness vocabulary, but the recurring commitment evidence concerns whether something is supported, current, uncommitted, complete, exhausted, or eligible for movement under evidence discipline.

Supported contrast: cadence emphasizes temporal or continuation rhythm; commitment emphasizes status and lawful promotion or non-promotion.

Unsupported: a lifecycle connecting cadence and commitment.

### Commitment != Waiting

Waiting terrain includes stop, wait, unsupported, not-yet, insufficient evidence, and preserved unknowns. Commitment terrain overlaps where unsupported or insufficient evidence leaves a subject uncommitted.

Supported contrast: commitment preserves the status of non-promotion; waiting preserves a lawful hold, stop, or not-yet condition. The overlap is visible but not resolved.

Unsupported: a waiting mechanism, queue, scheduler, or state transition model.

### Commitment != Pressure

Pressure may orient attention or preserve unresolved tension, but recurring evidence says pressure is not command authority, recovery, implementation readiness, or truth. Commitment terrain is visible when pressure remains frontier-only, unsupported, active, current, or unable to promote a claim.

Supported contrast: pressure is orientation or unresolved tension; commitment is the status discipline that prevents pressure from becoming truth, authority, or implementation without evidence.

Unsupported: a pressure-to-commitment bridge or topology.

### Commitment != Inquiry Movement

Inquiry Movement includes orientation, question, selection, answer, termination, and exhaustion. Commitment terrain appears inside inquiry movement when evidence is insufficient, a subject remains uncommitted, or completion/exhaustion status is reached.

Supported contrast: inquiry movement describes visible inquiry steps or surfaces; commitment describes status constraints on whether movement can legitimately promote or stop.

Unsupported: an inquiry lifecycle, behavior model, or commitment owner.
