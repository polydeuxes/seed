## Invariant identity

### Constitutional work the grammar must always perform

The Competency Interrogation Grammar is a boundary-recovery discipline for constitutional review.

Across competencies, it must always reduce an apparent surface to repository-supported constitutional answers about:

- the subject being interrogated;
- the repository evidence that permits claims;
- what must already be true before the subject can answer lawfully;
- what output, artifact, preserved evidence, or handoff may leave the subject;
- where the subject begins and ends;
- what authority is actually granted;
- what apparent authority is not granted;
- what remains unknown;
- what later work may and must not rely on;
- how confident the characterization may be.

The invariant constitutional work is not to make the competency useful. It is to keep usefulness from expanding into unsupported authority.

The grammar must expose the smallest repository-supported subject, bind claims to evidence, separate granted authority from apparent authority, preserve lawful ignorance, and stop before redesign, aspiration, recommendation, execution, planning, framework creation, or implementation recovery.

### What cannot change without ceasing to be the recovered grammar

The recovered grammar would cease to be itself if it stopped doing any of the following recurring work:

1. **Subject reduction.** It must prevent surface names, CLI flags, presentation labels, candidate phrases, public outputs, or investigation titles from becoming owners or authorities.
2. **Evidence binding.** It must prevent vocabulary, elegance, repetition, or operator interest from replacing repository evidence.
3. **Precondition discipline.** It must prevent a competency or artifact from appearing self-grounding.
4. **Output boundary discipline.** It must distinguish what leaves the subject from what does not cross the boundary.
5. **Boundary and locality discipline.** It must prevent a local competency from becoming universal governance, registry, planning, gateway, or implementation ownership.
6. **Granted-authority separation.** It must distinguish participation, invocation, consumption, trust, constraints, and lawful use from mere visibility.
7. **Negative authority.** It must name tempting but unsupported authority.
8. **Unknown preservation.** It must preserve unsupported conclusions and lawful unknowns instead of inferring through them.
9. **Reliance narrowing.** It must state what Seed may rely on and must not rely on.
10. **Confidence calibration.** It must present bounded findings as bounded findings, not as complete recovery.

The exact section names and strict ordering are not invariant. Repository evidence supports recurring constitutional work, not a mandatory sequence.

## Invariant inputs

### What must already exist before this grammar can lawfully operate

The grammar can lawfully operate only when there is enough recurring repository evidence to interrogate a bounded subject without inventing one.

Invariant prerequisites are:

- a selected or recoverable subject whose identity can be bounded by repository evidence;
- implementation evidence, tests, app behavior, diagnostics, or prior repository reports sufficient to ground claims;
- observable evidence classes that can distinguish what is present, absent, preserved, movement-permitting, or stop-causing;
- some possible constitutional question about authority, boundary, reliance, unknowns, or stop conditions;
- enough locality to avoid universalizing the subject.

The subject may be implementation-backed, diagnostic, CLI-visible, report-backed, bridge-like, candidate-like, district-like, neighborhood-like, or completion/evaluation-backed. The common invariant is not the substrate. The common invariant is repository-supported interrogability.

### What never enters the grammar

The grammar does not admit the following as lawful inputs by themselves:

- operator desire for implementation;
- recurring vocabulary without implementation or repository evidence;
- a public name treated as ownership;
- output appearance treated as authority;
- candidate presence treated as readiness;
- visibility treated as permission;
- verification treated as action authorization;
- related material treated as importance;
- agreement treated as truth;
- missing evidence treated as license to infer;
- aspiration treated as architecture.

These may appear as subjects of refusal or boundary analysis. They do not enter as authority.

## Invariant outputs

### What must always leave a completed interrogation

A completed interrogation must leave a bounded constitutional characterization, not an implementation plan.

At minimum, completed interrogation output must preserve:

- the subject's smallest supported identity;
- the evidence basis for claims;
- preconditions or eligible evidence required before lawful operation;
- outputs or handoff boundaries where a handoff exists;
- boundaries and neighboring responsibilities;
- granted authority and constraints;
- negative authority;
- unsupported answers, unknowns, and stop conditions;
- lawful reliance and non-reliance;
- confidence.

The output may be more compressed or differently labeled in small reports. It may fold inputs into evidence, outputs into handoff or reliance, locality into boundaries, or negative authority into counterexamples. It must not lose the constitutional work.

### What must never leave

The completed interrogation must never leave as if recovered:

- an implementation design;
- an interrogation engine;
- a runtime framework;
- a campaign or slice recommendation;
- a mandatory universal ordering;
- an owner not supported by evidence;
- a route from inquiry to execution;
- policy permission;
- planner authority;
- mutation authority;
- cluster truth from diagnostic-only findings;
- resolved unknowns that evidence did not resolve;
- stabilized vocabulary merely because the report used it.

A truthful interrogation can make implementation pressure visible. It must not convert that visibility into implementation recovery.

## Invariant negative authority

The grammar must never become any of the following, regardless of locality:

- **Implementation recovery.** It may characterize constitutional shape; it may not recover code, owners, slices, engines, or runtime mechanisms.
- **A universal framework.** It may observe recurring grammar; it may not impose a global interrogation framework or mandatory sequence.
- **Inquiry execution.** It may review competency boundaries; it may not merge itself with routing, planning, dispatch, work selection, or execution.
- **Authority generator.** It may name granted authority; it may not create authority from visibility, verification, output form, inventory presence, recommendation-like phrasing, or agreement.
- **Truth promotion.** It may preserve diagnostic or inquiry findings; it may not silently promote findings into cluster truth or domain facts.
- **Vocabulary stabilizer.** It may use labels to explain evidence; it may not promote presentation vocabulary into repository knowledge without implementation evidence.
- **Unknown resolver.** It may preserve typed unknowns and unsupported answers; it may not resolve them by compression, elegance, analogy, or recurrence alone.
- **Governance owner.** It may state lawful reliance; it may not become policy, permission, consumer governance, registry ownership, or universal competency governance.

The recurring negative authority is the constitutional refusal that apparent usefulness is not constitutional permission.

## Invariant lawful termination

### When the grammar must stop

The grammar must stop when repository-supported characterization has reached the evidence boundary.

Recurring stop conditions are:

1. **Evidence boundary reached.** Claims can no longer be supported by implementation evidence, tests, diagnostics, app behavior, or prior repository reports.
2. **Unknown preserved.** A question remains unsupported and must be named rather than inferred through.
3. **Authority boundary reached.** The subject's lawful participation, reliance, or output has been stated, and any further movement would become permission, planning, policy, execution, or implementation.
4. **Boundary distinction recovered.** Neighboring responsibilities have been separated enough to prevent collapse, and further separation would require new evidence.
5. **Negative authority named.** Apparent-but-ungranted authority has been refused, and further work would elaborate beyond evidence.
6. **Reliance bounded.** Seed's lawful reliance and non-reliance have been stated.
7. **Confidence calibrated.** The certainty level has been bounded by the evidence.

Lawful termination is not project completion, implementation readiness, or campaign selection. It is the point where continued movement would become redesign, recommendation, aspiration, framework creation, implementation recovery, or unsupported constitutional law.

## Confidence

High confidence that the invariant constitutional work includes subject identity, evidence binding, boundaries, granted authority, negative authority, unknown/stop preservation, lawful reliance, lawful termination, and confidence calibration. These recur across competency interrogations, methodology reconciliation, the grammar investigation, bridge investigations, candidate characterizations, surveys, and completion/evaluation artifacts.

Medium-high confidence that preconditions/inputs and outputs/handoff are invariant work even when their labels are not invariant. Medium confidence that locality, adjacency, resolution, maturity, continuity, and methodology audit are always distinct grammar elements. Repository evidence supports them as recurring or useful, but not always independent from boundaries, unknowns, completion, evidence, or confidence.

Low confidence in any claim that the grammar has a mandatory ordered sequence or that repository evidence supports implementation of an interrogation engine, runtime framework, or universal methodology owner.
