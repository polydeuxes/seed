## Recurring higher-order patterns observed

The reviewed characterization supports recurring higher-order groupings only at Scout resolution. These are observational groupings of recurring characteristics, not districts, topology, ownership, bridge recovery, implementation recommendations, or stabilized vocabulary.

### Pattern 1: Temporal provenance and authority separation

**Recurring characteristics supporting the grouping**

- Several neighborhoods are distinguished by what kind of temporal authority a timestamp or temporal statement has.
- The recurring pressure is non-collapse: external occurrence, Seed observation, upstream source assertion, Seed justification, Seed preservation, and supported temporal claim are repeatedly kept separate.
- The characterization repeatedly refuses to treat one timestamp as automatically authoritative for another temporal meaning.
- The recurring implementation evidence, as carried forward by the characterization, is that observation paths, source samples, event timestamps, evidence/support use, and temporal claims each carry different authority limits.

**Participating neighborhoods**

- Occurrence / external-reality time
- Observation / collection time
- Source sample / source-asserted time
- Knowledge / justification time
- Preservation / event-ledger time
- Temporal claim / revision time

**Supporting recurring implementation evidence**

- Occurrence time recurs as the external happening that Seed often does not directly know.
- Observation time recurs as collection, reading, reporting, ingestion, or acceptance timing.
- Source sample time recurs as upstream source-supplied timestamp evidence, most clearly around Prometheus sample timestamps.
- Knowledge time recurs as Seed's justified use of support rather than external occurrence or ledger persistence.
- Preservation time recurs as the `Event.timestamp` meaning in append-only history.
- Temporal claim / revision time recurs as supported, revisable understanding of time rather than a raw timestamp field.

**Confidence**

Medium-high. The recurring non-collapse pattern is explicit across six reviewed neighborhoods. Confidence is not high because this Scout did not inspect implementation directly and does not recover a higher-order owner, district, topology, or shared schema.

### Pattern 2: Operational visibility timing separate from temporal provenance

**Recurring characteristics supporting the grouping**

- Several neighborhoods concern Seed-facing visibility of work rather than temporal truth about external conditions or evidence provenance.
- The recurring pressure is separation between measuring or showing operational paths and making temporal claims about reality.
- The characterization repeatedly distinguishes local elapsed duration reporting, transient progress emission cadence, and cache-path explanation from occurrence, observation, source, knowledge, preservation, and temporal-claim authority.
- The recurring implementation evidence, as carried forward by the characterization, is that diagnostic phase timings, status cadence, and cache visibility appear in local operational surfaces without earning a repository-wide timing schema.

**Participating neighborhoods**

- Diagnostic elapsed / phase timing
- Progress cadence / status timing
- Cache visibility time

**Supporting recurring implementation evidence**

- Diagnostic elapsed / phase timing recurs in local timing structures such as projection diagnostics, state-build/current-facts cache debug timing, knowledge reachability timing, and observation ingestion timing.
- Progress cadence / status timing recurs as elapsed wall-clock gating for transient status visibility rather than duration reporting.
- Cache visibility time recurs around state, projection, current-facts, and knowledge-reachability cache path explanation: snapshot identity, hit/miss, incremental replay, and events applied.

**Confidence**

Medium-high. The recurring distinction between operational visibility timing and temporal provenance is explicit. Confidence is not high because the characterization also preserves that these local surfaces have not converged into a shared timing architecture.

### Pattern 3: Claim/support separation inside time reasoning

**Recurring characteristics supporting the grouping**

- Some reviewed neighborhoods recur around support material, while another recurs around the supported temporal statement built from support.
- The grouping appears because the characterization repeatedly separates raw or contextual timestamps from claims about when something happened.
- The recurring implementation evidence, as carried forward by the characterization, is that source sample timestamps, observation timestamps, knowledge/justification timing, and preservation events may support or contextualize a temporal claim without becoming the claim or the external occurrence itself.

**Participating neighborhoods**

- Observation / collection time
- Source sample / source-asserted time
- Knowledge / justification time
- Preservation / event-ledger time
- Temporal claim / revision time
- Occurrence / external-reality time as the external condition a temporal claim may concern, not as support material itself

**Supporting recurring implementation evidence**

- Source sample time may support occurrence claims but is not automatically occurrence time.
- Observation time is evidence provenance, not occurrence authority.
- Knowledge time marks justified use of support.
- Preservation time records Seed's append-only preservation act.
- Temporal claim / revision time organizes support into revisable statements with possible exact timestamps, intervals, bounds, estimates, confidence, support, derivation method, source clock metadata, revision, and supersession.

**Confidence**

Medium. The claim/support distinction is recurring and directly visible in the characterization, but the exact temporal-claim schema remains future work in the reviewed evidence.

## Answer to the bounded question

According to recurring repository evidence preserved in the reviewed characterization, the already characterized Time neighborhoods have earned limited recurring higher-order groupings at Scout resolution:

1. temporal provenance and authority separation;
2. operational visibility timing separate from temporal provenance;
3. claim/support separation inside time reasoning.

These groupings are natural recurrences of characteristics across already characterized neighborhoods. They are not recovered districts, topology, identities, ownership, bridges, runtime behavior, scheduling, implementation guidance, or architectural importance.
