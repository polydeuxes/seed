## Independently surveyed bridge positions

#### What is preserved?

Provenance, event support, replayability, auditability, authority boundary, unknowns, and separation between history, projection, current belief, evidence, facts, diagnostics, views, and output are preserved.

#### Which neighboring responsibilities remain independent?

Append-only history remains independent from affected-state projection, cache/debug report assembly, read models, diagnostics, current lawful condition, truth verification, runtime behavior, scheduling, lifecycle, and host or cluster mutation.

#### Confidence

High that the topology is availability and replay movement from preserved history toward projection-facing consumers, with strong non-crossing truth and mutation boundaries.

## Observable bridge properties

Across the surveyed positions, repository evidence supports these observable properties without naming bridge kinds:

- **What crosses:** selected artifacts, bounded content, exact identities, evidence posture, provenance, selected values or relations, authority boundaries, negative authority, confidence, dimensions, status fields, and replayable history.
- **What cannot cross:** truth by existence, external authority by visibility, mutation authority, ownership, runtime responsibility, universal architecture, unregistered vocabulary, unsupported semantic inference, presentation labels as knowledge, and provider-native excess meaning.
- **Directionality:** some positions are strongly directional from source to consumer, such as history toward projection input, provider grammar toward observation, selected request toward consumer execution, and constitutional representation toward consumer views. Others are reciprocal only as constrained evidence or discipline, such as Question Shape and Survey Warrant Family.
- **Translation:** provider/external grammar positions visibly change representation while preserving warrant. Request construction changes selected pieces into invocation-ready request shape. Projection-facing positions change preserved or collected material into derived view/report material.
- **Promotion:** promotion is repeatedly bounded or refused. Question shape becomes implementation-backed only through exact registered Question Family rows. Observations or external material become stronger claims only through evidence and promotion rules. History does not promote itself to truth.
- **Authority movement:** authority generally narrows rather than expands. Provider and external authority are reduced. Survey warrant constrains use. Request execution receives only namespace mutation authority for selected fields. Projection receives read/view authority, not truth or mutation authority.
- **Identity movement:** exact identities can survive crossing when supported: event identity, question-family identity, provider-shaped subject identity, observation subject, dispatch surface, and source identity. Identity does not generalize into ontology or ownership without evidence.
- **Information movement:** information is selected and reduced. Raw forms, unsupported fields, and excess meanings are often discarded. Preserved information commonly includes provenance, selected values, relations, dimensions, statuses, and confidence.
- **Warrant movement:** warrant can survive translation as bounded lawful reliance, but it does not automatically strengthen. It carries negative authority and unknown preservation.
- **Replayability:** strongest in preserved-history and event/projection positions. It is weaker or absent in request, question, and external translation positions unless the artifact is explicitly preserved as history or evidence.
- **Dependency:** many crossings require already completed neighboring work: selected invocation requires eligibility and selection first; projection-facing reports require collected evidence streams; observation translation requires provider validation; survey warrant requires multiple artifacts rather than a single phrase.
- **Availability:** history changes availability for replay and audit; Question Family rows make exact public inquiry identities available; provider translation makes selected observations available; consumer projections make bounded views available.
- **Locality:** locality is repeatedly preserved as scope, source path, host, endpoint, repository path, unit, package dimension, family row, diagnostic surface, or consumer view. Crossings do not erase locality unless the receiving artifact explicitly changes resolution.
- **Confidence:** confidence is commonly preserved or calibrated rather than inflated. Several bridge positions explicitly state high confidence for narrow artifacts and lower confidence for universal models or stronger architectural claims.

## Observable differences

The recovered bridge positions differ by observable topology:

1. **Crossing artifact differs.** Some positions move event history, some move exact request objects, some move row evidence, some move provider-derived observations, some move constitutional warrant, and some move projected view material.
2. **Directionality differs.** History-to-projection, provider-to-observation, selected-request-to-consumer, and constitutional-representation-to-view are mostly one-way. Question-shape/warrant/family positions have reciprocal evidence or discipline movement without authority merger.
3. **Replayability differs.** Preserved history is replayable transition input. Provider observations can become preserved evidence. Request execution is a local handoff rather than replay authority. Question-shape bridges preserve recurrence evidence but not replay semantics.
4. **Translation depth differs.** Provider grammar may pass through separated intermediate records or compressed direct observation construction. External grammar may change form extensively. Selected invocation construction performs narrow field copying. Projection performs derivation from already preserved material.
5. **Promotion rules differ.** Question Shape requires exact registration to become a Question Family. External/provider material requires observation/evidence/promotion rules to become represented claims. Preserved history enables projection but does not become truth. Consumer views do not promote presentation vocabulary into knowledge.
6. **Authority movement differs.** Selected invocation grants a narrow namespace mutation. Provider/external translation reduces authority. Survey warrant constrains conclusions. Projection/view positions grant read/view authority. History supplies audit/projection authority but not truth authority.
7. **Identity movement differs.** Dispatch identity is exact and field-level. Question Family identity is row-level. Provider identity is selected and normalized. External identity is admitted only at supported resolution. Reality-layer transitions preserve source/provenance identity without claiming external truth.
8. **Information loss differs.** Provider grammar drops raw syntax and excess semantics. Question bridges drop unsupported free-text inference. History preserves event material but refuses current truth. Projection selects and summarizes rather than preserving all possible interpretations in the view.
9. **Dependency differs.** Some bridges depend on prior eligibility, selection, or provider validation. Some depend on multiple artifacts showing recurrence. Some depend on append-only preservation. Some depend on already projected or collected evidence streams.
10. **Locality differs.** Provider and observation crossings carry concrete source or dimension locality. Question-family crossings carry inventory-row locality. History/projection crossings carry event/workspace/projection locality. Reality-layer crossings carry source, import, and consumer-surface locality.
11. **Confidence differs.** Implementation-backed request and row bridges have high confidence for exact fields. Constitutional translation has high confidence for warrant substance but medium confidence on naming. Universal architecture claims remain low or unsupported.
