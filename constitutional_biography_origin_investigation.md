## Candidate boundary 1: world observation exists before organism participation

### What exists before the organism?

External observations may already exist as world-side or source-side material: operator memories, external artifacts, provider records, files, prior reports, testimony, or observations possessed by other participants. Repository evidence supports that external / provider / user material can enter as evidence or observation with provenance, and that provider-local or external forms may precede Seed-local representation.

This candidate accepts the operator's orientation only in bounded form: Null does not mean the universe is unobserved. Null concerns this organism's commitment posture, not the total absence of external observers or external artifacts.

### What belongs to the organism?

The organism owns only what has crossed a repository-supported constitutional boundary into Seed-local bounded presence, preservation, observation, event history, evidence, fact, projection, or read surface under a competent local authority. Before such a boundary, external observations may be real to their holders, but they are not Seed's constitutional biography.

### What constitutional transition separates them?

The separating transition is local admission of external material into Seed's authority boundary:

```text
external/source-side observation or artifact
→ bounded external representation present to a competent local responsibility
→ local preservation / inspection / validation / observation / event recording if warranted
```

The transition is not truth acquisition. It is the first repository-visible boundary at which Seed may lawfully say that a bounded representation or signal is present or preserved, with negative authority over its represented truth.

### What enters the organism's biography?

Only the Seed-local constitutional occurrence enters biography: that this bounded representation, signal, note, input event, artifact, observation, evidence item, or event was received, preserved, validated, observed, or recorded by a competent local responsibility, to the degree repository evidence supports.

If recorded as append-only event history, it may become part of preserved constitutional history. If preserved outside event history, it may remain a bounded orientation or probe record rather than event-ledger biography.

### What explicitly does not?

The external participant's entire prior history does not enter. The truth of the represented claim does not enter merely because it was supplied. External memories, world chronology, provider semantics, operator intent, artifact provenance beyond what is locally preserved, and objective world history do not become Seed biography by contact.

### What constitutional responsibility begins at that boundary?

The organism becomes responsible for preserving the local authority boundary: provenance where available, source / path / content limits, confidence or support limits, negative authority, unknowns, lawful stop, and non-promotion. It may rely only on the bounded content that repository evidence warrants.

### What explicitly is this boundary not?

It is not a universal first observation. It is not a claim that Seed creates the world's first observation. It is not objective world history. It is not a global receiver, global intake engine, or runtime bootstrap. It is not ownership of all external history.

## Candidate boundary 2: first preserved append-only event begins biography

### What exists before the organism?

Before the first lawfully recorded event, there may be external observations, artifacts, and testimony, and there may be source-side reality. For the organism itself, recurring evidence supports no preserved append-only constitutional history yet.

### What belongs to the organism?

The first append-only event belongs to the organism's preserved constitutional history for its scope when it is lawfully recorded by a competent local responsibility. It begins a preserved constitutional past that later bounded derivation, inspection, orientation, and explanation may use.

### What constitutional transition separates them?

The transition is:

```text
no preserved append-only constitutional history
→ first lawful append-only event
→ first preserved constitutional past
```

This is a history boundary, not necessarily an observation boundary.

### What enters the organism's biography?

The event's existence in append order enters preserved constitutional history: its identity, kind, workspace, actor, payload, timestamp, correlation / causation / provenance fields where modeled, and the fact that this event was lawfully recorded.

### What explicitly does not?

The event does not import the whole external world, the source's prior biography, the truth of all payload contents, or a complete self-understanding. It does not automatically produce projection, verification, orientation, explanation, or recognized self-history. It does not decide a universal first event kind.

### What constitutional responsibility begins at that boundary?

A preserved-history responsibility begins: later projections, audits, and explanations must keep event history as replayable input, preserve provenance and conflicts, keep caches and views subordinate, and refuse to treat event existence as objective truth.

### What explicitly is this boundary not?

It is not the universal boundary between pre-existing external observations and biography in every path, because some local records may be preserved outside event-ledger history. It is not automatically first observation. It is not runtime startup, lifecycle, boot, scheduler tick, or Begin event.

## Candidate boundary 3: first canonical Observation begins biography

### What exists before the organism?

External observations, provider-local records, files, operator testimony, and bounded external representations may exist before any Seed-local canonical `Observation` exists. Some repository paths inspect or preserve such material before constructing an Observation.

### What belongs to the organism?

A canonical Observation belongs to the organism's observation-level biography only after a source / provider / import contract emits or validates a local Observation under repository rules. If ingested, downstream observation and evidence events may also belong to preserved history.

### What constitutional transition separates them?

The transition is:

```text
external/provider/local intake material
→ accepted source/provider contract
→ valid Seed-local Observation
→ optional observation/evidence/fact events under local ingestion rules
```

### What enters the organism's biography?

The local Observation's bounded content enters: source, subject, predicate or relation, value, time, confidence, metadata, dimensions, and provenance to the extent preserved. If recorded, the corresponding events enter append-only history.

### What explicitly does not?

The provider's full native meaning, excess syntax, external truth, operator intent, or objective world history does not enter. Observation does not equal evidence or fact by identity. Observation does not become verification by appearing.

### What constitutional responsibility begins at that boundary?

Observation grammar and warrant responsibilities begin: preserve source identity, relation/value, provenance, confidence, dimensions, negative authority, and non-promotion; separate observation from evidence, fact, projection, and truth.

### What explicitly is this boundary not?

It is not the general origin of constitutional biography. The repository rejects canonical Observation as a universal first constitutional observation and shows bounded representation can precede Observation in some paths.

## Candidate boundary 4: first recognition or account of self-history begins biography

### What exists before the organism?

Before recognition, preserved events or local records may already exist. External observations and external histories may also exist. The organism may have preserved constitutional past without yet having a bounded self-history account.

### What belongs to the organism?

A recognition or account belongs to the organism only as a bounded read, inspection, orientation, replay, or explanation over preserved history or derived evidence. It is not the history itself; it is a later account of already preserved material.

### What constitutional transition separates them?

The transition is:

```text
preserved local material exists
→ bounded read / inspection / orientation / replay / explanation surface accesses it
→ bounded self-history visibility or account
```

### What enters the organism's biography?

The account may enter biography only if it is itself preserved or warranted as a local artifact. Otherwise it is visibility over biography, not biography by identity.

### What explicitly does not?

Recognition does not retroactively import external world history. It does not turn projection into verification. It does not make presentation vocabulary constitutional law. It does not mutate cluster truth unless a separate lawful event records such mutation.

### What constitutional responsibility begins at that boundary?

The organism becomes responsible for keeping inspection and explanation bounded: distinguish history, projection, cache, view, evidence, fact, answer, and mutation; preserve unknowns and unsupported conclusions.

### What explicitly is this boundary not?

It is not the origin of biography. It is the origin of self-history visibility or account. It is not projection by identity, startup architecture, or a universal recognition mechanism.

## Comparison

### Recurring history boundaries

- External world-side history can pre-exist Seed-local participation, but repository evidence does not give Seed ownership over that history.
- Seed preserved constitutional history begins only when a competent local responsibility records a lawful append-only event.
- Append-only history is projection / audit authority, not objective reality, complete memory, verification, or truth.
- Non-ledger preservation may create bounded orientation material without becoming event-ledger history.

### Recurring biography boundaries

- Biography is narrower than world history and narrower than all external observations.
- The organism's biography begins where a local constitutional occurrence is admitted, preserved, observed, or recorded under Seed authority.
- The strongest recurring boundary is not the world's first observation, but the crossing from external/source-side material into bounded Seed-local presence or preservation with warrant and negative authority.
- Preserved event history is a stronger biography boundary for append-only constitutional past, but it is not the only possible local bounded record because current evidence includes inquiry-note preservation outside event-ledger projection.
- Self-history recognition is later than biography-origin material; it is an account over preserved or derived evidence.

### Recurring ownership boundaries

- External observations remain owned by their external/source-side holders unless and until a specific local artifact is admitted under Seed authority.
- Seed owns the local record and its warranted limits, not the external participant's whole observation, memory, intent, semantics, or prior chronology.
- Warrant governs what may be carried forward: selected bounded content, provenance/support, role, authority limit, negative authority, unknowns, and confidence.
- Presence, recurrence, vocabulary, testimony, projection, or event existence alone does not create ownership of truth.

### Recurring transition boundaries

- Null to bounded external representation present to a competent local responsibility is the recurring earliest transition after Null where supported by implementation evidence.
- Bounded representation to canonical Observation occurs only through source/provider/import contracts that validate or construct Observation-level content.
- No preserved event history to first preserved event creates first preserved constitutional past for that scope.
- Preserved history to self-history account requires bounded read / inspection / orientation / replay / explanation.
- Any unsupported transition must stop at Null, unknown, unsupported, candidate, or insufficient evidence.

### Surviving candidate distinctions

1. **External/source-side observation versus Seed-local bounded presence** survives as the core biography-origin distinction. It answers the task most directly: pre-existing observations remain external until a competent local responsibility admits a bounded representation or signal.
2. **No preserved Seed history versus first preserved append-only event** survives as the strongest preserved-history boundary, but it is narrower than the total biography-origin boundary because not every bounded local preservation is event-ledger history.
3. **External/provider material versus canonical Observation** survives as an observation-level boundary, but not as the universal origin of biography.
4. **Preserved history versus self-history account** survives as a recognition boundary, not an origin boundary.

### Rejected candidate distinctions

- **A universal first-observation claim** is rejected. Recurring evidence has not earned one unique first constitutional observation.
- **Objective World History becomes Seed biography** is rejected. External history does not become organism biography merely because it exists.
- **First preserved event is always first observation** is rejected. First event kind is not universal and may be input, observation, evidence, fact, decision, or another kind.
- **Canonical Observation begins all biography** is rejected. Bounded representation and local preservation can precede canonical Observation.
- **Operator testimony as truth** is rejected. Testimony may be preserved as testimony or bounded representation, but it does not become truth, fact, implementation, authority, mutation, or biography of the world by being supplied.
- **Projection or self-history account as biography origin** is rejected. Projection and recognition are downstream visibility or derivation over preserved material.
- **Runtime architecture or central ownership** is rejected. The repository supports distributed local responsibilities and constitutional boundaries, not a recovered universal owner.
